### How To Wash Ruggable Outdoor Rug: A Quick Answer

Washing your Ruggable outdoor rug is straightforward. Simply detach the rug cover from its pad and machine wash it in cold water on a delicate cycle using mild, non-bleach detergent. Tumble dry on low heat or, ideally, hang it to air dry completely before reattaching it to the rug pad.

## Pre-Washing Steps: Preparing Your Outdoor Ruggable

Before you put your Ruggable outdoor rug cover into the washing machine, some preparation is key. This initial work helps remove surface dirt. It also makes the machine wash more effective. Taking these steps ensures a thorough cleaning. It also protects your washing machine from excess debris.

First, take the rug cover outside. Give it a good shake. This removes loose dirt, leaves, and other small debris that might have gathered. For larger rugs, you might need help with this step. Shaking off the surface layer is simple. It makes a big difference in the cleaning process.

Next, vacuum the rug cover. Use a strong vacuum cleaner to pick up any remaining dirt or pet hair. Pay attention to any matted areas or places where dirt seems embedded. Regular vacuuming helps keep your rug clean between washes. ### Dealing with Specific Outdoor Stains

Outdoor rugs often face tough stains. Mud, food spills, and even mildew can appear. Spot treating these stains before washing is important. Use a mild stain remover or a mixture of gentle soap and water. Apply it directly to the stain. Let it sit for a few minutes. Then gently blot with a clean cloth. Do not rub the stain, as this can spread it.

For mildew, act quickly. Mildew loves damp outdoor conditions. A solution of white vinegar and water often works well. Spray it on the affected area. Let it sit for a bit, then gently brush. Always test any stain remover on a small, hidden area first. This prevents potential damage to the rug's color or material.

## Drying Your Ruggable Outdoor Rug for Longevity

Proper drying is as important as washing for your Ruggable outdoor rug. Incorrect drying can lead to damage. It can cause shrinkage, mildew, or odors. Always aim for a complete and thorough dry. This step ensures your rug stays fresh and lasts for many seasons.

The best way to dry your Ruggable outdoor rug cover is to air dry it. Hang it over a sturdy clothesline or railing. Ensure good airflow around the rug. This helps it dry evenly and quickly. Air drying prevents potential heat damage. It also conserves energy. It is the most gentle method for the rug fibers.

Air drying also helps prevent mildew. Outdoor rugs are prone to mildew if they stay damp. The outdoor Ruggable materials resist mildew, but complete drying is still key. If you live in a humid area, choose a sunny day for drying. This speeds up the process. Make sure both sides of the rug get exposed to air. This prevents moisture from getting trapped. If air drying is not possible, you can use a dryer. Use the lowest heat setting available. Tumble dry on low heat or air fluff. Remove the rug cover as soon as it feels dry to the touch. Over-drying can damage the rug's fibers. It can also cause the backing to degrade over time. Check it frequently to avoid this.

Never put your Ruggable outdoor rug cover back on its pad if it is still damp. Even slightly damp spots can lead to mildew growth. This can cause unpleasant smells and damage the rug over time. ## FAQ Section

### Can I put my Ruggable outdoor rug in a regular washing machine?
Yes, you can. Ruggable outdoor rug covers are designed to be machine washable. For larger rug sizes, you might need a commercial-sized washing machine. Always ensure your home machine has enough capacity. Overloading can prevent proper cleaning and may damage your machine.

### How often should I wash my outdoor Ruggable rug?
The frequency depends on use and local conditions. If your rug sees heavy traffic or is in a dusty area, wash it every few weeks. For less frequent use or cleaner environments, every 2-3 months might be enough. Regular shaking and vacuuming will help extend the time between washes.

### What if my Ruggable outdoor rug has stubborn stains?
For stubborn stains, pre-treat them before machine washing. Apply a mild stain remover or a mixture of gentle soap and water directly to the stain. Let it sit for a few minutes. Then gently blot the area with a clean cloth. Avoid rubbing, as this can spread the stain.

### Can I use bleach on my Ruggable outdoor rug?
No, do not use bleach on your Ruggable outdoor rug. Bleach can damage the rug's fibers and cause discoloration. Always use a mild, non-bleach detergent for washing. Harsh chemicals can shorten the lifespan of your rug. They can also make the colors fade over time.

### How do I prevent mildew on my outdoor Ruggable?
To prevent mildew, ensure your Ruggable outdoor rug is completely dry after washing. Air drying is best. If using a dryer, use the lowest heat setting. Also, ensure your outdoor area drains well. Do not reattach the cover to the pad if it is still damp. Regular cleaning helps prevent mildew growth.
