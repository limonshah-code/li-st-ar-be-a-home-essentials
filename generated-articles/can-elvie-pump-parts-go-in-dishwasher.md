#### Elvie Pump Original Components

The Elvie Pump Original is discreet and consists of several parts. These parts work together inside your bra. You can clean many of these parts in the dishwasher.

### Beyond the Dishwasher: Manual Cleaning and Sterilization

While dishwashers offer convenience, they are not the only, or always the best, cleaning method. Some Elvie parts are not dishwasher safe. Also, manual cleaning and sterilization provide deeper sanitization for all components. I always incorporate both methods into my cleaning routine to ensure everything is thoroughly clean.

The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) provides clear guidelines for breast pump hygiene. These recommendations emphasize the importance of cleaning and sterilizing. This helps protect your baby from harmful germs. Understanding these methods gives you more flexibility and control over your pump care.

#### Hand Washing Techniques

Hand washing is a reliable method for cleaning all Elvie pump parts, especially those not suitable for the dishwasher. This includes the delicate silicone valves and diaphragms. I find hand washing ensures every nook and cranny is addressed.

Follow these steps for effective hand washing:
1.  **Wash Your Hands:** Always start with clean hands. Use soap and water for at least 20 seconds.
2.  **Disassemble All Parts:** Take apart all pump components that came into contact with breast milk. Separate each piece.
3.  **Rinse Thoroughly:** Hold each part under cool, running water immediately after pumping. This removes milk residue before it dries.
4.  **Prepare a Wash Basin:** Fill a clean wash basin (not your kitchen sink directly) with hot, soapy water. Use a mild, unscented dish soap.
5.  **Scrub Each Part:** Scrub each component with a clean brush dedicated only for pump parts. Pay attention to crevices and openings. For small parts, a specialized bottle brush or nipple brush works well.
6.  **Rinse Again:** Rinse all parts thoroughly under running water to remove soap residue.
7.  **Air Dry:** Place cleaned parts on a clean, dedicated drying rack or a clean paper towel. Allow them to air dry completely. Do not use a cloth towel, as it can transfer germs.

Hand washing allows for a detailed cleaning process. It helps extend the life of delicate components that might otherwise be damaged by a dishwasher.

#### Sterilization Methods

Sterilization provides an extra layer of protection by killing germs that might survive regular washing. This is especially important for newborns or babies with weakened immune systems. The CDC recommends sterilizing pump parts at least once daily for infants under two months, preterm infants, or those with compromised immune systems. After this period, daily sterilization may not be necessary. Regular washing is often sufficient.

Several methods are effective for sterilizing Elvie pump parts:

*   **Boiling:**
    *   Place cleaned, disassembled parts in a large pot.
    *   Cover them with water.
    *   Bring the water to a rolling boil for 5 minutes.
    *   Carefully remove parts with clean tongs.
    *   Place them on a clean drying rack to air dry completely.
    *   Be cautious, as boiling can be harsh on some plastics over time.

*   **Steam Sterilization:**
    *   **Microwave Steam Bags:** These bags are convenient. Place cleaned parts inside with a small amount of water. Microwave according to bag instructions. They are often used for bottles and pump parts.
    *   **Electric Steam Sterilizers:** Dedicated electric sterilizers are effective. Follow the manufacturer's instructions for your specific unit.
    *   Steam sterilization is generally gentler than boiling. It is a popular choice for many parents.

*   **Dishwasher "Sanitize" Cycle (with caution):**
    *   As mentioned, some dishwashers have a sanitize setting. This uses high heat to kill germs.
    *   Use this option only if your Elvie manual confirms it is safe for specific parts.
    *   Be aware that repeated use may still degrade plastics faster than other methods.

*   **UV Sterilizers:**
    *   These devices use UV light to kill germs. They are a dry sterilization method.
    *   Ensure your Elvie parts are compatible with UV sterilizers before use.
    *   These are a good alternative if you want to avoid heat or moisture.

Always ensure parts are completely dry before storing them. This prevents mold and bacterial growth. Proper sterilization gives you peace of mind, knowing your baby is getting milk from thoroughly cleaned equipment.

### Extending the Lifespan of Your Elvie Pump Parts

Breast pump parts are an investment. Proper care extends their lifespan and maintains pump efficiency. I have learned that a little extra attention goes a long way in saving money and frustration. Knowing when and how to care for them can significantly reduce replacement costs.

The delicate nature of Elvie components, especially the silicone valves and diaphragms, requires gentle handling. Over time, these parts experience wear and tear. This can affect suction and overall pump performance. Regular inspection and proper care are key to making them last.

#### Proper Drying and Storage

After cleaning, proper drying and storage are just as important as the washing itself. This prevents bacterial growth and helps maintain the integrity of the materials. I always make sure parts are bone dry.

*   **Air Drying is Best:** After washing or sterilizing, place all parts on a clean, dedicated drying rack. You can also use a clean paper towel. Do not use cloth towels. Cloth towels can harbor germs and leave lint on the parts.
*   **Ensure Complete Dryness:** Allow parts to air dry completely before storing them. Even a small amount of moisture can encourage mold and bacterial growth. This poses a health risk.
*   **Clean and Covered Storage:** Once completely dry, store the parts in a clean, covered container or a clean, sealed bag. This protects them from dust and other contaminants.
*   **Avoid Direct Sunlight or Heat:** Do not store parts in direct sunlight or near heat sources. Excessive heat can degrade plastic and silicone over time. This shortens their lifespan.
*   **Store Disassembled:** Store parts disassembled rather than assembled. This helps prevent moisture from getting trapped. It also reduces stress on delicate connections.

Following these drying and storage tips will ensure your Elvie pump parts remain hygienic and ready for use. It helps them last as long as possible.

#### When to Replace Parts

Even with the best care, pump parts do not last forever. They are designed to be consumables. Recognizing when to replace them is crucial for both hygiene and pumping efficiency. I make it a habit to check my parts regularly.

*   **Signs of Wear and Tear:**
    *   **Reduced Suction:** If you notice your pump is not collecting as much milk, or the suction feels weaker, worn parts are often the culprit. Delicate silicone valves or diaphragms can lose their elasticity. This causes air leaks.
    *   **Cracks or Tears:** Visually inspect all plastic and silicone parts for any cracks, tears, or holes. Even tiny imperfections can affect performance.
    *   **Discoloration:** While some discoloration is normal over time, significant changes in color might indicate material degradation or mineral buildup.
    *   **Loss of Elasticity:** Silicone parts, like valves, should be flexible and spring back into shape. If they feel stiff or floppy, they need replacement.
    *   **Odd Odors:** If parts retain a persistent sour milk smell even after thorough cleaning, they might need to be replaced.

*   **Manufacturer Recommendations:** Elvie, like other pump manufacturers, provides guidelines on how often to replace parts. For heavily used items like valves and diaphragms, replacement might be needed every 2-3 months. For less stressed parts like bottles and flanges, it could be every 6 months to a year, or as needed. Consult your specific Elvie manual for recommended replacement schedules.
*   **Maintain a Supply:** Consider keeping a spare set of essential parts on hand. This ensures you are never caught without a working pump. It allows for immediate replacement when needed.

Regularly replacing worn parts ensures your Elvie pump continues to operate effectively and hygienically. This supports your breastfeeding journey without interruption.
