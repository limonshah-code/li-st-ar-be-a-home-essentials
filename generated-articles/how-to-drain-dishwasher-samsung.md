## How To Drain Your Samsung Dishwasher: A Complete Guide

No one likes a dishwasher full of dirty, standing water after a wash cycle. It is frustrating. If your Samsung dishwasher is not draining properly, you are likely facing this exact issue. This problem can prevent your dishes from getting clean and even cause unpleasant odors in your kitchen. But do not worry. You can often fix this issue yourself.

This guide will show you how to drain your Samsung dishwasher and troubleshoot common draining problems. We will cover everything from simple checks like cleaning the filter to more involved steps like inspecting the drain hose or pump. My goal is to help you get your dishwasher back to working order, ensuring your dishes come out sparkling clean every time. Let us dive in and tackle this common appliance issue together.

### Takeaway

*   **Always disconnect power:** Before touching any internal parts, unplug the dishwasher.
*   **Check the filter:** A clogged filter is a very common reason for draining issues. Clean it regularly.
*   **Inspect the drain hose:** Look for kinks, clogs, or blockages in the hose.
*   **Verify the air gap/garbage disposal:** Ensure these are clear if your dishwasher connects to them.
*   **Listen for the drain pump:** A humming noise without water draining might mean a blocked pump.
*   **Consider professional help:** If basic troubleshooting fails, call a technician.

To drain a Samsung dishwasher, you generally need to remove the standing water manually first. Then, troubleshoot the cause of the non-draining issue. Common causes include a clogged filter, a kinked or blocked drain hose, an issue with the air gap or garbage disposal connection, or a faulty drain pump. Addressing these components helps restore proper drainage.

## Understanding Your Samsung Dishwasher's Drainage System

Your Samsung dishwasher uses a specific system to remove water after a wash or rinse cycle. Understanding how this system works helps you pinpoint drainage problems. The process starts inside the tub. Water flows through a filter system. Then it goes into the drain pump.

The drain pump pushes the water through a drain hose. This hose usually connects to your kitchen sink's drain or a garbage disposal unit. Sometimes, it connects to a dedicated drain standpipe. An air gap might be part of the system too. This prevents dirty water from flowing back into the dishwasher. Knowing these parts exist makes troubleshooting much easier.

### How Water Exits the Dishwasher

Water inside your dishwasher follows a path designed for efficient removal. First, it goes through a coarse filter and a fine filter. These filters catch food particles. This prevents them from entering the drain pump. Next, the drain pump activates. It creates the pressure needed to move water out.

The water then travels through the drain hose. This hose typically runs from the dishwasher base to your sink area. It usually connects to the garbage disposal inlet or a specific drain connection. If the hose has a low loop, it helps prevent backflow. An air gap on the countertop also stops dirty water from re-entering. Any blockage along this path stops the draining process.

### Common Reasons for Drainage Issues

Many things can cause your Samsung dishwasher to not drain. One of the most common issues is a clogged filter. Food bits, grease, and debris build up here. This blocks water from reaching the drain pump. Another frequent problem is a kinked or blocked drain hose. The hose can get bent behind the dishwasher. Food particles or grease can also clog it over time.

Issues with the garbage disposal can also stop draining. If your dishwasher drains into a garbage disposal, the disposal must be clear. A plug might also be in the disposal inlet if it is a new installation. Lastly, the drain pump itself might be faulty or blocked. Understanding these common culprits helps you diagnose the problem faster.

## Step-by-Step Guide to Manually Draining Your Dishwasher

When your Samsung dishwasher has standing water, the first step is to remove it manually. This prepares the appliance for troubleshooting. It also prevents water from overflowing when you start looking for the problem. This process is simple. You do not need special tools for this initial step.

Always remember safety first. Dealing with water and electricity requires care. Make sure the dishwasher is completely off. Unplug it from the wall outlet. If you cannot reach the plug, turn off the breaker for the dishwasher at your home's electrical panel. This prevents electric shock.

### Removing Standing Water Safely

Once the power is off, you can start removing the water. Open the dishwasher door. You will see the standing water inside the tub. Use a large towel or a sponge to soak up the water. Squeeze the water into a bucket. Repeat this until most of the water is gone. For larger amounts of water, a wet/dry shop vacuum can be very helpful.

Place old towels around the base of the dishwasher. This will catch any spills as you work. You can also use a cup or small scoop to remove larger volumes of water quickly. My personal experience says a turkey baster is excellent for reaching small puddles. Make sure the bottom of the dishwasher is as dry as possible before moving to the next steps. This makes it easier to work and helps you see any blockages.

### Preparing for Troubleshooting

With the water removed, your dishwasher is ready for inspection. Keep the power disconnected. Do not plug it back in until you finish all checks and repairs. Make sure you have good lighting. A flashlight can be very useful for looking into dark areas. Gather a few basic tools.

You might need a screwdriver (Phillips or flathead), pliers, and a bucket. Having some paper towels or old rags nearby is also smart. They help clean up any remaining drips or debris you find. Now you can safely move on to checking the internal parts. This preparation saves you time and prevents accidental messes.

## Checking and Cleaning the Dishwasher Filter

A clogged filter is the most frequent reason a Samsung dishwasher will not drain. Food particles, grease, and debris accumulate on the filter over time. This creates a barrier. Water cannot pass through it to the drain pump. Regularly cleaning the filter prevents many draining issues. It also helps your dishwasher clean dishes better.

Cleaning the filter is a simple maintenance task. It does not require special tools. You should aim to do this every few weeks, or more often if you use your dishwasher daily. My own dishwasher gets a filter clean once a month, and it makes a big difference. This step is often the quickest fix for a non-draining problem.

### Locating and Removing the Filter

To find the filter, open your Samsung dishwasher door. Look at the bottom of the tub. You will see a cylindrical or flat filter assembly. Samsung dishwashers usually have a two-part or three-part filter system. This includes a coarse filter, a fine filter, and sometimes a main filter. You might need to twist a handle or unlatch a clip to remove it.

Refer to your Samsung dishwasher's user manual if you have trouble finding it. The manual will show you the exact location and removal method for your model. Gently lift the filter assembly out. Be careful not to drop any debris back into the drain opening. Some models might require a slight twist and lift.

### Cleaning the Filter Thoroughly

Once you remove the filter, take it to your sink. Rinse it under warm running water. Use a soft brush, like an old toothbrush, to scrub away any stuck-on food. Pay attention to both the coarse mesh and the fine mesh areas. You will often see a lot of gunk come off. This gunk is what was blocking your drain.

You can use a small amount of dish soap to help cut through grease. Make sure to rinse all soap residue off completely. Ensure all holes in the mesh are clear. If you have a multi-part filter, separate the parts and clean each one individually. Once clean, reassemble the filter parts. Put the filter back into the dishwasher. Ensure it clicks or twists securely into place. This prevents it from moving during cycles and ensures proper filtration. ## Inspecting the Drain Hose and Air Gap

After checking the filter, your next step is to examine the drain hose. The drain hose carries water from the dishwasher to your sink drain or garbage disposal. Problems with this hose are common causes of draining issues. A simple kink can stop water flow. Blockages inside the hose are also frequent.

If your dishwasher has an air gap, it is crucial to check that too. The air gap prevents dirty water from flowing back into the dishwasher. It is usually a small cylindrical fixture on your countertop near the faucet. Both the drain hose and air gap need to be clear for proper drainage. Checking these components can solve many non-draining problems quickly.

### Checking for Kinks and Blockages in the Drain Hose

Start by pulling the dishwasher out slightly from its cabinet if you can. This lets you access the drain hose behind the unit. Look for any visible kinks or bends in the hose. A hose bent at a sharp angle can restrict water flow. Straighten any kinks you find. My own dishwasher once had a hidden kink that caused draining issues for weeks until I finally pulled it out to check.

Next, feel along the hose for any hard spots or bulges. These might indicate a blockage inside. If you suspect a blockage, you will need to disconnect the hose. Place a bucket underneath where you disconnect it. Water and debris might come out. You can use a long brush or even a straightened coat hanger to clear blockages. Remember to be gentle to avoid damaging the hose. If the hose is severely damaged or clogged beyond repair, you might need to replace it. [Find out how to remove your Samsung dishwasher drain hose](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-remove-samsung-dishwasher-drain-hose) if replacement is necessary.

### Verifying Air Gap and Garbage Disposal Connection

If your Samsung dishwasher drains into a garbage disposal, make sure the disposal is empty and clear. Run the disposal for about 30 seconds with cold water to ensure it is clear of food particles. Sometimes, a new garbage disposal installation requires removing a knock-out plug inside the disposal's dishwasher inlet. If this plug is still present, your dishwasher will not drain.

For dishwashers connected to an air gap, check the air gap fixture on your countertop. Unscrew its cover. Remove any debris or gunk you find inside. There are typically two hoses connected to the air gap. One comes from the dishwasher. The other goes to the disposal or drain. Make sure both hoses are clear and not clogged where they connect to the air gap. Clear any visible blockages. Reassemble the air gap. These connections are vital for your dishwasher to drain correctly. [Discover more tips if your dishwasher does not drain](https://homeessentialsguide.com/when-dishwasher-does-not-drain).

## Troubleshooting the Drain Pump and Other Electrical Issues

If the filter, drain hose, and air gap are clear, the problem might be with the drain pump. The drain pump is responsible for pushing water out of your Samsung dishwasher. It is a crucial component in the drainage system. Sometimes, small objects can get past the filter and jam the pump impeller. Other times, the pump motor itself might fail.

Electrical issues can also prevent the drain pump from working. This includes a tripped circuit breaker or a faulty control board. It is important to approach these issues with caution. Always disconnect power before inspecting electrical components. Safety comes first when dealing with your appliance.

### Inspecting the Drain Pump for Obstructions

The drain pump is usually located at the bottom of the dishwasher, often behind the filter assembly. You might need to remove a few screws or clips to access it. Once you can see the pump, look for any small objects that might be jamming the impeller. Common culprits include broken glass, food debris, or even small pieces of plastic. I once found a tiny fruit seed that stopped my friend's dishwasher from draining.

Carefully remove any obstructions you find. You might need pliers or needle-nose pliers for small items. Once cleared, try to spin the impeller manually. It should turn freely. If it feels stiff or does not move, the pump might be damaged or still blocked. Be gentle when clearing obstructions. For specific instructions on accessing the pump for your model, refer to your Samsung dishwasher's service manual. [Learn how to make your Samsung dishwasher drain effectively](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-make-samsung-dishwasher-drain).

### Checking Electrical Connections and Circuit Breaker

After inspecting the pump for physical blockages, check its electrical connections. Make sure all wires leading to the drain pump are secure. Loose connections can prevent the pump from receiving power. If wires look damaged or frayed, they might need professional repair. Do not touch exposed wires without disconnecting power.

Also, check your home's circuit breaker box. Find the breaker that controls your dishwasher. It might be labeled "Dishwasher" or "Kitchen Appliances." If it is tripped (usually in the middle position), reset it by flipping it off and then back on. A tripped breaker means the dishwasher lost power. This prevents the drain pump from running. If the breaker trips again immediately, there might be a short circuit or a faulty component, indicating a deeper electrical problem. At this point, I usually recommend calling a qualified technician.

## Utilizing Samsung Dishwasher's Drain Cycle and Error Codes

Many Samsung dishwashers have a specific drain cycle or button combination that can activate the drain pump. This is useful for manually draining water or testing the pump's function. Knowing how to use these features can save you time. It helps confirm if the pump is receiving commands from the control board.

Your Samsung dishwasher also uses error codes to tell you about problems. When a drainage issue occurs, an error code might appear on the control panel. Understanding these codes helps you diagnose the problem quickly. They provide a specific starting point for troubleshooting. Checking the user manual for your specific model is key.

### Activating a Drain Cycle or Resetting the Dishwasher

To activate a drain cycle, consult your Samsung dishwasher manual. Some models have a "Drain & Dry" or "Drain" button. You might need to press and hold a combination of buttons, like "Cancel" and "Drain," for a few seconds. This forces the dishwasher to attempt to drain. If the water drains, the issue might be intermittent or related to a specific wash cycle setting. Performing a hard reset can also sometimes resolve temporary glitches. Unplug your Samsung dishwasher from the power outlet for 5-10 minutes. Then plug it back in. This can clear the control board's memory and reset any minor electronic faults. After the reset, try to run a short cycle or the drain cycle again. ### Interpreting Samsung Dishwasher Error Codes

Samsung dishwashers display various error codes for drainage problems. Common codes include:

*   **LE or 4E:** This often indicates a water leakage error or an issue with the water supply or drainage. It suggests the dishwasher detected too much water.
*   **OE:** This code points to an overflow error. It means the dishwasher cannot drain water within a set time. This is a direct indicator of a drainage problem.
*   **HE1/HE2:** While usually heating errors, sometimes they can relate to water not draining properly, causing heating elements to malfunction or activate incorrectly.

When you see an error code, look it up in your Samsung dishwasher's user manual. The manual provides specific troubleshooting steps for each code. These codes are a valuable diagnostic tool. They guide you directly to the potential source of the problem. If the error code points to the drain pump or water level sensor, you know where to focus your efforts. ### Examining the Check Valve and Sump Area

The check valve is a small, one-way valve in the drain line. It prevents dirty water from flowing back into the dishwasher. This valve is usually located near the drain pump or in the sump area at the bottom of the dishwasher. If this valve gets stuck open or closed by debris, it can cause draining issues. You might need to remove the pump or sump cover to access it.

Carefully inspect the check valve for any trapped food particles or mineral buildup. Clean it gently. Ensure it can open and close freely. While you are in the sump area, check for any other debris that might have bypassed the filter. Small bones or fruit pits can get lodged in surprising places. Use a flashlight to get a clear view. ## Preventing Future Dishwasher Drainage Problems

Once you have fixed your Samsung dishwasher's draining issue, you want to keep it running smoothly. Preventing problems is always easier than fixing them. Regular maintenance is key. Simple habits can extend your dishwasher's lifespan and ensure consistent performance. This saves you time, money, and frustration in the long run.

Think of your dishwasher as an investment. Taking good care of it protects that investment. A few minutes of preventative action each week or month can prevent a lot of headaches. I have learned this the hard way with my own appliances.

### Regular Cleaning and Maintenance Schedule

Establish a regular cleaning routine for your dishwasher. This starts with pre-scraping dishes. Remove large food scraps before loading dishes. This prevents them from clogging the filter. Clean your dishwasher filter at least once a month, or more often if you notice residue. This is the simplest and most effective preventative step.

Run an empty cycle with a dishwasher cleaner or white vinegar once a month. This helps remove grease buildup and hard water deposits. Place a cup of white vinegar on the top rack and run a hot water cycle. This keeps the interior clean and fresh. Check the spray arms for clogged holes. Clean them with a toothpick if needed.

### Tips for Proper Dishwasher Usage

Load your dishwasher correctly. Do not overcrowd it. Overcrowding can block spray arms and prevent water from reaching all dishes. It also allows food particles to settle in places they should not. Make sure tall items do not block the dispenser or spray arm rotation.

Use the right amount and type of detergent. Too much detergent can create excessive suds. This can sometimes interfere with draining. Too little might leave food residue. Also, consider using a rinse aid. Rinse aid helps water sheet off dishes and prevents spots, which means less residue can get left behind in the drain system. Proper usage habits make a big difference in maintaining your Samsung dishwasher's drainage efficiency.
