## Initial Checks: Is Your Samsung Dishwasher Cycle Simply Paused?

Before diving into complex diagnostics, always start with the simplest checks. Sometimes, the "Express 60" light blinking on your Samsung dishwasher is merely an indication that the cycle is paused. This pause can happen for several reasons. Often, it is as straightforward as the dishwasher door not being fully closed or latched. Modern dishwashers have safety features that stop operation if the door is ajar. A loose latch or an obstruction can prevent the door from sealing completely. I always make sure to give the door a firm push and listen for the click of the latch.

Another common reason for a paused cycle is a power interruption. Even a brief flicker in your home's electricity can cause the dishwasher to stop and blink. In such cases, a simple power reset can clear the issue. You can perform a power reset by unplugging the dishwasher from its power outlet for about 5 minutes. If unplugging is not possible, you can also turn off the dishwasher's circuit breaker at your home's electrical panel for the same duration. After waiting, plug it back in or flip the breaker back on. This action often clears minor electronic glitches and resets the control board, allowing the cycle to resume. Many times, I have found this simple step resolves various blinking issues with my appliances. For more general blinking problems that also prevent starting, you might find answers by checking [why is my Samsung dishwasher blinking and not starting](https://homeessentialsguide.com/why-is-my-samsung-dishwasher-blinking-and-not-starting).

Check for any objects or dishware blocking the door from closing completely. Sometimes a large plate or a utensil can protrude slightly, preventing a secure seal. Look carefully around the door edges. Also, inspect the door latch itself for any damage or debris. A broken or sticky latch might prevent it from engaging properly. Ensuring these basic elements are in order often saves time and effort. I have learned to always start with the easiest solutions first; they frequently solve the problem.

## Common Causes of Samsung Dishwasher Blinking Express 60

The blinking "Express 60" light on your Samsung dishwasher can stem from several underlying issues beyond just a paused cycle. These causes often relate to the fundamental operations of the dishwasher: water intake, drainage, and internal cleanliness. Understanding these potential culprits helps you narrow down the problem effectively. One common reason involves the dishwasher's water supply. If the appliance is not receiving enough water, or if water pressure is low, it can trigger an error. The dishwasher needs a specific amount of water to operate efficiently, and any disruption can cause it to pause and signal a problem. I always check the water valve connected to my dishwasher to ensure it is fully open.

Drainage problems are another frequent cause. If the dishwasher cannot properly drain water after a cycle or during a wash phase, it will often stop and indicate an error. This can be due to a clogged drain hose, a malfunctioning drain pump, or issues with your home's sink drain connection. Accumulated food particles or foreign objects can block the drain path, leading to water backing up. My experience has taught me that a simple blockage can cause significant disruption. If you notice your [Samsung dishwasher holding water in the bottom](https://homeessentialsguide.com/why-is-my-samsung-dishwasher-holding-water-in-the-bottom), it's a strong indicator of a drainage issue.

Furthermore, internal component issues like a dirty filter or blocked spray arms can also lead to the "Express 60" light blinking. These components are critical for effective cleaning and water circulation. When they are dirty or obstructed, the dishwasher cannot perform its functions correctly. This leads to sensor errors or cycle interruptions. The dishwasher's sensors detect issues with water flow or levels. If these sensors detect an anomaly, they will often halt the cycle. Addressing these common areas often resolves the blinking light and gets your dishwasher back to normal.

## Troubleshooting Water Supply and Drainage Problems

When your Samsung dishwasher blinks "Express 60," water supply and drainage issues are primary suspects. Let's tackle these one by one. First, for water supply, check the water inlet valve. This valve is typically located behind the dishwasher where the water line connects. Make sure it is fully open. If it is only partially open, the dishwasher may not get enough water pressure. Also, inspect the water inlet hose for any kinks or bends. A kinked hose restricts water flow, mimicking a low-pressure situation. I often pull my dishwasher out slightly to check the hose behind it, ensuring it is straight and free of twists.

Next, consider drainage. The drain hose carries dirty water out of the dishwasher. This hose can become kinked, blocked by debris, or even improperly installed. Ensure the drain hose is not crimped or bent behind the dishwasher. If it connects to your sink's garbage disposal, confirm the knockout plug inside the disposal was removed during installation. A common problem is a clogged drain pump filter or the drain pump itself. You might hear the pump humming but no water moving. Accessing the drain pump often involves removing the lower access panel of the dishwasher. Before doing this, always disconnect power to the appliance.
