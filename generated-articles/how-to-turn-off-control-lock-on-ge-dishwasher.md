## FAQ Section

### How do I know if the control lock is on my GE dishwasher?
You can usually tell if the control lock is on by looking for an indicator light or message. Most GE dishwashers display "LOC" or a padlock icon on the control panel. If this light is illuminated, or if the buttons do not respond when pressed, the control lock is likely engaged.

### What if my GE dishwasher won't unlock after holding the button?
If your GE dishwasher does not unlock after holding the button for 3-5 seconds, try a power cycle. Turn off the dishwasher's circuit breaker for 1-2 minutes, then turn it back on. This resets the control board. If the issue persists, the control panel or main board might be faulty, requiring professional service.

### Can a power outage cause the GE dishwasher control lock to activate?
Yes, a power outage or a brief power surge can sometimes cause the control lock to activate on a GE dishwasher. The appliance's electronics may default to a locked state as a safety measure during power fluctuations. A power cycle usually resolves this issue by resetting the system.

### Is the control lock the same as the child lock on a GE dishwasher?
Yes, the terms "control lock" and "child lock" generally refer to the same safety feature on GE dishwashers. This function disables the control panel buttons. It prevents accidental operation by children or pets. The label may vary slightly depending on the specific GE model.

### How do I prevent the control lock from turning on accidentally?
To prevent accidental activation, ensure children understand not to touch the dishwasher panel. Avoid draping cloths over the control panel. Regularly clean the panel to prevent debris from interfering with touch sensors. You can also check your manual for settings to disable automatic locking features, if available.

### What should I do if my GE dishwasher is locked and also showing an error code?
If your GE dishwasher is locked and simultaneously displaying an error code, this indicates a more complex issue. First, try to clear the control lock using the standard method and a power cycle. If the error code persists or the lock remains, consult your user manual for the specific error code, or contact a qualified GE appliance technician.
