## Where to Locate the Model Number on Your Whirlpool Dishwasher

Finding the model number on your Whirlpool dishwasher is usually a quick task. Manufacturers place these labels in consistent spots. Knowing these common locations will save you a lot of searching. Most Whirlpool dishwashers have a data plate or sticker with the model and serial numbers printed clearly. This tag is often white, silver, or black.

The most common place to look is on the inner door frame. Open your dishwasher door fully. Then, carefully examine the edges of the door itself. Also, check the frame of the dishwasher opening. The tag might be on the side, top, or bottom edge of this frame. It is often a thin sticker or a small metal plate. Look closely, as it can be hidden near the hinges or latch mechanism.

Another frequent spot is along the kickplate. This is the removable panel at the very bottom front of your dishwasher. You might need to gently pull it off or unscrew it to reveal the tag. This location is popular for built-in models. The tag can sometimes be tucked away, so be thorough in your search. A flashlight can be helpful here.

Less common, but still possible, locations include the side of the dishwasher tub. You might see it on the outer edge, near the top, once the door is fully open. Some older Whirlpool models could have the tag on the back of the unit. This makes it harder to access. However, for most modern appliances, the tag is accessible from the front. If you have trouble finding it, remember that patience is key. Finding the right spot makes all the difference for tasks like [how to find Whirlpool dishwasher model number](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-find-whirlpool-dishwasher-model-number) or ordering parts.

## Deciphering Your Whirlpool Dishwasher's Data Tag

Once you locate the data tag, you will see several numbers and letters. It can look like a jumble at first glance. However, each part of this information has a specific meaning. The most important details are the model number and the serial number. Understanding which is which prevents confusion.

The **model number** identifies the specific version or series of your appliance. It is a unique code that tells you the features, specifications, and design of your dishwasher. Whirlpool model numbers often start with letters followed by a series of numbers and sometimes more letters. For example, a model number might look like WDT730PAHZ. This number is what you provide when looking for parts or repair instructions.

The **serial number** is different from the model number. It identifies your specific unit. This means two identical Whirlpool dishwasher models will have the same model number. However, they will have different serial numbers. The serial number indicates the date and location of manufacturing. It is crucial for warranty registration and tracking individual units. It usually contains a long string of numbers and letters, often without obvious breaks.

Sometimes, the tag might also include a manufacturing date or code. This is useful for understanding the age of your appliance. It helps with troubleshooting or knowing if a part is still available. You might also see electrical specifications like voltage or amperage. These are important for safety and installation purposes. When you are looking for information about your appliance, focus on extracting both the model and serial numbers. These are the two primary identifiers you will need.
