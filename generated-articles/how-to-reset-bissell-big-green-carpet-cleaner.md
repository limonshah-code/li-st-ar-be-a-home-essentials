## How To Reset Your Bissell Big Green Carpet Cleaner for Optimal Performance

Is your trusty Bissell Big Green Carpet Cleaner acting up? Perhaps it lost suction, stopped spraying, or simply refuses to turn on. These issues can be frustrating, especially when you are ready to tackle dirty carpets. Many times, these problems do not require a costly repair. This guide will walk you through various troubleshooting steps and "reset" procedures. You will learn to diagnose common problems and get your carpet cleaner back in top shape.

### Takeaway

Resetting your Bissell Big Green often solves common operational issues. Follow these key steps:

*   Perform a basic power cycle by unplugging and re-plugging the machine.
*   Inspect tanks, hoses, and filters for blockages or improper seating.
*   Address suction loss by checking seals and cleaning filters.
*   Troubleshoot motor problems by verifying brush roll movement.
*   Prevent future issues with consistent cleaning and maintenance.

### Clear Answer to the Main Query

You can "reset" your Bissell Big Green Carpet Cleaner primarily through a power cycle. Unplug the machine from the power source for 5-10 minutes, then plug it back in. ### Essential Pre-Reset Checks for Your Carpet Cleaner

Before you attempt any form of reset on your Bissell Big Green Carpet Cleaner, conducting a few essential pre-checks is critical. This step ensures safety and helps identify obvious problems. It often saves you from deeper troubleshooting. I always start here when my machine isn't performing.

First, always prioritize safety. **Unplug the machine** from the electrical outlet. This prevents accidental startup and reduces the risk of electrical shock. Your safety should be paramount. Take a moment to ensure the power cord is completely disconnected.

Next, inspect both water tanks. The clean water tank should be properly seated and filled with the correct solution. An improperly seated tank can prevent water flow or suction. The dirty water tank should be empty before use. An overfilled dirty tank can cause suction loss or leaks. Make sure both tanks click firmly into place. This small detail often gets overlooked.

Then, examine all hoses and connections. Look for any kinks, tears, or loose fittings. The suction hose should be securely attached. The spray hose needs to be free of obstructions. A loose connection can lead to significant performance drops. I once found a tiny piece of debris jamming a hose.

Finally, check the brush rolls and nozzles. Accumulated hair, debris, or dried solution can block spray nozzles. They can also prevent brush rolls from spinning effectively. Use scissors or a comb to remove any tangled hair from the brushes. Ensure the spray nozzle is clear and free of blockages. This step is vital for effective cleaning. Often, simply clearing a clog can restore full function. ### The Standard Power Cycle Reset: A First Troubleshooting Step

When your Bissell Big Green Carpet Cleaner behaves unexpectedly, the first and often most effective "reset" you can perform is a simple power cycle. This method is the equivalent of rebooting a computer. It clears temporary electronic glitches and can restore normal operation. I rely on this technique for many household appliances.

To perform a power cycle reset, begin by ensuring the machine is turned off. Then, locate the power cord and **unplug it completely** from the wall outlet. This action cuts off all electrical supply to the unit. It allows any residual electrical charge within the machine's capacitors to dissipate. This dissipation is key to a true reset.

Once unplugged, wait patiently for about 5 to 10 minutes. This waiting period is crucial. It gives the internal components enough time to de-energize and clear their memory of any errors. You might feel tempted to plug it back in sooner. Resist that urge for the best results. A full discharge ensures a fresh start.

After the waiting period, plug the power cord firmly back into a functioning electrical outlet. Ensure the connection is secure. Then, attempt to turn on your Bissell Big Green Carpet Cleaner as you normally would. Often, this simple power cycle resolves issues like the machine not turning on, inconsistent spraying, or minor motor hesitations. It's a quick, easy, and safe first troubleshooting step. Many issues stem from transient electrical errors. A power cycle effectively clears these errors. This process often brings your cleaner back to full functionality. Remember this as your go-to initial fix. You might be surprised how often it works. ### Addressing Specific Performance Issues: Suction Loss & Motor Problems

Sometimes, a general power cycle isn't enough. Your Bissell Big Green Carpet Cleaner might still struggle with specific performance problems. These often include a noticeable loss of suction or issues with the motor and brush roll. These problems require targeted "reset" methods. They involve checking mechanical components rather than just electrical ones. I have spent time figuring these out firsthand.

#### Revitalizing Suction Power

A sudden drop in suction power is a common complaint. This makes cleaning less effective. Several factors can cause this. First, check the clean and dirty water tanks again. Ensure they are correctly seated. The seals around the tanks must form a tight vacuum. If a seal is worn or misaligned, it will lead to suction loss. Gently remove and reseat the tanks to ensure a proper fit.

Next, inspect the dirty water tank for blockages. Dried solution or collected debris can clog the intake port. This restricts airflow and reduces suction. Empty and rinse the tank thoroughly. Also, examine the recovery tank filter. A dirty or clogged filter severely impacts suction. It prevents air from flowing freely. Cleaning this filter is vital for restoring performance. Regular cleaning of your filter is essential to prevent these issues. You can find detailed instructions on [how to clean a Bissell carpet cleaner filter](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-clean-bissell-carpet-cleaner-filter) for best results. This small part makes a big difference.

Finally, check the suction path from the floor nozzle to the dirty water tank. Look for any visible obstructions in the hose. Sometimes, a clump of hair or a small object gets lodged. Clearing these blockages will immediately restore suction.

#### Troubleshooting Motor and Brush Roll Function

Motor and brush roll issues often manifest as unusual noises or a failure of the brushes to spin. A spinning brush roll is crucial for agitating carpet fibers and lifting dirt. If your brushes are not moving, your cleaning will be subpar.

Start by unplugging the machine again. Then, turn the machine over to access the brush roll area. Carefully inspect the brush rolls for any obstructions. Hair, threads, or carpet fibers commonly wrap around the brushes. These can prevent them from spinning freely. Use scissors to cut away tangled debris. Remove any foreign objects you find. Sometimes, it might just be too much build-up. Learning [how to remove the brush from a Bissell carpet cleaner](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-remove-brush-from-bissell-carpet-cleaner) can make this task easier and more effective.

Also, check the brush belt. This belt connects the motor to the brush rolls. If the belt is broken, stretched, or dislodged, the brushes will not spin. A broken belt requires replacement. A dislodged belt can often be re-seated. Always refer to your user manual for specific instructions on accessing these parts. If the motor itself sounds strained or produces burning smells, stop immediately. This indicates a more serious internal issue. These targeted checks and "resets" can often get your machine working perfectly again.

### Dealing with Leaks and Water Flow Glitches

Leaks and inconsistent water flow are incredibly frustrating when cleaning carpets. Not only do they make a mess, but they also compromise the cleaning effectiveness of your Bissell Big Green. Addressing these issues often involves checking seals, connections, and proper tank seating. It's a different kind of "reset" that focuses on physical integrity. I've had to mop up puddles more than once before learning these tricks.

First, **always unplug your machine** before inspecting for leaks. Safety first! Start by checking the clean and dirty water tanks. Cracks in either tank can cause leaks. Carefully examine both tanks for any visible damage. If you find cracks, the tank likely needs replacement. Also, ensure the caps and stoppers on both tanks are securely tightened. A loose cap is a common culprit for drips.

Next, focus on the seals and gaskets. The Bissell Big Green uses various seals to prevent water from escaping. Check the rubber gasket around the clean water tank opening. Ensure it is clean and free of debris. A dirty or damaged gasket will not form a tight seal. The same applies to the dirty water tank's seal. Inspect the suction nozzle gasket. This gasket creates the vacuum seal necessary for water recovery. If it is worn or out of place, water can leak or suction will be poor.

Inconsistent water flow or no spray at all can also be related to blockages. Remove the spray nozzle and check for any dried solution or debris. You can use a small pin or paperclip to carefully clear the spray jets. Sometimes, the issue is not a leak but simply a failure to dispense water properly. This often comes down to clogged spray nozzles.

Finally, ensure all hoses are securely connected. Check where the solution hose connects to the clean water tank. Also, check its connection to the spray nozzle. Loose connections can spray water where it shouldn't go. Ensure the dirty water hose is firmly attached to the recovery tank. If your machine includes internal valves that regulate water flow, ensure they are not stuck. Gently tapping the machine (when unplugged!) can sometimes free a stubborn valve. These specific checks effectively "reset" your machine's ability to hold and distribute water correctly.

### FAQ Section

**Q1: Is there a specific "reset button" on the Bissell Big Green?**
No, the Bissell Big Green Carpet Cleaner does not feature a dedicated "reset button." The primary method for resetting electrical glitches is performing a power cycle. This involves unplugging the machine from its power source for 5-10 minutes. Then, you plug it back in. This process clears temporary electrical errors.

**Q2: How often should I clean the filters to prevent issues?**
You should clean your Bissell Big Green's filters after every major cleaning session. If you clean small areas frequently, aim for at least once a month. Regular filter cleaning prevents blockages and maintains strong suction. It also extends the overall lifespan of your machine.

**Q3: What solutions are safe to use in my Bissell Big Green?**
Always use genuine Bissell carpet cleaning formulas designed for deep cleaners. You can also use recommended third-party solutions that are compatible with hot water extraction machines. Avoid using harsh chemicals, bleach, or non-foaming detergents. These can damage your machine or leave sticky residues.

**Q4: My machine still won't turn on after a power cycle. What next?**
If your Bissell Big Green still doesn't power on after a full power cycle, check the electrical outlet with another appliance. Ensure the circuit breaker hasn't tripped. If the outlet works, the issue might be internal. Contact Bissell customer support or a certified repair technician for further assistance.

**Q5: Can I reset my Bissell Big Green if it's leaking?**
A "reset" for a leaking machine involves checking physical components. Unplug the machine. Inspect tanks for cracks, and ensure they are properly seated with secure caps. Check all seals and gaskets for damage or debris. Examine hose connections for looseness. Addressing these physical issues effectively "resets" its watertight integrity.

**Q6: How long should I wait before replugging after a power reset?**
You should wait 5 to 10 minutes after unplugging your Bissell Big Green for a power cycle reset. This duration allows sufficient time for any residual electrical charge within the machine's internal components to dissipate completely. This ensures a true and effective electrical reset.
