## Samsung Dishwasher Not Running? Here's Why!

Imagine loading your dishwasher, adding detergent, and pressing start. Nothing happens. Your **Samsung dishwasher won't run**. This can be incredibly frustrating. I understand that feeling of a seemingly simple chore becoming a major headache.

Many reasons can cause your Samsung dishwasher to stop working or fail to start. These issues range from simple user errors to more complex mechanical or electrical problems. This article will help you understand the common causes and provide clear steps to troubleshoot your appliance. We will cover power issues, door problems, water supply issues, and internal component failures. My goal is to help you get your dishwasher running smoothly again.

### Takeaway:

*   **Check Power First:** Ensure the dishwasher receives electricity.
*   **Inspect the Door Latch:** A secure door is vital for operation.
*   **Verify Water Supply:** The appliance needs water to start.
*   **Look for Clogs:** Filters and drain hoses can block water flow.
*   **Understand Error Codes:** These codes provide clues to the problem.

When your Samsung dishwasher refuses to run, the problem usually stems from a lack of power, an unlatched door, an issue with the water supply, or a clogged internal component. Addressing these common areas often resolves the starting issue.

## Addressing Power Supply Problems for Your Samsung Dishwasher

Your **Samsung dishwasher won't run** if it does not receive power. This is the first thing you should check. A lack of power is a very common reason for any appliance to not start. I always begin troubleshooting here.

### Check the Power Cord Connection

First, verify the dishwasher's power cord. Make sure it is fully plugged into a working electrical outlet. Sometimes, the cord can become loose. A loose connection prevents electricity from reaching the appliance.

### Inspect the Circuit Breaker

Next, go to your home's main electrical panel. Locate the circuit breaker that controls the kitchen or dishwasher outlet. It might be tripped, meaning it flipped to the "off" position or is between "on" and "off." Simply flip it completely to "off" and then back to "on." This often restores power. If the breaker trips again immediately, you might have a short circuit, which needs professional help. A common reason a Samsung dishwasher won't turn on can be linked to tripped breakers.

### Test the Wall Outlet

You can test the outlet itself. Unplug the dishwasher. Plug another small appliance, like a phone charger or a lamp, into the same outlet. If the other appliance does not work, the outlet itself may have no power. This could indicate a wiring issue in your home. You should consult an electrician if the outlet is dead.

### Examine the Thermal Fuse

Many Samsung dishwashers have a thermal fuse. This fuse protects the dishwasher from overheating. If the dishwasher overheats, the fuse blows, cutting off power. A blown thermal fuse means the dishwasher **won't run** until you replace the fuse. This repair often requires disassembling part of the dishwasher. You can find more specific troubleshooting steps for power issues on [why my Samsung dishwasher won't turn on](https://homeessentialsguide.com/why-my-samsung-dishwasher-wont-turn-on). This step is more advanced and may require a multimeter to test for continuity.

## Troubleshooting Door Latch and Switch Issues

A properly closed and latched door is essential for your **Samsung dishwasher** to run. Dishwashers have safety mechanisms. They prevent water from spilling out during operation. If the door is not perfectly secure, the cycle will not begin.

### Ensure the Door is Fully Closed

First, push the dishwasher door firmly shut. Sometimes, dishes or the detergent dispenser can block the door from closing completely. Rearrange any items that might be in the way. Listen for a click, which indicates the latch has engaged.

### Inspect the Door Latch Mechanism

Examine the door latch itself. This is the component that holds the door closed. Look for any visible damage, cracks, or misalignment. The latch might be worn out or broken. A broken latch will not engage properly.

### Test the Door Latch Switch

Inside the latch mechanism is a small switch, or sometimes two switches. These switches tell the dishwasher's control board that the door is closed and secure. If these switches are faulty, the dishwasher thinks the door is still open. Even if the door looks closed, the switch might not be making contact. You cannot easily test this without a multimeter and some disassembly. A faulty door latch or switch is a common reason why your dishwasher won't start. You can find more details on troubleshooting door issues specifically for Samsung dishwashers if your [Samsung dishwasher door won't close](https://homeessentialsguide.com/why-samsung-dishwasher-door-wont-close) properly.

### Clean Around the Latch Area

Sometimes, food debris or detergent residue can build up around the door latch. This buildup can prevent the latch from fully engaging. Use a damp cloth to clean the area thoroughly. Ensure there is no obstruction. A clean latch mechanism works more reliably.

## Resolving Water Supply and Filling Problems

Your **Samsung dishwasher won't run** if it cannot get water. The dishwasher needs water to fill the tub for washing. If the water supply is cut off or restricted, the dishwasher will pause or refuse to start a cycle. This is a common point of failure.

### Check the Water Supply Valve

First, locate the water supply valve. This valve is usually under the kitchen sink, near the dishwasher's water line. Make sure this valve is fully open. If it is partially closed or completely shut, water cannot enter the dishwasher. Turn it counter-clockwise to open it fully.

### Inspect the Inlet Water Hose

Examine the water inlet hose connected to the back of the dishwasher. Look for any kinks, bends, or damage. A kinked hose can severely restrict water flow. Straighten any kinks you find. Ensure the hose is not pinched behind the dishwasher.

### Clean the Inlet Valve Screen

The water inlet valve has a small screen filter. This screen traps sediment and debris from your home's water supply. Over time, this screen can become clogged. A clogged screen restricts water flow into the dishwasher. You may need to disconnect the water hose to access and clean this screen. Turn off the water supply before disconnecting the hose. Gently clean the screen using a brush or rinse it under running water. A similar issue can cause other dishwashers not to fill, like when a [GE dishwasher won't fill with water](https://homeessentialsguide.com/why-wont-my-ge-dishwasher-fill-with-water).

### Test the Water Inlet Valve

The water inlet valve is an electrical component. It opens to allow water into the dishwasher when signaled by the control board. If this valve is faulty, it will not open, and the dishwasher will not fill. You can test the valve with a multimeter for continuity, but this usually requires professional help. A humming sound without water filling could indicate a faulty valve.

### Check the Float Switch

Inside the bottom of your Samsung dishwasher is a float switch. This switch detects the water level in the tub. If the float switch is stuck in the "full" position, or if it is faulty, the dishwasher will think it already has enough water. It will then not allow more water to enter, preventing the cycle from starting. Make sure the float moves freely. Clean any debris around it.

## Diagnosing Drainage System Issues

Sometimes, your **Samsung dishwasher won't run** because it thinks there is still water in the tub from a previous cycle. This is a safety mechanism. The dishwasher needs to drain completely before starting a new wash. A clogged or faulty drainage system can prevent new cycles from beginning.

### Clean the Dishwasher Filter

Samsung dishwashers have filters at the bottom of the tub. These filters catch food particles and debris. If the filters become clogged, water cannot drain properly. This causes dirty water to remain in the tub. Remove the filters and clean them thoroughly under running water. You can use a brush to scrub away stubborn residue. This is a maintenance task you should do regularly.

### Inspect the Drain Hose

The drain hose carries wastewater from the dishwasher to your sink drain or garbage disposal. Check the drain hose for any kinks, bends, or blockages. Make sure it is properly installed and not pinched behind the appliance. Sometimes, food debris or grease can build up inside the hose. You might need to disconnect the hose and clear it. Use a long brush or snake if necessary.

### Check the Air Gap (If Applicable)

Some plumbing systems use an air gap device on the sink. This prevents dirty drain water from flowing back into the dishwasher. The air gap can become clogged with food particles. If your sink has an air gap, check it for blockages. Clear any debris you find.

### Examine the Drain Pump

The drain pump is responsible for expelling water from the dishwasher. If the drain pump is faulty or obstructed, water will not drain. You might hear a humming sound but no water movement. Sometimes, small objects like broken glass or fruit pits can get stuck in the pump impeller. This prevents it from spinning. Accessing the drain pump requires some disassembly of the dishwasher. This is a more advanced repair. If the drain pump is seized or burnt out, it will need replacement.

### Garbage Disposal Connection (If Applicable)

If your dishwasher drains into a garbage disposal, ensure the disposal is clear. Run the disposal for a few seconds before starting the dishwasher. Also, if you recently installed a new garbage disposal, check if the knockout plug was removed. This plug blocks the dishwasher drain line. If it is still there, your dishwasher **won't run** because water cannot drain.

## Understanding Control Panel and Electronics Problems

Your **Samsung dishwasher won't run** if its brain, the control panel, is faulty. The control panel manages all dishwasher functions. Electronic issues can prevent cycles from starting or cause unexpected behavior. This area can be tricky to troubleshoot.

### Perform a Hard Reset

Often, electronic glitches can be resolved with a simple reset. Turn off the circuit breaker for the dishwasher for at least 5 minutes. Then, turn the breaker back on. This is like rebooting a computer. It can clear temporary errors in the control board. This is my first step for many electronic issues.

### Look for Error Codes

Samsung dishwashers often display error codes on their control panel. These codes are crucial clues. They indicate specific problems the dishwasher is detecting. For example, an "OE" code usually points to a draining issue. A "LE" code might indicate a leak. Consult your Samsung dishwasher's user manual to decipher the error codes. The manual explains what each code means and suggests troubleshooting steps. Knowing the code helps you narrow down the cause when your **Samsung dishwasher won't start**.

### Inspect the Control Panel for Damage

Visually examine the control panel itself. Look for any signs of damage, such as cracked buttons, non-responsive touchpads, or dim displays. Water damage or power surges can sometimes damage the control panel. If buttons are sticky or unresponsive, it might need cleaning or replacement.

### Main Control Board Issues

The main control board is the central processing unit of your dishwasher. If this board is faulty, it cannot send the correct signals to other components. This means the dishwasher will not start a cycle. Sometimes, you might see random lights flashing or no lights at all. Testing the main control board usually requires a multimeter and advanced diagnostic skills. Replacing the main control board is an expensive repair. This is often the last resort after checking all other possibilities.

### Child Lock Feature

Samsung dishwashers have a child lock feature. This feature prevents accidental operation. If child lock is activated, the dishwasher **won't run** even if you press the start button. The control panel display might show an icon like a padlock. Refer to your manual to learn how to deactivate the child lock feature for your specific model. It usually involves pressing and holding a combination of buttons for a few seconds.

## Addressing Motor and Pump Assembly Failures

If your **Samsung dishwasher won't run** and you've ruled out power, door, and water issues, the problem might lie with the motor or pump. These components are critical for washing and draining cycles. Issues here often mean internal component failure.

### The Wash Motor

The wash motor, or circulation pump, powers the spray arms. If this motor fails, the dishwasher may fill with water but will not spray. You might hear a faint hum but no washing sound. Sometimes, objects can get stuck in the impeller of the wash motor. This prevents it from spinning. This motor is typically located at the bottom of the dishwasher. You would need to remove the lower spray arm and potentially the filter assembly to access it. A faulty wash motor needs replacement.

### The Drain Pump

As discussed earlier, the drain pump removes water. If it fails, water stays in the tub. The dishwasher then refuses to start a new cycle. A humming sound without water draining suggests a blocked or faulty drain pump. Small, hard objects like glass shards or fruit pits can jam the impeller. If the pump is burnt out, it needs to be replaced. Replacing a drain pump involves disconnecting the power and water supply and removing the lower panels of the dishwasher.

### Identifying Motor Failure Symptoms

*   **No Spraying, but Water Fills:** This often points to a wash motor issue.
*   **Humming Noise, No Action:** Could be a seized motor (wash or drain) or something stuck in it.
*   **Burning Smell:** A strong burning smell indicates a motor has overheated or shorted out.
*   **Error Codes:** Specific error codes might indicate motor or pump failure (check your manual).

### Advanced Diagnostics and Replacement

Diagnosing motor or pump issues often requires more advanced tools. You might need a multimeter to test electrical continuity and resistance. Replacement parts must be specific to your Samsung model. If you are not comfortable with appliance repair, I recommend calling a qualified technician. Repairing these components involves working with water and electricity. This can be dangerous if not handled correctly.

## Common Obstructions and Maintenance Issues

Sometimes your **Samsung dishwasher won't run** due to simple obstructions or a lack of routine maintenance. These are often easy fixes. They can prevent costly repairs if addressed promptly. I always recommend regular cleaning.

### Clogged Spray Arms

The spray arms have small holes that jet water to clean dishes. Over time, these holes can become clogged with food debris or mineral deposits. If the holes are blocked, water pressure decreases, and dishes do not get clean. In severe cases, it can impede the water circulation enough to prevent a cycle from starting properly. Remove the spray arms (they usually twist off) and clean the holes with a toothpick or small wire. Rinse them thoroughly.

### Dirty Filters

As mentioned before, the dishwasher's filters collect food particles. If these filters are not cleaned regularly, they become severely clogged. A very clogged filter can prevent proper water circulation or drainage. This will stop the dishwasher from running. Make it a habit to clean your dishwasher filters at least once a month. This simple step keeps your **Samsung dishwasher** running efficiently.

### Detergent Dispenser Issues

If the detergent dispenser is faulty or blocked, it may not open during the cycle. This means detergent doesn't get released, and your dishes won't get clean. While this might not completely stop the dishwasher from running, it impacts performance. Ensure the dispenser is not sticky or blocked by dried detergent. Clean it if necessary.

### Overloading the Dishwasher

Overloading your dishwasher can prevent the spray arms from rotating freely. It can also block water from reaching all areas. If the spray arms are blocked, the water pressure might not build correctly. This could cause the dishwasher to stall or fail to start a cycle. Always load your dishwasher according to the manufacturer's guidelines. Ensure there is enough space between dishes for water to circulate.

### Hard Water Build-up

Hard water can lead to mineral deposits (limescale) inside your dishwasher. This buildup can affect pumps, hoses, and heating elements. Significant limescale can hinder the functioning of various components. Use a dishwasher cleaner specifically designed to remove limescale. Run an empty cycle with vinegar or a commercial descaler periodically. This helps maintain your dishwasher's internal components.

## FAQs About Your Samsung Dishwasher Not Running

### Q1: Why does my Samsung dishwasher click but not start?
A: A clicking sound often indicates the door latch is attempting to engage, but it might be faulty or misaligned. It could also point to a problem with the water inlet valve trying to open. Check the door closure first, then inspect the latch mechanism.

### Q2: How do I reset my Samsung dishwasher when it won't run?
A: To reset your Samsung dishwasher, first try holding the "Start/Reset" button for a few seconds. If that does not work, turn off the circuit breaker that powers the dishwasher. Wait for about 5 minutes, then turn the breaker back on. This hard reset can clear many electronic glitches.

### Q3: What do I do if my Samsung dishwasher has power but won't start?
A: If your Samsung dishwasher has power but won't start, check the door latch for proper closure and function. Verify the water supply valve is open. Look for any error codes on the display. The issue might be a faulty control board, a blocked water inlet, or a problem with the float switch.

### Q4: Can a clogged filter prevent my Samsung dishwasher from starting?
A: Yes, a severely clogged filter can prevent your Samsung dishwasher from running a cycle. If water cannot drain properly from the previous cycle, the dishwasher's sensors may detect standing water. This will prevent a new cycle from starting as a safety measure. Clean the filters regularly.

### Q5: What does a "LE" or "OE" error code mean on a Samsung dishwasher?
A: An "LE" error code typically indicates a leak detected by the dishwasher. An "OE" error code usually means an overflow or drainage issue, suggesting water is not draining properly from the tub. Both codes will stop the dishwasher from running a normal cycle. Consult your manual for exact meanings and troubleshooting steps.
