## LG Dishwasher Not Working? Simple Fixes to Try

Your LG dishwasher keeps your kitchen running smoothly. When your LG dishwasher is not working, it can disrupt your daily routine. You might find yourself with a pile of dirty dishes. This article helps you understand why your LG dishwasher might stop working. We will look at common problems and provide clear steps to fix them. You can save time and money by troubleshooting basic issues yourself. This guide covers everything from power problems to clogged filters. We aim to help you get your dishwasher cleaning dishes again fast.

### Takeaway

Here are the key actions to take when your LG dishwasher stops working:

*   **Check Power and Water:** Ensure the dishwasher has power and water supply.
*   **Inspect Door Latch:** Make sure the door closes fully and latches.
*   **Clear Blockages:** Look for clogs in filters, spray arms, and the drain hose.
*   **Reset the Appliance:** Perform a hard reset to clear minor glitches.
*   **Examine Controls:** Check if the control panel or buttons respond correctly.

When your LG dishwasher is not working, common causes include power interruptions, issues with water supply or drainage, a faulty door latch, or clogged internal components like filters and spray arms. Minor glitches often resolve with a simple reset.

## Understanding Electrical Issues: Why Your LG Dishwasher Has No Power

Imagine you load your dishwasher, press start, and nothing happens. This is often an electrical issue. Your LG dishwasher needs consistent power to run. The first step is always to check the power source. This simple check can save you a lot of time and worry.

Many times, a tripped circuit breaker is the problem. Circuit breakers protect your home from electrical overload. If your dishwasher pulls too much power, the breaker might trip. This cuts power to the dishwasher. Go to your home's electrical panel. Find the breaker marked for the kitchen or dishwasher. It might be in the "off" position or in the middle. Flip it completely off, then firmly flip it back to the "on" position. If it trips again immediately, you might have a deeper electrical problem. You may need an electrician for this.

Another power issue can be the power cord itself. Sometimes, the cord can be loose from the outlet. It might even be damaged. Make sure the cord is fully pushed into the wall outlet. Check the cord for any visible fraying or cuts. If you see damage, do not use the dishwasher. A damaged cord is a fire hazard. Unplug it and call for service. Remember, proper power is essential for any appliance to work.

Sometimes, issues like these extend to other LG appliances. For instance, if you are also experiencing issues with your LG refrigerator, you might find similar troubleshooting steps useful for power-related problems. You can learn more about general LG appliance power issues by checking out [why did my lg refrigerator stop working](https://homeessentialsguide.com/why-did-my-lg-refrigerator-stop-working). It is good to check your home's electrical system if multiple appliances show problems. Make sure the outlet itself works. You can plug in a small lamp or another appliance to test it. If the outlet does not work, it is not a dishwasher problem. This means you need to fix the outlet.

### H3: Troubleshooting Power Source Problems

You need to systematically check each part of the power supply.

1.  **Check the Outlet:** Unplug the dishwasher. Plug another small appliance, like a hair dryer, into the same outlet. If that appliance works, the outlet is fine. If not, the outlet or its circuit is the problem.
2.  **Reset the Circuit Breaker:** Locate your home's electrical panel. Find the circuit breaker labeled for the dishwasher. It usually says "Dishwasher" or "Kitchen." Flip the breaker completely to the "OFF" position. Wait 30 seconds. Then, flip it firmly back to the "ON" position. This resets the electrical flow.
3.  **Inspect the Power Cord:** Visually check the dishwasher's power cord. Look for any kinks, cuts, or signs of burning. Ensure the plug is fully seated in the wall outlet. A loose plug can cause intermittent power. If the cord is damaged, do not use the dishwasher. You will need a replacement cord or professional help.
4.  **GFCI Outlet Check:** If your dishwasher is plugged into a Ground Fault Circuit Interrupter (GFCI) outlet, check it. GFCI outlets have "TEST" and "RESET" buttons. Press the "RESET" button firmly. If it trips repeatedly, there might be a ground fault issue. This means a wiring problem. You may also notice your [LG dishwasher has no power](https://homeessentialsguide.com/why-does-my-lg-dishwasher-have-no-power) if the GFCI trips.

## Addressing Water Supply and Drainage Problems

Your LG dishwasher needs a consistent supply of water. It also needs a clear path to drain dirty water. If your LG dishwasher is not working, it might be due to issues with water intake or drainage. Without proper water flow, the wash cycle cannot start or complete. This is a very common reason for a dishwasher to stop.

First, check the water supply line. The water supply valve for the dishwasher is usually under the kitchen sink. Make sure this valve is fully open. If it is closed, no water can enter the dishwasher. Sometimes, the supply hose itself can get kinked. A kink in the hose restricts water flow. Straighten any kinks you find. Also, check for any visible leaks around the hose connections. A small leak can indicate a loose fitting or damaged hose. This means you need to tighten it or replace the hose.

Next, consider drainage issues. Your dishwasher removes dirty water through a drain hose. This hose usually connects to your sink's garbage disposal or a drain pipe. If the drain hose is clogged or kinked, water cannot leave the dishwasher. This often leads to standing water in the bottom of the tub. A common culprit is food debris or grease buildup inside the hose. Disconnect the drain hose if you can and check for blockages. You may need to clear it out.

A blocked air gap can also prevent draining. An air gap is a small cylinder on your sink. It helps prevent dirty water from flowing back into your clean water supply. If it gets clogged, the dishwasher cannot drain properly. Check for debris in the air gap and clean it if necessary. Lastly, the garbage disposal itself can cause drainage issues. If your drain hose connects to the disposal, make sure the disposal is clear. Run the disposal for a minute or two before starting the dishwasher. This helps clear any food particles. If your LG dishwasher has water in the bottom, drainage issues are almost certainly the cause. You can find more specific guidance on this problem here: [why does my lg dishwasher have water in the bottom](https://homeessentialsguide.com/why-does-my-lg-dishwasher-have-water-in-the-bottom).

### H3: Steps to Resolve Water Flow Issues

Follow these steps to diagnose and fix water supply and drainage problems.

1.  **Verify Water Supply Valve:** Locate the water shut-off valve under your kitchen sink. This valve controls water flow to the dishwasher. Ensure it is fully open by turning it counter-clockwise. A closed or partially closed valve will prevent water from entering the unit.
2.  **Inspect Water Inlet Hose:** Check the hose that connects the water supply valve to the dishwasher. Look for any kinks, bends, or damage. Straighten any kinks. Feel the hose for blockages if possible. If the hose is damaged or leaking, it needs replacement.
3.  **Clean the Air Gap:** If your sink has an air gap (a small cylindrical device on the countertop near the faucet), remove its cover. Check for debris, especially food particles. Clean out any blockages with a small brush or toothpick. This device is crucial for proper drainage and preventing backflow.
4.  **Clear the Drain Hose:** The drain hose runs from the dishwasher to your sink drain or garbage disposal. Disconnect it (often secured with a clamp) and check for clogs. Food particles and grease can accumulate inside, blocking water flow. You might need to flush it with water or use a drain snake.
5.  **Check Garbage Disposal:** If your dishwasher drains into a garbage disposal, ensure the disposal is clear. Run the disposal for about 30 seconds with water before starting the dishwasher. A new disposal might have a plug in the dishwasher inlet port that needs to be removed.
6.  **Inspect Dishwasher Filters:** Inside the dishwasher, at the bottom, there are filters that catch food debris. If these are excessively clogged, they can impede both water circulation during washing and proper drainage. Remove and thoroughly clean them under running water.

## Examining Door Latch and Switch Malfunctions

Your LG dishwasher has safety features. One of the most important is the door latch and switch system. If the dishwasher door is not closed perfectly, the dishwasher will not start. This is a common reason why your LG dishwasher is not working. The machine needs to know the door is secure to prevent water leaks during operation.

The door latch ensures the door stays shut during the cycle. It also activates a small switch. This switch sends a signal to the control board. The signal tells the dishwasher it is safe to begin. If the latch is broken, bent, or misaligned, it cannot properly engage the switch. Even a slight misalignment can stop the dishwasher from starting. You should visually inspect the latch mechanism on the door and the strike plate on the dishwasher frame. Look for any physical damage.

Sometimes, the door switch itself can fail. Even if the latch looks fine and engages, the internal switch might not register the closure. You might hear a click when you close the door. This click means the latch is engaging. But the switch might still be faulty. There is no easy way to test the switch without specific tools. However, you can try gently pushing on the door while attempting to start the cycle. Sometimes, a slight pressure helps a weak switch make contact.

If the door latch mechanism is loose, tighten any visible screws. If the latch is broken, it will need replacement. This is usually a straightforward repair. However, a faulty door switch often requires professional service or specific parts. Remember, the dishwasher is designed not to run if it detects an open door. This prevents flooding. So, always ensure the door clicks shut firmly. Sometimes, other parts of the dishwasher can fail unexpectedly, similar to when your LG dishwasher keeps turning off. Such issues can sometimes be linked to general safety mechanisms, making a robust door latch even more critical. You might find it helpful to learn more about how to ensure your LG dishwasher is operating correctly and not encountering random shutdowns by exploring articles like [why does my lg dishwasher keep turning off](https://homeessentialsguide.com/why-does-my-lg-dishwasher-keep-turning-off).

### H3: Steps to Check and Fix Door Latch Issues

Ensure your dishwasher door is securely closed and latched.

1.  **Visual Inspection:** Examine the door latch mechanism. This is the part on the door itself that hooks onto the dishwasher frame. Also, look at the strike plate on the dishwasher frame. Look for any visible damage. See if anything is bent, broken, or misaligned.
2.  **Test the Latch Mechanism:** Close the dishwasher door firmly. You should hear a distinct click as the latch engages. Try to open the door without pressing the release. If it opens easily, the latch might not be engaging properly.
3.  **Check for Loose Screws:** Sometimes, the latch assembly or strike plate can become loose over time. Look for any loose screws on the door edge or the dishwasher frame around the latch area. Tighten them carefully. Do not overtighten.
4.  **Door Alignment:** A misaligned door can prevent the latch from engaging. Check if the door sags or looks crooked. If it does, you might need to adjust the dishwasher's leveling feet. This can help correct the door's position.
5.  **Push and Test:** With the door closed, gently push on the door while trying to start the cycle. Sometimes, a slight push helps a weak or misaligned switch make contact. If the dishwasher starts, it confirms an issue with the latch or switch.
6.  **Professional Assessment:** If the latch looks fine but the dishwasher still does not start, the door switch itself might be faulty. Replacing a door switch often requires disassembling part of the door panel. This may require a technician.

## Troubleshooting Control Panel and Button Problems

Modern LG dishwashers use electronic control panels. These panels manage all functions of the appliance. If your LG dishwasher is not working, a faulty control panel or unresponsive buttons can be the cause. You press a button, and nothing happens. Or, the display might be blank or show strange codes. This indicates a problem with the dishwasher's brain.

A simple power reset can often fix minor electronic glitches. Just like restarting a computer, unplugging the dishwasher for a few minutes can clear the control board's memory. This might resolve temporary software freezes or communication errors. If the dishwasher has no physical plug, you can trip the circuit breaker for 5-10 minutes. This is a common first step for many appliance problems. If your LG dishwasher is not turning on at all, a control board issue could be why. This can prevent them from making proper contact. Carefully inspect each button. Look for stickiness or visible damage. Try pressing each button firmly. If only certain buttons do not work, it points to a specific button failure.

Sometimes, the entire control board is faulty. This is less common but can happen. A faulty control board might cause random errors, unresponsive buttons, or complete failure to start. If the display is completely blank and a power reset does not help, a new control board might be needed. This is a more complex repair. It often requires professional help. ### H3: Resolving Control Board and Button Issues

Address non-responsive controls or error displays.

1.  **Perform a Hard Reset:** Disconnect the dishwasher from power. You can do this by unplugging it from the wall outlet or by turning off its dedicated circuit breaker at your home's electrical panel. Leave it unplugged or off for 5 to 10 minutes. Then, restore power. This can clear minor electronic glitches and restore functionality.
2.  **Inspect the Control Panel and Buttons:** Carefully look at the entire control panel. Check for any physical damage, cracks, or signs of water intrusion. Press each button firmly to see if it feels stuck or if it responds. Sometimes, food debris or liquid spills can cause buttons to stick or become unresponsive. Clean around the buttons with a damp cloth if needed.
3.  **Check for Error Codes:** LG dishwashers often display error codes when there is a problem. If your display shows letters and numbers, consult your owner's manual. The manual lists what each code means and suggests troubleshooting steps. Understanding the error code provides specific direction for repair.
4.  **Test Individual Buttons:** If some buttons work but others do not, the issue might be with the individual buttons rather than the entire control board. This can be due to wear and tear. If multiple buttons are unresponsive, especially those next to each other, the problem is more likely with the control panel ribbon cable or the board itself.
5.  **Consider Child Lock/Control Lock:** Many LG dishwashers have a "Child Lock" or "Control Lock" feature. This feature disables the buttons to prevent accidental use. Check your manual to see how to activate and deactivate this lock. It is often indicated by a lock icon on the display or a specific button combination. Ensure this feature is not accidentally engaged.

## Cleaning Clogged Filters and Spray Arms

A dishwasher's job is to clean dishes. But if its own cleaning components are dirty, your LG dishwasher is not working efficiently. Clogged filters and spray arms are very common culprits. They prevent proper water circulation and cleaning performance. When these parts are blocked, water cannot reach all areas of your dishes. This leaves food particles behind.

The dishwasher has a filter system at the bottom of the tub. This filter catches food debris and prevents it from recirculating. Over time, food particles, grease, and even small pieces of broken glass can accumulate in this filter. If the filter becomes too clogged, water flow is restricted. The dishwasher might not clean well. It might even leave dishes dirty or full of grit. You should regularly remove and clean the filter. Most LG filters twist out easily. Rinse them under warm running water. Use a soft brush to remove stubborn debris.

Spray arms are another crucial component. These are the spinning arms with small holes (nozzles) that spray water onto your dishes. If these nozzles get clogged, the water jets become weak or uneven. This means some dishes might not get cleaned at all. Food particles, mineral deposits from hard water, or detergent residue can block these tiny holes. You can remove the spray arms. They usually unclip or unscrew. Use a toothpick or small wire to clear each hole. Make sure all holes are clear before reattaching them.

Ignoring these cleaning tasks can lead to poor wash results. It can also put strain on the dishwasher's pump. This can lead to more serious issues over time. Regular cleaning of filters and spray arms is part of routine maintenance. It helps keep your LG dishwasher running well. It also ensures it lasts longer. A properly maintained dishwasher washes dishes better.

### H3: Steps to Clean Dishwasher Components

Regular cleaning of filters and spray arms is vital for optimal performance.

1.  **Locate the Filters:** Open your dishwasher door. Look at the bottom of the tub. You will see a cylindrical filter assembly, usually in the center. Some LG models might have a two-part filter system: a coarse filter and a microfilter.
2.  **Remove the Filters:** Twist the top of the cylindrical filter counter-clockwise to unlock it. Lift it straight up. Then, remove any flat filter screens that might be underneath. Be careful not to let debris fall back into the drain opening.
3.  **Clean the Filters:** Rinse the filter parts under warm, running water. Use a soft brush (an old toothbrush works well) to gently scrub away any food particles, grease, or grime. Make sure all mesh areas are clear. Do not use harsh chemicals or abrasive sponges, as they can damage the filter material.
4.  **Locate and Remove Spray Arms:** Your dishwasher has at least one lower spray arm. Some models also have an upper spray arm. These usually unclip or unscrew from their mounts. Carefully remove them from the dishwasher.
5.  **Clean Spray Arm Nozzles:** Inspect the small holes (nozzles) on the spray arms. Use a toothpick, thin wire, or straightened paper clip to poke through each hole. This will dislodge any trapped food particles, mineral deposits, or detergent residue. Ensure water can spray freely from all holes.
6.  **Reinstall Components:** Once cleaned, carefully reattach the spray arms. Make sure they click securely into place and can spin freely. Reinsert the filter components, aligning them correctly, and twist the cylindrical filter clockwise to lock it back into position. Running an empty cycle with dishwasher cleaner after cleaning can help remove any remaining residue.

## Diagnosing Motor and Pump Failures

If your LG dishwasher is not working, and you hear unusual noises or no water is circulating, it could point to a motor or pump issue. These components are essential. The wash pump circulates water throughout the tub and spray arms. The drain pump removes dirty water. If either fails, the dishwasher cannot perform its core functions.

A failing wash pump might produce a loud humming noise. You might also hear grinding sounds during the wash cycle. If the pump is completely seized, you might hear nothing at all, even though the dishwasher has power. The spray arms will not spin, and dishes will not get wet. This is a serious issue that often requires professional repair. The pump impeller (the part that spins to move water) can become clogged with hard objects. Pieces of glass or plastic can jam it. This prevents the motor from turning.

Similarly, a faulty drain pump will prevent water from leaving the dishwasher. You will likely see standing water in the bottom of the tub after a cycle. A failing drain pump might also make a loud buzzing or grinding sound. Sometimes, a foreign object is trapped in the drain pump. Small bones, fruit pits, or broken glass can get lodged in the pump's impeller. This can prevent it from spinning. You can sometimes access the drain pump from inside the dishwasher tub after removing the filters. Look for any obstructions.

These issues are generally beyond simple DIY fixes. Replacing a dishwasher motor or pump involves disassembling the unit. It also requires working with electrical connections and plumbing. If you suspect a motor or pump failure, it is best to consult an appliance repair technician. They have the tools and expertise to diagnose and fix these problems safely. While this article focuses on LG dishwashers, problems with pumps and motors can happen across brands. For example, some Frigidaire models can experience similar issues with internal components, impacting their operation. You might find general troubleshooting for appliances useful if you are experiencing issues with other appliances, like if your [Frigidaire microwave is not working](https://homeessentialsguide.com/why-is-my-frigidaire-microwave-not-working).

### H3: Identifying Wash and Drain Pump Issues

Pay attention to sounds and water presence to diagnose pump problems.

1.  **Listen for Unusual Noises:** Start a wash cycle and listen carefully. A healthy wash pump runs smoothly. If you hear loud grinding, buzzing, or humming noises, it could indicate a failing motor or an obstruction in the pump impeller. If you hear a click but no motor sound, the motor might be seized.
2.  **Check Water Circulation:** Open the dishwasher door shortly after starting a cycle (be careful, hot water). The spray arms should be actively spraying water. If there is no water circulation, or very weak spray, the wash pump is likely not functioning correctly. Dishes will remain dry or poorly cleaned.
3.  **Inspect for Standing Water:** After a cycle completes, check the bottom of the dishwasher tub. If there is standing water, it means the drain pump is not working. The water should have been completely removed.
4.  **Look for Drain Pump Obstructions:** If you have standing water, there might be an obstruction in the drain pump. Unplug the dishwasher. Remove the filters at the bottom of the tub. Carefully reach into the drain pump area (you might need gloves). Feel for any foreign objects like broken glass, food debris, or small utensils that could be jamming the impeller.
5.  **Professional Diagnosis:** Motor and pump issues are often complex. Replacing these components requires significant disassembly of the dishwasher. It also involves working with electrical and plumbing connections. If you suspect a motor or pump failure after basic checks, it is advisable to contact a qualified appliance technician. They can accurately diagnose the issue and perform the repair safely.

## Sensor Malfunctions and Error Codes

Modern LG dishwashers use various sensors. These sensors monitor things like water temperature, turbidity (how dirty the water is), and water level. If your LG dishwasher is not working, a faulty sensor can send incorrect information to the control board. This can cause the dishwasher to stop mid-cycle, not start at all, or display an error code.

Error codes are your dishwasher's way of telling you what is wrong. When a sensor fails, the control board often displays a specific code on the digital screen. For example, a common LG error code related to water issues might be OE (Drainage Error) or LE (Leakage Error). It is important to consult your LG dishwasher owner's manual. The manual explains what each code means. It also provides basic troubleshooting steps for that specific error. Keep your manual handy.

Sometimes, a sensor might not be faulty. It might just be dirty. The turbidity sensor, which detects how dirty the water is, can get covered in gunk. This happens if you do not scrape dishes properly before loading. If this sensor is dirty, the dishwasher might run longer cycles. It might also use more water than needed. It could also stop early. Cleaning this sensor, usually located at the bottom of the tub, can resolve the issue.

Water level sensors can also cause problems. If the sensor thinks there is not enough water, the dishwasher might not start. If it thinks there is too much, it might activate the drain pump prematurely. These sensors can sometimes get stuck or give false readings. A common fix for sensor glitches, especially those causing error codes, is a power reset. Unplugging the dishwasher or flipping its circuit breaker for 5-10 minutes can often clear temporary sensor errors. If error codes persist after a reset, or if the dishwasher is behaving strangely without a clear code, professional diagnosis might be necessary. It's similar to how an LG microwave might have issues with its start button not working, where a sensor or control panel fault can prevent operation. You can refer to articles like [why is my lg microwave start button not working](https://homeessentialsguide.com/why-is-my-lg-microwave-start-button-not-working) for examples of similar control issues across different LG appliances.

### H3: How to Interpret and Address Error Codes

Error codes provide clues. Use them to diagnose specific problems.

1.  **Identify the Error Code:** Look at your dishwasher's display panel. If an error is detected, it will usually show a combination of letters and numbers (e.g., "OE," "LE," "HE," "FE"). Write down the exact code you see.
2.  **Consult Your Owner's Manual:** This is the most crucial step. Your LG dishwasher owner's manual contains a detailed list of error codes specific to your model. It explains what each code signifies and often provides initial troubleshooting steps you can perform. If you do not have the physical manual, you can usually find a digital version on the LG website by searching for your model number.
3.  **Perform a Power Reset:** Many error codes are temporary glitches. Disconnect power to the dishwasher for 5-10 minutes by unplugging it or turning off its circuit breaker. Then, restore power and see if the error code clears. This often resets the sensors and control board.
4.  **Address Code-Specific Issues:**
    *   **OE (Drainage Error):** This indicates a drain blockage. Check the drain hose, air gap, and pump area for obstructions, as discussed in the "Water Supply and Drainage Problems" section.
    *   **LE (Leakage Error):** This means the dishwasher detects a leak. Look for visible water leaks under or around the appliance. You might need to check hose connections or the tub for cracks.
    *   **IE (Water Inlet Error):** This means the dishwasher is not getting water. Check the water supply valve and the water inlet hose for kinks or blockages.
    *   **HE (Heating Error) or TE (Temperature Sensor Error):** These indicate a problem with the heating element or temperature sensor. This usually requires a technician.
5.  **Clean Sensors (If Accessible):** Some sensors, like the turbidity sensor (which looks at water cleanliness), can get dirty. This sensor is often at the bottom of the tub near the filters. Carefully clean it with a soft cloth if it is accessible and appears grimy.
