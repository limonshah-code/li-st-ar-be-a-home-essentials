## Keep Your Dishes Sparkling: How to Clean Your GE Profile Dishwasher Filter

Having clean dishes after every wash is what we all expect from our dishwashers. But what happens when your plates come out spotty, or your machine starts to smell? Often, the culprit is a dirty filter. If you own a GE Profile dishwasher, understanding how to clean the filter is a simple yet crucial skill. This guide will walk you through each step. I will show you how to maintain your appliance. You can keep your dishes sparkling clean. We will cover finding the filter, removing it, cleaning it thoroughly, and putting it back. Let's make sure your GE Profile dishwasher performs its best every time.

### Takeaway

*   **Locate the Filter:** Find the cylindrical filter assembly at the bottom of the dishwasher tub.
*   **Twist and Lift:** Rotate the fine filter counter-clockwise and lift it out.
*   **Separate Components:** Pull the coarse filter from the bottom of the fine filter.
*   **Rinse and Scrub:** Use warm water, mild soap, and a soft brush to remove food particles.
*   **Reassemble:** Put the coarse filter back into the fine filter, then place the assembly into the dishwasher.
*   **Secure:** Twist clockwise until the filter locks into place.
*   **Regular Cleaning:** Clean your filter monthly or more often with heavy use.

To clean the filter in your GE Profile dishwasher, first locate the cylindrical filter assembly at the bottom of the wash tub. Twist the fine mesh filter counter-clockwise and lift it out. Separate the coarse filter. Rinse both parts under warm water, scrubbing away any food debris with a soft brush and mild dish soap. Reassemble and twist back into place until secure.

## Locating Your GE Profile Dishwasher Filter

Finding the filter in your GE Profile dishwasher is usually quite simple. It sits at the bottom of the dishwasher tub. This area is typically below the lower spray arm. You will see a cylindrical component. This is the filter assembly. It may look like a small tower. This assembly often has two parts: a fine mesh filter and a coarse filter.

I always recommend removing the lower dish rack first. This gives you clear access to the bottom of the dishwasher. Look for a circular or cylindrical component. This component usually twists out. Its design allows it to trap food particles from the wash water.
