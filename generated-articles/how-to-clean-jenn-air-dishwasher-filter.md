## How To Clean Jenn Air Dishwasher Filter for Spotless Dishes

Dirty dishes coming out of your dishwasher are frustrating. You expect sparkling results, but sometimes you get grimy residue. Often, the culprit is a clogged filter. Cleaning your Jenn Air dishwasher filter is a simple task that makes a big difference. This routine maintenance step ensures your dishes come out perfectly clean every time.

In this guide, I will show you exactly how to clean your Jenn Air dishwasher filter. We will cover why it matters, what tools you need, and a step-by-step process. You will learn to remove, clean, and reinstall the filter correctly. Keeping your dishwasher filter clean improves its efficiency and extends its life. Let's get your Jenn Air dishwasher working its best.

### Takeaway:
*   Regularly clean your Jenn Air dishwasher filter to prevent food particles from recirculating.
*   Gather simple tools like dish soap, a brush, and a cloth before you start.
*   Follow specific instructions to safely remove, clean, and reinstall your filter.
*   Clean the filter every few weeks, or more often with heavy use, to maintain performance.

To clean a Jenn Air dishwasher filter, first remove the lower rack and spray arm. Twist and lift out the cylindrical filter, then separate its mesh and coarse components. Rinse all parts under warm running water, scrubbing away food debris with a soft brush and mild dish soap. Reassemble the filter and firmly twist it back into place.

## Understanding Your Jenn Air Dishwasher Filter System

Your Jenn Air dishwasher works hard to clean your dishes. A key part of its cleaning process involves a filter system. This system catches food particles and debris, stopping them from redepositing on your dishes. Without a clean filter, those particles recirculate, leading to less effective cleaning.

Jenn Air dishwashers use a manual filter system, unlike some older models with self-cleaning grinders. This means you must manually remove and clean the filter. This design often leads to quieter operation. However, it also requires your active participation in maintenance. Ignoring the filter can cause unpleasant odors and poor washing performance.

A dirty filter reduces water pressure to the spray arms. It also allows food bits to stick to your supposedly clean dishes. You might notice cloudy glasses or food residue on plates. These are clear signs that your Jenn Air dishwasher filter needs attention. Regular cleaning ensures peak performance and spotless results. Knowing your filter system helps you keep your appliance in top shape. You can check if your specific model has a manual filter by referring to its manual or by looking inside the dishwasher. ## Gathering Your Tools for a Thorough Clean

Before you begin cleaning your Jenn Air dishwasher filter, collect the right tools. Having everything ready saves time and makes the process smooth. You likely have most of these items around your home already. Preparation helps ensure a thorough and effective clean.

Here is a list of what you will need:
*   **Rubber gloves:** These protect your hands from food debris and cleaning solutions.
*   **Mild dish soap:** A gentle detergent works best for breaking down grease and food particles.
*   **Soft brush or old toothbrush:** This helps scrub away stubborn residue without scratching the filter.
*   **Clean sponge or cloth:** Useful for wiping down the filter housing and other areas.
*   **Small towel:** Keep this handy for drying your hands or wiping up spills.
*   **Empty garbage can:** You will need a place to discard any large food debris.

Always prioritize safety during any appliance maintenance. Unplugging the dishwasher is usually not necessary for filter cleaning. However, you can turn off the circuit breaker for added peace of mind. Make sure the dishwasher is empty and cool before you start. This prevents any accidental burns or injuries. With your tools ready, you are now prepared to tackle the filter cleaning.

## Step-by-Step Guide to Removing Your Jenn Air Dishwasher Filter

Removing the filter from your Jenn Air dishwasher is a straightforward process. You do not need special tools. Just follow these steps carefully to ensure you do not damage any components. Locating the filter is the first key step.

Follow these simple instructions:
1.  **Empty the dishwasher:** Ensure there are no dishes or racks obstructing the filter area. You need clear access to the bottom of the tub.
2.  **Remove the lower dish rack:** Gently pull out the lower rack and set it aside. This gives you full access to the dishwasher's floor.
3.  **Locate the filter assembly:** Look in the center of the dishwasher tub floor. You will see a cylindrical filter, often with a handle or an arrow indicating how to turn it. This is your main filter. If you are not sure where to look, you can often find assistance. [How to find your dishwasher filter](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-find-your-dishwasher-filter) guides you to its location.
4.  **Twist and unlock the filter:** Most Jenn Air filters twist counter-clockwise to unlock. You might hear a click as it disengages. Hold the top part of the filter and turn it firmly.
5.  **Lift out the main filter:** Once unlocked, gently lift the cylindrical filter straight up and out. Be careful not to spill any debris.
6.  **Separate the filter components:** Jenn Air filters usually consist of two parts: a coarse filter (often the larger cylindrical part) and a fine micro-filter (a flat, mesh screen underneath or inside). Carefully separate these pieces. You might need to twist them apart slightly. Knowing how to get the filter out is crucial for proper cleaning. [How to get dishwasher filter out](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-get-dishwasher-filter-out) can offer more specific visual aid if needed.

You now have the filter components ready for cleaning. Take them to your sink for the next phase.

## Deep Cleaning the Jenn Air Dishwasher Filter Components

With the filter components removed, it is time for a thorough cleaning. This step removes all the accumulated food particles, grease, and grime. A clean filter ensures water flows freely and your dishes get truly clean. Proper cleaning also eliminates odors.

Here is how to deep clean each part:
*   **Rinse under running water:** Hold each filter component under warm running water. Rinse away any loose food particles. You will be surprised how much debris collects here.
*   **Apply mild dish soap:** Put a small amount of mild dish soap onto your soft brush or old toothbrush.
*   **Scrub the coarse filter:** Gently scrub the mesh and plastic parts of the coarse filter. Pay close attention to any areas with stuck-on food. The brush bristles reach into small crevices.
*   **Clean the micro-filter:** The micro-filter is typically a flat, fine mesh screen. Scrub this carefully with the brush and soap. Ensure all small holes are free of obstruction. You can hold it up to a light to check for blockages.
*   **Rinse again thoroughly:** Rinse both components thoroughly under warm water. Make sure all soap residue is gone. Any leftover soap could cause sudsing in your dishwasher.
*   **Inspect for remaining debris:** Visually inspect both filter parts. Look for any remaining food bits or greasy film. If you find any, repeat the scrubbing and rinsing process.
