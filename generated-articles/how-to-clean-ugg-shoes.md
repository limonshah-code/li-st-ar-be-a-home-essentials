## How To Clean Ugg Shoes: Your Ultimate Guide to Fresh Footwear

Ugg shoes bring warmth and comfort, but their soft suede and sheepskin easily show dirt. Keeping them looking new can seem hard. Many people wonder how to clean Ugg shoes properly without causing damage. This guide shows you simple steps to restore your favorite footwear. We cover everything from initial preparation to careful drying and long-term protection. By following these methods, your Uggs will stay fresh and soft for a long time.

### Takeaway

To keep your Ugg shoes clean and extend their life, follow these simple steps:

*   **Gather Tools:** Collect specialized Ugg cleaner, a soft brush, cloths, and paper towels.
*   **Dry Brush:** Gently remove loose dirt and debris from the surface.
*   **Spot Treat:** Address small stains with a suede eraser or a damp cloth and cleaner.
*   **Clean Uniformly:** If needed, lightly dampen the entire shoe and apply cleaner with a sponge.
*   **Rinse Gently:** Use a clean, damp sponge to wipe away cleaner residue.
*   **Air Dry Safely:** Stuff shoes with paper towels and let them dry away from heat.
*   **Protect:** Apply a water and stain repellent spray after drying for future defense.

### How to Clean Ugg Shoes

To effectively clean Ugg shoes, first remove loose dirt with a dry brush. Then, use a damp cloth and specialized Ugg cleaner to gently blot away stains, avoiding saturation. Rinse with a clean damp cloth, stuff the shoes with paper to maintain shape, and air dry completely away from direct heat. Finish with a protective spray.

### Understanding Ugg Materials: Suede and Sheepskin Care

Uggs are more than just boots; they are a blend of specific materials. Most Ugg shoes use twin-face sheepskin. This means the soft suede is on the outside, and the warm wool is on the inside, all from one piece of sheepskin. These materials give Uggs their unique comfort and warmth. However, they also make Uggs delicate.

Suede is a type of leather with a napped finish. It stains easily and can get water spots. The wool lining can also trap dirt and odors. Standard cleaning methods, like washing in a machine or using harsh soaps, can harm these materials. Such methods can shrink the sheepskin, stiffen the suede, or ruin the shoe's shape. This is why you need a special approach for proper [Ugg cleaning](https://www.homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-clean-ugg-shoes).

It is important to understand these materials to clean your Uggs safely. Knowing their properties helps you choose the right tools and methods. Treating suede and sheepskin with care ensures your shoes last longer. We aim to keep both the outer suede soft and the inner wool cozy. Proper care respects the natural qualities of the sheepskin.

Cleaning Uggs is different from cleaning other shoes. Think of the delicate nature of fine fabrics. You would not machine wash a silk scarf. Similarly, Uggs need gentle, specific care to maintain their look and feel. This section sets the stage for why our steps are important. It helps you avoid common mistakes that damage Uggs.

### Preparing Your Uggs for a Deep Clean

Before you apply any liquid, your Ugg shoes need proper preparation. This initial step is vital for effective cleaning. It removes surface debris that might turn into mud once wet. Skipping this step can make cleaning harder and less successful. Taking a few minutes to prepare your Uggs saves time later.

Start by grabbing your soft suede brush. Gently brush the entire surface of each Ugg shoe. Use short, light strokes in one direction. This helps lift away loose dirt, dust, and any dried mud. If your Uggs have a lot of mud, let it dry completely first. Then, you can brush it off more easily. Do not scrub hard, as this can damage the suede's nap. This initial brushing also helps to restore the suede's original texture. It is like giving your shoes a good dusting before cleaning.

Next, check your Uggs for any major scuffs or dry stains. For these, a suede eraser works wonders. Gently rub the eraser over the mark. This often lifts away surface imperfections. Be patient and use light pressure. The eraser removes the top layer of dirt without using water. It is a dry method for common blemishes. This method is also effective for general [how to keep shoes clean](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-keep-shoes-clean) for minor scuffs on many shoe types.

Once the outside is brushed, you should stuff your Uggs. Use paper towels or plain white newspaper for this. Crumple the paper and push it firmly inside the shoes. This helps them hold their natural shape during cleaning and drying. It prevents them from shrinking or collapsing. The paper also absorbs extra moisture from the inside during the drying process. Ensure you do not overstuff, as this can stretch the shoes. Just enough to maintain their form is perfect.

This preparation stage makes the actual cleaning much safer and more efficient. It is a foundation for getting your Uggs truly clean. Without these steps, dirt can be pushed deeper into the material. Taking the time here ensures a better outcome. Your Uggs are now ready for the next cleaning stages.

### Spot Cleaning Uggs: Tackling Minor Stains and Dirt

Sometimes, your Ugg shoes only have a few small dirty spots. You do not need to clean the entire shoe for these. Spot cleaning helps you address minor stains quickly and effectively. This method uses less water and cleaner, making it gentle on your Uggs. It is a good first approach for everyday dirt.

For small, dry scuffs or light dirt, a suede eraser is your best friend. Gently rub the eraser over the scuff mark. The friction helps lift the dirt from the suede's surface. This is a dry method and often works well for recent marks. You can also use the suede brush to gently rub the spot. Brush in one direction to lift the nap and remove the dirt. This technique works for very superficial dirt.

If you have a slightly more stubborn stain, like a small food spill, use your specialized Ugg cleaner. Do not apply the cleaner directly onto the shoe. Instead, put a small amount of cleaner onto a clean, damp cloth. The cloth should be just damp, not soaking wet. Then, gently blot the stained area. Use light dabs, not harsh rubbing. Rubbing can spread the stain or damage the suede. Work from the outside of the stain inward. This helps to contain the dirt.

For water stains, the approach can be different. Water marks can be tricky on suede. If you have specific [how to clean water stains off Ugg boots](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-clean-water-stains-off-ugg-boots), you might need to dampen the entire area evenly to prevent a new water ring. Apply a light, even layer of cool water with a sponge to the affected section. Then, use your cleaner as described above. Always remember to use cold water for Uggs. Hot water can damage the sheepskin.

After blotting the stain, use a separate clean, damp cloth to blot away any cleaner residue. Make sure you remove all soap from the area. Then, let the spot air dry naturally. Do not use heat. Once dry, gently brush the area with your suede brush to restore the nap. Spot cleaning is ideal for maintaining your Uggs between deeper cleans. It helps to keep your shoes looking fresh with minimal effort.

### Deep Cleaning Your Ugg Shoes: A Step-by-Step Guide

When spot cleaning is not enough, your Ugg shoes need a full, deep clean. This method revives their overall look. It addresses general grime and set-in dirt. Remember to be gentle throughout this process. Aggressive cleaning can damage the delicate sheepskin.

First, lightly dampen the entire exterior surface of your Ugg shoes with cool water. Use a clean sponge or cloth. Do not saturate the shoes. The goal is to make the surface uniformly damp, not soaking wet. This prepares the suede for the cleaner. This also helps to prevent water marks during the cleaning process. Ensure the water is cold; hot water can shrink the sheepskin.

Next, apply a small amount of the specialized Ugg cleaning solution to a clean, soft sponge. Read the cleaner's instructions for exact dilution. Usually, you mix it with an equal part of cold water. Gently rub the sponge over the entire surface of the shoe. Use soft, circular motions. Work on one shoe at a time. Pay extra attention to dirty areas, but do not scrub hard. The solution creates a gentle lather. This helps lift dirt from the suede. It penetrates the fibers to clean deeply.

Once you have cleaned the entire shoe, it is time to rinse. Do not rinse the shoes under a running faucet. This can over-saturate them. Instead, use a fresh, clean sponge or cloth, dampened with cool water. Gently wipe away all the cleaning solution from the surface. You may need to rinse and wring out your sponge multiple times. It is important to remove all cleaner residue. Leftover soap can dry stiff or attract more dirt. Continue wiping until no suds appear.

After cleaning, gently reshape your Uggs. Make sure they are still stuffed with paper towels or newspaper from the preparation stage. This helps absorb internal moisture. It also ensures the shoes maintain their shape as they dry. Now your Uggs are clean and ready for proper drying. This deep cleaning process, combined with careful drying, will bring your Uggs back to life. This process also applies to [how to clean Ugg slippers](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-clean-ugg-slippers) which are made of similar materials.

### Proper Drying Techniques for Ugg Shoes

Drying your Ugg shoes correctly is a crucial step. Incorrect drying can shrink the sheepskin, stiffen the suede, or cause permanent damage. You must be patient and avoid rushing this process. Proper drying ensures your Uggs retain their shape and softness. It protects your cleaning efforts.

First, ensure your Uggs are still stuffed with paper towels or plain white newspaper. This is vital for two reasons. The paper absorbs excess moisture from the inside of the shoe. It also helps the Uggs keep their original shape. Change the paper after a few hours if it becomes very damp. Replacing the paper helps speed up drying and prevents mildew. Make sure the paper is not colored, as ink can transfer to the sheepskin.

Place your cleaned Uggs in a cool, well-ventilated area to air dry. Do not place them in direct sunlight. Strong sunlight can fade the suede color and dry out the sheepskin too quickly. This can lead to cracking or stiffness. Similarly, avoid placing them near direct heat sources. This includes radiators, fireplaces, or using a hairdryer. High heat will shrink the sheepskin and make it stiff and uncomfortable. The natural fibers need to dry slowly and gently.

The drying process can take 24 to 48 hours, sometimes longer. This depends on how wet the shoes became and the humidity in your home. It is important that the Uggs are completely dry before you wear them again. Wearing damp Uggs can encourage mold growth and stretch the material improperly. You can gently feel inside the shoes to check for any dampness. The suede should feel soft and dry to the touch.

Once your Uggs are fully dry, you may notice the suede feels a bit stiff. Use your suede brush to gently brush the exterior. This restores the nap of the suede. Brush in one direction to bring back its original texture and softness. This step is important for the final look and feel of your clean Uggs. This careful drying process helps ensure your [clean wool shoes](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-clean-wool-shoes) remain soft and comfortable.

### Protecting Your Uggs: Preventing Future Stains and Damage

After all your hard work cleaning your Ugg shoes, the last thing you want is for them to get dirty again right away. Protection is key to extending the time between deep cleans. A good protection routine helps your Uggs resist water and stains. This step saves you effort in the long run.

The most important protective measure is applying a water and stain repellent spray. Use a spray specifically designed for suede and sheepskin. Make sure your Uggs are completely dry before applying. Hold the spray can about six to eight inches away from the shoes. Apply an even, light coat over the entire surface. Do not over-saturate the shoes. A light, uniform mist is better. Let the first coat dry for about 10-15 minutes, then apply a second light coat. This creates a barrier against spills.

Allow the protective spray to dry completely before wearing your Uggs. This usually takes at least 24 hours. The spray works by creating an invisible shield on the suede. It makes water bead up and roll off, rather than soaking into the material. This helps prevent water spots and makes future stains easier to blot away. Reapply the protector every few months, especially before wet weather seasons.

Regular maintenance also plays a big role in keeping your Uggs clean. Use your suede brush regularly to remove loose dust and dirt. A quick brush after each wear can prevent dirt from setting in. Store your Uggs properly when you are not wearing them. Keep them in a cool, dry place. Avoid storing them in direct sunlight or damp areas. You can use shoe trees or stuff them with paper to help them keep their shape during storage.

Avoid wearing your Uggs in heavy rain or snow whenever possible. While the protector helps, it does not make them completely waterproof. If they do get wet, let them air dry naturally and then reapply the protective spray. For issues like internal odors, regular airing can help. For stubborn smells, you might need to learn [how to clean the inside of shoes](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-clean-the-inside-of-shoes) with baking soda. These simple habits keep your Uggs looking fresh and clean, making your cleaning efforts last.

### FAQ Section

**Q1: Can I machine wash Ugg shoes?**

No, you should not machine wash Ugg shoes. The harsh agitation and heat can damage the delicate sheepskin and suede. Machine washing can cause Uggs to shrink, stiffen, and lose their shape permanently. It is best to stick to hand-cleaning methods with specialized products. This will preserve the quality and extend the life of your Uggs. This applies to both shoes and [Ugg slippers](https://homeessentialsguide.com/can-ugg-slippers-be-machine-washed).

**Q2: How do I remove water stains from Uggs?**

To remove water stains, lightly dampen the entire affected area of the Ugg shoe with cool water. This helps prevent new water rings. Then, gently apply a specialized Ugg cleaner with a damp sponge. Blot the area, rinse with a clean damp cloth, and let the shoe air dry completely. Brush the suede once dry. For deeper marks, refer to our specific guide on [how to clean water stains off Ugg boots](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-clean-water-stains-off-ugg-boots).

**Q3: What if my Uggs smell bad inside?**

Bad smells inside Uggs come from moisture and bacteria. To fix this, sprinkle a generous amount of baking soda inside each shoe. Let it sit overnight or for 24 hours to absorb odors. Then, shake out the baking soda. You can also use cedar shoe inserts. Ensure your Uggs dry completely after each wear to prevent future smells.

**Q4: How often should I clean my Ugg shoes?**

The frequency of cleaning depends on how often you wear your Uggs and where. For regular wear, a light dry brushing after each use is good. Deep cleaning is usually needed every few months, or as soon as you notice visible dirt or stains. Apply protector spray every few months too.

**Q5: Can I use regular soap to clean Uggs?**

No, avoid using regular household soaps or detergents on Ugg shoes. These products often contain harsh chemicals or dyes that can strip the natural oils from the sheepskin. This can cause the suede to become dry, stiff, discolored, or damaged. Always use cleaning solutions specifically made for suede and sheepskin.

**Q6: How do I restore the suede texture after cleaning?**

After your Uggs are completely dry, the suede might feel a bit flat or stiff. Use a soft suede brush to gently restore the nap. Brush in one direction over the entire surface. This helps to lift the fibers and bring back the original soft, velvety texture. Do not brush too hard, just gentle strokes.
