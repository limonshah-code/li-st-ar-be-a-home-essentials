### What is Convection Cooking?

Convection cooking means hot air circulates throughout the oven cavity. A fan inside the Greystone convection microwave moves heated air around. This provides even cooking and browning. Food cooks faster and more uniformly. You can roast meats, bake cookies, or toast bread. The circulated air removes moisture from the surface of foods. This creates a crispy texture.

### Microwave vs. Convection: When to Use Which

Choosing the right mode depends on your food. Use the microwave for reheating leftovers or cooking vegetables quickly. It is ideal for speed and simple warming. Use convection for baking, roasting, or browning. This mode works best for items needing a crispy exterior or thorough cooking, like chicken or casseroles. Your Greystone convection microwave handles both.

### Basic Microwave Operations

Starting a microwave cycle is easy. Follow these steps for your Greystone unit:

1.  **Place Food:** Put your food in a microwave-safe dish. Place it on the turntable inside the oven.
2.  **Close Door:** Shut the door firmly.
3.  **Set Time:** Use the numerical keypad to enter the desired cooking time.
4.  **Adjust Power (Optional):** Press the "Power Level" button. Then use the numbers to select a percentage (e.g., 7 for 70% power).
5.  **Start:** Press the "Start" button. The microwave will begin cooking.

You can pause or stop the cycle anytime. Just press the "Stop" or "Cancel" button. This lets you stir food or check its doneness. Remember, cooking times vary. This depends on food quantity and density.

### Defrosting with Your Greystone Microwave

The defrost function saves time. Your Greystone convection microwave offers this specific setting. It thaws frozen foods safely. You can usually select defrost by weight or time.

*   **Defrost by Weight:**
    1.  Place frozen food on the turntable.
    2.  Press the "Defrost" or "Weight Defrost" button.
    3.  Enter the food's weight using the number pad.
    4.  Press "Start." The microwave calculates the time. It often pauses for you to turn the food.
*   **Defrost by Time:**
    1.  Place frozen food in the microwave.
    2.  Press the "Defrost" or "Time Defrost" button.
    3.  Enter the desired defrosting time.
    4.  Press "Start."

Always check food during defrosting. Separate pieces when possible. This ensures even thawing. Once defrosted, cook the food immediately. Do not refreeze thawed food.

### Setting Convection Temperature and Time

Setting up convection cooking on your Greystone appliance is simple:

1.  **Place Food:** Put your food on the metal rack supplied with the oven. Place the rack on the turntable or directly on the oven floor if specified in your manual.
2.  **Close Door:** Shut the door firmly.
3.  **Select Convection:** Press the "Convection" button.
4.  **Set Temperature:** Use the number pad or temperature up/down buttons to select your desired temperature. Common temperatures range from 250°F to 425°F (120°C to 220°C).
5.  **Preheat (Optional but Recommended):** Press "Start" to preheat. The oven will beep when it reaches the set temperature.
6.  **Set Cook Time:** After preheating (or if skipping preheat), enter the cooking time using the number pad.
7.  **Start:** Press "Start." The convection fan and heating element will activate.

Always refer to your Greystone manual for specific temperature ranges. Cooking times may be shorter than conventional oven recipes. Adjust as needed.

### Best Practices for Convection Cooking

To get the best results with your Greystone convection microwave:

*   **Preheat:** Always preheat the oven for at least 5-10 minutes. This ensures the correct cooking temperature from the start.
*   **Use Metal Racks:** Place food on the provided metal rack. This lifts food, allowing hot air to circulate all around it.
*   **Adjust Recipes:** Convection ovens often cook faster. Reduce your recipe's temperature by 25°F (15°C) or shorten the cooking time by 25%.
*   **Avoid Overcrowding:** Do not pack too much food into the oven. Leave space for air to circulate.
*   **Use Proper Cookware:** Choose bakeware made for oven use, like metal, ceramic, or glass. Do not use plastic.
*   **Check Doneness:** Use a food thermometer for meats. Visually check baked goods for browning.

Follow these tips for crispy, evenly cooked meals every time.

## Frequently Asked Questions About Greystone Convection Microwaves

Here are some common questions people ask about using a Greystone convection microwave.

### Can I use metal in my Greystone convection microwave?
Yes, you can use metal cookware and the metal rack provided when using the convection mode or combination convection mode. However, **never use metal in microwave-only mode**. Metal causes sparks in microwave mode. Always check the manual for specific cookware guidelines for your Greystone model.

### How do I preheat my Greystone convection microwave?
To preheat, select the convection mode, set your desired temperature, and then press "Start" or "Preheat." The oven will heat up to the set temperature. It usually beeps when it is ready. This is similar to preheating a regular oven for baking or roasting.

### What is the difference between combination cooking and convection on a Greystone?
Convection mode uses only hot circulating air for baking and roasting, like a traditional oven. Combination cooking uses both microwave energy and convection heat simultaneously. This offers faster cooking times with browning and crisping results, making it highly versatile for various dishes.

### How do I clean stubborn stains from my Greystone microwave interior?
For stubborn stains, place a microwave-safe bowl with a cup of water and a few lemon slices inside the oven. Heat on high for 5-10 minutes until steam fills the cavity. The steam loosens the grime. Then, carefully remove the hot bowl and wipe the interior clean with a damp cloth.

### Can I bake a cake in my Greystone convection microwave?
Yes, you can bake a cake in your Greystone convection microwave using the convection mode. Use an oven-safe baking pan made of metal, glass, or ceramic. Adjust the recipe's temperature and time as convection ovens often cook faster. You might reduce the temperature by 25°F.

### How do I turn off my Greystone convection microwave if it's stuck?
Most Greystone models have a "Cancel" or "Stop" button that ends any cooking cycle immediately. If the appliance is unresponsive, you can unplug it from the wall outlet for a few minutes. Plug it back in to reset the system. This often resolves temporary glitches.
