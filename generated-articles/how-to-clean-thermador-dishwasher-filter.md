## Locating and Removing Your Thermador Dishwasher Filter

Finding your Thermador dishwasher filter is simpler than you think. Most modern dishwashers have the filter in the same general spot. It is located at the very bottom of the dishwasher tub. Look for a cylindrical or flat mesh component. It sits under the lower spray arm. This is where all the food particles collect. Thermador models typically have a two-part filter system. There is a coarse filter and a fine filter.

To begin, pull out the lower dish rack. This gives you full access to the bottom of the tub. Look directly under the lower spray arm. You will see the filter assembly. It usually consists of a larger cylindrical piece. This piece has a handle or an arrow for turning. Below it, there is often a flat mesh screen. This screen collects smaller particles. Knowing [how to find your dishwasher filter](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-find-your-dishwasher-filter) is the first step. It is often visible right away.

The removal process is straightforward. First, grasp the cylindrical filter. It usually has a handle or markings that indicate which way to turn. Twist it counter-clockwise. You will feel it unlock from its base. Once unlocked, lift the cylindrical filter straight up and out. This part is easy to remove. Underneath the cylindrical filter, you will find the flat fine filter. This filter often lifts straight up or slides out. Some models may require a slight tug. Be gentle when pulling it out. You want to avoid damaging any parts. Successfully removing both parts sets you up for thorough cleaning. This process is key to getting the filter clean. ### Identifying Filter Types (Coarse vs. Fine)

Thermadore dishwashers use a two-part filter system. This system is very effective. It catches both large and small food bits. Understanding each part helps you clean them better.

The first part you remove is the coarse filter. This is the larger, cylindrical component. It has a mesh screen. This part catches bigger food particles. Think of things like rice, pasta, or small bones. It prevents these items from blocking the drain. The coarse filter is the first line of defense. It is usually easy to spot and remove.

The second part is the fine filter. This is typically a flat, mesh screen. It sits underneath the coarse filter. The fine filter traps tiny food particles. These are crumbs or fine sediment. These small bits can make dishes look cloudy. They can also cause odors if they build up. Both filters work together. They ensure your wash water stays clean. Clean water means clean dishes. Inspect both parts for buildup before cleaning. This helps you target the dirtiest areas.

## The Step-by-Step Cleaning Process

Now that you have removed the filter parts, it's time to clean them. This process is simple and requires minimal effort. I use warm water and mild dish soap. This combination is effective and gentle. Avoid harsh chemicals. They can damage the filter material. They can also leave residues inside your dishwasher.

First, take the cylindrical (coarse) filter. Hold it under warm, running water. Use a soft brush, like an old toothbrush, to gently scrub the mesh. Pay attention to any stuck-on food particles. Work the brush around all surfaces. Ensure water flows freely through the mesh. You can add a drop of mild dish soap to the brush. This helps cut through grease. Rinse it thoroughly until all soap is gone.

Next, clean the flat (fine) filter. This part often has very small holes. Food debris can get trapped easily. Rinse it under warm water. Again, use your soft brush to scrub the mesh gently. Make sure to get into all the tiny holes. If food is stubborn, let the filter soak for a few minutes. A small bowl of warm soapy water works well. This softens dried-on bits. After soaking, scrub again. Rinse both filter parts completely. No soap residue should remain. A clean filter shines. Knowing [how to clean filter in dishwasher](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-clean-filter-in-dishwasher) is a basic skill. It keeps your appliance performing well.

Once both parts are clean, inspect them. Hold them up to the light. Look for any remaining debris. If you see any, repeat the scrubbing and rinsing. A truly clean filter will allow light to pass through easily. This step is crucial for sparkling dishes. A thorough cleaning ensures your Thermador dishwasher works its best. ## Reinstalling Your Thermador Dishwasher Filter

After cleaning, putting the filter back is just as important as taking it out. Proper reinstallation ensures your dishwasher works correctly. It prevents leaks or performance issues. This step is usually quick and easy. I always double-check the placement.

Start with the flat, fine filter. Place it back into its slot at the bottom of the dishwasher tub. It should sit flat and flush with the bottom surface. Some models might have small grooves or indicators. These show the correct orientation. Make sure it is seated firmly. Do not force it. It should slide or drop into place easily.

Next, take the cylindrical (coarse) filter. Align its base with the opening in the fine filter and the bottom of the tub. Many Thermador filters have arrows or markings. These guide you on alignment. Twist the cylindrical filter clockwise. You will feel it lock into place. It should be snug. If it feels loose, it is not properly seated. A loose filter can cause water to bypass the filter. This results in dirty dishes. It can also lead to drain issues. Once the filter is secure, gently push down on it. It should not wobble or lift easily. Put the lower dish rack back into position. Before running a full cycle, I sometimes run a quick rinse cycle. This ensures everything is working as expected. Look for any unusual noises or leaks. If all looks good, your Thermador dishwasher is ready for its next load. It will now deliver truly sparkling clean dishes. You have successfully cleaned and reinstalled your filter.

Households with large families or those who do not scrape plates well may benefit from bi-weekly cleaning. On the other hand, if you only use your dishwasher a few times a week and pre-rinse dishes, you might get away with cleaning it every two to three months. It is always best to check. ## FAQ Section

### Q1: What happens if I do not clean my Thermador dishwasher filter?
If you do not clean your Thermador dishwasher filter, food particles and grease build up. This buildup causes several problems. Dishes come out dirty, gritty, or with a film. Your dishwasher may develop bad odors. Water may not drain properly. The appliance works harder, potentially shortening its lifespan and increasing wear.

### Q2: Can I put my Thermador dishwasher filter in the dishwasher?
No, you should not put your Thermador dishwasher filter in the dishwasher. The high heat and harsh detergents in a dishwasher can damage the filter material. Hand washing with warm water and mild dish soap is the safest and most effective method. This approach ensures the filter's integrity and prevents damage.

### Q3: How do I know if my Thermador dishwasher has a filter?
Most modern Thermador dishwashers have a manual filter system. You can check by pulling out the lower dish rack. Look at the very bottom of the dishwasher tub, usually under the lower spray arm. You will typically see a cylindrical component or a flat mesh screen. This is your dishwasher filter.

### Q4: What tools do I need to clean the filter?
You only need a few simple tools to clean your Thermador dishwasher filter. You will need warm running water, mild dish soap, and a soft brush. An old toothbrush works perfectly for scrubbing away trapped food particles and grime from the mesh. A towel for spills is also helpful.

### Q5: How often should I check my Thermador dishwasher filter?
You should check your Thermador dishwasher filter at least once a month. If you use your dishwasher very frequently, or if your dishes often have a lot of food debris, you might need to check it more often, perhaps every two weeks. Regular checks prevent excessive buildup and maintain performance.

### Q6: Can I use harsh chemicals to clean my Thermador filter?
No, you should avoid using harsh chemicals or abrasive cleaners on your Thermador dishwasher filter. These can damage the plastic or mesh components of the filter. They can also leave residues that might affect your dishes. Stick to warm water, mild dish soap, and a soft brush for safe and effective cleaning.
