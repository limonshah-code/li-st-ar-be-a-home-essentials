### The Secret to a Non-Stick Blackstone: The Art of Seasoning

This coating fills the microscopic pores in the steel. It also protects the metal from rust. This seasoned layer is a natural barrier between your food and the griddle. ### Common Issues Affecting Blackstone Non-Stick Performance and Solutions

Even with the best intentions, you might face issues with your Blackstone's non-stick performance. Food sticking is the most common complaint. This usually points to problems with your seasoning layer or cooking practices. Do not worry; most problems have simple solutions.

One reason food sticks is insufficient seasoning. Perhaps the initial seasoning was not thorough enough. Or maybe too much of the seasoned layer has worn off. The solution is to re-season the griddle. You will apply more layers of oil and heat, just like the first time. Another common reason for sticking is using too little oil during cooking. Or, sometimes, you might be cooking at too low a temperature, which can also cause food to adhere.

Flaking seasoning is another issue. This occurs when the seasoned layer becomes brittle and peels off. This often happens if too much oil was applied during seasoning, creating a thick, uneven layer. It can also result from using improper cleaning methods. If your seasoning is flaking, you need to strip it off. Then you must re-season the griddle from scratch. ### FAQ Section

**Q: Can I use soap on my Blackstone griddle?**
A: You should avoid using soap on your Blackstone griddle. Soap can strip away the seasoned, non-stick layer. This will make food stick. Instead, clean your griddle using a scraper and hot water. Dry it completely. Then, apply a thin coat of cooking oil to protect the surface.

**Q: How often should I season my Blackstone?**
A: After the initial seasoning, you do not need to perform a full re-seasoning often. You should apply a thin layer of oil after almost every use. This maintains the non-stick surface. If food starts sticking or you see rust, a partial or full re-seasoning may be needed.

**Q: What oil is best for seasoning a Blackstone?**
A: High smoke point oils work best for seasoning. Examples include flaxseed oil, grapeseed oil, canola oil, and vegetable oil. These oils polymerize well. They create a strong, durable non-stick layer. Avoid olive oil as it has a lower smoke point and can become sticky.

**Q: Why is food sticking to my seasoned Blackstone?**
A: Food often sticks due to insufficient seasoning, using too little cooking oil, or improper heat management. Ensure your griddle is fully preheated. Always use a small amount of oil when cooking. If the seasoning is thin, consider adding a few more layers.

**Q: How do I remove rust from my Blackstone griddle?**
A: To remove rust, use a metal scraper or a griddle pumice stone. Scrub the rusted areas thoroughly until the rust is gone. Then, clean the griddle surface. Once dry, perform a full re-seasoning to protect the exposed metal and restore the non-stick layer.

**Q: Is a Blackstone griddle safe for high heat?**
A: Yes, Blackstone griddles are designed for high heat cooking. Their rolled steel construction handles high temperatures well. This makes them ideal for searing. The seasoned surface is also durable at high heat, unlike some traditional non-stick coatings that can degrade.
