## Extending and Maintaining Your KitchenAid Microwave

A warranty is important, but proper care is even more crucial. You want your KitchenAid microwave to last many years. Regular maintenance helps achieve this. It also reduces the need for warranty claims. A well-maintained appliance performs better.

First, keep your microwave clean. Food splatters can build up. This grime can cause odors. It can also affect heating efficiency. Use a damp cloth to wipe down the interior and exterior regularly. For tough stains, use a mild cleaner. Avoid harsh chemicals. They can damage the surface.

Second, use proper microwave-safe dishes. Metal containers should never go in the microwave. This can cause arcing and damage. Always check dish labels. Ensure they are safe for microwave use. Overloading the microwave can also stress its components. Do not place items that are too heavy or large.

### Tips for Longevity

*   **Clean Regularly:** Wipe inside and outside after use.
*   **Use Proper Cookware:** Only microwave-safe dishes.
*   **Avoid Overloading:** Do not place excessively heavy items inside.
*   **Ensure Proper Ventilation:** Keep vents clear for airflow.
*   **Handle Door Gently:** Avoid slamming the door.
*   **Unplug When Not in Use (Optional):** Can protect against power surges.
*   **Consider a Surge Protector:** Protects electronics from voltage spikes.

Proper ventilation is key for all appliances. Make sure the microwave's vents are not blocked. This allows heat to escape. Blocked vents can cause the microwave to overheat. Overheating shortens its lifespan. Ensure enough space around the microwave for air circulation. This is important for freestanding models. For built-in models, ensure the cabinet design provides proper airflow.

Handle the microwave door gently. Slamming it can damage the latch mechanism. A faulty latch means the microwave will not start. This can lead to costly repairs. A little care goes a long way.

Consider an extended service plan or protection plan. While not a warranty, these plans offer coverage beyond the manufacturer's warranty. They can cover breakdowns after the standard warranty expires. This can provide long-term peace of mind. Many retailers offer them at the time of purchase.
