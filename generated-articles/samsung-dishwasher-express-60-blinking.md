### Addressing Water Supply and Drainage Issues

Water flow is vital for any dishwasher cycle. If your Samsung Dishwasher Express 60 is blinking, check the water supply first. Ensure the water valve connected to the dishwasher is fully open. This valve is usually under the sink. A partially closed valve restricts water flow. This causes the dishwasher to pause and blink.

Next, inspect the water inlet hose. This hose connects the dishwasher to the water supply. Look for any kinks or bends in the hose. A kinked hose blocks water flow. Straighten any bends you find. Make sure the hose is not crimped behind the dishwasher. This simple check can save you a lot of hassle.

Drainage problems are equally disruptive. The dishwasher needs to remove dirty water efficiently. If it cannot, cycles stop, and lights blink. Start by checking the dishwasher filter. This filter collects food particles. A clogged filter restricts drainage. Remove the filter and clean it thoroughly under running water.

Also, examine the drain hose. This hose carries water from the dishwasher to the sink drain or garbage disposal. Look for kinks, clogs, or blockages. The hose should slope downwards for proper drainage. If it connects to a garbage disposal, ensure the knock-out plug is removed. If the drain hose connects to an air gap, check that for clogs as well. I once had a small piece of food stuck in my air gap, causing endless drainage issues. Resolving these water flow issues can often fix the blinking problem directly. ### FAQ Section

#### Why does my Samsung dishwasher keep blinking Express 60?
Your Samsung dishwasher often blinks Express 60 due to a cycle interruption. This usually means a power glitch, an improperly latched door, or issues with water supply or drainage. The dishwasher stops to signal a problem. You should perform a hard reset and check the door first.

#### How do I reset a Samsung dishwasher that is blinking?
To reset your blinking Samsung dishwasher, unplug it from the power outlet. Wait for about 1-2 minutes. Then, plug it back in. This power cycle often clears temporary electronic glitches. It allows the dishwasher to restart its system.

#### What does it mean if my Samsung dishwasher has an LC error code?
An LC error code on your Samsung dishwasher indicates a leak detected. This usually means water is pooling in the bottom tray. It could be due to a loose hose, a damaged seal, or a faulty sensor. You should turn off the water supply and call for service.

#### Can a clogged drain cause the Express 60 light to blink?
Yes, a clogged drain can definitely cause the Express 60 light to blink. If water cannot drain properly, the dishwasher detects an issue. It then halts the cycle and signals a problem. Check and clean your dishwasher filter and drain hose for blockages.

#### Is the Express 60 cycle blinking a serious problem?
The Express 60 cycle blinking is not always a serious problem. Often, it indicates a minor issue that is easy to fix. Simple troubleshooting steps like resetting the dishwasher or checking the door can resolve it. However, persistent blinking might point to a more complex component failure.

#### Should I call a technician immediately for a blinking Express 60 light?
You do not need to call a technician immediately. Try basic troubleshooting steps first. These include resetting the power, checking the door latch, and inspecting water supply and drain lines. If these steps do not resolve the issue, then contacting a professional technician is the next step.
