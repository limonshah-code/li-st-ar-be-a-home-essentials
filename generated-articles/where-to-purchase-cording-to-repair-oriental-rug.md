### Understanding Oriental Rug Cording: Types and Materials

Cording is important for an oriental rug. It helps protect the edges from unraveling. This part of the rug is called the selvedge or side cord. It keeps the rug's structure sound. Knowing the different types of cording helps you choose the right one for your repair.

Most oriental rugs use cording made from natural fibers. The material choice depends on the rug's original make. Wool, cotton, and silk are the most common materials. Each material has its own feel and look. Choosing the correct material ensures a natural repair.

Wool cording is very popular for hand-knotted rugs. These rugs often use wool pile and foundation. Wool is strong and durable. It blends well with the rug's existing fibers. This makes the repair less noticeable. It also lasts a long time.

Cotton cording is common for rugs with cotton foundations. Some finer rugs use cotton for their side cords. Cotton offers a smooth finish. It is also strong and can take dye well. This makes it good for color matching.

Silk cording is for very fine, expensive rugs. These rugs often have silk piles. Using silk cording keeps the luxurious feel. Silk is delicate and shines. It requires special care. Matching silk cording needs precision.

The thickness of the cording also matters. It must match the original cording. A too-thin or too-thick cord will look odd. It can also affect the rug's stability. Measure your existing cord if possible. This helps you get the right size.

#### Wool Cording for Durability

Wool cording is excellent for most hand-knotted oriental rugs. Many rugs have wool piles. This makes wool cording a natural fit. Wool fibers are tough. They resist wear. This durability helps your rug last longer after repair.

Finding matching wool cording is often easier than other materials. Wool also accepts dyes very well. This is good if you need to custom-color the cording. A well-chosen wool cord blends seamlessly. It helps restore the rug's original beauty.

#### Cotton Cording for Finer Weaves

Cotton cording works well for rugs with cotton foundations. Some rugs use cotton for their selvedge. Cotton is smooth and clean. It provides a crisp edge to the rug. This material is strong but less bulky than wool.

Cotton cording is good for rugs with a tight, fine weave. It does not add much thickness. This keeps the rug's profile consistent. Cotton can also be dyed to match many colors. It is a versatile choice for specific rug types.

#### Silk Cording for Luxury Rugs

Silk cording is for high-end oriental rugs. These rugs often have silk in their pile. Silk cording has a beautiful sheen. It feels soft and luxurious. It ensures the repair keeps the rug's original quality.

Silk cording can be harder to find. It is also more expensive. Matching its luster and texture needs care. Using silk maintains the rug's value. It also preserves its delicate appearance.

<h3>Matching Cording to Your Oriental Rug: Important Considerations</h3>

A clean rug is crucial for any repair. Dirt and dust can weaken fibers. They can make new stitches less secure. Start with gentle vacuuming. Flip the rug over and vacuum the back too. This dislodges deeply embedded dirt.

If there are stains, spot clean them carefully. Use only rug-safe cleaners. Test any cleaner on a hidden part of the rug first. If the rug is very dirty, consider a professional cleaning. A clean foundation ensures the new cording holds firm.

### FAQ Section

**Q1: Can I use regular craft cording for my oriental rug?**
Regular craft cording is often made from synthetic fibers or cotton blends. These may not match the natural fibers, like wool or silk, of an oriental rug. Using unsuitable cording can look out of place and wear differently. It is best to use cording specifically for rug repair. This ensures a proper match and a durable repair.

**Q2: How do I match the color of the cording?**
Matching color requires great care. Take a small piece of your rug, or the rug itself, to compare directly with cording samples in natural light. Colors can appear different under artificial lights or on computer screens. Some specialized suppliers offer custom dyeing services, which helps achieve a perfect match.

**Q3: What material cording should I choose for a wool oriental rug?**
For a wool oriental rug, you should choose wool cording. This ensures consistency in material, texture, and durability. Using wool cording helps the repair blend seamlessly with the original rug fibers. It provides a long-lasting and aesthetically pleasing result. Always match the primary fiber of your rug.

**Q4: Is it better to buy cording online or in person?**
Buying in person allows you to see and feel the cording, which helps with color and texture matching. However, online stores often have a wider selection. If buying online, ask for samples or detailed photos. Always check customer reviews. A combination of both might be best, starting with online research, then in-person checks if possible.

**Q5: How much cording do I need for a typical repair?**
Measure the length of the damaged section of your rug's edge. Add an extra 10-20% to this measurement. This provides a buffer for any mistakes or if you need to redo a section. It is always better to have a little extra cording than to run short during the repair process.

**Q6: Can I dye cording to match my rug?**
Yes, you can dye cording to match your rug's color. This is a common practice, especially if you cannot find an exact color match. Use appropriate dyes for the cording material (e.g., wool dyes for wool cording). Test the dye on a small piece first to ensure the color is correct.
