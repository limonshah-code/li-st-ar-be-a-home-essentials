## How To Disinfect Your Levoit Humidifier for a Healthier Home

Do you use a Levoit humidifier to improve your home's air quality? Many people rely on humidifiers for comfort, especially during dry seasons. However, these devices can become breeding grounds for mold and bacteria if not properly maintained. Knowing how to disinfect your Levoit humidifier is crucial for your health and the longevity of your appliance.

A dirty humidifier can release harmful microorganisms into your air. This can trigger allergies, asthma, and other respiratory issues. Regular disinfection ensures your humidifier delivers clean, beneficial mist. This article explains simple, effective ways to disinfect your Levoit humidifier. We will cover methods using common household items like vinegar and bleach. You will learn the best practices for thorough cleaning and ongoing maintenance.

### Takeaway

To ensure your Levoit humidifier provides clean, healthy mist:

*   **Disassemble fully:** Take apart all removable components before cleaning.
*   **Clean daily/weekly:** Rinse the water tank daily and perform a deeper clean weekly.
*   **Use vinegar:** White vinegar is excellent for removing mineral buildup and mild disinfection.
*   **Consider bleach (carefully):** A diluted bleach solution can tackle stubborn mold or bacteria.
*   **Rinse thoroughly:** Always rinse all parts many times after using any cleaning agent.
*   **Air dry completely:** Let all parts dry fully before reassembly to prevent immediate mold growth.

### Clear, Concise Answer

To disinfect your Levoit humidifier, first disassemble and clean all parts with soap and water. Then, soak components in a diluted white vinegar solution for 30 minutes to an hour to remove mineral buildup and kill germs. For tougher disinfection, use a diluted bleach solution (one teaspoon bleach per gallon of water), rinse thoroughly, and air dry completely.

### Why Disinfecting Your Levoit Humidifier Matters for Your Health

Humidifiers add moisture to the air. This helps relieve dry skin, chapped lips, and sinus congestion. However, a humidifier also works with water. Stagnant water can quickly become a perfect environment for microorganisms. These include bacteria, mold, and even algae. When these contaminants grow inside your Levoit humidifier, they can become airborne. The device then sprays these particles into your living space.

Breathing in mold spores or bacteria from a dirty humidifier can cause serious health problems. You might experience respiratory infections, allergy flare-ups, or asthma attacks. Symptoms often include coughing, wheezing, eye irritation, and even flu-like illness. Children, the elderly, and people with compromised immune systems are especially vulnerable. I always make sure to keep my humidifier clean because my family's health is a priority. Keeping your Levoit humidifier free from these invisible threats is essential for maintaining a healthy home environment.

Regular disinfection also extends the life of your appliance. Mineral buildup from tap water can clog components over time. This reduces efficiency and can cause your humidifier to stop working properly. Mold and mildew can degrade plastic parts and filters. Disinfecting helps prevent this damage. It keeps your Levoit humidifier running smoothly and effectively. This saves you money on repairs or replacements in the long run. A clean humidifier works better and lasts longer. It also ensures you get the full health benefits you expect from your device. ### Essential Supplies for Disinfecting Your Levoit Humidifier

Before you begin the disinfection process, gathering the right tools and cleaning agents makes the job easier and safer. You likely have most of these items already in your home. Having everything ready before you start helps streamline the process. This prevents you from stopping mid-clean to search for a missing item.

Here is a list of what you will need:

*   **White Vinegar:** This is your primary non-toxic disinfectant. It is highly effective at dissolving mineral buildup and killing common bacteria and mold. It leaves no harsh chemical residues. Many people prefer vinegar due to its natural cleaning properties.
*   **Unscented Bleach (optional, for tough cases):** Household bleach is a powerful disinfectant for severe mold or bacterial growth. Always use it with caution and in a highly diluted solution. Never mix bleach with vinegar or other cleaners, as this creates dangerous fumes.
*   **Soft Brushes:** A soft-bristled brush, an old toothbrush, or even a bottle brush is useful. These help scrub away grime and reach tight corners inside the tank and base. You want something gentle enough not to scratch the plastic.
*   **Microfiber Cloths or Sponges:** These are great for wiping down surfaces and ensuring a streak-free finish. They are absorbent and will not leave lint.
*   **Clean Towels:** You will need these for drying parts after rinsing. Ensuring everything is completely dry is key to preventing immediate mold regrowth.
*   **Measuring Cups and Spoons:** Essential for accurately diluting bleach solutions. This ensures safety and effectiveness.
*   **Rubber Gloves:** Protect your hands, especially when working with bleach or even prolonged contact with vinegar.
*   **Eye Protection:** Safety glasses are a good idea, particularly if using bleach, to prevent splashes from reaching your eyes.
*   **Access to a Sink or Tub:** You will need a place to rinse all the components thoroughly.

Having these supplies ready ensures a smooth and effective disinfection process. Your safety should always be the top priority.

### Step-by-Step Guide to Disinfecting Your Levoit Humidifier with Vinegar

Vinegar is my go-to choice for disinfecting my Levoit humidifier. It's effective, natural, and safe for your household. This method not only disinfects but also tackles stubborn mineral buildup from hard water. Many Levoit users find this method sufficient for regular deep cleaning.

#### Pre-Disinfection Cleaning Steps

Before you even think about disinfecting, you must clean. This initial cleaning removes any loose dirt, dust, or slime. It makes the disinfection process much more effective. Neglecting this step means you are just trying to disinfect *over* existing gunk.

1.  **Unplug the Humidifier:** Always disconnect the power cord first. Safety is paramount when working with electrical appliances and water.
2.  **Empty the Water Tank and Base:** Pour out any remaining water from both the tank and the base reservoir. You do not want any old water present during cleaning.
3.  **Disassemble the Humidifier:** Take apart all removable components. This usually includes the water tank, lid, mist nozzle, base, and sometimes the filter tray. Refer to your Levoit manual for specific model disassembly instructions. If you need help, our guide on [how to take apart your Levoit humidifier](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-take-apart-levoit-humidifier) can assist you.
4.  **Rinse All Parts:** Rinse the water tank, base, and all other removable parts under cool running water. This washes away loose mineral deposits and any slimy residue.

#### The Vinegar Soaking and Scrubbing Process

Now that your humidifier parts are clean, it's time to disinfect them. Vinegar works by creating an acidic environment. This breaks down minerals and kills most bacteria and mold.

1.  **Prepare the Vinegar Solution:** Fill the water tank halfway with undiluted white vinegar. For the base, pour enough undiluted white vinegar to cover the heating plate or ultrasonic nebulizer. You can also mix equal parts white vinegar and water for less severe buildup, but I prefer full strength for a deep clean.
2.  **Soak the Parts:** Let the vinegar sit in the tank and base for at least 30 minutes. For heavy mineral scale or visible mold, let it soak for an hour or even longer. For smaller components like the mist nozzle, place them in a bowl and cover them with the vinegar solution. The soaking loosens mineral deposits and allows the vinegar to penetrate and kill germs. This is the heart of the disinfection process.
3.  **Scrub Thoroughly:** After soaking, use a soft brush or an old toothbrush to gently scrub the inside surfaces of the tank and base. Pay close attention to corners, crevices, and the nebulizer/heating element. You will see the mineral deposits begin to flake off. Do not use abrasive scrubbers, as they can scratch the plastic. For detailed steps on using vinegar, check out our article on [how to clean Levoit humidifier with vinegar](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-clean-levoit-humidifier-with-vinegar).
4.  **Focus on the Nebulizer/Heating Element:** This part is critical. Mineral buildup here can greatly impact mist output. Gently scrub around it with a brush or a cotton swab. Make sure to remove all visible residue.

#### Rinsing and Reassembly

Proper rinsing is just as important as the cleaning itself. You do not want any vinegar smell or residue left behind. Any lingering vinegar can make your mist smell unpleasant.

1.  **Rinse All Parts Extensively:** After scrubbing, empty the vinegar solution. Rinse every single component thoroughly under cool, running water. I usually rinse each part multiple times. I fill the tank with clean water, shake it, and empty it, repeating this process until I cannot smell any vinegar.
2.  **Wipe Down and Air Dry:** Use a clean, dry microfiber cloth to wipe down all surfaces. Then, place all components on a clean towel or drying rack. Allow them to air dry completely. This is a crucial step to prevent mold growth. Moisture left behind can immediately create a breeding ground for new mold. I often let my parts dry for several hours, sometimes even overnight, just to be sure.
3.  **Reassemble the Humidifier:** Once all parts are completely dry, put your Levoit humidifier back together. Ensure everything clicks into place properly. If you need a refresher on reassembly, our guide on [how to put Levoit humidifier back together](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-put-levoit-humidifier-back-together) can help.
4.  **Fill and Operate:** Your Levoit humidifier is now disinfected and ready for use. Fill it with fresh, clean water. Using distilled water can significantly reduce future mineral buildup.

By following these steps, you can ensure your Levoit humidifier stays clean, disinfected, and provides healthy mist.

### Disinfecting Your Levoit Humidifier with Bleach: When and How

While vinegar is excellent for regular cleaning and mild disinfection, sometimes you need a stronger solution. If you find persistent mold growth, a strange odor that won't go away, or suspect bacterial contamination, a bleach solution might be necessary. Bleach is a powerful disinfectant, but it requires careful handling. Always use it as a last resort and follow safety guidelines strictly.

#### Bleach Solution Preparation

Safety is the absolute priority when working with bleach. Poor ventilation or incorrect dilution can lead to dangerous fumes. Ensure you are in a well-ventilated area, like a bathroom with a fan running or near an open window.

1.  **Pre-Clean First:** Just as with the vinegar method, begin by unplugging, emptying, and disassembling your humidifier. Rinse all parts to remove loose debris. Bleach works best on already clean surfaces.
2.  **Prepare the Diluted Bleach Solution:** Never use undiluted bleach. Mix **one teaspoon of household bleach with one gallon of water**. This dilution is sufficient for effective disinfection without being overly harsh. I always measure carefully to ensure I get the correct ratio.
3.  **Wear Protective Gear:** Put on rubber gloves and eye protection. Bleach can irritate skin and eyes.

#### Applying the Bleach Disinfectant

This process is similar to the vinegar method, focusing on ensuring the bleach solution contacts all affected surfaces. Do not rush this step.

1.  **Fill the Tank and Base:** Pour the diluted bleach solution into the water tank. Fill it about halfway. Pour enough solution into the base to cover the entire water reservoir and the ultrasonic nebulizer.
2.  **Soak Smaller Parts:** Place smaller components like the mist nozzle or any other removable parts into a bowl filled with the diluted bleach solution. Ensure they are fully submerged.
3.  **Soaking Time:** Allow the parts to soak for **30 minutes**. Do not exceed this time. Prolonged contact with bleach can degrade plastic over time. This short soak is enough to kill most bacteria, viruses, and mold spores.
4.  **Gentle Scrub (if needed):** After soaking, use a soft brush or sponge to gently scrub any remaining stains or visible mold. The bleach should have loosened most of it. Remember to be gentle.
5.  **Empty and Discard:** Carefully pour out the bleach solution. Dispose of it safely down a drain with plenty of running water.

#### Thorough Rinsing and Air Drying

This is the most critical step after using bleach. Any lingering bleach residue can be harmful when inhaled as mist. I cannot stress enough the importance of rinsing until there is no smell.

1.  **Rinse Repeatedly:** Rinse every single component, inside and out, under running water. Fill the tank with clean water, shake vigorously, and empty. Repeat this several times. Do this for the base and all small parts too. My goal is to rinse until I can no longer detect any bleach smell whatsoever. This might take many rinses.
2.  **Air Dry Completely:** Place all rinsed components on a clean towel or drying rack. Allow them to air dry completely. This can take several hours. Ensure every crevice and corner is dry. This step prevents any immediate mold regrowth from trapped moisture.
3.  **Reassemble:** Once everything is bone dry, carefully reassemble your Levoit humidifier. For guidance on putting it back together, see our instructions on [how to put Levoit humidifier back together](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-put-levoit-humidifier-back-together).
4.  **First Use After Bleach:** When you first use the humidifier after a bleach disinfection, fill it with fresh, clean water. Let it run on a high setting for an hour or two in a well-ventilated room. This helps purge any remaining fumes before you begin normal use in a bedroom or living area.

Using bleach should be reserved for situations where vinegar is not enough. When used correctly, it is a very effective disinfectant. Remember, safety first! You should also avoid putting humidifier parts into a dishwasher, as high heat and harsh detergents can damage them. This applies even if you are considering a bleach cycle, as specific materials in your Levoit humidifier are not designed for machine washing. For more information, you can check articles like [can you put Levoit humidifier in dishwasher](https://homeessentialsguide.com/can-you-put-levoit-humidifier-in-dishwasher).

### Beyond Disinfection: Maintaining a Clean Levoit Humidifier

Disinfecting your Levoit humidifier is a crucial part of its maintenance, but it is not a one-time event. Consistent daily and weekly habits will prevent the rapid regrowth of mold and bacteria. This reduces the need for frequent, intensive disinfection. Proper maintenance also ensures your humidifier operates at peak efficiency. It delivers the best air quality possible.

Here are key practices for ongoing cleanliness:

*   **Daily Maintenance:**
    *   **Empty and Rinse Daily:** I make it a habit to empty any leftover water from the tank and base every single day. Standing water is the primary cause of mold and bacterial growth. After emptying, I give both the tank and the base a quick rinse with fresh water. This simple step prevents slime from forming.
    *   **Refill with Fresh Water:** Always refill your Levoit humidifier with fresh, clean water. Do not just top off old water.
*   **Weekly Deep Cleaning:**
    *   **Disassemble and Clean:** Once a week, commit to a more thorough clean. Disassemble your humidifier according to the manufacturer's instructions. Wash all removable parts with warm soapy water and a soft brush. Pay attention to crevices.
    *   **Vinegar Soak:** Even if you do not see visible mold, a weekly vinegar soak (as described in the previous section) is a great preventative measure. This dissolves mineral buildup before it becomes a problem.
    *   **Rinse and Dry Thoroughly:** After cleaning, rinse all parts extensively. Then, allow them to air dry completely before reassembly. This prevents new mold growth.
*   **Use Distilled or Demineralized Water:**
    *   **Reduce Mineral Buildup:** This is perhaps the most significant step you can take to maintain your humidifier. Tap water contains minerals that create white dust and hard water scale. Distilled or demineralized water is free of these minerals. Using it drastically reduces the frequency and intensity of cleaning needed. It also prolongs the life of your unit.
*   **Change Filters Regularly (if applicable):**
    *   **Follow Manufacturer Guidelines:** Some Levoit humidifier models come with filters or demineralization cartridges. These trap minerals and impurities. Check your humidifier's manual for specific instructions on filter replacement frequency. A clogged filter can reduce efficiency and harbor contaminants. Knowing [how to replace your Levoit humidifier filter](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-replace-levoit-humidifier-filter) or [how to change filter in Levoit humidifier](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-change-filter-in-levoit-humidifier) is important.
*   **Position Your Humidifier Wisely:**
    *   **Air Circulation:** Place your humidifier on a hard, flat surface. Ensure there is good air circulation around it. Avoid placing it directly on carpet or near walls, which can restrict airflow and encourage moisture buildup.
    *   **Away from Electronics:** Keep it away from electronics and wooden furniture that can be damaged by excessive moisture.
*   **Store Properly:**
    *   **Clean Before Storage:** If you plan to store your humidifier for an extended period (e.g., during summer), perform a full disinfection. Ensure all parts are completely dry before storing them in a cool, dry place. This prevents mold from growing during storage.

By incorporating these maintenance habits into your routine, you will keep your Levoit humidifier running cleanly and efficiently. This provides you with healthy, comfortable air year-round. Consistent care saves time and effort in the long run.

### Troubleshooting Common Disinfection Challenges with Levoit Humidifiers

Even with regular cleaning and disinfection, you might encounter some common issues. Understanding how to troubleshoot these problems can save you frustration and ensure your Levoit humidifier remains effective. I have run into a few of these myself, and knowing how to handle them makes a big difference.

*   **Persistent Odor After Cleaning:**
    *   **Problem:** You have cleaned and disinfected, but a strange smell (like stale water, mold, or even the cleaning agent) lingers.
    *   **Solution:**
        *   **Rinse More Thoroughly:** The most common cause is insufficient rinsing. If you used vinegar or bleach, chemical residue might be present. Re-rinse all parts repeatedly with fresh water. Fill the tank, shake vigorously, empty, and repeat several times.
        *   **Air Dry Longer:** Incomplete drying can lead to immediate mold regrowth, causing a musty smell. Ensure all parts are bone dry before reassembly.
        *   **Check Filters:** If your model has a filter or demineralization cartridge, a dirty one can hold odors. Replace it if it's old.
        *   **Consider Filtered Water:** If you use tap water, minerals or impurities might contribute to the smell over time. Switch to distilled water.
*   **Stubborn Mineral Buildup (White Scale):**
    *   **Problem:** Despite cleaning, white, crusty deposits (limescale) remain, especially on the heating element or ultrasonic nebulizer.
    *   **Solution:**
        *   **Extended Vinegar Soak:** For really tough scale, soak the affected parts in undiluted white vinegar for several hours, or even overnight.
        *   **Gentle Scrubbing:** Use an old toothbrush or a small, stiff-bristled brush. You can also use a cotton swab for very tight spots. Gently scrub the scale. Avoid metal tools or abrasive pads that can scratch the surface.
        *   **Repeat Treatment:** If necessary, drain the old vinegar, add fresh vinegar, and repeat the soak and scrub process.
        *   **Prevention:** Use distilled or demineralized water consistently to prevent future buildup.
*   **Visible Mold Regrowth Soon After Disinfection:**
    *   **Problem:** You cleaned and disinfected, but mold reappears quickly.
    *   **Solution:**
        *   **More Frequent Cleaning:** You might need to clean and disinfect more often, perhaps twice a week, depending on your water quality and environmental humidity.
        *   **Ensure Complete Drying:** This is critical. Any residual moisture will invite mold back. Allow parts to air dry for longer periods.
        *   **Bleach Treatment:** If you used vinegar and mold returned, switch to a diluted bleach solution for disinfection (following all safety precautions).
        *   **Check the Environment:** Is your room extremely humid? Consider running a dehumidifier in that room in addition to your humidifier in another area.
        *   **Replace Parts:** If mold is embedded in plastic that cannot be cleaned, consider replacing that specific part.
*   **Humidifier Not Misting After Cleaning:**
    *   **Problem:** Your Levoit humidifier turns on, but no mist comes out after cleaning. This could also be a reason why [your Levoit humidifier is not working](https://homeessentialsguide.com/why-is-my-levoit-humidifier-not-working).
    *   **Solution:**
        *   **Check Assembly:** Ensure all parts are correctly assembled. A misaligned tank or mist nozzle can prevent mist production.
        *   **Nebulizer/Heating Element Obstruction:** Mineral buildup or debris might still be blocking the nebulizer or heating element. Re-clean this area thoroughly with vinegar and a soft brush.
        *   **Water Level Sensor:** Sometimes, the water level sensor might be covered in mineral scale or dislodged. Ensure it is clean and properly positioned. If your [Levoit humidifier is red](https://homeessentialsguide.com/why-is-my-levoit-humidifier-red), it often indicates a low water level or sensor issue.
        *   **Power Connection:** Double-check that the unit is fully plugged in.
        *   **Filter Check:** If your model has a filter, ensure it's not completely clogged or improperly inserted.
*   **Leakage After Cleaning:**
    *   **Problem:** Your humidifier starts leaking from the bottom or seams after you clean it.
    *   **Solution:**
        *   **Improper Assembly:** This is almost always due to parts not being seated correctly. Carefully reassemble the unit, ensuring all seals, gaskets, and the water tank are properly aligned and firmly in place. The leak could also be caused if [your Levoit humidifier is leaking from the bottom](https://homeessentialsguide.com/why-is-my-levoit-humidifier-leaking-from-the-bottom) or if [your Levoit humidifier is leaking](https://homeessentialsguide.com/why-is-my-levoit-humidifier-leaking) in general.
        *   **Damaged Seal:** Inspect the rubber seals and gaskets for cracks or tears. If damaged, they may need to be replaced.
        *   **Overfilling:** Ensure you are not overfilling the water tank beyond the marked max line.

By systematically addressing these common issues, you can keep your Levoit humidifier in top working condition and ensure a clean, healthy mist.

### Safety Tips for Disinfecting Your Levoit Humidifier

Disinfecting your Levoit humidifier is important for health, but it also involves handling water, electricity, and cleaning agents. Prioritizing safety during the process protects both you and your appliance. I always keep these tips in mind every time I clean my humidifier.

*   **Always Unplug First:** Before doing anything else, always disconnect your humidifier from the power outlet. This is the golden rule for any appliance cleaning involving water. Never clean a humidifier while it is plugged in. Water and electricity are a dangerous combination.
*   **Work in a Well-Ventilated Area:**
    *   **Vinegar:** While vinegar is natural, its strong smell can be irritating in confined spaces.
    *   **Bleach:** This is especially crucial when using bleach. Bleach fumes can be harmful to your respiratory system. Open windows, turn on exhaust fans, or work in a garage if possible. Good ventilation helps dissipate fumes quickly.
*   **Wear Protective Gear:**
    *   **Gloves:** Always wear rubber gloves when handling cleaning solutions, particularly bleach. Both vinegar and bleach can cause skin irritation with prolonged contact.
    *   **Eye Protection:** Safety glasses are highly recommended when using bleach to protect your eyes from accidental splashes.
*   **Never Mix Cleaning Agents:** This is an extremely important safety rule.
    *   **DO NOT Mix Bleach and Vinegar:** Mixing bleach with acidic cleaners like vinegar creates highly toxic chlorine gas. This gas can cause severe respiratory problems, eye irritation, and even be fatal in high concentrations.
    *   **Avoid Other Cleaners:** Do not mix bleach with ammonia-based cleaners (like some window cleaners) or rubbing alcohol. Stick to one cleaning agent at a time.
*   **Dilute Bleach Properly:** If you choose to use bleach, always dilute it according to the instructions (one teaspoon per gallon of water). Using undiluted bleach or too strong a solution can damage your humidifier's plastic components over time. It also increases the risk of harmful fumes.
*   **Rinse Thoroughly:** After using any cleaning solution, especially bleach, rinse all humidifier parts meticulously. Any lingering residue can be aerosolized when the humidifier runs, potentially causing respiratory irritation. Rinse until you can no longer detect the smell of the cleaning agent.
*   **Air Dry Completely:** Ensure all parts are completely dry before reassembling the humidifier and plugging it back in. Trapped moisture can lead to mold regrowth and, in rare cases, could pose an electrical hazard if it gets into sensitive components.
*   **Keep Cleaning Solutions Out of Reach of Children and Pets:** Store all cleaning agents in their original containers, clearly labeled, and in a secure place away from curious hands or paws.
*   **Follow Manufacturer's Instructions:** Always refer to your specific Levoit humidifier's user manual. It may have particular cleaning recommendations or warnings regarding certain cleaning agents or methods. Some materials might react differently.
*   **Do Not Submerge Electrical Components:** Never submerge the main electrical base unit or any parts with electrical wiring or plugs in water or cleaning solutions. Only clean parts designed to hold water.

By adhering to these safety tips, you can effectively disinfect your Levoit humidifier without putting yourself or your household at risk. Your health and safety are paramount.

### FAQ Section

**Q1: How often should I disinfect my Levoit humidifier?**
A1: You should rinse your Levoit humidifier's water tank daily and perform a deep clean with vinegar weekly. If you use tap water, have hard water, or live in a humid environment, consider disinfecting with vinegar twice a week. Use a bleach solution for disinfection only as needed for persistent mold or bacteria, typically once a month or less frequently.

**Q2: Can I use other cleaners like hydrogen peroxide for disinfection?**
A2: While hydrogen peroxide can be an effective disinfectant, Levoit specifically recommends only white vinegar or a diluted bleach solution for cleaning their humidifiers. Using other chemicals like hydrogen peroxide might void your warranty or damage the humidifier's plastic parts. Always stick to the manufacturer's recommendations or commonly accepted safe methods.

**Q3: What if I see mold in my humidifier that vinegar doesn't remove?**
A3: If vinegar is not strong enough to remove visible mold, it's time to use a diluted bleach solution. Prepare a solution of one teaspoon of bleach per gallon of water. Soak the affected parts for 30 minutes, then scrub gently. Rinse the parts very thoroughly multiple times to remove all bleach residue and smell, then air dry completely.

**Q4: Is it safe to use bleach on my Levoit humidifier's plastic?**
A4: Yes, a properly diluted bleach solution (one teaspoon per gallon of water) is generally safe for short contact (30 minutes) with your Levoit humidifier's plastic components. However, using undiluted bleach or soaking for too long can degrade the plastic over time. Always ensure excellent ventilation
