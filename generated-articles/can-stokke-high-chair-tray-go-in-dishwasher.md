## Common Problems and Troubleshooting When Cleaning Stokke Trays

Even with regular cleaning, Stokke high chair trays can develop stubborn issues. Food stains, odors, and stickiness are common challenges. Knowing how to troubleshoot these problems helps maintain the tray's condition. It keeps it hygienic for your child. I have encountered all these issues with my kids' high chairs.

**Tough Food Stains:**
Deeply colored foods like tomato sauce, berries, or carrots can leave stains. For these, a gentle approach is best. Create a paste with baking soda and a small amount of water. Apply this paste directly to the stain. Let it sit for 15-30 minutes. Then, gently scrub with a soft brush or cloth. Rinse thoroughly. Baking soda is a natural, mild abrasive. It lifts stains without damaging the plastic. For more severe stains, a diluted solution of white vinegar can work. Mix equal parts white vinegar and water. Apply it, let it sit, then scrub and rinse. Always patch test in an inconspicuous area first.

**Persistent Odors:**
Leftover food can cause odors. If handwashing does not remove smells, try a vinegar soak. Fill your sink with warm water. Add half a cup of white vinegar. Submerge the tray and let it soak for an hour. Vinegar neutralizes odors. Afterward, wash the tray with soap and water as usual. Ensure complete rinsing. Sunlight is another natural odor neutralizer. Place the clean, wet tray in direct sunlight for a few hours. The UV rays can help eliminate lingering smells.

**Sticky Residue:**
Sometimes a sticky film remains even after washing. This often happens with sugary foods or certain baby purees. Try using a small amount of rubbing alcohol on a cotton ball. Gently wipe the sticky areas. Alcohol dissolves many sticky residues. Follow up with a soap and water wash. This removes any alcohol residue. For a natural alternative, coconut oil can also dissolve sticky substances. Apply a tiny bit, rub, then wash.

**Preventing Mold and Mildew:**
Moisture can lead to mold, especially in crevices. Always dry the tray completely after washing. Pay attention to the edges and any small gaps. Use a clean towel or allow it to air dry thoroughly before reattaching. Regular cleaning and complete drying are the best defense against mold. It is important to address moisture quickly. Preventing these issues ensures a clean and safe eating surface. ### Is the Stokke Tripp Trapp tray dishwasher safe?

### How often should I clean my high chair tray?
You should clean your high chair tray after every use. A quick wipe-down with a damp cloth prevents food from drying and sticking. This maintains hygiene. ### Can I use harsh cleaners on my Stokke tray?
No, avoid harsh chemical cleaners, abrasive sponges, or strong detergents. These can damage the tray's plastic, dull its finish, or leave residue. Use mild dish soap or baby-safe cleaning solutions. A soft cloth or sponge is best for scrubbing. Gentle cleaning preserves the tray's material and ensures it remains safe.

### What if my tray gets warped in the dishwasher?
If your tray warps, it is likely due to excessive heat. This often happens on the bottom rack or with high-heat dry cycles. Unfortunately, warping is usually permanent. To prevent this, always use the top rack. Disable the heated dry setting. If damage occurs, consider contacting Stokke customer service for advice or replacement options.

### Are all Stokke high chair trays dishwasher safe?
Most modern Stokke high chair trays made of plastic, like those for the Tripp Trapp and Clikk models, are dishwasher safe. However, material compositions can vary.
