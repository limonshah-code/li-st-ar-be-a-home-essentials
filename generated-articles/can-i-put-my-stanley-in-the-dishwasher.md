## FAQ Section

### Is the Stanley Quencher H2.0 FlowState Tumbler dishwasher safe?
Yes, most Stanley Quencher H2.0 FlowState Tumblers are dishwasher safe. Always place the main cup body and lid components on the top rack of your dishwasher. This protects the finish and insulation from extreme heat. Check the bottom of your specific tumbler for a dishwasher-safe symbol or instructions to confirm.

### Can I put my Stanley's lid and straw in the dishwasher?
Yes, generally Stanley lids and straws are dishwasher safe. For best cleaning, disassemble the lid by removing any sliding parts or silicone gaskets. Place all lid components and the straw on the top rack or in the utensil basket. This ensures all parts get thoroughly cleaned and prevents damage.

### What happens if I put a non-dishwasher-safe Stanley in the dishwasher?
Putting a non-dishwasher-safe Stanley in the dishwasher can damage it. The high heat can break the vacuum seal, causing the cup to lose its insulating properties. It might also fade the exterior finish or warp plastic components. Always hand wash if your Stanley does not have a "dishwasher safe" label.

### How can I tell if my Stanley cup is dishwasher safe?
To determine if your Stanley cup is dishwasher safe, look for a symbol or text on the bottom of the cup. This often includes a dishwasher-safe icon or explicit written instructions. If you cannot find any indication, it is safest to assume it requires hand washing to avoid potential damage.

### Is hand washing better for my Stanley, even if it's dishwasher safe?
Hand washing can sometimes offer a more thorough clean for stubborn stains or hard-to-reach areas. While dishwasher-safe Stanley cups are convenient, occasional hand washing allows you to pay extra attention to gaskets, straw interiors, and small crevices. This can help maintain hygiene and extend the cup's life.

### Can dishwasher detergent damage my Stanley cup?
Standard dishwasher detergents are safe for dishwasher-safe Stanley cups. However, avoid using harsh, abrasive, or industrial-strength detergents. These can potentially scratch the finish or corrode the stainless steel over time. Stick to regular household dishwasher detergents for optimal care.
