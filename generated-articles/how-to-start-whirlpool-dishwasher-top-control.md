## Guide to Starting Your Whirlpool Top Control Dishwasher

My Whirlpool dishwasher with top controls is a truly helpful appliance in my home. It tackles dirty dishes with ease, saving me time and effort every day. You might have just installed a new Whirlpool dishwasher or are trying to understand its controls better. Sometimes, getting it to start can seem a bit tricky.

This guide will explain how to start your Whirlpool dishwasher top control model properly. We will cover important pre-wash steps, how to choose the right cycle, and what to do if your dishwasher does not begin washing. By the end, you will feel confident using your appliance for sparkling clean dishes every time. Let's get your dishes spotless!

### Takeaway:

*   Load dishes correctly, ensuring spray arms spin freely.
*   Add detergent and rinse aid to their designated dispensers.
*   Select the wash cycle that best fits your dish load.
*   Press the START button and close the door firmly within seconds.
*   If it does not start, check for power, an open door, or active control locks.

To start your Whirlpool dishwasher with top controls, first load your dishes and add detergent. Select your desired wash cycle from the options. Press the START button, often located on the top edge of the door. Finally, close the dishwasher door firmly within a few seconds for the cycle to begin.

## Essential Pre-Cycle Checks for Your Whirlpool Dishwasher

Before you even think about pushing the start button, some crucial checks make sure your Whirlpool dishwasher is ready. These steps help your machine run smoothly and clean dishes well. Skipping them can lead to poor washing results or issues with the cycle starting. I always do these simple checks before every load.

First, ensure your dishwasher has power. The control panel should light up when you open the door or press a button. Next, check the door latch. The dishwasher will not start if the door is not fully closed and latched. A loose door is a common reason for a Whirlpool dishwasher not to start. For more details on power issues, you can read [why won't my Whirlpool dishwasher start but has power](https://homeessentialsguide.com/why-wont-my-whirlpool-dishwasher-start-but-has-power).

### Power Supply and Door Latch

Your Whirlpool dishwasher needs a constant power supply to work. Make sure it is plugged in correctly, or its circuit breaker is not tripped. Sometimes, a quick check of your home's electrical panel can solve a non-starting issue. I recall one time when my dishwasher simply would not respond, and it turned out the breaker had tripped. It was a quick fix.

The door latch is also critical for your Whirlpool dishwasher top control model to begin a cycle. These dishwashers have safety features that stop them from running if the door is not secure. A firm push until you hear a click usually means the door is latched. If the door mechanism is faulty, the dishwasher will not receive the signal to start. Ensure nothing blocks the door from closing completely.

### Loading Dishes Correctly

Loading dishes might seem simple, but it impacts cleaning performance and can even affect starting. Place larger items in the bottom rack and smaller items, glasses, and cups in the top rack. Make sure no dishes or utensils block the spray arms. Spin the spray arms with your hand to check for obstructions.

Proper loading ensures water and detergent reach all surfaces. Overloading can prevent proper cleaning and may even interfere with the door closing. This can stop your Whirlpool dishwasher from starting. Take a moment to arrange everything neatly.

### Adding Detergent and Rinse Aid

Using the correct detergent and rinse aid is vital for clean dishes. Locate the detergent dispenser on the inside of the dishwasher door. Fill it with the recommended amount of automatic dishwasher detergent. Do not use regular dish soap, as it creates too many suds. This can damage your machine.

The rinse aid dispenser is usually next to the detergent dispenser. Fill it with liquid rinse aid. Rinse aid helps water drain from dishes, preventing spots and streaks. My dishes always look shinier when I remember to add rinse aid. Check your dishwasher's indicator light for rinse aid levels. ### Locating the Control Buttons

When you open the dishwasher door, look along the top edge. You will see a row of buttons. The exact layout varies by model, but common buttons include:

*   **Options:** Heated Dry, Sani Rinse, High Temp Wash, Delay Start.

### Common Cycle Options Explained

Whirlpool dishwashers offer various wash cycles to suit different needs. Understanding them helps you choose the most efficient option for your load.
*   **Normal:** This is my go-to cycle for daily loads with average soil. It balances cleaning power with energy efficiency.
*   **Heavy:** Use this for heavily soiled pots, pans, and casseroles. It uses more water and longer wash times.
*   **Sensor:** This smart cycle detects how dirty your dishes are and adjusts water temperature and wash time. It is great for mixed loads.
*   **Light/Quick Wash:** For lightly soiled dishes or when you need items cleaned fast. It is a shorter cycle, ideal for pre-rinsed dishes.
*   **Sanitize:** This option adds a high-temperature rinse to kill bacteria. It is good for baby bottles or when you want extra sanitation.

Choosing the right cycle improves cleaning results and saves energy. I usually pick the Sensor cycle because it is so convenient and effective for my varied loads.

## Step-by-Step Guide to Starting a Wash Cycle

Once you have loaded your dishes, added detergent, and understand your control panel, you are ready to start the wash cycle. This process is straightforward, but timing and attention to detail are important for your Whirlpool dishwasher top control. I always follow these steps to make sure my dishwasher runs perfectly. It's a routine that ensures clean dishes every time.

Starting the wash cycle involves a few simple actions. You will select your cycle, press the start button, and then close the door. Each step plays a part in getting your dishwasher to begin its cleaning process. If any step is missed or done incorrectly, your dishwasher may not start.

### Selecting Your Desired Cycle

First, open the dishwasher door to reveal the top control panel. Look at the available wash cycles, such as Normal, Heavy, Sensor, or Quick Wash. Think about how dirty your dishes are and choose the cycle that best fits. For example, I select "Heavy" for pots and pans and "Normal" for everyday plates.

To select a cycle, simply press the corresponding button on the control panel. An indicator light usually illuminates next to the chosen cycle, confirming your selection. If you want to add any options like "Heated Dry" or "Sani Rinse," press those buttons now as well. Make sure your selections are correct before moving on. This is where you tell your Whirlpool dishwasher top control what job it needs to do.

### Initiating the Wash with the Start Button

After choosing your cycle and options, locate the "START" button. This button is typically on the right or left side of the top control panel. Press and hold the "START" button for a few seconds. You might hear a beep or see the indicator light on the button illuminate steadily.

This action sends the signal to your Whirlpool dishwasher to prepare for the wash. The dishwasher briefly pauses before starting the wash cycle. Some models might even show a small countdown or display a "cycle ready" message. Make sure you press the START button firmly. If your [Whirlpool dishwasher won't start but has power](https://homeessentialsguide.com/why-wont-my-whirlpool-dishwasher-start-but-has-power), this step might be where the issue lies.

### Closing the Dishwasher Door Properly

This is a critical step for Whirlpool dishwashers with top controls. Once you press the START button, you have a short window—usually about 3 to 5 seconds—to close the dishwasher door. You must close the door firmly and completely. Listen for a click, indicating the door latch has engaged.

If you do not close the door quickly enough or if it is not fully latched, the cycle will not begin. The dishwasher may beep at you or flash lights on the control panel to indicate an issue. You will need to open the door, press the START button again, and re-close it properly. This quick action ensures the dishwasher receives the signal to begin operation. Some Whirlpool models, especially those with top controls, require this specific sequence to confirm the start command. ## Troubleshooting Common Starting Issues

Sometimes, even after following all the steps, your Whirlpool dishwasher with top controls might refuse to start. This can be frustrating, but many common issues have simple solutions. I have encountered several of these problems myself. Understanding what to look for can save you a service call. Most problems relate to power, door closure, or control settings.

When your dishwasher does not respond, it is often a matter of checking the basics. Do not panic right away. Take a deep breath and go through a checklist. This systematic approach helps pinpoint the exact problem quickly. This section will walk you through the most frequent reasons a Whirlpool dishwasher might not start and how to fix them.

### Power Problems and Solutions

The most fundamental requirement for any appliance is power. If your Whirlpool dishwasher top control model shows no signs of life, check its power source. First, ensure the dishwasher is plugged securely into its outlet. Sometimes, a loose plug is all it takes. Next, check your home's circuit breaker box. The breaker for the dishwasher might have tripped. Find the correct breaker, usually labeled, and flip it completely off, then back on.

If the power is fine, but the dishwasher still does not respond, there might be an internal electrical issue. This is less common but can happen. In such cases, it is best to consult your owner's manual or a qualified technician. Remember, if your [Whirlpool dishwasher won't start but has power](https://homeessentialsguide.com/why-wont-my-Whirlpool-dishwasher-start-but-has-power), the issue is likely something other than the electrical supply itself.

### Child Lock or Control Lock Activation

Whirlpool dishwashers often include a "Control Lock" or "Child Lock" feature. This feature disables the control panel buttons. It stops accidental starts or changes to cycles, especially useful with curious children around. If this feature is active, your dishwasher will not respond to button presses, including the START button. You might see a lock icon light up on the panel.

To deactivate the Control Lock, you usually press and hold a specific button for 3-5 seconds. This button is often labeled "Control Lock" or indicated by a lock icon. Sometimes, it is part of another button, like "Delay Start" or "Sanitize." Consult your owner's manual for the exact procedure for your model. I have accidentally activated this feature more than once. It is a simple fix once you know how. The process is similar to [how to get a Maytag dishwasher off control lock](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-get-maytag-dishwasher-off-control-lock), as these systems often share design principles.

### Door Latch and Sensor Issues

We covered the importance of a properly closed door earlier, but what if the door *seems* closed but the dishwasher still does not start? The door latch mechanism might be faulty or misaligned. The dishwasher has a sensor that detects if the door is fully closed and latched. If this sensor does not send the correct signal, the dishwasher will not begin.

Inspect the door latch mechanism for any obstructions or visible damage. Sometimes, food debris can get lodged in the latch, preventing it from fully engaging. Clean the area around the latch. If the latch is physically broken, it will need replacement. In some cases, adjusting the door itself can help. A blinking start button can sometimes indicate an issue with the door latch or a similar sensor; checking [why your Whirlpool dishwasher start button is blinking](https://homeessentialsguide.com/why-is-my-whirlpool-dishwasher-start-button-blinking) might offer more insights.

### Error Codes and What They Mean

Modern Whirlpool dishwashers can display error codes on their control panels. These codes are important clues about what is wrong with the appliance. If your dishwasher is not starting, look for any flashing lights or alphanumeric codes on the display. Common error codes relate to water supply, drainage, or sensor malfunctions.

Refer to your owner's manual to interpret specific error codes. The manual will provide a list of codes and their meanings, along with suggested troubleshooting steps. For example, an error code related to water supply might mean checking your water inlet valve or hose. Addressing the error code will often resolve the starting problem.

### Regular Cleaning and Maintenance

Regular cleaning is vital for optimal performance. Over time, food particles, grease, and mineral deposits can build up inside your dishwasher. This can lead to odors, poor cleaning, and even blockages that prevent proper operation. I make sure to clean my dishwasher regularly.

Here are key cleaning tasks:
*   **Clean the Filter:** Most Whirlpool dishwashers have a removable filter at the bottom. Check and clean it weekly or bi-weekly. Remove any trapped food debris. ### Using the Right Detergents and Rinse Aids

The type of detergent and rinse aid you use significantly impacts cleaning results and your dishwasher's health. Always use automatic dishwasher detergent specifically designed for dishwashers. ### Understanding Your Dishwasher's Sensors

## FAQ Section

### Q: My Whirlpool dishwasher top control won't start after I press the button. Why?
A: Check if the door is fully latched and closed within a few seconds of pressing START. Also, ensure the control lock is not active; you usually deactivate it by holding a specific button for 3-5 seconds. Verify the dishwasher has power, and its circuit breaker has not tripped.

### Q: How do I turn off the control lock on my Whirlpool dishwasher?
A: To turn off the control lock, locate the button labeled "Control Lock" or with a lock icon on your top control panel. Press and hold this button for 3 to 5 seconds until the lock indicator light turns off. The exact button can vary by model, so consult your user manual.

### Q: What is the normal wash cycle on a Whirlpool top control dishwasher?
A: The Normal wash cycle on a Whirlpool top control dishwasher is for everyday dish loads with typical food soil. It balances cleaning performance and energy efficiency. This cycle is suitable for a wide range of dishes, including plates, glasses, and silverware.

### Q: Do I need to pre-rinse dishes before loading them into a Whirlpool dishwasher?
A: For most modern Whirlpool dishwashers, you do not need to pre-rinse dishes. Simply scrape off large food particles and put the dishes directly into the dishwasher. The dishwasher's sensors and powerful wash cycles handle the rest, which saves water and time.

### Q: How long does a typical Whirlpool dishwasher cycle take?
A: A typical Whirlpool dishwasher cycle, such as the "Normal" cycle, can take anywhere from 2 to 3.5 hours. The exact duration depends on the selected cycle, options like "Heated Dry" or "Sani Rinse," and the soil level detected by the dishwasher's sensors.
