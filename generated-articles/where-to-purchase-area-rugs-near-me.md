### FAQ Section

**1. What is the best size area rug for a living room?**
The best size depends on your living room's dimensions and furniture layout. For most living rooms, the front legs of all main furniture pieces should sit on the rug. This creates a cohesive look. A common size is 8x10 feet or larger for larger rooms. Smaller rugs can define specific zones.

**2. Are online area rugs good quality?**
Online area rugs can be very good quality. Many reputable online retailers sell high-quality rugs from various brands and materials. Always read customer reviews and check the return policy before buying. Look for detailed product descriptions and multiple photos to assess quality.

**3. How often should I clean my area rug?**
The frequency of cleaning depends on foot traffic, pets, and spills. For average households, vacuuming once or twice a week is good. Deep cleaning should happen every 12 to 18 months. Spot clean spills immediately to prevent stains.

**4. Can I use an area rug on carpet?**
Yes, you can use an area rug on carpet. This helps define a space, add warmth, or introduce color. Use a rug pad made for carpet-on-carpet use. This prevents slipping and protects the underlying carpet fibers. The pad also helps prevent the area rug from "crawling" or bunching up.

**5. What is the best material for a high-traffic area rug?**
For high-traffic areas, durable materials are best. Wool is a top choice for its resilience and natural stain resistance. Synthetic fibers like nylon and polypropylene are also excellent. They are very durable, easy to clean, and resist fading. Avoid delicate materials like silk in high-traffic zones.
