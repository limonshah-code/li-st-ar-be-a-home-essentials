## Understanding HEPA Filtration in Your Home

A HEPA filter is a very important part of air purification. HEPA stands for High-Efficiency Particulate Air. This filter type meets a strict standard set by the US Department of Energy. It must capture 99.97% of airborne particles. These particles must be 0.3 microns or larger. This includes pollen, pet dander, dust mites, and mold spores. I find it amazing how such a simple filter can make such a big difference in air quality.

When a vacuum claims to have a HEPA filter, it means it can remove tiny pollutants from your home. This is especially good for people with allergies or asthma. Regular vacuum cleaners often pick up visible dirt. However, they might release smaller particles back into the air. A true HEPA filter prevents this. It traps those microscopic irritants inside the vacuum. Understanding [what HEPA filter does](https://homeessentialsguide.com/what-does-hepa-filter-do) helps you appreciate its value.

Not all filters are HEPA filters. Some vacuums have "HEPA-type" or "HEPA-like" filters. These filters may capture some small particles. They do not meet the strict 99.97% efficiency standard of a true HEPA filter. Always check the product specifications carefully. You want to ensure your vacuum has a certified HEPA filter. This ensures you get the best air purification possible.

Choosing a vacuum with a HEPA filter means cleaner air for your family. It reduces exposure to common allergens. This creates a healthier living environment. I always recommend considering a HEPA vacuum. It offers superior filtration for your home. ## The Importance of a Sealed System with HEPA Filters

A HEPA filter alone is not enough for perfect air quality. A vacuum must also have a sealed system. This means the air path from the brush roll to the exhaust is completely sealed. No air should leak out before passing through the filter. If a vacuum has a HEPA filter but a leaky system, particles can escape. These particles re-enter your home's air.

Imagine a bucket with a tiny hole. Even if you fill it with pure water, some will still leak out. The same applies to a vacuum. A great filter means little if the dust escapes through unsealed cracks. This is why a sealed system is so important. It ensures that all the air picked up by the vacuum passes through the HEPA filter. This traps allergens inside.

Bissell designs many of its higher-end vacuums with this in mind. They often pair HEPA filters with sealed systems. This combination delivers superior air purification. It is especially beneficial for those with severe allergies. This prevents fine dust and allergens from recirculating back into your breathing space. I always tell people to look for this feature. It makes a big difference.

When you invest in a vacuum with a sealed system and a true HEPA filter, you are investing in cleaner air. You are minimizing the release of irritants. This creates a healthier home environment for everyone. Make sure to check for both features when choosing your Bissell vacuum. This ensures maximum effectiveness in capturing allergens.

## Maintaining Your Bissell HEPA Filter for Optimal Performance

Maintaining your Bissell HEPA filter is key for its effectiveness. A dirty filter cannot trap particles efficiently. It can also reduce your vacuum's suction power. If your Bissell HEPA filter is washable, follow the manufacturer's instructions. Usually, this means rinsing it under cool water until the water runs clear. Allow it to air dry completely for at least 24 hours before putting it back. Never put a wet filter back into your vacuum. This can cause mold growth and damage the motor.
