## The Culprits: Why Your Miele Needs a Hygiene Cycle

Several factors contribute to the "Hygiene Info" message appearing on your Miele washing machine. Understanding these causes helps you prevent the message from recurring too frequently. I have noticed certain habits always trigger this reminder. Most often, the primary cause is the frequent use of low-temperature wash cycles. Modern detergents are effective at cooler temperatures. This leads many people to wash clothes at 30°C or 40°C. While energy-efficient, these lower temperatures do not effectively kill bacteria and mold. They also do not break down detergent residues as thoroughly as hot water. Over time, this leads to a buildup of biofilm inside the machine's drum, hoses, and seals. This biofilm creates a perfect breeding ground for microorganisms. These microorganisms produce the musty odors often associated with dirty washing machines.

Another significant contributor is the accumulation of detergent and fabric softener residue. Even with proper detergent usage, some residue can stick to the dispenser drawer and the inside of the machine. Fabric softeners, especially, can leave a waxy film. This film traps dirt, lint, and bacteria. Over time, this residue hardens and creates blockages. It can also cause leaks from the dispenser drawer if it impedes water flow. If you notice your [Miele washing machine leaking from the detergent drawer](https://homeessentialsguide.com/why-is-my-miele-washing-machine-leaking-from-detergent-drawer), residue buildup is a common culprit. This residue provides nutrients for mold and mildew. These grow in the dark, damp environment of a washing machine.

Moisture inside the drum and seals also plays a crucial role. After a wash cycle, some water always remains. If the door and detergent drawer are closed immediately, this moisture cannot evaporate. This creates a humid, enclosed space that mold loves. I always make sure to air out my machine. Ignoring this practice contributes significantly to hygiene issues. Finally, hard water can exacerbate these problems. Hard water contains high levels of minerals like calcium and magnesium. These minerals combine with detergent and dirt to form limescale deposits. Limescale acts as a rough surface where bacteria and mold can easily attach and grow. These deposits also reduce the efficiency of your machine. They can even damage internal components over time. Regular cleaning helps remove these mineral buildups. You can use various detergents with Miele machines, but knowing [can I use any detergent with Miele washing machine](https://homeessentialsguide.com/can-i-use-any-detergent-with-miele-washing-machine) properly is key. The type and amount you use directly affect residue buildup.

## Your First Step: Running the Miele Maintenance Program

When your Miele washing machine displays "Hygiene Info," the most important first step is to run its dedicated maintenance program. This program is specifically designed to address the issues that trigger the message. It is a powerful internal cleaning cycle. The program typically runs at a very high temperature, often 75°C or 90°C (167°F or 194°F). This high heat effectively kills bacteria, mold, and other microorganisms that thrive in cooler, damp environments. It also helps to dissolve stubborn detergent residues and biofilm that have accumulated over time. This process is crucial for deep cleaning the drum, the pipes, and other internal parts of the machine.

To activate the maintenance program, you will usually find it labeled as "Maintenance Wash," "Machine Clean," or "Intensive Clean" on your program selector. Consult your Miele user manual for the exact name and steps for your specific model. Most Miele models require you to run this program without any laundry inside. This ensures the water and cleaning agents can circulate freely throughout the machine. I make sure my drum is completely empty before starting this cycle. Some models might suggest adding a special Miele machine cleaner, which further enhances the cleaning power. This cleaner targets specific buildups like grease, limescale, and detergent residues.

The program usually lasts longer than a regular wash cycle. It involves thorough rinses to ensure all dissolved residues are flushed out. You will notice a significant improvement in the machine's smell after this cycle. The recommended frequency for running this program varies based on your usage habits. If you primarily use low-temperature washes, you might need to run it more often. Miele generally suggests running it every 1-2 months. If the "Hygiene Info" message appears more frequently, adjust your cleaning schedule. Running this cycle consistently prevents major buildup. It ensures optimal hygiene for your laundry. Remember, this step is vital for maintaining the cleanliness and efficiency of your Miele washing machine. It directly addresses the reason your machine says "Hygiene Info."

## Targeted Cleaning: Dispenser Drawer and Door Seal

While the internal maintenance program cleans the drum and pipes, some areas need direct attention. The detergent dispenser drawer and the rubber door seal are prime locations for grime. These spots often harbor mold, mildew, and detergent residue. Neglecting them can lead to persistent odors and hygiene issues. I always check these areas when cleaning my machine. They are critical for overall machine cleanliness.

Let's start with the dispenser drawer. This component handles all your detergents and fabric softeners. Over time, these products can accumulate as sticky, hardened residue. This buildup can block the dispensing nozzles. It can also create a damp environment where mold thrives. Many Miele detergent drawers are removable for easy cleaning. To clean it, pull the drawer out completely. Some models have a release tab or button. Soak the drawer in warm, soapy water for about 15-30 minutes. Use a small brush, like an old toothbrush, to scrub away any residue. Pay special attention to the corners and crevices. These spots are common hiding places for mold. Rinse the drawer thoroughly under running water. Ensure no soap residue remains. Dry it completely before putting it back into the machine. While you have the drawer out, look inside the dispenser housing in the machine. Use a damp cloth to wipe away any visible residue. This thorough cleaning helps prevent [Miele washing machine leaks from the detergent drawer](https://homeessentialsguide.com/why-is-my-miele-washing-machine-leaking-from-detergent-drawer). A clear dispenser ensures proper detergent distribution.

Next, focus on the rubber door seal, also known as the gasket. This seal forms a watertight barrier around the door. It also collects lint, hair, and moisture from every wash cycle. This dark, damp, and often warm environment is perfect for mold and mildew growth. You will likely see black spots or a slimy film on the rubber. To clean the seal, gently pull back the folds of the rubber. Use a cloth or sponge dampened with warm water and a mild detergent. Wipe thoroughly around the entire circumference of the seal. You might need to use a small brush for stubborn mold spots. For more intense mold, a diluted bleach solution (1 part bleach to 10 parts water) can be effective. Always wear gloves when using bleach. Wipe the area carefully, then rinse with a clean, damp cloth. Dry the seal completely afterward. Failing to clean this area can lead to musty smells transferring to your clean laundry. If the mold is very bad, it can even cause leaks from the machine's bottom. For issues like [Miele washing machine leaking from the bottom](https://homeessentialsguide.com/why-is-my-miele-washing-machine-leaking-from-the-bottom), moldy seals could be a contributing factor. Regular cleaning of these two areas is simple. It makes a big difference in maintaining your Miele's hygiene.

These products target specific issues like limescale buildup and stubborn internal grime. Understanding when and how to use them is key to maintaining your machine's longevity and performance. I find these cleaners especially useful if I live in a hard water area.

Limescale is a common problem, particularly in regions with hard water. It forms when minerals like calcium and magnesium, present in water, deposit on heating elements and other internal components. Limescale reduces heating efficiency. It can also damage parts over time. If you live in a hard water area, you might notice white, chalky deposits. These deposits are a clear sign of limescale. Miele offers its own brand of descaling agents. These are formulated to be safe and effective for Miele machines. Using a Miele descaler regularly helps prevent hard water damage. It ensures your machine heats water efficiently. You typically add the descaler directly to the detergent dispenser or drum. Then, you run a specific maintenance program. Always follow the product instructions for dosage and cycle selection.

For general deep cleaning and addressing persistent odors or biofilm, Miele also provides specific washing machine cleaners. These cleaners are different from descalers. They contain agents that break down grease, soap scum, and bacteria. These are the primary culprits behind the "Hygiene Info" message. These cleaners work in conjunction with the machine's maintenance wash program. They provide a more intensive cleaning action than water alone. For instance, you could run an empty hot wash with a cup of white vinegar. This helps freshen the drum. However, vinegar does not offer the same comprehensive cleaning as a dedicated machine cleaner. One of the most important habits is to leave the washing machine door and detergent dispenser ajar after every wash cycle. This allows air to circulate inside the drum and dispenser. Airflow helps dry out any residual moisture. This prevents the humid environment where mold and mildew thrive. Another good practice is to wipe down the surfaces of your machine. After each wash, take a moment to wipe the inside of the door. ## Troubleshooting Persistent "Hygiene Info" Messages

Even after running the maintenance program and performing manual cleaning, some Miele owners might find the "Hygiene Info" message reappears quickly. This can be frustrating. However, it indicates a deeper issue or an overlooked aspect of maintenance. I have encountered this myself and learned to investigate further. It is important to systematically troubleshoot the problem.

First, double-check your usage habits. Are you consistently running very low-temperature cycles? Is the machine door and dispenser always closed immediately after a wash? If so, try to adjust these habits. Increase the frequency of hot washes (e.g., 60°C or 90°C) for specific loads, such as towels or bedding. Ensure proper ventilation after each use. These small changes can significantly impact the internal environment of your machine.

Next, consider the machine's drainage system. A partially blocked drain filter or hose can leave more standing water in the drum. This contributes to moisture and odor issues. Your Miele washing machine has a drain filter, usually located at the bottom front. Refer to your manual for its exact location and instructions on how to clean it. Regularly cleaning this filter removes lint, coins, and other small items that can impede drainage. A blocked filter can also lead to other issues, like your [Miele washing machine not spinning properly](https://homeessentialsguide.com/why-is-my-miele-washing-machine-not-spinning-properly) due to a sensing error. Ensure the drain hose itself is not kinked or blocked. Proper water drainage is essential for a dry, hygienic drum.

Also, evaluate the type and amount of detergent you are using.
