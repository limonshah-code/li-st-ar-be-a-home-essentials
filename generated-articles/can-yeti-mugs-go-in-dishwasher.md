### FAQ Section:

**1. Are all Yeti products dishwasher safe?**
Most Yeti stainless steel drinkware, including Rambler mugs, tumblers, and bottles, is dishwasher safe. Always check the specific product's packaging or Yeti's official website for confirmation. Certain older models or specialized products might have different care instructions. When in doubt, hand washing is a safe alternative.

**2. Can the color of my Yeti fade in the dishwasher?**
Yeti's powder coating is designed for durability. However, prolonged exposure to very high heat, especially on the bottom rack, could potentially lead to minor fading or dulling over many years. Yeti recommends placing mugs on the top rack of the dishwasher to minimize this risk and preserve the finish.

**3. Is the heat cycle safe for Yeti mugs?**
Yes, the standard heat cycles in most dishwashers are safe for Yeti mugs. The double-wall vacuum insulation and stainless steel construction are built to withstand these temperatures. The key is placing them on the top rack, where the heat is typically less intense, to protect the exterior finish.

**4. How often should I clean my Yeti mug?**
You should clean your Yeti mug after every use, just like any other dish. This prevents residue buildup, odors, and bacterial growth. For a more thorough clean, especially for lids and gaskets, disassemble them periodically, or whenever you notice a persistent smell or stain.

**5. Can I put my Yeti lid on the bottom rack?**
No, it is best to place Yeti lids, like the main mug body, on the top rack of the dishwasher. The bottom rack's higher heat and stronger water jets can be too harsh for the plastic components and rubber gaskets, potentially causing warping or damage over time.

**6. What if my Yeti gets a strange smell after dishwashing?**
A strange smell often indicates that some residue was not fully removed. This usually happens when lids, gaskets, or straws are not properly disassembled before washing. Try hand washing all parts thoroughly with soap and a brush, focusing on crevices. A baking soda and vinegar soak can also neutralize stubborn odors.
