## Preparing Your Dishes for the Sanitize Cycle

Proper preparation of your dishes is key for any dishwasher cycle. This is true for the sanitize cycle too. Good preparation helps the cycle work its best. It ensures that the high heat reaches all surfaces effectively. You will get the cleanest, most sanitized dishes this way.

First, scrape off large food particles from plates and bowls. You do not need to pre-rinse dishes thoroughly. Modern detergents and dishwashers handle stuck-on food well. However, removing big pieces prevents drain clogs and ensures water can wash away grime. This step makes the sanitize cycle more efficient.

Next, load your dishes correctly into the dishwasher racks. Do not overcrowd the racks. Overcrowding can block water spray from reaching all items. Make sure items face the spray arms. This allows proper water circulation and heat distribution. Proper loading helps every dish get the full benefit of the sanitize wash. ## Activating the Whirlpool Dishwasher Sanitize Cycle

Turning on the sanitize cycle on your Whirlpool dishwasher is a simple process. The steps may vary slightly by model. However, the basic procedure remains similar across most Whirlpool appliances. You will typically find a dedicated button for this function. Knowing where to look makes activation easy.

First, close your dishwasher door firmly. Then, select your preferred wash cycle. Common cycles include Normal, Heavy, or Light. The sanitize option usually works with most standard wash cycles. It acts as an enhancement to the chosen cycle. Choose the wash setting that best suits your dish load.

After selecting your wash cycle, locate the "Sanitize" button on your control panel. This button might also be labeled "Sani Rinse" or "High-Temp Wash." Press this button to activate the sanitize option. An indicator light usually turns on to confirm your selection. This light confirms the cycle will now include the high-temperature sanitize phase.

Once you have selected both the wash cycle and the sanitize option, press the "Start" button. Your Whirlpool dishwasher will begin the wash cycle. It will automatically heat the water to the required temperature during the sanitize phase. The cycle will take longer than a standard wash. This extra time allows for thorough heating and germ elimination. Do not open the door during the cycle to ensure the full sanitization process completes. ## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

### What is the difference between a normal wash and a sanitize cycle?
A normal wash cycle cleans dishes using hot water and detergent. The sanitize cycle goes further. It includes a final rinse where water reaches extremely high temperatures, often above 150째F (65째C). This high heat is specifically designed to kill bacteria and reduce germs by 99.9%.

### How long does the Whirlpool dishwasher sanitize cycle take?
The sanitize cycle extends the overall wash time. It typically adds 15 to 30 minutes to a standard wash cycle. The exact duration depends on the selected wash cycle and your dishwasher model. This extra time allows the water to reach and maintain the necessary sanitizing temperature.

### Can I sanitize all types of dishes in my Whirlpool dishwasher?
Most dishwasher-safe items can go through the sanitize cycle. This includes glass, ceramics, and most plastics. However, avoid sanitizing delicate items, antique dishes, or heat-sensitive plastics. Always check the manufacturer's recommendations for your specific dishware. Extreme heat can damage some materials.

### Does the sanitize cycle use more energy?
Yes, the sanitize cycle uses slightly more energy than a normal wash. This is because the dishwasher must heat the water to a much higher temperature. The heating element works longer to maintain this intense heat. However, the energy consumption increase is minimal compared to the hygiene benefits gained.

### Is using rinse aid necessary with the sanitize cycle?
While not strictly necessary for sanitization, using rinse aid is highly recommended. Rinse aid helps water sheet off dishes, preventing water spots. It also improves drying performance. This means dishes come out sparkling and dry, making the high-temperature wash even more effective in practice.
