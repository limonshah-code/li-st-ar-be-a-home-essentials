## Can You Safely Machine Wash Saucony Running Shoes?

Running shoes take a lot of punishment. They get dirty from trails, pavement, and everyday wear. Saucony running shoes are known for comfort and performance. You might wonder if you can machine wash Saucony running shoes to keep them fresh. It is a common question among shoe owners.

Many people consider the washing machine for quick cleaning. However, throwing your valuable Saucony sneakers directly into the laundry can cause damage. We will explain how to clean your Saucony shoes safely. This guide covers proper preparation, careful washing steps, and safe drying methods. You will learn important tips to protect your shoes' quality and extend their life.

### Takeaway

*   You can machine wash Saucony shoes, but use caution.
*   Always remove laces and insoles before washing.
*   Use a mesh laundry bag to protect shoes and the machine.
*   Wash shoes with cold water on a gentle cycle.
*   Air dry your Saucony shoes completely, avoiding direct heat.

### Can you machine wash Saucony running shoes?

Yes, you can machine wash Saucony running shoes, but you must follow specific steps to avoid damage. Use a gentle cycle, cold water, and a laundry bag. Air drying is essential to protect materials and adhesives. Skipping these steps risks shrinking or deforming your shoes.

## Understanding Your Saucony Running Shoes and Materials

Saucony running shoes use different materials. Knowing what your shoes are made of helps you clean them right. Most Saucony shoes feature mesh uppers and synthetic overlays. These materials are generally machine washable.

However, heat can be a problem. Hot water or a machine dryer can damage the foam midsoles. This heat can also weaken the glues that hold the shoe together. Understanding your shoe's construction helps prevent damage. It helps ensure your shoes stay in good condition for longer.

### Mesh and Synthetic Fabrics

Many Saucony models use mesh for breathability. They also use synthetic materials for structure. These parts are usually safe for washing machines. They handle water and mild soap well. The open weave of mesh allows dirt to wash out easily.

Synthetic fabrics are durable. They can withstand gentle cleaning cycles. Always check your specific shoe model if you are unsure. Most modern running shoe fabrics tolerate machine cleaning.

### Foam Midsoles and Glues

The midsole is crucial for cushioning. Saucony often uses EVA foam for this part. This foam can degrade with high heat. It can also lose its cushioning properties. Glues that bond shoe parts together are also sensitive to heat.

Extreme temperatures can cause these glues to weaken. This can lead to parts of your shoe coming apart. Always choose cold water washing. Never use a machine dryer for your Saucony shoes. Proper care keeps the foam and glues strong.

## Preparing Your Saucony Shoes for the Washing Machine

Good preparation makes a big difference. It helps clean your shoes better. It also protects your washing machine. You should always start by removing loose dirt. This step prevents dirt from spreading inside the washer.

Pre-cleaning is vital for a successful wash. My experience shows that proper prep avoids many issues. This process keeps your shoes in good shape. It also keeps your washing machine clean.

### Remove Laces and Insoles

First, take out the laces from your Saucony shoes. Remove the insoles as well. Laces can tangle in the washing machine. They can also get damaged or stretched. Insoles absorb a lot of sweat and odor.

Washing them separately allows for a more thorough clean. You can hand wash laces and insoles with soap and water. This gives them a deeper clean. Dry them fully before putting them back in your shoes.

### Brush Off Loose Dirt

Before placing shoes in the machine, brush off loose dirt. Use a soft brush or an old toothbrush. Scrape off any mud stuck to the soles. Knock the shoes together outside. This removes excess debris.

This step prevents dirt from clogging your washer. It also keeps your machine cleaner. Removing large pieces of dirt makes the machine washing more effective. It prepares the shoes for a deep clean.

### Place Shoes in a Mesh Laundry Bag

Always put your Saucony shoes into a mesh laundry bag. This is a very important step. The bag protects your shoes from getting tossed around too much. It prevents them from hitting the washer drum hard. The bag also protects your washing machine.

Shoes can be rough on the inside of the machine. The bag keeps the shoes from damaging the agitator or drum. It also keeps shoelaces or small parts from getting stuck. ## The Washing Process: Step-by-Step for Clean Saucony Shoes

Once everything is ready, the washing process is simple. Follow these steps for a safe and effective clean. Do not rush this part. Proper execution ensures the best results.

### Load Machine Carefully

Load your washing machine with care. Place the mesh bag containing your Saucony shoes inside. Add 2-4 old towels around the shoes. This helps distribute the weight evenly. It also prevents uneven washing.

Ensure the machine is not overloaded. Overloading can reduce cleaning effectiveness. It can also put stress on your machine. Proper loading protects both your shoes and the washer.

### Start the Gentle Cycle

Select the cold water, delicate, or gentle cycle. Start the washing machine. Let the machine complete its cycle without interruption. This ensures the shoes get a full, proper wash.

I always choose the shortest gentle cycle available. This minimizes the time shoes spend in water. Shorter cycles are less harsh on shoe materials. This approach keeps your shoes safe.

### Remove Shoes Promptly

As soon as the cycle finishes, remove your Saucony shoes immediately. Do not leave them sitting in the wet drum. Leaving them wet can encourage mold or mildew growth. This can also cause unpleasant odors.

Prompt removal helps prevent colors from running. It also helps start the drying process quickly. Quick removal keeps your shoes fresh and clean. It sets them up for proper drying.

## Proper Drying Techniques to Preserve Saucony Shoe Life

Drying your Saucony running shoes correctly is as important as washing them. Incorrect drying can cause irreversible damage. Heat is the biggest enemy. It can ruin the shape and integrity of your shoes.

My personal experience confirms that air drying is the only safe option. I always ensure my shoes are completely dry before wearing them. This careful drying preserves shoe quality. ### Air Drying is the Only Safe Method

Never put your Saucony running shoes in a tumble dryer. The heat from a dryer will shrink, warp, or melt shoe materials. It will also break down the adhesives holding the shoe together. This can cause the sole to detach.

Air drying is the only safe method. It slowly removes moisture. This preserves the shoe's original shape and structure. Always choose a well-ventilated area for drying.

### Avoid Direct Heat

Keep your shoes away from direct heat sources. This includes radiators, direct sunlight, or hair dryers. Even indirect heat can harm your shoes. Heat damages the foam and glues within the shoe.

Find a cool, dry spot indoors. A shaded outdoor area also works if weather permits. Good air circulation is key. It speeds up the drying process without damaging materials.

### Stuff Shoes with Paper Towels or Newspaper

To absorb moisture quickly, stuff your shoes. Use clean paper towels or unprinted newspaper. These materials pull water out from inside the shoes. They also help the shoes keep their shape as they dry.

Change the paper every few hours. This ensures continuous moisture absorption. This method helps the shoes dry faster. It also prevents any misshaping.

### Allow Shoes to Dry Completely

Your Saucony shoes need to dry completely. This can take 24 to 48 hours. The drying time depends on humidity and air circulation. Do not wear them until they are fully dry. Wearing damp shoes can cause odors.

It can also lead to mildew growth. Check the insides carefully. Make sure all parts are dry before use. This final step ensures your shoes are fresh and ready. This also applies to [how to clean white running shoes](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-clean-white-running-shoes).

## FAQ Section

### Can I put Saucony insoles in the washing machine?
No, it is best not to machine wash Saucony insoles. Machine washing can damage their cushioning and shape. Instead, hand wash them with mild soap and water. Scrub gently, rinse well, and air dry completely. This method preserves their support and fit.

### What detergent is best for Saucony running shoes?
Use a mild, liquid laundry detergent for your Saucony running shoes. Avoid harsh chemicals, bleach, or powder detergents. A small amount of gentle detergent is enough to clean without damaging materials. Look for dye-free and perfume-free options to protect colors and prevent residue.

### How often should I clean my Saucony running shoes?
Clean your Saucony running shoes as needed, typically when they look dirty or start to smell. For light use, a quick wipe-down after each wear is good. For heavy use or mud, a deeper clean every few weeks may be necessary. Avoid over-washing to preserve shoe integrity.

### Can machine washing damage Saucony shoe cushioning?
Yes, machine washing can damage Saucony shoe cushioning if done improperly. High heat and harsh cycles can degrade the EVA foam in midsoles. Always use cold water and a gentle cycle to protect the cushioning. Air dry thoroughly to maintain the shoe's shock absorption and comfort.

### Are all Saucony models machine washable?
Most Saucony running shoe models made with mesh and synthetic uppers are machine washable. However, models with leather, suede, or special embellishments might not be. Always check the manufacturer's care instructions if available. When in doubt, hand wash delicate models to be safe.

### What if my Saucony shoes still smell after washing?
If your Saucony shoes still smell after washing, they might not have dried completely. Ensure they are bone dry inside. You can also try using baking soda or commercial shoe deodorizers. Place them inside the shoes overnight. For stubborn odors, repeat the gentle washing process, focusing on quick and complete drying.
