The goal is always to provide a safe and effective cooking experience. This ensures your food releases easily from the basket.

It gives you peace of mind while cooking. Understanding these materials helps you use your appliance safely.

## The Truth About PTFE and PFOA in Non-Stick Cookware

Let us clarify what PTFE and PFOA are. PTFE is a synthetic fluoropolymer. It is known for its non-stick properties. It is chemically inert and stable at typical cooking temperatures. The brand name for PTFE is Teflon. Many non-stick pans use this material. It allows food to slide off easily.

PFOA is different from PTFE. PFOA was a processing aid. Manufacturers used it to produce some PTFE coatings. It was not a part of the finished non-stick product itself. However, small amounts could remain after manufacturing. Scientific studies raised concerns about PFOA's potential health effects. This led to a global effort to remove it from manufacturing processes. By 2013, major manufacturers stopped using PFOA in non-stick cookware.

Today, nearly all non-stick cookware, including air fryers, is PFOA-free. This includes Ninja products. While Ninja air fryers often use a form of PTFE for their non-stick surfaces, it is important to know that these coatings are free of PFOA. This distinction is vital for consumer safety. Modern non-stick coatings meet strict safety standards. They allow for convenient cooking without the past concerns.

## Caring for Your Ninja Dual Air Fryer's Non-Stick Surface

Proper care extends the life of your Ninja Dual Air Fryer's non-stick coating. Even durable coatings can wear down without good care. Following simple cleaning steps is key. Always let the baskets cool down after cooking. Cleaning hot surfaces can damage the coating over time. Use warm, soapy water and a soft sponge or cloth. Avoid abrasive cleaners, steel wool, or harsh scrubbing pads. These can scratch the non-stick surface.

These materials are gentle on the non-stick surface. For stubborn food residue, soak the baskets in warm, soapy water for a few minutes. This helps loosen stuck-on bits.
