## Understanding the Bosch Dishwasher E24 Error: A Complete Guide

A dishwasher that will not drain is frustrating. You open the door, expecting sparkling clean dishes, only to find standing water. If you own a Bosch dishwasher, you might see an E24 error code on the display. This specific code tells you your appliance has a problem. You might wonder, "What is E24 error on Bosch dishwasher?"

This article gives you clear answers. We explain what this error means. We then walk you through common causes. You will learn easy steps to fix it yourself. We cover everything from clogged filters to blocked drain hoses. We also discuss when to call a professional. My goal is to help you get your Bosch dishwasher working correctly again.

### Takeaway

If your Bosch dishwasher displays an E24 error, it means the appliance has a drainage issue. Here are the key steps to fix it:

*   **Check and clean the filter:** This is the most common cause.
*   **Inspect the drain hose:** Look for kinks or clogs.
*   **Clear the air gap or sink connection:** These external components can cause blockages.
*   **Reset the dishwasher:** Sometimes a simple reset resolves minor glitches.

The E24 error on a Bosch dishwasher indicates a problem with water draining out of the appliance. This usually means the dishwasher cannot pump water out properly, often due to a blockage in the drain system or an issue with the drain pump itself.

## What Does the E24 Error Mean on Your Bosch Dishwasher?

The E24 error code on a Bosch dishwasher specifically signals a drainage issue. It means your dishwasher is unable to empty the water from its tub. This happens at various stages of the wash cycle. Most often, the error appears during the final drain. The machine tries to pump water out, but it senses an obstruction or malfunction.

This error is a safety measure. It prevents water from overflowing or damaging internal components. When the E24 code shows up, your dishwasher stops its cycle. It waits for you to resolve the problem. Ignoring it can lead to bigger issues or dirty dishes. Understanding this code helps you identify the correct troubleshooting steps.

The E24 error can be triggered by several common culprits. These range from simple clogs to more complex pump failures. My experience tells me that most E24 errors are fixable by the homeowner. You just need to know where to look. We will explore each potential cause in detail. This helps you pinpoint the exact reason for your E24 error.

### Initial Steps to Address E24 Error

When the E24 error appears, do not panic. The first step is to perform a simple reset. This clears temporary glitches. Turn off the dishwasher at the power outlet for a few minutes. Unplug it if possible. Then plug it back in. Turn the dishwasher back on. See if the error code reappears.

If the error persists, you need to begin a more thorough check. Before starting any physical inspection, turn off the power to the dishwasher again. Safety is important when working with appliances. Get a towel and a shallow pan ready to catch any residual water. Some water might spill when you open the filter or drain area. Taking these initial precautions makes the troubleshooting process smoother.

## Checking and Cleaning Your Dishwasher Filter System

The most common reason for an E24 error on a Bosch dishwasher is a clogged filter system. Food particles, grease, and debris build up over time. This buildup stops water from flowing freely into the drain pump. A blocked filter prevents proper drainage. You will see standing water at the bottom of the tub.

Bosch dishwashers have a multi-part filter system. It usually consists of a coarse filter, a fine filter, and sometimes a micro-filter. These filters catch food scraps. They keep large items from reaching the drain pump. Cleaning them regularly is essential for optimal performance. I clean my dishwasher filter monthly. This prevents many common drainage problems.

A dirty filter reduces the dishwasher's efficiency. Dishes might not come out clean. The E24 error is a clear sign that cleaning is overdue. It is a simple task that can save you money. You do not need special tools. Just your hands and some cleaning solution.

### Removing and Cleaning the Filter

Accessing the filter is usually straightforward in Bosch models. Open the dishwasher door completely. Look at the bottom of the wash tub. You will see a cylindrical filter assembly. This assembly typically twists to unlock. Give it a gentle turn, usually counter-clockwise. You can then lift it out. Be careful as there might be standing water.

Once the filter is out, separate its components. Rinse each part under running water. Use a soft brush, like an old toothbrush, to scrub away stubborn food particles. You can also use a mild dish soap solution. Ensure all holes and mesh screens are clear. Hold the filter up to the light. You should be able to see through the mesh. For more detailed instructions, you can follow a guide on [how to clean your Bosch dishwasher filter](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-clean-your-bosch-dishwasher-filter).

This step is critical for resolving the E24 error. Neglecting filter cleaning causes poor drainage. It also makes your dishwasher work harder. This can shorten its lifespan. So, take your time and do a thorough job.

### Reassembling the Filter

After cleaning, it is time to put the filter back. This step is as important as removing it. Make sure each part clicks back into place. Ensure a tight seal. An improperly seated filter can also cause drainage issues. It might even allow large debris to reach the pump. This can lead to more serious problems.

Carefully place the fine filter back into its housing. Then, insert the coarse filter assembly. Twist it clockwise until it locks securely. You should feel it click into place. If it feels loose, remove it and try again. A correctly assembled filter system creates an effective barrier against food debris. It also ensures smooth water flow to the drain pump.

Once the filter is clean and reassembled, run a short test cycle. Fill the dishwasher with some water. Observe if it drains properly at the end. If the E24 error reappears, the problem is not the filter alone. You then need to move on to other potential causes. For general maintenance, learning [how to clean your Bosch dishwasher](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-clean-your-bosch-dishwasher) regularly can prevent many errors.

## Inspecting the Drain Hose for Obstructions

If your dishwasher filter is spotless, the next place to check is the drain hose. The drain hose carries wastewater from the dishwasher to your sink drain or garbage disposal. This hose can become kinked, bent, or clogged. Any of these conditions will restrict water flow. This triggers the E24 error. It is a common problem, especially if the dishwasher has been moved recently.

A kinked hose is easy to spot. It looks like a sharp bend in the hose. This bend acts like a dam, stopping water. Clearing a kink is a quick fix. However, clogs inside the hose are harder to see. Food particles, grease, or even small foreign objects can accumulate. These blockages build up over time. They narrow the hose's path. Eventually, they stop drainage entirely.

Check the entire length of the drain hose. It runs from the back of the dishwasher to the drain connection under your sink. Look for any visible signs of trouble. This inspection should be done with the dishwasher unplugged. Make sure the area is clear and accessible.

### Straightening Kinks

Locating kinks in the drain hose is usually simple. Pull the dishwasher away from the wall slightly. This gives you better access to the back of the machine. Trace the drain hose from the dishwasher to its connection point. Look for any sharp bends or crimps. These are common culprits. They can happen if the dishwasher was pushed too far back against a wall.

Gently straighten any kinks you find. Ensure the hose has a smooth, unobstructed path. Give it some slack if it is too taut. This prevents future kinks. You might need to secure the hose properly to prevent it from kinking again. Use zip ties or clips if necessary. Make sure the hose is not pinched by other appliances or cabinets. A clear, straight path is vital for proper drainage.

Once straightened, run a test cycle. See if the E24 error clears. If it does, you have found your problem. If the error persists, the hose might have an internal blockage. This requires a different approach.

### Clearing Clogs in the Hose

If the hose is not kinked, it might have a clog. This is more difficult to address. You need to disconnect the drain hose from both ends. First, disconnect it from the dishwasher. This connection is usually at the back, near the bottom. Have a bucket ready. Some water will likely spill out. Next, disconnect the hose from the sink drain or garbage disposal. This connection is typically under the sink.

Once disconnected, inspect the hose ends for any obvious blockages. Use a flashlight to look inside. If you see debris, try to pull it out with pliers or a wire hanger. For deeper clogs, you can try flushing the hose. Take the hose outside or to a large sink. Run water through it to push out any buildup. You can also use a plumber's snake if the clog is stubborn. Do not use chemical drain cleaners. They can damage the hose or dishwasher components. After clearing, reattach the hose securely. Ensure all connections are tight. Then, test the dishwasher.

## Examining the Dishwasher's Sump and Drain Pump

If the filter is clean and the drain hose is clear, the problem might be with the dishwasher's internal components. The sump and drain pump are the next areas to inspect. The sump is the basin at the bottom of the dishwasher where water collects. The drain pump is responsible for forcing this water out through the drain hose. If either of these is blocked or faulty, you will get an E24 error.

A common issue in the sump area is broken glass or small, hard objects. These items can bypass the filter if it is not seated properly. They can then fall into the sump. From there, they can reach the drain pump impeller. The impeller is a small fan-like part. It spins rapidly to push water. If something jams the impeller, the pump cannot operate. This immediately triggers the E24 error.

Inspecting these parts requires a bit more effort. You may need to remove some internal components. Always disconnect power to the dishwasher before starting. Prepare for more water to spill. Have towels ready.

### Accessing the Drain Pump

Accessing the drain pump varies by Bosch model. In many cases, you must remove the lower spray arm and the filter assembly. You might also need to unscrew the pump cover. This cover is usually located at the bottom of the sump. It might be secured by a few screws. Consult your dishwasher's manual for specific instructions. Every model can be slightly different.

Once you have access, you will see the pump impeller. This is a small, paddle-like fan. It spins to move water. Feel around the impeller with your fingers. Wear gloves if you are concerned about sharp objects. Look for any foreign objects. This could be shards of glass, pieces of plastic, or even fruit pits. Be very careful. Sharp objects can cut you.

If you find any debris, carefully remove it. Use pliers or tweezers if it is stuck. Make sure the impeller can spin freely. If the impeller does not spin, or if it feels stiff, the pump might be damaged. This indicates a more serious problem.

### Checking for Blockages

Once you access the drain pump, you need to check for blockages. Even if the impeller seems free, debris can still be hidden. Look closely at the inlet and outlet ports of the pump. These are where water enters and exits the pump. Small food particles can accumulate in these areas. Grease buildup can also restrict flow. Use a flashlight for better visibility.

If you find any soft blockages, clear them out manually. Use a paper towel or a small brush. For hard-to-reach areas, a pipe cleaner can be helpful. Ensure there is no resistance when you spin the impeller by hand. It should move freely and smoothly. If the impeller is damaged, chipped, or broken, the pump will need replacement. This is a job for a professional.

After clearing any blockages, reassemble all components. Make sure the pump cover is sealed properly. Secure any screws. Reinstall the filter and spray arm. Then, reconnect power and run a test cycle. This will tell you if the drain pump blockage was the cause of your E24 error.

## Troubleshooting Your Kitchen Sink Siphon Connection and Air Gap

Sometimes, the E24 error is not caused by the dishwasher itself. It can originate from your kitchen sink's drainage system. This is especially true if your dishwasher drains into the sink's garbage disposal or a dedicated siphon connection. Two common external culprits are a blocked sink siphon connection or a clogged air gap. These components can prevent the dishwasher water from flowing out.

The sink siphon connection is where the dishwasher drain hose connects to your home's main drainage system. This is usually under the sink. It might connect directly to the garbage disposal unit or a dedicated drain pipe. If your sink is also draining slowly, this is a good indicator of a shared problem. Food particles or grease can build up here. This restricts flow from the dishwasher.

An air gap is a small cylindrical device on top of your sink. It is less common now, but some older homes or installations have them. The dishwasher drain hose connects to the air gap, and another hose runs from the air gap to the disposal or drain. The air gap prevents dirty water from flowing back into the dishwasher. If it gets clogged, it can also cause an E24 error.

### Clearing the Siphon Connection

To check the siphon connection, look under your kitchen sink. Locate where the dishwasher drain hose attaches. If it connects to a garbage disposal, ensure the knock-out plug inside the disposal was removed during installation. If this plug was not removed, water cannot pass through. This is a common oversight during new installations.

If the plug is confirmed as removed, the problem is likely a clog. Disconnect the dishwasher drain hose from the garbage disposal or drain pipe. Have a bucket ready to catch water. Use a screwdriver or pliers to clear any blockages at the connection point. You can also run water through the garbage disposal to flush it out. Make sure the disposal is clear and operating properly. If your sink has slow drainage, this area is a likely source of the problem.

Reattach the drain hose securely after clearing. Use a clamp if necessary to ensure a tight, leak-free connection. Then, run a test cycle on your dishwasher to see if the E24 error has gone away. This external connection is often overlooked.

### Inspecting the Air Gap

If your kitchen sink has an air gap, it is another potential source of the E24 error. The air gap is usually a small, chrome cylinder on your countertop near the faucet. It will have two hoses connected to it underneath the sink. One hose comes from the dishwasher. The other goes to the garbage disposal or drain.

To inspect the air gap, first, twist off its top cap on the countertop. You might need a small wrench. Inside, you will see a small chamber. This chamber can collect food debris or mineral buildup. Use a brush or a paperclip to clear out any visible obstructions. Make sure water can flow freely through both ports. You can also carefully disconnect the hoses from the bottom of the air gap, one at a time, to check for clogs inside the hoses themselves.

After clearing any blockages, reassemble the air gap. Ensure the hoses are securely attached. Replace the cap. Then, run a dishwasher test cycle. If your air gap was the problem, the E24 error should now be resolved. An air gap ensures proper drainage and prevents contamination. Keeping it clear is part of good dishwasher maintenance.

## Understanding Common E24 Error Triggers and Prevention

The E24 error on a Bosch dishwasher often points to drainage issues. While we have covered the common fixes, it is helpful to understand what triggers this error. Knowing the causes helps you prevent it from happening again. Most E24 errors are preventable through proper use and regular maintenance. I have seen many cases where simple habits make a big difference.

One major trigger is improper pre-rinsing of dishes. While Bosch dishwashers are efficient, they are not garbage disposals. Large food scraps, bones, or excessive grease can overload the filter. They can also pass through it and clog the pump. Another trigger is incorrect loading. Dishes blocking the spray arms can affect how water flows. This might not directly cause E24 but can contribute to debris buildup.

Ignoring the error is also a trigger for worse problems. Continuing to run the dishwasher with an E24 code can damage the pump. This turns a simple clog into a costly repair. Understanding prevention is key. It saves you time, money, and hassle in the long run.

### Proper Dishwasher Loading

How you load your dishwasher significantly impacts its drainage. Overloading the dishwasher is a common mistake. It restricts water flow. It also prevents proper rinsing of dishes. Food particles might not wash away effectively. They can then settle into the filter or sump, leading to clogs. Make sure you do not block the spray arms. These arms distribute water. If they cannot spin freely, dishes will not clean well, and debris might not be flushed away.

Always scrape large food scraps off plates before loading. You do not need to rinse dishes thoroughly, but remove solid waste. This reduces the burden on your dishwasher's filter system. Place large items on the bottom rack. Cups and glasses go on the top. Leave enough space between items. This allows water to circulate effectively. Proper loading reduces the chance of food bits causing an E24 error. It helps maintain the dishwasher's efficiency.

### Regular Maintenance Schedule

A regular maintenance schedule is the best prevention for E24 errors. The most important task is cleaning your dishwasher filter. I recommend doing this monthly, or even more often if you use your dishwasher daily. A clean filter is your first line of defense against clogs. This simple habit keeps your drain system running smoothly.

Beyond the filter, regularly check the drain hose for kinks. Do this every few months. Look under the sink to ensure the siphon connection or air gap remains clear. You can also run a dishwasher cleaner or a cup of white vinegar through an empty cycle periodically. This helps break down grease and mineral buildup. It keeps the internal components clean. Proactive maintenance extends the life of your appliance. It also prevents frustrating error codes like E24. By following these steps, you will enjoy a problem-free dishwasher for years.

## FAQ Section

### Q1: Can I run my Bosch dishwasher with an E24 error?

No, you should not run your Bosch dishwasher with an E24 error. This error indicates a drainage problem. Running the dishwasher could lead to water overflowing onto your kitchen floor. It could also cause damage to the drain pump or other internal components. Always address the E24 error before attempting to run another cycle.

### Q2: How often should I clean my dishwasher filter to prevent E24?

I recommend cleaning your Bosch dishwasher filter at least once a month. If you use your dishwasher frequently, or if you often wash dishes with a lot of food debris, you might need to clean it every two weeks. Regular cleaning prevents buildup and helps avoid the E24 error.

### Q3: Is E24 error a serious problem?

The E24 error is usually not a serious problem in itself. It commonly indicates a simple clog. However, if left unaddressed, it can become serious. Continued attempts to drain water with a blockage can damage the drain pump. This turns a minor issue into a more costly repair.

### Q4: What's the difference between E24 and E25 error?

The E24 error means a drainage problem, often due to a blockage. The E25 error, however, specifically indicates a problem with the drain pump itself. This could mean the pump is blocked or faulty. While both relate to drainage, E24 often points to clogs, and E25 points directly to the pump. For more details on the E25 error, you can check out [why does my Bosch dishwasher keep getting E25 error](https://homeessentialsguide.com/why-does-my-bosch-dishwasher-keep-getting-e25-error).

### Q5: Will resetting my dishwasher fix the E24 error?

Sometimes, a simple reset can resolve the E24 error if it is due to a temporary electronic glitch. Turn off the dishwasher, unplug it for a few minutes, then plug it back in. This often clears minor issues. However, if a physical blockage exists, resetting will not fix it. You will need to address the underlying cause.
