### Understanding Filtration Systems: HEPA and Activated Carbon in Budget Models

The core of any effective air purifier lies in its filtration system. Even the **best air purifier under 150** relies on powerful filters. You should focus on models that feature True HEPA and activated carbon filters. These two types work together to tackle a wide range of indoor pollutants. Knowing how they function helps you appreciate their value.

A True HEPA filter is your primary defense against particulate matter. This filter physically traps particles. It removes dust, pollen, pet dander, mold spores, and dust mite allergens. This is crucial for anyone with allergies or asthma. It means you breathe in fewer irritants. I always ensure a purifier has a True HEPA filter for maximum benefit. For more details, you can read about [what a HEPA filter is in an air purifier](https://homeessentialsguide.com/what-is-hepa-filter-air-purifier).

Activated carbon filters address gaseous pollutants and odors. They work through a process called adsorption. This means gas molecules stick to the surface of the carbon. Common odors from cooking, pets, smoke, and volatile organic compounds (VOCs) are absorbed. This leaves your air smelling fresh. The combination of these two filters creates a comprehensive cleaning system. You will find that [a carbon filter is good for an air purifier](https://homeessentialsguide.com/is-carbon-filter-good-for-air-purifier). It ensures both particulate and odor issues are handled.

Many budget models combine these filters into a single unit or a multi-layer filter. This design makes maintenance simpler. You usually replace one cartridge that contains all layers. Understanding these systems helps you choose wisely. It ensures your affordable purifier performs effectively.

### Room Size and Coverage: Matching Your Air Purifier to Your Space

Choosing the right size air purifier for your room is critical for its effectiveness. A small purifier in a large room will not clean the air properly. Similarly, a large purifier in a small room is often overkill and wastes energy. When looking for the **best air purifier under 150**, pay close attention to its Clean Air Delivery Rate (CADR). This metric helps you match the unit to your space.

CADR measures how fast an air purifier removes pollutants from a room. It provides numbers for smoke, pollen, and dust. A higher CADR means the purifier cleans air more quickly. For example, a CADR of 150 for smoke can clean a certain size room faster than one with a CADR of 100. Most manufacturers also list a recommended room size in square feet. This makes selection easier for you.

To find the right CADR, multiply the square footage of your room by two-thirds. This gives you a good target CADR. For example, a 150-square-foot room needs a CADR of at least 100. Always buy a purifier that matches or exceeds your room's requirements. This ensures the air changes enough times per hour. I always check the room size specifications carefully before buying. This simple step guarantees optimal performance. It prevents disappointment and ensures you get cleaner air.

### Easy Maintenance: Keeping Your Affordable Air Purifier Running Efficiently

Proper maintenance ensures your **best air purifier under 150** continues to perform well. The most important task is filter replacement. Air purifier filters, especially HEPA and carbon, have a limited lifespan. They become less effective as they collect pollutants. Most manufacturers recommend replacing HEPA filters every 6 to 12 months. Carbon pre-filters may need changing more often, perhaps every 2-3 months. You can learn more about [how often to change a HEPA filter in an air purifier](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-often-to-change-hepa-filter-air-purifier).

Replacing filters is usually a straightforward process. Check your specific model's manual for instructions. Most units have easily accessible filter compartments. Always use genuine replacement filters or high-quality compatible ones. This ensures the purifier works as intended. Ignoring filter changes can lead to decreased efficiency. It can also cause the purifier to work harder, consuming more energy.

Beyond filter replacement, regular cleaning of the exterior is important. Use a soft, dry cloth to wipe down the unit. Some pre-filters are washable. If your air purifier has a washable pre-filter, rinse it under cold water. Make sure it is completely dry before putting it back. However, be aware that [you generally cannot wash a HEPA filter on your air purifier](https://homeessentialsguide.com/can-you-wash-hepa-filter-air-purifier). Attempting to wash a non-washable HEPA filter will destroy its effectiveness. Following these simple maintenance steps keeps your purifier running optimally. It extends its lifespan and continues to provide you with clean air.

### Maximizing Your Investment: Tips for Optimal Air Purifier Performance

You have found the **best air purifier under 150**, now maximize its performance. Proper placement and usage habits significantly impact how well it cleans your air. I learned that small adjustments can make a big difference. You want the purifier to work as efficiently as possible for your clean air goals.

First, consider placement. Place your air purifier in the room where you spend the most time. Common locations include bedrooms or living rooms. Position the unit away from walls and furniture. Give it enough space for air to circulate freely around it. Do not block the air intake or output vents. Placing it in a corner or under a table can reduce its effectiveness. Always aim for an open area in the room.

Second, understand continuous operation. Many people wonder if they should run their air purifier all the time. For consistent air quality, continuous operation on a low to medium setting is best. This maintains a steady clean air level. If you only run it intermittently, pollutants can build up. However, if noise is a concern, use a timer or night mode when you are sleeping. Adjusting fan speeds based on air quality or activity also helps. For example, run it on high during cooking or after vacuuming. Then reduce the speed during quiet times.

Finally, keep doors and windows closed when the purifier is running. Opening them allows outdoor pollutants back in. This defeats the purpose of purification. Also, consider the impact of humidifiers. A humidifier releases water vapor into the air. This can sometimes affect the sensor of an air purifier. Ensure adequate spacing between the two devices. By following these simple tips, you ensure your affordable air purifier delivers the cleanest air possible. You will get the most out of your investment.
