## Washing Spanx: Can You Use A Washing Machine?

Spanx garments offer incredible support and a smooth silhouette. Many people love how they feel and look while wearing them. However, like any cherished item, Spanx requires proper care to maintain its shape and elasticity. A common question that comes up is: **Can you put Spanx in the washing machine?** Understanding the correct way to clean your shapewear is essential for its longevity.

It is easy to throw everything into the washing machine for convenience. Yet, some items, especially delicate ones, need special attention. Spanx, with its unique blend of materials, falls into this category. This article will help you understand if machine washing your Spanx is a good idea. We will also share the best methods for cleaning your shapewear. Proper care extends the life of your Spanx and keeps it performing its best. I want to help you keep your favorite shapewear in excellent condition for many years.

### Takeaway: Your Spanx Care Cheat Sheet

*   **Machine Wash (Carefully):** Yes, you can wash Spanx in a machine on a delicate or hand wash cycle.
*   **Cold Water Only:** Always use cold water to protect fabric elasticity.
*   **Mesh Laundry Bag:** Place Spanx inside a mesh bag to prevent snags and stretching.
*   **Mild Detergent:** Use a gentle, mild detergent, and use less of it.
*   **Air Dry:** Never put Spanx in the dryer; air dry flat or hang dry away from direct heat.

### A Quick Answer: Can You Put Spanx in the Washing Machine?

Yes, you can put Spanx in the washing machine, but with specific precautions. Machine washing is acceptable if you use a delicate or hand wash cycle with cold water, place the garment in a mesh laundry bag, and use a mild detergent. Avoid hot water, harsh chemicals, and the dryer at all costs to preserve your Spanx's elasticity and shape.

## Understanding Spanx Fabric and Construction

Spanx garments are not just ordinary pieces of clothing. They are designed with specific goals: to smooth, shape, and support. This means they are made from special fabrics. These fabrics give Spanx its unique properties. Knowing about these materials helps you understand why proper care is so important.

The main materials in most Spanx products are nylon and spandex, also known as elastane. Nylon provides a smooth, durable base. Spandex is the magic ingredient that gives Spanx its stretch and recovery. Spandex fibers are incredibly elastic. They can stretch many times their original length and then return to their shape. This elasticity is what creates the compression and shaping effect that Spanx is famous for. My own Spanx items have lasted years because I respect their fabric.

The construction of Spanx also plays a big role. These garments often feature seamless designs. They have targeted compression zones. Strong seams and specialized stitching hold them together. All of these elements work in harmony to give you a smooth look. When you wash Spanx, you need to protect these fibers and the garment's structure. Harsh washing conditions can damage the spandex fibers. This causes them to lose their stretch. They will not bounce back as effectively. This leads to your Spanx losing its shaping power. Over time, poor washing habits can make your favorite Spanx feel loose or saggy.

Traditional washing methods, like hot water or aggressive cycles, can be very harsh on these delicate materials. Hot water can break down the elastic fibers. Strong agitation can stretch and distort the fabric. This is why a gentle approach is necessary. You want to preserve the integrity of the fabric and its ability to shape. Treat your Spanx with care, and it will continue to provide the support you expect. Understanding the fabric helps you make better washing decisions.

## The Truth About Washing Spanx in a Machine

Many people wonder if they can machine wash their Spanx. The good news is, yes, you usually can. However, it is not as simple as just tossing them in with your regular laundry. Spanx are delicate items. They need special care, even in a washing machine. The key is to protect the fabric's elasticity.

First, always use a delicate or hand wash cycle. These cycles are much gentler than normal wash settings. They use less agitation. This protects the delicate fibers of your Spanx. High agitation can stretch out the elastic. This will make your Spanx lose its shape over time. I always set my machine to the gentlest cycle available.

Second, cold water is non-negotiable. Hot water is the enemy of spandex. It can break down the elastic fibers. This causes them to lose their stretch and rebound capabilities. Washing in cold water helps preserve the integrity of the fabric. This keeps your Spanx fitting snugly for longer. Using cold water also saves energy, which is a bonus. Understanding how your washing machine operates can help you choose the correct temperature and cycle. Third, a mesh laundry bag is your best friend. Always place your Spanx inside a mesh bag before putting it in the washing machine. This bag acts as a protective barrier. It prevents the Spanx from getting snagged on zippers, hooks, or rough textures from other clothes. It also reduces tangling. This stops the fabric from stretching during the spin cycle. I find that a good quality mesh bag is worth the small investment.

Finally, choose a mild detergent. Harsh detergents can contain chemicals that degrade elastic fibers over time. Look for detergents designed for delicates or natural fabrics. Use only a small amount. Too much soap can leave residue. This can make the fabric feel stiff. It can also lead to issues like excessive suds, which can be problematic for your machine. ## Step-by-Step Guide: Machine Washing Spanx Safely

Washing your Spanx in a machine can be safe and effective if you follow the right steps. This detailed guide ensures your shapewear gets clean without losing its integrity. I have used these steps myself for years. They work.

### Prepare Your Spanx

Before you even touch your washing machine, prepare your Spanx. This simple step makes a big difference.

*   **Check the Label:** First, always read the care label inside your Spanx garment. While my general advice applies to most Spanx, specific products may have unique instructions. The manufacturer's label is the ultimate guide.
*   **Pre-Treat Stains (If Any):** If your Spanx has any visible stains, pre-treat them gently. Use a small amount of mild detergent or a stain remover for delicates. Apply it directly to the stain. Gently rub it with your fingers or a soft cloth. Do not scrub harshly, as this can damage the fabric. Let it sit for a few minutes before washing.
*   **Place in a Mesh Laundry Bag:** This is a crucial step. Put your Spanx inside a mesh laundry bag. This bag protects the delicate fabric from stretching, snagging, and tangling with other clothes. It also reduces friction during the wash cycle. Buy a good quality mesh bag. It will save your delicates.

### Select the Right Settings

Your washing machine has many settings. Choosing the correct ones for Spanx is important.

*   **Delicate or Hand Wash Cycle:** Set your washing machine to the "delicate" or "hand wash" cycle. These cycles have less agitation. They are much gentler on the fabric. This prevents stretching and damage to the elastic fibers.
*   **Cold Water Temperature:** Select cold water only. Hot water can break down the elastic in your Spanx. This causes it to lose its shape and compression. Cold water helps preserve the fabric's integrity. It keeps your Spanx supportive for longer.

### Choose Your Detergent

The type and amount of detergent you use are also key.

*   **Mild Detergent:** Use a mild, gentle detergent. Avoid harsh chemicals, bleach, or fabric softeners. Harsh detergents can degrade the elastic fibers. Fabric softeners can leave a residue that clogs the fibers. This reduces their elasticity and breathability.
*   **Use Less Detergent:** You only need a small amount of detergent for delicate items like Spanx. Too much detergent can create excessive suds. It also leaves residue on the fabric. For guidance on appropriate detergents for cleaning delicate items, you may find helpful information in this resource on [what to put in a washing machine to clean it](https://homeessentialsguide.com/what-to-put-in-a-washing-machine-to-clean-it).

### Load the Machine

How you load your machine affects the wash.

*   **Separate from Heavy Items:** Wash Spanx with other lightweight delicates only. Never wash them with heavy items like jeans, towels, or items with zippers or hooks. These can snag and damage the delicate fabric of your Spanx, even inside a mesh bag.
*   **Do Not Overload:** Make sure your washing machine is not too full. An overloaded machine does not clean well. It can also put stress on delicate items. Leave enough space for items to move freely in the water. For more insights on efficient loading, check out this article on [how full washing machine should be](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-full-washing-machine-should-be).

Following these detailed steps ensures your Spanx stays in great condition. You protect your investment by giving your shapewear the care it needs. This means it will continue to provide the smooth look you love.

## The Gentle Alternative: Hand Washing Your Spanx

While machine washing Spanx is possible with care, hand washing remains the gold standard for preserving delicate garments. It offers the most control and gentleness. Hand washing extends the life of your Spanx significantly. I personally prefer hand washing my most cherished Spanx pieces.

Hand washing minimizes stress on the fabric. There is no harsh agitation from a machine. This reduces the risk of stretching or snagging. You can also target specific areas if needed. This method is especially good for Spanx that is very expensive or particularly delicate. It ensures the elastic fibers stay strong and supportive for as long as possible.

Here is a simple, step-by-step guide to hand washing your Spanx:

*   **Fill a Basin with Cold Water:** Find a clean sink or basin. Fill it with cool or cold water. Warm or hot water can damage the elastic in your Spanx. Always stick to cold water for best results.
*   **Add a Mild Detergent:** Add a very small amount of mild liquid detergent to the water. A capful is usually plenty. Swish the water around gently with your hand to mix the detergent. Make sure it dissolves completely. Avoid harsh soaps or powders. They can be difficult to rinse out.
*   **Submerge and Soak:** Place your Spanx garment into the soapy water. Gently push it down until it is fully submerged. Let it soak for about 10 to 15 minutes. This allows the detergent to loosen any dirt or body oils. Do not leave it soaking for too long.
*   **Gently Agitate:** After soaking, gently swirl the Spanx in the water. You can also lightly squeeze the garment to work the soapy water through the fabric. Do not twist, wring, or scrub the Spanx. Harsh movements can stretch and damage the elastic. Focus on gentle compression and release.
*   **Rinse Thoroughly:** Drain the soapy water from the basin. Refill it with clean, cold water. Submerge your Spanx again. Gently press and squeeze the garment to rinse out all the soap. You may need to change the water a few times until no suds appear. Ensure all detergent is gone. Leftover soap can make the fabric stiff. It can also irritate your skin.
*   **Remove Excess Water:** Once rinsed, do not wring your Spanx. Instead, gently press the garment against the side of the basin or between two clean towels. This removes excess water. You want to get it damp, not dripping wet. Be very careful not to twist or pull the fabric. This preserves its shape.

Hand washing is a bit more time-consuming than machine washing. But it is worth the extra effort for your Spanx. It helps maintain its original shape, elasticity, and comfort. This keeps your shapewear feeling new for a long time.

## Drying Spanx: Air Dry is Always Best

After washing your Spanx, the drying process is just as important as the washing process. This is where many people make a critical mistake. The golden rule for drying Spanx is simple: **Always air dry.** Avoid machine dryers at all costs. I cannot stress this enough.

Heat is the absolute enemy of elastic fibers. Spanx relies heavily on spandex (elastane) for its shaping and supportive qualities. When exposed to high heat from a dryer, these delicate elastic fibers break down. They lose their ability to stretch and return to their original shape. This means your Spanx will become baggy, saggy, and lose its compression. It will no longer provide the smooth silhouette you bought it for. It can also cause the fabric to become brittle or rough.

Here are the best ways to air dry your Spanx effectively:

*   **Lay Flat:** The best method is to lay your Spanx flat on a clean, dry towel. Reshape the garment gently to its original form. This prevents stretching or distortion as it dries. Laying it flat ensures even drying and supports the fabric's structure. Change the towel if it becomes too damp. This speeds up the drying process.
*   **Hang Dry (with care):** If space is limited, you can hang your Spanx. Use a padded hanger to avoid creases or marks. Be careful not to hang it from a thin strap. This can stretch the shoulder straps or waistbands over time due to the weight of the damp garment. A better option is to hang it over a shower rod or a drying rack. Ensure the entire garment is supported. Place it away from direct sunlight or heat sources. Direct sun can fade colors and still cause heat damage.
*   **Ensure Good Airflow:** Choose a well-ventilated area for drying. Good airflow helps the Spanx dry more quickly. Avoid damp or humid environments. These can lead to mildew or a musty smell. A fan can help circulate air.
*   **Be Patient:** Air drying takes time. It might take several hours, or even overnight, depending on humidity and airflow. Do not rush the process. Make sure the Spanx is completely dry before storing it. Storing a damp garment can lead to mildew growth and damage.

Never iron your Spanx either. The high heat from an iron will cause the same damage as a machine dryer. It can also scorch the delicate fabric. By committing to air drying, you protect your investment. You ensure your Spanx maintains its elasticity, shape, and supportive power for a long time.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid When Cleaning Spanx

Even with the best intentions, it is easy to make mistakes when cleaning delicate items like Spanx. Avoiding these common errors ensures your shapewear lasts longer and performs better. I have learned these lessons the hard way, so you do not have to.

*   **Using Hot Water:** This is perhaps the biggest mistake. Hot water damages the elastic fibers in Spanx. It causes them to break down and lose their stretch. Always use cold water for both machine and hand washing.
*   **Using Harsh Detergents or Bleach:** Regular detergents, especially those with strong chemicals, can be too aggressive for delicate fabrics. Bleach is an absolute no-go. It will weaken and discolor the fabric. Stick to mild, gentle detergents designed for delicates.
*   **Adding Fabric Softener:** While it might seem like a good idea for softness, fabric softener can be detrimental to Spanx. It leaves a residue on the fibers. This residue can clog the fabric's pores. It reduces its breathability and elasticity. It can also make the Spanx feel sticky or slimy over time.
*   **Machine Drying:** As discussed, machine dryers are the arch-nemesis of Spanx. The high heat destroys the elastic. This leads to permanent loss of shape and compression. Always air dry your Spanx.
*   **Washing with Rough Items:** Tossing Spanx in with jeans, towels, or items with zippers, hooks, or rough embellishments is a recipe for disaster. Even in a mesh bag, these items can snag, tear, or stretch the delicate fabric. Always wash Spanx with other lightweight delicates.
*   **Overloading the Washing Machine:** A crammed washing machine does not allow items to clean properly. It also creates more friction and agitation. This can stress delicate fabrics. Give your Spanx room to move gently in the water. Knowing [how full a washing machine should be](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-full-washing-machine-should-be) can help prevent damage to your garments.
*   **Wringing or Twisting Excess Water:** After washing, resist the urge to wring or twist your Spanx to remove water. This violent motion can permanently distort the garment's shape. Gently press out excess water instead.
*   **Ironing:** Never iron Spanx. The direct heat will melt or scorch the elastic fibers. This causes irreversible damage. If you need to smooth out wrinkles, a garment steamer on a very low setting might be an option. But generally, air drying flat prevents most wrinkles.
*   **Over-Washing:** Washing your Spanx too often can also shorten its lifespan. Unless it is visibly dirty or sweaty, you do not need to wash it after every wear. Often, airing it out after use is enough. Consider washing it every 2-3 wears, depending on how much you sweat.

By being mindful of these common mistakes, you can significantly prolong the life and effectiveness of your Spanx. Proper care is an investment in your comfort and appearance.

## Extending the Life of Your Spanx: Beyond Washing

Proper washing and drying are foundational to Spanx care. Yet, maintaining the longevity of your shapewear involves more than just laundry day habits. How you store, wear, and even maintain your washing machine can impact its lifespan. I have found these extra steps truly make a difference.

### Smart Storage Solutions

How you store your Spanx between wears matters. Incorrect storage can lead to stretching or damage.

*   **Fold Neatly or Roll:** Do not just wad up your Spanx and toss it into a drawer. Gently fold it flat or roll it. This preserves its shape and prevents creases.
*   **Avoid Over-Stuffing:** Store Spanx in a drawer or bin where it has enough room. Over-stuffing can compress the fabric and cause it to lose its elasticity over time.
*   **Separate from Sharp Objects:** Keep Spanx away from items with zippers, hooks, or sharp edges that could snag the delicate fabric.

### Mindful Wearing Habits

Even how you put on and take off your Spanx can affect its durability.

*   **Roll It On:** Instead of pulling Spanx up like regular underwear, roll it up like pantyhose. Step into it, then slowly unroll it up your legs and torso. This minimizes stress on the seams and elastic.
*   **Avoid Rings and Nails:** Be cautious of rings, jewelry, or long fingernails when handling Spanx. These can easily snag the fabric and create runs.
*   **Choose the Right Size:** Wearing Spanx that is too small can put excessive stress on the seams and fabric. This leads to premature wear and tear. Always choose the correct size for a comfortable and effective fit.

### Frequency of Washing

You do not always need to wash your Spanx after every single wear. Over-washing can degrade the fabric.

*   **Assess After Each Wear:** If you wore your Spanx for a short period and did not sweat much, simply airing it out might be enough. Hang it in a well-ventilated area for a few hours.
*   **Wash When Needed:** If you wore it for an extended period, especially in warm weather, or if you sweated, then it is time for a wash. Generally, washing every 2-3 wears is a good rule of thumb.

### Washing Machine Maintenance

A clean washing machine is vital for clean clothes, especially delicates. Residue or grime in your machine can transfer to your Spanx.

*   **Regular Cleaning:** Periodically clean your washing machine. This removes detergent residue, mold, and mildew. A clean machine ensures your Spanx comes out truly clean and fresh. Many guides exist to help with this. For example, you can learn [how to clean an LG washing machine](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-clean-an-lg-washing-machine) or other brands.
*   **Check for Snags:** Before each delicate load, quickly check the inside of your washing machine drum for any rough spots or burrs that could snag fabric. If you find any, they may need repair. Understanding how to perform basic [do it yourself washing machine](https://homeessentialsguide.com/do-it-yourself-washing-machine) maintenance can prolong the life of both your machine and your garments.

By incorporating these practices into your routine, you ensure your Spanx maintains its quality. Your favorite shapewear will continue to provide comfort and confidence for many years. It is about treating your investment with the respect it deserves.

## Frequently Asked Questions About Spanx Care

Here are answers to common questions about cleaning and maintaining your Spanx. These insights will help you further extend the life of your shapewear.

### How often should I wash my Spanx?

You do not need to wash Spanx after every single wear. If you wore it for a short time and did not sweat, airing it out is often enough. For longer wears or when you sweat, wash it. A good general rule is to wash your Spanx every 2-3 wears. Over-washing can break down the elastic fibers over time.

### Can I use fabric softener on Spanx?

No, you should avoid using fabric softener on Spanx. Fabric softener leaves a residue on the elastic fibers. This residue can clog the fabric. It reduces its breathability and elasticity. It can also make the Spanx feel sticky or less effective over time. Stick to mild detergents only.

### What if my Spanx loses its shape after washing?

If your Spanx loses its shape after washing, it is usually due to incorrect washing or drying methods. This often happens from using hot water, harsh detergents, or machine drying. Once the elastic fibers are damaged by heat, they cannot fully recover. Always wash in cold water on a delicate cycle and air dry to maintain its shape.

### Is it okay to wash Spanx with other delicates?

Yes, it is perfectly fine, and even recommended, to wash Spanx with other delicates. Make sure these other items are also lightweight and do not have zippers, hooks, or rough embellishments that could snag the Spanx. Always place your Spanx in a mesh laundry bag to provide an extra layer of protection during the wash cycle.

### Do all Spanx products have the same washing instructions?

While most Spanx products, especially those made primarily of nylon and spandex, follow the same general delicate care guidelines (cold wash, air dry), it is always best to check the specific care label on each garment. Some specialized Spanx items, like those with lace, embellishments, or different fabric blends, might have slightly varied instructions. The care label is your definitive guide.
