## Unboxing Your Ooni Oven: What to Expect First

Opening a new Ooni pizza oven box is exciting. The first step is to carefully remove all packaging materials. I always lay everything out on a clean, flat surface. This helps me see all the parts clearly. Make sure to keep the manual handy, as it lists every component.

Your Ooni oven box typically contains the main oven body, folding legs, a pizza stone, and a fuel burner. Depending on your model, you might find a gas hose and regulator, or a fuel tray for wood pellets or charcoal. Some models also include a chimney or a door. Double-check everything against the packing list in your manual. I find it useful to identify each part before I start assembly. This prevents confusion later on.

Handle the pizza stone with care. It is fragile and can break if dropped or bumped hard. If any part seems damaged, contact Ooni support before you proceed. Having all parts visible makes the assembly process smooth and efficient. It also helps you confirm nothing is missing from your new oven.

## Assembling the Ooni Legs and Body Components

Assembling the Ooni oven typically starts with the legs. Most Ooni models feature folding legs that simply snap into place. You will unfold them until they lock securely. Ensure each leg is fully extended and stable before moving on. This provides a solid base for your oven.

Next, you will position the main oven body. Gently lift the oven body and place it onto the extended legs. Some models might have specific grooves or attachments for alignment. Make sure the oven body sits firmly on the leg structure. It should not rock or feel unstable. Always check the manual for your specific Ooni model. Each model, whether it's an Ooni Koda, Fyra, or Karu, might have slight variations in how the legs attach.

For models with a chimney, this is usually the next step. The chimney often twists or slides into a designated slot on the top of the oven. Ensure it is securely locked in place to prevent it from falling during operation. A secure chimney allows for proper airflow and smoke ventilation. Take your time with these steps. A well-assembled base means a safe and effective cooking experience.

## Connecting Your Ooni to Fuel: Gas, Wood, or Charcoal

Connecting your Ooni oven to its fuel source is a critical step. If you have a gas-powered Ooni, like the Ooni Koda, you will connect the gas hose and regulator to a propane tank. First, attach the regulator to the gas inlet on the back of your Ooni. Tighten it by hand until it's snug. Then, connect the other end of the hose to your propane tank. I always ensure all connections are tight.

After connecting, perform a leak test using soapy water. Apply a solution of soap and water to all gas connections. Turn on the gas supply for a moment. If you see bubbles, you have a leak. Turn off the gas immediately and re-tighten the connections. Repeat the test until no bubbles appear. This safety check is very important. ## First Fire-Up and Curing Your Ooni Oven

Once your Ooni oven is fully assembled and connected to its fuel source, it is time for the exciting first fire-up. This initial burn is known as "curing" the oven. Curing helps burn off any manufacturing oils or residues. It also acclimates the pizza stone to high temperatures. You will light your Ooni as you normally would for cooking. Our guide on [how to light an Ooni pizza oven](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-light-an-ooni-pizza-oven) provides detailed steps for various models.

Allow your Ooni oven to run at its highest temperature for at least 30 minutes. This process makes the oven ready for food. You will notice some smoke or a slight odor during this initial burn. This is normal and will dissipate. Ensure the area is well-ventilated. Do not cook any food during this curing period. The goal is to get the oven very hot. Your oven is now seasoned and ready for making delicious pizzas. This curing step is essential for the oven's longevity and performance. ## FAQ Section

### How long does it take to put an Ooni pizza oven together?
Assembling an Ooni pizza oven usually takes 10 to 30 minutes. The time depends on the specific model and your familiarity with its components. Gas models might take slightly longer due to hose and regulator connection. Wood-fired models are often quicker to set up.

### Do Ooni ovens come pre-assembled?
No, Ooni ovens do not come pre-assembled. They require some basic assembly upon arrival. This typically involves attaching legs, inserting the pizza stone, and connecting the fuel source. The assembly process is designed to be simple and user-friendly for everyone.

### Can I use my Ooni oven right after assembly?
It is recommended to perform a "curing" burn after assembly. This involves running the oven at its highest temperature for 30 minutes. This process burns off manufacturing residues and prepares the pizza stone. After curing and cooling, your Ooni is ready for cooking.

### What tools do I need to assemble an Ooni?
Most Ooni pizza ovens require no special tools for assembly. Components usually click or slide into place. For gas models, a wrench might be helpful to tighten the regulator connection, though hand-tightening is often sufficient. Always refer to your specific model's manual.

### How do I know if my Ooni oven is assembled correctly?
A correctly assembled Ooni oven will be stable and level. All legs should be locked, and the pizza stone should sit flat inside. For gas ovens, perform a leak test with soapy water to ensure all connections are secure and bubble-free. The chimney, if present, should also be firmly attached.

### What should I do if a part is missing or damaged?
If you find a missing or damaged part when unboxing your Ooni, do not proceed with assembly. Contact Ooni customer support immediately. They can help arrange for replacement parts. Have your purchase details and model number ready for a faster resolution.
