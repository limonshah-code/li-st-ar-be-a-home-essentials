### Lull's Commitment to CertiPUR-US Certification

When talking about mattress safety, CertiPUR-US certification is a key point. This certification matters a lot for foam products, including Lull mattresses. It assures consumers that the foam meets strict standards for content, emissions, and durability. I always look for this label myself.

CertiPUR-US means the foam is made without ozone depleters. It also ensures the foam is free from certain flame retardants like PBDEs, TDCPP, or TCEP. Heavy metals, such as mercury and lead, are absent from certified foams. Furthermore, CertiPUR-US foams contain no formaldehyde. Low VOCs contribute to better indoor air quality.

Lull explicitly states that its mattress foams are CertiPUR-US certified. This commitment shows they prioritize consumer health and safety regarding foam components. This certification is a strong indicator that the Lull mattress is free from many chemicals people worry about. Choosing a mattress with this certification offers a measure of confidence. ### Addressing Off-Gassing Concerns with Lull Mattresses

Many people worry about a "new mattress smell" when they unbox a foam mattress. This smell is often called off-gassing. Off-gassing happens when Volatile Organic Compounds (VOCs) are released from the mattress materials into the air. It is a common occurrence with new foam products. Understanding what causes it and if it is harmful helps to ease concerns.

The foams used in mattresses, even CertiPUR-US certified ones, can release a temporary odor. These VOCs are usually harmless at the low levels found in certified mattresses. CertiPUR-US certification sets limits on these emissions. This means the VOCs released from a Lull mattress are much lower than from uncertified foams. The smell is often described as chemical-like or similar to a new car. It typically fades within a few days.

To minimize the effects of off-gassing, I always recommend proper ventilation. When you first unbox your Lull mattress, place it in a well-ventilated room. Open windows and doors to allow fresh air to circulate. You might even let the mattress air out for 24 to 72 hours before sleeping on it. This simple step helps dissipate any lingering odors quickly. While the smell can be noticeable, it rarely indicates harmful levels of chemicals with certified foams. The initial aroma usually clears up, leaving a fresh sleep surface.
