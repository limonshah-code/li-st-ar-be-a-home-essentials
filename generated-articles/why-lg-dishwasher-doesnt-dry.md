Some LG dishwashers also feature specialized drying options. Dynamic Dry uses a fan to improve air circulation. Night Dry allows for a longer, quieter drying period. ### Rinse Aid: The Unsung Hero of Drying

Rinse aid is perhaps the most overlooked component for proper dishwasher drying. It is not just for shine; it is crucial for water removal. Rinse aid reduces the surface tension of water. This causes water to sheet off dishes rather than forming droplets. Droplets leave spots and take longer to dry.

If your LG dishwasher is not drying dishes well, check the rinse aid dispenser first. It is usually located near the detergent dispenser inside the dishwasher door. Most dispensers have an indicator to show the rinse aid level. If the level is low or empty, refill it. I always keep my rinse aid topped up.

Using the correct type and amount of rinse aid is also important. Always use a rinse aid specifically designed for dishwashers. Do not use dish soap. Adjust the rinse aid dispenser setting if your dishes are still wet or have streaks. Your dishwasher manual explains how to change this setting. A higher setting dispenses more rinse aid.

Many LG owners wonder how to properly add this crucial liquid. It is a simple process once you know the steps. The rinse aid cap usually twists or pulls open. Just pour the liquid into the dispenser until it reaches the "max" fill line. Be careful not to overfill it. ### Loading Dishes Correctly for Optimal Drying

How you load your dishwasher has a big impact on drying. Proper loading allows hot air and rinse aid to reach all surfaces. It also allows water to drain off dishes easily. Incorrect loading can block spray arms or create water traps. This leaves dishes wet, even with a perfect cycle.

Avoid overcrowding the dishwasher. Overfilling traps moisture and prevents proper air circulation. Leave space between items. Make sure larger items do not block the spray arms. These arms distribute water and aid in drying. If they cannot spin freely, cleaning and drying both suffer.

Place dishes so water can drain off them. Cups, bowls, and other concave items should face downwards. This prevents water from pooling in them. Angle plates slightly if possible. This helps water run off. Tines in the racks are designed to hold dishes at the correct angle.

Consider the material of your dishes when loading. Plastic items often do not dry as well as glass or ceramic. Place plastic items on the top rack. This keeps them away from the heating element in some models. The lower rack gets hotter. This helps prevent melting or warping.

Ensuring dishes are loaded correctly also helps with cleaning. If water cannot reach all surfaces, dirt remains. Wet dishes that are also dirty are doubly frustrating. Proper loading is a foundational step for both cleaning and drying. It helps avoid many common dishwasher issues.

### Selecting the Right Drying Cycle and Options

Your LG dishwasher offers different wash cycles and drying options. Choosing the right one is crucial for good drying results. Some cycles are specifically designed for better drying. Others prioritize energy savings, which may result in less dry dishes.

Most LG dishwashers have a "Heat Dry" or "Extra Dry" option. Always select this if you want maximum dryness. These options extend the drying phase or use more heat. They can add to the cycle time and energy use. However, they are very effective for getting dishes completely dry.

The "Energy Saver" or "Eco" cycles often reduce drying time or heat. This saves energy but can leave dishes wetter. If you use these cycles, expect some moisture. Consider air-drying dishes by slightly opening the door after the cycle. This helps evaporation.

Some models have an "Auto" or "Sensor Dry" setting. These settings detect how dirty or wet the dishes are. They adjust the cycle automatically. While convenient, they might not always provide the absolute driest results. If you consistently find wet dishes, try a specific "Heat Dry" or "Extra Dry" setting instead.

I find that using the "Sanitize" option often helps with drying. It uses very hot water during the rinse cycle. The extra heat helps dishes dry faster through condensation. ### Addressing Water Temperature and Supply Issues

Hot water is essential for effective dishwasher cleaning and drying. Dishwashers do not heat water from cold as much as you might think. They rely on your home's hot water supply. If the incoming water is not hot enough, dishes will not dry properly.

The water should be at least 120°F (49°C) for optimal performance. You can test your hot water temperature at the kitchen sink. Let the hot water run for a minute. Then, use a meat thermometer to check the temperature. If it is too low, adjust your water heater thermostat. This often resolves drying issues.

Make sure your dishwasher is connected to the hot water line. Sometimes, during installation, a cold water connection is mistakenly used. This is rare but worth checking if you bought a new home or had recent plumbing work. Cold water simply cannot provide the heat needed for drying.

Running the hot water at your kitchen sink before starting the dishwasher also helps. This flushes cold water from the pipes. It ensures the dishwasher fills with hot water right away. This simple step can make a big difference, especially in colder climates. It ensures the wash cycle starts with the necessary heat.

Insufficient water pressure can also indirectly affect drying. If water pressure is too low, spray arms may not distribute water and rinse aid effectively. This leads to poor rinsing. Poor rinsing means more water spots and less effective drying. Ensure your home's water pressure is within normal limits.

### Maintenance Matters: Filters, Spray Arms, and Vents

Regular maintenance keeps your LG dishwasher working its best. Clogged filters, blocked spray arms, or a faulty vent can all impact drying performance. Ignoring these components can lead to dishes staying wet.

**Dishwasher Filter:** Most LG dishwashers have a filter at the bottom of the tub. This filter catches food particles. If it gets clogged, water cannot drain properly. This leaves dirty water on dishes, which then struggles to dry. Remove and clean the filter regularly. Most manuals recommend cleaning it once a month. To clean it, simply twist and lift it out. Rinse it under running water to remove debris.

**Spray Arms:** The spray arms have small holes that jet water onto dishes. If these holes get clogged with food particles or mineral deposits, water distribution suffers. This means rinse aid and hot water do not reach all surfaces. Check the spray arms for blockages. Use a toothpick or small wire to clear any clogged holes. Spin them manually to ensure they rotate freely.

**Drying Vent:** Some LG dishwashers have a vent that opens during the drying cycle. This vent releases moist air from the tub. If the vent or its fan is blocked or faulty, humid air gets trapped. This prevents dishes from drying. Check for physical obstructions around the vent. The vent is usually located on the side or top of the dishwasher door.

Regular cleaning of your dishwasher is not just for hygiene. It directly affects its performance. A clean dishwasher cleans and dries better. Food debris and hard water buildup reduce efficiency. ### Troubleshooting Heating Element and Vent Fan Problems

If you have checked the basics (rinse aid, loading, cycles, maintenance) and your LG dishwasher still does not dry, you might have a component issue. The heating element or the vent fan are common culprits. These parts play a direct role in the drying process.

**Heating Element:** In models with a visible heating element, it should get hot during the drying cycle. Carefully open the dishwasher after the wash cycle ends. Touch the bottom of the tub or the heating element (be careful, it can be hot!). If it is cold, the element might be faulty. A faulty heating element needs replacement. This is usually a job for a professional. It requires testing with a multimeter to confirm the fault.

**Vent Fan:** Newer LG models use a vent fan in combination with condensation drying. This fan expels humid air from the tub. If the fan motor fails or the vent is stuck closed, moisture stays inside. You might hear a buzzing sound if the fan is trying to run but is blocked. Or, you might hear nothing at all if the motor is completely dead. Checking the vent fan often requires disassembling part of the door or control panel. This also usually requires a professional.

Before calling a technician, you can try resetting your dishwasher. Sometimes, a simple power cycle can clear minor electronic glitches. Unplug the dishwasher from the wall outlet for a few minutes. Then, plug it back in. This can sometimes resolve issues with sensors or control board errors.

If you suspect a part failure, it is best to consult your LG dishwasher manual. It provides specific troubleshooting steps for your model. ### Understanding Dishware Materials and Their Drying Behavior

The material of your dishes significantly affects how well they dry in a dishwasher. Different materials absorb and release heat differently. This impacts the effectiveness of both heat-dry and condensation-dry systems. Understanding these differences can help you manage expectations and optimize loading.

**Plastics:** Plastic is the most challenging material to dry in a dishwasher. It does not retain heat well. This means that in condensation drying systems, plastic items remain cooler. Water vapor does not condense on them as easily. They tend to come out with more water droplets or a lingering dampness. If you have many plastic items, you might need to manually dry them or choose an "Extra Dry" setting. Placing them on the top rack is often recommended as it is typically cooler, preventing warping while still receiving some drying benefit.

**Glass:** Glass items generally dry very well. Glass retains heat effectively from the wash and rinse cycles. This heat allows water to evaporate quickly. It also encourages condensation on the cooler dishwasher walls, drawing moisture away from the glass. You should still use rinse aid for spot-free drying on glass. Without it, water spots can be very visible.

**Ceramics and Porcelain:** Dishes made from ceramic or porcelain also tend to dry well. Like glass, these materials absorb and retain heat efficiently. This aids in the evaporation process. However, some ceramic items, especially those with unglazed bottoms or rims, might retain a little moisture. This is due to the porous nature of the material in those specific areas.

**Stainless Steel:** Stainless steel items, like cutlery or specific bowls, usually dry very well. They conduct and retain heat. This allows for effective water evaporation. Ensure they are not nested together to allow water and heat to reach all surfaces. Nesting can create water traps.

**Wood and Non-Dishwasher Safe Items:** Items made of wood or cast iron should never go into a dishwasher. They are not designed to withstand the high temperatures and harsh detergents. This also applies to delicate crystal or hand-painted items. These materials can warp, crack, or lose their finish. This is not a drying issue, but an important consideration for what goes into your dishwasher. Knowing how different materials behave helps troubleshoot what you can expect from your LG dishwasher's drying performance.

### FAQ Section

#### Why are my plastic items still wet?

Plastic does not hold heat well compared to glass or ceramic. In condensation drying dishwashers, this means water does not evaporate from plastic easily. Use an "Extra Dry" setting or a higher rinse aid dose. You may also need to hand dry plastic items sometimes.

#### Does my LG dishwasher always use heat dry?

Not all LG dishwashers use a constant heat dry. Many modern LG models use condensation drying, which relies on residual heat and rinse aid. A separate "Heat Dry" or "Extra Dry" option often needs manual selection. Check your model's specific drying technology and cycle options.

#### How often should I add rinse aid?

The frequency depends on how often you run your dishwasher and the rinse aid setting. For daily use, you might need to refill the dispenser every 2-4 weeks. The rinse aid indicator light on your control panel will tell you when it is low. Always check it when dishes are not drying well.

#### Can a clogged drain affect drying?

Yes, a clogged drain or filter can indirectly affect drying. If water does not drain completely, dirty water can sit at the bottom of the tub. This moisture can re-deposit on dishes, making them appear wet and spotted. A clear drain ensures all water is removed.

#### When should I call a technician for drying issues?

Call a technician if you have tried all common troubleshooting steps and your dishes are still wet. This includes checking rinse aid, loading, cycles, and cleaning the filter. If you suspect a faulty heating element, vent fan, or control board, a professional can diagnose and repair it safely.

#### Is it normal for some water to remain on dishes?

For modern, energy-efficient dishwashers, especially those with condensation drying, it can be normal for a small amount of moisture to remain. This is particularly true on plastic items or in crevices. Perfectly bone-dry dishes are less common with these systems compared to older, high-heat models.
