### What is the FD number on a Bosch dishwasher?

## Decoding the FD Number on Your Bosch Dishwasher

Many people confuse the FD number with a serial number. Let me clarify this point right away. The FD number is a production code, not a unique serial identifier for your specific unit. It simply tells you when your dishwasher was made. It acts like a timestamp for the factory.

Bosch uses this code for internal tracking purposes. It helps them manage product batches. This allows them to identify products from certain manufacturing periods. If there are minor changes in components, the FD number helps pinpoint them. It is a very practical system for Bosch.

The FD number usually appears as a four-digit code. You might see numbers like "8903" or "1507." These digits have a specific meaning, which we will explain soon. This code helps to tell the age of your dishwasher. It gives you an idea of its production life.

Understanding this number is simple once you know the pattern. It is a straightforward way to date your appliance. This insight becomes very useful for various reasons. It links directly to the history of your machine.

## Locating the FD Number on Your Bosch Dishwasher

Finding your Bosch dishwasher's FD number is usually easy. Bosch places this label in a few common spots. I will guide you through where to look. Always ensure good lighting when you search for it. Sometimes, the label can be hard to read.

Most often, you will find the label inside the dishwasher door. Open the door fully. Look along the top edge of the door. Check the side edges or the inner panel of the door as well. It is often a white or silver sticker. The numbers are usually printed clearly on it.

Another place to check is on the side of the dishwasher tub. This is the inner metal or plastic part where dishes go. Sometimes, the label is near the hinges. It might be on the right or left side. Again, it is usually a sticker with codes.

A less common spot is behind the kick plate. This is the removable panel at the bottom front of the dishwasher. You might need a screwdriver to take it off. This location is less likely but worth checking if you cannot find it elsewhere. Make sure the dishwasher is off before you do this. Take a moment to look carefully.

Do not confuse the FD number with the model or serial number. These are also on labels, but they serve different purposes. The FD number typically starts with "FD" or is listed next to it. Take a clear photo of the label once you find it. This saves you from searching again later.

## Interpreting the Bosch FD Number's Date Code

Once you find the FD number, reading it is straightforward. This number is always a four-digit code. It tells you the exact month and year your dishwasher was manufactured. Let me show you how to decode it.

The first two digits represent the year of manufacture. For instance, if you see "89," it means 1989. If you see "03," it means 2003. A code like "15" stands for 2015. Bosch uses the last two digits of the year. This makes the code compact and easy to read.

The last two digits indicate the month of manufacture. This part is also simple. "01" means January, "02" means February, and so on. "12" would represent December. It is a standard numerical representation of months.

Let's use an example to make this clear. Imagine your Bosch dishwasher has an FD number of "8903." The "89" tells us the year is 1989. The "03" tells us the month is March. So, your dishwasher was manufactured in March 1989. Another example: "FD 1507" means July 2015.

This system is clear and consistent across Bosch appliances. It avoids confusion once you understand the pattern. Remember, this is about the manufacturing date, not when you bought it. The FD number helps establish its age right from the factory.

## FD Number vs. Model and Serial Numbers

Many people mix up the different numbers on their appliances. It is easy to confuse them. Let me clarify the distinct roles of the FD number, model number, and serial number. Each code serves a unique purpose. Understanding the differences is key for proper appliance management.

The **model number** identifies the specific product line of your dishwasher. It tells you about its features and specifications. For instance, a model number like "SHEM63W55N" describes a particular series of Bosch dishwashers. It indicates the design and capabilities of that specific type. When you need a manual or look for general information, you usually search by model number.

The **serial number**, on the other hand, is unique to *your specific unit*. Think of it like a vehicle identification number (VIN) for a car. No two Bosch dishwashers will have the same serial number. This number is crucial for warranty registration. It also helps track individual units for service history. It uniquely identifies your machine among all others.

The **FD number** is different. As we discussed, it primarily indicates the manufacturing date. It tells you when your dishwasher rolled off the assembly line. It is not unique to your single unit. Many dishwashers made in the same month and year will share the same FD number. It is a batch identifier.

All three numbers are important for different reasons. You might need the model number for general information. The serial number is for unit-specific tracking and warranty. The FD number is for age-related parts compatibility and service insights. Make sure you keep a record of all these numbers. They all play a role in supporting your appliance effectively.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is the FD number the same as the serial number?
No, they are different. The FD number tells you the manufacturing date. The serial number identifies your unique unit. Both are important for service.

### Can I find the FD number in the user manual?
Typically, no. The FD number is on a sticker directly on the appliance. The manual lists model and serial numbers, but usually not the specific FD production code.

### Why do I need the FD number for replacement parts?
Parts can change slightly between production runs. The FD number ensures you get parts compatible with your dishwasher's exact manufacturing period, preventing installation issues.

### What if my FD number sticker is gone or unreadable?
Try looking for alternate stickers or checking your appliance registration if you completed it. A Bosch service technician might also be able to help identify the unit by other means.

### Does the FD number indicate warranty status?
It helps establish the start date of the warranty period. However, you also need your purchase receipt to prove the warranty is active from the date of sale.

### Does every Bosch appliance have an FD number?
Yes, most Bosch appliances use a similar FD (or production) number system. This system helps Bosch track their products across all categories efficiently.
