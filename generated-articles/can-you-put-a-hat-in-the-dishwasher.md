## Hats You Should Never Put In The Dishwasher

While some hats tolerate dishwasher cleaning, many others do not. Putting the wrong hat in a dishwasher can cause irreversible damage. I have seen many good hats ruined this way. It is important to know which hats to protect from the dishwasher's harsh environment. The primary culprits are heat and water saturation. These factors can shrink, warp, or dissolve delicate materials.

Hats made from natural fibers like wool or felt should never go into a dishwasher. Wool is known to shrink severely when exposed to hot water and agitation. This turns your adult-sized hat into a child's toy. Felt hats are also made of compressed fibers. Hot water and soap can cause them to lose their shape. They can also become stiff and brittle. My advice is to always hand wash these types of hats. You can learn [how to clean a felt hat](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-clean-a-felt-hat) properly. This protects the fabric and shape.

Leather and suede hats are another big no-no. Water can stain and stiffen leather. It can also cause cracking as it dries. Suede is even more delicate. Water spots are very hard to remove from suede. The dishwasher's detergent will strip natural oils from these materials. This makes them dry and brittle. If you have a [suede hat](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-clean-suede-hat), specialized cleaning methods are necessary. These hats require gentle care.

Straw hats, including woven straw or paper straw, will also suffer. Water makes straw brittle and can cause it to lose its shape. The weaving can unravel. This completely ruins the hat's structure. Dishwasher detergents are too strong for these delicate fibers. For [straw hat cleaning](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-clean-straw-hat), always opt for spot cleaning with a damp cloth. Never submerge a straw hat in water.

Vintage hats are a special case. They often have cardboard brims. Cardboard will disintegrate when wet. They also use older dyes that can bleed. Embellishments like feathers, beads, or delicate stitching might not survive the wash cycle. Always treat vintage hats with extreme care. Hand cleaning is almost always the best option for them. If you are unsure, it is always safer to choose a gentle hand wash. This protects your cherished item.

### Hats to Avoid in the Dishwasher:

*   **Wool Hats:** Prone to severe shrinkage and misshaping.
*   **Felt Hats:** Can lose shape, become stiff, or develop permanent creases.
*   **Leather and Suede Hats:** Water causes staining, stiffening, cracking, and loss of natural oils.
*   **Straw Hats:** Become brittle, lose shape, and fibers can unravel.
*   **Vintage Hats:** Often have cardboard brims, delicate dyes, and fragile embellishments that cannot withstand water or agitation.
*   **Hats with Cardboard Brims:** The cardboard will break down and ruin the hat's structure.

## The Proper Method: How to Wash a Hat in the Dishwasher

If you have a dishwasher-safe hat, following the correct steps is important. This ensures your hat gets clean without damage. The goal is a gentle wash with no harsh drying. I have learned the best way to do this through practice. This method protects your hat's shape and color. It also helps remove stubborn dirt.

First, always pre-treat your hat. Look for any visible stains, especially sweat stains on the brim or band. Apply a small amount of mild detergent or a stain remover directly to these areas. Let it sit for about 10-15 minutes. This helps break down the grime before the main wash. You can also use an old toothbrush to gently scrub the stained spots. Pre-treating makes a big difference in the final result.

Next, you need to secure your hat. A hat cage or hat frame is your best friend here. These plastic frames hold your hat's shape during the wash cycle. This prevents it from getting crushed or deformed by water pressure. You simply place your hat inside the cage. If you do not have a hat cage, you can place the hat on the top rack of your dishwasher. Make sure it is positioned securely between the tines. This is important so it does not move around. The top rack is safer because it is further from the heating element at the bottom. Getting your hat ready is key to a successful wash. Many people follow these steps for [how to clean your hat in the dishwasher](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-clean-your-hat-in-the-dishwasher).

For the dishwasher cycle, choose carefully. Use a cold water cycle. This is often labeled "light," "delicate," or "rinse only." Never use hot water, as it can cause shrinking or fading. Crucially, turn off the heated dry cycle. Heat is the enemy of most hats. If your dishwasher does not have a "no heat dry" option, simply open the door after the wash cycle finishes. Let the hat air dry naturally.

Regarding detergent, use a small amount of mild dishwashing detergent. Avoid any harsh detergents or those with bleach. Bleach can strip color from your hat. Place the detergent in the main dispenser. You can also skip the detergent entirely if your hat is only lightly soiled. The water jets alone can do a good job. Make sure the dishwasher is empty of dirty dishes. Food particles can stick to your hat. Follow these steps to [dishwash a hat](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-dishwash-a-hat) properly.

### Step-by-Step Dishwasher Hat Cleaning:

1.  **Inspect and Pre-treat:** Check for stains. Apply mild detergent to spots.
2.  **Secure the Hat:** Place the hat in a hat cage or securely on the top rack.
3.  **Choose the Right Cycle:** Select a cold water, gentle, or "rinse only" cycle.
4.  **Turn Off Heat Dry:** This is critical to prevent shrinking and warping.
5.  **Add Mild Detergent:** Use a small amount of mild dishwashing detergent. Avoid bleach.
6.  **Run the Cycle:** Start the dishwasher. Ensure no food particles are present.

## Air Drying Your Hat After Dishwasher Cleaning

The drying process is just as important as the washing process. Proper drying helps your hat keep its shape. It prevents shrinking or damage. After your hat comes out of the dishwasher, it will be wet. Do not put it in a clothes dryer. The heat from a clothes dryer is far too intense for hats. It will almost certainly cause shrinking and warping. I always tell people to air dry their hats.

Take your hat out of the dishwasher. Remove it from the hat cage if you used one. Now, gently reshape the hat with your hands. Pay attention to the crown and the brim. Make sure they are in their original form. The brim is especially important for baseball caps. You want it to retain its curve. A good method is to place the hat on something round. You can use a clean, dry towel rolled up. Some people use a coffee can or a ball. This helps the hat maintain its structure as it dries.

Place your hat in a well-ventilated area. Room temperature is ideal. Avoid direct sunlight. Strong sunlight can cause colors to fade. It can also dry the hat too quickly, leading to stiff spots. A good spot might be on a clothes rack indoors. You can also place it on a countertop away from windows. Ensure good air circulation around the hat. This helps it dry evenly and prevents mildew. My advice for [how to wash a hat](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-wash-a-hat) always stresses the importance of air drying.

Drying time can vary. It depends on the hat's material and humidity. Most hats take 12 to 24 hours to dry completely. Do not wear the hat until it is fully dry. Wearing a damp hat can stretch it out. It can also make it lose its shape. It can also cause skin irritation or mildew if not completely dry. Patience is a virtue when drying your hats. This ensures they look great for a long time.

### Key Air Drying Tips:

*   **No Heat Drying:** Never use a clothes dryer or the dishwasher's heat dry cycle.
*   **Reshape Gently:** After washing, manually reshape the hat's crown and brim.
*   **Use a Form:** Place the hat on a round object (e.g., a rolled towel, coffee can) to maintain shape.
*   **Ventilated Area:** Dry indoors in a spot with good air circulation.
*   **Avoid Direct Sun:** Sunlight can fade colors and cause uneven drying.
*   **Be Patient:** Allow 12-24 hours for complete drying before wearing.

## Hand Washing Hats: A Gentle Alternative

Sometimes, the dishwasher method feels too risky. Or, your hat simply is not dishwasher-safe. In these cases, hand washing is a fantastic and gentle alternative. It gives you precise control over the cleaning process. This makes it suitable for almost any hat material, as long as you use the right approach. I often choose hand washing for my more delicate or favorite hats. This ensures they get the care they need.

To start, gather your supplies. You will need a clean basin or sink. You also need mild detergent. Look for a gentle laundry soap or even baby shampoo. Warm water is usually fine, but cold water is safer for delicate fabrics. You might also want a soft brush or a clean cloth. This helps with spot cleaning. Prepare your workspace. This makes the process smooth.

Fill your basin with cool or lukewarm water. Add a small amount of mild detergent. Stir the water to create some suds. Now, submerge your hat into the soapy water. Let it soak for about 10 to 15 minutes. This helps loosen dirt and sweat. For very dirty hats, you can let it soak a bit longer. However, avoid extremely long soaks for hats with unknown dyes.

After soaking, gently scrub the hat. Use a soft brush or a cloth for this. Pay close attention to the sweatband and brim. These areas usually collect the most grime. For white hats with stubborn yellow sweat stains, you might need a special pre-treatment. You can learn [how to clean a white hat](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-clean-a-white-hat) effectively. Gently work the detergent into the fabric. Avoid harsh scrubbing. This can damage the material or fade colors. Rinse the hat thoroughly under cool, running water. Ensure all soap residue is gone. Squeeze out excess water gently. Do not twist or wring the hat. This can distort its shape.

Finally, air dry your hat. As with the dishwasher method, never use a machine dryer. Reshape your hat and let it air dry. You can place it on a clean towel. A hat stand or a stuffed towel inside the crown helps keep its shape. For tough [sweat stains from hats](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-clean-sweat-stains-from-hat), hand washing gives you direct access to scrub them out. Hand washing is a safe and effective way to keep many different types of hats clean.

### Hand Washing Steps:

1.  **Prepare:** Fill a basin with cool or lukewarm water and mild detergent.
2.  **Soak:** Submerge the hat and let it soak for 10-15 minutes.
3.  **Clean:** Gently scrub stained areas with a soft brush or cloth.
4.  **Rinse:** Rinse thoroughly under cool, running water until all soap is gone.
5.  **Dry:** Gently press out excess water and air dry, reshaping the hat.

## Machine Washing Hats: When It's Acceptable

Some hats can go into a washing machine. This is different from a dishwasher. A washing machine uses different motions and temperatures. Like the dishwasher, this method is only suitable for certain hat types. It is often a good option for durable, all-fabric hats. These are usually hats without a plastic or cardboard brim.

When considering the washing machine, the same rules apply to brim type. Avoid hats with cardboard brims at all costs. Hats with plastic brims can survive, but the agitation can be harsher than a dishwasher. Hats made entirely of soft fabric, like bucket hats or some cloth sun hats, often do best. My main advice for [how to wash a hat in washing machine](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-wash-a-hat-in-washing-machine) is to be cautious.

Always use a gentle cycle with cold water. This is crucial for protecting the fabric and preventing shrinkage. Many washing machines have a "delicate" or "hand wash" setting. These settings use less agitation. They are much safer for hats. Never use hot water. Hot water causes colors to bleed. It also makes fabrics shrink. I always set my machine to cold.

Using a laundry bag is highly recommended. A mesh laundry bag protects your hat from snagging or stretching during the wash. It also helps the hat maintain its shape better. Place your hat inside the bag before putting it in the washing machine. You can also wash the hat with a small load of similar colored items. This helps cushion the hat. Do not wash it with heavy items like jeans or towels.

Use a small amount of mild liquid detergent. Powder detergents can sometimes leave residue on fabric. Avoid bleach or harsh stain removers. These can damage your hat's color and fabric. Once the wash cycle finishes, take your hat out immediately. Do not leave it sitting in the wet machine. This can cause wrinkles or mildew.

Always air dry your hat after machine washing. Reshape it carefully. Place it on a clean towel or a hat form. Let it dry completely in a well-ventilated area. Avoid direct sunlight. This ensures your hat maintains its shape and color. You might ask, "[Can you wash a hat in the washing machine](https://homeessentialsguide.com/can-you-wash-a-hat-in-the-washing-machine)?" The answer is yes, if you follow these careful steps. This method is a good balance between convenience and hat safety for appropriate types.

### Washing Machine Guidelines:

*   **Hat Type:** Best for soft, all-fabric hats without rigid brims. Plastic brim caps are a riskier option compared to a dishwasher.
*   **Cycle:** Use a "delicate" or "hand wash" cycle with cold water.
*   **Protection:** Place the hat in a mesh laundry bag.
*   **Detergent:** Use a small amount of mild liquid detergent; avoid bleach.
*   **Load:** Wash with a small load of similar light items, or by itself.
*   **Drying:** Always air dry, reshaping the hat first.

## Maintaining Your Hats: Preventing Future Grime

Keeping your hats clean is easier when you prevent them from getting too dirty in the first place. Good hat maintenance extends their life. It also means you do not have to wash them as often. Regular care keeps your hats looking fresh. I follow a few simple habits to keep my hats in top condition.

First, wipe your hat down regularly. Use a soft, clean cloth to remove dust and light surface dirt. Do this after each wear. A lint roller can also pick up fuzz and small debris. This prevents dirt from building up and becoming ingrained in the fabric. This simple step makes a big difference.

Spot clean small stains immediately. Do not wait for a stain to set. If you get a smudge or a drop of something on your hat, address it quickly. Use a damp cloth with a tiny bit of mild soap. Gently dab the stain. Always blot, do not rub. Rubbing can spread the stain. It can also damage the fabric. Prompt spot cleaning means you often avoid a full wash. This is especially true for sweat stains. You can clean [sweat stains from hat](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-clean-sweat-stains-from-hat) effectively with this method.

Proper storage is also vital. Do not just toss your hat on a chair or the floor. Store hats on a hat rack or in a hat box. This helps them maintain their shape. It also protects them from dust and getting crushed. If you stack hats, place softer hats on top of stiffer ones. This prevents permanent creases. Good storage makes your hats last longer.

Consider using sweatband liners. These adhesive liners stick to the inside of your hat's sweatband. They absorb sweat and oils. This prevents these elements from staining the hat's actual fabric. Liners are replaceable. This means you just swap out a dirty liner for a fresh one. This keeps your hat's sweatband clean. It reduces the need for deep cleaning. This is a very effective way to prevent yellowing on the inside band.

Finally, rotate your hats. Do not wear the same hat every day. Giving your hats a break allows them to air out. It also prevents excessive wear and tear on one specific hat. This simple rotation extends the life of your entire hat collection. These small habits add up. They help you keep your hats looking new for much longer.

### Hat Maintenance Tips:

*   **Regular Wiping:** Use a soft cloth or lint roller after each wear to remove dust.
*   **Immediate Spot Cleaning:** Address small stains quickly with a damp cloth and mild soap.
*   **Proper Storage:** Store hats on a rack or in a box to maintain shape and prevent dust buildup.
*   **Use Sweatband Liners:** Absorb sweat and oils to protect the hat's internal band.
*   **Rotate Hats:** Wear different hats to allow them to air out and reduce wear on any single hat.
