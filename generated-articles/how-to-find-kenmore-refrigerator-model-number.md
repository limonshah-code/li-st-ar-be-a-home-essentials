## Common Places to Find Your Kenmore Refrigerator Model Number

Finding your Kenmore refrigerator model number often involves looking in a few key areas. Manufacturers place these labels in spots that are usually out of sight but accessible. These locations ensure the label does not get damaged easily. They also keep the refrigerator's appearance clean.

Some Kenmore refrigerator models have the label on the back panel of the appliance. This location is less convenient. You often need to pull the refrigerator away from the wall to see it. ### Inside the Fresh Food Compartment

Check the inner walls of the refrigerator section. The label can be on the left or right side. Sometimes, it is on the top ceiling area. This label often lists both the model and serial numbers.

The kickplate is the panel at the bottom front of your Kenmore refrigerator. It is designed to cover the components underneath. This location keeps the label protected.

The back of the refrigerator is another possible spot. This location is less common for newer models. However, it is worth checking if you cannot find the label elsewhere.

*   **Pull the refrigerator out:** Carefully slide the refrigerator away from the wall. Make sure to unplug it first.

*   **Use a flashlight:** The area can be dusty or dark. A flashlight helps you see the label clearly.

## How to Read Your Kenmore Refrigerator Model Number Tag

Once you find the tag, reading the Kenmore refrigerator model number is straightforward. The tag usually contains several pieces of information. The model number is typically clearly labeled. It often says "Model No." or "M/N" next to it. The serial number is also present, usually labeled "Serial No." or "S/N".

The model number consists of a series of letters and numbers. For Kenmore appliances, these numbers often start with a three-digit prefix. This prefix indicates the original equipment manufacturer (OEM) that built the appliance for Kenmore. For example, a model number starting with "253" indicates it was made by Frigidaire. A "795" prefix means LG made it. The rest of the model number details the specific features and design. It identifies the color, capacity, and other unique characteristics. Sometimes, there is a revision number or version listed too. This helps distinguish between similar models made in different production runs. Write down the entire model number exactly as it appears. Any small mistake can lead to ordering the wrong parts.

The serial number is different from the model number. It is a unique identifier for that specific unit. No two appliances have the same serial number. This number is used for warranty tracking and product recalls. Always record both the model and serial numbers. This ensures you have complete information for any future needs. Keeping a photo of the tag on your phone is a smart idea.

## Understanding Kenmore Refrigerator Serial Numbers

Beyond the Kenmore refrigerator model number, there is also a serial number. This number is equally important, but it serves a different purpose. While the model number tells you what *type* of refrigerator you have, the serial number identifies your *specific unit*. Think of it like a car's VIN (Vehicle Identification Number).

Every Kenmore refrigerator produced has a unique serial number. No two refrigerators, even if they are the exact same model, will share the same serial number. This uniqueness makes the serial number vital for tracking and record-keeping. It ties your particular appliance to its manufacturing date and location.

The serial number is used for several key functions. It helps with warranty claims. When you register your appliance or need a repair, the serial number confirms its purchase date and warranty status. It also assists in product recalls. If Kenmore or the original manufacturer issues a recall for a certain batch of refrigerators, the serial number helps identify affected units.

Additionally, the serial number can sometimes reveal the manufacturing date of your Kenmore refrigerator. Different manufacturers encode this information in various ways within the serial number itself. Some use the first few digits or letters to indicate the year and week of production. This information can be useful for determining the age of your appliance. It also helps estimate its remaining lifespan. Always record both the model number and the serial number when you find them. This complete record will save you trouble later.

This buildup makes the refrigerator work harder. Clean them at least once a year. Use a vacuum cleaner with a brush attachment to gently remove debris.
*   **Check Door Gaskets:** The rubber seals around the refrigerator and freezer doors are important. They keep cold air in and warm air out. Check them regularly for cracks or tears. *   **Keep Vents Clear:** Inside your refrigerator, there are vents that help circulate cold air. Make sure nothing blocks these vents. Proper airflow ensures even cooling throughout the compartments. Do not overfill your refrigerator, as this can block airflow.

## Frequently Asked Questions About Kenmore Refrigerator Model Numbers

### Q1: Can I find the model number on my Kenmore refrigerator's receipt?

Yes, many retailers include the full model number on the purchase receipt. Check your original sales receipt or invoice. This is often the easiest way to find it if the physical label is hard to locate or read. It saves you from searching inside or behind the appliance.

### Q2: Is the Kenmore refrigerator model number the same as the serial number?

No, the model number and serial number are different. The model number identifies the specific type or series of your refrigerator. The serial number identifies your unique unit, much like a car's VIN. You need both for different purposes, like parts ordering and warranty claims.

### Q3: Why is my Kenmore model number starting with three digits like "253" or "795"?

The first three digits of a Kenmore model number indicate the original manufacturer. Kenmore is a brand, not a manufacturer. For example, "253" often means the refrigerator was made by Frigidaire. "795" means LG manufactured it. This helps identify the original parts source.

### Q4: What should I do if the model number label is worn off?

If the label is worn off, first check your owner's manual or purchase receipt. If those are unavailable, look for any online records from where you bought the refrigerator. As a last resort, contact Kenmore customer service. Provide them with details like the age and features of your appliance.

### Q5: Can I find parts for my Kenmore refrigerator using only the serial number?

While the serial number is important for warranty and manufacturing details, parts are typically ordered using the model number. The model number tells parts suppliers exactly which components are compatible. The serial number helps confirm the correct model within a specific production range.

### Q6: How often should I check my Kenmore refrigerator's model number?

You do not need to check it frequently. Find it once and record it in a safe place. A good practice is to take a photo of the label with your smartphone. Store this photo in a dedicated folder. This way, you have it instantly available whenever you need it for parts or service.
