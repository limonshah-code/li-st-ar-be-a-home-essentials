## How To Clean Decorative Pillows: A Guide to Freshening Your Decor

Decorative pillows add comfort and style to any room. They brighten spaces and offer a cozy feel. However, these beautiful accents collect dust, dirt, and spills over time. Knowing how to properly clean decorative pillows helps keep them looking new. It extends their life and maintains a fresh home environment. This guide will walk you through safe and effective cleaning methods for various types of decorative pillows. We will cover everything from identifying fabric types to drying your pillows.

### Takeaway: Keep your decorative pillows fresh and beautiful with these steps:

*   Always check the care label first.
*   Identify the fabric type for proper cleaning.
*   Spot clean minor stains quickly.
*   Hand wash or machine wash only when the label allows.
*   Air dry pillows completely to prevent mildew.

### Clear, Concise Answer:

To clean decorative pillows, first check their care tag. Based on the fabric and tag instructions, choose between spot cleaning, hand washing, machine washing, or professional dry cleaning. Always use gentle products and ensure the pillow dries fully to avoid damage or mildew.

## Understanding Decorative Pillow Types and Fabrics

Decorative pillows come in many materials, each needing a special cleaning approach. Identifying your pillow's fabric is the first step before you start cleaning. Common fabrics include cotton, linen, silk, velvet, wool, and synthetic blends. Some pillows have delicate embellishments like beads, embroidery, or tassels. These features require extra care.

Cotton and linen are usually durable and machine washable. Silk and velvet, however, are delicate materials. They often need hand washing or dry cleaning. Wool can shrink or felt with improper washing. Synthetic blends like polyester are often easier to clean. They typically handle machine washing well.

You must handle embellished pillows with care. The decorations can come loose or get damaged during washing. Consider the fill material of your pillow too. Down, feathers, foam, or synthetic fibers all react differently to moisture. Knowing these details helps you pick the safest cleaning method. This ensures your decorative pillows stay in good condition.

### H3: Checking the Care Label

The care label is your best friend when cleaning decorative pillows. It provides specific instructions from the manufacturer. Look for symbols indicating washing method, water temperature, bleach use, drying instructions, and ironing. A "dry clean only" label means you should not submerge the pillow in water. Attempting to wash such pillows at home can ruin them.

If the label says "spot clean only," avoid full immersion. This means you should clean small areas rather than washing the entire pillow. For washable pillows, the label might specify machine wash settings. It might suggest cold water, gentle cycle, or delicate detergent. Always follow these guidelines to prevent shrinking, fading, or damage. Misinterpreting or ignoring the care label can lead to irreversible damage.

Sometimes, a decorative pillow might not have a care label. This often happens with handmade or very old items. In these cases, you must identify the fabric by touch and appearance. If unsure, assume the fabric is delicate and opt for spot cleaning or professional dry cleaning. This protects your pillow from potential harm.

## Essential Tools and Cleaning Solutions

Gathering the right supplies makes cleaning decorative pillows easier and safer. You do not need many items, but the correct ones make a big difference. Having everything ready before you start cleaning saves time. It also prevents mid-process searches for needed items.

You will need a few basic things. Soft cloths or sponges are good for spot cleaning. Mild detergents, specifically for delicate fabrics, are also important. Distilled water is better than tap water for some delicate fabrics. This avoids mineral stains. For more advanced cleaning, you might need a vacuum cleaner with a brush attachment. This helps remove surface dust.

Remember, strong chemicals or harsh bleaches can damage delicate fabrics. Always choose gentle, pH-neutral cleaners. Test any solution on an inconspicuous area first. This ensures it does not cause discoloration or damage. A small spray bottle is useful for mixing and applying spot cleaning solutions.

### H3: Gentle Cleaning Agents

Choosing the right cleaning agent is very important for decorative pillows. Harsh chemicals can strip colors or damage fibers. Look for detergents labeled "gentle," "mild," or "for delicates." Baby shampoo is a good option for silk or wool. It is very mild and designed for sensitive materials. You can also use a small amount of liquid dish soap for spot cleaning. Make sure it is clear and dye-free.

For specific issues, baking soda is excellent for odor absorption. You can sprinkle it on the pillow, let it sit, and then vacuum it off. This helps freshen pillows between deeper cleans. White vinegar is another natural cleaner. It helps with light stains and odors. Mix it with water for a mild cleaning solution. Always dilute vinegar, as it can be strong.

When dealing with tough stains, enzymatic cleaners might be necessary. These break down organic stains like food or pet accidents. Apply them carefully, following product instructions. Remember, less is often more when it comes to cleaning solutions. Use only a small amount to avoid over-saturating the pillow.

## Spot Cleaning Techniques for Minor Stains

Spot cleaning is the safest method for most decorative pillows. This approach targets small stains without wetting the entire pillow. It is ideal for pillows with "spot clean only" labels or delicate fabrics. Act fast when a spill happens. Fresh stains are always easier to remove.

Start by blotting the stain with a clean, dry cloth. Do not rub, as this can spread the stain. For liquid spills, blot from the outside of the stain inward. This keeps the stain from expanding. If there is solid debris, gently scrape it off first. Use a dull knife or spoon.

Once you remove excess material, prepare a gentle cleaning solution. Mix a small amount of mild detergent with water. Dampen a clean cloth with this solution. Gently dab the stained area. Work in small circular motions. Continue blotting with a separate clean, damp cloth to remove the soap residue. Finish by blotting dry with a clean towel. Ensure the area is as dry as possible. Repeat the process if the stain remains. You can find more tips on cleaning pillows with specific ingredients by checking out how to clean pillows with baking soda if you want to use a natural option.

### H3: Addressing Different Stain Types

Different stains respond best to specific treatments. Understanding the stain type helps you choose the right approach. This improves your chances of complete removal without damaging the fabric. Oil-based stains, like grease or makeup, need a different method than water-based stains.

For oil or grease stains, sprinkle cornstarch or baking soda on the fresh stain. Let it sit for 15-30 minutes. This powder absorbs the oil. Then, gently brush or vacuum the powder away. Follow up with a mild detergent solution as described above. For water-based stains like coffee or juice, blot immediately. Then, use a mix of water and mild soap.

Pet accidents need quick action. Blot up as much as possible. Then, use an enzymatic cleaner specifically for pet stains. These cleaners break down proteins in urine, which removes odor. Always follow the product's instructions. For ink stains, rubbing alcohol or hairspray can sometimes work. Apply a tiny amount to a cloth, then dab the stain. Test this in an hidden area first. Remember, some stains might require professional help. Do not force removal if you fear damaging the pillow.

## Hand Washing Delicate Decorative Pillows

Hand washing is a gentle option for decorative pillows that cannot go into a machine. This method gives you control over the washing process. It protects delicate fabrics and embellishments. Before hand washing, make sure the pillow is genuinely suitable for water immersion. This is vital for its safety.

First, remove the pillow cover if possible. Many decorative pillows have removable covers, which makes cleaning easier. If the cover is not removable, you will wash the entire pillow. Fill a clean sink or tub with cool or lukewarm water. Add a small amount of mild, liquid detergent. Swish the water to create suds.

Submerge the pillow or cover gently into the soapy water. Gently squeeze and agitate the fabric. Do not twist or scrub vigorously, especially with delicate materials like silk or velvet. Let it soak for 10-15 minutes. This allows the detergent to break down dirt. Rinse the pillow thoroughly under cool running water until all soap residue is gone. Squeeze excess water out carefully, without wringing. For very dirty pillows, you might be interested in how to deep clean pillows.

### H3: Washing Embellished and Unique Fabrics

Some decorative pillows feature beads, sequins, or intricate embroidery. These demand extra care during hand washing. The goal is to clean without damaging the adornments. Always handle these items very gently.

When washing an embellished pillow, avoid rubbing the decorative elements. Focus on gently squeezing the fabric around them. If the embellishments seem fragile, consider spot cleaning only, even if the fabric itself might be washable. For unique fabrics like velvet or silk, use very cool water. Velvet can get crushed or marked easily when wet. Silk can lose its sheen with hot water or harsh chemicals. Lay silk flat to dry.

Wool decorative pillows need special attention. Use cold water and a wool-specific detergent to prevent shrinking. Do not agitate wool vigorously, as it can cause felting. Gently press the water through the fabric. Rinse carefully and press out excess water. Never put wool in a dryer. Lay it flat on a towel to dry. Remember, for very precious or high-value items, professional cleaning is often the safest bet.

## Machine Washing Washable Decorative Pillows

Some decorative pillow covers are machine washable. This is often the case for cotton, linen, and some synthetic blends. Always check the care label first. If the label permits machine washing, this method can be convenient and effective. However, proper settings are essential to prevent damage.

Before placing the cover in the washing machine, zip it up or close any fasteners. This protects the fabric and the machine. Turn the cover inside out if it has delicate embroidery or printed designs. This helps preserve the appearance. Place the pillow cover in a mesh laundry bag. This offers an extra layer of protection against snags and stretching.

Select the gentle or delicate cycle on your washing machine. Use cold water to prevent shrinking and color fading. Add a mild, liquid detergent. Avoid using harsh detergents or bleach. Bleach can damage fibers and strip colors. Run the cycle. Once finished, remove the cover promptly. Do not leave it sitting in the machine, as this can cause mildew or wrinkles. For more general advice on machine washing, you can refer to how to clean your pillows.

### H3: Tips for Optimal Machine Cleaning

To get the best results from machine washing decorative pillow covers, keep a few tips in mind. Do not overload the washing machine. Give the pillow cover plenty of space to move freely. Overloading can prevent proper cleaning and may damage the fabric. Wash similar colors together to avoid color transfer.

Consider washing pillow covers separately from very heavy items like jeans or towels. The heavy items can cause friction and abrasion, which can harm delicate pillow fabrics. For heavily soiled covers, you might consider a pre-soak. Add a small amount of detergent to water and let the cover soak for 30 minutes before washing. This helps loosen dirt.

Adding a cup of white vinegar to the rinse cycle can help soften the fabric and remove any lingering detergent residue. It also acts as a natural deodorizer. After the wash cycle, shake out the pillow cover to smooth any wrinkles before drying. This makes the cover easier to iron later, if needed.

## Drying Decorative Pillows Properly

Drying decorative pillows correctly is as important as washing them. Improper drying can lead to mildew, odor, shrinking, or damage to the fill material. Patience is key when drying. Rushing the process can cause more harm than good.

Air drying is the safest and most recommended method for most decorative pillows. Lay the pillow or cover flat on a clean, dry towel. You can also hang pillow covers on a clothesline or drying rack. Ensure good air circulation around the pillow. Turn the pillow periodically to promote even drying. This helps prevent moisture from getting trapped inside.

For pillows with inserts, stand them on their side to help water drain. Fluff and rotate them every few hours. This ensures the fill dries completely. It can take 24-48 hours for a pillow to dry fully, depending on its size and fill. Never put a damp pillow back on your couch or bed. This can cause mold growth. You can also explore how to clean bed pillows if you are looking for information on general pillow cleaning.

### H3: Avoiding Heat and Damaging Effects

Heat is the enemy of most decorative pillows, especially those with delicate fabrics or foam inserts. High heat can shrink fabrics, melt synthetic fibers, or damage embellishments. Never put "dry clean only" pillows or those with delicate details in a dryer.

Even for machine-washable covers, low heat or air fluff settings are preferred if you use a dryer. Use a dryer ball or two to help fluff up the cover and speed up drying. Check the cover every 15-20 minutes. Remove it while it is still slightly damp. Then, air dry it completely. This minimizes wrinkles and heat exposure.

Pillows with foam or memory foam inserts should never go into a dryer. High heat can break down the foam structure. This makes the pillow lumpy and unusable. Air dry foam pillows flat, turning often. It can take several days for a foam pillow to dry completely. Ensuring complete dryness prevents mildew and bad odors inside your pillows. This also applies to general foam pillows; you can learn more by checking out how to clean foam pillows.

## Dry Cleaning "Dry Clean Only" Pillows

Many high-end or uniquely embellished decorative pillows carry a "dry clean only" label. This instruction is very important. It means the fabric or construction cannot tolerate water or traditional washing machine cycles. Attempting to wash these pillows at home risks irreversible damage, such as shrinking, fading, or losing shape.

Professional dry cleaners have specialized equipment and solvents that clean delicate materials without water. They also know how to handle various fabrics and embellishments. If your decorative pillow has this label, trusting it to a professional is the safest choice. Explain any stains or specific concerns to them.

Before taking your pillow to the dry cleaner, gently brush off any loose dirt or debris. Point out any visible stains. This helps the dry cleaner target specific areas. Remember, dry cleaning can be more expensive than home washing. However, it preserves the integrity and beauty of your valuable decorative pillows. You can also learn how to clean dry-clean-only items at home if you prefer to tackle it yourself.

### H3: Alternative Home Methods for "Dry Clean Only"

If a professional dry cleaner is not an option, you might try a very gentle home alternative for "dry clean only" decorative pillows. Please note that these methods carry a risk of damage and are not a substitute for professional cleaning. Proceed with extreme caution and only if you are willing to accept the risk.

One common home method is localized spot cleaning. Use a minimal amount of dry-cleaning solvent or a very mild, water-free cleaner designed for specific fabrics. Apply the cleaner to a clean white cloth, not directly to the pillow. Gently dab the stained area. Always test on an inconspicuous spot first. This helps check for colorfastness.

Another option is to use a home dry cleaning kit. These kits usually come with a bag and a cleaning cloth or sheet. You place the pillow inside the bag with the cleaning sheet and tumble it in your dryer on a low-heat or air-fluff setting. This method refreshes the pillow and removes odors. It does not deep clean stains. Always follow the kit's instructions precisely. Remember, these home kits are generally for refreshing, not for removing heavy dirt or stains.

## Maintaining Freshness and Preventing Future Stains

Keeping your decorative pillows clean is an ongoing task. Regular maintenance helps reduce the need for deep cleaning. This extends the life of your pillows and keeps your home looking tidy. Simple habits can make a big difference in how your pillows hold up over time.

Dust your decorative pillows regularly. Use a vacuum cleaner with a brush attachment on a low setting. This removes surface dust, pet hair, and crumbs that accumulate. Fluffing your pillows daily also helps. It prevents dust from settling deep into the fibers. Fluffing also helps the fill maintain its shape and air circulation.

Consider adding pillow covers that are easy to remove and wash. Many decorative pillows come with removable covers. If yours does not, you might find or have custom covers made. This allows for frequent washing of the cover without needing to clean the entire pillow. This simple step makes maintenance much easier.

### H3: Using Protective Measures

Protecting your decorative pillows from spills and stains is the best way to keep them clean. Accidents happen, but some measures can lessen the impact. Acting quickly after a spill is crucial. The faster you address a stain, the easier it is to remove.

For high-traffic areas, consider using fabric protector sprays. These sprays create an invisible barrier that repels liquids and stains. Always test the spray on an hidden area of the pillow first. Ensure it does not alter the fabric's color or texture. Reapply according to product instructions.

If you have children or pets, choose decorative pillows made from durable, washable fabrics. Cotton and synthetic blends are often good choices. Avoid delicate silks or heavily embellished items in areas where spills are common. Having a spare set of washable covers is also a smart idea. You can swap them out for cleaning as needed. This ensures your home decor always looks its best.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### Q1: How often should I clean my decorative pillows?
A1: The frequency depends on use. For pillows on beds or often used, a light clean every few months helps. For rarely used pillows, a yearly refresh is fine. Spot clean spills immediately. Regular dusting and fluffing also extends time between washes.

### Q2: Can all decorative pillows be machine washed?
A2: No, not all decorative pillows are machine washable. You must check the care label first. Delicate fabrics like silk, velvet, or heavily embellished pillows often require spot cleaning or professional dry cleaning. Machine washing can cause irreversible damage.

### Q3: What if my decorative pillow has no care tag?
A3: If there is no care tag, assume the pillow is delicate. Opt for spot cleaning as the safest method. You can also try a small patch test with water and mild soap on an hidden area. If in doubt, consult a professional dry cleaner.

### Q4: How do I remove a specific stain from a decorative pillow?
A4: First, identify the stain type (oil, water, pet). Blot fresh stains immediately. For oil stains, use cornstarch. For water-based stains, use mild soap and water. Enzymatic cleaners work for pet stains. Always test cleaners on an hidden spot.

### Q5: Can I put decorative pillows in the dryer?
A5: It depends on the pillow. Some machine-washable covers can go into the dryer on a low heat or air fluff setting. However, air drying is safest for most decorative pillows and covers. Never tumble dry pillows with foam inserts or delicate embellishments.

### Q6: How can I refresh decorative pillows without washing them?
A6: You can refresh decorative pillows by vacuuming them with a brush attachment to remove dust. Sprinkle baking soda over them, let it sit for 15-30 minutes, then vacuum it up to absorb odors. Fluffing them regularly also helps air circulation.
