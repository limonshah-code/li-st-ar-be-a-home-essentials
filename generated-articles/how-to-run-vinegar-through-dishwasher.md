## Step-by-Step Guide: How to Run Vinegar Through Dishwasher

Running vinegar through your dishwasher is a straightforward process. I have found these steps to be the most effective. This method helps tackle grime and odors. It also leaves your machine sparkling clean. Follow these simple instructions for a refreshed appliance.

**1. Empty Your Dishwasher Completely:**
Begin by ensuring your dishwasher is empty. Remove all dishes, silverware, and racks if you plan to clean them separately. The vinegar needs to work on the internal parts of the dishwasher, not your dinnerware. This allows for maximum contact with the walls, spray arms, and filter.

**2. Prepare the White Vinegar:**
You will need white vinegar for this cleaning method. White vinegar is best because it is strong enough to cut through grease and hard water. It also leaves no residue. I typically use about 1 to 2 cups of vinegar. The exact amount can vary. It depends on how dirty your dishwasher is or the size of your machine. For more details on the right quantity, check out [How much vinegar to put in dishwasher to clean](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-much-vinegar-to-put-in-dishwasher-to-clean).

**3. Place the Vinegar in the Dishwasher:**
This is a common question: where exactly do you put the vinegar? Do not put it in the detergent dispenser. Instead, pour the 1-2 cups of white vinegar into a dishwasher-safe bowl or mug. Place this bowl upright on the top rack of your empty dishwasher. The open container allows the vinegar to disperse slowly during the wash cycle. This ensures it reaches all internal parts. Our article on [Where to put vinegar in dishwasher to clean](https://homeessentialsguide.com/where-to-put-vinegar-in-dishwasher-to-clean) offers more specific guidance.

**4. Select the Hottest Wash Cycle:**
Close the dishwasher door. Now, select the hottest available wash cycle. This might be a "Sanitize," "Heavy Duty," or "Pot Scrubber" setting. The hot water helps activate the acetic acid in the vinegar. This increases its cleaning power. Do not add any dish detergent during this cycle. The vinegar alone will do the work. Adding detergent can create too many suds. It might also counteract the vinegar's effects.

**5. Run the Dishwasher Cycle:**
Start the cycle. Allow the dishwasher to run through its full wash and rinse phases. During this time, the hot water and vinegar mixture will circulate. It will dissolve mineral deposits, soap scum, and grease. You might notice a strong vinegar smell during the cycle. This is normal and will dissipate.

**6. Post-Cycle Wipe Down (Optional but Recommended):**
Once the cycle completes, open the dishwasher door. Look inside. You should see a much cleaner interior. Sometimes, a small amount of residue might remain. If you see any, wipe it down with a damp cloth. This ensures a spotless finish. This final wipe-down is a good habit. It makes sure no lingering grime is left. You can refer to [How to clean dishwasher with vinegar](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-clean-dishwasher-with-vinegar) for additional tips on this step.

Following these steps will help you achieve a clean and fresh dishwasher. Regular cleaning prevents future buildup. It keeps your appliance working efficiently.

## FAQ Section

### Q1: Is it safe to use vinegar in my dishwasher?
Yes, using white vinegar in your dishwasher is generally safe. Most dishwashers are made with materials like stainless steel and durable plastics that tolerate mild acids well. Vinegar helps clean without causing damage to the internal components or rubber seals when used as directed.

### Q2: How much vinegar should I use to clean my dishwasher?
For effective cleaning, use 1 to 2 cups of white vinegar. The amount can depend on the size of your dishwasher and how much grime has built up. Start with 1 cup for regular maintenance. Use 2 cups for a more intense clean.

### Q3: Where do I put the vinegar in the dishwasher for cleaning?
Do not put vinegar in the detergent dispenser. Instead, pour the 1-2 cups of white vinegar into a dishwasher-safe bowl or mug. Place this open container upright on the top rack of your empty dishwasher. This allows the vinegar to disperse slowly during the wash cycle.

### Q4: Can I add dishes during a vinegar cleaning cycle?
No, you should not add dishes during a vinegar cleaning cycle. The vinegar needs to focus its cleaning power on the dishwasher's interior. Adding dishes might prevent a thorough clean of the appliance itself. Run the cycle with an empty dishwasher for the best results.

### Q5: How often should I clean my dishwasher with vinegar?
The recommended frequency for cleaning your dishwasher with vinegar is every one to three months. If you have hard water or use your dishwasher daily, a monthly cleaning might be more beneficial. Pay attention to signs like odors or cloudy dishes for immediate cleaning needs.

### Q6: Will vinegar damage my dishwasher's rubber seals?
No, white vinegar typically does not damage dishwasher rubber seals. When used in the recommended amounts and for cleaning cycles, its mild acidity helps remove soap scum and mildew. These can actually degrade seals over time. Vinegar helps maintain the seals rather than harm them.
