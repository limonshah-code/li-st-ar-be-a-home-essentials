### Decoding the KitchenAid Serial Number: Understanding the Format

KitchenAid is part of Whirlpool Corporation. Their serial numbers often follow a specific format. This format helps you understand when your appliance was made. It usually includes a letter followed by numbers. This code tells you the year and week of manufacture.

The first character is usually a letter. This letter represents the year of production. Following this letter are two digits. These two digits typically indicate the week of that year. For example, 'C' might stand for 2013, and '35' for the 35th week. Understanding this system is simple.

Whirlpool uses a rolling letter sequence for years. So 'C' for 2013 would be followed by 'D' for 2014, and so on. After 'Z', they often cycle back or use a different system. Always confirm the exact year codes if possible, as they can sometimes vary. This simple decoding helps you determine the age of your KitchenAid dishwasher.

#### Whirlpool's Serial Number System

Whirlpool's serial numbers for their appliances, including KitchenAid, typically use a standard format. The first character is an alphabet letter. This letter represents the year of manufacture. The next two digits indicate the week of that year. The remaining digits are sequential for that week.

For example, a serial number like "F23XXXXXXX" would mean different things. 'F' could correspond to a specific year, like 2016. '23' would then be the 23rd week of that year. The remaining X's are unique identifiers for that specific appliance. This format applies across many Whirlpool brands.

It is important to note that the year code changes. The letter sequence is not always perfectly alphabetical and continuous over decades. Sometimes, letters are skipped, or the cycle restarts. Consulting an online Whirlpool manufacturing date decoder tool can confirm the exact year for your letter.

Let us use a hypothetical KitchenAid serial number for decoding practice. Imagine your serial number is "G151234567". The first letter is 'G'. The next two digits are '15'. This format helps us find the manufacturing date.

First, we decode 'G'. Based on typical Whirlpool patterns, 'G' might represent the year 2017. (Note: These year codes are examples and can vary, so always cross-reference with official Whirlpool charts if precision is crucial.) Then, '15' signifies the 15th week of that year. So, this dishwasher was manufactured in the 15th week of 2017.

The remaining digits, '1234567', are the unique sequence number for that specific unit. This part does not contain date information. This part identifies your specific appliance. This simple method allows you to quickly determine your dishwasher's age. It is a handy skill for appliance owners.
