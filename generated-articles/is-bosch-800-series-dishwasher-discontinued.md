## Maintaining Your Bosch 800 Series Dishwasher for Longevity

Owning a Bosch 800 Series dishwasher means you have a high-quality appliance. Proper maintenance helps ensure it runs efficiently for years. Regular cleaning and simple care steps prevent issues. They also keep your dishes sparkling clean. A well-maintained dishwasher works better and lasts longer.

Start with routine cleaning of the filters. Bosch dishwashers have a multi-stage filter system. This system catches food particles. Over time, these particles can accumulate. This can reduce cleaning performance. Locate the filter assembly at the bottom of the dishwasher. Twist and pull it out. Rinse it under running water. Use a soft brush to remove stubborn debris. I clean my dishwasher filter once a month. This simple step makes a big difference.

Next, periodically clean the spray arms. Food particles can clog the small holes in the spray arms. This prevents water from reaching all your dishes. Check for any blockages. Use a toothpick or small brush to clear them. Also, wipe down the interior walls and door gasket. Food residue and mildew can build up there. This can cause odors. A damp cloth with a mild cleaner works well. You can learn more details on [how to clean your Bosch dishwasher](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-clean-your-bosch-dishwasher).

Use a dishwasher cleaner occasionally. These products help remove mineral buildup and grease. Bosch recommends using a descaler or a specialized dishwasher cleaning tablet. Run an empty cycle with the cleaner. This keeps the internal components free of grime. This is especially helpful if you live in an area with hard water. Additionally, always use a good quality dishwasher detergent and rinse aid. Rinse aid helps with drying and prevents water spots. For a complete guide, check out [how to clean dishwasher Bosch](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-clean-dishwasher-bosch). Following these steps ensures your 800 Series dishwasher remains a reliable workhorse in your kitchen.
