## Common Issues Covered and Excluded by Whirlpool Microwave Warranty

Understanding what your Whirlpool microwave warranty covers is crucial. It also helps to know what it does not cover. This knowledge prevents surprises. It ensures you do not waste time on an invalid claim. I always think about whether the issue is a factory defect or something I caused.

### What is Typically Covered:

*   **Defects in Materials:** This means a problem with the actual components. For example, a faulty control panel or a defective magnetron. These are parts that failed due to manufacturing issues.
*   **Defects in Workmanship:** This refers to errors during the manufacturing process. An example might be improper wiring or assembly. The microwave did not operate correctly from the start.
*   **Non-functional Components:** If a part breaks under normal use within the warranty period, and it is due to a defect. This includes the turntable motor or door latch.

### What is Typically Excluded:

*   **Accidental Damage:** This includes dropping the microwave or spilling liquids inside. User-induced physical damage is not covered.
*   **Misuse or Abuse:** Using the microwave for purposes other than cooking food. Overloading the microwave. These actions can cause damage.
*   **Improper Installation:** If the microwave was not installed correctly, any resulting issues are not covered. This is especially true for over-the-range models.
*   **Normal Wear and Tear:** Scratches, dents, or faded finishes from regular use are not covered.
*   **Cosmetic Damage:** Minor aesthetic issues that do not affect functionality.
*   **Damage from External Factors:** Power surges, natural disasters, or pest infestations.
*   **Unauthorized Service:** Repairs or modifications made by unapproved technicians. This voids the warranty.
*   **Commercial Use:** Whirlpool warranties are usually for residential use only.

Always consult your specific warranty document for a complete list. Knowing these distinctions saves you effort. This helps with any appliance repair. It is similar for other brands too. For instance, if you're wondering [is your GE microwave still under warranty](https://homeessentialsguide.com/is-my-ge-microwave-still-under-warranty), the coverage terms will have similar inclusions and exclusions.

*   **Read the Owner's Manual:** This document contains important usage and care instructions. It details what not to put in your microwave. It also explains proper cleaning methods.
*   **Clean Regularly:** Food spills can cause arcing or damage components. Wipe down the interior after each use. Use mild soap and water. Clean the turntable and roller ring.

### What is needed to make a Whirlpool microwave warranty claim?
