## Master Removing Your Whirlpool Dishwasher Filter

Have you ever unloaded dishes from your dishwasher, only to find them still covered in grime? It is a frustrating experience. Often, a dirty or clogged filter is the reason behind those disappointing wash results. Keeping your dishwasher filter clean is a simple step. It ensures your dishes come out sparkling.

A clean filter makes your dishwasher work better. It also prevents nasty odors from building up inside the appliance. This guide provides clear instructions. I will show you exactly how to remove the filter from a Whirlpool dishwasher. We will cover locating it, removing it, cleaning it, and putting it back. By following these steps, you will restore your dishwasher's performance.

### Takeaway

*   Locate the filter at the bottom of the dishwasher tub.
*   Twist the cylindrical part counter-clockwise.
*   Lift both filter parts straight up.
*   Rinse thoroughly under running water to clean.
*   Reinsert and twist clockwise to secure.

Removing the filter from a Whirlpool dishwasher involves locating the cylindrical filter in the bottom of the tub, twisting it counter-clockwise to unlock, and then lifting it out along with the lower mesh screen for cleaning.

## Identifying the Filter Type in Your Whirlpool Dishwasher

Before you can remove the filter, you need to know what kind you have and where to find it. Most modern Whirlpool dishwashers use a two-piece filtration system. This system is easy to access and clean. Older models sometimes had a self-cleaning filter system. Those systems chopped food into tiny pieces. However, this often made more noise and was less effective for larger debris.

Your Whirlpool dishwasher likely has a manual clean filter system. You will find it located at the bottom of the dishwasher tub. It sits directly under the lower spray arm. The main component is a cylindrical filter. This filter usually has a handle or small tabs on top. It locks into place with a simple twist.

Below this cylindrical filter, you will find a flat mesh screen. This screen collects smaller particles. Both parts work together to keep the wash water clean. It is important to remove and clean both parts. You will not miss them. Once you lift the lower rack, they will be clearly visible in the center of the tub's base. Knowing these parts exist makes the removal process simple.

## Step-by-Step Guide: How to Remove the Filter from Your Whirlpool Dishwasher

Removing the filter from your Whirlpool dishwasher is a straightforward process. It only takes a few minutes. I will walk you through each step. You will have your filter out and ready for cleaning in no time.

### Step 1: Empty the Dishwasher and Locate the Filter

First, make sure your dishwasher is empty. Remove all dishes, silverware, and the lower dish rack. This gives you clear access to the bottom of the tub. The filter system sits in the center of the dishwasher's floor. It is a round or cylindrical component. You will see it surrounding the drain opening.

### Step 2: Unlock and Lift the Cylindrical Filter

Look for a small handle or two tabs on the top of the cylindrical filter. Gently grasp this part. Twist the filter counter-clockwise. You will only need to turn it about a quarter-turn. You should feel it unlock. Once unlocked, lift the cylindrical filter straight up and out of the dishwasher. It may have some collected food debris, so be careful.

### Step 3: Remove the Lower Filter Screen (if applicable)

After removing the cylindrical filter, you will see a flat, round mesh screen. This screen sits directly below the main filter. It covers the sump area. This part helps catch smaller particles. Gently lift this mesh screen straight up. It should come out easily. Now you have both parts of your filter system ready for cleaning.

## What to Do After Removal: Cleaning Your Whirlpool Dishwasher Filter

Now that you have successfully removed the filter parts, it is time for the cleaning process. This step is crucial for getting your dishwasher working its best again. I find cleaning a dirty filter is quite satisfying. You can see the immediate difference.

Take both the cylindrical filter and the flat mesh screen to your sink. Rinse them thoroughly under warm running water. You want to wash away all loose food particles and debris. Hold them under the faucet until the water runs clear. Sometimes, dried-on food or greasy residue needs a bit more effort.

For stubborn bits, use a soft brush. An old toothbrush or a small scrubbing brush works perfectly. Gently scrub the mesh and plastic parts. You can use a mild dish soap if grease is present. Avoid harsh abrasive cleaners or sponges. These can damage the filter material. Rinse the filter parts again to remove all soap residue. Inspect both parts carefully. Ensure there are no lingering food particles or blockages. ## Reinstalling the Filter: Getting Your Whirlpool Dishwasher Ready

Once your filter parts are clean and dry, it is time to put them back into the dishwasher. Proper reinstallation ensures your dishwasher functions correctly. This step is just as important as the removal process. I always double-check everything at this stage.

First, place the flat mesh screen back into its spot at the bottom of the dishwasher tub. Make sure it sits flat and flush against the floor of the tub. It should fit perfectly into its designated area. Do not force it. It should drop in easily.

Next, take the cylindrical filter. Align its tabs or notch with the corresponding slots in the dishwasher floor. Lower the cylindrical filter into place. Once it sits correctly, twist it clockwise. You will feel it lock securely into position. It should not wiggle or spin freely. Give it a gentle tug to confirm it is locked. The arrow indicators on the filter or the tub can guide you. They show the lock and unlock positions. A properly installed filter prevents debris from getting into the pump. It keeps your dishwasher running efficiently.

## Troubleshooting Common Filter-Related Dishwasher Problems

Even after removing and cleaning your filter, you might still encounter some dishwasher issues. Most problems relate to the filter. It is good to know how to address them. I have faced these challenges myself.

A common issue is a filter that feels stuck. If you cannot twist or lift it, do not force it. Food particles or mineral buildup can cause this. Try wiggling it gently while twisting counter-clockwise. You can also pour a cup of white vinegar into the sump area. Let it sit for 30 minutes. This helps loosen mineral deposits. Then, try to remove the filter again.

If your dishes are still dirty after cleaning the filter, other issues might be present. Check your spray arms. Food particles can clog their tiny holes. Ensure they spin freely. Also, check your detergent dispenser. It should open correctly during the wash cycle. Sometimes, using too little or too much detergent causes problems. Using a rinse aid also helps with spotless drying. If you notice persistent bad odors, clean the entire dishwasher. Use a dishwasher cleaner. These solutions target grime inside the tub and hoses. ## FAQ Section

### How often should I clean my Whirlpool dishwasher filter?

I recommend cleaning your Whirlpool dishwasher filter once a month. If you run your dishwasher daily or often wash dishes with heavy food residue, you might clean it more often. Regular cleaning prevents clogs. It keeps your dishwasher performing well.

### Can I put my dishwasher filter in the washing machine?

No, do not put your dishwasher filter in the washing machine. Washing machines are too rough for delicate plastic filter parts. The high heat and agitation can damage or warp the filter. Always hand wash the dishwasher filter with warm water and mild soap.

### What happens if I do not clean the filter?

If you do not clean the filter, it will become clogged with food particles and grease. This causes dishes to come out dirty. You will see grime or film on them. It can also lead to foul odors inside the dishwasher. Your appliance will not clean effectively.

### My dishwasher smells bad, is it the filter?

A dirty filter is a common cause of bad dishwasher odors. Trapped food particles decompose and create a foul smell. Cleaning the filter often solves this problem. If the smell persists, you might need to clean the entire dishwasher interior. Also check the drain hose for blockages.

### Are all Whirlpool dishwasher filters the same?

Most modern Whirlpool dishwashers use a similar two-piece manual clean filter design. However, there can be slight variations in size or locking mechanism. Always refer to your specific dishwasher model's user manual. This ensures you have the correct information for your appliance.
