### A Quick Answer: How to Clean Your Lamona Dishwasher Filter

Finally, reassemble and secure the clean filter parts back into place.

## Identifying and Locating Your Lamona Dishwasher Filter

Finding your Lamona dishwasher filter is usually straightforward. Most Lamona dishwashers have a filter system located at the bottom of the wash tub. This area is typically just below the lower spray arm. You will need to pull out the bottom dish rack to access it. The filter system usually consists of two main parts.

The first part is a cylindrical filter. This looks like a small, perforated cylinder. It often has a handle or an arrow indicating which way to twist it. This primary filter catches larger food particles. It prevents them from reaching the pump. You will see this filter sitting in a circular opening in the base of the dishwasher.

Beneath this cylindrical filter, you will find a second, flat mesh filter. This part lies flat against the bottom of the tub. It collects smaller food bits and sediment. Some Lamona models might have a slightly different design, but this two-part system is very common. Always consult your Lamona dishwasher's user manual if you are unsure. The manual provides specific diagrams for your model. Knowing where to look makes the cleaning process much easier. It is similar to [where is filter in Samsung dishwasher](https://homeessentialsguide.com/where-is-filter-in-samsung-dishwasher), as many brands use a common design.

## Step-by-Step Guide to Removing Your Lamona Dishwasher Filter

Removing your Lamona dishwasher filter is simple. Following these steps ensures you do it correctly. This prevents any damage to the filter or your appliance. Always remember safety first when working with appliances.

### Prepare Your Dishwasher

First, make sure your Lamona dishwasher is empty. Remove all dishes, racks, and utensils from the bottom tub. It is best to wait until the dishwasher has fully cooled down if you have just run a cycle. Next, turn off the power to your dishwasher. You can do this at the circuit breaker or by unplugging the appliance if it has an accessible plug. This step is important for safety.

### Locate the Filter

Pull out the lower spray arm or move it to one side. Look at the very bottom of the dishwasher tub. You will see a circular component. This is your main cylindrical filter. It might be in the center or slightly off to one side. Its design allows for easy removal.

### Twist and Lift the Cylindrical Filter

Grasp the cylindrical filter. It usually has a small handle or marked arrows. Turn it counter-clockwise. You will only need to turn it a quarter or half turn. You should feel it unlock. Once unlocked, gently lift the entire cylindrical filter straight up and out of the dishwasher. It might contain some trapped food particles and water. Be careful not to spill them.

### Remove the Flat Mesh Filter

After removing the cylindrical filter, you will see a flat, usually fine-mesh filter lying underneath it. This filter covers a larger area of the tub floor. Gently lift this flat filter out. Some models may have small clips or tabs holding it in place. Simply unclip them if present. Handle this mesh filter carefully. It can be delicate. Now you have both parts of your Lamona dishwasher filter ready for cleaning. This process is common to many dishwashers, much like instructions on [how to clean filter in dishwasher](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-clean-filter-in-dishwasher) generally.

## Effective Methods for Cleaning a Lamona Dishwasher Filter

Once you have removed your Lamona dishwasher filter, cleaning it is quite easy. You will be amazed at how much debris it collects. My own filter is always full of surprising bits. I find that regular cleaning keeps it from becoming too gross.

### Rinse Under Running Water

Take both parts of the filter (the cylindrical and the flat mesh filter) to your sink. Hold them under warm running water. Allow the water to rinse away any loose food particles. You might need to use a decent water pressure to dislodge stuck-on bits. Many larger pieces will wash off quickly. This is the first and simplest step.

### Scrub with Soap and Brush

For stubborn grime, use your mild dish soap and a soft brush. Apply a small amount of soap directly to the filter. Gently scrub all surfaces, especially the mesh screens. Pay close attention to the crevices and perforations. Food residue and grease tend to accumulate here. An old toothbrush works wonders for reaching tight spots. Scrub both sides of the flat mesh filter. Make sure to get rid of any slime or greasy film. This film can make your dishes cloudy.

### Address Stubborn Buildup or Odors

If your filter has a greasy film or smells bad, try soaking it. Fill a basin with warm water and a few drops of dish soap. You can also add a quarter cup of white vinegar. Let the filter parts soak for about 10-15 minutes. This helps loosen tough grime and neutralize odors. After soaking, scrub again and rinse thoroughly. Vinegar is especially good for hard water deposits. This method is similar to [how to clean Bosch dishwasher filter with vinegar](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-clean-bosch-dishwasher-filter-with-vinegar). Make sure all soap residue is gone. Any leftover soap can create suds during the next wash cycle.

### Dry the Filter Parts

After cleaning, shake off excess water. You can air dry the filter parts or wipe them with a clean cloth. Ensuring they are reasonably dry helps prevent water spots. It also ensures a smooth reinstallation. Your Lamona dishwasher filter is now clean and ready to go back in.

## Reinstalling Your Lamona Dishwasher Filter Correctly

Putting your Lamona dishwasher filter back in correctly is as important as cleaning it. A poorly installed filter can lead to leaks, poor cleaning results, or even damage to the dishwasher. I always double-check mine. It just takes a moment and saves potential headaches.

### Replace the Flat Mesh Filter First

Start by placing the flat mesh filter back into the bottom of the dishwasher tub. This filter lies flush against the floor of the appliance. Ensure it sits perfectly flat and covers the entire designated area. Some models have small tabs or slots that help align this filter. Make sure any clips are secure if your model has them. It should fit snugly without any gaps around the edges.

### Insert the Cylindrical Filter

Next, take the cylindrical filter. Align it with the circular opening in the center of the flat filter. The cylindrical filter usually has an arrow or a specific notch. These help you align it properly. Gently push it down into the opening. Make sure it seats fully. You should feel it drop into place. Do not force it. If it does not go down easily, recheck your alignment.

### Twist and Lock into Place

Once the cylindrical filter is fully seated, twist it clockwise. You will hear or feel a click. This indicates it is locked securely into position. The arrow on the filter should now point to the "locked" symbol or line up with a specific mark. This ensures the filter will not come loose during a wash cycle. A loose filter can cause water to bypass the filtration system. This results in dirty dishes.

### Test for Secure Fit

Give the reinstalled filter a gentle tug. It should not lift or wiggle. If it feels loose, repeat the twisting motion until it locks firmly. Confirming a secure fit is crucial. It ensures proper water drainage and efficient cleaning. This entire process is very similar to [how to put dishwasher filter back in](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-put-dishwasher-filter-back-in) for other brands. Once the filter is secure, you can replace the bottom dish rack and restore power to your Lamona dishwasher. Your appliance is now ready for its next wash cycle.

## Maintaining a Clean Lamona Dishwasher Filter: Tips and Frequency

Cleaning your Lamona dishwasher filter is not a one-time task. Regular maintenance is key to keeping your appliance performing its best. Establishing a routine for filter care prevents major buildup. It ensures your dishes are always spotless. I make it a habit to check mine every couple of weeks.

### How Often to Clean

The frequency of cleaning depends on how often you use your dishwasher and what kind of food goes into it. As a general rule, I recommend cleaning your Lamona dishwasher filter at least once a month. If you use your dishwasher daily, or if you often wash dishes with a lot of heavy food residue (like rice, pasta, or sticky sauces), consider cleaning it every two weeks. For very light use, once every two to three months might be sufficient. Paying attention to your dishes is a good indicator. If they start looking less clean, it is probably time for a filter check. This aligns with advice on [how often should you clean your dishwasher filter](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-often-should-you-clean-your-dishwasher-filter).

### Scrape Plates Before Loading

One simple habit can significantly reduce filter clogging: scrape plates before loading them into the dishwasher. You do not need to pre-rinse every dish, but removing large food scraps, bones, and seeds helps immensely. These larger particles are the main culprits for clogging your filter quickly. A quick scrape into the bin saves you future filter cleaning time.

### Run a Monthly Deep Clean Cycle

Beyond cleaning the filter manually, consider running a monthly deep clean cycle for your entire Lamona dishwasher. You can use a dishwasher cleaner specifically designed for this purpose. Alternatively, place a bowl of white vinegar on the top rack and run a hot cycle without dishes. This helps remove mineral buildup and grease from the interior, including areas the filter might not catch. This step can also prevent issues like [why is my dishwasher filter full of water](https://homeessentialsguide.com/why-is-my-dishwasher-filter-full-of-water). It keeps the entire system fresh.

### Check for Blockages

Occasionally, do a quick visual check of the filter before loading. If you see visible large food particles, remove them then and there. You do not need to do a full filter removal and wash every time you do this. Just picking out the obvious bits helps. This proactive approach keeps the filter from becoming completely overwhelmed between thorough cleanings. A little prevention goes a long way.

## Frequently Asked Questions About Cleaning Your Lamona Dishwasher Filter

### How often should I clean my Lamona dishwasher filter?
You should clean your Lamona dishwasher filter at least once a month. If you use your dishwasher daily or wash dishes with heavy food residue, consider cleaning it every two weeks. Regular checks ensure optimal performance and prevent odors.

### What happens if I don't clean my Lamona dishwasher filter?
If you neglect to clean your Lamona dishwasher filter, food particles will build up. This leads to poor washing results, leaving grime on dishes. It can also cause unpleasant odors inside the dishwasher and put strain on the appliance's motor, potentially shortening its lifespan.

### Can I put my Lamona dishwasher filter in the dishwasher?
No, you should not put your Lamona dishwasher filter in the dishwasher. The dishwasher itself cannot properly clean its own filter. Manual cleaning under running water with a brush and mild soap is necessary to effectively remove all trapped food debris and grease.

### Why does my Lamona dishwasher still smell after cleaning the filter?
If your Lamona dishwasher still smells after cleaning the filter, the odor may stem from other areas. Check the spray arms for clogged holes. Run a hot cycle with a dishwasher cleaner or a bowl of white vinegar on the top rack. This helps eliminate grease and mildew from the tub and pipes.

### My Lamona dishwasher filter is very sticky/greasy, what should I do?
For a very sticky or greasy Lamona dishwasher filter, soak it in warm water with a generous amount of mild dish soap for 15-20 minutes. You can also add half a cup of white vinegar to the soaking solution. This will help break down the grease, making it easier to scrub away with a soft brush.

### How do I know if my Lamona dishwasher filter is clogged?
Signs of a clogged Lamona dishwasher filter include dishes not coming out clean, food particles redeposited on dishes, standing water at the bottom of the dishwasher, or unpleasant odors. If you notice any of these issues, it is a clear sign your filter needs immediate cleaning.
