## Are Blackstone Accessories Dishwasher Safe? The Ultimate Guide

Grilling with a Blackstone griddle is a fantastic experience. You cook delicious meals and create lasting memories. After the fun, cleaning up is next. Many people wonder, "Are Blackstone accessories dishwasher safe?" This question comes up often. Knowing how to properly clean your tools keeps them in great shape.

It also extends their lifespan. Some accessories can go into the dishwasher without issue. Others need a different cleaning method. We want to help you understand which is which. This guide will cover different accessory types. We will explain the best cleaning practices for each. You will learn how to care for your spatulas, scrapers, and more. This information helps you maintain your Blackstone gear. Let's make your post-grilling cleanup easy and effective.

### Takeaway:

*   Most metal Blackstone accessories are not dishwasher safe due to rust risk.
*   Plastic squeeze bottles are often dishwasher safe, but check labels.
*   Silicone items typically withstand dishwasher cycles.
*   Hand washing is the safest and best method for most Blackstone accessories.
*   Always check product labels for specific cleaning instructions.

### The Concise Answer:

Many Blackstone accessories are *not* dishwasher safe, especially those made from carbon steel, stainless steel with wooden handles, or certain plastics. Dishwashing can cause rust, dull finishes, or material damage. Always hand wash these items with warm, soapy water to ensure their longevity and performance. Some silicone or specific plastic items might be safe; check individual product labels for confirmation.

### Understanding Dishwasher Safety Labels on Blackstone Accessories

When you buy Blackstone accessories, you might see care instructions. These instructions tell you how to clean each item. Sometimes, products have a symbol that shows if they are dishwasher safe. This symbol usually looks like a dishwasher with water drops or dishes inside. It is important to look for these labels. They guide you on proper cleaning.

Different materials react differently to dishwashers. Hot water, harsh detergents, and high-pressure sprays can damage certain items. For instance, metal accessories can rust if they are not meant for dishwashers. Plastics might warp or melt. Wood can crack or splinter. Always check the packaging or the item itself for a clear label. If you do not see a label, it is always safer to hand wash. This protects your accessories from potential damage. I always check my labels first.

#### Common Materials and Dishwasher Suitability

Blackstone accessories use various materials. Each material has unique properties. These properties determine if it can go into a dishwasher. Understanding these materials helps you make good cleaning choices.

*   **Stainless Steel:** Many spatulas and scrapers are stainless steel. Pure stainless steel is often dishwasher safe. However, stainless steel can still develop water spots or discoloration. Accessories with wooden handles attached to stainless steel are *not* dishwasher safe. The wood will get damaged.
*   **Carbon Steel/Cast Iron:** Items like griddle press weights or some scrapers might be carbon steel or cast iron. These materials are definitely *not* dishwasher safe. They will rust quickly. They also lose their seasoning. Seasoning is a protective layer that forms on these metals.
*   **Plastics:** Squeeze bottles, some handles, and measuring cups are plastic. Some plastics are dishwasher safe, especially those marked with a "top rack safe" symbol. Plastics rated "5" (polypropylene) are generally safer for dishwashers. Others, like thin plastics, might melt or warp. You can learn more about plastic safety [Is Plastic Safe in Dishwasher](https://homeessentialsguide.com/is-plastic-safe-in-dishwasher). For specifics, check [Is 5 Plastic Dishwasher Safe](https://homeessentialsguide.com/is-5-plastic-dishwasher-safe).
*   **Silicone:** Silicone is very heat resistant. Items like spatulas or basting brushes made of silicone are usually dishwasher safe. They do not melt or warp easily. Silicone is a great material for dishwasher use.
*   **Wood:** Wooden handles or cutting boards are never dishwasher safe. Water and heat will cause wood to swell, crack, and lose its finish. Always hand wash wood items. Dry them immediately to prevent damage.

Ignoring these guidelines can ruin your favorite tools. It is best to take a moment to understand each accessory. Proper care saves you money and keeps your cooking enjoyable.

### Specific Blackstone Accessory Types and Their Cleaning Needs

Blackstone offers many useful accessories. Each one helps you cook better. Knowing how to clean each type is important. We will look at common accessories. This section gives specific advice for each.

#### Griddle Spatulas and Turners

You use spatulas and turners constantly on your griddle. Most Blackstone spatulas have stainless steel blades. Some feature wooden handles. Others have plastic or heat-resistant composite handles.

*   **Stainless Steel Blades with Wooden Handles:** These are *not* dishwasher safe. The wooden handles absorb water. This causes them to swell, crack, and eventually break. The wood finish can also strip away. Always hand wash these. Use warm, soapy water. Rinse them well and dry them immediately.
*   **Stainless Steel Blades with Plastic or Composite Handles:** Many of these are dishwasher safe. Check for a "dishwasher safe" label. If present, place them on the top rack. High heat on the bottom rack can sometimes affect plastic handles over time. If no label exists, hand wash to be safe.
*   **Full Stainless Steel Spatulas:** These are generally dishwasher safe. They are very durable. They can still get water spots if not dried quickly. You might want to hand wash them for a cleaner finish.

#### Griddle Scrapers and Choppers

Griddle scrapers help clean the surface. They also cut food. They often have sturdy metal blades. Handles can be wood, plastic, or composite.

*   **Metal Blades with Wooden Handles:** Like spatulas, these are *not* dishwasher safe. Wood and water do not mix well in a dishwasher. Hand wash them carefully. Dry them completely after washing.
*   **Metal Blades with Plastic or Composite Handles:** These are often dishwasher safe. Again, look for the symbol. Put them on the top rack if possible. The metal blade can be sharp. Handle with care when loading or unloading.
*   **Heavy-Duty Griddle Scrapers (Cast Iron/Carbon Steel):** Absolutely *not* dishwasher safe. These will rust very fast. They also need seasoning. Hand wash gently with minimal soap, if any. Dry completely and re-oil if needed. This is similar to how you care for cast iron cookware.

#### Squeeze Bottles and Dredge Shakers

Squeeze bottles hold oil or water. Dredge shakers hold seasonings. They are typically made of plastic.

*   **Plastic Squeeze Bottles:** Most plastic squeeze bottles are dishwasher safe. They are designed for food contact. Check the bottom of the bottle for a recycling symbol (e.g., #2 HDPE or #5 PP). Many #5 plastics are safe for dishwashers. Usually, they are best placed on the top rack. This prevents warping from intense heat. Make sure to remove the cap and wash it separately.
*   **Plastic Dredge Shakers:** Similar to squeeze bottles, these are generally dishwasher safe. Ensure they are completely empty before washing. Again, place them on the top rack.

#### Griddle Press Weights and Covers

These items are often larger and heavier. They require different cleaning approaches.

*   **Griddle Press Weights (Cast Iron/Metal):** These are *not* dishwasher safe. Cast iron presses need careful cleaning. Hand wash them. Dry them immediately. Re-season them with oil to prevent rust. Neglecting this will cause rust.
*   **Griddle Covers/Lids:** Most griddle covers are metal. They are often too large for a dishwasher. Even if they fit, the material might not be suitable. Hand wash covers with warm, soapy water. Rinse and dry thoroughly. This prevents water spots and potential rust.
*   **Accessory Kits:** Blackstone sells accessory kits. These usually contain a mix of items. Check each item in the kit for specific cleaning instructions. A kit might include items that are dishwasher safe and items that are not. For general tips on cleaning with a kit, see [How to Clean Blackstone Griddle with Kit](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-clean-blackstone-griddle-with-kit).

By following these guidelines, your Blackstone accessories will last longer. They will also perform better. Taking care of your tools is part of the fun of griddling.

### Why Some Blackstone Accessories Are Not Dishwasher Safe

You might wonder why dishwashers are bad for some items. Dishwashers use high temperatures. They also use strong detergents. The water jets are powerful. These elements can harm certain materials. Understanding these reasons helps you make smart cleaning choices.

#### Rust and Corrosion

Many Blackstone accessories are made from carbon steel or a blend of metals. These materials are excellent for cooking. However, they are prone to rust. A dishwasher exposes items to hot water for a long time. It also uses strong chemical detergents. These conditions strip away protective coatings. They remove the vital seasoning on carbon steel or cast iron. Once this coating is gone, rust forms quickly.

For example, a cast iron press weight will rust after one dishwasher cycle. Even some stainless steel items can corrode. They might develop pitting or dull spots over time. This happens if the protective chromium oxide layer breaks down. This layer makes stainless steel "stain-less," not "stain-proof." Dishwasher salts can also contribute to corrosion. My own experience shows that a quick hand wash preserves tools much better. You can find more about preventing rust here: [How to Clean Rust Off of Blackstone](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-clean-rust-off-of-blackstone).

#### Material Degradation and Warping

Dishwashers use high heat. This heat can be very damaging to plastics and wood.

*   **Plastics:** Many plastic accessories are not designed for high heat. The hot water in a dishwasher can cause plastics to warp. They might lose their original shape. This makes them unusable. Colors can also fade or leach out. This is especially true for plastics that are not "top rack safe." Top rack positions usually have lower heat exposure. Cheaper plastics are more susceptible to this damage.
*   **Wood:** Wooden handles on spatulas or scrapers are particularly vulnerable. Hot water and detergents strip natural oils from the wood. This causes the wood to dry out. It can swell during the wash and then shrink when drying. This leads to cracks and splinters. The wood can also become rough or discolored. Over time, the handle might detach from the metal part. This makes the accessory unsafe to use.

#### Dulled Finishes and Stripped Coatings

Some accessories have special finishes or coatings. These enhance their look or function. Dishwashers can ruin these.

*   **Non-Stick Coatings:** While less common on griddle accessories, some specialized tools might have a coating. Dishwashers can scratch or degrade these coatings.
*   **Polished Metals:** Stainless steel items might have a polished finish. Dishwashers can dull this shine. They can also leave water spots that are hard to remove. This affects the appearance of your tools.
*   **Seasoning:** For cast iron or carbon steel items, seasoning is a vital, thin layer of polymerized oil. It creates a natural non-stick surface. It also protects against rust. Dishwashers aggressively remove this seasoning. You then have to re-season the item completely. This is a time-consuming process.

In general, dishwashers are convenient for many kitchen items. However, for specialized griddle accessories, they pose too many risks. Hand washing is almost always the safer and better option. It preserves the integrity and lifespan of your Blackstone tools. I always recommend hand washing for anything not explicitly labeled "dishwasher safe." For a broader understanding of what to avoid, consider reading [What is Not Dishwasher Safe](https://homeessentialsguide.com/what-is-not-dishwasher-safe).

### Manual Cleaning Methods for Blackstone Accessories

Hand washing is the best way to clean most Blackstone accessories. It protects them from harsh conditions inside a dishwasher. Manual cleaning extends the life of your tools. It also keeps them looking new. I always prefer hand washing for my griddle accessories. It gives me control and ensures proper care.

#### Basic Hand Washing Steps

Cleaning your accessories by hand is simple. It does not take much time. You need a few basic supplies.

1.  **Gather Supplies:** Get warm water, dish soap, a non-abrasive sponge or brush, and a clean towel.
2.  **Rinse Immediately:** After cooking, rinse accessories with warm water. This removes loose food bits before they dry. It makes cleaning much easier.
3.  **Wash with Soap:** Apply a small amount of dish soap to your sponge. Scrub each accessory thoroughly. Pay attention to all surfaces, including handles. Make sure to get rid of all grease and food residue.
4.  **Rinse Again:** Rinse the accessories under warm running water. Ensure all soap is removed. Soap residue can leave a film or affect food taste later.
5.  **Dry Immediately:** This is crucial, especially for metal items. Use a clean, dry towel to wipe each accessory. Do not let them air dry. Air drying can lead to water spots or rust, even on stainless steel.

#### Specialized Cleaning for Different Materials

Certain materials need specific care during hand washing. Knowing these details helps preserve your accessories.

*   **Wooden Handles:** Wash wooden handles gently with a damp cloth and mild soap. Do not soak them in water. Soaking causes warping and cracking. Rinse quickly and dry immediately. You can occasionally condition wood with food-grade mineral oil. This prevents drying and maintains the wood's integrity.
*   **Carbon Steel/Cast Iron:** For items like griddle presses, avoid strong soaps. A quick rinse and gentle scrub with warm water are often enough. If soap is needed, use a very mild one. Rinse well. Immediately dry the item completely. Then, apply a very thin layer of cooking oil. This helps maintain the seasoning and prevents rust. This method is similar to general griddle cleaning, as detailed in [How to Clean My Blackstone Griddle](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-clean-my-blackstone-griddle).
*   **Squeeze Bottles and Shakers:** These can be tricky to clean inside. Use a bottle brush for thorough cleaning. Warm soapy water works well. Rinse completely to remove all soap residue. You can also let them air dry upside down to ensure no water remains.
*   **Silicone Tools:** Silicone is very easy to clean. Warm soapy water is usually sufficient. Rinse well. Silicone does not rust or warp. It can be air dried or towel dried.
*   **Heavy-Duty Scrapers:** These often have tough food residue. Use a sturdy brush or even a plastic scraper to remove stuck-on bits. Then wash with soap and water. Dry thoroughly.

Hand washing is a small effort that yields big rewards. It keeps your Blackstone accessories in top condition. You will enjoy using them for many years. Regular and proper cleaning ensures they are always ready for your next griddling adventure.

### Extending the Life of Your Blackstone Accessories

Taking good care of your Blackstone accessories goes beyond just cleaning. Proper storage, maintenance, and even usage habits can significantly extend their lifespan. I have learned that a little extra effort here saves money and hassle in the long run.

#### Proper Storage Techniques

Where and how you store your accessories matters. Incorrect storage can lead to damage, rust, or degradation.

*   **Dry Environment:** Always store accessories in a dry place. Humidity is the enemy of metal. It encourages rust. Ensure items are completely dry before storing them.
*   **Organized Storage:** Use hooks, drawers, or tool caddies to keep accessories organized. This prevents them from knocking against each other. It reduces scratches and damage. A dedicated storage bag or box for smaller items is helpful.
*   **Away from Elements:** If storing outdoors with your griddle, use a waterproof cover for the griddle. For accessories, consider bringing them indoors. Outdoor elements like rain, dew, and extreme temperatures are harsh.

#### Regular Maintenance and Care

Maintenance is key to longevity. It involves a few simple steps.

*   **Seasoning Metal Items:** For carbon steel or cast iron accessories (like griddle presses), re-season them periodically. After cleaning and drying, apply a thin layer of high-smoke-point oil (like flaxseed or grapeseed oil). Heat the item briefly on low heat until the oil smokes. Wipe off excess oil. This maintains the protective, non-stick layer and prevents rust.
*   **Conditioning Wooden Handles:** Wood dries out over time. Apply a food-grade mineral oil to wooden handles every few months. This keeps the wood supple. It prevents cracking and splitting. Wipe off any excess oil.
*   **Checking for Wear:** Regularly inspect your accessories. Look for signs of rust, loose handles, or cracks. Addressing small issues early prevents bigger problems.
*   **Cleaning Your Griddle:** A clean griddle also helps. If your griddle itself is rusty or dirty, it might affect accessories too. Keeping your primary cooking surface clean is vital. Learn more about it here: [How to Clean My Blackstone Griddle](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-clean-my-blackstone-griddle).

#### Mindful Usage Habits

How you use your tools also impacts their lifespan.

*   **Use the Right Tool for the Job:** Avoid using your griddle scraper as a pry bar. Do not use metal spatulas on non-stick surfaces (if you have them) to avoid scratches. Using tools for their intended purpose prevents unnecessary stress and damage.
*   **Avoid Excessive Heat Exposure:** While griddle accessories are made for heat, do not leave them sitting on a hot griddle unnecessarily. Prolonged exposure can degrade materials, especially plastic and wood handles.
*   **Gentle Handling:** Dropping tools can bend them, chip handles, or damage blades. Handle your accessories with care.

By incorporating these practices, you ensure your Blackstone accessories remain functional and reliable. They will be ready for every cooking adventure. It is a small investment of time for a significant return in durability.

### Common Mistakes When Cleaning Blackstone Accessories

Even with good intentions, people make mistakes when cleaning. These errors can shorten the life of your Blackstone accessories. Avoiding these common pitfalls helps keep your tools in prime condition. I have made some of these mistakes myself, and learned valuable lessons.

#### Using Abrasive Cleaners or Scouring Pads

Abrasive materials can damage surfaces.

*   **Scratching Metal:** Metal spatulas and scrapers often have a smooth finish. Using steel wool, highly abrasive scrubbers, or harsh scouring pads can scratch this finish. Scratches not only look bad but can also create tiny crevices where food and moisture can hide, potentially leading to rust or bacterial growth.
*   **Damaging Seasoning:** For cast iron or carbon steel items, abrasive cleaners strip away the crucial seasoning layer. This makes the item vulnerable to rust and less non-stick. Always opt for a soft sponge or a brush with plastic bristles.

#### Soaking Wooden or Metal Items

Soaking seems like an easy way to loosen grime. However, it is very harmful for certain materials.

*   **Wooden Handles:** Soaking wooden handles causes them to swell and then crack as they dry. It also weakens the bond between the wood and the metal part. This makes the handle loose or break off.
*   **Metal Items:** Soaking metal items, especially carbon steel or cast iron, for extended periods in water will almost guarantee rust formation. Even stainless steel can develop water spots or pitting if left in standing water. It's best to wash quickly and dry immediately.

#### Not Drying Accessories Immediately and Thoroughly

This is one of the most common and damaging mistakes.

*   **Rust Formation:** Any residual water on metal accessories, particularly carbon steel or cast iron, will lead to rust. Even "stainless" steel can rust if left wet for too long, especially in hard water areas.
*   **Water Spots:** Water spots can form on stainless steel. They are unsightly and sometimes hard to remove.
*   **Bacterial Growth:** Moisture can also encourage bacterial growth on any surface, even if it does not cause rust. Always use a clean, dry towel to wipe down all accessories immediately after washing. This ensures they are completely dry before storage.

#### Ignoring Product Labels and Manufacturer Instructions

Manufacturers provide care instructions for a reason.

*   **Specific Material Needs:** Different materials require different care. A manufacturer knows the specific composition of their product. Their instructions are tailored to prevent damage.
*   **Voiding Warranties:** Improper cleaning can sometimes void a product's warranty. If an item is damaged because you put it in the dishwasher against instructions, the company might not replace it. Always check the packaging or the item itself for cleaning symbols and text. If in doubt, hand wash.

Avoiding these common mistakes will significantly extend the life and appearance of your Blackstone accessories. A little attention to detail during cleaning saves you money and keeps your griddling experience top-notch.

### When to Replace Your Blackstone Accessories

Even with the best care, accessories do not last forever. Knowing when to replace your Blackstone accessories is important. Using damaged tools can affect your cooking. It can also pose safety risks. I always check my tools regularly.

#### Signs of Wear and Tear

Look for specific signs that tell you an accessory is past its prime. These indicators suggest it is time for a replacement.

*   **Excessive Rust:** Some minor surface rust can be cleaned. However, deep or widespread rust indicates the metal is compromised. Rust can flake off into food. It can also weaken the tool's structure. If rust returns quickly after cleaning, it is time for a new one. You can try to clean it first, as outlined in [How to Clean Rust Off of Blackstone](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-clean-rust-off-of-blackstone), but know when to let go.
*   **Bent or Damaged Blades:** Spatula and scraper blades can bend or get nicks. A bent blade does not work effectively. It cannot scrape the griddle surface cleanly. Nicks can harbor food particles. They might also scratch your griddle surface.
*   **Loose or Cracked Handles:** Wooden handles can crack. Plastic handles can become brittle. If a handle is loose, it is unsafe. The metal part can detach while you are cooking. A cracked handle can harbor bacteria. It is also uncomfortable to hold.
*   **Melted or Warped Plastic:** Squeeze bottles or other plastic items can warp from heat. This makes them difficult to use. Warped plastic might also leach chemicals into food over time. If a bottle no longer seals correctly, it is useless.
*   **Difficulty Cleaning:** If an accessory consistently holds onto grime, even after thorough cleaning, it might be too worn. Deep scratches or pitting can make cleaning impossible. This creates an unhygienic tool.

#### Safety Concerns

Safety is paramount when cooking. Damaged tools can be dangerous.

*   **Risk of Injury:** A loose handle could cause a hot spatula to slip. A sharp, bent blade could cut you. Using a compromised tool increases the risk of accidents.
*   **Food Contamination:** Rust flakes, degraded plastic, or splintering wood can all get into your food. This is a health hazard. Ensuring your tools are in good condition protects your family's health.
*   **Reduced Performance:** Damaged accessories simply do not work well. A dull scraper cannot clean the griddle. A warped squeeze bottle cannot dispense oil properly. This frustration detracts from the cooking experience.

#### When to Consider Upgrading

Sometimes, replacement is not due to damage but for improvement.

*   **New Materials/Designs:** Blackstone regularly updates its accessory line. New materials might offer better heat resistance or durability. New designs might provide better ergonomics or functionality.
*   **Expand Your Collection:** As you cook more diverse meals, you might need specialized tools. A dedicated burger press, a different type of spatula, or an additional oil bottle can enhance your cooking.

Replacing worn-out accessories is a small investment. It ensures your safety and continues your enjoyment of griddle cooking. Do not wait until an item completely breaks. Act when you see the early signs of wear.

### FAQ Section

#### Q1: Can I put my Blackstone spatulas in the dishwasher?
Most Blackstone spatulas with wooden handles are not dishwasher safe. The wood will crack and splinter from the heat and water. Spatulas made entirely of stainless steel or with durable plastic handles may be dishwasher safe, but always check the product label first. Hand washing is the safest method for all spatulas.

#### Q2: Are Blackstone squeeze bottles dishwasher safe?
Yes, most Blackstone plastic squeeze bottles are designed to be dishwasher safe, especially on the top rack. They are typically made from durable plastics like HDPE or PP. Always remove the cap and wash both parts separately. Check the bottom of the bottle for a "dishwasher safe" symbol or plastic recycling number (e.g., #5).

#### Q3: Will my cast iron griddle press rust in the dishwasher?
Absolutely. Cast iron griddle presses are never dishwasher safe. The hot water and harsh detergents will strip away the seasoning and cause the cast iron to rust quickly. Hand wash cast iron presses with minimal soap, dry them immediately, and re-season them with a thin layer of cooking oil to protect them.

#### Q4: How do I clean my Blackstone accessories if they are not dishwasher safe?
Hand wash non-dishwasher safe accessories with warm water and mild dish soap. Use a non-abrasive sponge or brush. Rinse thoroughly to remove all soap residue. Crucially, dry all metal and wooden items immediately with a clean towel to prevent rust or water damage. Avoid soaking wooden handles.

#### Q5: Can I put accessories with silicone components in the dishwasher?
Yes, silicone components are generally very heat resistant and dishwasher safe. Many Blackstone tools, like basting brushes or spatulas with silicone heads, can go into the dishwasher without issue. Silicone does not rust, warp, or absorb water, making it an excellent material for dishwasher cleaning.

#### Q6: What happens if I accidentally put a non-dishwasher safe accessory in the dishwasher?
If you accidentally put a non-dishwasher safe item in the dishwasher, check it immediately. For wooden handles, they might swell or crack. For carbon steel/cast iron, rust may form quickly. Remove the item, hand wash, dry thoroughly, and if it's carbon steel/cast iron, re-season it. Some damage, like severe warping or cracking, may be irreversible.
