## Diagnose Your Dishwasher: Test Its Drain Pump With An Ohmmeter

A dishwasher that does not drain dirty water leaves you with a big mess. This problem often points to a faulty drain pump. Knowing **how to test a dishwasher drain pump with an ohmmeter** can save you frustration and repair costs. I will guide you through the exact steps for this important diagnostic task.

This article covers everything from preparing for the test to interpreting your ohmmeter readings. We will discuss safety measures and the tools you need. You will learn how to access the pump and perform a precise electrical check. By the end, you will understand if your drain pump needs replacement or if another issue is present.

### Takeaway:

*   Always shut off electrical power to the dishwasher before any work.
*   Locate and safely detach the dishwasher drain pump.
*   Use an ohmmeter to check the pump's internal electrical path.
*   An "OL" reading or infinite resistance means the pump is bad.
*   Replace the pump if the ohmmeter test shows it is faulty.

### Clear Answer:

To test a dishwasher drain pump with an ohmmeter, first disconnect the dishwasher's power. Access the drain pump and detach its electrical wires. Set your ohmmeter to the ohms function. Touch the ohmmeter probes to the pump's electrical terminals. A functional pump shows a specific resistance reading, while an "OL" reading indicates an open circuit and a failed pump.
