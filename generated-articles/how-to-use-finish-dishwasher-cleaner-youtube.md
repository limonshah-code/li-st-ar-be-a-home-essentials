### Troubleshooting Common Dishwasher Cleaning Issues

Even with regular cleaning, you might encounter some common dishwasher issues. Knowing how to address them can save you time and frustration. I have helped many friends solve these problems over the years.

**Issue 1: Dishwasher still smells after cleaning.**
If your dishwasher still has an odor, it might be a deeper issue. First, recheck the drain filter. It might still have some stubborn food particles. A thorough scrub with a brush and dish soap can often solve this. Also, check the bottom of the dishwasher tub for any standing water. Sometimes, a partial clog in the drain hose can prevent full drainage. Running another hot cycle with a cleaner can also help.

**Issue 2: White film on dishes or inside the dishwasher.**
A white film usually indicates hard water problems or insufficient rinse aid. If you have hard water, consider using dishwasher salt in addition to detergent. Increase the rinse aid setting if your machine allows it. A second cleaning cycle with Finish Dishwasher Cleaner can help remove existing film inside the machine. Make sure you are using enough detergent. Sometimes, using too little detergent can cause this.

**Issue 3: Food particles remain after wash cycle.**
This points to issues with water circulation or spray arm blockages. Ensure the spray arms spin freely and their holes are clear. Remove the filter and clean it thoroughly. Check if dishes are loaded correctly and not blocking the spray arms. If these steps do not help, your water pump might be weak, or there could be a clog in the water lines. Running the Finish cleaner helps clear internal blockages.

**Issue 4: Dishwasher not draining completely.**
A common cause is a clogged drain filter. Clean the filter as described earlier. Check the drain hose for kinks or blockages. Sometimes, food debris gets trapped in the hose. If your dishwasher connects to a garbage disposal, ensure the disposal is clear. Run the disposal before starting the dishwasher. For more serious issues, you might need a plumber.

**Issue 5: Dishwasher cleaning takes too long.**
The length of a dishwasher cycle can vary. The hottest or heavy-duty cycles will naturally take longer. If a regular cycle seems unusually long, it might be due to sensor issues or heating problems. Make sure your water heater is set to an adequate temperature. A dishwasher needs hot water to activate detergents and sanitize. The Finish cleaner cycle should match your selected hot wash program.

**Issue 6: Finish cleaner bottle doesn't empty.**
Ensure you placed the bottle upside down in the bottom rack. The wax plug must be facing up. The water temperature must be hot enough to melt the wax. Run the hottest cycle available. If the water is not hot, the wax plug will not dissolve. Make sure your hot water supply is working correctly. A common mistake is using a cold or quick wash cycle.

These troubleshooting tips cover most common issues. Addressing them promptly keeps your dishwasher working efficiently. Regular use of Finish cleaner often prevents these problems from starting.
