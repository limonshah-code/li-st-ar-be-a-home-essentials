## Fix Your Bosch Dishwasher's "Check Water" Error

Imagine this: you load your Bosch dishwasher, press start, and instead of sparkling dishes, you see a "Check Water" message on the display. This error stops your appliance from working. It means your dishwasher is not getting enough water, or it detects a water supply issue. You need to fix this problem for your dishwasher to operate again.

This guide helps you understand why your Bosch dishwasher displays "Check Water." We cover common causes, from simple water supply issues to parts that need inspection. You will learn easy steps to diagnose and solve the problem yourself. We also discuss when it is time to call a professional. This article gives you the tools to get your Bosch dishwasher back in action.

### Takeaway:

*   Verify the water supply valve is open.
*   Inspect water inlet hoses for kinks or clogs.
*   Clean the dishwasher filters and check the drain hose.
*   Reset your Bosch dishwasher to clear temporary errors.
*   Check for leaks in the base pan, which can activate the flood switch.

When your Bosch dishwasher shows "Check Water," it means the appliance cannot fill with water or senses a supply issue. To fix this, ensure the water supply valve is open, check the inlet hose for kinks, clean filters, and confirm proper drainage.

## Understanding the "Check Water" Error on Your Bosch Dishwasher

The "Check Water" error on a Bosch dishwasher indicates a problem with water entering the machine. Your dishwasher needs a specific amount of water to clean dishes effectively. Understanding the root cause is the first step in fixing it.

Several factors can trigger this error. Sometimes, it is as simple as a closed water valve. Other times, it involves internal components like the water inlet valve or a clogged filter. The error is a warning system. It tells you to investigate the water flow into and out of the appliance. You need to address this warning to restore normal function. A prompt response helps prevent further issues or damage.

The "Check Water" error is essentially a water supply warning. Your Bosch dishwasher detects that it is not receiving the expected water input. This could be due to no water entering the machine at all. It might also happen if the water pressure is too low. Another reason could be that the dishwasher cannot drain properly, which prevents new water from entering. ## Initial Troubleshooting: The Easy Fixes

Before diving into complex repairs, always start with the simplest solutions. Many "Check Water" errors on Bosch dishwashers have straightforward causes. These initial steps are quick and often resolve the issue without needing tools or special expertise. Always unplug the dishwasher from its power source before touching any components. Safety is paramount when working with appliances.

First, check your home's main water supply. Ensure that water flows normally to other faucets in your house. Sometimes, a house-wide water outage or low pressure can cause this error. Next, locate the specific water supply valve for your dishwasher. This valve is usually under the sink or behind the dishwasher itself. Make sure the valve is fully open. A partially closed valve restricts water flow.

Another common culprit is a kinked water inlet hose. The hose connects your dishwasher to the water supply. It can get bent or twisted, especially if the dishwasher was recently moved. Gently pull the dishwasher out a bit and inspect the hose. Straighten any kinks you find. Also, check for any visible damage or leaks in the hose. If the hose is crimped, water cannot pass through.

Finally, a simple power cycle can sometimes clear temporary glitches. Unplug the dishwasher from the wall outlet. Wait for about 5-10 minutes, then plug it back in. This reset can clear minor electronic errors. After plugging it back in, try running a short cycle to see if the "Check Water" message disappears. This helps determine if the issue was a temporary software glitch. Always try these easy steps before moving to more advanced solutions.

## Inspecting the Water Inlet System

If the initial checks did not resolve the "Check Water" error, the problem might lie within the dishwasher's water inlet system. This system includes the inlet hose, the water inlet valve, and sometimes a flow meter. Each component plays a vital role in getting water into your Bosch dishwasher. You need to inspect them for blockages or malfunctions. This inspection often requires accessing the back or bottom of the dishwasher. Remember to disconnect power before starting this work.

Begin by examining the water inlet hose more closely. You already checked for kinks, but now look for clogs. Sediment or mineral deposits can build up inside the hose over time. Disconnect the hose from both the water supply and the dishwasher. Hold the hose over a bucket and turn on the water supply briefly to check for flow. If water trickles out, the hose might be partially blocked. You may need to clean or replace it. For more details on checking water flow, see [how to check water in Bosch dishwasher](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-check-water-in-bosch-dishwasher).

Next, focus on the water inlet valve. This valve is an electrically controlled device that opens to allow water into the dishwasher. It is typically located at the bottom-front of the dishwasher, behind the kickplate. You can test the valve for power using a multimeter if you are comfortable. When the dishwasher is running, the valve should receive 120V AC. If it gets power but does not open, the valve is likely faulty and needs replacement. A faulty water inlet valve is a common reason [why is Bosch dishwasher not filling with water](https://homeessentialsguide.com/why-is-bosch-dishwasher-not-filling-with-water).

Some Bosch models also have a flow meter. This small device measures the amount of water entering the dishwasher. It is often located near the water inlet valve or inside the dishwasher tub. If the flow meter malfunctions, it might send incorrect signals to the control board. The control board then thinks there is no water, even if water is entering. Inspect the flow meter for debris or damage. A quick cleaning might fix it. If it looks fine but the error persists after checking the valve, the flow meter might be faulty and require replacement. These components are critical for proper water intake.

## Examining the Drainage System

The "Check Water" error might seem to point to a filling problem, but sometimes drainage issues are the real cause. If your Bosch dishwasher cannot drain water properly from a previous cycle, it will not allow new water to enter. The machine detects that the tub is not empty and pauses to prevent overfilling. Therefore, inspecting the drainage system is a crucial step in troubleshooting this error. You must ensure clear pathways for water to exit the appliance.

Start by checking the dishwasher's filters. Most Bosch dishwashers have a multi-part filter system at the bottom of the tub. Food particles, grease, and debris can accumulate here. A clogged filter severely restricts water flow, both for draining and potentially for proper circulation during a wash cycle. Remove the filter assembly according to your dishwasher's manual. Clean it thoroughly under running water, using a brush if necessary, to remove all debris. This is a vital maintenance step. For detailed instructions, refer to [how to clean your Bosch dishwasher filter](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-clean-your-bosch-dishwasher-filter).

Next, inspect the drain hose. This hose carries dirty water from the dishwasher to your sink drain or garbage disposal. Look for kinks or blockages along its entire length. The hose should have a high loop to prevent backflow into the dishwasher. If it sags, water can sit in the hose, causing issues. Disconnect the drain hose from both ends and check for internal clogs. You might find food debris or grease buildup inside. Clear any obstructions you find.

Consider the air gap, if your dishwasher has one. An air gap is a small cylindrical device mounted on your sink or countertop. It prevents drain water from siphoning back into the dishwasher. Air gaps can become clogged with food particles. Unscrew its cap and remove any debris inside. Ensure the air gap allows water to flow freely. If your Bosch dishwasher has standing water at the bottom, even after cleaning filters and checking hoses, it indicates a drainage issue. This issue could prevent new water from entering, causing the "Check Water" message. For further help, consider exploring resources on [how to fix Bosch dishwasher not draining](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-fix-bosch-dishwasher-not-draining).

Finally, the drain pump might be at fault. This pump pushes water out of the dishwasher. If it is faulty or has debris stuck in its impeller, water will not drain. You can sometimes hear if the pump is trying to work during the drain cycle. A humming noise with no water movement suggests an issue. Inspecting or replacing the drain pump usually requires disassembling parts of the dishwasher's base. This step might require a professional if you are uncomfortable with appliance repair.

## Addressing Water Pressure and Supply Issues

Sometimes, the "Check Water" error is not due to a faulty dishwasher part but an issue with your home's water supply. Low water pressure can prevent the dishwasher from filling adequately. Even if the water inlet valve opens, insufficient pressure means the dishwasher does not receive the required volume of water within the set time. Your Bosch dishwasher sensors detect this low fill rate and trigger the error. Therefore, checking your home's water pressure is an important diagnostic step.

You can easily check your home's general water pressure. Turn on a faucet in your kitchen sink or bathroom. Observe the strength of the water flow. If the flow seems weaker than usual, you might have low household water pressure. This can be a neighborhood-wide issue, especially during peak usage times. It could also be specific to your home's plumbing. If other appliances also struggle with water, like a washing machine or shower, then the problem is likely with your main water supply.

Beyond general pressure, check the specific water supply line connected to your Bosch dishwasher. The fill hose itself has a screen filter where it connects to the water supply valve. This small screen can become clogged with sediment or rust particles over time. Turn off the water supply valve to the dishwasher. Disconnect the fill hose from the valve and check the screen inside the hose fitting. Clean it thoroughly with a small brush or toothpick to remove any debris. Reconnect the hose and turn the water supply back on. A clean screen ensures unrestricted water flow to the inlet valve.

Another consideration is how the water line is connected. Some Bosch dishwashers can be connected to hot water. While not directly related to a "Check Water" error, an improper hot water connection or very high temperatures could, in rare cases, affect sensor readings or component life. Ensuring proper installation helps avoid future issues. If you are unsure about your dishwasher's water connection, consult its manual.

Finally, if you suspect very low water pressure is the core problem, you might need to consult a plumber. They can measure your home's water pressure using specialized tools. They can also identify if a pressure regulator is faulty or if there are other issues with your main water line. A licensed plumber can properly assess and resolve house-wide water pressure problems. This ensures all your appliances, including your Bosch dishwasher, receive optimal water flow for efficient operation.

## Checking for Leaks and AquaStop System Activation

One critical reason for a "Check Water" error on a Bosch dishwasher, often overlooked, is the activation of the AquaStop system. Bosch dishwashers are equipped with this safety feature to prevent water leaks and potential flooding. If water collects in the dishwasher's base pan, the AquaStop system's float switch activates. This activation immediately cuts off the water supply to the dishwasher and triggers the "Check Water" error or a specific leak error code. This means your dishwasher is working to prevent a flood, not necessarily that it is lacking water.

To check for AquaStop activation, you must access the dishwasher's base pan. This usually involves carefully pulling the dishwasher out from its cabinet enclosure. For some models, you might need to remove the front kickplate or even tip the dishwasher back slightly (at a 45-degree angle, but be careful not to spill any residual water). Look into the base of the appliance. You are searching for any standing water in the pan beneath the dishwasher's tub. Even a small amount of water can trigger the float switch. If you find water, it indicates a leak somewhere within the dishwasher.

Identifying the source of the leak is the next step. Common leak points include:
*   **Door seal:** Check the rubber gasket around the door. If it is torn, cracked, or improperly seated, water can escape.
*   **Hoses and connections:** Inspect all internal hoses (sump hose, dispenser hose) for cracks, loose clamps, or corrosion.
*   **Pump seals:** The circulation pump or drain pump might have worn seals allowing water to escape.
*   **Water inlet valve:** A faulty water inlet valve can sometimes drip internally, leading to water accumulation in the base.

Once you find water in the base, you must remove it. Use sponges or towels to soak up all the water. Ensure the base pan is completely dry. Once dry, the float switch should reset. After drying the base pan and resolving the leak, try running a test cycle. If the "Check Water" error persists, the float switch itself might be faulty. However, this is less common than an actual leak.

Fixing the leak source is crucial. Simply drying the base pan will only temporarily resolve the error. The water will accumulate again, reactivating the AquaStop system. Addressing the leak often involves replacing a specific part, such as a hose, seal, or pump. This step might require a professional technician if the leak source is not obvious or requires complex disassembly. The AquaStop system is a valuable safety feature. It protects your home from water damage. When it activates, it is a clear signal that your appliance needs attention for a leak.

You should consider calling a professional if:

*   **The error persists after all troubleshooting:** You have checked the water supply, inlet system, drainage system, and for leaks. The "Check Water" message still appears. This suggests a deeper, perhaps electronic, issue that requires advanced diagnostics.
*   **You cannot identify the leak source:** If the AquaStop system activates and you find water in the base pan but cannot pinpoint where it is coming from, a technician can help. Leaks can be tricky to locate and often require disassembling parts of the dishwasher.
*   **Testing components requires a multimeter:** While I mention checking for voltage, if you do not own a multimeter or are not comfortable using one to test electrical components like the water inlet valve, a professional should perform these checks. Incorrect testing can cause damage or injury.
*   **Replacement of internal parts is needed:** If you determine a part like the water inlet valve, drain pump, or circulation pump needs replacement, but you lack the skills or tools, a technician can handle the repair. They ensure the correct Bosch-specific parts are used and installed properly.
*   **The issue is intermittent or complex:** Sometimes the "Check Water" error appears randomly, or it is accompanied by other unusual behaviors. ## Frequently Asked Questions About Bosch Dishwasher "Check Water" Error

### Q1: What does "Check Water" mean on a Bosch dishwasher?
The "Check Water" error on a Bosch dishwasher signals a problem with the water supply. It means the appliance is not receiving enough water, or it detects an issue with the water intake. This could be due to low water pressure, a closed supply valve, a clogged inlet, or even a drainage problem preventing new water from entering. The dishwasher stops to prevent damage.

### Q2: How do I reset a Bosch dishwasher to clear the error?
To reset your Bosch dishwasher, simply unplug it from the power outlet for about 5-10 minutes. Then, plug it back in. This power cycle can clear temporary electronic glitches or sensor errors that might cause the "Check Water" message. After resetting, try running a short wash cycle to see if the error is gone.

### Q3: Can a clogged filter cause a "Check Water" error?
Yes, a severely clogged filter can indirectly cause a "Check Water" error. If the filter at the bottom of the dishwasher tub is full of food debris, it can restrict proper drainage. If the dishwasher cannot drain previous water, it will not allow new water to fill the tub. This triggers the "Check Water" error message. Cleaning the filter regularly is important.

### Q4: Should I check my water pressure for this error?
Yes, checking your home's water pressure is a good step. If the water pressure coming into your house or specifically to the dishwasher is too low, the dishwasher might not fill with enough water within the allotted time. This can cause the "Check Water" error. Turn on a nearby faucet to observe water flow strength as a first check.

### Q5: What is the AquaStop system and how does it relate to this error?
The AquaStop system is a safety feature in Bosch dishwashers. It detects water leaks in the base pan of the appliance. If water collects there, a float switch activates AquaStop, which cuts off the water supply and often triggers the "Check Water" error. This means the error is due to a detected leak, not a lack of water, protecting your home from flooding.

### Q6: How much does it cost to fix a "Check Water" error?
The cost to fix a "Check Water" error varies greatly. Simple fixes like opening a valve or cleaning a filter cost nothing. Replacing a water inlet valve might cost $100-$300 for parts and labor. A new drain pump or a professional leak repair could range from $200-$500 or more. Complex control board issues are generally the most expensive.
