### Optimal Dishwasher Settings for Your Yeti Mug

Putting your Yeti mug in the dishwasher is straightforward. However, using the right settings helps ensure the best cleaning and longevity. This also prevents potential contact with other items that might cause scratches.

When loading, make sure the mug is upright and stable. This allows water jets to clean both the inside and outside effectively. Avoid crowding the dishwasher. Crowding can prevent proper water circulation. It also increases the chance of items knocking against your Yeti.

For detergents, use your regular dishwasher detergent. There is no need for special or extra-strong cleaners. If your dishwasher has a heated dry cycle, you might consider turning it off. ### Disassembling Your Yeti Mug for Deep Cleaning

A thorough cleaning involves more than just putting the mug in the dishwasher. Disassembling certain parts ensures every nook and cranny gets clean. This is especially true for the lid and gasket. These areas often trap residue and moisture.

Start by removing the lid from your Yeti mug. Most Yeti lids are easy to take apart. For example, the MagSlider Lid has a removable MagSlider component. Simply slide it off the lid for separate cleaning. This allows you to clean both sides of the magnetic slider and the lid opening.

Next, remove any silicone gaskets or O-rings. These are typically found around the lid's edge or sealing mechanism. Gaskets can harbor mold or odors if not cleaned regularly. Gently pull them off the lid. They are flexible and designed for easy removal.

Once disassembled, place all these parts into the dishwasher. Small parts like the MagSlider can go into a mesh bag. This prevents them from moving around or falling to the bottom. Place gaskets and lids on the top rack. Ensure they are positioned so water can reach all surfaces.

Reassemble your Yeti mug once everything is dry. Make sure all parts are completely dry before putting them back together. This prevents moisture buildup and mold. Taking these extra steps ensures a truly clean and hygienic Yeti mug every time.

### Common Dishwasher Mistakes to Avoid with Your Yeti

Putting your Yeti mug in the dishwasher is convenient. Yet, some common mistakes can affect its longevity or appearance. I have learned a few things over time. Avoiding these pitfalls helps keep your Yeti in prime condition.

One mistake is placing your Yeti mug on the bottom rack. The heating element is usually at the bottom. This area gets much hotter. While Yeti mugs are durable, prolonged exposure to extreme heat can test the vacuum seal. Always use the top rack for safety.

Another error is not disassembling the lid and gasket. These parts can trap food particles and moisture. A dishwasher cleans them, but separation allows for a more thorough wash. If not separated, you might find residue left behind. This can lead to odors or mold.

Overcrowding the dishwasher is also a problem. When too many items are packed in, water cannot reach all surfaces. Your Yeti mug might not get properly cleaned. Leave enough space around your mug for water and detergent to circulate freely.

Using abrasive cleaning pads or strong chemicals in the dishwasher is unnecessary. Yeti mugs clean easily. Harsh products can dull the finish or damage the stainless steel. Stick to standard dishwasher detergents. Avoid "deep clean" pods that contain harsh abrasives. By avoiding these simple mistakes, you help extend the life and beauty of your Yeti mug. No, dishwashing will not damage your Yeti's DuraCoat finish. Yeti engineered this colored coating to be highly durable. It resists fading, peeling, and cracking, even with regular exposure to dishwasher cycles. Your colored Yeti mug will maintain its vibrant appearance over time.

To prevent water spots on your Yeti after dishwashing, use a rinse aid in your dishwasher. Also, opt for an air-dry setting or turn off the heated dry cycle.
