## The Core Dimensions: Length and Width Explained

When you first look at a runner rug, its length and width are the most obvious dimensions. These two measurements define the basic footprint of the rug. They tell you how much floor space the rug will cover. Selecting the right length and width is crucial for a pleasing look. These dimensions set the stage for your rug's presence.

Length dictates how far the rug extends down a hallway or staircase. A rug that is too short can look out of place, breaking the visual flow. One that is too long might bunch up, creating a trip hazard, or extend into other rooms. The ideal length typically leaves some floor visible at both ends, usually 6 to 12 inches. This creates a framed effect that is visually appealing and neat.
