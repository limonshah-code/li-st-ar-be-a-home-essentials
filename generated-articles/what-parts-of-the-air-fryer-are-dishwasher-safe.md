### What Air Fryer Parts to Never Put in the Dishwasher

While many air fryer components offer the convenience of dishwasher cleaning, some parts absolutely must stay out. Putting the wrong parts in the dishwasher can lead to severe damage. It can also create dangerous electrical hazards. I always stress this point because safety is paramount. You need to know which parts require manual cleaning.

The main housing of your air fryer is the most important part to keep out of the dishwasher. This is where all the electrical components are located. This includes the power cord, the plug, the control panel, and the internal wiring. Submerging these parts in water, whether in a sink or a dishwasher, will destroy the appliance. It also creates a risk of electric shock when you try to use it again. This is why it's crucial to understand [what is not dishwasher safe](https://homeessentialsguide.com/what-is-not-dishwasher-safe).

The heating element and the fan are also located within the main unit. These parts are responsible for generating and circulating hot air. They must never come into contact with water. If you try to put the entire air fryer body into a dishwasher, you will damage these critical internal workings. The fan might rust, or the heating element could short circuit. You need to keep these areas dry.

Some removable parts might also be unsuitable for the dishwasher. For example, if a part is made of a specific type of plastic not labeled as "dishwasher safe," it could warp or melt. High heat from a dishwasher can deform these materials. This deformation can make the part unusable. Always look for the dishwasher-safe symbol or instructions in your manual. When asking [is my air fryer dishwasher safe](https://homeessentialsguide.com/is-my-air-fryer-dishwasher-safe) about the main body, the answer is a firm no.

To clean these forbidden parts, use a damp cloth or sponge. Wipe down the exterior of the main unit. For the inside, gently wipe around the heating element and fan. Make sure the air fryer is unplugged and completely cool before cleaning. Use a soft brush to remove any food debris from the heating coil if necessary. Never use harsh chemicals or immerse the main unit. This careful manual cleaning ensures the longevity and safe operation of your air fryer.

### Tips for Dishwasher Cleaning Your Air Fryer Components

Cleaning your air fryer parts in the dishwasher can be very efficient. However, following certain practices helps ensure a thorough clean and protects your appliance. I have learned a few tricks over time that make a big difference. These simple steps can extend the life of your air fryer parts and keep them looking new.

First, always pre-soak or scrape off stubborn food residue. This step is crucial, especially for greasy or burnt-on food. A quick soak in warm, soapy water helps loosen tough bits. Scraping with a plastic spatula prevents hard food from getting baked onto the surfaces during the wash cycle. This also keeps food particles from clogging your dishwasher's filter. This little effort upfront saves a lot of hassle later.

Next, consider the dishwasher cycle you use. If your dishwasher has a "gentle" or "eco" cycle, it is often best for items with non-stick coatings. These cycles use lower temperatures and less aggressive spray pressure. This can help preserve the non-stick surface. Always place lighter items, especially those with non-stick coatings, on the top rack. This protects them from the highest heat and strongest water jets, which are usually found on the bottom rack.

Choosing the right detergent also matters. Avoid using harsh, abrasive detergents. These can be too strong for delicate non-stick surfaces. A standard dishwashing detergent is usually sufficient. Over time, strong detergents can erode the coating. You want to clean, not strip, the surface. After the wash cycle, you can air dry the parts or use a low-heat drying setting. High-heat drying can sometimes be too much for certain materials.

It is also important to check for any residue after the wash. Sometimes, food particles or detergent residue can remain. If this happens, a quick rinse under the tap or a wipe with a clean cloth will fix it. Regular cleaning prevents significant buildup on your air fryer parts. This regular care means less intensive cleaning sessions down the road.

### FAQ Section

#### Can I put an air fryer basket with a non-stick coating in the dishwasher?
Yes, most air fryer baskets with non-stick coatings are designed to be dishwasher safe. To best preserve the coating, place the basket on the top rack. Use a mild detergent and a gentle wash cycle. Pre-rinse to remove large food particles before washing. This helps prevent damage and ensures a good clean.

#### How often should I clean my air fryer's removable parts?
You should clean your air fryer's removable parts after every use. This prevents food buildup, grease accumulation, and lingering odors. Regular cleaning keeps your air fryer hygienic and helps it perform better. It also makes the cleaning process easier each time, as food won't get caked on.

#### What is the best way to clean the inside of the main air fryer unit?
The main air fryer unit, which contains electrical parts, should never be submerged in water. Always unplug it and let it cool completely. Use a damp cloth or sponge with a mild soap solution to wipe down the interior and exterior. For stubborn spots, a soft brush can help. Ensure no water gets into the electrical components.

#### Does putting air fryer parts in the dishwasher damage them?
Putting the correct air fryer parts in the dishwasher usually does not cause damage. However, incorrect loading, harsh detergents, or high heat cycles can sometimes affect non-stick coatings or plastic components over time. Always follow your manual's instructions and consider hand washing for delicate items.

#### Are all air fryer accessories dishwasher safe?
No, not all air fryer accessories are dishwasher safe. While many racks, skewers, and silicone liners are designed for dishwasher use, always check each item. Some plastics or specialized accessories may require hand washing to prevent warping or damage. Refer to your air fryer's manual for specific accessory care.

#### Why is my air fryer's heating element getting dirty, and how do I clean it safely?
Your air fryer's heating element can get dirty from grease splatter and food particles. This is normal. To clean it safely, unplug the air fryer and let it cool completely. Use a soft, damp cloth or sponge to gently wipe the element. For stubborn grease, a soft brush and a little dish soap can help. Avoid water dripping onto electrical parts.
