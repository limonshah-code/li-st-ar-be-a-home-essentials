## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q: Can I get Cuisinart parts if my warranty expires?
A: Yes, Cuisinart typically sells replacement parts directly from their website or through authorized retailers. You can purchase new bowls, lids, blades, and other accessories. This helps you extend the life of your food processor even after the warranty period ends. Always use genuine Cuisinart parts.

### Q: Does Cuisinart offer international warranties?
A: Cuisinart's warranty policies are usually specific to the region or country where the product was purchased. An appliance bought in the US might not have warranty coverage in Europe, for example. Always check the warranty terms for your specific region. Contact local Cuisinart customer service for details.

### Q: What if I bought my Cuisinart food processor used?
A: Cuisinart's limited warranty usually applies only to the original purchaser. If you bought your food processor used, the warranty is likely not transferable. You will not be able to make a claim. Always verify this directly with Cuisinart for clarification.

### Q: How do I find my Cuisinart model and serial number?
A: Your Cuisinart food processor's model and serial number are typically located on a label. This label is usually on the bottom of the motor base. It might also be on the back of the unit. Keep your manual; it often shows where to find this information.

### Q: Are Cuisinart accessories covered by the warranty?
A: Accessories that come with the Cuisinart food processor are generally covered by the main unit's limited warranty. This means if they have manufacturing defects within the warranty period, they are covered. Wear and tear on accessories like blades is not typically covered.

### Q: Is a receipt necessary for a Cuisinart warranty claim?
A: Yes, a receipt or other proof of purchase is almost always necessary for a Cuisinart warranty claim. It verifies the purchase date and that you are the original owner. Without it, Cuisinart may deny your claim. Keep your receipt in a safe place.
