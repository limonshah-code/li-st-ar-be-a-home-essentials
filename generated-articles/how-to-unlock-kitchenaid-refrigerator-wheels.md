## How To Unlock KitchenAid Refrigerator Wheels

Moving a large appliance like your KitchenAid refrigerator can seem like a tough job. You might want to clean behind it, retrieve a lost item, or simply rearrange your kitchen. Many people struggle with getting their refrigerator to budge. The reason is often that the wheels are not unlocked.

Your KitchenAid refrigerator has wheels for easy movement, but it also has leveling feet. These feet stabilize the appliance and prevent it from rolling. To move your refrigerator, you must retract these leveling feet. This action allows the wheels to make full contact with the floor. This guide will show you exactly how to unlock KitchenAid refrigerator wheels. We will cover the tools you need and the step-by-step process.

### Takeaway:

*   **Locate Leveling Feet:** Find the two adjustable feet at the bottom front of your KitchenAid refrigerator.
*   **Use Proper Tools:** Use a wrench or pliers to turn the leveling feet counter-clockwise.
*   **Retract Fully:** Turn the feet until they no longer touch the floor, allowing the wheels to engage.
*   **Pull Out Safely:** Gently pull the refrigerator forward once the wheels are free.

To unlock KitchenAid refrigerator wheels, you need to retract the front leveling feet. Locate these feet at the bottom front of the appliance. Use a wrench or pliers to turn them counter-clockwise until they lift completely off the floor. This action allows the rear rollers and front wheels to bear the full weight, making the refrigerator movable.

### Understanding Your KitchenAid Refrigerator's Mobility Features

Your KitchenAid refrigerator is not just a heavy box. It has features designed for both stability and movement. This balance helps keep your food fresh and your kitchen safe. Understanding these parts is the first step to moving your appliance.

Most KitchenAid refrigerators use a combination of leveling feet and wheels. The leveling feet are at the front. They screw up and down. When you screw them down, they touch the floor. This raises the front of the refrigerator slightly. It also prevents the refrigerator from wobbling. These feet also keep the appliance from rolling forward.

The wheels, or rollers, are usually at the back. Some models may have small wheels at the front too, behind the leveling feet. These wheels are what allow the refrigerator to roll. They are not visible when the leveling feet are extended. When you retract the leveling feet, the refrigerator's weight shifts onto these wheels. This allows for easy movement across the floor.

Proper adjustment of these components is vital. If the leveling feet are too low, the refrigerator cannot move. If they are too high, the refrigerator might wobble. Learning about these parts helps you unlock the KitchenAid refrigerator wheels effectively. It ensures you can move your appliance safely and without damage. ### Step-by-Step Guide to Unlocking Front Leveling Feet

Unlocking the front leveling feet is the main step to making your KitchenAid refrigerator movable. This process allows the wheels to engage fully. Follow these steps carefully to free your appliance. I remember doing this for the first time; it felt like a puzzle, but it's quite straightforward.

**Step 1: Locate the Leveling Feet**
The leveling feet are typically found at the front bottom corners of your KitchenAid refrigerator. They are usually small, threaded posts. You might need to gently tilt the refrigerator back slightly to see them. Some models have a kick plate at the bottom. You may need to remove this kick plate first. It usually snaps off or is held by a few screws.

**Step 2: Identify the Adjustment Mechanism**
Each leveling foot has a mechanism for adjustment. This is usually a hex-shaped nut or a slotted screw head. A wrench fits around the hex nut. A screwdriver fits into the slotted head. Make sure you use the correct tool to avoid stripping the nut or screw.

**Step 3: Retract the Leveling Feet**
To unlock the wheels, you must raise the leveling feet. Turn the leveling feet counter-clockwise. This action will screw the feet upwards, away from the floor. Keep turning until each foot no longer touches the floor. You will notice the front of the refrigerator lowering slightly as you do this. If they are very tight, pliers might help get them started. Sometimes, the feet can be stubborn, especially if the refrigerator has been in place for a long time.

*   **Using a Wrench:** Place the wrench around the hex nut on the leveling foot. Turn it counter-clockwise.
*   **Using a Screwdriver:** If it has a slotted head, insert the screwdriver and turn it counter-clockwise.

Ensure both front feet are fully retracted. This allows the KitchenAid refrigerator wheels to bear the weight. This is a common method across various brands. ### Troubleshooting Common Issues When Wheels Don't Unlock

Sometimes, despite your best efforts, the KitchenAid refrigerator wheels still refuse to unlock. This can be frustrating, but common issues often have simple solutions. I've encountered a few of these myself.

One common problem is stubborn leveling feet. If the refrigerator has been in the same spot for many years, the leveling feet might be corroded or stuck. You can try applying a penetrating oil, like WD-40, to the threads of the leveling feet. Let it sit for a few minutes. Then, try turning them again with your wrench or pliers. A gentle tap with a rubber mallet on the wrench handle can also help loosen them. Remember to be gentle to avoid damage.

Another issue could be an uneven floor. If your floor has dips or high spots, the leveling feet might seem retracted but are still catching on an uneven surface. Try moving the refrigerator slightly from side to side. Sometimes, shifting its position by an inch or two can help it clear a snag. You can also try lifting one corner of the refrigerator gently. Use a sturdy block of wood to support it temporarily. This allows you to get a better look at the leveling feet and ensure they are fully off the ground.

Damaged wheels or debris under the appliance can also prevent movement. Over time, dirt, dust, or small objects can accumulate under the refrigerator. This debris can jam the wheels. Use a flashlight to look under the refrigerator. If you see debris, try to remove it with a broom handle or a long stick. If a wheel is visibly damaged or broken, you might need to replace it. This is a more involved repair. In such cases, it is best to consult the KitchenAid service manual or call a professional appliance technician. ### FAQ Section

**Q1: Why won't my KitchenAid refrigerator move after retracting the leveling feet?**
A1: Your KitchenAid refrigerator might still be stuck if the leveling feet are not fully retracted. Check for any small gap between the feet and the floor. Debris under the wheels can also prevent movement. Shine a flashlight under the appliance to look for obstructions.

**Q2: How do I know if my KitchenAid refrigerator has wheels?**
A2: Most modern KitchenAid refrigerators come with hidden rollers or wheels at the back. Some models also have them at the front, behind the leveling feet. You usually cannot see them until the front leveling feet are lifted off the floor.

**Q3: Can I use a regular screwdriver to unlock the leveling feet?**
A3: Some KitchenAid leveling feet have slotted screw heads, allowing you to use a large flat-head screwdriver. However, many use hex-shaped nuts that require an adjustable wrench or the specific wrench that came with your appliance. Check your model's design first.

**Q4: What if my KitchenAid refrigerator is too heavy to move even with the wheels unlocked?**
A4: Even with unlocked wheels, a KitchenAid refrigerator is very heavy. If you cannot move it, consider getting assistance from another person. For very heavy or large models, a furniture dolly designed for appliances can provide extra help and stability.

**Q5: How often should I clean around my refrigerator wheels?**
A5: It is a good practice to clean around your refrigerator wheels and leveling feet once or twice a year. This prevents dust and debris buildup. Regular cleaning ensures the mechanisms remain free and easy to adjust or move when needed.

**Q6: Is it bad for my floor if I move the refrigerator frequently?**
A6: Moving a refrigerator frequently, especially on delicate flooring, can cause scratches or damage. Use protective mats or moving blankets under the wheels. Always move it slowly and carefully. This reduces the risk of floor damage.
