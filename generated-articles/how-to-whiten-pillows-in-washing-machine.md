## Effortlessly Whiten Pillows in Your Washing Machine

Do your once-pristine white pillows now show an unsightly yellow hue? You are not alone. Pillows absorb body oils, sweat, and makeup over time. This makes them look dingy and less inviting. Learning how to whiten pillows in washing machine safely is a skill every homeowner needs. It restores comfort and hygiene to your bed.

This guide helps you revive your pillows. We will explore simple methods and common household products. You will learn steps for preparing, washing, and drying your pillows. We cover different whitening agents. This article provides all the information you need to get your pillows looking new again.

### Takeaway

*   **Check Care Labels First:** Always ensure your pillows are machine-washable.
*   **Pre-Treat Stains:** Address specific yellow spots before washing.
*   **Use Proper Agents:** Choose between bleach, oxygen booster, or natural alternatives.
*   **Wash Two Pillows:** Balance the load in your washing machine for effective cleaning.

Whitening pillows in a washing machine involves a few simple steps. First, check the pillow's care label to confirm it is machine washable. Then, pre-treat any visible yellow stains. Load two pillows into your washing machine to balance the drum. Add your chosen detergent and a whitening agent. Select a hot water cycle and an extra rinse. Finally, dry the pillows completely, ideally with dryer balls.

### Pre-Washing Steps: Preparing Your Pillows for Whitening

Before you begin the whitening process, proper preparation is key. Taking these initial steps ensures a safer and more effective cleaning. Skipping these might damage your pillows. It could also lead to disappointing results. I always take a few minutes for this preparation. It makes the entire process smoother.

First, locate the care tag on your pillow. This tag provides important instructions. It tells you if the pillow is machine washable. It specifies water temperature. It also advises on drying methods. Most down and synthetic pillows are machine washable. Memory foam and some specialty pillows are not. Washing them in a machine can ruin their structure. If your pillow is not machine washable, you will need to spot clean it. You can find more details on general pillow care instructions by learning how to clean bed pillows.

Next, remove all pillowcases and protectors. These items need separate washing. This ensures the pillow itself is fully exposed. It allows the whitening agents to reach all discolored areas. Examine your pillow for any obvious stains. Yellow spots are common. You may see drool marks or makeup stains. Pre-treating these spots helps achieve better results. Apply a small amount of liquid laundry detergent directly to the stain. Gently rub it in with your fingers or a soft brush. Let it sit for about 15-30 minutes before washing. This helps break down stubborn discoloration.

Finally, perform a quick "sink test" if you are unsure about your pillow's durability. Fill a sink with water. Submerge your pillow. If the pillow soaks up water and becomes very heavy, it might not hold its shape well in the washing machine. This test also reveals if the pillow is still supportive. A pillow that folds easily and does not spring back might be too old. You might need to replace it rather than just whiten it. ### Step-by-Step Guide to Whitening Pillows in Your Washing Machine

Now that your pillows are ready and you have your supplies, it's time to wash. This systematic approach ensures a thorough and effective cleaning. I follow these steps every time. It gives me consistent results. Your pillows will look much brighter.

First, load your pillows into the washing machine. Always wash two pillows at a time. This balances the load. An unbalanced load can cause your machine to shake violently. It can also prevent thorough cleaning. Place them opposite each other in the drum. This distributes weight evenly. If you only have one pillow to wash, add a towel or a few smaller items to balance it. This simple trick protects your machine.

Next, add your cleaning agents. For two standard pillows, I typically use 1/2 cup of liquid laundry detergent. Add this to the detergent dispenser. Then, choose your whitening agent. If using chlorine bleach, add 1/2 cup to the bleach dispenser. If using oxygen bleach, add 1/2 to 1 cup directly to the wash drum before adding pillows. For borax, add 1/2 cup with the detergent. If you prefer the baking soda and vinegar method, add 1/2 cup of baking soda to the drum. Then add 1/2 cup of white vinegar to the fabric softener dispenser. This allows the vinegar to dispense during the rinse cycle. This prevents it from reacting with the baking soda too early.

Select the correct wash cycle. A "bulky" or "heavy duty" setting works best for pillows. Choose the hottest water temperature suitable for your pillow's material. Hot water is crucial for effective whitening. It helps dissolve body oils and activate cleaning agents. However, always check the care label first. Some delicate pillows may require warm water. Choose an extra rinse cycle. This ensures all detergent and whitening agents are removed. Residue can lead to skin irritation. It can also make pillows stiff.

After the wash cycle finishes, check your pillows. They should look noticeably brighter. Feel them for any remaining dampness or stiffness. If they still feel soapy, run another rinse cycle. This is a common step for thicker items like pillows. It ensures all cleaning agents are gone. This process is part of a thorough cleaning routine. ### Specific Whitening Methods: Beyond Just Bleach

While bleach is a powerful whitener, other effective methods exist. These alternatives offer gentler options. Some are more environmentally friendly. They can be just as good at restoring pillow brightness. I find it useful to know various techniques. This way, I can choose the best one for each pillow type.

#### Oxygen Bleach Method

Oxygen bleach, often called oxygen booster, is a popular choice. It is safer for colors. It does not contain harsh chlorine. This makes it gentler on fabrics. It works by releasing oxygen when mixed with water. This oxygen helps lift stains and brightens fabrics. You can use it on most pillow types. It is excellent for synthetic and down pillows. It is less likely to cause damage.

To use oxygen bleach, simply add it to your wash cycle. Follow the product's instructions for dosage. Typically, 1/2 to 1 cup is sufficient for a pillow load. Add it directly to the washer drum. Then add your pillows and detergent. Wash as usual on a hot or warm water cycle. The oxygen bleach will work with the detergent to break down stains. It will also brighten the pillow's material. This method leaves pillows fresh. It does not have the strong odor of chlorine bleach.

#### Baking Soda and Vinegar Method

This natural duo is a powerhouse for cleaning. Baking soda is a natural deodorizer and mild abrasive. It helps scrub away grime. White vinegar is acidic. It breaks down mineral deposits and soap scum. Together, they whiten and freshen. This method is excellent for sensitive skin. It is also good for those who prefer chemical-free cleaning. You can learn more about using these ingredients by checking [how to clean pillows with baking soda](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-clean-pillows-with-baking-soda).

First, add 1/2 cup of baking soda to the washing machine drum. Place your pillows inside. Then, pour 1/2 cup of white vinegar into the fabric softener dispenser. Start a hot water cycle. The baking soda will clean and deodorize during the wash. The vinegar will be released during the rinse. This helps to remove any residual soap. It also acts as a natural fabric softener. This method leaves pillows soft and bright. They will smell clean. This method is also safe for down pillows. It cleans gently without harsh chemicals.

#### Borax Boost Method

Borax is a mineral compound. It is a natural laundry booster. It helps soften hard water. Hard water contains minerals that can make clothes look dull. Borax neutralizes these minerals. This allows your detergent to work more effectively. It also has natural whitening properties. Borax helps remove tough stains. It also eliminates odors. It is a versatile cleaning agent.

To use borax, add 1/2 cup along with your regular detergent. Add it directly into the wash drum. Load your pillows. Select a hot water cycle. Borax will dissolve in the water. It will boost the cleaning power of your detergent. It helps lift deeply set yellow stains. It also leaves pillows feeling cleaner. This method is safe for most pillow types. It is especially useful if you have hard water. It enhances the overall cleaning process.

### Drying Whitened Pillows Properly

After washing, proper drying is critical. Incomplete drying can lead to big problems. It can cause mildew. It can also create an unpleasant smell. Dampness damages pillow fillings. This makes them lumpy and uncomfortable. I always take my time drying pillows. It makes sure they stay fluffy and fresh.

First, remove your pillows from the washing machine. Give them a good shake. This helps redistribute the filling. It prevents lumps from forming during drying. If your pillows are wet, they will be heavy. They might feel flat. This is normal at this stage. You need to ensure the filling separates evenly. This helps air circulate.

Next, place your pillows in a dryer. Use a low heat setting. High heat can damage some pillow fillings. It can melt synthetic fibers. It can also make down feathers brittle. A low heat setting is gentler. It ensures the pillow dries evenly. Add two or three dryer balls to the dryer. Clean tennis balls also work well. These balls help fluff the pillows. They prevent the filling from clumping. They also speed up the drying time. The balls hit the pillows as they tumble. This action breaks up wet spots. It ensures air reaches all parts of the pillow.

Allow the pillows to tumble dry for a long cycle. This might take several hours. Check them periodically. Feel the center of the pillow for any dampness. It often feels dry on the outside but damp inside. Continue drying until the pillow is completely dry. Any lingering moisture can lead to mildew. It can also cause odors. If you can, air dry them outside on a sunny, breezy day. Sunlight is a natural sanitizer. It also helps with lingering odors. However, always finish with a dryer cycle if air drying. This ensures complete dryness. It also helps fluff the pillow.

Once completely dry, your pillows should feel light and fluffy again. They should have no lingering moisture. They should also smell fresh. Proper drying maintains the pillow's shape. It extends its lifespan. It also prevents the growth of mold or mildew. This makes your bed a healthier place to rest.

### FAQ Section

#### Can I wash all pillow types in the washing machine?

No, not all pillow types are machine washable. Most down, feather, and synthetic pillows are safe for machine washing. Memory foam and buckwheat hull pillows are usually not. Always check the care label first. Washing improper pillow types can ruin their shape and filling. If you have foam pillows, refer to [how to clean foam pillows](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-clean-foam-pillows) for specific care instructions.

#### How often should I whiten my pillows?

You should aim to wash and whiten your pillows every three to six months. This frequency helps prevent significant yellowing and odor buildup. However, if you sweat a lot, use heavy hair products, or if your pillows show stains sooner, you can whiten them more often. Regular maintenance keeps them looking and feeling fresh.

#### Can I use regular bleach for all white pillows?

Regular chlorine bleach works well on cotton and synthetic fiber pillows. It is a powerful whitener. However, avoid using it on down or feather pillows, as it can damage the natural fibers. Also, use it carefully on synthetic pillows, as too much can weaken the fabric over time. Always dilute bleach and ensure good ventilation.

#### What if my pillows still look yellow after washing?

If your pillows still show yellowing, try repeating the whitening process. You might increase the amount of whitening agent slightly. Consider soaking them in a hot water solution with oxygen bleach for a few hours before washing again. If multiple attempts fail and the pillows are old, it might be time to replace them.

#### Will whitening affect the pillow's softness or support?

Proper whitening and drying should not negatively affect softness or support. In fact, a good wash can actually fluff up and revitalize the pillow's filling. Using dryer balls helps maintain softness and prevents clumping. Over-drying or using too much heat can, however, make some fillings brittle. Always use a low heat setting.

#### Do I need special detergent to whiten pillows?

You do not need special detergent. Your regular liquid laundry detergent works well. However, adding a specific whitening booster, like oxygen bleach, borax, or even baking soda, is key. These additives enhance the detergent's power. They specifically target and break down the yellowing substances.
