## Post-Dishwasher Care: Drying and Reassembly

### Air Drying for Optimal Hygiene

After the dishwasher cycle, remove the bottle parts. Air drying is the preferred method. High-heat drying cycles in the dishwasher can still warp plastic. They can also degrade nipples. Instead, place all parts on a clean drying rack. Ensure good airflow around each piece. This allows them to dry completely.

A bottle drying rack is a good investment. It keeps parts upright and separate. This promotes faster and more hygienic drying. Avoid using dish towels. Towels can transfer germs. They may also leave lint behind. Allow ample time for all parts to air dry. This ensures they are free from moisture.

If you find any damage, replace the part immediately. Worn nipples can be a choking hazard. Damaged bottles might not function correctly. If there is residue, hand wash that specific part again. ## FAQ Section

### Can I put Dr. Brown's bottle nipples in the dishwasher?
Yes, you can put Dr. Brown's bottle nipples in the dishwasher. Always place them securely in a baby bottle basket. Position the basket on the top rack of your dishwasher. Use a gentle wash cycle to prevent damage or warping. This ensures they are thoroughly cleaned without being exposed to excessive heat.

### Do I need to sterilize Dr. Brown's bottles after dishwashing?
For most healthy, full-term babies, dishwashing with hot water and detergent is sufficient for cleaning. However, for babies under three months, premature babies, or those with weakened immune systems, the CDC recommends daily sanitization. Many dishwashers have a sanitize cycle that can fulfill this requirement.

### How often should I clean Dr. Brown's bottle parts?
You should clean all Dr. Brown's bottle parts after every single feeding. Milk and formula residues can quickly grow bacteria. Even if your baby did not finish the bottle, clean it. This regular cleaning prevents bacterial buildup and keeps your baby safe and healthy.

### Will Dr. Brown's bottles melt in the dishwasher?
Plastic Dr. Brown's bottles can warp or melt if exposed to excessively high heat. This often happens on the bottom rack or during a very hot, intense cycle. Always place all plastic components on the top rack. Select a gentle wash cycle to avoid heat damage and ensure bottle longevity.

### What if my Dr. Brown's bottle parts get cloudy after washing?
Cloudiness on plastic Dr. Brown's bottle parts can occur due to hard water deposits or detergent residue. Try using a rinse aid in your dishwasher. You can also hand wash with vinegar to remove mineral buildup. Ensure thorough pre-rinsing before dishwashing to prevent milk film.
