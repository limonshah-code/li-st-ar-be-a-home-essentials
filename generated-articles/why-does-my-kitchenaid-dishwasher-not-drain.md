### Why does my KitchenAid dishwasher not drain?

Your KitchenAid dishwasher does not drain usually because of a clog. This clog can be in the filter, drain hose, or air gap. Food particles are often the cause. Sometimes, a problem with your garbage disposal can stop drainage. Less often, the drain pump or other internal parts may have failed.

### Understanding Your KitchenAid Dishwasher's Drainage System

It helps to know how your KitchenAid dishwasher gets rid of water. When a wash cycle ends, the dishwasher needs to drain. It pushes the dirty water out through a drain pump. This pump sends water into a drain hose. The hose connects to your kitchen sink drain or a garbage disposal. An air gap may also be part of the system. This prevents dirty water from flowing back into the dishwasher.

When one part of this system fails, water stays inside. Understanding these parts helps you find the problem. Each part has a specific job. If the pump does not work, water cannot move. If the hose is blocked, water cannot exit. Your KitchenAid dishwasher relies on all these parts working together. If you find water at the bottom, something is stopping this flow. We will look at each part in detail. Knowing this helps you troubleshoot effectively.

A fully functioning drainage system is key. It ensures your dishes get clean and dry. It also prevents smells inside the appliance. Many problems relate to simple blockages. These are often easy for you to fix. Sometimes, the problem is more complex. Knowing the system gives you a good starting point. This knowledge saves you time and money.

### Common Culprits: Clogged Filters and Drain Traps

The most frequent cause of a KitchenAid dishwasher not draining is a clogged filter or drain trap. I often find this to be the first place to check. Food debris, grease, and other small items build up over time. These block the water flow. Your dishwasher uses a filter to catch these particles. This stops them from reaching the drain pump.

You can usually find the filter assembly at the bottom of the dishwasher tub. It might be under the lower spray arm. KitchenAid models typically have a cylindrical filter and a coarse filter screen. Both parts collect debris. If you do not clean them regularly, water struggles to pass through. This makes your dishwasher leave standing water.

Cleaning these filters is simple. First, make sure the dishwasher is off. Remove the lower dish rack. Unscrew or twist to remove the filter assembly. Rinse it under running water. Use a soft brush to scrub away stuck-on food. You can use a mild soap if needed. Ensure all debris is gone. A clean filter allows water to drain freely. This often fixes the KitchenAid dishwasher not draining issue right away. Cleaning the drain trap area is also important. This is the area around where the filter sits. Food can collect there too.

*   **Steps to Clean Your Dishwasher Filter:**
    1.  Turn off power to the dishwasher.
    2.  Remove the lower dish rack.
    3.  Locate the filter at the bottom of the tub.
    4.  Twist the cylindrical filter counter-clockwise to unlock it. Pull it up and out.
    5.  Remove the coarse filter screen underneath.
    6.  Rinse both filters under warm running water.
    7.  Use a soft brush (like an old toothbrush) to remove any stubborn food particles or grease.
    8.  Clean the filter housing area inside the dishwasher.
    9.  Reinstall the coarse filter first, then the cylindrical filter. Turn clockwise to lock it.
    10. Replace the lower dish rack.

Cleaning your KitchenAid dishwasher's filter should be a routine task. I recommend cleaning it once a month. This prevents future clogs. It helps your dishwasher run efficiently. It also stops bad smells. Many times, just cleaning this part fixes the draining problem. For more detailed steps, you can check guides on [how to clean your KitchenAid dishwasher](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-clean-your-kitchenaid-dishwasher). If your dishwasher has water in the bottom, a dirty filter is a likely cause. This quick fix can save you from bigger headaches. You can also refer to specific instructions on [how to clean a dishwasher drain](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-clean-dishwasher-drain).

### Addressing Drain Hose Issues: Kinks and Blockages

After checking the filters, the next place I look is the drain hose. The drain hose carries water from your KitchenAid dishwasher to your home's drain system. This hose can develop kinks or blockages. Both stop water from draining. A kink is a bend in the hose. It acts like a pinched garden hose. Water cannot flow past it. This is a common problem, especially if the dishwasher was recently moved.

Check the drain hose behind the dishwasher. Look for any sharp bends or crimps. Straighten any kinks you find. Ensure the hose has a gentle slope. This helps water flow downwards. If the hose runs up and then down (a high loop), make sure it is installed correctly. A proper high loop prevents dirty water from flowing back into the dishwasher. It also stops water from the sink drain from entering the dishwasher.

A blockage inside the drain hose is another common issue. Food debris, grease, or even small broken glass pieces can get stuck. You might need to disconnect the hose to check for blockages. First, turn off the power to your dishwasher. Disconnect the hose from both the dishwasher and the sink drain or garbage disposal. Have a bucket ready. Water may spill out. Look inside the hose for any obstructions. You can use a straightened coat hanger or a long brush to clear blockages. Push it gently through the hose. Be careful not to puncture the hose.

*   **Steps to Inspect the Drain Hose:**
    1.  Shut off power to the dishwasher at the circuit breaker.
    2.  Pull the dishwasher out from the cabinet slightly if possible. This allows access to the hose.
    3.  Examine the entire length of the drain hose. Look for any visible kinks or twists. Straighten them out.
    4.  Ensure the drain hose is installed with a proper "high loop." This means the hose goes up high under the sink before connecting to the drain. This prevents backflow.
    5.  If you suspect an internal clog, disconnect the hose from both ends (dishwasher and sink/disposal).
    6.  Place the end of the hose into a bucket. Use a flashlight to look inside.
    7.  Carefully push a long, flexible brush or a straightened wire through the hose to dislodge any debris.
    8.  Reconnect the hose securely. Ensure clamps are tight.

After clearing any kinks or blockages, reconnect the hose firmly. Run a short cycle on your KitchenAid dishwasher. See if the water drains properly. This simple check and fix often solves the problem. A clear drain hose is essential for good drainage. If your KitchenAid dishwasher still does not drain, you can learn more about general solutions by checking [how to get the dishwasher to drain](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-get-the-dishwasher-to-drain).

### Garbage Disposal Problems Affecting Dishwasher Drain

If your KitchenAid dishwasher drains into a garbage disposal, the disposal can be the source of your problem. I have seen this often. Many dishwashers connect directly to the disposal unit. If the disposal is clogged, it can prevent the dishwasher from draining. It is a shared drainage pathway. Food particles from the disposal can back up into the dishwasher's drain hose.

First, check if your garbage disposal is working. Run it with some water. If it sounds weak or does not clear food well, it might be clogged. Clear any food waste from the disposal. You can use a plunger or a hex wrench (for the bottom of the disposal) to free up the grinding plates. Make sure it spins freely. Run the disposal for about 30 seconds with cold water. This helps clear any debris.

Another common issue for new garbage disposal installations is the drain plug. When you install a new garbage disposal, it often comes with a plug inside the dishwasher inlet. This plug must be removed for the dishwasher to drain. If you recently installed a new disposal and your KitchenAid dishwasher suddenly stopped draining, check for this plug. It is a small knockout plug that needs to be punched out. You can use a hammer and screwdriver. Make sure the dishwasher hose is disconnected first.

*   **Steps to Check and Clear Your Garbage Disposal:**
    1.  Ensure your garbage disposal is clear of food waste. Run it with cold water for a minute.
    2.  If the disposal hums but does not grind, it may be jammed. Turn off the power. Use a hex wrench in the center hole at the bottom of the disposal to manually turn the blades.
    3.  Look into the disposal opening. Remove any visible foreign objects.
    4.  If a new garbage disposal was installed, ensure the knockout plug for the dishwasher drain line has been removed. This plug is inside the disposal's dishwasher inlet.
    5.  To check the knockout plug: Disconnect the dishwasher drain hose from the disposal. Use a screwdriver and hammer to punch out the plug from inside the disposal's inlet.
    6.  Reconnect the dishwasher drain hose securely to the disposal.

After ensuring your garbage disposal is clear and the knockout plug (if applicable) is removed, run a short test cycle on your KitchenAid dishwasher. This often resolves the problem. A clear garbage disposal is vital for your dishwasher's drainage. This shared connection means problems in one affect the other.

### Malfunctioning Drain Pump: A Deeper Dive

If you have checked the filters, drain hose, and garbage disposal, and your KitchenAid dishwasher still does not drain, the problem might be the drain pump. The drain pump is a motor that pushes water out of the dishwasher. It is a critical part of the drainage system. When it fails, water simply sits at the bottom of the tub.

You might notice signs of a failing pump. The dishwasher may make a humming noise but no water moves. Or, it might make no sound at all during the drain cycle. Sometimes, the pump gets clogged with debris that bypassed the filter. Pieces of glass, plastic, or even large food chunks can get stuck in the pump impeller. This stops the impeller from spinning. This is a problem many homeowners face when their [dishwasher does not drain](https://homeessentialsguide.com/when-dishwasher-does-not-drain).

Accessing the drain pump requires some effort. First, you must disconnect the power to the dishwasher. Turn off the breaker for safety. Then, you may need to pull the dishwasher out from its cabinet. The pump is usually at the bottom, accessible from the front or bottom of the unit. You might need to remove the lower access panel. Once you locate the pump, look for any visible obstructions around the impeller. Sometimes, clearing a small item can fix the issue.

*   **Steps to Inspect the Drain Pump:**
    1.  **Disconnect Power:** Turn off the circuit breaker that supplies power to your KitchenAid dishwasher. This is a must for safety.
    2.  **Access Pump:** You may need to slide the dishwasher out from its cabinet opening. You can also try tilting it back slightly to access the bottom. Place towels down to catch any water.
    3.  **Locate Pump:** The drain pump is typically a small motor assembly found at the bottom front or side of the dishwasher tub. It will have drain hoses connected to it.
    4.  **Check for Obstructions:** Carefully remove the pump housing or access panel if possible. Look for foreign objects (broken glass, bones, plastic) jamming the impeller blades. Use pliers or tweezers to remove them.
    5.  **Test for Free Movement:** Once cleared, try to spin the impeller blades manually. They should move freely. If they are stiff or do not move, the pump might be damaged.
    6.  **Check Electrical Connections:** Ensure all wires leading to the pump are secure. Loose connections can prevent the pump from getting power.
    7.  **Consider Replacement:** If the pump is clear of debris but still does not work, it likely needs replacing. This often requires professional help.

Replacing a drain pump can be complex. If you are not comfortable with appliance repair, it is best to call a qualified technician. A faulty drain pump means your KitchenAid dishwasher will not drain. It is a major component. Make sure you check all other simpler causes first. For specific KitchenAid models, looking up a repair manual can help locate the pump and its removal process. This helps you to troubleshoot [why your KitchenAid dishwasher won't drain](https://homeessentialsguide.com/why-wont-my-kitchenaid-dishwasher-drain).

### Air Gap and High Loop: Essential for Proper Drainage

The air gap and high loop are often overlooked components. Yet, they are crucial for your KitchenAid dishwasher to drain correctly. They prevent dirty water from backing up into the dishwasher. This ensures sanitation. A backflow of water can cause your KitchenAid dishwasher not to drain.

An air gap is a small cylindrical device. It sits on top of your kitchen counter, usually next to the faucet. It has two hoses connected to it. One hose comes from the dishwasher. The other hose goes to the garbage disposal or sink drain. The air gap creates an actual air break in the drain line. This stops contaminated water from the sink backing into the clean dishwasher. If this air gap gets clogged, water from the dishwasher cannot flow through it. Food particles or grease can build up inside. You might see water overflowing from the air gap cover.

To check the air gap, first remove its cap. This usually twists off. Then, remove the decorative cover. Inspect the two nipples underneath for clogs. Use a small brush or a piece of wire to clear any debris. You can also disconnect the hoses and clean them. Reconnect everything tightly.

The high loop is an alternative to an air gap, or it can be used in conjunction with one. It is a simple U-shaped bend in the dishwasher's drain hose. The hose loops up high under the sink, typically secured to the underside of the counter or sink basin. This loop should be above the flood level of the sink. It uses gravity and air pressure to prevent backflow. If the high loop is missing, installed too low, or sags, water can flow back into the dishwasher.

*   **Checking Your Air Gap:**
    1.  Locate the air gap on your countertop, usually near the faucet.
    2.  Twist off the chrome cap and plastic cover.
    3.  Inspect the two hose connections for any visible debris or clogs.
    4.  Use a wire or small brush to clear any blockages.
    5.  Run your garbage disposal (if connected) with water to flush the lines.
    6.  Reinstall the covers.

*   **Checking Your High Loop:**
    1.  Look under your sink. Follow the drain hose from the dishwasher.
    2.  Ensure the hose rises to a point higher than the bottom of the sink before dropping down to connect to the drain or disposal.
    3.  If it sags, use a zip tie or hose clamp to secure it higher under the countertop.
    4.  Make sure there are no kinks in the hose where it forms the high loop.

These two components are simple but important. Fixing problems with them can quickly resolve your KitchenAid dishwasher not draining issue. They are part of a good installation. Always ensure they are clear and correctly positioned. They help maintain your dishwasher's hygiene too.

### Troubleshooting Advanced Electrical or Component Failures

After checking all the common mechanical issues, what if your KitchenAid dishwasher still does not drain? This points to more advanced problems. These issues often involve electrical components or other internal parts. This is where troubleshooting gets a bit more complex. You might need a multimeter or specific tools.

One possibility is a faulty drain pump motor. Even if it is clear of debris, the motor itself can burn out. It might make a humming sound but not spin. Or it might be completely silent. This requires replacing the entire pump assembly. Another electrical issue could be a malfunctioning timer or control board. The control board sends signals to all dishwasher components. If it fails, it might not tell the drain pump to activate. This would cause your KitchenAid dishwasher not to drain. These failures are less common than clogs. However, they are serious when they occur.

Sometimes, the float switch can be stuck. The float switch is a safety device. It detects if too much water is in the tub. If it gets stuck in the "water present" position, the dishwasher might not start a drain cycle. It thinks the tub is already full. You can usually find the float assembly at the bottom of the dishwasher tub. It is a small cup or disc that floats up with water. Make sure it moves freely up and down. Clear any debris around it.

*   **Signs of Electrical or Component Failure:**
    *   **No Drain Pump Sound:** The dishwasher runs a cycle but you hear no sound during the drain phase.
    *   **Humming, No Action:** The drain pump hums but does not push water out, even after clearing clogs.
    *   **Dishwasher Not Starting/Stopping Mid-Cycle:** This can indicate a control board issue affecting all functions, including drain.
    *   **Error Codes:** Your KitchenAid dishwasher display may show a specific error code. Consult your owner's manual for what these codes mean. They can point to a pump failure or other sensor issues.

*   **Basic Checks for Advanced Issues:**
    1.  **Reset Power:** Turn off the dishwasher's circuit breaker for 5-10 minutes. Then turn it back on. This can reset the control board and clear minor glitches.
    2.  **Check Float Switch:** Locate the float switch assembly at the bottom of the tub. Ensure it moves freely and is not stuck by debris.
    3.  **Inspect Wiring:** Visually check wiring connections to the drain pump and control board (if accessible). Look for loose, burnt, or corroded wires. *Only do this if comfortable and after disconnecting power.*
    4.  **Listen for Motor Sounds:** During the drain cycle, listen closely. A loud grinding or humming noise without water movement points to a bad drain pump or impeller.

If these advanced troubleshooting steps do not resolve the issue, it is time for professional help. Working with electrical components or replacing major parts like the control board can be dangerous. It also requires specific knowledge. Calling a certified KitchenAid appliance technician is the safest and most effective next step. They have the tools and expertise to diagnose and repair these complex problems. They can quickly figure out [why your KitchenAid dishwasher has water in the bottom](https://homeessentialsguide.com/why-does-my-kitchenaid-dishwasher-have-water-in-the-bottom) due to these less common issues.

### Preventive Maintenance for Optimal Drainage

Preventive maintenance is key to avoiding future KitchenAid dishwasher draining problems. I cannot stress this enough. A little effort now saves you a lot of frustration later. Regular care keeps your appliance running efficiently. It extends its lifespan too. Most drainage issues stem from buildup. By cleaning routinely, you stop these buildups before they become problems.

First, always scrape large food particles off your dishes before loading them. You do not need to rinse them thoroughly. However, remove chunks of food. These chunks are the primary cause of filter and drain clogs. This simple step helps immensely.

Regularly clean your dishwasher's filter. This is the most important maintenance task for drainage. I recommend doing this at least once a month. If you wash a lot of dishes or have hard water, clean it more often. A clean filter ensures smooth water flow. It prevents food from reaching the drain pump. It also stops bad odors.

Running a hot water flush through your sink drain or garbage disposal is also a good habit. Do this regularly, especially after a greasy meal. It helps clear any grease or food residue in the drain lines. This prevents blockages that can affect your dishwasher.

*   **Key Preventive Maintenance Tips:**
    1.  **Pre-scrape Dishes:** Remove large food scraps, bones, and debris from plates before loading them into the dishwasher.
    2.  **Clean Filter Regularly:** Remove and thoroughly clean your dishwasher's filter every 2-4 weeks. Use warm water and a soft brush. For specific instructions, refer to [how to clean KitchenAid dishwasher drain](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-clean-kitchenaid-dishwasher-drain).
    3.  **Run Disposal Frequently:** If your dishwasher drains into a garbage disposal, run the disposal with cold water daily to clear any potential clogs.
    4.  **Use Dishwasher Cleaner:** Periodically run an empty cycle with a specialized dishwasher cleaning product. This helps remove grease, mineral buildup, and odors from internal components. You can also use white vinegar or baking soda for a natural clean.
    5.  **Check Drain Hose for Kinks:** Periodically pull your dishwasher out slightly and visually inspect the drain hose for any kinks or sags. Ensure the high loop is maintained.
    6.  **Avoid Certain Items:** Do not put non-food items or very sticky/greasy items into the dishwasher without proper pre-cleaning, as these can easily cause clogs.

By following these simple maintenance steps, you can significantly reduce the chances of your KitchenAid dishwasher not draining. It keeps your appliance in top condition. This ensures your dishes come out clean. It also extends the life of your dishwasher. Consistent care is the best defense against common appliance problems.
