## Master Your Bissell Crosswave: A Complete Cleaning Guide

Your Bissell Crosswave works hard to keep your floors spotless. This multi-surface cleaner tackles spills, pet messes, and everyday dirt with ease. But like any hard-working appliance, it needs regular cleaning to perform its best. Neglecting to clean your Bissell Crosswave leads to bad odors, decreased suction, and less effective cleaning.

This guide will show you exactly how to clean your Bissell Crosswave. We will cover routine maintenance, detailed cleaning of key components, and vital troubleshooting tips. By following these simple steps, you extend your machine's life and ensure your floors remain sparkling clean. We will break down each part and explain how to clean it thoroughly.

### Takeaway: Keeping Your Crosswave Pristine

*   **Empty and Rinse Tanks Daily:** Always empty the dirty water tank and rinse both tanks after each use.
*   **Clean Brush Roll Regularly:** Remove and clean the brush roll often to prevent hair and debris buildup.
*   **Wash and Dry Filter Properly:** Clean the filter and allow it to air dry completely to maintain suction.
*   **Run Self-Cleaning Cycle:** Use the self-cleaning function after each significant cleaning session.
*   **Store Parts Dry:** Ensure all removable parts are dry before reassembling to prevent odors and mold.

To clean your Bissell Crosswave, empty and rinse the dirty water tank after each use. Remove and clean the brush roll, then wash the filter and let it air dry completely. Run the machine's self-cleaning cycle regularly, and ensure all parts are dry before storing your device.

## Understanding Your Bissell Crosswave's Anatomy for Effective Cleaning

Knowing your Bissell Crosswave's parts makes cleaning it much simpler. This machine has several components that collect dirt and debris during use. Each part needs specific attention to ensure your Crosswave runs efficiently. We will look at the key areas you should focus on when cleaning your device.

Understanding how each part functions helps you identify what needs cleaning. This knowledge helps you maintain your Crosswave properly. Proper maintenance prevents common problems like bad smells or weak suction. My goal is to make sure your machine serves you well for many years.

### Key Components to Focus On

The Bissell Crosswave has several key parts that need regular cleaning. The dirty water tank is where all the extracted grime collects. The brush roll, located at the bottom, scrubs your floors and picks up debris. A filter separates dirt from the air, protecting the motor.

The machine's base and internal pathways also accumulate residue. Forgetting any of these parts leads to poor performance. Cleaning them all ensures thorough hygiene and maximum efficiency. I always start by gathering my tools before I begin cleaning.

### Tools and Supplies You Will Need

Before you start cleaning, gather a few basic supplies. You will need warm water and a mild dish soap for general cleaning. A small brush or old toothbrush helps scrub stubborn areas. A pair of scissors can remove tangled hair from the brush roll.

Having a designated drying area, like a clean towel or drying rack, is also helpful. Sometimes, a vinegar solution works well for odor control, but always check your manual first. Using the right tools makes the cleaning process quick and effective. I keep these items handy near my cleaning supplies.

## Daily Maintenance: Cleaning the Dirty Water Tank and Strainer

The dirty water tank is one of the most important parts to clean after every use. This tank collects all the dirty water, hair, and debris from your floors. If you do not empty and clean it right away, unpleasant odors will quickly develop. Old dirt and grime can also harden, making future cleaning much harder.

My routine always starts here after I finish mopping. I believe prompt attention to this tank is crucial for overall hygiene. This simple step makes a big difference in how well your Crosswave functions. It also keeps your home smelling fresh and clean.

### How to Empty, Rinse, and Disinfect the Tank

To clean the dirty water tank, first, remove it from the machine. Carry it to a sink or toilet and carefully pour out the dirty water. Be prepared for what you might see; it is often quite surprising how much grime your Crosswave picks up! Rinse the tank thoroughly with warm water. I like to use a small amount of dish soap to help break down any greasy residue.

Swirl the soapy water inside the tank, then rinse again until the water runs clear. Pay attention to the inside corners and edges where dirt can hide. For an extra layer of disinfection, you can sometimes use a solution of white vinegar and water, though always consult your specific model's manual for guidance. After rinsing, let the tank air dry completely before putting it back. This prevents mildew growth and bad smells.

### Addressing the Dirty Water Tank Filter and Strainer

Inside the dirty water tank, you will find a filter basket or strainer. This component captures larger debris like hair and food particles. This prevents them from entering the main tank or clogging the machine's internal parts. This strainer needs cleaning every time you empty the tank.

Simply lift out the strainer and rinse it under running water. Use your fingers or a small brush to remove any trapped hair or debris. Ensure all holes in the strainer are clear for proper water flow. A clogged strainer reduces your machine's efficiency. Keeping this small part clean is vital for optimum performance.

## Tackling the Brush Roll: Keeping Your Floors Spotless

The brush roll is the heart of your Bissell Crosswave's cleaning power. It rotates at high speeds, scrubbing your floors and picking up dirt and debris. Because it comes into direct contact with all the mess, it tends to get very dirty. Hair, pet fur, and even string can wrap around it, reducing its effectiveness.

A dirty brush roll cannot clean your floors properly. It can also spread dirt around instead of removing it. My experience shows that keeping this part clean is essential for streak-free results. Regular brush roll maintenance ensures consistently clean floors and prolongs the life of your machine.

### Removing and Cleaning the Brush Roll

To access the brush roll, first, turn off and unplug your Crosswave for safety. Recline the machine to expose the brush window on the foot. You will find a release tab or button to unlock the brush window cover. Carefully remove the cover and then lift out the brush roll.

Now that it's removed, inspect the brush roll closely. You will likely see hair and debris wrapped around it. Use scissors to carefully cut away any tangled hair or fibers. Avoid pulling too hard, as this can damage the bristles. You can also clean other Bissell brush rolls, like those found in the [Bissell Revolution Pet Pro](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-clean-bissell-revolution-pet-pro).

### Washing and Drying the Brush Roll for Optimal Performance

Once the debris is removed, wash the brush roll under warm running water. You can use a mild soap if needed, but plain water usually suffices for daily cleaning. Gently rub the bristles to dislodge any stuck-on dirt or grime. Make sure to rinse until all soap residue is gone and the water runs clear. Pay attention to the ends of the brush roll where gunk can accumulate.

After washing, it is critical to let the brush roll air dry completely. Place it upright in a well-ventilated area for several hours or overnight. Do not put it back into the machine when it is wet. A damp brush roll can lead to mildew, odors, and even damage to your machine. ## Filter Care: Ensuring Optimal Suction Power

The filter in your Bissell Crosswave plays a crucial role in its performance. It traps fine dust and particles, preventing them from reaching and damaging the motor. A clean filter ensures strong suction power, allowing your Crosswave to pick up more dirt and leave your floors cleaner. If the filter becomes clogged with dust and debris, it restricts airflow.

This restricted airflow directly reduces the machine's suction, making it less effective. A dirty filter also puts more strain on the motor, which can lead to premature wear. I always make sure this part is spotless. Proper filter care is essential for maintaining your Crosswave's long-term efficiency.

### Locating and Cleaning the Filter

The filter is typically located near the dirty water tank or within the brush roll area, depending on your Crosswave model. Consult your owner's manual if you have trouble finding it. Once located, gently remove the filter. Some filters are made of foam or felt, while others might be pleated paper. Handle it carefully to avoid dislodging trapped dust.

For most Crosswave filters, you can rinse them under warm running water. If your filter is particularly dirty, you can use a small amount of mild dish soap. Gently squeeze or agitate the foam filters to release trapped dirt. For pleated filters, a soft brush can help dislodge debris between the pleats. Rinse until the water runs clear. Learning about [how to clean a water filter](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-clean-water-filter) in other contexts can provide useful insights into general filter maintenance principles.

### Drying Time and When to Replace the Filter

After cleaning, it is absolutely essential to let the filter air dry completely. This can take up to 24 hours. Placing it in direct sunlight or using a hair dryer is not recommended, as it can damage the filter material. A thoroughly dry filter prevents mold growth and ensures proper airflow. Reinstalling a damp filter can cause immediate issues, like foul odors or reduced performance.

Filters do not last forever, even with regular cleaning. Over time, they lose their ability to trap fine particles effectively. Bissell recommends replacing the filter every 3 to 6 months, or more often if you use your Crosswave frequently or have pets. I mark my calendar to remind myself to replace it. A fresh filter keeps your suction strong and protects your machine's motor. ## The Crosswave Self-Cleaning Cycle: An Essential Step

Many Bissell Crosswave models come with a convenient self-cleaning cycle. This feature is designed to flush out dirt and debris from the internal parts of the machine. It cleans the brush roll and the inside of the machine where dirt and odors can accumulate. This cycle is a fantastic way to maintain your Crosswave between deep cleanings.

It helps prevent clogs and keeps the machine smelling fresh. I always use this function after I finish cleaning my floors. While it does not replace manual cleaning of tanks and filters, it is a vital part of your routine. This cycle simplifies daily maintenance and extends the life of your device.

### How the Self-Cleaning Cycle Works

To use the self-cleaning cycle, you usually place your Crosswave on its storage tray or a designated dock. Fill the clean water tank with clean water up to the self-clean line, often with a small amount of Bissell cleaning formula. Then, you simply press the self-clean button on the machine. The Crosswave will then run a short cycle, typically lasting about 30 to 60 seconds. During this time, clean water flows through the brush roll and suction path, rinsing away residue.

The dirty water is collected in the dirty water tank. This process effectively flushes out hair, grit, and solution residue from the brush roll and internal components. It helps prevent buildup that can lead to clogs and bad odors. Some Bissell models, like the [Bissell Little Green](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-clean-bissell-little-green) or [Bissell Powerfresh steam mop](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-clean-bissell-powerfresh-steam-mop), have their own specific cleaning procedures, but the concept of internal rinsing is similar across various Bissell cleaning devices.

### When to Use It and What It Cleans

You should run the self-cleaning cycle after every major cleaning session. This is especially true if you have cleaned up a significant mess or used the machine on very dirty floors. It is also beneficial after cleaning up pet hair, as it helps clear the brush roll. This cycle primarily cleans the brush roll and the internal pathways.

It helps to wash away hair, pet fur, and dirty water that might otherwise dry and cause clogs. While the self-cleaning cycle is very effective, it does not clean the dirty water tank or the filter. These parts still require manual cleaning, as discussed in previous sections. Think of it as a rinse for the critical internal components, but not a full breakdown clean. ## Deep Cleaning and Odor Prevention for Your Bissell Crosswave

Even with regular cleaning, sometimes your Bissell Crosswave needs a deeper clean. Over time, residue can build up in hard-to-reach areas, leading to persistent odors or reduced performance. This is especially true if you frequently clean up pet messes or very dirty areas. Deep cleaning tackles these hidden grime pockets.

It ensures your machine remains hygienic and functions at its peak. Preventing odors is a major part of this deep cleaning process. I have found that a little extra effort here makes a huge difference. This also helps prolong the overall lifespan of your valuable appliance.

### Beyond Daily: Tubing and Base Cleaning

Once every few weeks or months, depending on usage, consider a more thorough cleaning. This involves inspecting the tubing that carries water and solution. Residue can sometimes accumulate here. If you notice any visible buildup, you might need to carefully flush it out with clean water. Do this by filling the clean water tank with plain water and running the machine without the dirty water tank attached, allowing water to flow through the system into a bucket.

Also, examine the machine's base, especially the area around the brush roll housing. Dirt and grime can get into crevices. Use a small brush or a damp cloth to wipe these areas thoroughly. Pay attention to the small channels and openings where dirty water passes. For similar deep cleaning considerations, you might find it useful to compare with methods for cleaning other specialized cleaners like [how to clean a Bissell Little Green](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-clean-bissell-little-green).

### Using Vinegar for Odor Control (with Caution)

Vinegar is a natural disinfectant and deodorizer. It can be useful for tackling persistent odors in your Bissell Crosswave. However, use vinegar with caution and always check your user manual first. Some manufacturers advise against using vinegar as it can potentially damage rubber seals or plastic components over time. If your manual allows, you can fill the clean water tank with a 50/50 mix of white vinegar and water. Run this solution through the self-cleaning cycle or use it to clean a small, non-critical area of your floor.

This helps flush out odor-causing bacteria from the internal pathways. Remember to follow up with a plain water rinse cycle to remove any vinegar residue. After any vinegar cleaning, ensure all parts are completely air-dried before reassembling the machine. Proper drying is key to preventing future smells.

### Storing Your Crosswave Correctly

How you store your Bissell Crosswave also impacts its cleanliness and lifespan. Always ensure all tanks, the brush roll, and the filter are completely dry before reassembling and storing the machine. Storing a damp machine fosters mold and mildew growth, which causes terrible odors. I always leave my parts disassembled and air dry overnight.

Store your Crosswave in a dry, well-ventilated area, away from extreme temperatures. Do not store it in a damp basement or garage if possible. Keeping the machine upright and ready for its next use in a dry environment preserves its components. This simple step helps maintain its efficiency and prevents unpleasant surprises.

## Troubleshooting Common Cleaning Issues

Even with regular cleaning, you might encounter some common issues with your Bissell Crosswave. Understanding how to troubleshoot these problems saves you time and frustration. Many issues stem from inadequate cleaning or improper drying of parts. I have faced these problems myself and found simple solutions.

Knowing these fixes helps you get your Crosswave back to peak performance quickly. We will cover bad odors, reduced suction, and streaking. These are the most frequent complaints users have.

### Addressing Persistent Bad Odors

If your Crosswave still smells bad after cleaning, it means trapped grime or mildew exists. First, check the dirty water tank and strainer again. Make sure you have thoroughly cleaned them. Sometimes, residue builds up in the tank's crevices. You can use a small brush to scrub these areas.

Next, inspect the brush roll housing and the dirty water pathway in the machine's base. These areas can harbor mildew. Use a damp cloth to wipe them clean. Ensure the filter and brush roll are completely dry before reassembly. If the smell persists, a gentle vinegar solution flush through the system might be necessary, as mentioned previously, with caution. Odors usually come from moisture and organic matter.

### Resolving Poor Suction After Cleaning

Poor suction usually indicates a blockage or a dirty filter. Even after cleaning, a damp filter can restrict airflow. Make sure your filter is bone dry before putting it back. If the filter is old, it might be time for a replacement. Filters lose efficiency over time.

Check the dirty water tank and the float mechanism inside it. If the float is stuck or dirty, it might prematurely cut off suction. Also, inspect the suction path from the brush roll housing to the dirty water tank for any clogs. Hair or large debris can sometimes get lodged in these pathways. A clear path is vital for strong suction.

### Eliminating Streaking on Floors

Streaking on floors often points to a dirty brush roll or too much cleaning solution. First, thoroughly clean the brush roll, ensuring all hair and debris are removed. A gunked-up brush roll will smear dirt. Also, make sure the brush roll is completely dry before use.

Next, check your cleaning solution usage. Using too much solution can leave a sticky residue that causes streaks. Follow Bissell's recommendations for solution-to-water ratios precisely. If you're using plain water, ensure the dirty water tank is completely empty before starting. Sometimes, using a fresh, clean brush roll can also resolve streaking issues.

## Frequently Asked Questions About Cleaning Your Bissell Crosswave

### How often should I clean my Bissell Crosswave?
You should empty and rinse the dirty water tank and clean the brush roll and filter after every use. Run the self-cleaning cycle after each major cleaning session. A deeper clean of the machine's base and pathways is recommended once a month or every few months, depending on how often you use it and how dirty your floors get.

### Can I put Bissell Crosswave parts in the dishwasher?
No, most Bissell Crosswave parts, including the dirty water tank, brush roll, and filter, are not dishwasher safe. The high heat and harsh detergents in a dishwasher can warp plastic components or damage sensitive filter materials. Always wash these parts by hand with warm water and mild soap, then allow them to air dry completely.

### Why does my Bissell Crosswave smell even after cleaning?
Persistent odors often indicate trapped moisture or organic matter. Ensure all parts, especially the brush roll and filter, are completely dry before reassembly. Check for residue in hard-to-reach areas like the dirty water tank crevices or the brush roll housing. A vinegar rinse (if allowed by your manual) can help eliminate stubborn smells.

### How do I know when to replace the filter or brush roll?
Bissell recommends replacing the filter every 3 to 6 months and the brush roll every 6 months to a year, or more often with heavy use. You will know it's time if suction power decreases (for the filter) or if the brush roll bristles are matted, worn, or no longer effectively picking up debris.

### Can I use my own cleaning solution in the Crosswave?
Bissell strongly recommends using only Bissell brand multi-surface cleaning formulas specifically designed for Crosswave machines. Using other solutions, especially those not designed for these machines, can void your warranty, damage internal components, or lead to excessive foaming and poor performance. Stick to the recommended products for best results.

### What if my Crosswave is leaving streaks on the floor?
Streaking is usually caused by a dirty brush roll, using too much cleaning solution, or an overfilled dirty water tank. Ensure your brush roll is clean and completely dry. Use the correct amount of cleaning solution as per the manual. Also, confirm the dirty water tank is empty before each cleaning cycle.
