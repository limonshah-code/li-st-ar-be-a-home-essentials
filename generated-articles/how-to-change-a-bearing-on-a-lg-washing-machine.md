## How to Change an LG Washing Machine Bearing

A noisy washing machine can be a real headache. I understand the frustration of a washer that grinds or rattles during its spin cycle. Often, worn-out drum bearings cause these sounds. This guide will show you how to change a bearing on an LG washing machine yourself. You can restore peace and quiet to your laundry room. This DIY repair saves you money on professional service.

This article covers everything you need. We start with identifying the problem. Then, we list the tools you require. We detail each step of the disassembly and reassembly process. We also offer tips for long-term washer care. By following these instructions, you gain confidence in repairing your appliance. You will soon have your LG washing machine running smoothly again.

### Takeaway

*   **Diagnose the noise:** Listen for grinding or loud thumping during the spin cycle.
*   **Gather tools and parts:** You will need specific tools like a bearing puller and a new bearing kit.
*   **Prioritize safety:** Always unplug the machine and turn off water before starting.
*   **Follow disassembly steps:** Carefully remove panels, the drum, and the old bearings.
*   **Install new bearings:** Ensure proper seating and lubrication of new parts.
*   **Reassemble and test:** Put the machine back together and run a test cycle for function and leaks.

### Concise Answer

To change a bearing on an LG washing machine, first unplug it and turn off the water supply. Disassemble the machine to access the outer tub and inner drum. Remove the old, worn bearings and seal from the tub. Install new bearings and a new seal, then reassemble the washing machine. Finally, test the unit with an empty cycle.

## Identifying a Faulty Bearing in Your LG Washing Machine

Hearing strange sounds from your LG washing machine can be alarming. A common culprit for loud noises, especially during the spin cycle, is a faulty drum bearing. I have experienced this firsthand, and the distinct sounds are usually clear indicators. Knowing these signs helps you decide if a bearing replacement is necessary.

Listen closely to your washer during operation. A healthy washing machine runs quietly, only making sounds from water sloshing or the motor humming. When bearings start to fail, the noise changes dramatically. You might hear a loud grinding sound. Sometimes, it sounds like metal scraping against metal. These noises typically get louder as the spin speed increases. A very loud "jet engine" sound during the final spin is a strong sign of worn bearings.

You can also test the drum by hand. Open the washer door. Grab the inner drum and try to move it up and down, or back and forth. A healthy drum will feel solid with minimal play. If you feel significant looseness or hear a rattling sound as you move the drum, the bearings are likely bad. This play allows the drum to wobble, causing the loud noises and potentially damaging other components. Addressing this issue promptly can prevent further problems. Replacing a drum bearing is a common repair for washing machines. [Learn more about replacing a drum bearing in a washing machine here](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-replace-a-drum-bearing-in-washing-machine).

### Why LG Washing Machine Bearings Fail

Washing machine bearings withstand constant stress. They support the heavy inner drum during high-speed spins. Over time, this stress leads to wear and tear. Water ingress is another major cause of failure. The main tub seal protects the bearings from water. If this seal degrades or cracks, water can leak past it. Water corrodes the metal bearings and washes away their lubrication.

Overloading your LG washing machine also contributes to bearing failure. Excess weight puts extra strain on the bearings. This accelerates their wear. Using too much detergent creates excessive suds. These suds can sometimes push past the tub seal, leading to water damage. Regular maintenance and avoiding overloading can extend the life of your bearings. Understanding the causes helps in preventing future issues.

## Essential Tools and Safety for LG Washer Bearing Replacement

Before starting any repair, gather all necessary tools and safety equipment. This prepares you for a smooth process. Having the right tools saves time and frustration. Safety is always paramount. I always make sure I have everything ready before I begin.

First, you need basic hand tools. A set of screwdrivers (Phillips and flathead) is essential. A socket wrench set with various sizes will be helpful for bolts. You will also need pliers, an adjustable wrench, and a utility knife. A rubber mallet and a block of wood are useful for gently tapping parts. A bearing puller or a punch and hammer set are critical for removing the old bearings. You will also need some high-quality waterproof grease for new bearings.

For safety, always start by unplugging the washing machine from the wall outlet. Turn off both hot and cold water supply valves. Disconnect the water hoses from the back of the washer. Wear heavy-duty work gloves to protect your hands. Safety glasses are also important to protect your eyes from debris. Clear your workspace to avoid tripping hazards. Make sure you have good lighting. Working carefully and methodically reduces the risk of injury.

### Sourcing Your LG Washing Machine Bearing Kit

Finding the correct replacement parts is crucial. You need a specific bearing and seal kit for your LG washing machine model. LG washing machines come in many types, and parts vary. Look for the model number on a sticker inside the washer door, on the back, or sometimes underneath. Use this model number to search for the exact bearing kit online or at an appliance parts store.

A typical kit includes two bearings (one large, one small) and a new tub seal. The tub seal is vital for protecting the new bearings from water. Never reuse the old seal. Sometimes, the spider arm (the metal part connecting the inner drum to the shaft) might also be corroded. Inspect it carefully. If it shows severe corrosion, consider replacing it as well. A corroded spider arm can weaken and fail, causing similar noise issues. Make sure you get genuine or high-quality aftermarket parts for durability. ## Disassembly: Accessing the Tub and Drum in Your LG Washer

Disassembling an LG washing machine requires a methodical approach. I always take pictures at each step to help with reassembly. Start by ensuring the machine is completely disconnected from power and water. Safety first is not just a saying; it is a necessity here.

Begin by removing the top panel. Usually, two or three screws secure it at the back of the washer. Remove these screws. Slide the top panel backward, then lift it off. Next, remove the control panel. This often involves screws hidden under the top panel or plastic clips. Be gentle with the wiring. You might need to disconnect some wire harnesses from the control panel. Keep track of where each wire connects. Labeling them can be a good idea.

The detergent dispenser also needs removal. Pull it out completely. Then, unclip or unscrew any retaining components. This allows you to remove the dispenser housing. If your LG is a front-loader, you must deal with the door and the door seal. Carefully unclip the wire retaining spring around the door boot. Peel back the door seal from the front. Remove the screws securing the door lock mechanism. You may need to replace the door seal later. [See how to change a door seal on an LG washing machine if you need more detail on that part](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-change-door-seal-on-lg-washing-machine).

### Removing the Front Panel and Components

With the top and control panels off, you can usually access the main front panel. For front-loaders, this often means removing screws at the top and bottom. Sometimes, they are hidden behind a kick plate. Tilt the machine back slightly to access bottom screws if needed. Once the front panel is loose, carefully disconnect any wires attached to it, such as the door lock. Remove the front panel and set it aside.

Next, you will address internal components. Locate the counterweights, usually heavy concrete blocks, bolted to the outer tub. These help balance the machine. Unbolt them carefully; they are heavy. Now, find the drive belt and the motor. The drive belt connects the motor to the inner drum pulley. Roll the belt off the motor pulley and then the drum pulley. Unbolt the motor from the outer tub. Disconnect any electrical connections to the motor. Be careful with wiring. Remove the motor and place it safely aside. At this point, you might also disconnect the drain pump. [Learn how to change a drain pump on an LG washing machine if you need to remove it](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-change-drain-pump-on-lg-washing-machine). This deep disassembly is essential to reach the heart of the problem: the bearings.

## Separating the Outer Tub and Inner Drum Assembly

Reaching the bearings means separating the washerâ€™s outer tub. This tub contains the inner drum. The bearings are pressed into the back of this outer tub. This step often requires some muscle and care. I find that patience is key here.

First, you need to disconnect the suspension rods. These rods support the outer tub assembly. There are typically four rods, one at each corner. Lift the outer tub slightly to relieve tension on the rods. Then, unhook them from the top frame of the washer. Carefully lower the tub. Disconnect all hoses attached to the outer tub. This includes the air trap hose, the dispenser hoses, and the drain hose leading to the pump. Make sure no water remains in these hoses or the tub itself.

The outer tub itself is usually made of two halves. These halves are either bolted together or held by numerous clips. If bolted, use your socket wrench to remove all the bolts around the seam. If clipped, carefully pry open the clips using a screwdriver or pliers. Work your way around the entire seam. Once all fasteners are removed, you can separate the front half of the outer tub from the back half. This reveals the inner drum inside.

### Extracting the Inner Drum

With the outer tub halves separated, you can now remove the inner drum. The drum's shaft passes through the bearings in the back half of the outer tub. You may need to gently tap the shaft with a rubber mallet to push it through the bearings. Be sure to support the inner drum as it comes out. You do not want it to drop suddenly. Once the inner drum is free, lift it out of the back half of the outer tub.

Examine the inner drum and its shaft. Check the spider arm, the metal cross-piece connecting the drum to the shaft. It is common for this part to corrode. If the spider arm is heavily corroded, you should replace it. Corrosion can lead to imbalance and stress on new bearings. Replacing the inner drum if the spider arm is integral might be an option if damage is severe. Cleaning any residue from the inner drum is also a good idea. Now, the back half of the outer tub, containing the faulty bearings, is accessible for repair.

## Replacing the Old Bearings and Seal on the LG Washing Machine

This is the core of the repair. Now you have the back half of the outer tub, which holds the old bearings and seal. We need to remove them and install the new ones. This step requires precision and a gentle touch to avoid damaging the tub. I always take my time with this part.

First, you need to remove the old tub seal. This seal sits at the front of the bearing housing. Use a flathead screwdriver to carefully pry it out. The seal is usually rubber or a rubber-coated metal ring. Once the seal is out, you will see the outer bearing. Before removing the bearings, clean any old grease, dirt, or detergent residue from the bearing housing. A wire brush and some degreaser can help. Cleanliness is important for the new bearings to seat properly.

Now, remove the old bearings. There are usually two bearings: an outer one and an inner one. They are pressed into the tub housing. You can often drive them out from the inside of the tub. Place the tub on a stable surface with the bearing housing facing down. Use a punch that fits inside the bearing race and a hammer. Tap evenly around the circumference of the bearing. It should slowly push out. For stubborn bearings, a bearing puller might be necessary. Work slowly and patiently to avoid damaging the plastic tub housing. Once the first bearing is out, repeat the process for the second inner bearing.

### Installing New Bearings and Seal

With the old parts removed, it is time for the new ones. Clean the bearing housing again thoroughly. Ensure no old debris remains. Apply a thin layer of high-quality, waterproof bearing grease to the new seal's outer edge. This helps it slide in. Carefully press the new tub seal into place. Ensure it sits flush and is evenly installed. The seal is critical. It protects the new bearings from water.

Next, install the new bearings. Start with the inner bearing first. Apply a thin layer of grease to the outer race of the new bearing. Place the bearing into the housing. Use a block of wood or a bearing driver that matches the outer diameter of the bearing. Gently tap the bearing into place with a rubber mallet. Make sure it goes in straight and evenly. Do not hit the inner race. That can damage the new bearing. Once the first bearing is fully seated, repeat the process for the second, outer bearing. Again, ensure it is flush and seated correctly. The replacement process for LG bearings is very similar to replacing tub bearings in other brands. [You can refer to how to replace a GE washing machine tub bearing for a similar method](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-replace-ge-washing-machine-tub-bearing). After both bearings are in, apply a little more grease around the seal and the exposed bearing areas. This provides initial lubrication and protection.

## Reassembly: Putting Your LG Washer Back Together

You have successfully replaced the bearings and seal. Now comes the task of putting everything back together. This is essentially the reverse of the disassembly process. I always find that having taken photos during disassembly really helps here. Go step by step and be thorough.

First, reinstall the inner drum into the outer tub. Carefully align the drum's shaft with the new bearings. Guide the shaft through the bearings. You might need to gently tap the end of the shaft with a rubber mallet to ensure it fully seats. Ensure the inner drum spins freely and quietly. If you feel any resistance or hear grinding, stop and recheck the bearings and shaft alignment. The drum should turn smoothly.

Next, rejoin the two halves of the outer tub. Make sure the gasket between the two halves is clean and properly seated. Align the halves carefully. Secure them with all the bolts or clips you removed earlier. Tighten the bolts evenly, working in a crisscross pattern if possible. This ensures a tight, leak-free seal. Reconnect all hoses to the outer tub. Ensure hose clamps are secure. You do not want any leaks when the machine runs.

### Reinstalling Components and Final Assembly

Now, you will reinstall the larger components. Reattach the motor to the outer tub. Connect its electrical wiring. Replace the drive belt onto the motor pulley and then the large drum pulley. Make sure the belt is properly aligned and tensioned. Reinstall the counterweights. Ensure they are bolted securely to the outer tub. Loose counterweights cause vibration and noise.

Attach the front panel of the washing machine. Connect any wires that go to the door lock mechanism. Secure the panel with all its screws. If you had a front-loader, reinstall the door seal. Secure it with its wire retaining spring. Push the door seal back into place around the drum opening. Reinstall the detergent dispenser housing and the dispenser drawer.

Finally, reattach the control panel. Connect all wire harnesses to their correct spots. Double-check your connections against any photos you took. Secure the control panel. Replace the top panel, sliding it forward and screwing it into place at the back. Ensure all panels sit flush. Take one last look around your workspace. Make sure no tools or loose parts remain inside the washer.

## Testing Your LG Washing Machine After Bearing Replacement

After all that hard work, it is time to see if your LG washing machine is working correctly. This testing phase is crucial. It confirms the repair was successful. It also identifies any remaining issues. I always approach this step with a mix of excitement and caution.

First, reconnect the water hoses to the back of the washer. Turn on both hot and cold water supply valves. Check for any leaks at the hose connections. Tighten them if necessary. Now, plug the washing machine back into the wall outlet. Carefully slide the washer back into its position. Make sure it is level. An unlevel machine can cause vibrations and premature wear on components, including new bearings. You can use a spirit level to check this.

Run a short, empty test cycle. Choose a cycle that includes a spin. I usually pick a rinse and spin cycle. This allows you to observe the machine's operation without the added weight of laundry. Stand by and listen carefully. The goal is to hear a quiet hum during the spin, not the loud grinding or rattling from before. Watch for any unusual vibrations. Also, inspect underneath and around the machine for any water leaks. Leaks indicate a problem with hose connections or the tub seal. If you notice any issues after the repair, you might need to reset your LG washing machine. [You can learn how to reset your LG washing machine if issues arise](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-reset-your-lg-washing-machine).

### Addressing Any Issues and Final Thoughts

If the machine runs smoothly and quietly, congratulations! You have successfully changed the bearings. You have saved yourself a significant repair bill. Enjoy your quiet laundry cycles once more.

If you encounter problems during the test cycle, do not panic.
*   **Loud noise:** Recheck the installation of the bearings. Make sure they are seated correctly. Verify the spider arm is not damaged.
*   **Leaks:** Inspect all hose connections and the main tub seal. You might need to retighten hose clamps or re-seat the tub gasket.
*   **Vibrations:** Ensure the machine is level. Check that counterweights are securely bolted. Make sure the drive belt is properly aligned.

If issues persist, you might need to re-open the machine to inspect your work. Sometimes, a part might not be seated perfectly. Do not hesitate to consult the service manual for your specific LG model if you get stuck. Your persistence will pay off with a fully functional and quiet washing machine.

## Preventing Future Bearing Failures and Extending Washer Life

Replacing the bearings on your LG washing machine is a significant repair. Taking steps to prevent future failures makes sense. Good maintenance habits can extend the life of your washer significantly. I believe that prevention is always better than cure.

One of the biggest culprits for bearing stress is overloading the machine. Always respect your washer's capacity limits. Stuffing too many clothes into the drum creates excessive weight and imbalance. This puts immense strain on the bearings during the spin cycle. Overloading also makes clothes less clean. Distribute laundry evenly in the drum. Avoid washing a single heavy item alone, like a large blanket. These single items can create an extreme imbalance.

Using the correct amount and type of detergent is also important. Excessive detergent creates too many suds. These suds can sometimes push past the tub seal. This allows water to reach and damage the bearings. Use high-efficiency (HE) detergent for HE machines. Always follow the manufacturer's recommendations for detergent quantities. Regular cleaning of your LG washing machine is also beneficial. It prevents detergent buildup and mold. [Learn how to clean an LG washing machine thoroughly](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-clean-an-lg-washing-machine).

### Regular Maintenance for Longevity

Maintaining your LG washing machine helps all components, including the bearings. Always ensure your machine is perfectly level. Vibrations from an unlevel machine put unnecessary stress on the bearings and other moving parts. Adjust the leveling feet as needed. Use a spirit level to check both front-to-back and side-to-side.

Periodically check the drain filter. A clogged filter can affect drainage and lead to issues, though not directly bearing failure. Keeping the machine clean inside and out contributes to overall health. Check hoses for cracks or leaks regularly. Address any small issues promptly before they become major problems. A well-maintained washing machine provides years of trouble-free service. By following these simple practices, you can significantly prolong the life of your new bearings and your LG washing machine.

The cost to replace LG washing machine bearings varies. If you do it yourself, parts typically cost between $50 and $150 for the bearing and seal kit. Hiring a professional service can cost significantly more. Professional labor fees can add $200 to $400, bringing the total to $250 to $550. DIY saves substantial money.

### How long does an LG washing machine bearing last?
LG washing machine bearings typically last between 5 to 10 years. Their lifespan depends on usage frequency, load sizes, and maintenance. Overloading, using too much detergent, and operating an unlevel machine can shorten their life. Proper care and avoiding excessive strain help extend their durability.

### Can I use my LG washer with a bad bearing?
No, you should not use your LG washer with a bad bearing. Continuing to use it can cause further damage. The inner drum can wear against the outer tub. This can create more expensive repairs, like replacing the entire tub. A bad bearing can also lead to more serious failures and potential safety hazards.

### What causes LG washing machine bearings to fail?
Several factors cause LG washing machine bearings to fail. The most common cause is wear and tear from normal use over time. Water leaking past a faulty tub seal corrodes the bearings. Overloading the washing machine puts excessive stress on them. Also, an unlevel machine causes constant vibrations, accelerating wear.

### Is it hard to change a washing machine bearing?
Changing a washing machine bearing is a challenging DIY repair. It requires significant disassembly of the appliance. You need specific tools and some mechanical aptitude. For LG washer bearing replacement, you will need screwdrivers, a socket wrench set, pliers, and an adjustable wrench. A rubber mallet, a punch, and a hammer are essential for removing old bearings.
