## FAQ Section

### Q1: Why is my Kitchenaid dishwasher stuck on control lock?

Your Kitchenaid dishwasher may be stuck on control lock due to a minor electronic glitch. It could also be from improper deactivation. First, try holding the designated control lock button for three seconds again. If that does not work, perform a power reset. Unplug the dishwasher or flip its circuit breaker off for 5-10 minutes. This often clears the issue.

### Q2: Can I run a wash cycle with the control lock active?

No, you cannot run a wash cycle or change settings when the control lock is active. The control lock disables all functions of the control panel. Its purpose is to prevent any interaction with the dishwasher. You must deactivate the control lock first to start or modify a cycle.

### Q3: Is the control lock feature standard on all Kitchenaid dishwashers?

Most modern Kitchenaid dishwashers include a control lock feature. It is a widely adopted safety standard. However, some older or very basic models might not have it. Always check your specific model's user manual to confirm its presence and activation method.

### Q4: How do I know if my control lock is active on my Kitchenaid dishwasher?

You will know the control lock is active if a specific indicator light is on. This light often displays a lock symbol, "LOC," or "Control Lock" on the panel. Also, when you press any other button, the dishwasher will not respond. It will remain in its current state.

### Q5: What if holding the unlock button for three seconds doesn't work?

If holding the unlock button does not work, try a power reset of the dishwasher. Disconnect it from power for several minutes. Reconnect it and try unlocking again. Ensure you are holding the correct button for the specified duration. Consult your user manual for model-specific instructions. If the issue persists, the control panel may need service.

### Q6: Does a power outage affect the control lock on my Kitchenaid dishwasher?

A power outage can sometimes affect the control lock. Some models may retain the lock setting after power is restored. Others might reset it. It largely depends on the specific dishwasher model. If it remains locked, simply follow the deactivation steps. Perform a power cycle if needed.
