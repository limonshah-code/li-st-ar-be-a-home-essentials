## Comparing Chem-Dry to Traditional Steam Cleaning

When considering carpet cleaning options, people often compare Chem-Dry to traditional steam cleaning. Both methods aim to clean carpets. However, they use very different approaches. Understanding these differences helps you make an informed decision for your home. I want to highlight these distinctions clearly.

Traditional steam cleaning, also known as hot water extraction, uses a lot of hot water. It sprays this hot water, often mixed with detergents, deep into your carpet fibers. Then, a powerful vacuum extracts the water and dirt. The main drawback is the sheer volume of water used. This can leave carpets saturated. Saturated carpets take a long time to dry, often 12-24 hours. During this period, mold and mildew can grow. This creates an unhealthy environment.

Chem-Dry, in contrast, uses its Hot Carbonating Extraction (HCE) method. This process relies on carbonated bubbles, not excessive water. It uses only about one-fifth of the water compared to steam cleaning. The carbonation lifts dirt to the surface. Then, it is extracted. This minimal water usage is a game-changer. It means your carpets dry in 1-2 hours. This significantly reduces the risk of mold and mildew. The difference in drying time is perhaps the most noticeable benefit.

Another key difference is residue. Steam cleaning often uses soapy detergents. These detergents can leave a sticky residue in your carpet. This residue acts like a magnet, attracting new dirt quickly. Your carpets may look clean initially. But they get dirty again faster. Chem-Dry's primary cleaning solution is non-toxic and leaves no sticky residue. Your carpets stay cleaner for a longer period. This is a big win for maintaining a fresh home.

Regarding effectiveness, both methods remove dirt. However, Chem-Dry's process is independently proven to remove 98% of allergens and 89% of airborne bacteria. Steam cleaning can also remove these contaminants. But the excessive moisture can create other issues. Many argue that Chem-Dry offers a healthier clean.
