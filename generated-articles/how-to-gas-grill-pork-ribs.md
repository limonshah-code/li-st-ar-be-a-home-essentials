## Grilling Pork Ribs on Your Gas Grill

Imagine biting into pork ribs that are juicy, smoky, and fall-off-the-bone tender. Many people think you need a dedicated smoker for this, but I am here to tell you that your gas grill is a powerful tool. It can produce incredible ribs. Learning **how to gas grill pork ribs** transforms backyard cooking. This guide shows you step-by-step how to achieve perfect results every time. We will cover selecting ribs, preparing them, setting up your grill, and cooking them to perfection. Get ready to impress everyone with your grilling skills.

### Takeaway

*   Choose meaty ribs and remove the membrane for better texture.
*   Set up your gas grill for indirect heat, maintaining a consistent low temperature.
*   Use a dry rub for flavor and consider wood chips for smoke.
*   Cook ribs low and slow until they are tender.
*   Apply sauce in the final cooking stage to prevent burning.

### Clear, Concise Answer

To gas grill pork ribs, prepare them by removing the membrane and applying a dry rub. Set your gas grill for indirect heat at 250-275°F (121-135°C). Cook the ribs low and slow for several hours, typically using a 3-2-1 method for optimal tenderness, adding wood chips for smoke, and saucing near the end.

## Selecting and Preparing Your Pork Ribs

Choosing the right ribs is the first step toward amazing results. I always look for meaty racks with good marbling. Avoid ribs with too much fat or very thin spots. The type of rib also matters for **gas grill pork ribs**. You often find baby back ribs or spare ribs. Baby back ribs are smaller and leaner. Spare ribs are larger, meatier, and have more connective tissue. They often become more tender with long cooking. St. Louis style ribs are spare ribs trimmed into a rectangular shape.
