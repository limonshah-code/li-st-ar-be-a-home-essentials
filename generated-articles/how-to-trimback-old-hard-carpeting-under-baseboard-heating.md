## Trim Old Hard Carpeting Under Baseboard Heating

Old carpet can become a real eyesore, especially when it bunches up or hardens near baseboard heating. You might feel daunted by the prospect of tackling this task yourself, given the tight spaces and the carpet's stubborn nature. Many homeowners face this exact challenge, unsure how to achieve a clean, professional finish without damaging their heating system or the floor underneath.

This guide provides a straightforward method for you to trimback old hard carpeting under baseboard heating. We will cover essential tools, critical safety measures, and step-by-step instructions. You will learn how to prepare your workspace, cut the hardened carpet effectively, and deal with any stubborn adhesive. We also provide tips for cleanup and long-term maintenance. This article makes a difficult job manageable, ensuring your room looks its best.

### Takeaway

Trimming old, hard carpeting under baseboard heating requires patience and the right tools.
*   **Prioritize Safety:** Always turn off heating and wear protective gear.
*   **Gather Tools:** A sharp utility knife, carpet shears, and a pry bar are essential.
*   **Prepare the Area:** Clean around baseboards and protect surfaces.
*   **Cut Carefully:** Make small, controlled cuts to avoid damage.
*   **Address Adhesion:** Use leverage or gentle heat for stubborn spots.
