### Step-by-Step Cleaning Process for Stubborn Cat Vomit Stains

Let's walk through the exact steps to effectively remove those stubborn **old cat vomit stains** from your carpet. This method combines the best practices for stain and odor removal.

1.  **Scrape and Vacuum:** As mentioned, first, use a dull knife or spoon to gently scrape away all dried vomit residue. Be careful not to press too hard or spread the material. Then, vacuum the area thoroughly to pick up any loose pieces. This initial cleaning prevents further spread.
2.  **Apply Your Chosen Cleaner:** Generously apply your chosen enzymatic cleaner or homemade solution (e.g., vinegar and water mix) directly onto the stain. Ensure the product saturates the carpet fibers. For enzyme cleaners, follow the label for recommended dwell time. This allows the enzymes to penetrate and break down the organic matter. Let the cleaner work.
3.  **Blot, Do Not Rub:** Use a clean, white cloth or paper towel to gently blot the stain. Start from the outside edge and work your way inward. This prevents the stain from spreading. Repeat blotting with fresh sections of the cloth until no more stain transfers. Avoid rubbing, as this can push the stain deeper and damage carpet fibers.
4.  **Rinse the Area:** Dampen a separate clean cloth with plain water. Blot the treated area again to rinse away any remaining cleaner and loosened stain particles. It is important to remove all cleaning solution residue. Leftover residue can attract dirt later. This keeps your carpet cleaner.
5.  **Repeat if Necessary:** For very old or deep-set stains, you might need to repeat steps 2-4 multiple times. Patience is key here. Each application should lift more of the stain. Do not get discouraged if it does not disappear instantly.
6.  **Dry Thoroughly:** Once satisfied with the stain removal, place several layers of clean, dry towels over the damp area. Apply pressure by standing on the towels or placing a heavy object on them. This helps absorb excess moisture. Air dry the area completely. You can use a fan or open a window to speed up drying.
