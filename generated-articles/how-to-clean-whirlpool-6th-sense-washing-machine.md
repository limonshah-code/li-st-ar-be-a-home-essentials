### Deep Cleaning the Washing Machine Drum and Interior

The drum is where all the washing happens, so keeping it clean is vital. This main part of your Whirlpool 6th Sense washing machine can harbor unseen dirt. A regular deep clean prevents musty odors and ensures your clothes are truly clean. You can use a specific washer cleaner or common household products.

#### Running a Cleaning Cycle with Vinegar and Baking Soda

This method is a popular natural choice for many homeowners. First, ensure your washing machine is empty of all clothes. Next, pour two cups of white vinegar directly into the detergent dispenser. You can also pour it into the main wash drum if you prefer. Then, select the hottest water setting available on your Whirlpool 6th Sense machine. Some Whirlpool models have a "Clean Washer" cycle. Use this if your machine has it. Start the cycle.

After the machine fills with water and agitates for a few minutes, pause the cycle. Let the vinegar-water solution soak inside the drum for about one hour. This allows the vinegar to break down soap scum and kill bacteria. After soaking, restart the cycle and let it complete. Once the cycle finishes, sprinkle half a cup of baking soda into the drum. Run another hot water cycle immediately. This second cycle helps rinse away any remaining grime and neutralizes odors effectively.

#### Using Commercial Washer Cleaners Like Affresh

Commercial cleaning tablets, such as Affresh, offer a convenient alternative. These tablets are formulated to dissolve and remove odor-causing residues. To use an Affresh tablet, place one directly into the washing machine drum. Do not put it in the dispenser. Then, close the washer door. Select the "Clean Washer" cycle on your Whirlpool 6th Sense machine. If your machine does not have a dedicated clean cycle, select the hottest water setting for a normal cycle.

Start the cycle and let it run completely. These cleaners work by penetrating and dissolving stubborn buildup. Always follow the specific instructions on the packaging of your chosen cleaner. Using a commercial cleaner periodically ensures a thorough deep clean. This helps maintain the freshness of your washing machine drum.

### Targeting Key Areas: Gasket, Dispenser, and Filter

Beyond the drum, specific parts of your Whirlpool 6th Sense washing machine collect significant buildup. These areas are often overlooked but are crucial for a truly clean machine. Paying attention to the door gasket, detergent dispenser, and drain pump filter prevents odors and improves performance. Let's tackle these hidden spots.

#### Cleaning the Door Gasket (Front-Load Washers)

The door gasket, also known as the rubber seal, on front-load washers is a common culprit for mold and mildew. This area stays damp and traps lint, hair, and detergent residue. To clean it, first, open the washer door wide. Carefully pull back the rubber seal to expose the interior folds. You will likely see dark spots of mold or grime here.

Create a cleaning solution by mixing equal parts white vinegar and water in a spray bottle. Spray this solution generously onto the gasket. Let it sit for a few minutes to loosen the grime. Then, use a microfiber cloth or an old toothbrush to scrub away the mold and mildew. Pay close attention to all the nooks and crannies. For tough stains, you can use a paste of baking soda and a little water. Apply it, let it sit, then scrub. Wipe the gasket thoroughly with a clean, damp cloth to remove all cleaning solution and residue. Ensure the gasket is completely dry before closing the door. #### Maintaining the Detergent Dispenser

The detergent dispenser drawer often gets sticky and moldy. Detergent and fabric softener residues build up here. These sticky residues can block the dispenser's function and cause odors. First, remove the dispenser drawer completely if your model allows. Many Whirlpool 6th Sense washers have a release tab to pull the drawer out.

Once removed, rinse the drawer under hot running water. Use a small brush, like an old toothbrush, to scrub away any hardened residue. For stubborn grime, soak the dispenser in hot soapy water for about 30 minutes. You can also use a vinegar-water solution. Scrub all compartments thoroughly. Clean the housing where the dispenser sits as well. Use a damp cloth to wipe inside the opening where the drawer slides. Ensure all parts are clean and dry before reinserting the dispenser.

#### Cleaning the Drain Pump Filter

The drain pump filter traps lint, coins, and other small items before they reach the pump. A clogged filter can cause drainage issues and unpleasant smells. This filter is usually located at the bottom front of your washing machine, behind a small access panel. First, place an old towel or shallow pan underneath the access panel to catch any water. The machine might release a small amount of water.

Open the access panel. Unscrew or unclip the filter cover. Carefully pull out the filter. You will likely find lint, hair, and possibly small objects like buttons or coins. Remove all debris from the filter. Rinse the filter under running water until it is clean. Use a small brush to clean any stubborn grime. Also, check inside the filter housing for any lingering debris. Once clean, screw or clip the filter back into place. Close the access panel. You may need to open the machine door to fully access the filter housing after cleaning. While the interior cleaning is crucial for machine performance, the exterior of your Whirlpool 6th Sense washing machine deserves attention too. ### Preventative Measures for a Fresh Whirlpool 6th Sense Washer

Regular deep cleaning is important, but daily habits also contribute significantly to a fresh washing machine. Implementing simple preventative measures can drastically reduce mold, mildew, and odor issues. These steps help your Whirlpool 6th Sense washer stay clean longer.

#### Leaving the Door Ajar

This is perhaps the simplest yet most effective preventative tip. After each wash cycle, leave the washing machine door slightly open. This allows air to circulate inside the drum. It helps evaporate any residual moisture. Mold and mildew thrive in dark, damp environments. By allowing the interior to air dry, you deny these unwelcome guests their ideal breeding ground. I always leave my washer door open for at least an hour after a wash. This simple act keeps the drum smelling fresh.

#### Using the Right Amount of Detergent

Many people use too much detergent, thinking it will make clothes cleaner. However, excess detergent does not rinse away completely. It leaves a sticky residue inside the drum, dispenser, and hoses. This residue acts as food for mold and mildew. Refer to your detergent's instructions and your washing machine's manual for proper dosing. High-efficiency (HE) washers require less detergent than traditional machines. Using HE detergent in an HE washer is also important. The wrong type creates too many suds, leading to residue.

#### Removing Wet Laundry Promptly

Do not leave wet laundry in the washing machine for extended periods. This creates a highly humid environment. It promotes rapid growth of mildew inside the drum and on the clothes. As soon as the wash cycle finishes, transfer your clothes to the dryer or hang them to dry. Prompt removal keeps both your clothes and your washing machine fresher.

#### Wiping Down the Gasket After Each Use

For front-load Whirlpool 6th Sense washers, quickly wipe down the door gasket after every wash. Use a clean cloth to absorb any standing water or moisture trapped in the folds of the seal. This prevents water from sitting there and growing mold. This quick wipe-down only takes a few seconds. It makes a big difference in maintaining a clean and odor-free gasket.

By adopting these habits, you will significantly reduce the need for frequent deep cleaning. Your Whirlpool 6th Sense washing machine will stay fresher and more hygienic.

### Troubleshooting Common Cleaning Issues

Even with regular cleaning, you might encounter specific issues with your Whirlpool 6th Sense washing machine. Persistent odors or stubborn stains can be frustrating. Knowing how to troubleshoot these common problems helps you maintain a pristine machine.

#### Addressing Persistent Foul Odors

If a musty smell lingers despite cleaning cycles, it often indicates deeper mold or residue buildup. First, re-check the door gasket thoroughly. Mold can hide in the deepest folds. If the drain pump filter has not been cleaned recently, inspect it. A clogged or dirty filter often causes bad smells. You might also need to run two consecutive cleaning cycles with a strong solution. Try using a cup of bleach (if safe for your machine) in a hot water cycle, followed by a rinse cycle. Always check your machine's manual for bleach compatibility. If the door to your washer seems stuck, you may need to consult resources on [how to unlock your Whirlpool washing machine](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-do-i-unlock-my-whirlpool-washing-machine) to gain access.

#### Removing Hard Water Stains

Hard water leaves mineral deposits, especially around the detergent dispenser and inside the drum. These appear as white or chalky buildup. Vinegar is excellent for breaking down hard water stains. For superficial stains, spray a vinegar solution directly onto the affected area. Let it sit for 10-15 minutes, then scrub with a brush or cloth. For heavier buildup in the drum, run a cleaning cycle with two cups of vinegar, letting it soak midway through the cycle. Regular use of vinegar in cleaning cycles helps prevent these deposits.

#### Dealing with Soap Scum Buildup

Soap scum is a sticky film left by detergent and fabric softener. It accumulates on the drum, dispenser, and even inside the hoses. If you notice a slimy residue, you might be using too much detergent. Reduce the amount of detergent you use. Run an empty, hot water cycle with two cups of white vinegar. The vinegar helps dissolve the soap scum. Periodically cleaning the dispenser drawer by soaking it in hot, soapy water also prevents this buildup. For persistent residue inside the machine, you might need to run a professional washer cleaner like Affresh.

By systematically addressing these issues, you can restore your Whirlpool 6th Sense washing machine to its clean and fresh state. Patience and thoroughness are key to successful troubleshooting.

### FAQ Section

#### Q1: How often should I clean my Whirlpool 6th Sense washing machine?
**A1:** You should clean your Whirlpool 6th Sense washing machine at least once a month. For households with heavy usage, hard water, or those prone to odors, cleaning every two to three weeks is beneficial. This schedule helps prevent the buildup of mold, mildew, and detergent residue. Consistent cleaning keeps your machine performing well.

#### Q2: Can I use bleach to clean my Whirlpool 6th Sense washer?
**A2:** Yes, you can typically use bleach to sanitize your Whirlpool 6th Sense washing machine. Add about one cup of liquid chlorine bleach to the dispenser or directly into the drum. Then, run a hot water "Clean Washer" cycle or a normal cycle on the hottest setting. Always check your washer's manual for specific instructions.

#### Q3: Why does my clean laundry still smell musty after washing?
**A3:** Musty smells in clean laundry usually indicate mold or mildew growth inside the washing machine. This often happens in the door gasket, detergent dispenser, or drum. Perform a deep cleaning cycle with vinegar or a commercial washer cleaner. Also, remember to leave the washer door ajar after each use.

#### Q4: Is it safe to mix vinegar and baking soda for cleaning the washer?
**A4:** Yes, it is safe and effective to use vinegar and baking soda separately for cleaning your washing machine. Do not mix them directly together in the same immediate application, as they neutralize each other. Instead, use vinegar in one cycle, and then follow with a baking soda cycle to get the full cleaning and deodorizing benefits.

#### Q5: What is the "Clean Washer" cycle on my Whirlpool 6th Sense machine?
**A5:** The "Clean Washer" cycle on your Whirlpool 6th Sense machine is a specialized setting. It uses a higher water level and specific agitation patterns with hot water to clean the inside of the wash drum. This cycle works best with a washer cleaner tablet or liquid bleach to remove odor-causing residues.

#### Q6: How do I prevent mold from growing in my washing machine gasket?
**A6:** To prevent mold in the gasket, always wipe the rubber seal dry after each wash cycle. Leave the washer door ajar between washes to allow air circulation. Regularly clean the gasket with a vinegar solution and scrub away any early signs of mildew. Proper detergent use also helps reduce residue buildup.
