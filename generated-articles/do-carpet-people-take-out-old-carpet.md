This article answers your question fully. We will cover what removal services involve and what to expect. You will learn about costs, preparation, and alternative options. We will also look at how to get rid of old carpet responsibly.

### Do Carpet People Take Out Old Carpet?

Yes, professional carpet installers often take out old carpet. This service is usually an extra option you can add to your new carpet installation order. It saves you time and physical effort. The "carpet people" handle the heavy lifting and disposal.

### Understanding Carpet Removal Services: What's Included?

When you opt for professional old carpet removal, you get a specific set of services. These services simplify the whole process for you. Knowing what is included helps you compare different quotes. It also sets your expectations for installation day.

Typically, **carpet removal services** cover several key steps. First, the team removes the existing carpet. This involves cutting it into manageable sections. They carefully pull it up from the floor. Next, they remove the old carpet padding. Padding often sticks to the subfloor or has staples. They also remove old tack strips. Tack strips have sharp points that hold the carpet down. Removing these strips makes the area safe and clear.

The service often includes rolling up the old carpet and padding. They bag these materials for disposal. Cleanup is another important part. Installers sweep or vacuum the subfloor after removal. This removes loose debris, dust, and any small staples. A clean subfloor is essential for installing new carpet. It prevents lumps and bumps. A clean surface also allows for proper adhesion.

However, some things are usually *not* included. Repairing a damaged subfloor is one example. If installers find mold or rot, they stop work. They will discuss repair options with you. Hauling away furniture is also often an extra charge. Most services require you to clear the room before they arrive. They focus only on the flooring material itself. Be sure to discuss all specific inclusions and exclusions with your installer. This clarity helps avoid misunderstandings.

Think of it as preparing a clean slate for your new floor. The removal service creates this clean slate. It ensures the space is ready for the new installation. Knowing these details helps you plan better. It ensures a smooth transition to your new flooring.

### Factors Affecting Carpet Removal Cost: What You Pay For

Paying for old carpet removal means understanding what drives the price. Several factors influence the final cost. These elements help installers determine the labor and resources needed. Knowing them helps you estimate your budget.

First, **the size of the area** plays a major role. Most companies charge per square foot or per room. Larger rooms or multiple areas mean more material to remove. This directly increases the total cost. Second, **the type of old carpet** matters. Some carpets are thicker or more firmly glued down. This makes them harder and slower to remove. Commercial carpet, for instance, often requires more effort.

Third, **the type of padding and installation method** affects the cost. Some padding is glued down very strongly. Removing this adhesive takes extra time and special tools. Tack strips might be heavily stapled. This also adds to the labor required. Fourth, **disposal fees** are a significant part of the price. Landfills charge to accept bulky items like old carpet. These charges vary by location. Your installer includes this fee in their quote.

Fifth, **accessibility of the space** can influence the cost. If the room is hard to access, or stairs are involved, labor increases. Installers might charge more for carrying heavy rolls up and down stairs. Lastly, **any additional prep work** adds to the cost. If you need help moving heavy furniture, expect an extra charge. Some installers might charge for extensive subfloor cleaning beyond basic sweeping.

Always get a detailed quote. Ask for a breakdown of costs. This helps you compare different services fairly. Understanding these factors empowers you to make an informed decision. It ensures you get good value for the removal service. This cost covers expertise, labor, and proper disposal.

### Preparing for Old Carpet Removal: Your Responsibilities

Even with professional help, you have tasks to do before the installers arrive. Your preparation helps the process go smoothly and efficiently. It ensures the team can start work right away. Good preparation also protects your belongings.

The most important step is **clearing the room**. Remove all furniture, electronics, and small items. This includes lamps, rugs, and decor. If you have large, heavy items, ask your installer if they offer furniture moving. Many charge extra for this service. Clearing the room allows installers full access to the carpet. It speeds up their work. When people get their carpet cleaned, they often [move furniture and clear the area](https://homeessentialsguide.com/what-do-people-do-when-their-carpet-is-being-cleaned) to allow for complete access. This same principle applies to carpet removal.

Next, **remove anything hanging on walls** that might be at risk. Pictures, mirrors, and delicate artwork should come down. Removing old carpet can create dust and vibrations. Protecting these items prevents accidental damage. Cover any remaining items with plastic sheeting. This protects them from dust during the removal.

Also, ensure a clear path for installers to bring in tools and take out old carpet. Make sure hallways and entryways are free of obstacles. Inform your installers about any specific concerns. For example, mention if there are delicate floors in other areas. Discuss any special disposal needs.

Your preparation saves time and money. It also helps the installers work more safely. A prepared space means a faster and cleaner job. It ensures the focus remains on flooring work. By handling these responsibilities, you contribute to a successful installation project. A little effort beforehand makes a big difference. This planning step ensures everything runs smoothly.

### DIY Carpet Removal vs. Professional Service: Making Your Choice

Deciding whether to remove old carpet yourself or hire professionals is a key decision. Both options have pros and cons. Your choice depends on your time, budget, and comfort with physical labor. I always consider these points before starting any home project.

**DIY carpet removal** offers significant cost savings. You avoid labor charges and disposal fees from installers. If you have the tools and time, it is a viable option. You will need a utility knife, pliers, a pry bar, and safety gear. The process involves cutting the carpet into strips. Then you pull up the carpet, padding, and tack strips. Disposal requires finding a local landfill or recycling center. This can be challenging for bulky materials. DIY removal is a physically demanding job. It can be messy and take a lot of time. You also risk damaging your subfloor if not careful.

**Hiring professional carpet removal services** provides convenience and expertise. Installers handle all aspects of removal and disposal. They work quickly and efficiently. They have the right tools for fast removal. This option saves you time and physical effort. It also ensures the subfloor is properly prepared for the new carpet. The main drawback is the added cost. This cost covers labor, tools, and responsible disposal.

Consider the amount of carpet to remove. A small room might be an easy DIY project. A whole house or multiple rooms make professional help more appealing. Think about your physical ability. Removing carpet involves kneeling, lifting, and pulling. Evaluate the condition of the old carpet. Heavily glued or stapled carpet is harder to remove yourself.

Ultimately, weigh the cost savings against the effort and time. For many homeowners, the convenience of professionals is worth the extra expense. For others, the satisfaction of saving money on a DIY project is appealing. Make the choice that best fits your situation.

### Disposing of Old Carpet: Environmental Considerations

Once old carpet is removed, the next step is disposal. This process has environmental impacts. Thinking about responsible disposal is important. Carpet is a bulky item and fills landfills quickly.

Many areas have specific rules for disposing of old carpet. Some landfills accept it directly. They often charge a fee based on weight or volume. It is wise to check local regulations before you plan disposal. Improper disposal can lead to fines. Recycling old carpet is a better environmental choice. However, carpet recycling programs are not available everywhere. They can also be limited by carpet type.

Carpet is made from various materials. These include nylon, polyester, and polypropylene. These materials can be difficult to break down. Some carpet recycling facilities break down old carpet. They turn it into new products. These products range from new carpet fibers to auto parts. Search for "carpet recycling" in your area. You might find a local drop-off center.

If you hire a professional service, they handle the disposal. This is a major benefit. They know local regulations. They often have established relationships with disposal sites. This ensures the carpet is removed legally and often more responsibly. Some companies even partner with recycling initiatives. Asking your installer about their disposal methods is a good idea. It lets you support greener practices.

Consider donating usable carpet if it is in good condition. Charities or local community groups might accept it. This gives the carpet a second life. It also keeps it out of the landfill. Reusing carpet is the most environmentally friendly option. However, most old carpet is removed because it is worn out. Responsible disposal is crucial for reducing waste.

### Subfloor Inspection and Preparation After Carpet Removal

After old carpet and padding come up, the subfloor is exposed. This is a critical moment in the new carpet installation process. A thorough subfloor inspection and proper preparation are essential. This ensures a flawless foundation for your new flooring.

First, **inspect the subfloor thoroughly**. Look for any signs of damage. This includes cracks, soft spots, or unevenness. Water damage, like stains or mold, needs immediate attention. These issues can affect the longevity of your new carpet. They can also indicate underlying problems in your home. Installers will typically point out major issues. However, a homeowner's awareness is also helpful.

Next, **address any necessary repairs**. Small cracks or holes in plywood subfloors can be patched. Loose floorboards need to be screwed down. Any protruding nails or staples must be removed or hammered flat. A smooth, even surface is crucial. Unevenness will show through the new carpet. It will cause premature wear in those areas. Mold or water damage requires professional remediation. Ignoring these issues can lead to health problems and damage to your new carpet.

Finally, **clean the subfloor completely**. After repairs, sweep or vacuum the entire area. Remove all dust, debris, and leftover staples. Any grit left behind can cause bumps under the new carpet. It can also reduce the lifespan of the new padding. A perfectly clean subfloor ensures the new padding lies flat. It also allows for proper adhesion of any required glues or tapes.

Professional installers take these steps seriously. They understand the importance of a well-prepared subfloor. This foundation impacts the look, feel, and durability of your new carpet. A good subfloor protects your investment. It ensures your new carpet installation is successful. This preparation prevents future problems.

## FAQ Section

### Q1: Is old carpet removal always included in new carpet installation?
No, old carpet removal is not always included in the base price for new carpet installation. It is typically an add-on service. Always confirm with your chosen carpet company. Get a detailed quote that clearly lists all services. This ensures you understand what you are paying for and avoids surprises on installation day.

### Q2: How much does it cost to have professionals remove old carpet?
The cost for professional carpet removal varies. It usually ranges from $0.75 to $2.00 per square foot. Factors like carpet type, padding, and local disposal fees influence the price. Complex removals or jobs involving stairs can increase costs. Always get a specific quote for your project.

### Q3: What should I do if I want to remove the carpet myself?
If you remove carpet yourself, you will need tools like a utility knife, pliers, and a pry bar. Wear gloves and a mask for safety. Cut the carpet into strips, then pull up the carpet, padding, and tack strips. You must then arrange for proper disposal at a local landfill or recycling center, often with a fee.

### Q4: Do carpet installers move furniture for old carpet removal?
Carpet installers might move furniture, but it is often an extra service with an additional charge. Most companies prefer that homeowners clear the room beforehand. Confirm their policy on furniture moving when you get your quote. Preparing the room yourself can save you money and time.

### Q5: What happens to the subfloor after old carpet is removed?
After old carpet removal, the subfloor is exposed and inspected. Installers look for damage, unevenness, or moisture issues. They usually remove old tack strips and clean the subfloor thoroughly. Any necessary repairs or extensive cleaning are typically discussed with you and may incur extra costs.

### Q6: Can old carpet be recycled?
Yes, some old carpet can be recycled, but options vary by location and carpet material. Many recycling centers accept certain types of carpet, breaking them down for new products. Check with your local waste management services or carpet recycling programs for availability. Professional installers may also have recycling partnerships.
