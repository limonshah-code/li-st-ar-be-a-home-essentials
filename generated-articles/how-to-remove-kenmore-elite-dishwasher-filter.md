## Easy Steps to Remove Your Kenmore Elite Dishwasher Filter

Do you often find your dishes are not as clean as they should be, even after a full wash cycle? A common culprit behind cloudy glassware and food residue is a clogged dishwasher filter. Specifically, if you own a Kenmore Elite dishwasher, its filter can accumulate food particles and grease over time. This build-up hinders proper water circulation and cleaning performance.

Learning how to remove Kenmore Elite dishwasher filter is a simple task that greatly improves your appliance's efficiency. Regular filter maintenance ensures your dishes come out sparkling clean every time. This guide will walk you through each step, making filter removal and cleaning easy for any homeowner. We will cover locating the filter, safely removing it, cleaning it, and putting it back.

### Takeaway

*   **Safety First:** Always unplug your Kenmore Elite dishwasher before starting.
*   **Locate the Filter:** Find the cylindrical filter assembly at the bottom center of the dishwasher.
*   **Twist and Lift:** Rotate the upper filter counter-clockwise to unlock, then lift it out.
*   **Separate Components:** Pull up the lower filter (if present) for complete access.
*   **Clean Thoroughly:** Rinse under running water, use a brush for stubborn debris.
*   **Reinstall Correctly:** Align, push down, and twist clockwise until locked.

### Clear, Concise Answer

To remove your Kenmore Elite dishwasher filter, first, open the dishwasher door and locate the cylindrical filter assembly at the bottom. Twist the upper filter counter-clockwise to unlock it, then lift it straight out. Next, separate the lower filter if your model has one, typically by pulling it up. This action exposes all parts for thorough cleaning.

## Why Your Kenmore Elite Dishwasher Filter Needs Cleaning

Many homeowners overlook the dishwasher filter, thinking their appliance is self-cleaning. However, your Kenmore Elite dishwasher, like most modern dishwashers, relies on a manual filter system. This filter catches food debris, preventing it from recirculating and redepositing on your dishes. Over time, this mesh screen and cylindrical filter assembly collects significant amounts of particles.

When the filter becomes clogged, water cannot flow freely through the spray arms. This reduces the cleaning power of your dishwasher, leading to dirty dishes, foul odors, and even potential pump strain. Regular filter cleaning is a simple maintenance task that extends the life of your appliance and ensures consistently clean dishes. You may also notice your dishwasher filter full of water if it is severely clogged. Knowing [how often should you clean your dishwasher filter](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-often-should-you-clean-your-dishwasher-filter) is key to maintaining optimal performance.

### Signs Your Filter Needs Attention

*   **Dishes are not clean:** You see food particles or a gritty film on items after a wash cycle.
*   **Unpleasant odors:** A stale or moldy smell comes from the dishwasher.
*   **Standing water:** Water remains in the bottom of the dishwasher after a cycle.
*   **Loud noises:** The dishwasher makes unusual grinding or whirring sounds.

Cleaning your Kenmore Elite dishwasher filter solves these problems efficiently. It also saves you from costly repairs down the road. This small step makes a big difference in your kitchen's cleanliness and your appliance's health.

## Locating the Filter in Your Kenmore Elite Dishwasher

Finding the filter in your Kenmore Elite dishwasher is the first crucial step in its removal and cleaning. Kenmore Elite dishwashers typically have their filter assembly located in the bottom of the wash tub. This placement is common across most modern dishwasher models. It ensures the filter effectively catches food debris before it can recirculate with the wash water.

Open your dishwasher door completely. Look down at the bottom of the dishwasher tub. You will see a cylindrical component, often with a handle or a ribbed top. This is the filter assembly. It usually sits near the center of the tub, directly below the lower spray arm. Some Kenmore Elite models might have a two-part filter system. This system includes an upper cylindrical filter and a flat, fine mesh lower filter. For specific details on where your filter is, you can check out our guide on [where is the filter in a Kenmore Elite dishwasher](https://homeessentialsguide.com/where-is-the-filter-in-a-kenmore-elite-dishwasher).

### Common Filter Configurations

*   **Cylindrical Filter:** Most Kenmore Elite models feature a primary cylindrical filter. This filter usually has a handle or tabs on top for easy gripping. It collects larger food particles.
*   **Fine Mesh Filter (Lower Filter):** Beneath the cylindrical filter, many models have a flat, fine mesh filter. This part catches smaller particles. It prevents them from clogging the spray arms or redepositing on dishes. You often need to remove the cylindrical filter first to access this lower mesh.
*   **Self-Cleaning vs. Manual Clean:** Remember that "self-cleaning" dishwashers often refer to older models with a hard food disposer. Modern Kenmore Elite dishwashers almost always have a manual-clean filter. This manual cleaning ensures better water quality for your dishes.

Once you have identified the filter components, you are ready to proceed with the removal process. Ensure you have good lighting to see all parts clearly.

## Step-by-Step Guide: Removing Your Kenmore Elite Dishwasher Filter

Now that you have located the filter and gathered your tools, it is time to remove your Kenmore Elite dishwasher filter. This process is straightforward, but it requires careful handling. Following these steps ensures you remove the filter without causing any damage. Remember, safety is always first. Always confirm the dishwasher is unplugged before you begin touching any internal components.

### 1. Unplug the Dishwasher

Before reaching into your dishwasher, cut off its power supply. Locate the power cord and unplug it from the wall outlet. If your dishwasher is hardwired, go to your home's main electrical panel and switch off the circuit breaker labeled for the dishwasher. This step protects you from electric shock. It also prevents the dishwasher from accidentally starting during filter removal.

### 2. Locate the Filter Assembly

Open the dishwasher door fully. Look at the bottom of the dishwasher tub. You will see the filter assembly, usually a round or cylindrical component. It is typically in the center, underneath the lower spray arm. Some models may have a small handle or an arrow indicating the unlock direction.

### 3. Remove the Upper Filter (Cylindrical Filter)

This is the main filter component. It looks like a plastic cylinder with a mesh screen.

*   **Twist Counter-Clockwise:** Grip the top of the cylindrical filter firmly. Turn it counter-clockwise. You will likely feel a slight click or resistance as it unlocks. This mechanism usually requires only a quarter turn to unlock.
*   **Lift Up:** Once unlocked, simply lift the cylindrical filter straight up and out of the dishwasher. Be careful, as it may contain some standing water or debris.

### 4. Remove the Lower Filter (Fine Mesh Plate)

Many Kenmore Elite dishwashers have a secondary, flat, fine mesh filter that sits beneath the cylindrical one.

*   **Lift the Plate:** After removing the cylindrical filter, you will see a flat, perforated plate at the bottom. This is the lower filter. Gently lift this plate straight up. It should come out easily.
*   **Handle with Care:** This mesh can be delicate. Avoid bending or forcing it.

Now both parts of your Kenmore Elite dishwasher filter assembly are removed. You are ready to clean them thoroughly. ## Thoroughly Cleaning Your Kenmore Elite Dishwasher Filter

Once you have successfully removed your Kenmore Elite dishwasher filter, the next step is to clean it thoroughly. A clean filter is crucial for optimal dishwasher performance and sparkling dishes. The filter traps food particles, grease, and other debris. Over time, these can harden and become stubborn. Effective cleaning removes these contaminants.

Cleaning your filter is not complicated, but it requires attention to detail. You need to remove all visible gunk and any invisible buildup that might cause odors or restrict water flow. I usually bring the filter components to my kitchen sink for this task. Running water and a small brush are your best friends here. You want to ensure every crevice is free of debris.

### Cleaning Steps

1.  **Rinse Under Running Water:** Hold the filter components under warm running water. Most loose food particles will wash away easily. Use your fingers to gently rub away any remaining soft debris.
2.  **Use a Brush for Stubborn Grime:** For stuck-on food or greasy residue, use a soft brush or an old toothbrush. Gently scrub the mesh screens and plastic frame of both the cylindrical and flat filters. Pay close attention to the crevices and the fine mesh, as these are common spots for buildup.
3.  **Apply Mild Dish Soap:** For extra stubborn grease or film, apply a small amount of mild dish soap to your brush. Scrub the filter components with the soapy brush. The soap helps break down oils and food residue. Rinse thoroughly under running water to remove all soap suds.
4.  **Check for Limescale:** If you live in an area with hard water, you might see white, chalky deposits. This is limescale. For removing limescale, you can soak the filter in a solution of equal parts white vinegar and warm water for about 15-30 minutes. Then, scrub and rinse. Learn more about [how to remove limescale from dishwasher filter](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-remove-limescale-from-dishwasher-filter) for best results.
5.  **Inspect and Dry:** After cleaning, hold the filter up to the light. Ensure there are no clogged holes or visible debris on the mesh. Shake off excess water, or gently pat the filter dry with a paper towel. It does not need to be perfectly dry before reinstallation, but removing most of the water helps.

A clean filter will look noticeably different from a dirty one. Its mesh should be clear, and there should be no lingering odors. This thorough cleaning ensures that your Kenmore Elite dishwasher can perform its best.

## Reinstalling the Kenmore Elite Dishwasher Filter Correctly

Once your Kenmore Elite dishwasher filter components are sparkling clean, the next critical step is to reinstall them correctly. Proper reinstallation ensures the filter functions as intended and prevents any issues during future wash cycles. An improperly installed filter can lead to poor cleaning performance, leaks, or even damage to your dishwasher. I always make sure to double-check my work here to avoid any problems.

The reinstallation process is essentially the reverse of removal. You will put the lower filter in first, followed by the upper cylindrical filter. It is important that each piece seats properly. Pay attention to any alignment arrows or notches during this step. Incorrect alignment can prevent the filter from locking securely or block water flow.

### Steps for Reinstallation

1.  **Insert the Lower Filter First:** If your Kenmore Elite model has a flat, fine mesh lower filter, place it back into the bottom of the dishwasher tub. It should sit flat and flush with the bottom. There might be a small indentation or guide to help it align correctly. Ensure it is fully seated before proceeding.
2.  **Place the Upper Filter:** Take the cylindrical upper filter. Align its bottom with the opening in the lower filter and the dishwasher tub. You may see arrows or notches that indicate the correct alignment. Gently push the filter down. It should slide smoothly into place.
3.  **Twist Clockwise to Lock:** Once the cylindrical filter is fully seated, twist it clockwise. You should feel it "click" into a locked position. This ensures the filter is secure and will not dislodge during a wash cycle. Most filters only require a quarter turn to lock. Confirm the handle or tabs are pointing in the locked direction, as indicated by arrows on the filter or tub.
4.  **Test for Security:** Give the filter a gentle tug or twist to ensure it is firmly in place. It should not wiggle or lift easily. A securely locked filter is vital for preventing food particles from bypassing the filtration system.
5.  **Restore Power:** Once you confirm the filter is properly reinstalled, plug your dishwasher back into the power outlet. If you turned off the circuit breaker, flip it back on. Your Kenmore Elite dishwasher is now ready for use with its freshly cleaned filter. For a more general guide on this process, you can refer to [how to put dishwasher filter back in](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-put-dishwasher-filter-back-in).

Regularly checking that your filter is properly seated after cleaning will prevent most common filter-related issues. This small detail ensures maximum efficiency and longevity for your Kenmore Elite appliance.

## Troubleshooting Common Kenmore Elite Filter Issues

Even with proper cleaning and reinstallation, you might encounter some minor issues with your Kenmore Elite dishwasher filter. Knowing how to troubleshoot these common problems saves you time and frustration. Most filter-related issues stem from improper cleaning or reinstallation. A proactive approach helps maintain your dishwasher's performance.

One common issue is the filter being difficult to remove. This often happens if the filter has not been cleaned in a long time. Dried food and mineral deposits can create a stubborn seal. Another issue is the filter not seating correctly upon reinstallation. This can lead to water bypass and dirty dishes. I always make sure to check for these issues during my routine maintenance.

### Issues and Solutions

*   **Filter is Difficult to Remove:**
    *   **Dried Debris:** If the filter is stuck, try gently wiggling it while twisting counter-clockwise. Avoid using excessive force, as this could break the plastic.
    *   **Limescale Buildup:** Hard water deposits can cause sticking. Pour a small amount of white vinegar around the base of the filter and let it sit for 15-30 minutes. The vinegar helps dissolve the mineral buildup, making it easier to twist and lift.
    *   **Check Manual:** Consult your specific Kenmore Elite dishwasher manual. Some models may have a unique locking mechanism.
*   **Filter Does Not Lock into Place:**
    *   **Misalignment:** The most common reason is improper alignment. Ensure the lower filter (if present) is perfectly flat and seated. Then, align the upper cylindrical filter correctly before pushing down and twisting clockwise. Look for arrows or notches.
    *   **Obstruction:** Check for any small food particles or debris preventing the filter from seating fully. Even a tiny piece of food can cause an issue.
    *   **Damaged Filter:** Inspect the filter for cracks or deformities. A damaged filter might not seat properly. If damaged, you might need a replacement part.
*   **Dishes Still Dirty After Cleaning Filter:**
    *   **Other Clogs:** The filter might not be the only problem. Check the spray arm nozzles for clogs. These small holes can get blocked by food particles or mineral deposits. Use a toothpick to clear them.
    *   **Water Temperature/Detergent:** Ensure your hot water heater is set to at least 120°F (49°C). Use fresh, quality dishwasher detergent. Old or poor-quality detergent is less effective.
    *   **Loading Issues:** Overloading the dishwasher or blocking the spray arms can cause poor cleaning. Ensure proper loading techniques.
    *   **Rinse Aid:** If dishes are spotty, you might need to add rinse aid. Kenmore Elite dishwashers benefit from rinse aid for streak-free drying. Find out [how to fill rinse aid in Kenmore Elite dishwasher](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-fill-rinse-aid-in-kenmore-elite-dishwasher).

Troubleshooting these issues helps keep your Kenmore Elite dishwasher running smoothly. Regular cleaning is the best preventive measure against most filter-related problems.

## Maintaining Optimal Dishwasher Performance

Removing and cleaning your Kenmore Elite dishwasher filter is a vital step for maintaining your appliance. However, optimal dishwasher performance requires a holistic approach. Beyond just the filter, several other factors contribute to how effectively your dishwasher cleans and how long it lasts. Integrating these practices into your routine ensures your Kenmore Elite works at its peak efficiency.

Think of your dishwasher as an essential partner in your kitchen. Just like any other appliance, it thrives on regular care and attention. I always make sure to give my dishwasher a bit of extra love beyond just filter cleaning. This includes checking other components and using the right products. These simple habits lead to spotless dishes and a longer appliance life.

### Beyond Filter Cleaning

*   **Clean the Spray Arms:** The upper and lower spray arms have small nozzles that can get clogged. Periodically remove the spray arms (they usually unscrew easily) and rinse them. Use a toothpick or small wire to clear any blocked holes. This ensures water sprays evenly and forcefully over your dishes.
*   **Wipe Down the Door Seal:** The rubber gasket around the dishwasher door can accumulate grime and mold. Wipe it down regularly with a damp cloth and mild soap. This prevents odors and ensures a tight seal during cycles.
*   **Run a Dishwasher Cleaning Cycle:** Once a month, run an empty dishwasher with a dishwasher cleaner or a cup of white vinegar placed in a top rack bowl. This helps remove grease buildup, mineral deposits, and odors from the interior and plumbing.
*   **Check Water Temperature:** Your dishwasher needs hot water to clean effectively. Ensure your hot water heater is set to at least 120°F (49°C). If the water is not hot enough, dishes may not get clean, and detergent may not dissolve properly.
*   **Use Proper Detergent and Rinse Aid:** Use high-quality dishwasher detergent. Avoid using too much or too little. If your dishes have water spots or film, use a rinse aid. Rinse aid helps water sheet off dishes, preventing spots and aiding drying.
*   **Proper Loading:** Do not overload your dishwasher. Ensure dishes do not block the spray arms or detergent dispenser. Air and water need to circulate freely for effective cleaning. Scrape off large food particles before loading, but do not pre-rinse excessively, as detergents need some food residue to activate.

By following these additional maintenance tips, you can greatly enhance the cleaning power and lifespan of your Kenmore Elite dishwasher. A well-maintained dishwasher is a happy dishwasher, and it will reward you with consistently sparkling dishes.

## FAQ Section

### How often should I clean my Kenmore Elite dishwasher filter?

You should clean your Kenmore Elite dishwasher filter at least once a month. If you frequently wash heavily soiled dishes or have a large household, cleaning it every two weeks is ideal. This regular maintenance prevents food buildup and ensures your dishwasher performs efficiently. Consistent cleaning also helps avoid odors and poor wash results.

### What happens if I don't clean my dishwasher filter?

If you do not clean your dishwasher filter, food particles and grease will accumulate. This clog prevents water from circulating properly, leading to dirty dishes, foul odors, and potentially a dishwasher full of water. The motor may also strain, reducing the appliance's lifespan. Ignoring the filter leads to poor cleaning performance and potential repair needs.

### Is it hard to remove a Kenmore Elite dishwasher filter?

No, removing a Kenmore Elite dishwasher filter is generally not hard. It usually involves twisting a cylindrical filter counter-clockwise and lifting it out. Some models also have a flat lower filter that lifts out easily. The process requires no special tools and is designed for quick, user-friendly maintenance.

### Can I put my Kenmore Elite dishwasher filter in the dishwasher?

No, you should not put your Kenmore Elite dishwasher filter in the dishwasher. The dishwasher's intense heat and strong spray can damage the delicate plastic and mesh of the filter. Always clean the filter by hand under warm running water with a soft brush and mild dish soap. This method ensures thorough cleaning without damage.

### Why is my Kenmore Elite dishwasher filter full of water?

Your Kenmore Elite dishwasher filter might be full of water because it is clogged. A clogged filter prevents water from draining properly, causing it to back up in the filter assembly. This issue also happens if the drain hose is kinked or the drain pump has a problem. Cleaning the filter often solves this issue.

### Do all Kenmore Elite dishwashers have a removable filter?

Most modern Kenmore Elite dishwashers, especially models from the last 15-20 years, feature a user-removable and manually cleanable filter. Older models sometimes had a "hard food disposer" that broke down food particles, effectively being self-cleaning. Always check your specific model's manual to confirm your filter type and location.
