You just bought a new dishwasher with a third rack. This extra tray feels like a bonus. ### Takeaway

*   **Utilize the third rack for small, flat, or oddly shaped items.** This includes silverware, spatulas, measuring spoons, and small lids.
*   **Arrange items with space between them.** This allows water and detergent to reach every surface. Avoid stacking or nesting.
*   **Place items securely.** Ensure they will not flip over during the wash cycle. Lying items flat generally works best.
*   **Consider your third rack design.** Different models handle various items better. Shallow racks suit flatware; deeper ones fit small bowls.
*   **Do not overload the third rack.** Overloading prevents proper cleaning and water circulation.
*   **Do not block spray arms.** Ensure tall items on the other racks do not prevent the third rack's spray from working.

### Clear Answer to Main Query

To load a third rack dishwasher, place small, flat, or shallow items like silverware, spatulas, measuring spoons, and small lids on the rack. Arrange them without stacking, allowing space for water circulation. Ensure items are secure and do not block the main spray arm below, maximizing cleaning efficiency and freeing up space in the lower racks.

### What Not to Load on the Third Rack

While the third rack is versatile, it has limits. Some items are not suitable for this space. Knowing what to avoid prevents damage and ensures effective cleaning. Not every item belongs on the top level.

Avoid placing tall items on the third rack. This includes tall serving spoons or ladles that stand upright. These items can block the upper spray arm. This prevents water from reaching the third rack itself. It also stops water from reaching items on the top rack. Always check that the spray arm spins freely after loading.

Do not load items that are too light or unstable. Very light plastic items might get tossed around. This can cause them to flip or fall. If they fall, they can block the drain or get caught in the spray arm. Always make sure items are secure. Delicate glass items, like wine glasses, also do not belong here. They are better suited for the top rack's prongs. ### FAQ Section

**Q1: Can I wash knives on the third rack?**
Yes, you can wash knives on the third rack. Lay them flat with the sharp edge pointing towards the center of the dishwasher. This position helps prevent accidental cuts when unloading. It also ensures the blade gets a proper wash without damaging other items. Make sure they are secure and do not shift during the wash.

**Q2: Is it okay to put plastic items on the third rack?**
Yes, many plastic items are safe for the third rack. This rack is further from the heating element at the bottom. This reduces the risk of melting or warping. Place small plastic lids, measuring spoons, or baby bottle parts here. Always check if your specific plastic items are labeled "dishwasher safe."

**Q3: How often should I clean my third rack?**
You should clean your third rack as part of your regular dishwasher maintenance. Wipe it down if you see food residue. Periodically, remove it and give it a thorough wash. This prevents buildup and ensures proper hygiene. A clean rack helps keep your dishes clean.

**Q4: My third rack doesn't get items clean; what am I doing wrong?**
If your third rack items are not clean, check a few things. You might be overloading it, blocking water spray. Ensure items are not stacked. Also, check that no tall items on the top rack block the third rack's spray arm. Lastly, heavily soiled items might need a pre-rinse or a stronger spray from the lower racks.

**Q5: Can I remove the third rack?**
Most third racks are designed to be removable. This feature is useful if you need extra space for very tall items on your top rack. Check your dishwasher's user manual for specific instructions on how to remove and reinsert it. Removing it can provide temporary flexibility for large loads.

**Q6: What is the best way to load small baby items?**
The third rack is excellent for small baby items like bottle nipples, rings, and small pacifiers. Lay them flat and separate. This ensures direct water contact and good cleaning. Avoid stacking them. If your third rack has deeper sections, small bottle parts or medicine dispensers also fit well.
