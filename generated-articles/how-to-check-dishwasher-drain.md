## How To Check Dishwasher Drain: A Simple Guide

Picture this: You open your dishwasher after a cycle, ready for sparkling dishes, but instead, you find a pool of dirty water at the bottom. This is a common and frustrating problem for many homeowners. A dishwasher that does not drain properly disrupts your routine. It can also leave your dishes unclean.

I know how annoying this issue can be. You do not need to call a professional right away. Often, you can find and fix the cause yourself. This guide shows you exactly how to check your dishwasher drain. We will cover common reasons for drainage failure. You will learn to perform simple troubleshooting steps.

### Takeaway

*   **Always disconnect power:** Ensure safety before touching any parts.
*   **Start with simple checks:** Inspect the filter, drain screen, and food trap first.
*   **Look for kinks:** Check the drain hose for bends or blockages.
*   **Clear the air gap:** Ensure the air gap is free from debris.
*   **Check the disposal:** Run your garbage disposal if you have one.
*   **Assess the pump:** Listen for sounds from the drain pump.

### A Quick Answer to How To Check Dishwasher Drain

To check your dishwasher drain, first turn off power to the unit. Then, inspect the filter for food debris. Next, examine the drain hose for kinks or clogs. Also, check the air gap and garbage disposal. These steps help identify and fix most common drainage problems.

## Preparing for Your Dishwasher Drain Check

Before you begin to check your dishwasher drain, safety is key. Working with appliances requires caution. Water and electricity are a dangerous mix. Always turn off power to the dishwasher first.

You can usually do this at your home's circuit breaker box. Find the breaker labeled for your kitchen or dishwasher. Flip it to the "off" position. This prevents accidental shocks. It also protects the appliance from damage.

Next, you need to clear the area around the dishwasher. Pull out the bottom rack. Remove any standing water from the tub. You can use towels or a wet vacuum for this. Be prepared for some mess.

Gather your tools. You will likely need a few basic items. A flashlight helps you see into dark spaces. Screwdrivers (Phillips and flathead) are useful for removing covers. Have some old towels or a bucket ready. These catch any remaining water. Having the right tools makes the process smoother.

*   **Safety First:**
    *   Locate your home's circuit breaker box.
    *   Turn off the circuit breaker that controls your dishwasher.
    *   Verify the power is off by trying to start the dishwasher.

*   **Clear the Workspace:**
    *   Remove all dishes and racks from inside the dishwasher.
    *   Use a towel or sponge to remove any standing water from the bottom of the tub.
    *   Place old towels on the floor around the dishwasher. This protects your flooring from spills.

*   **Gather Your Tools:**
    *   Flashlight: For seeing into dark areas.
    *   Screwdriver set: Both Phillips and flathead are usually needed.
    *   Pliers: Useful for hose clamps or stubborn fittings.
    *   Bucket or shallow pan: To catch water when disconnecting hoses.
    *   Gloves: Protect your hands from grime.

This preparation ensures you work safely and efficiently. You will have everything ready to tackle the drainage problem. This saves time and frustration during the checking process. My own experience shows preparation makes any DIY task easier.

## Inspecting the Dishwasher Filter and Drain Area

The most common reason a dishwasher does not drain is a clogged filter or drain area. This is also the easiest part to check. Food particles, grease, and even small pieces of broken dishes can build up here. This prevents water from flowing out. I always start my checks here.

Most dishwashers have a removable filter assembly at the bottom. It is usually located under the lower spray arm. Some models have a self-cleaning filter. Others require manual cleaning. Consult your owner's manual if you are unsure about your specific model.

To check the filter:
1.  **Remove the lower dish rack.** This gives you clear access.
2.  **Locate the filter.** It often looks like a cylinder or a flat screen.
3.  **Twist or lift the filter to remove it.** Some models may have a small clip or lever.
4.  **Inspect the filter for debris.** Look for food scraps, glass shards, paper, or other foreign objects. These block water flow.
5.  **Clean the filter thoroughly.** Rinse it under running water. Use a brush to scrub away stubborn grime and grease. You can also use a mild dish soap.
6.  **Check the drain screen.** This is often a flat mesh screen under or around the main filter. Clear any visible debris from this screen.
7.  **Inspect the drain area itself.** Once the filter is out, shine a flashlight into the opening. Look for anything blocking the opening to the drain pump. Sometimes, a forgotten utensil or a small toy can fall in there.

A dirty filter severely impacts performance. It slows draining and leaves dishes dirty. Cleaning it regularly prevents many problems. Many people forget this simple maintenance step. This quick check can often solve your drainage issue completely. ## Examining the Dishwasher Drain Hose

If your filter is clean, the next step is to check the drain hose. This hose carries the dirty water from your dishwasher to your sink drain or garbage disposal. It can easily become kinked or clogged. A kink in the hose stops water flow completely. A clog reduces water flow.

First, locate the drain hose. It typically connects to the back of your dishwasher. From there, it usually runs under the sink. It connects to either the garbage disposal or a dedicated drain stub on the sink drainpipe. Sometimes, the hose might be hidden behind a cabinet panel.

To check the hose:
1.  **Visually inspect the entire length of the hose.** Look for any sharp bends or kinks. These often happen if the dishwasher was moved or pushed back incorrectly. Straighten any kinks you find.
2.  **Check for blockages.** Even without a kink, the hose can get clogged. Grease, food particles, and mineral deposits build up inside. You may need to disconnect the hose to properly inspect it.
3.  **Disconnect the hose.** Place a bucket or towels underneath the connection points. Water will likely spill out. Use pliers to loosen hose clamps. Gently pull the hose free from both ends.
4.  **Inspect the hose opening.** Look into both ends of the hose. Check for any visible debris.
5.  **Clear the blockage.** If you see a clog, use a straightened coat hanger or a long brush. Push through the hose to dislodge the blockage. You can also flush the hose with water from a garden hose or faucet. Make sure the water flows freely through it.
6.  **Reconnect the hose securely.** Ensure all clamps are tight. Check for leaks by running a small amount of water through the sink.

A clear drain hose is essential for proper drainage. This step can often resolve the "dishwasher not draining" issue. Remember, patience is key when dealing with hoses under cabinets. Sometimes the hardest part is reaching them. ## Troubleshooting the Air Gap and Sink Drain

The air gap and sink drain are critical components in the dishwasher's drainage system. They work together to prevent dirty water from flowing back into the dishwasher. Issues with either of these can cause drainage problems. Many people overlook these parts when troubleshooting.

### Checking the Dishwasher Air Gap

If your dishwasher uses an air gap, it is a small, cylindrical fitting on top of your sink or countertop. It usually has a cap that covers it. The air gap's purpose is to prevent contaminated sink water from entering the dishwasher. This happens if the sink drain is clogged.

To check the air gap:
1.  **Remove the air gap cover.** It usually twists off or simply lifts up.
2.  **Inspect inside for debris.** Look for food particles, grease, or grime. These can block the air gap.
3.  **Clean the air gap.** Use a pipe cleaner, small brush, or even a toothpick. Clear any blockages you find.
4.  **Reattach the cover.**

Sometimes, the hoses connected to the air gap can also clog. If cleaning the air gap itself does not help, you might need to disconnect the hoses. Clear them of any blockages. This can be a bit more involved.

### Inspecting the Sink Drain and Garbage Disposal

The dishwasher drains into your main sink drain. If your sink drain is clogged, your dishwasher cannot drain either. This is especially true if your dishwasher drains into a garbage disposal.

To check your sink drain:
1.  **Run water in the sink.** See if the sink drains slowly or stands still. This indicates a clog in the main sink line.
2.  **If you have a garbage disposal, run it.** Make sure it is completely empty before running a dishwasher cycle. Food debris in the disposal can block the dishwasher drain. Run the disposal with cold water for at least 30 seconds.
3.  **Check the disposal knockout plug.** This applies only if you just installed a new garbage disposal. New disposals come with a knockout plug inside the dishwasher inlet. This plug must be removed before connecting the dishwasher drain hose. If it is still there, your dishwasher will not drain at all.
4.  **Clear sink clogs.** If your sink drain is clogged, use a plunger or a drain snake to clear it. Do not use chemical drain cleaners. They can damage dishwasher components.

Ensuring your sink drain and garbage disposal are clear is a vital step. A perfectly functioning dishwasher drain hose means nothing if the water has nowhere to go. This simple check often reveals the hidden cause of your problem.

## Assessing the Dishwasher Drain Pump

If you have checked everything else and your dishwasher still does not drain, the issue might be with the drain pump. The drain pump is responsible for forcefully expelling water from the dishwasher tub. If it fails, water remains stagnant. This is a more involved check.

First, listen to your dishwasher during its drain cycle.
*   **Do you hear a humming sound, but no water movement?** This suggests the pump is trying to work but cannot. It might be jammed by debris.
*   **Do you hear no sound at all during the drain cycle?** This could mean the pump is not receiving power or has failed completely.

Accessing the drain pump often requires pulling the dishwasher out from its cabinet. Some models allow access from the front kick plate. Others require the entire unit to be moved. Always refer to your dishwasher's service manual for specific instructions. Every brand has slightly different access points.

General steps to assess the pump:
1.  **Ensure power is off.** Double-check the circuit breaker.
2.  **Disconnect the water supply line and drain hose.** Have towels ready.
3.  **Carefully pull the dishwasher out.** You might need to tilt it.
4.  **Locate the drain pump.** It is usually at the bottom, near the motor.
5.  **Inspect the pump impeller.** This is the part that spins to push water. Debris like glass, bones, or plastic can jam it. Use needle-nose pliers or tweezers to remove any obstructions.
6.  **Test for power (optional, for experts).** If the pump makes no sound, you can use a multimeter to check for voltage at the pump motor. This confirms if the pump is receiving power. If it gets power but does not spin, the pump itself is likely faulty.
7.  **Consider replacement.** If the pump impeller is clear and it receives power but does not work, the pump needs replacement. This task is more complex. It might be best for a professional.

A faulty drain pump is a common cause of persistent drainage issues. Knowing how to check for issues can help you decide if you need a new part. ## FAQ Section

### Why is my dishwasher not draining completely?

Your dishwasher may not drain completely for several reasons. The most common cause is a clogged filter or drain screen. Other causes include a kinked or blocked drain hose, a clogged air gap, or an issue with your garbage disposal. A failing drain pump can also be the culprit. Start by checking the filter first.

### Can I use chemical drain cleaner in my dishwasher?

No, you should not use chemical drain cleaner in your dishwasher. These harsh chemicals can corrode internal plastic and rubber components. They can also damage the drain pump. It is always better to manually clear clogs. Use a brush, pliers, or a snake for safety.

### How often should I clean my dishwasher drain filter?

You should clean your dishwasher drain filter regularly. For typical use, cleaning it once a month is a good practice. If you use your dishwasher frequently, or wash many dishes with heavy food scraps, clean the filter every two weeks. Regular cleaning prevents build-up and drainage issues.

### What causes a dishwasher to not drain?

A dishwasher fails to drain due to blockages or mechanical issues. Clogs in the filter, drain hose, air gap, or garbage disposal are primary reasons. A malfunctioning drain pump or problems with the control board can also prevent proper drainage. Always inspect for physical obstructions first.

### How do I know if my dishwasher drain pump is bad?

You can identify a bad dishwasher drain pump by specific symptoms. If you hear a humming noise during the drain cycle but no water is moving, the pump impeller might be jammed. If you hear no sound at all, the pump might not be receiving power or has failed completely. A faulty pump often means water stands in the tub.
