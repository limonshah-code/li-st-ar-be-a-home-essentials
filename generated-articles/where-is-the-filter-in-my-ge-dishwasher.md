## Types of GE Dishwasher Filters

GE dishwashers typically come with two main types of filter systems. Understanding which type your model has is important for proper maintenance. The first type is the manual-clean filter system. These are common in many modern, quieter GE dishwashers. This filter needs regular removal and hand washing.

A manual-clean filter often consists of a two-part assembly. There is a coarse filter and a fine mesh filter. The coarse filter catches larger food particles. The fine mesh filter then stops smaller debris. These filters are designed for quiet operation because they do not have a loud food grinder. However, they do require user intervention for cleaning.

The second type is the self-cleaning filter system, also known as a hard food disposer. These systems are found in some older or specific GE models. They feature a built-in grinder that pulverizes food particles. This allows them to wash down the drain. While these filters require less direct manual cleaning, they still have screens.

Even self-cleaning systems benefit from occasional checks. You should look for any trapped large debris that the disposer missed. The screens in these systems can still become clogged with grease or mineral deposits. This can impact performance. ## Removing the GE Dishwasher Filter

Removing the filter from your GE dishwasher is generally a simple process. First, ensure your dishwasher is empty of dishes. Then, pull out the lower dish rack completely to get easy access. The filter assembly sits on the floor of the wash tub, usually in the center.

Most GE manual-clean filters are a two-piece design. There is an upper filter and a lower filter screen. To remove the upper filter, you will typically find a handle or a small arrow. This arrow indicates the "unlock" position. Turn the upper filter counter-clockwise about a quarter turn until it loosens.

Once the upper filter is loose, you can gently lift it out of the dishwasher. Below it, you will often find the fine mesh filter screen. This screen usually has a designated notch or slot. Lift it straight up and out of its housing. Take care not to spill any trapped debris back into the tub.

If your GE model has a different design, consult your owner's manual. Some filters might simply pull up. Others might require a slight twist and then a lift. Always be gentle when removing parts to avoid damage. This process prepares the filter for cleaning.

It is helpful to remember how the parts fit together as you remove them. This makes reinstallation easier later. Proper removal ensures you can clean the filter effectively. ## Cleaning Your GE Dishwasher Filter for Peak Performance

Cleaning your GE dishwasher filter is a vital step for optimal performance. Once you remove the filter assembly, take it to your sink. You will need a soft brush, warm water, and a little dish soap. Avoid harsh chemicals or abrasive scrubbers, which can damage the filter material.

First, rinse the coarse filter under warm running water. Use the soft brush to gently scrub away any food particles or grease. Pay close attention to the mesh openings. Ensure all debris washes away. Sometimes, stubborn particles may require a bit more scrubbing.

Next, address the fine mesh filter screen. This part often collects smaller bits of food and grease. Rinse it thoroughly under warm water. Use your soft brush to clean both sides of the screen. Hold it up to the light to confirm all holes are clear. If water flows freely through it, it is clean.

For older or more stubborn grime, you can soak the filter parts in warm, soapy water for a few minutes. This helps loosen dried-on food. After soaking, repeat the scrubbing and rinsing process. A clean filter prevents odors and ensures spotless dishes. ## Reinstalling the GE Dishwasher Filter Correctly

After cleaning your GE dishwasher filter, correct reinstallation is important. This prevents leaks and ensures your dishwasher cleans properly. Start by making sure all filter parts are completely dry. This helps prevent any immediate mold or mildew growth.

Place the fine mesh screen back into its designated slot at the bottom of the wash tub. Make sure it sits flat and is securely in place. It should align with the filter housing. Do not force it; it should slide in smoothly. Improper seating can cause water to bypass the filter.

Next, position the coarse or upper filter assembly over the mesh screen. Align the notches or arrows on the upper filter with the corresponding slots on the dishwasher floor. Gently push down. Then, turn the upper filter clockwise until it locks into place. You should feel it click or stop firmly.

Many GE filters have an arrow that points to a "lock" symbol when correctly installed. Always check for this visual cue. A loosely installed filter can cause water to leak past it, leading to poor cleaning. It can also cause food particles to recirculate onto your dishes. This would defeat the purpose of cleaning the filter.

If you have trouble, refer to your GE owner's manual for specific diagrams. ## FAQ Section

### Why does my GE dishwasher have a filter?

Your GE dishwasher has a filter to trap food particles and debris. This stops them from recirculating onto your dishes during a wash cycle. The filter ensures cleaner dishes and prevents clogs in the drain pump. It also helps your dishwasher run more efficiently and quietly.

### What happens if I do not clean the GE dishwasher filter?

If you do not clean your GE dishwasher filter, food particles will build up. This can cause poor cleaning performance, leaving residue on your dishes. It can also lead to unpleasant odors and potential drainage issues. A clogged filter puts strain on the dishwasher's pump.

### Can I put my GE dishwasher filter in the dishwasher for cleaning?

No, you should not put your GE dishwasher filter in the dishwasher for cleaning. The dishwasher's intense cycle will not effectively clean the filter. Instead, food debris might get stuck further. Always hand wash the filter under warm water with a soft brush and dish soap.

### How often should I check my GE dishwasher filter?

You should check your GE dishwasher filter at least once a month. If you use your dishwasher frequently or wash heavily soiled dishes, check it every two to three weeks. Regular checks prevent excessive buildup and ensure optimal cleaning performance.

### Are all GE dishwasher filters the same?

No, not all GE dishwasher filters are the same. GE uses different filter designs depending on the model. Some have a manual-clean, two-part filter assembly. Others might have a self-cleaning hard food disposer system with internal screens. Always refer to your specific model's manual.

### My GE dishwasher smells bad, is it the filter?

Yes, a bad smell in your GE dishwasher often points to a dirty filter. Trapped food particles and grease can decompose on the filter. This creates foul odors inside the appliance. Cleaning the filter is usually the first step to eliminating these smells and restoring freshness.
