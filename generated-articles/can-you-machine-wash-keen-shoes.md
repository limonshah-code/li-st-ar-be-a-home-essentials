### Can You Machine Wash Keen Shoes? Here's the Short Answer:

Most Keen shoes, especially those made from washable polyester webbing or synthetic materials, *can* be machine washed safely. However, models with leather or suede components require hand washing to prevent damage. Always check the specific care instructions for your Keen pair before you begin.

## Understanding Keen Footwear Materials and Washability

Keen makes a variety of shoes designed for outdoor use. They use different materials to create their durable and comfortable footwear. Knowing what your Keen shoes are made of is the first step in deciding how to clean them. This knowledge helps you avoid damaging your beloved pair during the washing process. I always check my shoe's material before starting any cleaning process.

Many popular Keen sandals and hikers feature washable polyester webbing. This material is robust and handles water well, making it ideal for machine washing. It resists shrinking and holds its shape after getting wet. Keen also uses synthetic meshes and rubber outsoles in many designs. These materials are generally very durable and can tolerate a washing machine cycle. They are built for outdoor conditions, including dirt and moisture.

However, some Keen models include leather or suede. These natural materials react differently to water and machine washing. Leather can stiffen, crack, or lose its natural oils when submerged in water or agitated heavily. Suede can become permanently matted, discolored, or lose its soft texture. If your Keen shoes have significant leather or suede parts, a machine wash is not the right choice. Instead, you need to use specific hand-washing methods designed for delicate materials. Always check the internal tag or Keen's website for specific care instructions for your model. Understanding these differences protects your investment and keeps your shoes looking great.

## When Machine Washing Keen Shoes is a Good Idea

Machine washing your Keen shoes is a great idea in several situations. My shoes often get really dirty after a long hike or a day at the beach. When shoes accumulate a lot of grime, hand washing might not be enough. A washing machine can provide a thorough clean that restores them.

One common reason is heavy dirt and mud. If your Keen shoes are caked in mud from a trail, a machine wash can tackle this mess effectively. The agitation helps dislodge stubborn dirt particles from the fabric webbing and crevices. Trying to scrub all that mud by hand can be time-consuming and less effective. Another major reason is persistent odors. Feet sweat, and shoes can start to smell over time, especially after repeated use without proper airing. Odors can be tough to remove with just spot cleaning. A full machine wash, especially with a good detergent, can help eliminate those unwanted smells and leave your shoes fresh.

General grime and stains from everyday wear also make machine washing a good option. Think about grass stains, dust, or spilled drinks on your shoes. While some small spots might be hand-cleaned, widespread dirt benefits from a more comprehensive wash. For my washable Keen sandals, after a season of use, they often need a full refresh. A machine wash revives them, making them feel cleaner and more comfortable for future use. It is important to know [how to wash shoes in the washing machine](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-wash-shoes-in-washing-machine) correctly to prevent damage. This method is particularly useful for Keen shoes made primarily of synthetic or polyester materials. It ensures they get a deep clean without compromising their structure.

## Step-by-Step Guide: Preparing Keen Shoes for the Washing Machine

Proper preparation is essential before you machine wash Keen shoes. Rushing this step can lead to a less effective clean or even damage. I always take my time to prepare my shoes correctly. This ensures they come out as clean as possible.

First, remove any loose dirt and debris. Take your Keen shoes outside and vigorously clap the soles together. This helps knock off any caked-on mud or larger pieces of dirt. You can also use a soft-bristled brush or an old toothbrush to gently scrub away surface grime. Pay attention to the tread of the sole where dirt often accumulates. Removing this excess dirt prevents it from clogging your washing machine filter. It also helps your shoes get cleaner during the wash cycle.

Next, remove the insoles and laces from your Keen shoes. The insoles can trap moisture and harbor odors. Washing them separately allows for a more thorough clean. You can wash insoles by hand with soap and water, or place them in the mesh bag with your shoes. Laces can tangle around other items or get caught in the machine. It is best to wash them separately in a mesh bag or by hand. Placing your Keen shoes inside a [mesh laundry bag](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-machine-wash-shoes) is a crucial step. This bag protects your shoes from banging against the washing machine drum. It also keeps them from catching on other items in the load. The mesh bag helps maintain their shape and prevents potential damage to the shoe or the machine.

## The Best Washing Machine Settings for Keen Shoes

Choosing the right washing machine settings is critical when you machine wash Keen shoes. Incorrect settings can cause shrinking, damage to materials, or excessive wear. I always follow these guidelines to protect my footwear. Your goal is a gentle yet effective clean.

Always select a gentle or delicate cycle for your Keen shoes. These cycles use less agitation and slower spin speeds. This reduces the stress on the shoe's materials and stitching. A harsh cycle can cause the shoes to lose their shape or components to come apart. Some washing machines might have a "hand wash" or "bulky items" setting, which can also work well. It is also important to use cold water. Hot water can cause certain materials to shrink or the glues used in shoe construction to weaken. Cold water effectively cleans without posing these risks. It also helps prevent colors from fading.

For detergent, use a small amount of a mild liquid detergent. Avoid harsh chemicals, bleach, or powder detergents. Powder detergents can sometimes leave residues on fabric, especially in cold water. About one-quarter to one-half of the recommended amount for a regular load is usually sufficient. You can also add a cup of white vinegar to the wash cycle. Vinegar is a natural deodorizer and can help eliminate tough odors from your shoes. When loading your washing machine, always include a few old towels. The towels help balance the load and prevent the shoes from banging too loudly against the drum. They also provide extra cushioning, further protecting your Keen shoes during the wash. Knowing [how long to wash shoes in washing machine](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-long-to-wash-shoes-in-washing-machine) ensures they get clean without over-agitating.

## Drying Your Keen Shoes After Washing

Drying your Keen shoes correctly after washing is just as important as the washing process itself. Improper drying can lead to damage, shrinking, or lingering odors. I always emphasize air drying to ensure my shoes maintain their shape and integrity. This step needs patience but it saves your shoes in the long run.

The most important rule is to *never* put your Keen shoes in a clothes dryer. High heat from a dryer can cause significant damage. It can melt glues, shrink materials, crack rubber, or distort the shoe's shape. This damage is often irreversible. The intense heat can also degrade the performance and durability of Keen's outdoor-ready materials. My advice is to avoid the dryer at all costs.

Instead, always air dry your Keen shoes. Find a well-ventilated area indoors or outdoors away from direct sunlight. Direct sunlight can also be too harsh, potentially fading colors or causing materials to dry out too quickly. If drying indoors, ensure good air circulation. You can place the shoes on a drying rack or hang them using clothespins. To help speed up the drying process and absorb internal moisture, you can stuff your shoes with crumpled newspaper or paper towels. Change the paper regularly as it becomes damp. This method also helps the shoes keep their shape as they dry. Allow at least 24-48 hours for your Keen shoes to dry completely, depending on humidity levels. Make sure they are thoroughly dry before wearing them again. This prevents mildew and odors from developing.

## Keen Shoes Not Suitable for Machine Washing (And How to Clean Them)

Not all Keen shoes are designed for the washing machine. Some materials require a different, more gentle approach. It is vital to identify these types of shoes to avoid irreversible damage. I learned this the hard way with a favorite pair once.

Keen models featuring leather or suede components should never go into the washing machine. Leather, when subjected to excessive water and agitation, can lose its natural oils. This causes it to become stiff, brittle, and prone to cracking. Suede is even more delicate; machine washing can permanently mat its texture, cause discoloration, and ruin its soft feel. [Can you machine wash leather shoes](https://homeessentialsguide.com/can-you-machine-wash-leather-shoes)? No, not if you want them to last. Likewise, [can you machine wash suede shoes](https://homeessentialsguide.com/can-i-machine-wash-suede-shoes)? Absolutely not. These materials need specialized care to maintain their appearance and durability.

For leather Keen shoes, start by brushing off loose dirt with a soft brush. Then, wipe the surface with a damp cloth and a small amount of mild soap designed for leather. Use minimal water to avoid saturating the material. After cleaning, wipe away soap residue and allow the shoes to air dry naturally. Once dry, apply a leather conditioner to restore moisture and flexibility. For suede Keen shoes, use a suede brush to gently remove dirt and scuff marks. For tougher stains, use a suede eraser or a small amount of white vinegar on a clean cloth. Dab the stain rather than rubbing. Always allow suede to air dry completely. After drying, re-brush the nap to restore its texture. Always prioritize hand cleaning for these delicate materials to preserve your Keen shoes.

## Maintaining Your Keen Shoes Between Washes

Keeping your Keen shoes clean between deep washes extends their life and keeps them looking good. I use a few simple habits to maintain my footwear. These small actions make a big difference. They help avoid the need for frequent machine washing.

After each wear, especially if you have been outdoors, take a moment to clean off any obvious dirt. A quick brush with a stiff-bristled brush can remove loose soil and debris from the soles and uppers. This prevents dirt from becoming caked on and harder to remove later. For muddy adventures, let the mud dry completely before brushing it off. This makes removal much easier. You can also wipe down the rubber outsoles and toe bumpers with a damp cloth. This helps keep the protective features of your Keen shoes clean.

Odor control is another important aspect of maintenance. Even if your shoes aren't visibly dirty, odors can build up. After wearing, remove the insoles from your Keen shoes and let them air out separately. This allows both the insoles and the shoe interior to dry completely. Moisture is a breeding ground for odor-causing bacteria. You can also sprinkle a little baking soda inside your shoes overnight to absorb smells. Just shake it out in the morning. For synthetic Keen shoes, a quick rinse with plain water after a really dirty outing can sometimes be enough. Just make sure to dry them thoroughly afterward. Regular spot cleaning for small marks with a damp cloth and mild soap can also keep your shoes presentable. These simple practices significantly reduce how often you need to machine wash your Keen shoes, making them last longer.

## FAQ Section

### Is it safe to machine wash all types of Keen shoes?

No, not all Keen shoes are suitable for machine washing. While many synthetic and webbing models are washable, Keen shoes with leather or suede components should never be machine washed. These materials can be damaged by water and agitation. Always check the specific care instructions for your model.

### Can I use regular laundry detergent to machine wash my Keen shoes?

Yes, you can use a small amount of mild liquid laundry detergent. Avoid harsh chemicals, bleach, or powder detergents, which can leave residue or damage materials. About one-quarter to one-half of the amount recommended for a regular load is usually sufficient.

### How do I prevent my Keen shoes from getting damaged in the washing machine?

To prevent damage, always place your Keen shoes in a mesh laundry bag. Use a gentle or delicate cycle with cold water. Include a few old towels in the load to balance the machine and provide cushioning. Remove insoles and laces before washing.

### How long do Keen shoes take to air dry after washing?

Keen shoes typically take 24-48 hours to air dry completely. This depends on humidity levels and air circulation. Stuffing them with crumpled newspaper or paper towels can help absorb moisture and speed up the process. Ensure they are fully dry before wearing.

### Can I put my Keen insoles in the washing machine?

Yes, you can usually machine wash Keen insoles. Place them in a mesh laundry bag along with your shoes. Alternatively, you can hand wash them separately with mild soap and water. Always air dry them completely before putting them back into your shoes.

### What should I do if my Keen shoes still smell after washing?

If your Keen shoes still smell after washing, ensure they are thoroughly dry. You can try another wash cycle, adding a cup of white vinegar as a natural deodorizer. Alternatively, try sprinkling baking soda inside the dry shoes overnight to absorb lingering odors.
