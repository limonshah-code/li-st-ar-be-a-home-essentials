### Understanding Tervis Tumbler Construction: Plastic vs. Stainless Steel

Tervis tumblers are famous for their insulation. This ability comes from their design. The company makes tumblers from different materials. Each material needs specific care. Knowing the construction helps you clean them correctly.

Plastic Tervis tumblers feature a double-wall design. Two layers of durable plastic create a vacuum. This vacuum provides the insulation. Between these two walls, designs or inserts often sit. The sealed nature of these walls protects the inner contents. This design is robust, but it has limits. Extreme heat can affect the seal. It can also warp the plastic. Most of these plastic tumblers are strong and resist breaking. They handle daily use very well. They are popular for their personalization options.

Stainless steel Tervis tumblers use a different insulation method. They have a vacuum-sealed space between two layers of steel. This creates a powerful thermal barrier. Stainless steel is known for its strength and sleek look. It keeps drinks hot or cold for many hours. However, the vacuum seal on stainless steel is sensitive to heat. High temperatures can cause the seal to break. This breakage reduces the tumbler's insulating power. The exterior finish can also suffer damage. Understanding these differences guides your cleaning choices.

### The Verdict: Can Plastic Tervis Go In The Dishwasher?

Many Tervis users ask about washing plastic tumblers. Good news: most plastic Tervis tumblers are dishwasher safe. This applies to their iconic double-wall insulated designs. You can place them in your automatic dishwasher. This makes cleaning easy and convenient.

However, there is a crucial condition. You must place these tumblers on the top rack. The top rack of a dishwasher uses less intense heat. The heating element in a dishwasher is usually at the bottom. The bottom rack gets much hotter. High heat can damage the plastic. It can warp the tumbler shape. It can also compromise the insulation seal. This can cause condensation between the layers. Always load your plastic Tervis cups carefully. Ensure they do not block spray arms. This ensures proper cleaning and avoids damage. Following this simple rule helps your Tervis last longer.

### Are Stainless Steel Tervis Tumblers Dishwasher Safe?

The answer for stainless steel Tervis tumblers differs from plastic ones. Stainless steel Tervis products are *not* designed for dishwasher use. This is an important distinction. The primary reason relates to their insulation method. These tumblers feature a vacuum-sealed construction.

High heat in a dishwasher can damage this vacuum seal. When the seal breaks, the tumbler loses its insulating properties. It will no longer keep your drinks hot or cold effectively. The exterior finish on stainless steel tumblers can also suffer. Harsh detergents and high temperatures can fade colors or chip coatings. To protect your investment, hand washing is the recommended method. A simple wash with warm soapy water is best. This gentle approach preserves the tumbler's function and appearance. ### Why Dishwasher Settings Matter for Tervis Products

Dishwasher settings play a big role in how your Tervis products fare. Different cycles use different water temperatures. They also use different drying methods. Understanding these settings is key to protecting your Tervis. Most dishwashers have cycles like "Normal," "Light," and "Heavy." They also often include a "Heat Dry" option.

For plastic Tervis tumblers, avoid high-heat cycles. The "Normal" or "Light" cycle is usually fine. Always skip the "Heat Dry" setting. The intense heat from this option can warp plastic. It can damage the double-wall seal. This leads to moisture getting trapped between the layers. If your dishwasher does not allow you to turn off heat dry, air drying is a better choice. You can simply remove the tumblers after the wash cycle. Let them air dry on a rack. This protects the integrity of your plastic Tervis.

### Best Practices for Washing Plastic Tervis in the Dishwasher

Washing your plastic Tervis tumblers in the dishwasher is easy with the right steps. Proper loading and cycle selection are vital. This ensures they come out clean and undamaged. Always place your Tervis tumblers on the top rack. The top rack provides a gentler wash. It keeps the tumblers away from the direct heating element at the bottom.

When loading, ensure tumblers are stable. They should not tip over during the wash cycle. Make sure nothing blocks the spray arms. This allows water to reach all surfaces. Use a standard dishwasher detergent. Avoid harsh or abrasive cleaners. Select a normal or light wash cycle. Crucially, turn off the "Heated Dry" option. If you cannot disable it, remove the tumblers before the drying cycle begins. Let them air dry. Following these tips helps you maintain your Tervis cups for years. ### Protecting Your Tervis: Avoiding Common Dishwasher Mistakes

Using a dishwasher incorrectly can harm your Tervis. Many common mistakes are easy to avoid. One major error is placing plastic Tervis on the bottom rack. The heating element at the bottom creates intense heat. This heat can warp the plastic. It can also break the insulation seal. Always use the top rack for plastic Tervis tumblers.

Another mistake is using the "Heat Dry" setting. This option, while convenient, can damage Tervis plastics. High drying temperatures lead to issues like cloudiness or trapped moisture. Always choose air dry or no heat dry. Overloading the dishwasher is also a problem. Tervis tumblers need space for water to clean them properly. Overloading can also cause items to shift and potentially damage each other. Lastly, using abrasive cleaners or harsh chemicals can etch the plastic. Stick to standard dishwasher detergents. These simple steps ensure your Tervis stays in good condition.

### The Role of Water Temperature in Tervis Dishwashing

Water temperature is a critical factor when cleaning Tervis in a dishwasher. Dishwashers use heated water for sanitization and cleaning. However, excessive heat can be detrimental to Tervis tumblers. This is especially true for plastic models. Most dishwashers heat water to between 120°F and 150°F (49°C and 66°C).

For plastic Tervis tumblers, this temperature range is usually acceptable on the top rack. The top rack receives less direct heat. The problem arises with the "Heated Dry" cycle. This cycle can push temperatures much higher. These extreme temperatures can cause plastic to soften and warp. They can compromise the sealed insulation of plastic Tervis cups. Always select a wash cycle that avoids high heat drying. Opt for an air-dry setting or simply open the dishwasher door after the wash cycle finishes. This allows your Tervis to air dry naturally. This simple step protects your Tervis from heat damage.

### FAQ Section

**Q1: Can I put any Tervis tumbler in the dishwasher?**
No, not all Tervis tumblers are dishwasher safe. Plastic Tervis tumblers are typically safe on the top rack. Stainless steel Tervis tumblers require hand washing. High heat can damage their vacuum seal and finish. Always check the specific care instructions for your Tervis product. This ensures proper cleaning and longevity.

**Q2: Why should I only use the top rack for plastic Tervis?**
The top rack of a dishwasher is farther from the heating element. This means it experiences lower temperatures during the wash and dry cycles. Placing plastic Tervis on the bottom rack exposes it to intense heat. This heat can warp the plastic, break the insulation seal, and cause condensation between layers.

**Q3: What happens if I put my stainless steel Tervis in the dishwasher?**
Putting a stainless steel Tervis in the dishwasher can damage its vacuum insulation. The high heat can cause the vacuum seal to break. This will make the tumbler lose its ability to keep drinks hot or cold. It can also degrade the exterior finish. Hand washing is essential for stainless steel models.

**Q4: Can Tervis lids and accessories go in the dishwasher?**
Yes, most Tervis lids and accessories are dishwasher safe. You should place them on the top rack. Ensure they are secure and won't fall during the wash cycle. Small parts might need extra attention to clean crevices. Always let them air dry completely before reassembly.

**Q5: Will using the dishwasher void my Tervis warranty?**
Following Tervis's recommended care instructions helps maintain your warranty. Placing plastic Tervis tumblers on the top rack is generally fine. However, putting stainless steel tumblers in the dishwasher or using high heat dry cycles on plastic Tervis can void the warranty. Always consult your product's specific care information.

**Q6: How can I tell if my Tervis is plastic or stainless steel?**
Plastic Tervis tumblers are lighter and often have a clear, glossy finish. You can often see the inner and outer walls with a design in between. Stainless steel Tervis tumblers are heavier, typically have a metallic or powder-coated finish, and are opaque. They feel colder to the touch when empty.
