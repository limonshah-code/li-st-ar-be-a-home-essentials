## Washing Clothes in Your Dishwasher: Is It Safe?

Your washing machine is broken. You have a few small items that need cleaning quickly. You might look at your dishwasher and wonder, "Can I wash clothes in the dishwasher?" It seems like a logical thought. Both machines use water and detergent to clean things. However, their cleaning methods are very different.

This article explores whether you can actually wash clothes in the dishwasher. We will cover which items are potentially safe for this unusual cleaning method. We also discuss what clothing items you should always keep out of your dishwasher. We will look at the steps to take if you decide to try it. We also consider the pros, cons, and hygiene factors. Let's find out if this kitchen appliance can really help with your laundry.

### Takeaway

*   **Limited Use:** You can wash only specific, durable small items in a dishwasher.
*   **Avoid Delicates:** Do not put delicate fabrics, embellished items, or large clothing in the dishwasher.
*   **Proper Detergent:** Use laundry detergent, not dish soap, for any clothing.
*   **Low Heat:** Select the coolest wash cycle and turn off the heat dry option.
*   **Hygiene First:** Clean your dishwasher thoroughly after washing non-food items.

### Can you wash clothes in the dishwasher?

You can wash certain small, durable items in a dishwasher. These include things like rubber shoes, plastic sandals, or baseball caps. Dishwashers are not suitable for regular clothes. They use very hot water, strong jets, and harsh detergents. These elements can damage fabrics, cause shrinkage, or fade colors. It is not a recommended method for general laundry.

## Understanding Your Dishwasher's Washing Action

A dishwasher cleans by spraying hot water and detergent at high pressure. It removes food particles from dishes. The water often reaches very high temperatures, sometimes over 150°F (65°C). Dishwasher detergents are strong chemicals. They break down grease and baked-on food. They are designed for hard surfaces like ceramics and glass.

Washing machines work differently. They tumble clothes gently or agitate them in a drum. Washing machines use cooler water temperatures for many cycles. Laundry detergents are formulated to clean fabric fibers. They also protect colors and textures. They create suds and rinse away dirt without harming the fabric.

The extreme heat and powerful water jets in a dishwasher can damage clothing fibers. Delicate fabrics can shrink, tear, or lose their shape. Colors might bleed or fade quickly. The harsh detergents also pose a risk. They can strip natural oils from fabrics. This leaves clothes stiff or damaged. Understanding these differences helps explain why a dishwasher is generally not a good choice for clothes.

Think about the mechanical action involved. A washing machine uses a gentle tumbling or agitation. It mimics hand washing or a gentle scrub. A dishwasher uses brute force. It blasts away grime. This force is too much for most textiles. It can quickly fray edges or pull threads. Dishwashers also lack a rinse cycle designed for fabric. This can leave detergent residue on your items. This residue can irritate skin or damage the fabric over time. For general laundry, a dedicated washing machine is always the best tool.

## What Clothes Should Never Go Near Your Dishwasher?

Most clothing items do not belong in a dishwasher. Dishwashers get very hot. They spray water with great force. This combination is bad for almost all fabrics. Delicate clothes are especially at risk. Silk, wool, lace, and linen will shrink, fray, or get ruined. The high heat can set stains and cause colors to bleed badly.

Items with embellishments are also a definite no. Beads, sequins, embroidery, or delicate trims will get tangled or torn. The harsh jets can pull off decorations. It can also damage the fabric they are attached to. Large items like sweaters, jeans, or sheets are unsuitable. They absorb too much water. This makes them very heavy. They can block the spray arms. This stops the dishwasher from cleaning properly. Heavy, wet items can also break components inside the machine.

Anything that requires a gentle cycle or cold water should never enter a dishwasher. This includes most everyday clothing. Even sturdy cotton shirts or pants will not fare well. They will likely come out wrinkled, faded, or shrunken. Dishwashers are for dishes. They are not for fabric care. When you have items that need gentle cleaning, consider other methods. *   **Natural Fibers:** Most cotton clothes, linen, rayon, which can shrink or get damaged.

## The Step-by-Step Process for Dishwasher Laundry

If you decide to wash a suitable item in the dishwasher, follow these steps carefully. First, make sure the item is ready. Remove any loose dirt or debris. For shoes, brush off mud. Take out insoles and shoelaces. You might want to hand wash these parts separately. This keeps your dishwasher cleaner.

Next, load your item correctly. Place baseball caps on the top rack. You can use a cap frame to help them keep their shape. For shoes, put them on the top rack too. Secure them with dish prongs. This stops them from moving around during the wash cycle. Make sure they do not block the spray arms. Proper loading is key to both cleaning and protecting your item and the machine.

Now, choose your detergent and cycle. **Never use regular dishwasher detergent for clothes.** It is too harsh. Instead, use a very small amount of laundry detergent. A tablespoon or two is usually enough. Put it in the main detergent dispenser. Select the coolest wash cycle available. A "light wash" or "rinse only" cycle often works best. It limits exposure to high heat. Crucially, turn off the "heated dry" option. High heat will definitely damage your items. Let them air dry instead.

After the wash, remove your items. Inspect them for cleanliness. If they are clean, let them air dry completely. You can hang them up or place them on a rack. Make sure they are fully dry before storing them. This prevents mold or mildew. Remember, this method is for occasional, specific items only. It is not a replacement for a washing machine. ## Pros and Cons of Using Your Dishwasher for Laundry

Using a dishwasher to wash clothes has both benefits and drawbacks. Understanding these helps you make an informed decision.

### Pros:

*   **Convenience for Small Items:** A dishwasher can be handy for small, sturdy items. Think about plastic toys or rubber shoes. These often do not justify running a full washing machine cycle. You can often run them with a small load of dishes.
*   **Sanitization:** Dishwashers use very hot water. This high temperature helps kill germs and bacteria. For items like pet toys or very dirty athletic shoes, this can be a plus. It offers a level of sanitization that some washing machine cycles might not.
*   **Emergency Solution:** If your washing machine breaks down, a dishwasher can be a temporary fix. It can clean specific non-fabric items in a pinch. This avoids waiting for a repair for those urgent cleaning needs.
*   **Shape Retention:** For items like baseball caps, the rigid structure of a dishwasher can help maintain their shape. They avoid the tumbling and agitation that can distort them in a washing machine.

### Cons:

*   **Damage Risk:** This is the biggest drawback. The high heat and strong water jets can easily damage clothes. Fabrics can shrink, fade, tear, or lose their texture. Embellishments can break off. This risk applies to almost all fabric types.
*   **Appliance Damage:** Wet clothes absorb a lot of water. Heavy, wet items can put strain on the dishwasher's racks and spray arms. Detergent residue from laundry soap might also build up over time. This can affect the machine's performance or drainage.
*   **Hygiene Concerns:** Your dishwasher cleans food particles. Mixing clothes with food residue can transfer germs or odors. It can also leave your "clean" clothes smelling like dirty dishes. Thorough cleaning of the dishwasher after is essential.
*   **Energy Inefficiency:** Dishwashers use a lot of hot water and energy per cycle. Washing a single pair of shoes or a cap is not energy-efficient. It uses more resources than hand washing or even a small load in a washing machine.
*   **Specific Detergent Needs:** You must use laundry detergent, not dish soap. Using the wrong detergent can cause excessive suds. This can overflow your machine or damage its internal parts.
*   **Limited Capacity:** A dishwasher can only hold a few small items. It cannot handle a full load of laundry. This makes it impractical for regular washing needs.

Using your dishwasher for clothes is a very specific solution. It works for a tiny niche of items. Most of the time, the risks outweigh the benefits. For any item you truly care about, stick to traditional washing methods. When faced with a broken washer, you might explore alternative ways to clean clothes, such as using a laundromat, which is far safer for your garments. ## Safety and Hygiene: Mixing Clothes and Dishes

Mixing clothes with dishes in your dishwasher raises significant safety and hygiene questions. Dishwashers are designed to clean items that come into contact with food. This means they must sanitize effectively. When you introduce non-food items, especially dirty ones, you change the dynamic.

One major concern is food residue. Even after a dishwasher cycle, tiny food particles can remain. These can transfer onto clothes during a subsequent wash. Conversely, dirt and grime from shoes or other items can get into the dishwasher. This can then transfer to your dishes later. This cross-contamination risk is why most people do not recommend the practice. It compromises the cleanliness of your eating utensils.

Using the correct detergent is also vital for safety. Dishwasher detergents contain powerful chemicals. They are not meant for skin contact. Residue on clothing can cause skin irritation or allergic reactions. Laundry detergents are formulated differently. They are designed to rinse cleanly from fabrics. Ensure you only use laundry detergent, and a minimal amount, when washing clothes in the dishwasher. After any non-food item wash, it is important to run an empty hot cycle with a dishwasher cleaner. This helps flush out any lingering grime or detergent residue. Regularly cleaning your appliance, as detailed in guides like [how to clean wash dishwasher](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-clean-wash-dishwasher), is a good practice, especially if you use it for non-traditional items.

To minimize risks:

*   **Wash separately:** Never wash clothes with dishes in the same cycle. Always run a dedicated cycle for non-food items.
*   **Pre-clean items:** Remove as much dirt as possible from shoes or caps before putting them in the dishwasher.
*   **Sanitize the dishwasher:** After washing non-food items, run an empty, hot cycle. Add a dishwasher cleaning tablet or white vinegar. This helps clean and sanitize the appliance for your next dish load.
*   **Use caution:** If anyone in your household has allergies or sensitive skin, avoid this method completely.

Ultimately, while you can technically wash some items, the hygiene implications are serious. It is better to use your dishwasher for its intended purpose. This maintains the highest standards of cleanliness for your kitchenware.

## FAQ Section

### Can you put regular clothes in a dishwasher?

No, you should not put regular clothes in a dishwasher. Dishwashers use extremely hot water, strong water jets, and harsh detergents. These factors can cause most fabrics to shrink, fade, tear, or become otherwise damaged. Dishwashers lack the gentle tumbling or agitation needed for proper fabric care.

### What cycle should I use for clothes in a dishwasher?

If you must wash specific, durable items like rubber shoes or caps, choose the coolest, shortest wash cycle available. A "light wash" or "rinse only" setting is often best. It limits the exposure to high heat and intense pressure. Always turn off the "heated dry" option to prevent damage.

### Is dishwasher detergent safe for clothes?

No, dishwasher detergent is not safe for clothes. It contains powerful chemicals designed for hard surfaces and baked-on food. These chemicals are too harsh for fabric. They can strip colors, damage fibers, or leave irritating residues. Always use a very small amount of liquid laundry detergent instead.

### How do you dry clothes washed in a dishwasher?

You must air dry items washed in a dishwasher. Never use the dishwasher's heated dry cycle. The high heat can cause severe shrinkage or damage to clothes. Hang items on a drying rack or clothesline. Ensure they are completely dry before storing them. This prevents mold or mildew growth.

### Can you wash shoes in a dishwasher?

You can wash certain types of shoes in a dishwasher. This includes plastic flip-flops, Crocs, or sturdy athletic sneakers without leather or delicate embellishments. Remove insoles and laces first. Place them securely on the top rack. Use a cool cycle and laundry detergent, then air dry.
