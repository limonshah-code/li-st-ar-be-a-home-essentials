### FAQ Section

**Q1: Are all Klean Kanteen bottles dishwasher safe?**
No, not all Klean Kanteen bottles are dishwasher safe. Only models with the chip-resistant Klean Coat finish and the original brushed stainless steel bottles are generally safe for dishwashers. Vacuum-insulated bottles and those with older, non-Klean Coat painted exteriors should be hand-washed to protect their thermal performance and finish. Always check the specific product's care instructions.

**Q2: How do I know if my Klean Kanteen has Klean Coat?**
You can identify a Klean Coat finish by checking the bottle's packaging or the product description on Klean Kanteen's official website. Sometimes, the term "Klean Coat" is printed directly on the bottom of the bottle. If your bottle is an older model, especially pre-2018, it likely has a traditional painted finish and should be hand-washed.

**Q3: Can I put Klean Kanteen caps in the dishwasher?**
Most Klean Kanteen caps, such as the Loop Cap, Classic Cap, and Sport Cap, are top-rack dishwasher safe. However, specialty caps like the Bamboo Cap are not. Always remove silicone gaskets for separate cleaning to prevent mildew buildup. Check the specific care instructions for your particular cap model to ensure proper cleaning and longevity.

**Q4: What happens if I put an insulated Klean Kanteen in the dishwasher?**
Putting an insulated Klean Kanteen in the dishwasher can damage its vacuum seal. The high heat and strong water jets can compromise the insulation, causing the bottle to lose its ability to keep drinks hot or cold. It can also cause the exterior paint to chip or fade. For these reasons, insulated bottles should always be hand-washed.

**Q5: How often should I clean my Klean Kanteen?**
You should rinse your Klean Kanteen after every use, especially if it contained anything other than water. For a thorough cleaning, wash your bottle with soap and water (either by hand or in the dishwasher, if safe) every few days, or daily if you use it for sugary drinks, coffee, or smoothies. Regular cleaning prevents bacterial growth and odor.

**Q6: Can I use bleach to clean my Klean Kanteen?**
No, you should not use bleach or chlorine-based cleaners to clean your Klean Kanteen. These harsh chemicals can damage the stainless steel, affecting its integrity and potentially leaving residues that are unsafe for consumption. Stick to mild dish soap, warm water, and a bottle brush for effective and safe cleaning. For tough stains, a mixture of vinegar and baking soda can be used.
