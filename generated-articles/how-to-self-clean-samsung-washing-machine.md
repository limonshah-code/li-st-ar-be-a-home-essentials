## Self-Cleaning Your Samsung Washing Machine: A Complete Guide

Your Samsung washing machine works hard to keep your clothes clean. But have you thought about cleaning the machine itself? Over time, detergent residue, fabric softener, dirt, and even mold can build up inside your washer. This build-up causes unpleasant odors and can transfer to your laundry. Nobody wants clothes that smell less than fresh.

Knowing how to self-clean your Samsung washing machine is key to keeping it in top condition. This process helps remove grime and ensures your clothes come out truly clean. A clean machine also works more efficiently and lasts longer. This guide will walk you through the simple steps to effectively self-clean your Samsung washing machine. We will cover why it is important, how to run the self-clean cycle, and what else you can do for a sparkling clean appliance.

### Takeaway

*   Regularly run the Self Clean cycle on your Samsung washing machine to prevent odor and residue buildup.
*   Perform manual cleaning of the detergent dispenser, door gasket, and filter for a deeper clean.
*   Use the right cleaning agents like white vinegar or a machine cleaner.
*   Follow specific steps for your model, usually involving an empty drum and hot water.

To self-clean your Samsung washing machine, select the "Self Clean" or "Pure Cycle" setting on the control panel. Start the cycle with an empty drum. The machine will use hot water and high spin speeds to remove detergent residue and odor-causing bacteria.

## Why Self-Clean Your Samsung Washing Machine?

Cleaning your washing machine may seem unnecessary. After all, it cleans with soap and water, right? The truth is, washing machines can get quite dirty inside. They deal with grime from clothes, leftover detergent, and fabric softener. These things combine with moisture to create a perfect environment for mold and mildew.

A dirty washing machine can make your clothes smell bad. You might notice a musty odor on freshly washed laundry. This smell comes from bacteria and mold growing inside the machine. Regular self-cleaning stops this problem. It keeps your washing machine fresh.

Self-cleaning also helps your washing machine work better. When residue builds up, it can affect how the machine operates. It might not rinse clothes as well, or it could even lead to clogs. A clean machine ensures optimal performance. This means cleaner clothes for you every time. It also extends the life of your appliance, saving you money on repairs or replacements.

## Understanding the Samsung Self-Clean Cycle

Samsung washing machines have a special cycle designed for internal cleaning. This cycle is usually called "Self Clean," "Pure Cycle," or "Eco Drum Clean." It is an automatic process. The machine uses hot water and high spin speeds to wash away grime. It does not require any special cleaning agents, though some people add a cleaner for better results.

The Self Clean cycle is different from a normal wash. It runs without any clothes inside the drum. The machine focuses solely on cleaning its internal components. This includes the drum, the pipes, and other hidden areas. The cycle runs at a very high temperature. This hot water helps dissolve detergent buildup and kill bacteria and mold. Understanding [what is Samsung washing machine Self Clean](https://homeessentialsguide.com/what-is-samsung-washing-machine-self-clean) helps you appreciate its purpose.

When the cycle finishes, your machine is internally clean. Some Samsung models even alert you when it's time to run the Self Clean cycle. This feature makes it easy to remember. Using this cycle regularly is a simple way to maintain your washer. It prevents those common musty smells. This means your laundry stays fresh and clean after every wash.

## Step-by-Step Guide: Running the Self-Clean Cycle

Running the self-clean cycle on your Samsung washing machine is straightforward. First, make sure the washing machine drum is completely empty. Do not put any clothes, towels, or other items inside. The cycle needs an empty drum to work effectively.

Next, close the washing machine door firmly. Then, turn on your washing machine. Look for the "Self Clean," "Pure Cycle," or "Eco Drum Clean" setting on your control panel. The exact name might vary slightly by model. Once you find it, select this cycle. You may need to press and hold the button for a few seconds.

After selecting the cycle, press the "Start" button. The machine will begin the self-cleaning process. This cycle usually takes about 2 to 4 hours to complete. It uses hot water and specific drum motions to scrub away residue. Let the cycle run its course without interruption. Once it finishes, the machine will power off, or display a "Done" message. Your washing machine is now internally clean. Remember to leave the door ajar after the cycle finishes to allow the drum to air dry fully. This helps prevent new mold growth.

## Beyond the Cycle: Manual Cleaning for a Deeper Clean

While the Self Clean cycle handles the internal drum, other parts of your Samsung washing machine also need attention. The detergent dispenser drawer is one such area. Soap scum and mold can build up here. To clean it, pull the drawer out completely. Some models have a release tab you need to press.

Once the drawer is out, wash it with warm, soapy water. Use an old toothbrush to scrub away any stubborn residue. Pay special attention to corners and crevices. Rinse the drawer thoroughly and let it dry before putting it back. You can also clean the area where the drawer sits in the machine with a damp cloth. This ensures no hidden grime remains. For a comprehensive guide, refer to instructions on [how to clean Samsung washing machine](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-clean-samsung-washing-machine).

The rubber gasket around the door is another common spot for mold. This is especially true for front-loader models. Wipe down the gasket thoroughly with a damp cloth. You can use a diluted bleach solution or white vinegar for stubborn mold spots. Make sure to lift the folds of the gasket to clean underneath. Finally, do not forget the drain filter. This filter traps lint and small items. Regularly cleaning it prevents clogs and odors. Check your user manual for steps on [how to clean Samsung washing machine filter top loader](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-clean-samsung-washing-machine-filter-top-loader) or front loader. These manual steps complement the self-clean cycle for a truly pristine machine.

## Frequency and Best Practices for Self-Cleaning

How often should you self-clean your Samsung washing machine? Samsung generally recommends running the Self Clean cycle once a month. Some models have a reminder light that illuminates after 40 washes. This serves as a helpful prompt. Sticking to a monthly schedule prevents significant buildup. If you do a lot of laundry, or if you live in a humid climate, consider cleaning it more often.

There are a few best practices to maximize the effectiveness of the self-clean cycle. First, always ensure the machine is empty. Adding items will interfere with the cleaning process. Second, consider using a specialized washing machine cleaner. These products are designed to break down tough residue and deodorize. You can often find them in the laundry aisle of your grocery store. Follow the product instructions for use. Some are added directly to the drum, while others go into the dispenser.

Third, after the self-clean cycle finishes, always leave the washing machine door ajar. This allows the interior to air dry completely. Moisture left inside can lead to mold and mildew growth. Air circulation is key to keeping your machine fresh between cleanings. Taking these simple steps ensures your Samsung washing machine stays clean and odor-free, helping it perform its best for years.

## Troubleshooting Common Self-Clean Issues

Sometimes, you might encounter issues while trying to self-clean your Samsung washing machine. A common problem is the cycle not starting. First, check if the door is fully closed. Many Samsung washers will not start any cycle if the door latch is not engaged. Also, make sure no clothes are left inside the drum. The self-clean cycle requires an empty machine.

Another issue can be a strange smell or residue remaining after the cycle. This might mean you have a heavy buildup inside your machine. In this case, run the self-clean cycle again. You could also try adding a cup of white vinegar directly to the drum before starting the cycle. Vinegar is a natural cleaner and deodorizer. For very stubborn buildup, a commercial washing machine cleaner might be necessary. Follow the product instructions for the best results.

If you hear unusual noises during the self-clean cycle, it could be a sign of something trapped. First, stop the machine. Carefully check the drum for any loose items that might have been overlooked. Also, inspect the drain filter for debris. Addressing these small issues quickly ensures your machine runs smoothly. These troubleshooting steps help keep your machine running well.

## Maintaining Your Samsung Washing Machine for Longevity

Regular self-cleaning is crucial, but other habits also contribute to your Samsung washing machine's lifespan. Proper detergent use is one key factor. Use only high-efficiency (HE) detergent if your machine is an HE model. HE detergents produce less suds, which prevents residue buildup. Using too much detergent, regardless of type, can also leave behind gunk. Always measure your detergent according to the package instructions. More soap does not mean cleaner clothes.

After each wash, it is a good idea to wipe down the inside of the door and the rubber gasket. This removes any standing water or lint. Leaving the door slightly ajar between washes is also very helpful. This allows air to circulate inside the drum. Good air circulation prevents moisture from getting trapped. Trapped moisture leads to mold and unpleasant odors. Think of it as airing out a damp towel.

Lastly, make sure your machine is level. An unbalanced machine can vibrate excessively, causing wear and tear over time. Check the feet of your washing machine regularly. Adjust them if needed to ensure the machine sits firmly on the floor. Following these simple maintenance tips, along with regular self-cleaning, will keep your Samsung washing machine running efficiently for many years. Proper care helps your [do it yourself washing machine](https://homeessentialsguide.com/do-it-yourself-washing-machine) last longer.

## FAQ Section

**Q1: How often should I run the Self Clean cycle on my Samsung washing machine?**
A1: Samsung recommends running the Self Clean or Eco Drum Clean cycle monthly. Some models have an indicator light that turns on after about 40 washes as a reminder. Regular cleaning prevents mold, mildew, and odors from building up inside the drum and other components, ensuring your laundry always smells fresh.

**Q2: Can I use bleach or other cleaners during the Self Clean cycle?**
A2: For the standard Self Clean cycle, most Samsung manuals advise against adding cleaning products. The cycle uses hot water and specific motions to clean the drum. However, for a very dirty machine, some users add a cup of white vinegar or a specialized washing machine cleaner directly to the empty drum before starting the cycle.

**Q3: What should I do if the Self Clean cycle does not remove all odors?**
A3: If odors persist after running the Self Clean cycle, try running it again with a cup of white vinegar or a commercial washing machine cleaner. Also, ensure you are manually cleaning the detergent dispenser drawer and the rubber door gasket thoroughly. These areas often harbor mold and mildew, contributing to odors.

**Q4: Is the Self Clean cycle the same as a normal wash cycle?**
A4: No, the Self Clean cycle is different. It runs with an empty drum and uses very hot water with specific, high-speed drum rotations designed to clean the machine's internal components. A normal wash cycle is for cleaning clothes and uses lower temperatures and different agitation patterns.

**Q5: Why is my washing machine still showing a "Self Clean" reminder after I ran the cycle?**
A5: After running the Self Clean cycle, you may need to manually reset the reminder on some Samsung models. Consult your washing machine's user manual for specific instructions on how to clear the "Self Clean" or "Eco Drum Clean" indicator light. Usually, it involves pressing and holding a button for a few seconds.
