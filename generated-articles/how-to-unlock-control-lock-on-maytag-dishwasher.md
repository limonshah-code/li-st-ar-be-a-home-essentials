### Performing a Power Cycle

A power cycle is often the first step for many appliance problems. It acts like a reboot for the dishwasher's internal computer.
1.  **Locate Power Source:** Find the dishwasher's power cord. It usually plugs into an outlet under the sink. If you cannot reach the cord, go to your home's electrical service panel.
2.  **Disconnect Power:** Unplug the dishwasher from the wall outlet. If you are using the circuit breaker, flip the breaker labeled for your dishwasher to the "Off" position.
3.  **Wait:** Leave the power disconnected for at least 1 to 5 minutes. This allows the internal control board to fully discharge any residual electricity.
4.  **Reconnect Power:** Plug the dishwasher back in or flip the circuit breaker back to the "On" position.
5.  **Test:** Try to unlock the control lock again using the standard press-and-hold method. Often, this simple reset resolves the issue.
