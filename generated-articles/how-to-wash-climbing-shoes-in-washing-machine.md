## Wash Climbing Shoes in Washing Machine Safely

My climbing shoes often become quite dirty and smelly after many sessions at the gym or on the rock. It makes me wonder about the best way to clean them. Many climbers ask if they can **wash climbing shoes in washing machine**. The good news is, you can!

This method offers a deep clean that hand washing cannot always match. However, you must follow specific steps to avoid damage. This guide will help you understand the process. We will cover preparation, the right settings, and proper drying techniques. Following these steps helps keep your shoes in good shape. It will also extend their lifespan and get rid of unpleasant odors. Let's make your climbing shoes fresh again.

### Takeaway

*   **Assess Shoe Material:** Check if your climbing shoes are synthetic or leather before washing.
*   **Pre-Clean Thoroughly:** Remove loose dirt, chalk, and debris before machine washing.
*   **Protect Your Shoes:** Place shoes in a mesh laundry bag for washing machine protection.
*   **Use Gentle Settings:** Opt for a cold water, delicate cycle with mild detergent.
*   **Air Dry Completely:** Never put climbing shoes in a dryer; always air dry away from direct heat.

### Can you wash climbing shoes in a washing machine?

Yes, you can wash climbing shoes in a washing machine. Most synthetic and some leather climbing shoes tolerate machine washing. You must use a gentle cycle, cold water, and mild detergent. Proper preparation and drying are also critical to prevent damage and maintain shoe shape.

---

### Understanding Climbing Shoe Materials and Machine Washing

Before you consider machine washing your climbing shoes, you must know their material. Climbing shoes come in two main types: synthetic and leather. Each type reacts differently to water and agitation. Knowing your shoe's material guides your cleaning choice. This knowledge prevents accidental damage.

Synthetic climbing shoes often contain materials like microfiber or various woven fabrics. These materials usually tolerate water well. They resist shrinking and stretching better than leather. Machine washing synthetic shoes is generally safer. The fabrics dry faster too. This reduces the risk of mildew growth.

Leather climbing shoes, especially unlined ones, are more delicate. Water can cause leather to shrink. It can also stiffen the material. This changes the shoe's fit and performance. Some leather shoes have linings that protect them from water effects. Always check the manufacturer's care instructions for leather shoes. If unsure, hand washing is a safer choice for leather.

Some shoes blend synthetic and leather parts. For these, always lean on the side of caution. Treat them like leather shoes or follow specific blend instructions. Misidentifying your shoe material leads to potential damage. I always check my shoe tags first. This simple step helps keep your shoes in top condition.

### Preparing Your Climbing Shoes for the Wash Cycle

Proper preparation is critical before you put your climbing shoes in the washing machine. This step removes larger debris and protects your shoes. Skipping this part can lead to poor cleaning results. It can also damage your washing machine. We want to avoid both outcomes.

First, remove as much loose dirt and chalk as possible. Take your shoes outside and clap them together. This knocks off dried mud and loose chalk. Use a stiff brush, like an old toothbrush, to scrub away any caked-on grime. Pay attention to the soles and rand areas. These parts collect a lot of dirt.

Next, remove the laces if your shoes have them. You can wash laces separately by hand. This ensures a more thorough cleaning for both the shoes and the laces. If your shoes have Velcro straps, close them securely. This prevents the Velcro from snagging on other items or the machine's drum. Snags can damage both the straps and the machine.

Finally, place your climbing shoes inside a mesh laundry bag. This is a crucial step for protection. The bag prevents the shoes from banging around inside the washing machine. It also protects the shoes from direct contact with the drum. You can also add a few old towels to the wash. These towels cushion the shoes further. They help balance the wash load. This preparation ensures a safer wash cycle for your beloved climbing gear.

### Choosing the Right Detergent and Wash Settings

Selecting the correct detergent and wash settings is paramount for safe machine washing. Using the wrong products or cycles can damage your climbing shoes. We aim to clean them, not ruin them. Your choices here make a big difference.

Always opt for a mild liquid detergent. Heavy-duty detergents can be too harsh for shoe materials. They can strip away natural oils from leather or break down adhesives. Avoid detergents with bleach or strong chemicals. These additives can discolor your shoes or degrade the rubber. A small amount of gentle detergent is enough for cleaning. Think about using a sports-specific detergent if you have one. These detergents are formulated to combat odors in athletic gear.

For wash settings, cold water is always the best choice. Hot water can cause materials to shrink. It can also weaken glues holding the shoe together. Heat is a major enemy for climbing shoes. Select the delicate or gentle cycle on your washing machine. This cycle uses less agitation. It mimics hand washing more closely. A fast spin cycle can also be too aggressive. If your machine offers it, choose a lower spin speed.

Never wash your climbing shoes with other delicate items that could be damaged. Washing them with old towels is fine. These towels add cushioning and balance the load. For other types of footwear, like [machine washing Skechers slip-on shoes](https://homeessentialsguide.com/can-you-machine-wash-skechers-slip-on-shoes), similar gentle settings are often recommended. I always set my machine to the lowest temperature and gentlest cycle. This ensures my shoes get clean without harm.

### The Step-by-Step Process: Machine Washing Your Climbing Shoes

Now that you have prepared your shoes and chosen the right settings, it is time to wash them. Following these steps ensures a thorough and safe cleaning process. This detailed guide makes the task simple. You will get clean shoes without worry.

**Step 1: Load the Washing Machine**
Place your mesh laundry bag containing the climbing shoes into the washing machine. Add two or three old towels to the drum. The towels provide cushioning. They also help balance the load. This prevents the shoes from rattling too much. An unbalanced load causes loud noises and extra wear on your machine.

**Step 2: Add Detergent**
Pour a small amount of mild liquid detergent into the detergent dispenser. Use about half the amount you would for a regular load of laundry. Too much soap creates excessive suds. It also leaves residue on your shoes. Less is more when washing delicate items.

**Step 3: Select Wash Cycle and Temperature**
Set your washing machine to the "Delicate" or "Gentle" cycle. This cycle uses minimal agitation. Choose "Cold" water temperature. Cold water prevents shrinking and glue degradation. If your machine allows, select a low spin speed. A low spin speed reduces stress on the shoe structure.

**Step 4: Start the Wash Cycle**
Close the washing machine door and start the cycle. Let the machine do its work. Stay near if this is your first time. Listen for any unusual banging sounds. The towels should dampen most of the noise. If the banging is too much, pause the machine and readjust the load. This prevents damage to your shoes or machine.

**Step 5: Remove Shoes Promptly**
Once the cycle finishes, remove your climbing shoes from the washing machine immediately. Leaving them wet inside the machine creates mildew. Mildew causes new odors and can damage the fabric. Prompt removal is key for freshness. Your shoes are now ready for drying.

### Drying Your Climbing Shoes After Machine Washing

Drying your climbing shoes correctly after washing is just as important as the wash cycle itself. Incorrect drying methods can cause irreversible damage. It can shrink materials or weaken adhesives. We want to avoid these issues. The goal is to dry them thoroughly and gently.

**Never put your climbing shoes in a tumble dryer.** The heat from a dryer is too intense for climbing shoe materials. It will shrink leather, melt synthetic components, and degrade the rubber. This ruins the fit and performance of your shoes. Air drying is the only safe method.

Find a well-ventilated area for drying. A spot with good airflow helps shoes dry faster. This prevents mold and mildew from growing. You can place the shoes outdoors in the shade on a warm day. Direct sunlight is too harsh. It can fade colors and dry out materials too quickly. Indoors, use a fan to circulate air around them. Hanging them upside down helps water drain out. You can use clothespins or a shoe drying rack.

Stuffing your shoes with crumpled newspaper or paper towels also helps. The paper absorbs moisture from the inside. This speeds up drying time. Change the paper every few hours until it comes out dry. This process also helps maintain the shoe's original shape. Drying can take anywhere from 24 to 72 hours. It depends on humidity and airflow. Make sure the shoes are completely dry before you wear them or store them. Any lingering dampness will invite odor-causing bacteria.

### Addressing Common Issues: Odor and Stubborn Stains

Even after washing, some climbing shoes might retain odors or have stubborn stains. It is important to tackle these issues directly. This ensures your shoes feel and smell truly clean. We want climbing shoes that perform well and do not offend.

**Dealing with Odor:**
Odor in climbing shoes often comes from bacteria. These bacteria thrive in moist, dark environments. If a machine wash does not fully remove the smell, consider an odor-neutralizing treatment.
*   **Baking Soda:** Sprinkle baking soda inside the dry shoes. Let it sit overnight to absorb odors. Vacuum or shake it out the next morning.
*   **Shoe Deodorizers:** Use specific shoe deodorizers or sprays designed for athletic footwear. These products often contain antibacterial agents.
*   **Tea Bags:** Unused tea bags can absorb odors. Place a few dry tea bags inside each shoe overnight.
*   **Freezing:** Some climbers freeze their shoes in a sealed bag. This can kill odor-causing bacteria. This is a temporary fix, not a cleaning method.

**Tackling Stubborn Stains:**
For stains that remain after washing, targeted treatment is needed.
*   **Mild Soap and Brush:** Mix a small amount of mild dish soap with water. Use a soft brush or old toothbrush to gently scrub the stained area. Rinse the spot with a damp cloth.
*   **Rubbing Alcohol:** For greasy or tar-like stains on rubber rands, a little rubbing alcohol on a cloth can work. Test a small, inconspicuous area first.
*   **Spot Cleaner:** Use a fabric-safe spot cleaner. Always read the product instructions. Test it on a hidden part of the shoe.

Remember to re-dry the shoes completely after any spot treatment. Preventing odor and stains is always easier than fixing them. Regular cleaning and proper storage help a lot.

### When Not to Machine Wash: Alternatives and Hand Washing

While machine washing works for many climbing shoes, it is not always the best option. Some shoes require a gentler touch. Knowing when to choose an alternative method protects your gear. We aim for optimal cleaning without risk.

You should avoid machine washing if your climbing shoes:
*   **Are made of unlined leather:** Leather shrinks, cracks, or stiffens when exposed to excessive water and agitation.
*   **Have visible damage or weak points:** Machine washing can worsen tears, loose stitching, or delaminating rubber.
*   **Are very old or delicate:** Older shoes often have weaker adhesives and materials. The washing machine's force can finish them off.
*   **Have specific manufacturer warnings against machine washing:** Always check the care tag or website. Some brands explicitly advise against it.

For these situations, hand washing is the safer and often better alternative. Hand washing allows for more control. It lets you target specific dirty areas.

**Hand Washing Steps:**
1.  **Preparation:** Remove loose dirt with a brush, just like before machine washing.
2.  **Soak (Optional):** Fill a basin with lukewarm water. Add a small amount of mild detergent. You can soak shoes for 15-30 minutes if they are very dirty.
3.  **Scrub:** Use a soft brush or sponge to gently scrub the shoes. Pay attention to the rubber rand, sole, and inside lining.
4.  **Rinse:** Rinse the shoes thoroughly under cool, running water. Ensure all soap residue is gone.
5.  **Dry:** Air dry your shoes completely in a well-ventilated area. Avoid direct sunlight or heat. Stuffing with newspaper helps.

Hand washing takes more effort but offers peace of mind. It ensures your specialized climbing shoes receive the care they need. This method prevents potential damage.

### Maintaining Your Climbing Shoes for Longevity

Cleaning your climbing shoes is only one part of their maintenance. Proper ongoing care makes a big difference in their lifespan. You invest a lot in your gear. Taking good care of it helps that investment last. Longevity means more climbs and better performance.

**Store Shoes Properly:**
Always store your climbing shoes in a cool, dry, and well-ventilated place. Avoid leaving them in hot cars or direct sunlight. Heat damages rubber and glues. Do not toss them into a dark, damp gym bag immediately after a climb. Let them air out first. Using a mesh bag for transport helps with airflow.

**Address Odor Regularly:**
Do not wait for intense smells to build up. Use odor-absorbing inserts or sprays after each use. A sprinkle of baking soda inside the shoes works wonders. This proactive approach prevents bacteria growth. It keeps shoes smelling fresher longer.

**Inspect for Wear and Tear:**
Regularly check your shoes for signs of wear. Look for thinning rubber on the sole or rand. Check for loose stitching or holes in the fabric. Addressing small issues early prevents larger problems. Re-soling services can extend the life of many climbing shoes significantly. Resoling is cheaper than buying new shoes.

**Clean After Each Use (Lightly):**
A quick wipe-down after each climbing session helps. Use a damp cloth to remove chalk and dirt from the rubber and uppers. This prevents grime from hardening. It also reduces the need for frequent deep washes. I always give my shoes a quick brush and wipe before putting them away.

**Avoid Harsh Chemicals:**
Never use strong chemicals or solvents on your climbing shoes. These can degrade the rubber, fabric, and adhesives. Stick to mild soaps and water for cleaning. Your shoes will thank you with many more climbs.

### FAQ Section

**1. How often should I wash my climbing shoes?**
Wash your climbing shoes when they become noticeably dirty or smelly. This often means every few weeks or months, depending on how frequently you climb and how much you sweat. Regular light cleaning after each use can reduce the need for deep washes.

**2. Can I use fabric softener when washing climbing shoes?**
No, avoid using fabric softener. Fabric softener can leave a residue on the shoe materials. This residue might affect the shoe's grip or breathability. It can also break down the adhesives over time. Stick to mild liquid detergent only.

**3. Will machine washing damage the rubber on my climbing shoes?**
Machine washing with cold water and a gentle cycle is generally safe for climbing shoe rubber. However, high heat or harsh detergents can degrade the rubber. Always air dry your shoes; never use a tumble dryer, as heat can melt or warp the rubber.

**4. What if my climbing shoes are made of an unknown material?**
If you are unsure of your shoe's material, always opt for hand washing. Hand washing provides more control. It minimizes the risk of damage. Check the manufacturer's website or contact them for specific care instructions.

**5. How can I remove chalk from my climbing shoes effectively?**
To remove chalk, first brush off as much loose chalk as possible with a stiff brush. For stubborn chalk residue, use a damp cloth with a little mild soap. Gently scrub the affected areas. Rinse thoroughly and air dry.

**6. Can I dry my climbing shoes in direct sunlight to speed up drying?**
It is best to avoid drying climbing shoes in direct sunlight. Intense UV rays and heat can fade colors. They can also dry out leather, degrade synthetic materials, and weaken glues. Air dry them in a shaded, well-ventilated area for optimal results.
