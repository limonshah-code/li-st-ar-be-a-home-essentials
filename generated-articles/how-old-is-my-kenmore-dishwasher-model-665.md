## Decoding the Serial Number: Uncovering the Manufacturing Date

Once you have located the serial number for your Kenmore Dishwasher Model 665, you are ready to decode its age. Kenmore, as a brand, uses a variety of dating systems within its serial numbers. This is because different manufacturers produce Kenmore appliances. The most common system involves using letters or numbers to represent the year and month or week of manufacture. ### Common Kenmore Serial Number Formats

Many Kenmore appliances, especially those produced by Whirlpool, use a specific dating scheme. The serial number is usually a long string of letters and numbers. You need to focus on certain characters, often the first few.

Here is a common format for Kenmore dishwashers, particularly the 665 series (often made by Whirlpool):

*   **Format 1 (Common):** The serial number might look something like "CSM1234567" or "F91234567."
    *   **First Letter/Number:** This typically represents the year. A common system uses letters where A=2000, B=2001, C=2002, and so on. Sometimes, a single digit is used for the last digit of the year (e.g., 9 for 2009 or 2019). Context often clarifies if it's 200x or 201x.
    *   **Second Character (often a number):** This usually represents the week of the year (01-52).
    *   **Example:** A serial number starting with "C35" would mean the 35th week of the year 'C'. If C represents 2002, then it's the 35th week of 2002.
    *   Another example: A serial number starting with "F10" could be the 10th week of the year 'F'. If F represents 2005, then it's the 10th week of 2005.

*   **Format 2 (Older/Different Manufacturers):** Some older Kenmore units or those from different manufacturers might use a simpler "YYMM" or "YYWW" format directly within the serial.
    *   **Example:** A serial number containing "0803" could mean March 2008.

It's important to look for patterns in the first 3-4 characters of your serial number. These are often the key indicators of the manufacturing date. Sometimes, it's just a direct year and week code. ### Factors Influencing Dishwasher Lifespan

Here are the main influences:

*   **Water Hardness:** Hard water can lead to mineral buildup (limescale) on internal components. This reduces efficiency and can cause parts to fail prematurely. Water softeners can mitigate this issue.
*   **Installation Quality:** Proper installation ensures the dishwasher operates as designed. Incorrect leveling or plumbing can cause stress on components. It might lead to leaks or premature wear.
*   **Quality of Parts:** While Kenmore appliances are generally reliable, the quality of specific components can vary. Some parts are more robust than others.
*   **Operating Habits:** Overloading the dishwasher or using excessive detergent can strain the machine. These habits can lead to unnecessary wear.

## Common Age-Related Issues in Older Kenmore Dishwashers

As your Kenmore Dishwasher Model 665 gets older, it is more likely to experience certain issues. These problems are often a natural consequence of wear and tear. Understanding these common age-related failures helps you anticipate repairs. It also guides your troubleshooting efforts. I have seen many of these issues manifest in older appliances. They are often fixable, but sometimes signal it's time for an upgrade.

The internal components of a dishwasher endure extreme conditions: hot water, powerful detergents, and constant agitation. Over many years, these elements take their toll. Seals dry out, motors become less efficient, and plastic parts become brittle. This breakdown is unavoidable. However, knowing what to look for can prevent small issues from becoming major headaches.

### Deterioration of Seals and Hoses

One of the most frequent problems in aging dishwashers is the deterioration of rubber and plastic components. These include door gaskets, pump seals, and water inlet/drain hoses. Over time, heat, detergents, and constant exposure to water cause these materials to stiffen, crack, or lose their elasticity.

Symptoms of failing seals and hoses include:

*   **Leaks:** Water seeping from around the door, underneath the dishwasher, or from connections. This is the most obvious sign.
*   **Poor Wash Performance:** If a door gasket fails, water pressure inside the tub can drop. This affects spray arm effectiveness.
*   **Unusual Odors:** Mildew or mold can grow in cracked or compromised seals.

Replacing these seals and hoses can be a straightforward repair. However, frequent failures might indicate a broader aging problem. Make sure to regularly inspect these areas.

### Motor and Pump Wear

The motor and pump assembly are the heart of your Kenmore Dishwasher Model 665. They circulate water and drain it out. After years of operation, these components naturally wear down. Bearings can seize, impellers can corrode, or motors can lose power.

Signs of motor or pump issues include:

*   **Loud Noises:** Grinding, humming, or squealing sounds during wash or drain cycles. This often points to worn bearings or debris in the pump.
*   **Incomplete Draining:** Water remaining in the bottom of the tub after a cycle. This indicates a weak or failing drain pump. If your [Kenmore dishwasher does not drain](https://homeessentialsguide.com/why-does-my-kenmore-dishwasher-not-drain), the pump is a prime suspect.
*   **Poor Water Spray:** Weak water pressure from the spray arms. This affects cleaning performance and can be due to a failing wash pump.
*   **Dishwasher Not Starting:** A completely failed motor will prevent the dishwasher from starting any cycle.

Motor and pump replacements can be costly. They are often a tipping point when deciding between repair and replacement.

### Heating Element Failure

The heating element ensures your water reaches the proper temperature for effective cleaning. It also dries your dishes. Over time, especially with hard water or frequent high-temperature cycles, the heating element can burn out or corrode.

Symptoms of a failing heating element include:

*   **Dishes Not Drying:** Dishes remain wet or damp after a full cycle.
*   **Cold Water Wash:** If the water is not heated, dishes will not get properly sanitized or cleaned.
*   **Error Codes:** Some newer models might display a specific error code for a heating element failure.
*   **Burn Marks:** In rare cases, visible burn marks on the element or around it.

A heating element replacement is usually a moderate cost repair. It is a common fix for older dishwashers.

### Electronic Control Board Problems

The control board is the brain of your Kenmore 665. It manages all functions, from cycle selection to water levels and drying times. Older electronics can develop glitches, cold solder joints, or complete failures. Power surges can also damage these boards.

Signs of control board issues include:

*   **Intermittent Operation:** Cycles starting and stopping unexpectedly.
*   **Buttons Not Responding:** Control panel buttons or touchpads failing to work.
*   **Incorrect Cycle Behavior:** Dishwasher performing actions out of sequence or getting stuck.
*   **Error Codes:** Many control board issues trigger specific diagnostic error codes.

Replacing a control board can be one of the most expensive repairs. Often, this cost makes replacement a more sensible option. This is especially true if the dishwasher is already quite old.

## Maintenance Tips for Your Aging Kenmore Dishwasher 665

Extending the life of your Kenmore Dishwasher Model 665, especially as it ages, relies heavily on consistent maintenance. Proper care can prevent many common age-related issues. It also helps your machine operate efficiently for longer. I have found that a little effort regularly goes a long way in appliance longevity. Think of it as preventative medicine for your dishwasher.

Ignoring maintenance allows mineral buildup, food debris, and grease to accumulate. These can clog spray arms, strain the pump, and create foul odors. A clean and well-maintained dishwasher works better. It also lasts longer. It is a win-win situation.

### Regular Cleaning of Filters and Spray Arms

One of the most important maintenance tasks is regularly cleaning the dishwasher's filter. The filter traps food particles, preventing them from recirculating onto your dishes or clogging the drain pump. Over time, this filter can become severely blocked.

Here's how to maintain your filter and spray arms:

1.  **Locate the Filter:** Most Kenmore Dishwasher Model 665 units have a removable filter assembly at the bottom of the tub. It often twists out. For specific instructions, you might find guidance here: [How to Clean Kenmore Dishwasher Filter 665](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-clean-kenmore-dishwasher-filter-665).
2.  **Remove and Rinse:** Twist and lift out the filter. Rinse it under warm running water to remove all food debris. Use an old toothbrush if needed for stubborn particles.
3.  **Clean Spray Arms:** Check the holes in the spray arms (the rotating arms that spray water). Food particles can get stuck in these small holes. Use a toothpick or small wire to clear any blockages. This ensures water sprays evenly.
4.  **Reinstall:** Securely replace the filter and spray arms. Ensure they click into place correctly.

I recommend cleaning the filter at least once a month, or more frequently if you wash heavily soiled dishes.

### Deep Cleaning and Descaling

Beyond basic filter cleaning, your Kenmore 665 benefits from periodic deep cleaning and descaling. This helps remove grease buildup, hard water deposits, and odors from the internal components.

Follow these steps for a thorough clean:

*   **Empty the Dishwasher:** Make sure the dishwasher is empty of dishes.
*   **Vinegar Wash:** Place a cup of white vinegar in a dishwasher-safe bowl on the top rack. Run a hot water cycle without detergent. The vinegar helps to break down grease and hard water scale.
*   **Baking Soda Refresh:** Sprinkle a cup of baking soda on the bottom of the tub. Run a short, hot cycle. Baking soda helps neutralize odors.
*   **Specialized Cleaners:** Consider using a commercial dishwasher cleaner once every few months. These products are formulated to tackle tough grease and mineral deposits.
*   **Clean the Door Gasket:** Wipe down the rubber door gasket with a damp cloth. This prevents mold and mildew buildup. Pay attention to all the folds and crevices.

These deeper cleaning routines can significantly improve your dishwasher's performance. They also prolong the life of parts like the heating element and pump.

### Checking for Leaks and Damage

Regular visual inspections can catch small issues before they become major problems. It's a simple habit that can save you a lot of trouble.

Here's what to look for:

*   **Inspect Hoses:** Periodically check the water inlet and drain hoses behind and under the dishwasher. Look for cracks, kinks, or signs of leaks. Replace any damaged hoses promptly.
*   **Door Gasket:** Ensure the door gasket is flexible and free of cracks. If it feels brittle or shows signs of wear, consider replacing it. A faulty gasket can lead to leaks.
*   **Racks and Wheels:** Check the dish racks for rust or damage to the plastic coating. Rust can spread. Replace damaged wheels so racks glide smoothly.
*   **Beneath the Dishwasher:** Occasionally check under the dishwasher for any signs of moisture. A small leak can grow into a large one.

By following these maintenance tips, you can keep your Kenmore Dishwasher Model 665 running effectively for many years. You will save money on repairs. You will also get cleaner dishes.
