### Removing the Old Kenmore Dishwasher Rack Adjuster

The first major step to install a Kenmore dishwasher rack adjuster is removing the old, broken one. This requires careful attention. Start by pulling the upper rack all the way out. Many Kenmore models have clips or stoppers at the end of the rack rails. You need to remove these to detach the rack. These clips prevent the rack from sliding out completely. Gently pry them off using a flathead screwdriver. Sometimes, they just slide or unclip.

Once the stoppers are off, you can slide the entire upper rack out of the dishwasher tub. Place it on a clean, flat surface, like a counter or table. This gives you a stable workspace. Now, examine the existing rack adjuster. It is usually located at each side of the upper rack, where the rack connects to the rails. You will see a plastic assembly. This assembly typically includes a wheel, a lever, and a housing.

My next move is to locate the fasteners. These often consist of small plastic tabs, screws, or clips. Use your Phillips head screwdriver to remove any screws. For plastic tabs, you might need to gently pry them open with a flathead screwdriver or use needle-nose pliers to squeeze them. Be careful not to break the plastic tabs on the rack itself. You only want to release the adjuster. The old adjuster assembly should then come apart or detach from the rack. Carefully note how the old one was assembled. Take pictures with your phone if it helps you remember the orientation. This makes reassembly much easier. Ensure all fragments of the old, damaged part are cleared away from the rack.

### Step-by-Step Guide to Install Your New Kenmore Rack Adjuster

Now we get to the core part: installing the new Kenmore dishwasher rack adjuster. With the old adjuster removed and your upper rack on a stable surface, you can begin. I always compare the new parts to the old ones first. Make sure everything matches. This ensures you have the correct replacement. Each side of the upper rack requires an adjuster. The process is the same for both.

First, identify the main housing of the new adjuster. This is the largest plastic piece. It typically has slots or pegs that fit onto the wire frame of the dishwasher rack. Position it correctly. Ensure the adjuster's wheel assembly faces the front or back, depending on your model. Some adjusters have specific left and right-hand components. Check your part's instructions or the markings on the adjuster itself. It is a critical step to avoid future issues.

Next, carefully push the adjuster housing onto the rack's wire frame. It should snap into place. You might hear a click. If there are screws, secure them now. Do not overtighten plastic screws. They can strip easily. After securing the main housing, attach any smaller components. These include levers, clips, or springs. Ensure these parts move freely. They are essential for the adjustment mechanism. Make sure the wheels are properly seated in their tracks. Proper alignment is key for smooth operation.

Repeat this process for the other side of the upper rack. Double-check that both adjusters are firmly attached. Ensure they are correctly oriented. A properly installed adjuster will feel secure. It will not wobble excessively. This careful installation ensures the rack glides smoothly. It also holds its position correctly. ### Reattaching the Dishwasher Rack and Testing Adjuster Functionality

With your new Kenmore dishwasher rack adjusters firmly installed on the upper rack, it is time to put everything back together. This step is straightforward. It should glide smoothly along the rails. If it catches, recheck the alignment of the wheels and rails. Sometimes, the rack needs a little jiggle to seat properly.

Once the rack is mostly in, reattach the rail stoppers. These are the small clips or caps you removed earlier. They prevent the rack from coming out too far. If one side sticks or does not adjust, check its installation. Ensure all components are correctly seated. Smooth operation confirms a successful installation. ### Troubleshooting Common Issues After Rack Adjuster Installation

Even after a careful installation, you might encounter minor issues. I find that troubleshooting is part of any DIY project. One common problem is the rack not sliding smoothly. If the upper rack feels stiff or grinds, check the wheels. Make sure they are perfectly aligned with the dishwasher rails. Sometimes, a wheel gets dislodged during reinstallation. Ensure no debris obstructs the rails. A quick check of the track can reveal small food particles.

Another issue is the rack not locking into position. Or it might be uneven. This usually points to the adjuster mechanism itself. First, check that the adjustment levers are fully engaged. They might be partially depressed. Next, examine the internal components of the adjuster. Are all springs correctly seated? Are any plastic tabs broken or bent? Sometimes, a new part can be defective. Or, it might not be installed with the correct alignment. You might need to gently adjust its position on the rack. This often fixes the problem.

If the rack feels wobbly, confirm all screws and clips are secure. The adjuster housing must be tight on the rack's wire frame. A loose connection causes instability. I always recommend gently shaking the rack to check for looseness. Make sure both adjusters are identical and installed in the same manner. An incorrectly installed adjuster on one side can cause an uneven rack. This can also lead to the rack failing to adjust properly. Addressing these common problems quickly helps maintain your Kenmore dishwasher's performance. It extends the life of your new components. For specific guidance on similar issues, you might refer to resources like [How To Install Top Rack Of GE Dishwasher](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-install-top-rack-of-ge-dishwasher).

*   **Common Troubleshooting Tips:**
    *   **Rack Not Sliding:** Check wheel alignment on rails. Clear any debris from the tracks. Ensure wheels are not dislodged.
    *   **Rack Not Locking/Uneven:** Verify adjustment levers are fully engaged. Inspect springs and internal tabs within the adjuster for proper seating. Ensure both adjusters are installed identically.
    *   **Rack Feels Wobbly:** Confirm all screws and clips securing the adjuster housing to the rack are tight. Gently test for looseness and tighten as needed.
    *   **One Side Sticks:** Compare the installation of both adjusters. Ensure no part is catching or misaligned on the problematic side.

### FAQ Section

#### Q1: How do I know my Kenmore dishwasher rack adjuster is broken?
You will notice your upper rack sagging, failing to adjust height, or detaching from the rails. The rack may also feel wobbly or refuse to slide smoothly. Visual inspection might reveal cracks in the plastic housing, broken clips, or worn-out wheels on the adjuster assembly. These are clear signs it needs replacement.

#### Q2: Can I use a universal rack adjuster for my Kenmore dishwasher?
I advise against using a universal rack adjuster. Kenmore dishwashers often require specific parts designed for their models. Universal adjusters may not fit correctly, function properly, or last as long. Always use genuine Kenmore replacement parts or verified aftermarket parts made for your specific model number. This ensures compatibility and durability.

#### Q3: What tools do I need to replace a Kenmore rack adjuster?
You typically need basic hand tools. A Phillips head screwdriver is essential. A flathead screwdriver can help pry open clips. Needle-nose pliers are useful for small, stubborn parts. Work gloves protect your hands. Having a small bowl or towel for minor water drips is also a good idea.

#### Q4: Is it hard to replace a dishwasher rack adjuster?
Replacing a dishwasher rack adjuster is a moderately easy DIY task. It requires patience and following instructions. Most people with basic mechanical skills can complete it in under an hour. The most challenging parts are ensuring correct orientation and securely fastening small clips. Proper preparation makes the job much simpler.

#### Q5: How long does it take to install a Kenmore dishwasher rack adjuster?
The installation typically takes about 30 to 60 minutes. This includes time for safety preparations, removing the old part, installing the new one, and testing. If you are organized and have the right tools, it often goes quicker. First-timers might take a bit longer, but it is still a manageable project.
