## How to Reset Your Kenmore Refrigerator Water Filter Light

That blinking light on your Kenmore refrigerator can be a bit of an annoyance. It is a reminder that your water filter needs attention. This light signals it is time to change the filter and reset the system. Keeping up with this simple task is important for clean water. It also helps your appliance run well.

In this guide, I will walk you through the process. We will cover why the light comes on and how to reset Kenmore refrigerator water filter light. I will explain steps for different Kenmore models. We will also look at what to do if the light does not turn off. My goal is to make this process easy for you. Let us get your refrigerator back to dispensing fresh, clean water.

### Takeaway

*   Replace your water filter before attempting to reset the light.
*   Locate the "Filter," "Reset," or "Water Filter" button on your Kenmore refrigerator's control panel.
*   Press and hold this button for 3-5 seconds until the filter light turns off.
*   If the light persists, check your refrigerator's user manual for specific model instructions.
*   A power cycle can sometimes resolve stubborn filter light issues.

To reset your Kenmore refrigerator water filter light, first replace the old filter with a new one. Then, find the "Filter," "Reset," or "Water Filter" button on your refrigerator's control panel. Press and hold this button for about three to five seconds until the indicator light goes out. This action signals to the refrigerator that a fresh filter is in place, and the monitoring system resets.

## Troubleshooting: When the Kenmore Refrigerator Water Filter Light Won't Reset

Sometimes, despite your best efforts, the Kenmore refrigerator water filter light remains on. This can be frustrating, but common solutions exist. Do not give up if the light persists after your first attempt. I have seen this issue many times, and it is usually fixable.

First, double-check that you are pressing the correct button. Some Kenmore models have multiple buttons on the panel. Ensure you are pressing the one specifically for the water filter reset. Verify you are holding it long enough, typically 3 to 5 seconds. A quick tap is not enough for most models.

Next, confirm the filter is installed correctly. A loosely fitted or incorrect filter might prevent the system from registering it. Remove the filter and reinsert it, ensuring it clicks securely into place. Sometimes, air trapped in the lines after a filter change can also cause issues. Dispense a few gallons of water to purge any air bubbles. This also helps flush out any carbon dust from the new filter.

If the light still won't go off, try a power cycle. Unplug your Kenmore refrigerator from the wall outlet for a few minutes, usually 5 to 10 minutes. Then, plug it back in. This hard reset can clear any electronic glitches preventing the light from turning off. After the power cycle, try the filter reset button again.

In rare cases, a faulty control board or sensor might be the problem. If all troubleshooting steps fail, it might be time to consult your Kenmore owner's manual for specific diagnostics or contact customer support. ## Troubleshooting Common Water Dispenser Issues After Filter Change

Sometimes, after you reset the Kenmore refrigerator water filter light, you might still face issues. The water dispenser might not work right. Or, the ice maker could be slow. These problems are often linked to the filter change process. I have learned to troubleshoot these common hiccups over time.

One frequent issue is slow water flow. After installing a new filter, air gets trapped in the lines. This air pockets make the water flow trickle out. To fix this, you need to flush the system. Dispense about two to three gallons of water through the dispenser. This pushes out the trapped air. The flow should then become strong and steady.

Another problem can be water spurting or sputtering. This also indicates air in the lines. The solution is the same: keep dispensing water. You might hear bubbling sounds as the air escapes. Continue until the water flows smoothly and without interruption. This ensures the water system is fully primed.

If water still does not dispense, check your water supply line. Ensure the shut-off valve behind the refrigerator is fully open. A partially closed valve can restrict water flow. Also, inspect the water line for kinks or bends. These can block water from reaching the filter or dispenser. This kind of problem can be very frustrating.

Finally, verify the new filter is properly seated. A filter that is not fully locked in place can prevent water flow. Remove the filter and reinsert it with a firm push and twist. Listen for a click, which indicates it is secure. Once fixed, try the water dispenser again. These steps usually resolve most post-filter change dispenser problems.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Why is my Kenmore filter light still on after changing the filter?
If your Kenmore filter light remains on after changing the filter, it means the system has not been reset. You must manually reset the light. Locate the "Filter," "Reset," or "Water Filter" button on your refrigerator's control panel. Press and hold it for 3-5 seconds until the light turns off. Ensure the new filter is properly installed, as a loose filter can also prevent the light from resetting.

### How often should I change my Kenmore refrigerator water filter?
Kenmore recommends changing your refrigerator water filter every six months. This period ensures optimal filtration performance and fresh-tasting water. However, if you have hard water or high water usage, you might need to replace it more frequently. The filter light usually comes on around the six-month mark to remind you.

### Can I use my Kenmore refrigerator without a water filter?
While it is technically possible to use your Kenmore refrigerator without a water filter, it is not recommended. The bypass plug must be in place if you remove the filter. Using it without a filter or bypass can lead to leaks or damage to the water system. More importantly, your water will not be filtered, affecting taste and purity.

### What if my Kenmore refrigerator does not have a filter reset button?
Some older Kenmore models or specific basic designs might not have a dedicated filter reset button. In such cases, a power cycle often acts as the reset mechanism. Unplug the refrigerator from the wall outlet for about 5-10 minutes. Then, plug it back in. This usually clears the filter light, assuming a new filter is installed.

### Does the filter light affect water dispensing?
The filter light itself does not directly affect water dispensing. It is simply an indicator. However, the condition it signals—a clogged or old filter—can significantly impact water flow and taste. An old filter can reduce water pressure, slow dispensing, and cause poor water quality. Resetting the light and replacing the filter are crucial for proper function.

### Where can I find the correct filter for my Kenmore refrigerator?
You can find the correct water filter for your Kenmore refrigerator by checking your owner's manual. The manual lists the specific part number needed. Alternatively, look for a label inside your refrigerator, usually in the fresh food compartment, which may include the model number. Online retailers and appliance parts stores also offer compatibility guides based on your Kenmore model.
