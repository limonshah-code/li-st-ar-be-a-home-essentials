## Bosch Dishwasher Flashing and Not Starting? Here's Why.

Few things disrupt a routine more than a broken appliance. You load your dishes, press start, and then your Bosch dishwasher is flashing and not starting. This situation can be frustrating. Flashing lights on your Bosch dishwasher often signal a problem. These lights are simple codes the dishwasher uses to communicate an issue.

I understand this problem well. Many Bosch owners encounter this exact scenario. This article will help you understand common reasons for this behavior. We will cover various components and simple fixes. You will learn how to troubleshoot your Bosch dishwasher. We aim to get your appliance working again quickly.

### Takeaway

If your Bosch dishwasher is flashing and not starting, check these key areas:

*   **Power and Door Latch:** Ensure the dishwasher has power and the door closes completely.
*   **Water Supply and Drain:** Verify water flow and clear any drain clogs.
*   **Flood Protection:** Reset the float switch if water collects in the base.
*   **Control Board/Motor:** These may need professional repair if other steps fail.
*   **Error Codes:** Identify specific codes for precise troubleshooting.

Your Bosch dishwasher flashes and does not start when it detects an issue preventing safe operation. This often points to problems with the door latch, water supply, drainage, or an internal fault like a faulty flood switch or control board. Addressing these common areas helps restore function.

### Understanding Bosch Dishwasher Flashing Lights

When your Bosch dishwasher is flashing and not starting, it usually signals an error. Bosch dishwashers have intelligent control systems. These systems detect malfunctions. They use flashing lights or display error codes to show the problem. This feature helps you diagnose the issue.

Each sequence of flashes can mean something specific. For example, some models flash when the door is not fully closed. Others flash when they cannot drain water. Knowing what these flashes mean is your first step to a fix. Always check your dishwasher's user manual. The manual provides a list of error codes and their meanings. This specific information helps you troubleshoot accurately. Many issues are simple to resolve.

### Checking Power Supply and Door Latch

Your Bosch dishwasher needs stable power to operate. A loose plug or tripped circuit breaker stops it from starting. Check the power outlet. Ensure the dishwasher plug is pushed in all the way. Sometimes, the circuit breaker for your kitchen may trip. Go to your home's electrical panel. Find the breaker labeled for your dishwasher. Flip it completely off, then back on. This simple reset can resolve many power-related issues.

The door latch is another common culprit. Your Bosch dishwasher will not start if the door is not shut correctly. The dishwasher has a safety switch. This switch detects if the door is latched. If the door is even slightly ajar, the dishwasher will flash and not start. Make sure the door closes firmly. You should hear a click when it latches properly. Inspect the latch itself. Look for any debris or damage that prevents it from engaging fully. Clean the latch area if you see any build-up. Sometimes, the latch mechanism itself breaks. If the latch is broken, it needs replacement. You can often find replacement parts online. This ensures the door switch activates correctly.

### Addressing Water Supply and Drainage Issues

Water supply and drainage are crucial for your Bosch dishwasher. If your Bosch dishwasher is flashing and not starting, these are key areas to check. First, ensure the water supply valve is open. This valve is usually under the sink. Turn it fully counter-clockwise. Check the water inlet hose for kinks or blockages. A kinked hose restricts water flow. This prevents the dishwasher from filling. The dishwasher needs water to start its cycle.

Drainage problems also stop the dishwasher. A clogged drain hose prevents dirty water from leaving the tub. This can trigger a fault. Check the drain hose for kinks. Make sure it connects properly to your sink drain or garbage disposal. Food particles can block the drain filter inside the dishwasher. This filter traps debris. It needs regular cleaning. Remove the lower rack and spray arm. Then, twist and lift the filter assembly. Rinse it under running water to clear any food bits. If your Bosch dishwasher has [water in the bottom](https://homeessentialsguide.com/why-does-my-bosch-dishwasher-have-water-in-the-bottom), it points to a drain issue. You might also want to [run a clean cycle on your Bosch dishwasher](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-run-clean-cycle-on-bosch-dishwasher) to prevent future clogs. These steps help ensure proper water circulation.

### Inspecting the Flood Protection System

Bosch dishwashers include a flood protection system. This system prevents water leaks onto your floor. It has a float switch located in the dishwasher's base. If water collects in the base, this float rises. The rising float activates a switch. This switch then stops the dishwasher from running. It also signals an error, causing the dishwasher to flash and not start. This is a safety feature.

To check this system, pull the dishwasher out slightly. Look underneath for any visible water. You may need to tilt the dishwasher gently to drain this water. A small amount of water in the base can trigger the switch. After draining, reset the dishwasher. This often clears the flood protection error. Sometimes, the float switch itself becomes stuck. It might get stuck in the "up" position. This signals a flood when no water is present. You can gently push the float down to ensure it moves freely. If water constantly appears in the base, you have a leak. This leak needs repair to prevent future issues. This system is key to preventing water damage.

### Troubleshooting the Control Board and Motor

The control board acts as the brain of your Bosch dishwasher. It sends signals to all components. If this board malfunctions, your Bosch dishwasher can start flashing and not starting. A faulty control board might send incorrect commands. It might not send any commands at all. This leads to the dishwasher freezing or refusing to begin a cycle. Sometimes, a power surge can damage the control board. You might see strange light patterns. The display could act erratically.

The wash motor powers the spray arms. If the motor fails, the dishwasher cannot clean dishes. This failure often triggers an error code. The dishwasher will flash lights to indicate a problem. A humming sound without any water circulation suggests a motor issue. You may also notice no water pressure in the spray arms. A professional technician usually needs to replace the control board or wash motor. These are complex parts. They require specific tools and knowledge for safe replacement. Before calling a technician, try a hard reset of your dishwasher. Disconnect the power for a few minutes. Then reconnect it. This can sometimes clear minor glitches in the control board. If your Bosch dishwasher is [stuck on 1 minute](https://homeessentialsguide.com/why-does-my-bosch-dishwasher-get-stuck-on-1-minute), it might also be a control board issue.

### Understanding Bosch Dishwasher Error Codes

Bosch dishwashers often display specific error codes. These codes provide precise information. They tell you why your Bosch dishwasher is flashing and not starting. The display panel shows numbers or letters. For example, E24 often means a drain issue. E25 also indicates a drain pump problem. E15 commonly signals a flood protection activation. Other codes relate to water supply, heating, or sensor faults.

Consult your Bosch dishwasher's user manual. It contains a comprehensive list of error codes. Each code has a suggested troubleshooting step. If you do not have the manual, look online. Bosch's official website provides digital copies. Knowing the exact error code saves time. It guides your repair efforts directly. It prevents guesswork. Some codes point to simple fixes. Other codes indicate a need for professional service. Always record the code before resetting the dishwasher. This helps if you need to call support.

### Examining the Water Inlet Valve and Heater

The water inlet valve controls water flow into the dishwasher. If this valve fails, your Bosch dishwasher will not fill. This causes the dishwasher to flash and not start. The valve can become clogged with mineral deposits. It can also fail electrically. You might hear a humming sound but no water entering. Check the water pressure at your tap first. Low water pressure can mimic a faulty valve. If your Bosch dishwasher keeps [filling up with water](https://homeessentialsguide.com/why-does-my-bosch-dishwasher-keep-filling-up-with-water) even when off, the inlet valve is likely stuck open.

The heating element heats the water for washing and drying. While not directly stopping the dishwasher from starting, a faulty heater can prevent cycles from completing. This might cause the dishwasher to stop mid-cycle or display an error. Your Bosch dishwasher [heats its own water](https://homeessentialsguide.com/does-bosch-dishwasher-heat-its-own-water) using this component. If the water stays cold, the heating element might be bad. This can cause errors and flashing lights. It might also prevent the dishwasher from advancing to the wash cycle. Testing these components requires a multimeter. Replacing them usually needs professional help.

### Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Why do my Bosch dishwasher lights keep flashing?**
A: Flashing lights on your Bosch dishwasher signal an error. This can be due to an open door, a water supply issue, a drain problem, or a sensor fault. Consult your user manual for specific error code meanings. Each flash pattern or code points to a particular issue.

**Q: How do I hard reset my Bosch dishwasher?**
A: To hard reset your Bosch dishwasher, first unplug it from the power outlet. Alternatively, trip the circuit breaker for 5-10 minutes. Then, restore power. This can clear minor electronic glitches. Wait a moment, then try starting a cycle again.

**Q: What does E24 mean on a Bosch dishwasher?**
A: An E24 error code on a Bosch dishwasher typically indicates a drainage problem. This means the dishwasher cannot pump out water. Common causes include a clogged drain filter, a kinked drain hose, or a faulty drain pump. Check these components first.

**Q: My Bosch dishwasher has power but won't start. Why?**
A: If your Bosch dishwasher has power but won't start, check the door latch first. The door must be completely closed and latched. Also, check the flood protection switch in the base. It might be tripped. A faulty control board or motor can also prevent starting.

**Q: Can I fix a Bosch dishwasher error code myself?**
A: Many Bosch dishwasher error codes indicate simple issues you can fix. These include clearing clogged filters or ensuring water supply. More complex codes, especially those related to control boards or motors, often require a professional technician. Always refer to your user manual first.

**Q: What if my Bosch dishwasher flashes an "AquaStop" error?**
A: An "AquaStop" error means the flood protection system is active. This typically indicates water has collected in the base of the dishwasher. You need to find and fix the leak. After fixing the leak and draining the water, the error should clear.
