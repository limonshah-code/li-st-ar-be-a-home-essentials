## Understanding the "Made in USA" Label for Appliances

The "Made in USA" label can be confusing. The Federal Trade Commission (FTC) sets rules for this claim. For a product to be "Made in USA," it must be "all or virtually all" made in the United States. This means all significant parts and processing must be of US origin.

For complex products like refrigerators, this standard is hard to meet. A refrigerator has thousands of components. These include compressors, control boards, shelves, and door seals. Sourcing all these parts from within the USA is challenging. Many appliance manufacturers use global supply chains.

Most appliances claiming "Made in USA" are "assembled in USA." This means the final product is put together in an American factory. However, internal components may come from other countries. LG's Clarksville plant fits this model. It assembles refrigerators using both US-sourced and globally-sourced parts.

When you see an LG refrigerator made in USA, it often means the final assembly occurred here. This supports US jobs and contributes to the local economy. For consumers, it means the product passed final quality checks on American soil. This distinction is important for understanding product origins.
