## Discover Your KitchenAid Dishwasher Model Number

Finding the model number for your KitchenAid dishwasher is simple. Many people need this information for various reasons. You might want to order parts or schedule service. ### Takeaway

Finding your KitchenAid dishwasher's model number is easy. Look in these main spots:

*   **Door Frame:** Check the inner edges, top, or sides of the dishwasher door.
*   **Kick Plate:** Inspect the panel at the bottom front of the appliance.
*   **Documentation:** Review your owner's manual or purchase receipts.
*   **KitchenAid Website:** Use online lookup tools with your serial number if available.

Knowing your model number ensures accurate service and part ordering for your KitchenAid dishwasher.

Your KitchenAid dishwasher model number is typically on a sticker or data plate. You will find it most often on the inner edge of the dishwasher door, usually on the right or left side. Other possible spots include the kick plate at the bottom front, or the back of the appliance. Check your owner's manual or KitchenAid's website if physical labels are missing.

## Inside the Door Frame: The Most Common Location

The inside of the dishwasher door frame is the primary place to look. This is the spot KitchenAid typically places the model number label. It is often a sticker or a small metal plate. The label will contain both the model number and the serial number. These numbers are vital for different needs.

Open your dishwasher door completely. Look along the edges of the door. The label is usually on the right or left side. Sometimes, it is on the top edge of the door. It might also be on the inner tub frame, where the door seals shut. This location keeps the label protected from spills and daily wear.

You might need to wipe down the area first. Food particles or grime can hide the label. Use a damp cloth to clean the surface. Be gentle not to damage the sticker. Once clean, the numbers should be clear. They are often printed in a bold font.

## Behind the Kick Plate: Another Key Spot

If you cannot find the label inside the door, check behind the kick plate. The kick plate is a removable panel. It sits at the very bottom front of your KitchenAid dishwasher. This panel usually matches the color and finish of your appliance. It hides components like the motor and electrical wiring.

To access this area, you often need a screwdriver. Most kick plates are held in place by two or four screws. They are usually Phillips head screws. Some models might use clips instead. First, ensure your dishwasher is not running. It is also good practice to unplug the appliance from the wall outlet for safety.

Carefully remove the screws. Then, gently pull the kick plate forward and down. The label could be on the back of the kick plate itself. More commonly, it is on the frame of the dishwasher behind the kick plate. Look at the lower right or left side of the dishwasher's frame. It might be on a small metal bracket or directly on the frame.

The label here might be less clean than inside the door. Dust and debris can accumulate in this area. Bring a flashlight to help you see clearly. Wipe the area if necessary. Once you find the label, note both the model and serial numbers. Replace the kick plate and screws when you finish. This location is a reliable backup spot for many KitchenAid models. ## Understanding KitchenAid Model and Serial Numbers

It is important to understand the difference between a model number and a serial number. Both are on the same label for your KitchenAid dishwasher. However, they serve different purposes. Knowing what each number represents helps you use them correctly.

A model number identifies the specific design or version of your appliance. Think of it like a product code. It tells you the type of dishwasher you have. For example, all KitchenAid KDTM404ESS dishwashers are the same model. They share the same parts, features, and specifications. When you need a replacement part or a specific manual, the model number is crucial. It ensures compatibility and accuracy.

The serial number is unique to your individual appliance. It is like your dishwasher's unique identification number. No two dishwashers, even of the same model, have the same serial number. This number tracks where and when the appliance was manufactured. It also helps with warranty claims and product recalls. When you register your product or need specific service history, the serial number is used.

Both numbers are usually a combination of letters and digits. They are often printed close to each other on the same label. Sometimes, the model number might be shorter and simpler. The serial number can be longer and more complex. Always write down both numbers when you find them. They are both valuable for different reasons. Keep them safe for future reference.
