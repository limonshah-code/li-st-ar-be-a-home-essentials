### Signs It's Time for a New Filter

## Understanding LG Dishwasher Filter Types

LG dishwashers typically use a two-part filter system. This system includes a cylindrical filter and a flat mesh filter. Both work together to trap food particles. Understanding each part helps you maintain your appliance better. It also makes replacement easier.

The cylindrical filter is usually in the center. It looks like a small cup with a handle. This filter captures larger food scraps. You can twist it to remove it. Below this, there is often a flat mesh filter. This part catches smaller particles. It prevents them from re-entering the water stream.

Some older LG models might have self-cleaning filters. These models grind up food particles. They then wash them away. However, most modern LG dishwashers use manual filters. These manual filters need regular cleaning or replacement. Checking your owner's manual helps confirm your specific filter type. This information is important for proper maintenance.

Identifying your filter type ensures you buy the correct replacement part. It also helps you perform the right maintenance steps. Always refer to your dishwasher's model number. This ensures compatibility. A quick search online or a call to LG support can confirm the filter type and part number.

Knowing your filter type simplifies maintenance. It helps you keep your dishwasher efficient. This knowledge saves you time and effort. ### Manual Filter System vs. Self-Cleaning Filter

*   **Manual Filter System:**
    *   This system requires regular user intervention.
    *   You must remove and clean the filter components by hand.
    *   Most modern LG dishwashers use this type for better energy efficiency and quieter operation.
    *   It typically consists of a cylindrical filter and a fine mesh screen.
*   **Self-Cleaning Filter System:**
    *   This system uses a grinder to break down food particles.

    *   Older dishwasher models might feature this system.
    *   It generally requires less frequent manual cleaning.
    *   These systems tend to be noisier than manual filter systems.

## Step-by-Step Guide to Replacing Your LG Dishwasher Filter

Replacing your LG dishwasher filter is a straightforward process. Take your time and follow each step carefully. Safety is important, so always start by cutting the power. This guide helps you through the entire procedure.

### 1. Disconnect Power to the Dishwasher

Safety comes first. Before touching anything inside your dishwasher, cut its power supply. Go to your home's circuit breaker box. Find the breaker labeled for your kitchen or dishwasher. Flip it to the "off" position. This prevents any accidental electrical shocks. If you are unsure, unplug the appliance from the wall outlet. This ensures no power reaches the unit.

### 2. Remove the Lower Dish Rack

Open the dishwasher door fully. Pull out the lower dish rack. Lift it clear of the tracks. Set it aside on a clean surface. This gives you clear access to the bottom of the dishwasher. You need this space to reach the filter assembly.

### 3. Locate the Filter Assembly

Look at the bottom of the dishwasher tub. You will see a circular or cylindrical component in the center. This is your filter assembly. It usually has a handle or an arrow indicating how to twist it. Most LG dishwashers have a two-part filter. This includes an outer mesh screen and an inner cylindrical filter.

### 4. Remove the Old Filter

Grasp the cylindrical filter. Twist it counter-clockwise to unlock it. It should lift straight up. Pull it out carefully. Below this, you will often find a flat, circular mesh filter. This filter usually has tabs or slots. Lift it up to remove it from the tub. Be prepared for some water and debris. Place the old filter on a paper towel.

### 5. Clean the Filter Housing

Before putting in the new filter, clean the area. Wipe away any food particles or grime inside the filter housing. Use a soft cloth or sponge. A small brush can help reach tight spots. Make sure the area is clean and free of debris. This ensures the new filter fits properly. It also prevents new clogs.

### 6. Install the New Filter

Take your new LG dishwasher filter. Insert the flat mesh filter first if your model has one. Make sure it sits flat and aligns with the slots. Then, take the new cylindrical filter. Lower it into the opening. Align the tabs or arrows on the filter with the slots in the housing. Push down gently. Twist it clockwise until it locks securely into place. You should feel it click or stop turning firmly. This ensures it is seated correctly. More details on putting filters back can be found in [how to put dishwasher filter back in](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-put-dishwasher-filter-back-in).

### 7. Replace the Lower Dish Rack and Restore Power

Slide the lower dish rack back into its tracks. Push it all the way in. Close the dishwasher door. Go back to your circuit breaker box. Flip the breaker back to the "on" position. Your LG dishwasher is now ready for use.

### 8. Run a Test Cycle

Run a short, empty cycle. This helps confirm the new filter is working. It also flushes any remaining debris. Check for any leaks. Listen for unusual noises. If everything seems normal, you have successfully replaced your LG dishwasher filter. Enjoy your clean dishes!

## Troubleshooting Common Filter-Related Issues

Even after replacing your LG dishwasher filter, you might encounter issues. These problems often stem from other parts of the system. Sometimes, they are simple oversights during installation. Knowing how to troubleshoot helps you solve these problems quickly.

One common issue is water remaining in the dishwasher tub. A full filter can cause this, but so can a clogged drain hose or a faulty drain pump. If your new filter is clean, check the drain hose for kinks or blockages. The problem might also be with the pump. If your dishwasher filter is full of water even after cleaning, you can find more information on [why is my dishwasher filter full of water](https://homeessentialsguide.com/why-is-my-dishwasher-filter-full-of-water). This provides specific advice for that situation.

Another persistent problem is dishes that are still not clean. This can happen even with a new filter. Check if you are overloading the dishwasher. Make sure the spray arms are not blocked. Ensure they can spin freely. Sometimes, using the wrong amount or type of detergent can cause this. Hard water can also leave mineral deposits. Consider using a dishwasher cleaner or a rinse aid.

Lingering odors are also frustrating. If a new filter doesn't fix it, the smell might come from other areas. Check the dishwasher's interior for trapped food particles. Clean the door gasket and spray arms. Running a hot cycle with white vinegar or baking soda can also help.

If your dishwasher makes strange noises, investigate the source. A new filter should not cause noise. The sound might come from a loose spray arm. It could also be a worn-out pump motor. Some noises indicate a more serious mechanical issue. Replacing parts like the pump motor is more complex. You can learn about it if needed by checking [how to replace LG dishwasher pump motor](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-replace-lg-dishwasher-pump-motor).

Knowing when to call a professional is important. If you have tried basic troubleshooting without success, a technician can diagnose deeper problems. They have specialized tools and expertise. This saves you time and prevents potential damage to your appliance.

### Common Troubleshooting Steps

*   **Check for Blockages:** Look for food particles or debris in the spray arms, drain hose, and drain pump area.
*   **Verify Detergent Use:** Ensure you are using the correct type and amount of dishwasher detergent for your water hardness.
*   **Inspect Spray Arms:** Confirm the spray arms can rotate freely. Make sure no dishes block their movement.
*   **Run a Cleaning Cycle:** Use a commercial dishwasher cleaner or a cup of white vinegar on an empty hot wash cycle.
*   **Examine Other Components:** Look for issues with the drain hose, water inlet valve, or heating element.
*   **Consult the Manual:** Your LG dishwasher manual has specific troubleshooting guides for common problems.

## FAQ Section

### How often should I replace my LG dishwasher filter?
You should typically replace your LG dishwasher filter if it becomes damaged or excessively worn. For general maintenance, it's recommended to clean the filter monthly. If you notice signs like poor cleaning, odors, or visible damage to the filter, consider replacing it immediately. Regular cleaning can extend its life.

### Can I clean my LG dishwasher filter instead of replacing it?
Yes, most LG dishwasher filters are designed for regular cleaning. You can remove the cylindrical and mesh filters, rinse them under running water, and use a soft brush with mild soap to scrub away debris. However, if the filter is cracked, broken, or severely clogged beyond cleaning, replacement is necessary.

### What happens if I don't replace my dishwasher filter?
If you do not replace a faulty dishwasher filter, food particles will recirculate. This leads to dirty dishes with stuck-on grime. You might also experience unpleasant odors from decaying food. Eventually, it can cause clogs in the drain pump or spray arms, leading to costly repairs and reduced appliance efficiency.

### Where can I find the model number for my LG dishwasher?
The model number for your LG dishwasher is usually on a sticker. You can find this sticker on the inner edge of the dishwasher door. It might also be on the side of the tub or near the controls. This number is essential for ordering the correct replacement parts.

### Do all LG dishwashers have a filter that needs replacement?
Most modern LG dishwashers use a manual filter system that needs regular cleaning and occasional replacement. Older models might have a self-cleaning filter that grinds food particles. Always check your specific model's manual to understand its filter type and maintenance requirements.
