## Make Your Runner Carpet Shine in the Bathtub

A dirty runner carpet can make your home look less inviting. These rugs often sit in high-traffic areas, collecting dirt, dust, and spills daily. Professional cleaning can be expensive. Washing your runner carpet at home, specifically in your bathtub, offers a budget-friendly and effective solution.

This method gives your rug a deep clean, removing hidden grime. It is a simple process you can do yourself. This article will show you how to clean a runner carpet in your bathtub. We will cover everything from gathering supplies to drying your rug. Follow these steps to bring your runner carpet back to life.

### Takeaway

*   **Prepare your rug:** Vacuum thoroughly and spot-treat any stains before wetting.
*   **Clean your bathtub:** Ensure your tub is spotless before putting the rug inside.
*   **Use the right solution:** Mix mild detergent with warm water in the tub.
*   **Scrub and rinse:** Gently scrub the rug, then rinse completely to remove all soap.
*   **Dry properly:** Squeeze out excess water and air dry your runner carpet fully to prevent mildew.

Cleaning a runner carpet in your bathtub involves vacuuming, spot-treating, soaking it in warm water with a mild detergent, and scrubbing gently. Rinse it thoroughly to remove all soap. Finally, squeeze out excess water and hang it to air dry completely.

## Pre-Cleaning Steps: Preparing Your Runner Carpet and Tub

Before you soak your runner carpet, some preparation steps are very important. These steps ensure a successful and effective clean. Skipping them can lead to less-than-perfect results or even damage. We start by preparing both the carpet and the bathtub itself.

### Preparing Your Runner Carpet

Begin by giving your runner carpet a thorough vacuuming. Use your vacuum cleaner to remove all loose dirt, dust, and debris. Pass over the rug multiple times, going in different directions. This lifts deeply embedded particles. If your vacuum has attachments, use them for edges and corners. This dry vacuuming prevents mud from forming when the carpet gets wet. It makes the actual washing much easier.

Next, check for any specific stains. Spot-treat these stains before soaking the entire rug. Apply a small amount of stain remover designed for carpets to the affected area. Gently blot the stain with a clean cloth. Do not rub, as this can spread the stain. Let the spot treatment sit for a few minutes as directed by the product.

Perform a colorfastness test on an inconspicuous area of the rug. Apply a tiny bit of your chosen cleaning solution. Blot with a white cloth. If color transfers to the cloth, choose a different, milder cleaning solution. This step prevents discoloration of your runner carpet.

### Cleaning Your Bathtub First

Your bathtub will be your cleaning basin. It needs to be sparkling clean before you put your rug inside. A dirty tub can transfer grime back onto your freshly cleaned carpet. Take a few minutes to scrub your bathtub clean. Use your regular bathroom cleaner. Pay attention to the sides and bottom. You want a spotless surface for your rug.

If your tub is particularly dirty, you might need a deeper clean. ## The Bathtub Cleaning Process: Washing Your Runner Carpet

Now that your runner carpet and bathtub are ready, it is time for the main event. Washing your runner carpet in the tub is a straightforward process. Follow these steps for a deep and effective clean. You will see a big difference in your rug.

### Soaking Your Runner Carpet

Carefully place your vacuumed and spot-treated runner carpet into the clean bathtub. Make sure it lays as flat as possible. Begin filling the tub with warm water. Use enough water to completely submerge the carpet. Hot water works best for breaking down dirt and grime. Add your chosen cleaning solution to the water. If using liquid laundry detergent, a few capfuls are usually enough. If you are washing other items in your tub, like learning [how to clean pillows in bathtub](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-clean-pillows-in-bathtub), the principle of soaking is similar.

Allow the carpet to soak for at least 15-30 minutes. For very dirty rugs, you can let it soak for an hour or even longer. Soaking helps loosen embedded dirt. You will likely see the water turn murky quickly. This shows the cleaning solution is working.

### Scrubbing the Rug

After soaking, it is time to scrub. Put on your rubber gloves. Use your stiff-bristled brush to gently scrub the carpet fibers. Work in sections, paying extra attention to high-traffic areas and any remaining stains. Scrub with the nap of the carpet, then against it. This helps to lift dirt from all angles. Do not scrub too aggressively, as this can damage the fibers. If your bathtub is a delicate material, like plastic, be mindful of your scrubbing tools. Knowing [how to clean plastic bathtub](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-clean-plastic-bathtub) helps keep your tub safe during cleaning projects.

Once you have scrubbed the entire rug, gently push down on it. This helps to squeeze out dirty water. You can roll up sections of the rug and press down to expel more water. Repeat the scrubbing and squeezing until the water looks clearer.

## Rinsing and Draining: Removing All Soap from Your Carpet

Rinsing your runner carpet thoroughly is a critical step. Any soap residue left in the fibers can attract dirt faster. It can also leave your rug feeling stiff or looking dull. You want your carpet to be soft and truly clean.

First, drain the dirty water from the bathtub. You will likely be amazed at how much grime comes out. As the water drains, gently press down on the rug to help push out more dirty liquid. Once the tub is empty, refill it with clean, warm water. Swish the carpet around in this fresh water. This helps rinse away the first layer of soap. Drain this water.
