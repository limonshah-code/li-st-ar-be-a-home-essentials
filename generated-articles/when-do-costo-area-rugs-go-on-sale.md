### Spotting Unadvertised Deals and Manager Markdowns

Not all Costco deals are in the coupon book. Some of the best savings come from unadvertised markdowns. These are a goldmine for savvy shoppers. I have found many incredible deals this way. Learning to spot these price drops is a valuable skill.

**Price Tags Ending in .97:** This is Costco's secret code for clearance items. When you see a price tag ending in .97, it means the item is on markdown. This markdown is specific to that warehouse. The item may not be available at this price in other stores. These are final clearance prices, and they often mean the item will not be restocked. I always grab these deals if the item is what I need.

**Price Tags Ending in .00 or .88:** These prices often signal a manager markdown. It means the local store manager has decided to clear out the item. These are less common than .97 items. However, they can represent deep discounts. They also mean the item is likely going fast.

**Asterisk on Price Tag:** Look for an asterisk (*) in the upper right corner of the price tag. This star means the item will not be reordered. It is the last chance to buy that item. While not always a markdown, it often precedes one. It can also mean current stock is all that is left.

These in-store cues are crucial for finding the absolute lowest prices. I always keep an eye out for these special price tags. They represent spontaneous deals that you cannot always plan for.

### Maintaining Your Costco Area Rug's Beauty and Longevity

Buying a beautiful area rug is just the first step. Proper care and maintenance ensure it stays looking great for years. This also extends the value of your purchase. I make sure to follow these tips for all my rugs.

**Regular Vacuuming:** Vacuum your rug regularly, especially in high-traffic areas. This removes dirt and dust before it settles deep into the fibers. Always use appropriate settings for the rug material.

**Spot Cleaning Spills Immediately:** Accidents happen. When a spill occurs, act quickly. Blot the spill with a clean cloth. Do not rub, as this can spread the stain. Use a mild rug cleaner or a mixture of water and gentle soap. Test any cleaning solution on an inconspicuous area first. Fast action prevents stains from setting.

**Rotate Your Rug:** If your rug is in an area with uneven wear, rotate it every few months. This ensures even exposure to foot traffic and sunlight. Even wear helps the rug last longer. It also prevents one part of the rug from fading more quickly than another.

**Professional Cleaning:** Consider professional cleaning every 1-2 years. Professional cleaners have specialized equipment and knowledge. They can deeply clean the rug without damaging its fibers. This extends the life and vibrancy of your rug.
