## FAQs About Shark HEPA Filter Removal

### How often should I remove my Shark HEPA filter?
You should remove and check your Shark HEPA filter at least every 6 months. This allows you to assess its condition. If you use your vacuum frequently, or if you have pets, check it more often. Removing it regularly helps you monitor its cleanliness.

### Can I wash a Shark HEPA filter?
Some Shark HEPA filters are washable, while others are not. Always check your specific vacuum model's user manual. If washable, use only cool water and allow it to air dry completely for at least 24 hours. Never use detergents or brushes.

### What if my Shark HEPA filter is stuck during removal?
If your Shark HEPA filter is stuck, first ensure all latches or buttons are fully disengaged. Gently wiggle the filter from side to side while pulling. Avoid excessive force. Dust buildup can make it tight. Refer to your manual for specific tips if it remains stuck.

### How do I know it's time to replace my HEPA filter?
It is time to replace your HEPA filter if it appears torn, significantly discolored, or if washing does not restore its cleanliness. Reduced suction power, a dusty smell from the vacuum, or signs of visible damage are also indicators. Most filters need replacement every 6-12 months.

### Are all Shark HEPA filters located in the same spot?
No, the location of the HEPA filter varies between different Shark vacuum models. In Navigator and Lift-Away models, it is often in the main canister or motor base. In DuoClean models, it might be under the dust cup. Always consult your specific model's user manual for exact placement.
