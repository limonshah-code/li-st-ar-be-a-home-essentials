## Do Carpet Guys Move Furniture: What to Expect

### Clear Answer: Do Carpet Guys Move Furniture?

Yes, many carpet installers offer furniture moving services. However, this is often an additional service. It may come with an extra charge. The exact service depends on the company policy and the type of furniture involved. Always confirm the details with your chosen carpet company beforehand.

### Understanding Standard Carpet Installation Services

When you hire professionals to install new carpet, you expect a certain level of service. This service usually includes removing the old carpet and padding. Some companies offer basic furniture moving as part of their standard quote. Others see it as an extra service. This means an additional charge will apply.

For very heavy or oversized pieces, you might need to hire a specialized moving company. These companies have the right equipment. They also have trained personnel. They can move items like a large refrigerator or a massive entertainment center. This specialized service comes at an extra cost. It is an important part of planning your carpet project if you have such items. Discussing these specific items with your carpet installer early is crucial. ### Preparing Your Home for Carpet Guys: The Pre-Installation Checklist

Proper preparation makes a big difference for your carpet installation. A well-prepared room helps the carpet guys work efficiently. It also protects your belongings. My experience shows that a little effort upfront saves a lot of hassle later.

Start by clearing all small, personal items from the room. This includes lamps, decor, books, and small electronics. Anything fragile or valuable should be moved to another room. You want to create a clear path for the installers. Think about what you would move if you were cleaning the room thoroughly.

Next, disconnect and move all electronic devices. This includes TVs, computers, and sound systems. Installers are not electricians. They will not disconnect your electronics for you. Pack cables and wires safely. Label them if it helps you remember where they go.

For items the carpet guys will move, empty them first. Dressers, cabinets, and bookshelves should be empty. This makes them lighter and easier to move. It also protects your items from shifting or breaking. Empty drawers before the crew arrives.

Consider removing doors from their hinges. This makes it easier for installers to carry in large rolls of carpet. It also prevents doors from getting scratched during the process. This step is usually easy to do.

Finally, ensure a clear pathway from your entrance to the room where carpet is installed. Remove any obstacles. Protect existing flooring if installers will be walking across it with tools. A drop cloth or cardboard can help. These steps ensure a smooth and efficient carpet installation day. Taking these steps yourself avoids potential extra fees or delays. ### Post-Installation: When to Return Furniture to Your New Carpet

The new carpet is down. Your room looks fresh and clean. Now, you want to put your furniture back. But how soon is too soon? This is a common question. You must allow the new carpet time to settle. This helps maintain its beauty and longevity.

Most carpet manufacturers recommend waiting at least 24 to 72 hours before placing heavy furniture back on new carpet. This waiting period allows the carpet to fully acclimate to the room. It also lets the adhesive (if used for certain installations) dry completely. Walking on the carpet is usually fine right away. However, moving heavy items can create indentations or shift the carpet before it settles.

When you do move furniture back, use furniture glides or protectors. These items spread the weight. They prevent deep indentations. They also make it easier to slide furniture without damaging the new fibers. Rotate your furniture layout every few months. This helps distribute wear and prevent permanent marks in high-traffic areas.

For very heavy items, consider placing them on larger, wider glides. These items include pianos or large entertainment centers. This further protects the carpet pile. ### FAQ Section

#### Do carpet installers move all furniture?
No, carpet installers usually do not move all furniture. They typically handle standard household items like sofas, chairs, and tables. Very heavy items, delicate pieces, or electronics are often excluded. You must check with your specific carpet company.

#### What furniture do I need to move before carpet installation?
You should move all small items, breakables, and electronics yourself. Empty dresser drawers and cabinets. Also, plan to move any extremely heavy items like pianos or large safes. These need specialized movers.

#### How much does it cost for carpet guys to move furniture?
The cost varies. Some companies include basic furniture moving. Others charge an extra fee. This fee can range from $25 to $100 per room or more, depending on the amount and type of furniture. Always get a detailed quote.

#### Are there items carpet installers will not move?
Yes, most carpet installers will not move grand pianos, slate pool tables, large safes, filled aquariums, or very delicate antiques. They also usually do not disconnect or reconnect electronics. These items require special handling.

#### Can I leave furniture in the room during carpet installation?
You can leave some furniture in the room if the installers agree to move it. However, it is best to move small items and valuables yourself. Heavy items the installers agree to move will incur an extra charge.

#### When can I put furniture back on new carpet?
It is best to wait 24 to 72 hours before placing heavy furniture back on new carpet. This allows the carpet to settle and any adhesives to fully dry. Light foot traffic is usually fine sooner. Use furniture glides when moving items back.
