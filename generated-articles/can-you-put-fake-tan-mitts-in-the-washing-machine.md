## Preparing Your Fake Tan Mitts for the Washing Machine

Before tossing your fake tan mitts into the washing machine, a little preparation goes a long way. This step ensures a better clean and helps protect your mitt. First, I always rinse my mitts immediately after use. This removes excess tanning product. A quick rinse under warm water until the water runs clear helps prevent stains from setting. This pre-rinse is very important for removing fresh residue.

Next, consider using a mesh laundry bag. I place all my mitts inside one of these bags. A mesh bag protects delicate items from harsh agitation. It also keeps small items, like mitts, from getting lost in the washing machine drum. This is a simple step that adds a layer of protection. This way, the mitt's fibers stay intact. The mitt remains soft for future use.

For heavily stained mitts, a pre-soak can be helpful. Fill a basin with cool water and a small amount of mild detergent. Let the mitts soak for 15-30 minutes before washing. This helps loosen stubborn tan stains. Some people might even consider a spot treatment. You can gently rub a tiny bit of laundry detergent directly onto the stain. This prepares the mitts for a thorough clean. Proper preparation makes a big difference in how well your mitts come out.

## The Right Washing Machine Settings for Tanning Mitts

Choosing the correct washing machine settings is critical for your fake tan mitts. Incorrect settings can damage the material. My main rule is always to use a gentle approach. ## Drying Your Fake Tan Mitts: Air Dry Only!

Once your fake tan mitts are clean, the drying process is just as important as washing. I cannot stress this enough: always air dry your mitts. Never put them in the dryer. The high heat of a clothes dryer is the enemy of synthetic materials like those used in tanning mitts. Heat can cause the mitt to shrink. It can also melt or distort the fibers. This makes the mitt rough. A rough mitt leads to uneven and streaky tan applications.

After washing, gently reshape your mitt. Squeeze out any excess water. Do not wring it aggressively, as this can stretch or damage the material. I usually hang my mitts to dry. A clothesline, a drying rack, or even a hanger works well. Ensure good air circulation around the mitt. This helps it dry evenly and prevents mildew. Hanging allows air to flow freely.

Make sure the mitt is completely dry before storing it or using it again. A damp mitt can develop mold or mildew. This creates unpleasant odors. It can also spread bacteria. I usually leave my mitts to air dry overnight. Sometimes, it takes a full 24 hours depending on humidity levels. Patience here pays off. Proper drying ensures your mitt stays soft, fluffy, and ready for your next perfect tan application.

## Hand Washing Fake Tan Mitts: The Safest Method

While machine washing is an option, I often prefer to hand wash my fake tan mitts. Hand washing is the gentlest method. It provides more control over the cleaning process. This helps extend the life of your mitts even further. I find it especially useful for newer mitts or those I want to preserve. Hand washing can be a quick process.

To hand wash, start immediately after you finish tanning. Rinse the mitt under warm running water. Squeeze it gently to remove as much product as possible. Keep rinsing until the water runs clear. This step is crucial for preventing stains from setting. It also removes the bulk of the self-tanner.

Next, apply a small amount of mild soap directly to the mitt. I use a gentle hand soap or a tiny bit of liquid laundry detergent. Work the soap into the mitt using your fingers. Rub the mitt against itself to create a lather. Focus on any stained areas. Rinse thoroughly again under warm water. Continue rinsing until all soap suds are gone. Squeeze out excess water. Then, reshape the mitt and hang it to air dry completely. Hand washing is truly effective. It ensures your mitt stays soft and ready for action. ### Step-by-Step Hand Washing Guide

Hand washing your fake tan mitt is a straightforward process. I follow these steps to ensure a thorough and gentle clean. This method keeps my mitts in excellent condition. It helps them last for many tanning sessions. It is my preferred way to care for these important beauty tools.

1.  **Immediate Rinse:** Right after applying self-tanner, rinse your mitt under warm running water. Squeeze the mitt gently to push out the tanning solution. Continue until the water runs clear. This prevents stains from setting deeply into the fabric.
2.  **Apply Soap:** Dispense a small amount of mild liquid soap onto the wet mitt. You can use gentle hand soap, body wash, or a tiny drop of liquid laundry detergent. Avoid harsh chemicals or abrasive cleaners.
3.  **Lather and Cleanse:** Rub the mitt against itself gently. Work the soap into a lather across the entire surface. Focus on any areas with visible tan residue or stains. Use your fingers to lightly scrub the fabric.
4.  **Rinse Thoroughly:** Rinse the mitt under warm running water again. Squeeze and rinse repeatedly. Make sure all soap suds are gone. Any leftover soap can affect your next tan application. It can also irritate your skin.
5.  **Remove Excess Water:** Gently squeeze the mitt to remove as much water as possible. Do not twist or wring it aggressively. This can damage the fibers or alter the mitt's shape.
6.  **Air Dry:** Reshape the mitt and hang it up to air dry. A clothesline, drying rack, or even a shower rod works well. Ensure good air circulation around the mitt. Allow it to dry completely before storing or using again. This prevents mildew and odors.

Following these steps keeps your mitt fresh and ready.

Another sign is a change in texture. If your mitt feels rough, stiff, or loses its soft, velvety feel, it might be past its prime. This often happens if the mitt has been washed improperly. It can also happen after many uses. A rough mitt can exfoliate your tan right off. It can also create an uneven finish. I expect my mitt to remain soft and plush.

Persistent stains are also a good indicator. If your mitt has deep, set-in tan stains that do not wash out, it is likely time to replace it. These stains can transfer old tan onto your fresh application. This can result in muddy or uneven color. Sometimes, despite best efforts, some stains just won't budge. Replacing the mitt ensures a clean slate. Generally, a well-cared-for mitt can last for several months or even up to a year. However, if you tan frequently, you might need to replace it sooner. Investing in a new mitt every now and then is a small price for a perfect tan.

## Preventing Common Tanning Mitt Issues

## FAQs About Washing Fake Tan Mitts

### Q1: Can I use fabric softener when washing my tanning mitt?

No, you should not use fabric softener on your tanning mitt. Fabric softeners leave a residue on the mitt's surface. This coating can reduce the mitt's effectiveness. It can also make your self-tan application streaky. Stick to a mild, liquid laundry detergent for the best results.

### Q2: How often should I wash my fake tan mitt?

You should wash your fake tan mitt after every single use. Immediate rinsing is critical to prevent stains. A full wash (hand or machine) should follow this. Regular cleaning prevents product buildup. It also maintains hygiene. This ensures a flawless and hygienic tan application every time.

### Q3: What if my fake tan mitt has stubborn stains?

For stubborn stains, a pre-soak can help. Soak the mitt in cool water with a little mild detergent for 15-30 minutes. Gently rub the stained areas before washing. Avoid using harsh stain removers or bleach, as these can damage the mitt. If stains persist after washing, it might be time for a new mitt.

### Q4: Can I put my fake tan mitt in the dryer?

No, absolutely do not put your fake tan mitt in the dryer. The high heat from a dryer can shrink the mitt. It can also melt or damage the synthetic fibers. This will make the mitt rough and unusable. Always air dry your mitt completely.

### Q5: Will machine washing shorten the life of my mitt?

Machine washing can potentially shorten the life of your mitt more than hand washing. However, if done correctly, the impact is minimal. Always use a delicate cycle with cold water. Place the mitt in a mesh laundry bag. Avoid harsh detergents and always air dry. Following these steps helps preserve your mitt's lifespan.

### Q6: Can I wash other clothes with my tanning mitt?

It is generally best to wash your tanning mitt separately. Or, you can wash it with similar delicate items. Avoid washing it with lint-shedding materials like towels. The tanning mitt can attract lint, which is not good for future applications. Also, ensure no dark colors bleed onto your mitt if you wash it with other items.
