## The Science Behind Finish Gel's Cleaning Power

Finish Gel employs specific chemical principles to achieve its cleaning results. Its formula includes several key ingredients. These ingredients work in harmony to tackle various types of food residue. Understanding these components explains its effectiveness. The gel consistency plays a big role in its cleaning process.

Firstly, Finish Gel contains enzymes. These are biological catalysts. Protease enzymes break down protein-based soils. Amylase enzymes target starch-based food residues. These include things like pasta or potato remnants. The enzymes start working early in the wash cycle. They pre-treat food particles, making them easier to remove. This biological action is powerful.

Secondly, surfactants are crucial ingredients. Surfactants lower the surface tension of water. This allows water to spread more effectively. They help lift grease and oil from dishes. Surfactants also create foam, though controlled foam is ideal in a dishwasher. They encapsulate dirt and prevent it from redepositing on clean items. This ensures a streak-free finish.

Thirdly, the gel format itself contributes to its efficacy. A gel disperses easily in water. It dissolves faster than some solid detergents. This quick dissolution means cleaning agents reach dishes sooner. It starts working immediately. This rapid action is beneficial for shorter wash cycles. It also helps prevent detergent residue. Using the correct amount is important. Knowing [how much dishwasher detergent to use](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-much-dishwasher-detergent-to-use-3) ensures optimal results.

Finish Gel also often includes builders. Builders soften hard water. Hard water contains minerals like calcium and magnesium. These minerals can reduce detergent effectiveness. They also lead to water spots. Builders bind to these minerals, neutralizing them. This allows the cleaning agents to work at their peak. The science behind Finish Gel ensures a deep, effective clean for your dishes.
