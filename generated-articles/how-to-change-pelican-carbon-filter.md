## Change Your Pelican Carbon Filter for Pure Water

Ensuring clean water at home is a top priority for many people. If you own a Pelican whole house water filtration system, you understand its value. These systems use carbon filters to remove chlorine, odors, and other impurities from your entire water supply. Over time, these filters need replacement to keep working properly.

Knowing how to change your Pelican carbon filter is a simple skill. It helps maintain water quality and extends the life of your system. This guide gives you clear steps to perform this essential task. We will cover why filter changes matter, what tools you need, and a step-by-step process. You can easily keep your home's water fresh and clean.

### Takeaway

*   Gather all necessary tools and the correct replacement filter before starting.
*   Turn off the main water supply and relieve system pressure for safety.
*   Drain the existing system completely before opening the filter housing.
*   Carefully remove the old filter and thoroughly clean the housing.
*   Install the new filter correctly, ensuring a tight seal, and then slowly restore water flow.

---

**How to change your Pelican carbon filter?**

To change your Pelican carbon filter, first, shut off your home's main water supply. Next, relieve pressure from the system and drain the housing. Then, open the filter housing, remove the old cartridge, clean the interior, and insert the new filter. Finally, reassemble the system and slowly restore water flow, checking for leaks.

## Why Pelican Carbon Filters are Essential for Your Home

Your Pelican whole house carbon filter plays a big role in your home's water quality. It works hard to remove many common contaminants. This includes chlorine, chloramines, and other chemicals that affect water taste and smell. I often notice a big difference in my water when the filter is fresh.

These filters also trap sediment and other particles that can damage your plumbing. A good carbon filter makes your water safer for drinking, bathing, and cooking. It protects your appliances from build-up. Many people ask, [what does carbon filter take out of water](https://homeessentialsguide.com/what-does-carbon-filter-take-out-of-water)? Carbon filters effectively remove organic compounds, pesticides, and herbicides. They do not remove dissolved minerals like calcium or magnesium.

Understanding [what is carbon filter made of](https://homeessentialsguide.com/what-is-carbon-filter-made-of) can help you appreciate its function. Activated carbon, often derived from coconut shells, wood, or coal, is the main component. This carbon undergoes a special process to create millions of tiny pores. These pores act like sponges, trapping impurities through adsorption. The large surface area of activated carbon makes it highly effective at purifying water. Over time, these pores become clogged with trapped contaminants. This reduces the filter's efficiency. Regular replacement keeps your water truly clean.

Knowing when to change your Pelican carbon filter is important for continuous clean water. These filters do not last forever. Their lifespan depends on your water usage and water quality. Beyond the recommended schedule, there are clear signs that your filter needs attention. The most common sign is a return of unpleasant tastes or odors in your water. If your tap water starts smelling like chlorine again, your carbon filter is likely exhausted. Another indicator is a drop in water pressure. A clogged filter can restrict water flow throughout your home. This means your filter is working too hard. Neglecting to change the filter can lead to reduced water quality and potential damage to your water system. Sometimes, a carbon filter might stop working effectively even before its expected lifespan. This can happen if the water quality is unusually poor or if the filter was improperly installed. You might notice a sudden change in water quality, even if it has not been long since the last change. ## Essential Tools and Supplies for Filter Replacement

Before you begin changing your Pelican carbon filter, gather all necessary tools and supplies. Having everything ready saves time and prevents interruptions. I always prepare my workspace first. This ensures a smooth process.

Here is a list of what you will need:

*   **New Pelican Carbon Filter:** Make sure you have the correct model and size for your specific Pelican system. Using the wrong filter can cause leaks or poor filtration. Double-check your system's manual or the label on your existing filter for the exact replacement part number.
*   **Filter Housing Wrench:** Most Pelican systems come with a large wrench for loosening the filter housing. If you cannot find yours, you may need to purchase a universal filter housing wrench. This tool gives you the leverage needed to open the large housing.
*   **Large Bucket or Pan:** You will need this to catch any water that drains from the filter housing. Even after turning off the water, some water remains inside the system. A bucket prevents spills and keeps your work area dry.
*   **Towels or Rags:** Keep several clean towels handy. They are useful for wiping up spills, drying parts, and cleaning the housing.
*   **Silicone Grease (Food-Grade):** This is optional but highly recommended. Applying a thin layer to the O-rings helps create a watertight seal. It also makes future filter changes easier by preventing O-rings from sticking. Ensure it is food-grade if it will be in contact with drinking water.
*   **Safety Glasses:** Eye protection is always a good idea when working with plumbing. Water can splash, and parts might unexpected move.
*   **Gloves:** Protect your hands from old filter grime and for better grip.
*   **A Helper (Optional):** The filter housing can be heavy when full of water. An extra set of hands can make the job easier and safer.

Having these items organized and ready before you start prevents frustration. It makes the entire filter change process much more efficient. Do not rush this preparation step.

## Step-by-Step Guide to Changing Your Pelican Carbon Filter

Changing your Pelican carbon filter is a straightforward process. Follow these steps carefully to ensure a successful replacement. I find it helpful to read through all steps before starting.

### Step 1: Prepare Your Workspace and Shut Off Water

First, clear the area around your Pelican system. Place your bucket and towels nearby. Locate your home's main water shut-off valve. This valve is usually near your water meter or where the main water line enters your house. Turn this valve to completely shut off the water supply to your home. This is a critical safety step. Do not skip it. If you have a bypass valve on your Pelican system, you can use that instead of shutting off the main water. This only isolates the filter system.

### Step 2: Relieve System Pressure

With the water supply off, open a faucet inside your house. Choose a cold water faucet on the lowest floor. Let the water run until it stops flowing. This drains water from your pipes and relieves pressure within the filtration system. Most Pelican systems also have a pressure release button on top of the filter housing. Press this button to release any remaining air pressure. You might hear a hissing sound as air escapes. This step makes opening the housing much easier and safer.

### Step 3: Drain the Filter Housing

Position your large bucket directly under the filter housing. Use the filter housing wrench to slowly loosen the housing. Turn it counter-clockwise. You will notice water starting to drain into the bucket. Allow all the water to drain completely. This can take a few minutes. The housing will become lighter as it empties.

### Step 4: Remove the Old Carbon Filter

Once the housing is empty, continue unscrewing it until it separates from the system head. Carefully lower the housing. The old carbon filter cartridge will be inside. It might be heavy and dirty. Lift the old filter out and dispose of it properly. You can recycle some filter components, but generally, the carbon itself goes into regular waste. For specific guidance on [how to remove the carbon filter](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-remove-the-carbon-filter), remember to handle it gently.

### Step 5: Clean the Filter Housing

Now is a good time to clean the inside of the filter housing. Use warm water and a mild soap (if necessary) to wash away any sediment or slime. Rinse it thoroughly to ensure no soap residue remains. Inspect the O-rings that sit around the housing opening. These are vital for a watertight seal. Remove the O-rings, clean them, and check for cracks or wear. If they look damaged, replace them with new ones. Apply a thin layer of food-grade silicone grease to the O-rings. This helps them seal better and prevents sticking. This step is similar to maintaining other water filtration systems, such as when you [how to change carbon filter in water softener](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-change-carbon-filter-in-water-softener). A clean housing and well-maintained O-rings are crucial for preventing leaks.

### Step 6: Install the New Carbon Filter

Carefully unwrap your new Pelican carbon filter. Ensure it is the correct type and orientation. Some filters have a specific direction for water flow. Slide the new filter cartridge into the cleaned housing. It should fit snugly. Make sure it is centered. Gently lift the housing with the new filter inside and align it with the system head. Begin screwing the housing back onto the head, turning it clockwise. Hand-tighten it as much as you can.

### Step 7: Tighten and Restore Water Flow

Once hand-tight, use the filter housing wrench to tighten the housing an additional half to three-quarters of a turn. Do not overtighten, as this can damage the housing or O-rings. Overtightening also makes it harder to remove next time. Slowly open the main water shut-off valve. Listen for water filling the system. Check for any leaks around the filter housing. Let water run through the system for a few minutes. Open the lowest faucet again to purge air from the lines. This also flushes any carbon fines or particles that might come from the new filter. The water might look cloudy at first. This is normal. It will clear up quickly.

If the leak persists, you may need to re-check the O-rings. Ensure they are properly seated and lubricated. Sometimes, an O-ring gets pinched or twisted. ## Troubleshooting Common Pelican Filter Replacement Issues

Even with clear instructions, you might face some common issues when changing your Pelican carbon filter. Do not worry; most problems have simple solutions. I have encountered a few over the years.

One common issue is the filter housing being too tight to open. This often happens if it was overtightened during the last installation. It can also happen if the O-rings have dried out and stuck. If the wrench does not provide enough leverage, you might need to try a rubber strap wrench for extra grip. Never use excessive force or tools like pipe wrenches, as this can damage the housing. Ensure all water pressure is fully relieved before attempting to open it. Releasing pressure makes it significantly easier.

Another frequent problem is water leaking after the new filter is installed. This is almost always due to an improperly seated O-ring or a damaged O-ring. Turn off the water supply again. Then, carefully loosen the housing and re-inspect the O-rings. Remove them, clean them, and apply fresh food-grade silicone grease. Make sure they sit perfectly in their grooves before re-tightening the housing. Ensure the O-ring is not twisted or pinched. O-rings wear out over time, so if it looks cracked or flattened, replace it.

Sometimes, after installing a new filter, the water flow might seem slow or weak. This can happen if air is trapped in the system. To fix this, open several faucets in your home, starting with the highest one, and let them run for a few minutes. This purges the air. Another cause could be a new filter releasing carbon fines. These are tiny black particles that can temporarily restrict flow or make water cloudy. This is normal with new carbon filters. Running water for 10-15 minutes will usually flush these out. If the flow remains low, double-check that the new filter is correctly oriented and seated. Some filters have an "in" and "out" direction. Always follow the arrows on the filter or in your manual.

If you notice a persistent strange taste or smell even after changing the filter, it could indicate a problem beyond the filter itself. Or, the new filter might be faulty. This is rare but possible. They can diagnose more complex issues.

## FAQ Section

### How often should I change my Pelican carbon filter?
You should change your Pelican carbon filter based on the manufacturer's recommendations. This is usually every 3-5 years or after a specific volume of water has passed through it, such as 600,000 to 1,000,000 gallons. Factors like water quality and usage can affect this. Always check your specific model's manual for precise guidelines.

### What happens if I don't change my Pelican carbon filter?
If you do not change your Pelican carbon filter, its effectiveness will decrease. You may notice a return of chlorine taste and odor in your water. The filter can also become clogged, leading to reduced water pressure throughout your home. A clogged filter can also allow contaminants to pass through.

### Is changing a Pelican carbon filter hard to do?
Changing a Pelican carbon filter is a straightforward task. It requires basic tools and following a few simple steps. Most homeowners can do it themselves. The most challenging part can be loosening a tightly secured housing. With the right tools and patience, it is a manageable DIY project.

### Can I clean my Pelican carbon filter instead of replacing it?
No, you cannot effectively clean a Pelican carbon filter for reuse. Carbon filters work by adsorbing contaminants onto their porous surfaces. Once these pores are full, the filter is exhausted. Attempting to clean it will not restore its filtration capabilities. Replacement with a new filter is necessary.

### How long does it take to change a Pelican carbon filter?
Changing a Pelican carbon filter typically takes about 30 minutes to an hour. This includes gathering tools, shutting off water, draining the system, replacing the filter, and flushing the lines. The time may vary based on your experience and how easily the housing opens.
