## Extending the Life of Your Area Rugs After Professional Cleaning

Getting your area rugs professionally cleaned by Stanley Steamer is a great start. But the care does not stop there. You can do several things to keep your rugs clean and extend their lifespan. This protects your investment.

Regular vacuuming is key. I vacuum my rugs weekly. This removes loose dirt and dust before it embeds into the fibers. Use a vacuum with strong suction. Make sure it has a proper brush or beater bar for rugs. For fringe, use caution. You do not want the fringe to get caught in the vacuum. Addressing spills quickly is also crucial. Blot up spills immediately with a clean, white cloth. Do not rub, as this can spread the stain. Use a mild cleaning solution if needed, but always test it in an inconspicuous area first.

Consider using rug pads under your area rugs. A rug pad provides cushioning. It prevents the rug from slipping. It also helps protect the rug fibers from wear. This is especially true in high-traffic areas. The pad acts as a barrier. It also absorbs some of the impact from foot traffic. Rotating your area rugs every six to twelve months helps too. This ensures even wear and tear. It also exposes different parts of the rug to sunlight. This prevents uneven fading. These simple steps can make a big difference. They keep your professionally cleaned rugs looking good for longer.

### Everyday Maintenance for Clean Rugs

Daily or weekly actions help maintain your rug's cleanliness. Vacuuming is your best friend here. Regular passes remove surface dirt. This prevents dirt from settling deep into the fibers. For high-traffic areas, vacuum more often. For low-traffic areas, once a week is fine.

Spot cleaning spills right away is crucial. Fresh spills are easier to remove. Always blot, do not rub. Rubbing can push the spill deeper. It can also damage rug fibers. Use a clean, absorbent cloth. You can also use a mild detergent solution. Test it on a hidden part of the rug first.

### Protecting Your Investment with Rug Pads and Rotation

Rug pads are more than just non-slip helpers. They protect your rug from wear. A good quality pad provides a buffer between your rug and the hard floor. This reduces friction. It also lessens the impact of footsteps. This keeps your rug fibers from crushing. It extends the rug's life.

Rotating your rug periodically is another smart move. It ensures even exposure to light and traffic. One side of a rug near a window might fade more quickly. Rotating it helps even out this exposure. This simple act can make your rug last many more years. It helps maintain its beauty evenly.
