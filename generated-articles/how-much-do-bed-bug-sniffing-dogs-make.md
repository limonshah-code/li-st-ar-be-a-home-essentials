## FAQ Section

### Q1: How accurate are bed bug sniffing dogs compared to human inspectors?
Bed bug sniffing dogs are significantly more accurate than human inspectors. A dog's sense of smell is far superior, allowing them to detect live bed bugs and eggs with up to 95-98% accuracy. Human visual inspections, which rely on finding visible signs, often achieve only 30-50% accuracy. The dogs can find hidden infestations that humans easily miss.

### Q2: How long does it take to train a bed bug detection dog?
Training a bed bug detection dog typically takes several months, often 600 to 1,000 hours of intensive work. This includes foundational obedience, scent discrimination, and specific bed bug odor recognition. The process ensures the dog can accurately identify bed bugs while ignoring other common household smells. Continued training and practice are essential throughout the dog's career.

### Q3: Do bed bug sniffing dogs always find bed bugs?
No, bed bug sniffing dogs are highly accurate but not 100% infallible. Factors like environmental distractions, the dog's health or focus, and the skill of the handler can affect performance. However, they are consistently the most reliable method available for early and precise detection. My dog often finds what others cannot.

### Q4: Can I train my own dog to sniff for bed bugs?
Training a dog for professional bed bug detection requires specialized knowledge and techniques. While you can teach your dog basic scent work, achieving the accuracy and reliability needed for professional services is very difficult without expert guidance. Most professional K9 units use dogs trained by certified instructors. This ensures high standards and effectiveness.

### Q5: How often should I get a bed bug sniffing dog inspection?
The frequency of inspections depends on your situation. Homeowners with no current issues might get one if they suspect bed bugs. Businesses, especially hotels or apartment complexes, often benefit from quarterly or semi-annual inspections. After a bed bug treatment, a K9 inspection can confirm eradication. My clients often schedule regular checks for peace of mind.

### Q6: What happens if a bed bug sniffing dog finds bed bugs?
If a bed bug sniffing dog indicates an infestation, the handler will mark the exact location. They often use small sticky notes or tape. This precise identification allows pest control technicians to target treatments only where necessary. This saves time and reduces chemical usage. It also helps confirm the presence of bed bugs before treatment begins.
