## Understanding FSA Eligibility for Medical Expenses

Flexible Spending Accounts (FSAs) help you save money on healthcare. You put pre-tax money into the account. Then, you use this money for qualified medical expenses. The IRS sets the rules for what counts as "qualified."

Generally, an expense must treat or prevent an illness or medical condition. This means things like doctor visits, prescriptions, and certain medical devices are covered. General health items, like toothbrushes or basic vitamins, usually are not. The key is medical necessity.

The IRS wants to make sure your FSA funds go to true healthcare needs. They define medical care strictly. Your expense must relate directly to a specific health problem. It cannot just be for general well-being. This distinction is very important when considering items like mattress covers.

If an item's primary purpose is medical, it is often eligible. If it serves a dual purpose, like a general household item that *also* has a minor health benefit, it usually does not qualify. This is where many common items become tricky. It is crucial to understand this core principle before making a purchase with FSA funds.

## When Mattress Covers Become Medical Necessities

A mattress cover moves from a household item to a medical necessity under specific circumstances. This happens when a doctor recommends it for a diagnosed medical condition. Conditions like severe allergies, asthma, or certain skin conditions often require environmental controls. These controls directly impact the patient's health.

For example, if you suffer from severe dust mite allergies, a doctor might suggest an allergen-proof mattress cover. This cover creates a barrier against dust mites and their allergens. This action directly helps reduce your allergy symptoms. It becomes part of your treatment plan.

The IRS requires a Letter of Medical Necessity (LMN) for many dual-purpose items. This letter comes from your doctor. It explains why the mattress cover is essential for your treatment. The LMN specifies your condition and how the cover helps manage it. Without this documentation, your claim will likely be denied.

Always speak with your doctor first. They can determine if a mattress cover is a medical necessity for your situation. They understand your health needs best. Getting their official recommendation is the first step toward FSA reimbursement.

### Essential Elements of a Letter of Medical Necessity

A strong Letter of Medical Necessity (LMN) includes specific details. It must clearly state your medical diagnosis. Your doctor should explain why the mattress cover is crucial for managing this condition. This explanation links the cover directly to your treatment plan.

The letter should also specify the type of mattress cover needed. For instance, it might recommend an allergen-proof or waterproof cover. It should mention how this particular cover addresses your symptoms. Your doctor's signature and the date are also vital. This confirms the recommendation is current and professional.

## FSA vs. HSA: Key Differences for Health Spending

Flexible Spending Accounts (FSAs) and Health Savings Accounts (HSAs) both help with health costs. They allow you to use pre-tax money for medical expenses. However, they have important differences. Understanding these differences can impact how you approach eligible purchases like mattress covers.

FSAs are "use-it-or-lose-it" accounts. This means you typically must spend most of your funds by the end of the plan year. A small carryover amount or grace period might exist, but it is limited. This encourages people to plan their medical spending carefully each year. If you have an FSA, you might feel more pressure to use your funds.

HSAs, on the other hand, are savings accounts. The money rolls over year after year. It can even grow with interest over time. You must have a High-Deductible Health Plan (HDHP) to open an HSA. This account offers more long-term flexibility. There is no deadline to use the funds.

For mattress covers, the eligibility rules are generally the same for both. If a mattress cover is medically necessary and supported by an LMN, it can qualify for both FSA and HSA reimbursement. The key difference lies in the account structure. With an FSA, you might want to purchase a qualifying mattress cover within the plan year. With an HSA, you can make the purchase whenever you need to, without worrying about an annual deadline. Always check the specific rules of your plan.
