## Heat for Drying: How LG Dishwashers Get Dishes Dry

After the wash and rinse cycles, your LG dishwasher shifts to drying. Heat plays a crucial role here. You want dishes to come out dry and ready for the cabinet. LG dishwashers use different methods to achieve this, all involving heat.

Many LG models use a heated dry option. This feature activates the heating element during the drying phase. The internal temperature can rise to about 170°F (77°C) or even higher. This high heat evaporates water from dishes quickly. It leaves them dry and spot-free. I prefer this method for most loads.

Some LG dishwashers use a hybrid drying system. This system combines heated drying with condensation drying. Condensation drying relies on the hot final rinse. The dishes become hot. Water on their surface turns into vapor. This vapor then condenses on the cooler stainless steel tub walls. The condensed water drains away. This method is more energy-efficient. It may not get plastics completely dry.

Rinse aid is very important for effective drying, especially with condensation drying. Rinse aid makes water sheet off dishes. This prevents water droplets from forming. When water sheets off, it dries faster. It also reduces water spots. Even with heated drying, rinse aid improves results significantly. I always make sure to keep the rinse aid dispenser full.

LG also offers options like "Extra Dry" or "Night Dry." The "Extra Dry" option extends the heated drying time. This ensures even better drying results, especially for plastics. The "Night Dry" option might use less heat or simply extend the condensation drying time. This saves energy and can be quieter.

Understanding the drying process helps you get the best results. For the driest dishes, select a heated dry option. Make sure your rinse aid dispenser is full. These steps ensure your LG dishwasher performs its best. They help deliver sparkling, dry dishes every time.

Lastly, **dishwasher maintenance** plays a role. Mineral buildup inside the machine can hinder performance. This includes buildup on the heating element or spray arms. ## Troubleshooting Temperature Issues with Your LG Dishwasher

Sometimes, your LG dishwasher might not get as hot as it should. This leads to dirty or wet dishes. It can be frustrating when your appliance does not work right. I have faced these issues myself. Here are some common problems and steps to fix them.

First, **check your home's hot water supply**. The dishwasher connects to your hot water line. If your water heater is set too low (below 120°F / 49°C), the dishwasher struggles. It might take longer or fail to reach desired temperatures. Ensure your water heater is set to at least 120°F (49°C). Also, run the kitchen faucet hot for a minute before starting the dishwasher. This ensures hot water is ready at the appliance. For no power issues, see [Why does my LG dishwasher have no power](https://homeessentialsguide.com/why-does-my-lg-dishwasher-have-no-power).

Second, **inspect the heating element**. The heating element is usually at the bottom of the tub. It heats the water during wash and dry cycles. If it is visibly broken, corroded, or covered in white mineral deposits, it might not work. A multimeter can test its continuity. If there is no continuity, the element is faulty. This part needs replacement. A broken element means no hot water.

Third, **check for drain issues**. If water does not drain properly, dirty, cool water might remain. The dishwasher could try to reheat this water, or it might not drain the final rinse effectively. This leaves dishes wet and sometimes dirty. Look for blockages in the drain hose or air gap. If your LG dishwasher has water in the bottom, it needs attention. Read more here: [Why does my LG dishwasher have water in the bottom](https://homeessentialsguide.com/why-does-my-lg-dishwasher-have-water-in-the-bottom).

Fourth, **verify the chosen wash cycle and options**. Make sure you selected a cycle that includes heated washing and drying. If you choose an 'Air Dry' or 'Energy Saver' option, the dishes will not get as hot. They might remain wet. Always check your cycle selection.

Fifth, **look for error codes**. LG dishwashers often display codes for problems. Refer to your owner's manual for error code meanings. A code might point to a heating element issue or a water supply problem. These codes guide your troubleshooting efforts.

If you are not comfortable checking components, consider professional help. Dealing with electrical parts or water lines can be risky. A technician can diagnose the problem safely. Proper troubleshooting ensures your LG dishwasher gets hot again. It ensures clean, dry dishes.

## LG Dishwasher Temperature and Energy Efficiency

Understanding temperature in your LG dishwasher also means thinking about energy. Higher temperatures use more electricity. Balancing cleanliness with energy savings is smart. I try to make my appliances work efficiently.

The 'Normal' cycle on your LG dishwasher typically uses less energy. It operates at around 120°F (49°C). This temperature is effective for most daily loads. It cleans well without excessive power consumption. This is a good choice for general use. It saves energy over the long run.

The 'Sanitize' cycle, while excellent for hygiene, uses the most energy. It heats water to 150-160°F (65-71°C) and holds it there. This sustained high heat requires significant electricity. Use this cycle when sanitation is critical, not for every load. Choosing this cycle wisely saves energy.

The drying method also impacts energy use. A heated dry option consumes more electricity. The heating element stays on for a longer period. This provides very dry dishes. Many LG models offer an 'Air Dry' or 'Energy Saver' option. These options use less or no heat for drying. They rely on condensation or ambient air. Dishes might not be bone-dry, especially plastics. However, this saves a lot of energy. If you are not in a hurry, it is a great choice.

LG dishwashers often include 'Sensor Dry' or 'SmartDry' technology. These features adjust drying time based on the moisture level. This prevents over-drying. It also saves energy. The machine detects when dishes are dry. It then turns off the heat. This smart technology optimizes energy use.

To improve energy efficiency:
*   **Load fully:** Run the dishwasher only when full. Half-empty loads waste water and energy.
*   **Scrape plates:** Remove large food particles before loading. This prevents re-washing.
*   **Choose the right cycle:** Select the lowest effective temperature. Use 'Sanitize' only when necessary.
*   **Use air dry:** If possible, choose the 'Air Dry' option. Open the door slightly after the cycle for faster drying.

By being mindful of these settings, you can save on utility bills. Your LG dishwasher will still deliver clean dishes. It just does so more efficiently. Making small changes can lead to big savings over time.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is 120°F hot enough for a dishwasher?
Yes, 120°F (49°C) is generally hot enough for a dishwasher's main wash cycle. This temperature helps detergents dissolve effectively. It also breaks down most food soils. Many standard wash cycles operate at or near this temperature. For heavy soil or sanitization, higher temperatures are better.

### Why are my dishes not hot after washing?
If your dishes are not hot, several things could be wrong. First, check your home's hot water heater setting. It should be at least 120°F. Second, the dishwasher's heating element might be faulty. Third, you might have chosen an energy-saving cycle or air-dry option. Lastly, cold water entering the dishwasher could be the cause.

### Does the sanitize cycle always use the hottest water?
Yes, the Sanitize cycle always uses the hottest water setting available on your LG dishwasher. It heats water to around 150-160°F (65-71°C). This temperature kills bacteria and germs. It is the highest temperature setting. This cycle meets specific sanitation standards.

### Can I adjust the temperature on my LG dishwasher?
You cannot directly set a specific temperature number. However, you adjust the temperature by selecting different wash cycles. Cycles like 'Heavy' or 'Sanitize' use higher temperatures. 'Normal' or 'Light' cycles use lower heat. Each cycle has a predefined temperature range.

### How long does the heating element last?
A dishwasher's heating element typically lasts 8 to 10 years. Its lifespan depends on usage and water hardness. Hard water can cause mineral buildup, reducing its life. Proper maintenance and regular cleaning can help extend its duration.

### Why do some LG dishwashers use condensation drying?
LG dishwashers use condensation drying for energy efficiency. This method relies on hot dishes and a cool stainless steel tub. Water vapor condenses on the cooler tub walls and drains. It uses less electricity than fan-assisted or heated drying. This makes it more environmentally friendly.
