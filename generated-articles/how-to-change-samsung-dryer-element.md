## How To Change Your Samsung Dryer Heating Element

There is little worse than pulling wet clothes from a dryer that simply will not heat. We all rely on our appliances daily. A broken dryer can disrupt laundry routines. If your Samsung dryer runs but does not produce heat, a faulty heating element is often the reason.

You might think fixing this requires a professional. Many homeowners can complete this repair themselves. Learning how to change a Samsung dryer element can save you significant money. This guide walks you through the entire process. We cover diagnosing the problem and completing the repair safely. You can restore your dryer's function.

### Takeaway

*   Check dryer symptoms to confirm a heating element failure.
*   Gather necessary tools before starting the repair process.
*   Always disconnect power to the dryer for safety.
*   Follow the detailed steps for element removal and installation.
*   Properly reassemble and test your dryer after the repair.

### Clear and Concise Answer

To change a Samsung dryer element, first disconnect the dryer from power. Remove the rear access panel. Locate the heating element housing. Disconnect the wires and mounting screws. Slide out the old element, install the new one, reattach everything, and then test the dryer.

### Diagnosing Your Samsung Dryer's Heating Issue

When your Samsung dryer stops heating, it is frustrating. Clothes remain damp even after a full cycle. This is the most common sign of a heating element problem. Sometimes, the dryer runs, but the air inside stays cold. Other times, clothes take an unusually long time to dry.

You might also notice the circuit breaker tripping when you use the dryer. This can indicate a short in the heating element. Before assuming the element is bad, check some simple things. Make sure your lint trap is clean. A clogged lint trap restricts airflow. This can cause the dryer to overheat and shut off the heat.

Also, inspect your dryer vent hose for blockages. A restricted vent traps hot air. This makes the dryer less efficient. It can also cause safety thermostats to trip. If lint and vent issues are clear, the heating element is a strong suspect. A multimeter helps confirm the diagnosis. You can test the heating element for continuity. No continuity means the element is broken. This shows it needs replacement. ### Gathering Tools and Prioritizing Safety First

Before you start any appliance repair, gather your tools. Proper tools make the job easier and safer. You will need a basic screwdriver set. Both Phillips head and flathead screwdrivers are helpful. Pliers, especially needle-nose pliers, assist with wire connections. A multimeter is essential for testing the old and new elements. This confirms the diagnosis and ensures the new part works.

Safety is your top priority. Always disconnect the dryer from its power source. Unplug the dryer from the wall outlet. If your dryer is hardwired, turn off the circuit breaker that controls it. Failing to do this can result in electric shock. Wear work gloves to protect your hands. Dryer components can have sharp edges. Having a small container for screws is also wise. This prevents losing small parts during disassembly. A camera on your phone helps too. Take pictures of wire connections before you remove them. This serves as a reference for reassembly.

Working in a well-lit area is important. Clear the space around the dryer. You will need room to maneuver the appliance. Ensuring a stable work surface for tools is also a good idea. This prevents accidents. Following these steps ensures your safety. It also makes the repair process smoother.

### Accessing the Samsung Dryer's Inner Workings

To get to the heating element, you must access the dryer's interior. Start by pulling the dryer away from the wall. This gives you enough room to work comfortably. Make sure to unplug the dryer completely from the electrical outlet. Safety is the first step. If the dryer is gas, also turn off the gas supply valve.

The heating element in most Samsung dryers sits at the back. It is housed in a metal box. You will need to remove the dryer's back panel. Look for screws around the edges of this panel. Most Samsung dryers use Phillips head screws. Use your screwdriver to remove all of these screws. Keep them in your small container. This prevents them from getting lost.

Once all screws are out, carefully pull the back panel away. It may be snug. Sometimes, a small amount of force is needed. Be gentle to avoid bending the metal. Lay the panel aside. Now, you can see the internal components of your dryer. This includes the fan, motor, and the heating element housing. This step opens up the appliance. It lets you get to the faulty part. Remember the sequence of removal. This helps later for putting it all back together.

### Locating and Disconnecting the Faulty Heating Element

With the back panel removed, you can now see the internal parts. The heating element in a Samsung dryer is usually inside a metal housing. This housing is often on the bottom right or left side, near the fan. It looks like a long, rectangular box. You will see several wires connected to it. These wires supply power to the heating coils.

Identify the heating element housing. Before you touch any wires, take a clear picture of the connections. This photo will be your guide during reassembly. It helps ensure every wire goes back to its correct terminal. Use your pliers to gently disconnect the wires. They might be spade connectors or screw terminals. Pull them straight off to avoid bending the terminals.

Some heating elements also have attached thermostats or thermal fuses. These are safety devices. They prevent the dryer from overheating. They will have their own wires connected. Disconnect these as well. Make sure you note their positions. Sometimes, these safety devices fail before the heating element itself. It is wise to inspect them. However, for a direct element replacement, focus on the main wires. Carefully handle all components. Avoid tugging on wires too hard. This ensures you do not damage other parts.

### Removing the Old Samsung Dryer Heating Element

Once the wires are disconnected, you can remove the heating element assembly. The heating element housing is typically secured with a few screws. Look for screws around the edges of the metal box that holds the element. These screws hold the entire assembly in place. Use your screwdriver to remove these screws. Place them with your other removed screws.

After removing the screws, the heating element housing should be loose. You might need to gently wiggle it. Carefully slide the entire assembly out of its position. Sometimes, the heating element coils are fragile. Avoid touching the coils directly if possible. Inside the housing, the actual heating element is usually nested. It is often a ceramic base with metal coils wrapped around it.

You will need to detach the element from its housing. There are usually a few more screws or clips holding the element itself inside the metal box. Remove these fasteners. Then, carefully slide the old heating element out. Inspect the old element for signs of breakage. Often, a coil will be visibly snapped or burnt. This confirms it was the problem. Check for any other small pieces that might have broken off. You may also want to ensure any safety thermostats or thermal fuses on the housing are not visibly damaged. ### Installing Your New Samsung Dryer Heating Element

Now it is time to install the new Samsung dryer heating element. First, ensure the new element is the correct part for your specific Samsung dryer model. Appliance parts are often model-specific. A wrong part might not fit or work properly. Carefully unbox the new heating element. Handle it gently. The heating coils are delicate.

Slide the new heating element into the metal housing. Make sure it sits correctly. Align any mounting holes. Secure the element with the screws or clips you removed earlier. These fasteners hold the element firmly inside its housing. Double-check that it is snug and does not wobble.

Next, place the entire heating element housing back into its position in the dryer. Align the mounting holes on the housing with the dryer frame. Insert and tighten the screws that hold the housing in place. Ensure these screws are secure. Finally, reconnect all the wires. Refer to the photos you took during disassembly. Match each wire to its correct terminal. Make sure connections are tight. Loose connections can cause future problems or fire hazards. Give each wire a gentle tug to confirm it is secure.

### Reassembling and Testing Your Samsung Dryer

With the new heating element installed and wired, you are ready to reassemble your dryer. Take the back panel you removed earlier. Carefully align it with the dryer frame. Make sure all edges match up. Insert the screws that hold the back panel in place. Start by hand-tightening a few screws. This helps align the panel. Then, use your screwdriver to tighten all screws securely. Do not overtighten them. This can strip the screw holes.

Once the back panel is firmly attached, push your dryer back into its original position. Reconnect the power cord to the electrical outlet. If it is a gas dryer, turn the gas supply back on. Now, it is time to test your repair. Turn on the circuit breaker if you had it off.

Run a short test cycle on your dryer. A good option is the "air fluff" cycle or a low heat setting. Listen for any unusual noises. Feel the air coming from the dryer vent. It should be warm or hot. If your clothes dryer now heats properly, you have successfully changed the heating element. You have saved yourself a service call and gained valuable DIY experience. ### FAQ Section

#### How do I know if my Samsung dryer heating element is bad?

Your Samsung dryer heating element is likely bad if the dryer runs but produces no heat. Clothes take a very long time to dry. The dryer might also trip the circuit breaker frequently. A visible break in the heating coil confirms the issue. Testing the element with a multimeter for continuity will provide a definite answer.

#### Can I replace a Samsung dryer heating element myself?

Yes, you can replace a Samsung dryer heating element yourself. This repair is common for many homeowners. It requires basic tools and careful attention to instructions. Always ensure the dryer is unplugged before starting any work. Following a detailed guide makes the process straightforward.

#### What tools do I need to change a dryer element?

You will need a Phillips head screwdriver, flathead screwdriver, and pliers. A multimeter is helpful for testing the old and new elements. Work gloves protect your hands. A small container for screws helps prevent losing parts. A camera is useful for taking reference photos of wire connections.

#### Where is the heating element located in a Samsung dryer?

In most Samsung dryers, the heating element is at the back of the appliance. It sits inside a metal housing. This housing is usually found on the lower left or right side when you remove the rear access panel. It contains the heating coil and sometimes safety thermostats.

#### How long does it take to change a dryer heating element?

Changing a Samsung dryer heating element typically takes 30 minutes to 1 hour. This time includes moving the dryer, removing panels, replacing the part, and reassembling everything. Experience with repairs can make the process quicker. Taking your time ensures safety and accuracy.
