## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### Q1: Why is my Frigidaire microwave displaying "LOC"?

The "LOC" message on your Frigidaire microwave means the control lock or child lock is active. This safety feature stops accidental use. To unlock it, find the "Cancel" or "Clear" button. Press and hold it for 3-5 seconds. The "LOC" message should disappear. This signals the control panel is now active.

### Q2: How do I turn off the control lock on my Frigidaire microwave?

To turn off the control lock, locate the button labeled "Cancel," "Clear," or sometimes "Stop/Cancel." Press and hold this button for 3 to 5 seconds. The display will show a change, like "OFF" or the lock icon vanishing. This action should disengage the control lock, allowing you to use the microwave normally.

### Q3: What should I do if my Frigidaire microwave door is stuck or won't close?

If your Frigidaire microwave door is stuck, first check for obstructions. Food debris around the latch can prevent proper closure. Gently clean the area. Push the door firmly to ensure it latches. If it still won't close, the door latch or a safety switch might be faulty. Avoid forcing the door.

### Q4: Can a power outage lock my Frigidaire microwave?

Yes, a power outage or a power surge can sometimes trigger the control lock on a Frigidaire microwave. It can also cause other temporary glitches. The microwave's control board might interpret the power interruption as a need to activate a safety lock. Performing a power cycle often resolves this issue.

### Q5: My Frigidaire microwave shows "F0" or "FO." What does that mean?

The "F0" or "FO" error code on your Frigidaire microwave typically indicates a control board issue or a stuck keypad button. The microwave might think a button is continuously pressed. A power cycle by unplugging the unit for 5-10 minutes often clears this code. If it persists, the control board may need professional inspection.
