### Finding the Serial and Model Numbers on Your Kenmore Dishwasher

To discover your Kenmore dishwasher's age, you must first find its serial and model numbers. These numbers are like the appliance's birth certificate. They hold the key to its manufacturing date. I will guide you through the most common places to look for these important identifiers. Always check carefully; sometimes these labels are small or hidden.

Most Kenmore dishwashers, regardless of their specific model, place these labels in accessible spots. You do not need special tools to find them. Just open the door and look around. Having these numbers handy will allow you to quickly verify the age. This step is the most important one in your quest.

#### Inside the Door Panel

The most common place to find your Kenmore dishwasher's serial and model numbers is on the inner edge of the dishwasher door. Open the dishwasher completely. Look along the sides, top, or bottom of the door frame itself. There is often a sticker or metal plate here.

This label is usually white or silver. It will clearly list "MODEL" and "SERIAL" followed by a series of alphanumeric characters. Take a clear picture or write these numbers down. This location is very common for most appliance brands, including Kenmore.

#### Side of the Tub

If the numbers are not on the door frame, check the side of the dishwasher tub. This means looking along the outer edge of the washing compartment. Sometimes the label is on the left side, sometimes on the right. It is typically just inside the opening.

You might need to pull out the bottom rack to see clearly. The sticker might be on the metal wall of the tub itself. This area is less exposed to wear and tear, so the label often remains legible for many years.

#### Kickplate Area

Another possible location for the serial and model numbers is near the kickplate. The kickplate is the panel at the very bottom front of the dishwasher. You might need to gently pull this panel off to see the label. It is usually held by a few screws or clips.

Look behind or beneath this panel. The sticker could be adhered to the dishwasher's frame. This spot is less common but worth checking if you cannot find the label elsewhere. Remember to reattach the kickplate securely after checking.

#### Common Kenmore Serial Number Formats

Kenmore serial numbers are typically a combination of letters and numbers. The specific character that denotes the manufacturing date can vary. For many Kenmore models, especially those made by Whirlpool, the first two characters of the serial number often indicate the manufacturing week and year. Other formats might use a letter for the year and a number for the month.

For example, a serial number like "FF1234567" might have "FF" as its date code. Or, a serial number "L123456789" might use "L" for the year. The key is to identify which part of the serial number holds this date information. This is where the decoding chart or online tools become helpful.

#### Understanding the Date Codes

Decoding the date codes requires a reference. For many Kenmore dishwashers, particularly those made by Whirlpool, the first two digits of the serial number can tell the story. Often, the first letter represents the year, and the second letter or number represents the month or week.

*   **Year Code:** Many manufacturers use an alphabetical system for the year. For example, 'A' might be 2000, 'B' 2001, 'C' 2002, and so on. This pattern sometimes repeats after 20-26 years.
*   **Month/Week Code:** The subsequent number or letter often indicates the month (1-12 or A-L) or the week (01-52).

Let's say a serial number begins with "KL". If K represents 2018 and L represents November, then your dishwasher was made in November 2018. If the code uses a number for the week, "18" could mean the 18th week of the year. Always remember that Kenmore is a brand, not a manufacturer. The actual manufacturer dictates the serial number system. This means the method for a Kenmore dishwasher model 665 might differ slightly from other models. ### Common Indicators of an Aging Kenmore Dishwasher

As your Kenmore dishwasher gets older, it might start showing signs of wear and tear. These indicators can suggest your appliance is nearing the end of its useful life. Recognizing these signs helps you anticipate potential problems. It also lets you decide whether to invest in repairs or consider a new unit. I always advise my friends to watch for these common issues.

Spotting these signs early can save you from unexpected breakdowns. It helps you budget for future appliance needs. Understanding what to look for makes you a more informed homeowner. It prepares you for making smart decisions about your older Kenmore dishwasher.

*   **Decreased Cleaning Performance:** Dishes are not as clean as they used to be. You might find food residue or spots. This often indicates weakening spray arms, a clogged filter, or a failing wash pump.
*   **Loud or Unusual Noises:** Rattling, grinding, or humming sounds can point to worn-out bearings, a failing motor, or issues with the wash impellers. Older units often become noisier.
*   **Leaks:** Water pooling around the dishwasher is a clear sign of trouble. Gaskets, hoses, or the pump seal can degrade over time. Leaks need immediate attention to prevent floor damage.
*   **Cycle Takes Too Long or Not Completing:** If your dishwasher cycles extend significantly or stop mid-wash, it could be a control board issue or a failing heating element.
*   **Water Not Draining:** Water remaining in the tub after a cycle is a common problem. It suggests a clogged drain filter, a faulty drain pump, or a blocked drain hose. *   **Frequent Repairs:** If you are constantly calling for service, the cost of repairs can quickly exceed the value of the appliance. This is a strong signal for replacement.

### Making the Right Choice: Repair vs. Replace for Your Old Kenmore

Once you know how old your Kenmore dishwasher is and what issues it has, you face a decision: repair or replace? This choice involves weighing costs, efficiency, and future reliability. It is a common dilemma for homeowners. I can help you think through the factors that influence this important decision.

There is no single right answer for everyone. Your budget, the severity of the problem, and your long-term plans all play a role. Making an informed decision saves you money and stress in the long run. Let's look at the key considerations for your old Kenmore dishwasher.

*   **The "50% Rule":** If the repair cost is more than 50% of the cost of a new comparable dishwasher, replacement is usually the better option. This rule helps prevent throwing good money after bad.
*   **Age of the Appliance:** As a general guideline, if your Kenmore dishwasher is over 8-10 years old and needs a major, expensive repair, replacement is often more practical. New dishwashers offer improved technology and energy efficiency.
*   **Energy Efficiency:** Newer dishwashers consume significantly less water and electricity. An old unit, even if repaired, might cost more to operate over time due to its lower efficiency. Consider the potential savings on utility bills.
*   **Frequency of Repairs:** If your dishwasher requires frequent service calls for various issues, it is a money pit. Constant breakdowns mean declining reliability and escalating costs.
*   **Availability of Parts:** For very old models, finding replacement parts can become difficult and expensive. Manufacturers stop producing parts after a certain period. This can make repairs impossible.
*   **Warranty Status:** If your dishwasher is still under warranty, repairs might be covered. However, if it is an older unit, the warranty has likely expired.
*   **Personal Preference:** Sometimes, you just want a new appliance with modern features and aesthetics. This personal desire is also a valid part of your decision-making process.
