It is not just about clean clothes. It is also about a healthy machine.

## How to Clean Your Whirlpool Washer's Drain Pump Filter

Cleaning your Whirlpool washing machine's drain pump filter is an important maintenance task. It ensures your machine runs well. This process helps prevent clogs and odors. It also makes sure water drains correctly. I recommend doing this every few months. You should do it more often if you wash very linty items. This includes pet bedding.

Follow these steps to clean the filter properly:

1.  **Prepare for Water Spillage:** Place a shallow pan or towels directly under the filter access area. Even if you use the small drain hose, some water will likely spill. This is normal.
2.  **Locate the Access Panel:** Find the small door or panel at the bottom front of your Whirlpool washing machine. Open it gently.
3.  **Drain Residual Water (If Applicable):** Some models have a small drain hose next to the main filter cap. Pull out this hose. Remove its stopper. Let all the water drain into your pan. Replace the stopper and put the hose back once it's empty.
4.  **Unscrew the Filter:** The main filter cap is usually a large, round knob. Turn it counter-clockwise to unscrew it. Be prepared for more water to gush out as you loosen it. Remove the filter completely.
5.  **Clean the Filter:** Take the filter to a sink. Remove any lint, hair, coins, or other debris caught in its mesh. You can use an old toothbrush to scrub away stubborn residue. Rinse it thoroughly under running water. Ensure all openings are clear.
6.  **Clean the Filter Housing:** Look inside the opening where the filter sits. Use a cloth or paper towel to wipe away any gunk or slime. Check for blockages. This ensures proper drainage when you put the filter back.
7.  **Reinsert the Filter:** Carefully screw the clean filter back into its housing. Turn it clockwise until it is tight. Make sure it is secure. Do not overtighten it.
8.  **Close the Access Panel:** Close the small door or panel. You are now done.

## Signs Your Whirlpool Washer Filter Needs Cleaning

Recognizing when your Whirlpool washing machine's drain pump filter needs cleaning is important. Ignoring it can lead to performance issues. It can also cause problems with your laundry. I have learned to watch for specific warning signs. These tell me it is time for maintenance. Being proactive saves time and prevents headaches.

Here are the key indicators that your filter needs attention:

*   **Excess Lint on Clothes:** This is a major sign. If your "clean" clothes still have a lot of lint, hair, or debris after a wash cycle, the filter is likely clogged. The machine cannot properly remove lint from the water.
*   **Drainage Issues:** You might notice water standing in the drum after a cycle. Or, the machine takes an unusually long time to drain. A blocked filter restricts water flow. This causes slow or incomplete draining.
*   **Unpleasant Odors:** Lint, hair, and other trapped debris can rot inside the filter housing. This creates a musty or mildew smell. The smell can transfer to your clothes. It can also fill your laundry room.
*   **Error Codes:** Many modern Whirlpool washing machines display error codes. These codes often relate to drainage or pump issues. Consult your owner's manual. An F9, E1, or similar code often points to a clogged drain pump filter.
*   **Machine Not Starting or Completing Cycles:** In severe cases, a completely blocked filter can prevent the machine from starting a cycle. It might also stop mid-cycle. The machine cannot proceed if it detects a drainage problem.
*   **Loud Noises During Drain Cycle:** You might hear grinding, humming, or vibrating noises when the machine drains. This indicates the pump is struggling to push water through a clogged filter.
*   **Longer Cycle Times:** If your wash cycles seem to take longer than usual, it could be due to inefficient draining. The machine waits for water levels to drop. A clogged filter slows this process.

If you observe any of these signs, clean your Whirlpool washing machine's drain pump filter right away. This simple step can resolve many common washer problems. ## Tips for Reducing Lint in Your Laundry

Even with a well-maintained drain pump filter, lint can still be a nuisance. Many factors contribute to lint production. Taking steps to reduce lint before and during washing helps. This complements your filter maintenance. I always try to reduce lint from the start.

Here are practical tips for minimizing lint in your laundry:

*   **Sort Clothes by Fabric Type:** This is one of the most effective strategies. Wash lint-shedding items separately. These include towels, fleece, and chenille. Wash them apart from lint-attracting items. These include synthetics, dark fabrics, and delicates. For example, do not wash a new fluffy towel with your black shirts.
*   **Turn Lint-Producing Items Inside Out:** Turning items like jeans, sweatshirts, and towels inside out can help. It traps lint on the inside of the garment. This prevents it from spreading to other clothes.
*   **Shake Out Clothes Before Washing:** Give clothes a good shake before putting them into the washer. This removes loose dirt and lint. It prevents these particles from entering the wash water.
*   **Avoid Overloading the Washer:** A packed washing machine does not allow clothes to move freely. This increases friction between fabrics. More friction means more lint production. Load your washer properly. Give clothes room to tumble.
*   **Use the Right Amount of Detergent:** Too much detergent can lead to excessive suds. These suds can trap lint. They redeposit it onto clothes. Use only the recommended amount of detergent for your load size.
*   **Clean Your Washing Machine Regularly:** Beyond the drain pump filter, occasionally run a cleaning cycle on your washing machine. This helps remove residue, mold, and trapped lint from the drum and internal hoses. Many machines have a "Clean Washer" cycle. Use it monthly.

## FAQs About Whirlpool Washing Machine Lint Filters

### Do all Whirlpool washing machines have a drain pump filter?
Most modern Whirlpool washing machines, especially front-loaders, feature a drain pump filter. This filter captures lint, debris, and small items to protect the pump and prevent clogs. Older top-loading models might rely more on self-cleaning systems that flush lint out with the wastewater. Always check your specific model's manual for confirmation and location.

### How often should I clean my Whirlpool washer's drain pump filter?
It is recommended to clean your Whirlpool washing machine's drain pump filter every 3 to 6 months. If you wash a lot of pet hair, towels, or very dirty items, you might need to clean it more frequently, perhaps every 2 to 3 months. Regular cleaning helps maintain machine efficiency and prevents issues.

### Can a clogged lint filter (drain pump filter) damage my washing machine?
Yes, a severely clogged drain pump filter can damage your washing machine. It can cause the drain pump to work harder, leading to premature wear or failure. It can also lead to drainage issues, water backing up, and potentially mold growth, which affects both your machine and laundry.

### Why do my clothes still have lint after washing in a Whirlpool machine?
If your clothes have lint after washing, even in a Whirlpool machine without a visible lint trap, the drain pump filter might be clogged. This prevents proper lint removal. Other reasons include overloading the machine, not sorting clothes by fabric type, or using too much detergent.

### Is there a difference between a lint filter and a drain pump filter?
Yes, there is a difference. A traditional "lint filter" is typically a removable mesh screen found in older washing machines, designed primarily to collect lint. A "drain pump filter" (also called a coin trap or debris filter) is present in most modern washers, including Whirlpool. It catches lint but also larger items like coins and buttons, protecting the drain pump.

### Can I run a cleaning cycle to clear the lint filter?
A regular cleaning cycle on your Whirlpool washer helps to flush out general buildup and reduce odors. However, it will not fully clear a physically clogged drain pump filter. This filter needs to be manually accessed and cleaned by hand to remove trapped lint and debris.
