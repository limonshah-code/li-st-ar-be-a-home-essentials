### Step-by-Step Guide: How to Clean Pura Vida Bracelets

Cleaning your Pura Vida bracelets is a straightforward process. Follow these steps for the best results. I find this method works very well for all my bracelets. It keeps them looking vibrant without causing damage. You will be surprised how easy it is to restore their sparkle.

#### 1. Prepare Your Cleaning Solution

First, fill a small bowl with cool water. Add a few drops of mild soap to the water. Gently stir the water to create a soapy solution. The water should have a small amount of suds. Do not use too much soap.

#### 2. Soak the Bracelets

Place your Pura Vida bracelets into the soapy water. Let them soak for about 5-10 minutes. This helps loosen any dirt or grime. Soaking makes it easier to clean the cords. If your bracelets are very dirty, you can extend the soak time slightly.

#### 3. Gentle Scrubbing

Take one bracelet out of the water. Dip your soft-bristled toothbrush into the soapy solution. Gently scrub the waxed cords. Pay attention to areas that look dirty or discolored. Be gentle around any charms or beads. Scrubbing removes surface dirt and grime.

You can also use a soft cloth for this step. Rub the bracelet gently with the cloth. This method is good for less soiled bracelets. Make sure to clean all sides of the cords. Repeat this for all your bracelets.

#### 4. Rinse Thoroughly

After scrubbing, rinse each bracelet under cool, running water. It is important to remove all soap residue. Leftover soap can make the bracelets stiff or attract more dirt. Rinse until the water runs clear. Ensure no suds remain on the cords or charms.

#### 5. Air Drying

Lay your clean bracelets on a clean, dry cloth or towel. Gently pat them to remove excess water. Do not wring or twist the bracelets. Allow them to air dry completely. Place them in a well-ventilated area, away from direct sunlight or heat. Direct heat can melt the wax coating. Drying can take several hours, or even overnight. Make sure they are fully dry before wearing or storing them. ### Special Care for Different Pura Vida Bracelet Types

While the basic cleaning method works for most Pura Vida bracelets, some types need extra attention. Different materials require specific care. Knowing these differences helps you protect your accessories. I always check the material before starting to clean.

#### Bracelets with Metal Charms

Pura Vida often uses silver or gold-plated charms. These metals can tarnish over time. Avoid soaking metal charms for too long. Prolonged exposure to water can speed up tarnishing. You can clean the cords as usual, but use a damp cloth for the metal parts.

Gently wipe metal charms with a soft, dry cloth after cleaning. This helps polish them and prevents water spots. For tarnished metal, use a specialized jewelry polishing cloth. Avoid harsh chemical cleaners on plated metals. They can strip the plating.

#### Bracelets with Fabric or Thread Accents

Some Pura Vida designs include fabric or delicate thread. These parts can be more sensitive to scrubbing. Use an even gentler touch on these areas. Avoid vigorous scrubbing to prevent fraying. A light pat with a soapy cloth is often enough.

Make sure these materials dry completely to prevent mildew. Fabric can hold moisture longer than waxed cords. Allow extra drying time. Proper care keeps these delicate elements intact.

#### Beaded Bracelets

Bracelets with natural stone or glass beads also need care. The beads themselves are usually durable. However, the stringing material can be delicate. Clean beads gently with a soft cloth. Do not use strong force that might dislodge them.

Ensure any elastic or thread holding the beads is not stretched or damaged during cleaning. Just like with metal charms, full air drying is important. This prevents water damage to the stringing material. Keep your bracelets away from harsh chemicals. This includes perfumes, lotions, and cleaning products. These substances can break down the wax coating. They can also fade the colors of the cords. Put on your bracelets after applying lotions or perfumes. This simple step protects them.

#### Remove Before Activities

Consider removing your bracelets before certain activities. Swimming in chlorinated pools or saltwater can affect the materials. Intense workouts can expose them to excess sweat. Removing them beforehand helps prevent unnecessary wear. It also reduces the frequency of deep cleaning.

#### Store Properly

How you store your bracelets also matters. Store them in a cool, dry place. Keep them away from direct sunlight. Sunlight can fade colors over time. You can use a jewelry box or a soft pouch. This prevents tangling and scratching. Proper storage keeps them clean and ready to wear.

#### Regular Inspections

Periodically check your bracelets for any signs of wear. Look for frayed cords or loose charms. Addressing these issues early can prevent further damage. A quick check helps you decide when it is time for a clean. This proactive approach saves your bracelets.

By following these maintenance tips, your Pura Vida bracelets will remain a cherished part of your collection. Consistent care ensures their lasting beauty.

### Frequently Asked Questions About Cleaning Pura Vida Bracelets

Cleaning your Pura Vida bracelets can bring up a few common questions. I get asked these often. Here are some quick answers to help you out. These tips ensure your bracelets stay in top shape.

**Q1: How often should I clean my Pura Vida bracelets?**
You should clean your Pura Vida bracelets as needed. If you wear them daily, a gentle wash every few weeks is good. If they look dull, feel sticky, or smell, it's time to clean them. Less frequent wear means less frequent cleaning. Trust your judgment for when they need a refresh.

**Q2: Can I put my Pura Vida bracelets in the washing machine?**
No, you should not put Pura Vida bracelets in a washing machine. The harsh agitation and high temperatures can damage the waxed cords. It can also cause charms to break or colors to fade. Stick to hand washing for the best and safest results. Hand washing is gentle and effective.

**Q3: Will cleaning fade the colors of my Pura Vida bracelets?**
Using mild soap and cool water for cleaning will not typically fade the colors. Harsh chemicals or hot water can cause fading. Always use gentle methods to protect the dyes. Air drying away from direct sunlight also helps preserve the vibrant hues. Proper cleaning protects colors.

**Q4: What if my bracelet has a lot of dirt stuck in the knots?**
For dirt stuck in knots, use your soft-bristled toothbrush. Gently scrub the knotted areas while the bracelet is soaking in soapy water. The bristles can reach into tight spaces. Be patient and use light pressure. A thorough rinse will then flush out the loosened dirt.

**Q5: Can I use rubbing alcohol or hand sanitizer to clean my bracelets?**
Avoid using rubbing alcohol or hand sanitizer. These products contain harsh chemicals that can strip the wax coating from the cords. They can also damage certain charms or fade colors. Stick to mild soap and water for safe and effective cleaning.

**Q6: How do I remove a strong odor from my Pura Vida bracelet?**
For strong odors, soak the bracelet in a mild soap and cool water solution for a longer period, perhaps 30 minutes. Add a teaspoon of baking soda to the water for extra deodorizing power. Scrub gently, rinse thoroughly, and air dry completely. Ensure it is fully dry before wearing again.
