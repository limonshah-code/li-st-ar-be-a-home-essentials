## Locating Your Kenmore Elite Dishwasher Filter for Better Cleaning

Are your dishes coming out of the dishwasher less than sparkling? You might wonder what is going wrong. Many people overlook one important part: the dishwasher filter. Finding the filter in a Kenmore Elite dishwasher is an important first step.

The filter traps food particles and debris. If you do not clean it, it can affect your dishwasher's performance. A dirty filter leads to poor cleaning, bad odors, and even drainage problems. This article shows you exactly where to find the filter in your Kenmore Elite dishwasher. We also discuss why it is important to clean it. You will learn simple steps to keep your Kenmore Elite running its best.

The filter in a Kenmore Elite dishwasher is located at the bottom of the wash tub, typically beneath the lower spray arm. It consists of a two-part system: a cylindrical coarse filter and a flat fine filter underneath it. Accessing and cleaning this filter system is essential for your dishwasher's optimal performance and to ensure sparkling clean dishes.

## Understanding the Kenmore Elite Dishwasher Filter System

Your Kenmore Elite dishwasher uses a filter system to keep water clean. This system catches food bits and debris. It stops these particles from recirculating onto your dishes. This filter system works silently. It is a critical component for clean dishes.

Most Kenmore Elite dishwashers use a two-part filter. One part is a coarse filter. It looks like a cylinder. This filter catches larger food pieces. The second part is a fine filter. It is often flat and located under the coarse one. This fine filter traps smaller particles. Together, they ensure only clean water washes your dishes.

The filter system also prevents clogs in your dishwasher's pump and drain. If too much debris passes through, it can cause problems. A clean filter helps your dishwasher drain properly. It also keeps your dishes spotless. Knowing about this system helps you maintain your appliance better.

## Pinpointing the Filter's Location in Your Kenmore Elite Model

Finding the filter in your Kenmore Elite dishwasher is usually straightforward. It sits in a specific spot. This location is designed for easy access. You do not need special tools to reach it. Most Kenmore Elite models follow a similar design.

Open the dishwasher door fully. Look at the bottom of the wash tub. You will see the lower spray arm. The filter is typically located directly under this spray arm. It is usually a cylindrical component. This cylinder often has a handle or an arrow for turning.

Some models might have a different setup. Always check your specific Kenmore Elite dishwasher's user manual. The manual provides diagrams. It also gives instructions for your exact model. This ensures you find the correct filter. Once you find it, removal is simple.

### Identifying the Two-Part Filter System

As mentioned, your Kenmore Elite likely has a two-part filter. You need to identify both parts for proper cleaning. Understanding each part helps you clean them correctly.

The upper part is the coarse filter. It is the cylindrical screen you see first. This screen prevents larger food scraps from entering the pump. It traps things like small bones or fruit pits. You turn this filter counter-clockwise to remove it.

Underneath the coarse filter, you will find the fine filter. This fine filter is usually a flat mesh screen. It sits flush with the bottom of the dishwasher. It catches tiny food particles and grease. You often need to lift out the coarse filter first to access this part. Cleaning both parts is crucial.

## Step-by-Step Guide to Accessing Your Kenmore Elite Dishwasher Filter

Accessing the filter in your Kenmore Elite dishwasher is simple. You can do it in a few minutes. Regular access for cleaning keeps your dishwasher running smoothly. Follow these steps carefully.

First, make sure the dishwasher is empty. Remove all dish racks. This gives you clear access to the bottom of the tub. Check for any large food pieces on the bottom. Remove them before you start.

Locate the lower spray arm. The filter is directly below it. It is usually a cylindrical piece. This piece has a handle or an arrow on top.

### Removing the Coarse Filter

To remove the coarse filter, twist it. Turn it counter-clockwise. You will hear a slight click. This means it is unlocked. Lift the filter straight up. It should come out easily.

Sometimes, food debris can make it stick. Gently wiggle it if it does not lift right away. Be careful not to force it. Forcing can damage the filter. Once removed, you will see the next part.

### Removing the Fine Filter

After removing the coarse filter, you can access the fine filter. This filter is a flat mesh screen. It sits at the bottom of the dishwasher. It might have small tabs or a designated lifting area.

Gently lift the fine filter. It should come out without much effort. Be careful not to bend it. This filter is delicate. Both parts are now ready for cleaning. I always find this part satisfying, knowing I am helping my dishwasher.

## Cleaning the Filter in Your Kenmore Elite Dishwasher

Cleaning the filter in your Kenmore Elite dishwasher is essential. It prevents many common dishwasher problems. You can easily do it with warm water and a brush. This simple task improves your dishwasher's performance greatly.

Once you remove both filter parts, take them to the sink. Rinse them under warm running water. Use a soft brush or an old toothbrush. Gently scrub away any trapped food particles. Pay close attention to the mesh screens. These areas can hold a lot of grime. ### Addressing Stubborn Buildup and Odors

Sometimes, filters can get very dirty. They might have a foul odor. This often happens if you do not clean them regularly. A dirty filter also makes your whole dishwasher smell bad. For a truly deep clean, or if your dishwasher has a lingering smell, you might want to try other cleaning methods.

If your Kenmore Elite dishwasher filter has heavy buildup, soak it. Mix warm water with a little vinegar in a basin. Let the filter soak for about 15-20 minutes. This helps loosen dried-on food and grease. After soaking, scrub it again. The grime should come off more easily. ## Reinstalling the Filter System in Your Kenmore Elite

After cleaning, reinstalling the filter system correctly is crucial. An improperly installed filter will not work well. It can also cause problems like poor drainage or noisy operation. The process is simple, but precision is important.

First, ensure both filter parts are completely dry. Place the fine filter back into its slot at the bottom of the wash tub. Make sure it sits flat. It should fit snugly. There should be no gaps.

Next, align the coarse filter. Line up its arrows or tabs. Insert it into the opening above the fine filter. Turn it clockwise until it locks into place. You should feel a click. This indicates it is secure. A secure filter prevents debris from bypassing the system.

### Checking for Proper Installation

After reinstallation, do a quick check. Gently try to wiggle the coarse filter. It should not move freely. If it spins or lifts easily, it is not properly locked. Remove it and try again. A properly installed filter stays firmly in place.

You can also run a short cycle without dishes. Listen for unusual noises. Look for any water pooling at the bottom. These could be signs of an incorrect installation. Proper installation ensures your Kenmore Elite dishwasher functions effectively. Taking this extra moment saves you trouble later.

## FAQs About Your Kenmore Elite Dishwasher Filter

### Q1: Can I run my Kenmore Elite dishwasher without the filter?
No, you should never run your Kenmore Elite dishwasher without the filter. The filter protects the drain pump from large food particles and debris. Operating it without the filter can cause severe damage to the pump. This leads to costly repairs or even needing a new dishwasher. Always ensure the filter is correctly installed.

### Q2: How do I know if my Kenmore Elite dishwasher has a self-cleaning filter?
Many newer Kenmore Elite models feature a self-cleaning filter. These systems typically use a hard food disposer that grinds down food particles. You will usually hear a grinding noise during the wash cycle. If you are unsure, check your user manual. Models with a manual filter, like the one discussed, require regular removal and cleaning.

### Q3: What kind of brush should I use to clean the filter?
Use a soft brush, like an old toothbrush or a small bottle brush. Harsh or stiff brushes can damage the delicate mesh of the fine filter. You want to gently dislodge food particles without scratching or tearing the filter material. A soft cloth can also be effective for general wiping.

### Q4: My dishes still have food particles after cleaning the filter. What else could be wrong?
If dishes remain dirty, several factors could be at play. Check your spray arms for clogged holes. Ensure you are using the correct amount of detergent. Verify the water temperature is hot enough. Also, proper loading prevents dishes from blocking water spray. Sometimes, hard water can also affect cleaning performance.

### Q5: Can a dirty filter cause my dishwasher to smell bad?
Yes, a dirty Kenmore Elite dishwasher filter is a very common cause of bad odors. Trapped food particles decompose over time, releasing foul smells. These odors can spread throughout the dishwasher and even onto your clean dishes. Regular filter cleaning is essential for a fresh-smelling appliance.

### Q6: Is it safe to put the filter in the washing machine or top rack of the dishwasher for cleaning?
No, it is not safe to put your dishwasher filter in a washing machine or the main wash cycle of another dishwasher. The filter is made of delicate plastic and mesh materials. The high heat and strong detergents in these machines can damage or deform the filter. Always clean it by hand using warm water and a soft brush.
