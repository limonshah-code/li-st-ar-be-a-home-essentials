## Step-by-Step Guide to Add Salt Miele Dishwasher

Adding salt to your Miele dishwasher is simple. Follow these steps carefully. This ensures you do it right. It protects your appliance.

1.  **Locate the Salt Dispenser:** First, open your Miele dishwasher door. Look at the bottom of the wash tub. You will see a large cap, usually circular. This is the salt dispenser. It is typically found near the drain filter.

2.  **Prepare for Refill:** Before opening, make sure the dishwasher is empty. It helps if it is dry. Unscrew the cap of the salt dispenser. Turn it counter-clockwise to loosen it. You will likely see water inside the dispenser. This is normal. It means the system is working.

3.  **Insert the Funnel:** Miele dishwashers often come with a special funnel. This funnel is designed to fit the salt dispenser opening. Using this funnel is important. It prevents salt from spilling into the wash tub. Spilled salt can cause corrosion. Place the funnel firmly into the dispenser opening.

4.  **Add the Salt:** Carefully pour Miele dishwasher salt into the funnel. Pour slowly. The water in the dispenser will overflow as you add salt. This is also normal. Keep pouring until the dispenser is completely full. Do not pack the salt down. Just fill it to the top. This step is about [how to put dishwasher salt in dishwasher](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-put-dishwasher-salt-in-dishwasher) efficiently. If you want to know [how to refill salt Miele dishwasher](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-refill-salt-miele-dishwasher), this is the process. Knowing [how full dishwasher salt should be](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-full-should-dishwasher-salt-be) ensures proper function.

5.  **Clean Up Any Spills:** Remove the funnel. Use a damp cloth to wipe away any salt grains. Ensure no salt remains on the bottom of the dishwasher or around the cap. This prevents pitting or corrosion.

6.  **Replace the Cap:** Screw the salt dispenser cap back on. Turn it clockwise until it is tight. Make sure it seals well. A loose cap can cause issues.

7.  **Run a Short Cycle:** After adding salt, it is best to run a short rinse program. This flushes any remaining salt particles. It helps dissolve the newly added salt. It also protects the stainless steel interior from salt corrosion. Do not put dishes in during this rinse cycle.

Following these steps ensures proper salt addition. It helps your Miele dishwasher work efficiently. It also protects your investment.

## Common Mistakes and Troubleshooting When Adding Dishwasher Salt

Even a simple task like adding salt can lead to mistakes. Knowing these common errors helps you avoid them. It keeps your Miele dishwasher running smoothly.

One major mistake is using the wrong type of salt. As discussed, only use coarse-grained dishwasher salt. Using table salt, Epsom salts, or rock salt can damage the softening unit. These salts contain impurities or are too fine. They can cause blockages. They can also lead to corrosion. Always double-check the salt label before adding it.

Another error is spilling salt outside the dispenser. If salt granules land on the stainless steel tub, wipe them immediately. Wet salt can cause pitting or corrosion. This looks like small rust spots. Always use the provided funnel to avoid spills. If a spill happens, clean it thoroughly with a damp cloth. This is part of [how to clean dishwasher salt dispenser](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-clean-dishwasher-salt-dispenser) area.

Forgetting to replace the cap tightly is another issue. A loose cap allows water to enter the salt compartment too quickly. It also allows dissolved salt to escape. This means the salt will not last as long. Ensure the cap is screwed on firmly and properly sealed.

Sometimes, the salt light stays on after refilling. Do not panic. This can happen for a few reasons. The salt takes time to dissolve and reach the sensor. Run a short rinse cycle. This helps the salt dissolve faster. It also helps the sensor detect the new salt level. If the light remains on after a few cycles, check the water hardness setting. Ensure it is set correctly. If problems persist, consult your Miele manual.

Adding salt when the dishwasher is loaded with dishes is also a mistake. Salt can spill onto the dishes. While not immediately harmful, it is not ideal. It can leave a salty residue on your dishes. It is better to add salt when the dishwasher is empty. It is a common question, [can you put dishwasher salt in with dishes](https://homeessentialsguide.com/can-you-put-dishwasher-salt-in-with-dishes)? The answer is no, always add salt to an empty dishwasher.

Being aware of these common mistakes helps. It allows you to correctly add salt to your Miele dishwasher. This prevents potential damage and ensures best performance.

## FAQ Section

### Q1: How often do I need to add salt to my Miele dishwasher?
The frequency depends on your water hardness and how often you use the dishwasher. Your Miele dishwasher has a salt indicator light. This light will illuminate when the salt level is low. Refill the salt when this light appears on your control panel.

### Q2: Can I use regular table salt instead of dishwasher salt in my Miele?
No, you should never use regular table salt in your Miele dishwasher. Table salt is too fine and contains additives like iodine. These can clog the water softening unit and damage your appliance. Always use coarse-grained dishwasher salt designed for this purpose.

### Q3: What happens if I do not add salt to my Miele dishwasher?
If you do not add salt, the built-in water softener will not function. Hard water minerals like calcium and magnesium will remain in the water. This can lead to cloudy dishes, water spots, and limescale buildup inside the dishwasher. Over time, this buildup can damage components.

### Q4: Why does my Miele dishwasher salt light stay on after refilling?
It is normal for the salt light to stay on for a short time after refilling. The salt needs time to dissolve and for the sensor to detect the new level. Run a short rinse program to help the salt dissolve faster. If the light stays on after a few cycles, check your water hardness setting.

### Q5: Is it normal for water to be in the Miele salt dispenser?
Yes, it is completely normal for the salt dispenser to contain water. This water is part of the softening system. When you add salt, the water will overflow. This is a sign that the dispenser is full and the system is working as intended.

### Q6: Can I use rinse aid and salt together in a Miele dishwasher?
Yes, you should use both rinse aid and salt in your Miele dishwasher for optimal results. Salt softens the water, preventing mineral buildup. Rinse aid helps water sheet off dishes, preventing spots and aiding drying. They perform different, complementary functions.
