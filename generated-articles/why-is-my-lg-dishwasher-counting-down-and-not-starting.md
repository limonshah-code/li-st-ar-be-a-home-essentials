## LG Dishwasher Counts Down But Won't Start? Fix It!

Is your LG dishwasher counting down but not starting the wash cycle? This can be incredibly frustrating. You load the dishes, press start, and watch the numbers tick away, yet no water enters the tub, and no washing sounds begin. This common issue often points to a safety mechanism, a power problem, or a simple sensor glitch preventing the machine from operating. Understanding the cause helps you find the right solution. I will guide you through the troubleshooting process step-by-step. This article covers various reasons your LG dishwasher shows a countdown but fails to start, from simple checks to component issues. We will explore electrical supply, door latch problems, water intake, drainage issues, and control panel malfunctions. By the end, you will know how to diagnose and potentially fix your LG dishwasher.

### Takeaway

*   Check power supply, door latch, and child lock first.
*   Ensure proper water supply and drainage.
*   Reset the dishwasher to clear minor glitches.
*   Address specific error codes if they appear.
*   Inspect components like the inlet valve or float switch.

### Why Your LG Dishwasher Counts Down But Doesn't Start

Your LG dishwasher counts down but does not start because a safety sensor or system check has not been met. This often involves issues with the door latch, water supply, drain line, power connection, or an activated child lock, preventing the wash cycle from initiating.

### Power & Basic Connections: First Checks

When your LG dishwasher counts down but does not start, the first place to look is at the basic connections and power supply. A dishwasher needs consistent power to run its cycle. Even if the display lights up and shows a countdown, there might be insufficient power for the wash cycle. Always check these simple things before moving to more complex diagnostics. It saves time and prevents unnecessary worry.

#### Confirming Power Supply

The dishwasher needs a dedicated electrical circuit. Check the circuit breaker in your home's main electrical panel. A tripped breaker will cut power to the dishwasher. Simply flip the breaker off, then back on to reset it. Ensure the dishwasher's power cord is fully plugged into the wall outlet. Sometimes, a loose plug can cause intermittent power or prevent a full cycle start.

#### Is the Door Latch Fully Engaged?

The door latch is a critical safety feature. Your LG dishwasher will not start if the door is not completely closed and latched. The countdown might progress, but the wash cycle will not begin. Close the door firmly until you hear a click. Sometimes, dishes or the dishwasher rack can block the door from closing properly. Adjust the racks and dishes to ensure nothing obstructs the latch.

#### Deactivating Child Lock

Many LG dishwashers include a child lock feature. When activated, this lock prevents any cycle from starting, even if a countdown displays. It is a safety measure to stop accidental operation. Look for a padlock icon or a specific button on your control panel. Often, you press and hold a specific button (like "Control Lock," "Delay Wash," or "Half Load") for three to five seconds to deactivate it. Refer to your LG dishwasher's user manual for the exact procedure for your model. This simple step often solves the "countdown but no start" problem.

#### Control Panel Glitches

A temporary glitch in the control panel can also cause this problem. The control panel is like the brain of your dishwasher. It manages all functions. If it gets confused, it might display a countdown without sending the start signal to the other components. Try a hard reset of the dishwasher. Turn off the power at the circuit breaker for about 5 minutes, then turn it back on. This clears the control board's memory and can resolve many electronic hiccups.

### Water Supply & Inlet Valve Issues

Your LG dishwasher needs a steady supply of water to begin its wash cycle. If the machine counts down but never starts filling, it indicates a problem with the water intake. The dishwasher's internal systems will prevent operation without adequate water. This is a common safety measure to prevent damage to the pump and heating elements. Ensuring proper water flow is essential for any wash cycle to begin.

#### Checking Water Faucet and Supply Hose

First, check the water shut-off valve under your kitchen sink. This valve controls the water flow to the dishwasher. Make sure it is fully open. A partially closed valve can restrict water flow, preventing the dishwasher from starting. Next, inspect the water supply hose connected to the dishwasher. Look for any kinks, twists, or blockages. A bent hose will stop water from reaching the machine. Ensure the hose is securely connected at both ends.

#### Inspecting the Water Inlet Valve

The water inlet valve is an important part. It opens to allow water into the dishwasher. If this valve is faulty, it will not open even when the dishwasher signals it to. You might hear a humming sound from the dishwasher as it tries to pull water, but no water enters the tub. This valve can become clogged with mineral deposits or fail electrically. You can often test the valve's functionality with a multimeter for continuity, or by listening for a distinct click when the dishwasher should be filling. If the valve does not activate, it likely needs replacement.

#### Low Water Pressure

Sometimes, the issue is not with the dishwasher itself, but with your home's water pressure. If the water pressure is too low, the dishwasher's pressure sensor may not detect enough water to begin the cycle. This sensor prevents the dishwasher from running dry, which could damage the pump. You can test your home's water pressure by filling a one-gallon container from your kitchen faucet. If it takes more than 15-20 seconds, your water pressure might be too low for the dishwasher to start properly. This could be a house-wide issue or specific to the line supplying the dishwasher. Addressing low water pressure might require a plumber's help.

Ensuring your LG dishwasher has proper water intake is critical for it to start. The machine will not proceed with the wash cycle until it detects sufficient water. This prevents damage and ensures effective cleaning. If you hear humming but no water, or no sound at all, the water inlet system is a prime area to investigate. ### Drainage System & Float Switch

Even though your LG dishwasher might be counting down and not starting a *new* cycle, a problem with its drainage system can prevent it. This often ties into the float switch, a safety mechanism designed to prevent overflowing. If the float switch detects standing water in the tub from a previous cycle, or a continuous high water level, it will prevent the dishwasher from starting. It tells the machine that the tub is already full, or that there is a drainage problem.

#### Checking for Standing Water

Open your dishwasher door and inspect the bottom of the tub. Is there standing water from a previous cycle? If so, the dishwasher will not start a new cycle. This indicates a drain problem. A clogged drain filter is a common culprit. Food particles and debris accumulate over time, blocking the water from draining out. You need to remove and clean the filter thoroughly. Many LG dishwashers have a removable filter assembly at the bottom of the tub. [How to clean filter in dishwasher](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-clean-filter-in-dishwasher) offers detailed steps.

#### Inspecting the Float Switch

The float switch is a small component located at the bottom of the dishwasher tub. It usually looks like a plastic cup or disc that floats up when water fills the tub. If water rises too high, it triggers the switch, which sends a signal to the control board to stop water intake or even stop the cycle from starting. If the float switch is stuck in the "up" position, due to debris or a malfunction, the dishwasher will think there's always too much water. This will prevent it from starting any new cycle. Gently push down on the float to ensure it moves freely. If it feels sticky or does not spring back, it might be stuck. Clean around it to remove any debris.

#### Clearing the Drain Hose and Air Gap

A clogged drain hose will prevent water from exiting the dishwasher. This can lead to standing water, activating the float switch. Disconnect the drain hose from the sink drain or garbage disposal. Check for blockages inside the hose. Sometimes, food particles or grease buildup can create a stubborn clog. If your dishwasher connects to an air gap on your sink, check it for blockages too. An air gap prevents dirty water from flowing back into your clean water supply. Clogs in the air gap can also prevent proper drainage. Ensuring these components are clear allows the dishwasher to drain properly, signaling the float switch to allow a new cycle to begin. Regularly cleaning your dishwasher filter is a good preventative measure to avoid these issues. ### Error Codes & Diagnostic Mode

LG dishwashers are smart appliances. They often provide error codes when something prevents them from running correctly. When your LG dishwasher counts down but does not start, it might be trying to tell you something with a specific code. Understanding these codes helps pinpoint the exact problem, making troubleshooting much easier. These codes appear on the display panel.

#### Common LG Dishwasher Error Codes

Different LG dishwasher models have different codes. However, some codes are common. For example, "IE" often means an inlet error, indicating a water supply problem. "OE" signals a drain error, meaning water is not draining properly. "LE" can indicate a motor lock error. "FE" points to a water fill error, suggesting the dishwasher is overfilling or there is a faulty water level sensor. If you see a code, note it down. Consult your LG owner's manual or the LG website for a full list of codes specific to your model. Each code points to a particular system failure or condition.

#### Entering Diagnostic Mode

Many LG dishwashers have a diagnostic or test mode. This mode helps service technicians identify problems. You can often activate it by pressing a specific combination of buttons, usually listed in the service manual for your model. Once in diagnostic mode, the dishwasher might run through a brief test cycle or display more detailed internal codes. This can help confirm which component is failing. For instance, if the diagnostic mode immediately throws an "IE" code, it strengthens the suspicion of an inlet valve problem. Use caution when entering diagnostic modes, and follow instructions precisely.

#### Resetting Error Codes

Sometimes, an error code is temporary. It might be due to a power surge or a minor sensor glitch. After addressing the potential issue (e.g., clearing a drain, opening a water valve), you need to reset the error code. The simplest way is a power cycle: turn off the dishwasher at the circuit breaker for a few minutes, then turn it back on. This often clears the control board's memory, including any stored error codes. If the code reappears after a reset, the underlying problem still exists. Then, a component might truly be faulty. ### Advanced Component Failures

If you have gone through the basic checks, water supply, and drainage systems, and still find your LG dishwasher counting down but not starting, the issue might lie with a failing internal component. These parts are critical for the dishwasher's operation. When they fail, the control board prevents the cycle from initiating. This protects the machine from further damage. Diagnosing these requires a bit more technical comfort, and sometimes, specialized tools.

#### Faulty Main Control Board

The main control board is the central processing unit of your dishwasher. It receives signals from all sensors and sends commands to all components. If the control board is faulty, it might incorrectly register conditions, fail to send the "start" signal, or simply become unresponsive. This can manifest as a countdown that never leads to a cycle. Signs of a failing control board include erratic behavior, unresponsive buttons, or persistent error codes despite resolving other issues. Replacing a control board is complex and often requires a professional technician.

#### Defective Wash Motor or Circulation Pump

The wash motor powers the circulation pump, which sprays water throughout the dishwasher tub. If the motor or pump is defective, the dishwasher will not start the wash cycle. You might hear a brief hum or click, then nothing. Some models might even display an "LE" (motor lock error) code. The dishwasher performs a self-test before starting. If it detects a problem with the motor or pump, it will prevent operation. This prevents the motor from overheating or burning out. Testing these components typically involves checking electrical continuity and resistance with a multimeter. A common issue might also be related to how your appliance behaves with other similar issues, like your dryer [Why is my LG dryer turning on but not starting](https://homeessentialsguide.com/why-is-my-lg-dryer-turning-on-but-not-starting).

#### Heating Element Failure

While the heating element primarily heats the water, a complete failure can sometimes prevent the wash cycle from starting. Some dishwashers perform a pre-wash check to ensure the heating element is functional. If it is shorted or completely open, the control board might halt the cycle before it begins. This is less common than other issues but still a possibility. A multimeter can test the heating element for continuity. No continuity means the element is broken.

#### Door Latch Assembly Microswitch

Even if the door latch appears to close firmly, the internal microswitch within the assembly can fail. This switch tells the control board that the door is securely closed. If the microswitch is faulty, the dishwasher will not receive the "door closed" signal, and thus will not start the cycle, even if the countdown runs. You might hear the latch click, but the electrical signal is not sent. Testing this requires access to the microswitch, usually by disassembling the door panel. It is a common point of failure due to repeated use.

### Other Less Common Causes

After covering the primary culprits, there are still a few less common reasons why your LG dishwasher might be counting down but not initiating a cycle. These issues are often related to specific sensors, unusual electrical problems, or overlooked settings. While not as frequent, they are worth considering if other troubleshooting steps have failed.

#### Thermal Fuse Blown

A thermal fuse is a safety device designed to blow and cut off power if the dishwasher overheats. This is a rare occurrence for a dishwasher that isn't starting, as overheating usually happens during operation. However, a faulty thermal fuse, or one that blew previously and was not replaced correctly, will prevent any power from reaching critical components, thus stopping the cycle before it begins. The dishwasher might appear to have power for the display but lacks power for the motor or heating elements. If the fuse is blown, it needs replacement. This often indicates another underlying issue that caused the overheating.

#### Pressure Sensor Malfunction

Your LG dishwasher uses a pressure sensor or turbidity sensor to detect water levels and clarity. If this sensor malfunctions, it might incorrectly report low water pressure or an incorrect water level. Even if the water inlet valve is working, a faulty pressure sensor could tell the control board there isn't enough water, preventing the wash cycle from starting. This can lead to the dishwasher counting down but never initiating the fill sequence. Testing these sensors often requires specific diagnostic tools or comparison with a known good sensor.

#### Wiring Issues

Damaged or loose wiring within the dishwasher can prevent various components from receiving power or signals. Wires can fray over time, especially near moving parts or areas subject to heat and moisture. A loose connection at a component, like the motor or control board, can also disrupt the power flow. While the display might illuminate, the internal connections necessary for the wash cycle could be compromised. Inspecting wiring requires disassembling parts of the dishwasher, and it is best done by someone comfortable with electrical work. Always disconnect power before inspecting wiring.

#### Software Glitches and Updates

Like any modern appliance, LG dishwashers run on software. Occasionally, a software glitch can cause unexpected behavior. This might include the dishwasher counting down but not starting. Sometimes, a full power reset can clear these glitches. Disconnect the dishwasher from power for a prolonged period, say 15-30 minutes, then reconnect it. For some newer LG models, software updates might be available. While less common for fixing a "no start" issue, ensuring your dishwasher has the latest firmware can improve its overall performance and resolve minor bugs. This is usually done by a service technician.

### Troubleshooting Steps Checklist

When your LG dishwasher counts down but won't start, a systematic approach helps. You can methodically check common issues. I find this checklist useful for organizing my troubleshooting process. It helps ensure I do not miss any simple fixes before moving to more complex diagnostics. Following these steps helps diagnose the problem efficiently.

#### Initial Power and Door Checks

*   **Check Circuit Breaker:** Go to your home's main electrical panel. Find the breaker labeled for the dishwasher. Flip it off, then back on. This resets the power.
*   **Verify Power Cord Connection:** Ensure the dishwasher's power cord is fully pushed into the wall outlet. A loose plug prevents full power delivery.
*   **Confirm Door Latch:** Firmly close the dishwasher door. You should hear a distinct click. Make sure no dishes or racks block the door from closing completely.
*   **Deactivate Child Lock:** Look for a "Control Lock" or padlock icon on your control panel. Press and hold the corresponding button for 3-5 seconds to turn off the child lock feature.
*   **Perform a Hard Reset:** Turn off power to the dishwasher at the circuit breaker for 5-10 minutes. Then, restore power. This clears the control board's memory.

#### Water Supply & Drainage Verification

*   **Check Water Shut-Off Valve:** Locate the water valve under your kitchen sink, connected to the dishwasher's supply line. Ensure it is fully open.
*   **Inspect Water Supply Hose:** Look for any kinks, twists, or bends in the hose behind the dishwasher. Straighten any obstructions.
*   **Check for Standing Water:** Open the dishwasher door and look for water at the bottom of the tub. If present, it indicates a drainage issue.
*   **Clean Dishwasher Filter:** Remove and thoroughly clean the dishwasher's filter. This is usually located at the bottom of the tub. Clogged filters prevent drainage. (Refer to [How to clean filter in dishwasher](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-clean-filter-in-dishwasher) for detailed instructions).
*   **Test Float Switch:** Gently push down on the float at the bottom of the tub to ensure it moves freely. It should spring back up without sticking.
*   **Check Drain Hose and Air Gap:** Inspect the drain hose for clogs. If you have an air gap on your sink, check it for blockages.

#### Advanced Diagnostics

*   **Look for Error Codes:** Check the display for any error codes. Note them down and consult your LG manual or LG's support website for their meaning.
*   **Listen for Inlet Valve:** When you try to start the dishwasher, listen for a hum or click near the bottom of the dishwasher. This indicates the inlet valve trying to open. No sound might mean a faulty valve.
*   **Consider Professional Help:** If all troubleshooting steps fail, it is time to call a qualified appliance technician. Issues like a faulty control board, wash motor, or internal wiring often require professional diagnosis and repair.

This systematic checklist helps identify the problem. You can often resolve minor issues yourself. For complex internal component failures, however, professional help is the best course of action. They have the tools and knowledge to diagnose and repair your LG dishwasher safely.

### FAQ Section

#### Why does my LG dishwasher display numbers but not start?

Your LG dishwasher displays numbers but does not start because it is usually counting down a delay start, or it is in a paused state. If it is not on delay, a safety interlock or system check has failed. Common reasons include an open door, activated child lock, or a water supply issue. The display shows time, but the cycle initiation is held until conditions are met.

#### How do I reset my LG dishwasher when it's counting down?

To reset your LG dishwasher when it is counting down, first, try pressing and holding the "Start/Pause" or "Cancel" button for a few seconds. If that does not work, go to your home's circuit breaker panel. Locate the breaker that controls your dishwasher. Flip the breaker off for 5-10 minutes, then turn it back on. This performs a hard reset, clearing the control board's memory.

#### What does a blinking light mean on my LG dishwasher?

A blinking light on your LG dishwasher often signals an error or a specific status. The meaning depends on which light is blinking. It could indicate a problem with the water supply, drainage, or a component failure. Sometimes, it signifies that the child lock is active. Refer to your LG dishwasher's user manual for a precise explanation of blinking lights for your model.

#### Can a clogged filter stop an LG dishwasher from starting?

Yes, a clogged filter can stop an LG dishwasher from starting. If the filter is severely clogged with food particles, it can prevent water from draining properly from the tub. This can activate the float switch, making the dishwasher think there is still standing water. The machine then refuses to start a new cycle to prevent overflow, even if it shows a countdown.

#### Is it a DIY fix or do I need a technician for this issue?

Many "countdown but not starting" issues are simple DIY fixes. Checking the circuit breaker, door latch, child lock, water valve, and cleaning the filter are common, easy repairs. However, if the problem involves a faulty water inlet valve, wash motor, control board, or complex wiring, a professional technician is typically needed. These repairs require specialized knowledge and tools.

#### What are common error codes that cause this?

Common error codes that cause an LG dishwasher to count down but not start include "IE" (Inlet Error, water supply problem), "OE" (Drain Error, water not draining), "LE" (Motor Lock Error, wash motor issue), and sometimes "FE" (Water Fill Error, overfilling or faulty water level sensor). These codes indicate specific issues that prevent the cycle from beginning.
