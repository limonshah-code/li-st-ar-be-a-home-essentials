### Frequently Asked Questions About Corelle Dishwashing

#### Q1: Will Corelle dishes break easily in the dishwasher?
No, Corelle dishes are highly resistant to breaking and chipping in the dishwasher. They are made from Vitrelle, a unique glass laminate known for its exceptional strength. This material is designed to withstand normal dishwasher cycles, including impacts from water jets and other dishes.

#### Q2: Do Corelle patterns fade after multiple washes in the dishwasher?
Corelle patterns are fused into the glass material itself during manufacturing. This process makes them highly resistant to fading or scratching, even after countless washes in the dishwasher. Your Corelle dishes should maintain their vibrant designs for many years of regular use.

#### Q3: Is it necessary to pre-rinse Corelle dishes before putting them in the dishwasher?
Pre-rinsing Corelle dishes is generally not necessary. The non-porous surface of Vitrelle glass prevents food from sticking stubbornly. For heavily soiled dishes, a quick scrape or light rinse might be helpful, but Corelle is designed for effective cleaning without extensive pre-treatment.

#### Q4: Can I use any type of dishwasher detergent with Corelle dishes?
Yes, you can use most standard automatic dishwasher detergents with Corelle dishes. Avoid highly abrasive cleaners or steel wool, as these could potentially scratch any dinnerware over time. Regular detergents are perfectly effective for cleaning Corelle's smooth, non-porous surface.

#### Q5: Will Corelle dishes get too hot in the dishwasher and crack?
Corelle dishes are designed to withstand significant temperature changes, including the high heat of a dishwasher. They will not crack from the hot water or drying cycles. Corelle is safe for use in microwaves and conventional ovens up to specific temperatures, further proving its thermal resilience.

#### Q6: What if my Corelle dishes have gray marks after dishwashing?
Gray marks on Corelle dishes are usually caused by rubbing against metal utensils or racks, not damage to the dish itself. These marks can typically be removed with a non-abrasive cleaner such as Bar Keepers Friend or a baking soda paste. Simply rub gently until the marks disappear.
