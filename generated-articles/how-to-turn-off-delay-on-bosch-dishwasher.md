### FAQ Section

#### Q1: Can I stop a Bosch dishwasher mid-cycle after the delay has passed?
Yes, you can stop a Bosch dishwasher mid-cycle, even if it started after a delay. Pressing the "Start/Reset" or "Cancel" button will typically pause or cancel the active wash cycle. The dishwasher will then drain the water before stopping. This allows you to open the door and add forgotten items or make adjustments.

#### Q2: What does "delay" mean on a Bosch dishwasher?
"Delay" on a Bosch dishwasher refers to the delay start function. This feature lets you postpone the beginning of a wash cycle for a set number of hours. For example, you can set it to start 3, 6, or 9 hours later. This is useful for running the dishwasher during off-peak energy hours or at a more convenient time.

#### Q3: Is it bad to frequently cancel the delay start?
No, it is not bad to frequently cancel the delay start. The dishwasher is designed to handle this. Canceling a delay simply resets the control panel. It does not cause any wear or damage to the appliance. Feel free to adjust your wash schedule as needed without worry.

#### Q4: My delay button is stuck, what should I do?
If your delay button feels stuck or unresponsive, first try a power cycle. Unplug the dishwasher or turn off its circuit breaker for 5-10 minutes. This often resolves control panel glitches. If the button remains physically stuck or non-responsive after a power cycle, contact Bosch customer support for a service inspection.

#### Q5: How do I know if the delay is successfully turned off?
You will know the delay is successfully turned off when the delay timer disappears from the display. Any indicator lights for the delay function should also turn off. The dishwasher's display might show a standard cycle time or simply be blank, ready for you to select a new wash program.

#### Q6: Does cancelling delay start waste water or energy?
Cancelling the delay start function before the wash cycle begins does not waste water or energy. The dishwasher has not yet started filling or heating water. If you cancel a cycle after it has already started, it might drain any water present. This would consume a small amount of water and electricity.
