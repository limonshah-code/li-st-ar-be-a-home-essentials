## Clean Your Speed Queen Washing Machine Filter for Peak Performance

A clean washing machine works better. If you own a Speed Queen, you know it builds tough machines. Even robust washers need regular care. I find many people overlook one crucial part: the washing machine filter. Knowing how to clean a Speed Queen washing machine filter is essential.

Cleaning your Speed Queen filter prevents common problems. These problems include bad odors, long drain times, and even water leaks. A blocked filter can reduce your washer's efficiency significantly. This guide will walk you through finding and cleaning your Speed Queen filter. We will discuss preventive care too. Let's keep your laundry fresh and your Speed Queen running smoothly.

### Takeaway:

*   **Locate the Filter:** Find your Speed Queen's drain pump filter, usually behind a access panel or near the drain hose.
*   **Prepare for Cleaning:** Gather towels, a shallow pan, and basic cleaning tools.
*   **Drain Water Safely:** Always drain residual water before opening the filter cap to prevent spills.
*   **Clean Thoroughly:** Remove debris from the filter and its housing using warm water and a brush.
*   **Regular Maintenance:** Clean the filter every 3-6 months to ensure optimal machine performance.

To clean a Speed Queen washing machine filter, first locate the drain pump filter, typically found at the machine's lower front or rear. Turn off power and drain residual water. Unscrew the filter cap, remove debris, rinse the filter under warm water, and scrub any stubborn buildup. Then, reinsert the clean filter and secure the cap.

### Understanding Your Speed Queen Washing Machine Filter System

Many Speed Queen washing machines feature a drain pump filter. This component catches lint, debris, and small items. Items like coins or buttons can otherwise clog your drain pump. This filter is a primary defense system for your machine. It ensures water drains efficiently and prevents pump damage.

You might wonder where this filter is. For most Speed Queen models, especially front-loaders, the drain pump filter is at the machine's front. It is often behind a small access panel near the bottom. Some top-loader models may not have an easily accessible filter for the user. Always check your specific model's manual for exact locations and instructions. However, many Speed Queen top loaders effectively filter lint through their agitation system or use a self-cleaning pump. For specific guidance on maintaining various Speed Queen models, including top loaders, you can refer to our guide on [how to clean a Speed Queen washing machine top loader](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-clean-speed-queen-washing-machine-top-loader).

The drain pump filter traps things that slip out of pockets or shed from clothes. These include lint, hair, coins, and small fabric pieces. Over time, these items accumulate. A buildup in the filter causes several issues. Your washing machine might not drain properly, leading to standing water. The wash cycle might take longer. You might even notice unpleasant odors from trapped grime.

Regular cleaning of this filter is crucial. It extends the life of your appliance. It also keeps your laundry fresh and clean. Ignoring this simple task can lead to costly repairs. A clean filter ensures smooth operation and peak performance. I always recommend checking your filter every few months.

### Gathering Your Tools for Effective Filter Cleaning

You need the right tools for any cleaning job. Cleaning your Speed Queen washing machine filter is no different. Having everything ready saves you time and prevents frustration. I always prepare my workspace before starting. This ensures a smooth and efficient cleaning process.

First, you will need a few old towels or absorbent cloths. These are essential for catching spilled water. Even after draining, some water always remains. I place several towels on the floor in front of the machine. This protects my flooring from any drips or puddles.

Next, find a shallow pan or baking sheet. This container will sit under the filter housing. It collects the water that drains when you open the filter. A low-profile pan works best for fitting into tight spaces. I sometimes use a small plastic storage container if I have one handy.

You will also need a small brush. An old toothbrush works perfectly for scrubbing the filter. It helps remove stubborn lint, grime, and debris. Sometimes, a flathead screwdriver is useful. You might need it to pry open the access panel, depending on your Speed Queen model. A pair of gloves can protect your hands, especially if the filter contains mold or mildew.

Finally, have some warm water and mild detergent ready. This mixture is for cleaning the filter itself. You might also want a small bucket to rinse the filter thoroughly. Having these items on hand makes the filter cleaning process much easier. It helps you complete the task quickly and effectively.

### Step-by-Step Guide: Accessing the Speed Queen Drain Pump Filter

Accessing the drain pump filter requires care and attention. I find it is best to follow steps carefully. This ensures your safety and prevents damage to your machine. Always start by prioritizing safety.

**1. Disconnect Power:**
First, unplug your Speed Queen washing machine from the wall outlet. This step is critical. It eliminates any risk of electrical shock. Do not proceed until the machine is completely disconnected from power. Safety comes first when working with appliances.

**2. Locate the Access Panel:**
The drain pump filter is typically at the bottom front of your Speed Queen. Look for a small square or rectangular panel. This panel usually blends into the machine's design. It might have a latch, clips, or a screw. For different models, the process of accessing internal components varies; you might find additional insights on [how to take apart a Speed Queen washing machine](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-take-apart-speed-queen-washing-machine) if the panel is particularly stubborn.

**3. Open the Access Panel:**
If there are screws, use a screwdriver to remove them. If it has clips, gently pry them open. Some panels just snap open. Be careful not to force anything. Forcing the panel can cause damage.

**4. Prepare for Water Drainage:**
Once the panel is open, you will see the filter cap. It often has a small hose next to it. Place your shallow pan and towels directly under the filter area. Even if your wash cycle finished, water often remains in the pump housing.

**5. Drain Residual Water:**
Many Speed Queen models have a small drain hose attached near the filter cap. This hose has a stopper. Pull the hose out from its clip. Remove the stopper and allow the water to drain into your pan. This process can take a few minutes. If your model lacks a small drain hose, slowly unscrew the main filter cap to let water trickle out into the pan. This prevents a large gush of water. Remember, this step is essential for a mess-free clean. For deeper understanding of how to manage water and hoses, especially when performing maintenance tasks, check out our guide on [how to remove drain hose from Speed Queen washing machine](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-remove-drain-hose-from-speed-queen-washing-machine).

### Cleaning the Speed Queen Washing Machine Filter Thoroughly

Once you access the filter and drain the residual water, it's time for the actual cleaning. This part requires a bit of elbow grease. A thorough cleaning ensures optimal performance from your Speed Queen. I find a methodical approach works best here.

**1. Remove the Filter Cap:**
Gently twist the filter cap counter-clockwise. It should unscrew easily after the water drains. Be prepared for a small amount of remaining water to trickle out. Carefully pull the filter straight out from its housing.

**2. Inspect and Remove Debris:**
Once the filter is out, you will likely see an accumulation of lint, hair, and maybe even small objects. I once found a forgotten crayon! Carefully pick out all the visible debris. Use your fingers or tweezers for smaller items. This initial removal gets rid of the biggest offenders.

**3. Scrub and Rinse the Filter:**
Take the filter to a sink. Rinse it under warm running water. Use your small brush or old toothbrush to scrub away any remaining lint, slime, or residue. Pay close attention to the mesh screen. Ensure all openings are clear. You can use a mild detergent solution to clean stubborn grime. A general guide on [how to clean washing machine filter](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-clean-washing-machine-filter) may offer additional universal tips for this step.

**4. Clean the Filter Housing:**
Do not forget the filter housing itself. Look inside the opening where the filter sits. You might see grime or mold buildup there. Use a damp cloth or your brush to clean the inside of the housing. Make sure it is free of any obstructions. This ensures the filter fits back correctly and maintains a clean environment.

**5. Check for Damage:**
Before reinserting, quickly inspect the filter. Look for any cracks or damage to the plastic or rubber seal. A damaged filter might not perform effectively. If you find damage, consider replacing the filter. A properly cleaned filter, free of damage, is crucial for your machine's function.

### Reassembling and Testing Your Speed Queen Washer

After a thorough cleaning, it is time to put everything back together. Reassembly is just as important as disassembly. Doing it correctly ensures your Speed Queen runs properly and without leaks. I always double-check my work during this stage.

**1. Reinsert the Clean Filter:**
Carefully slide the clean filter back into its housing. Make sure it aligns properly. It should slide in smoothly without force. If it feels stuck, check for any remaining debris in the housing.

**2. Secure the Filter Cap:**
Twist the filter cap clockwise until it is tight. It should feel snug. Do not overtighten, as this can damage the threads. A hand-tight seal is usually sufficient. Ensure the rubber gasket on the filter cap makes a good seal. This prevents water leaks.

**3. Replace the Drain Hose Stopper (If Applicable):**
If your model has a small drain hose, put the stopper back into the hose. Then, clip the hose back into its holder. This keeps it secure and out of the way.

**4. Close the Access Panel:**
Reattach the access panel. If there were screws, put them back in. If it was clips, snap them shut. Ensure the panel is securely closed. This protects the filter and completes the machine's aesthetic.

**5. Reconnect Power and Test for Leaks:**
Plug your Speed Queen washing machine back into the wall outlet. Now, perform a quick test run. I recommend running a short cycle, perhaps a rinse and spin. This lets you check for any leaks. During the cycle, carefully observe the area around the filter. Look for any drips or puddles. If you see any, immediately unplug the machine. Then, recheck the filter cap's tightness and the panel's seal. A successful test means your filter cleaning is complete. Your Speed Queen is ready for normal use again. This entire process contributes to the overall longevity and smooth functioning of your Speed Queen washer. ### Preventative Maintenance and Filter Cleaning Frequency

Cleaning your Speed Queen filter regularly is a simple habit. This habit prevents many common washing machine problems. I compare it to changing the oil in your car. It is a small effort that yields big returns in longevity and performance. Consistency is key here.

**Why Regular Cleaning Matters:**
A clean filter allows water to drain freely. This prevents excessive strain on your drain pump. It also stops lint and debris from recirculating onto your clothes. Moreover, a clogged filter creates an environment for mold and mildew. This causes unpleasant odors in your washer and on your laundry. Regular cleaning actively fights these issues. It keeps your clothes smelling fresh. It also helps your Speed Queen run efficiently, potentially saving energy.

**Recommended Cleaning Frequency:**
I suggest cleaning your Speed Queen washing machine filter every three to six months. This frequency is a general guideline. Your household's specific usage patterns influence the ideal schedule. If you wash a lot of pet hair, blankets, or heavily soiled items, you might need more frequent cleaning. For example, if you find excessive lint or debris after three months, consider cleaning it quarterly. Conversely, if your filter is relatively clean after six months, you might extend the interval slightly. Pay attention to your machine's signals. Slow draining or unusual noises are indicators that your filter needs attention sooner.

**Tips for Preventing Future Buildup:**
*   **Check Pockets:** Always empty pockets before washing clothes. Coins, tissues, and small toys are common filter culprits.
*   **Pre-Rinse Heavily Soiled Items:** Shake off excess dirt or debris from clothes before putting them in the washer.
*   **Wash Pet Beds Separately:** Items with heavy pet hair can quickly clog a filter. Consider using a lint roller first.
*   **Use Appropriate Detergent:** Too much detergent can create excessive suds, which can contribute to residue buildup. For guidance, refer to [what laundry detergent does Speed Queen recommend](https://homeessentialsguide.com/what-laundry-detergent-does-speed-queen-recommend).
*   **Run a Washer Cleaning Cycle:** Periodically run an empty hot water cycle with a washing machine cleaner. This helps remove soap scum and residue throughout the machine, including the filter area.

By following these preventative steps, you reduce the workload on your filter. This contributes to the overall health of your Speed Queen.

### Troubleshooting Common Filter-Related Issues

Even with regular cleaning, sometimes issues arise. Knowing how to troubleshoot can save you time and potential service calls. Most problems related to your Speed Queen washing machine filter are straightforward. I find that a little detective work often reveals the solution.

**1. Washer Not Draining or Draining Slowly:**
This is the most common sign of a clogged filter. If your machine leaves standing water or takes an unusually long time to complete a drain cycle, check the filter first. A filter packed with lint and debris restricts water flow. Clean the filter thoroughly as outlined in this guide. Also, inspect the drain hose for kinks or blockages. If the filter is clean and the hose is clear, the issue might be with the drain pump itself, requiring further investigation or professional help.

**2. Unpleasant Odors from the Washer:**
A dirty filter can harbor mold, mildew, and bacteria. These create foul smells. The odors can transfer to your clean laundry. If your clothes don't smell fresh, clean the filter. Also, clean the filter housing. Then, run a hot water cleaning cycle with white vinegar or a specialized washing machine cleaner. This removes any lingering odors from the entire system. For comprehensive guidance on internal cleaning, consider [what to put in a washing machine to clean it](https://homeessentialsguide.com/what-to-put-in-a-washing-machine-to-clean-it).

**3. Washer Making Unusual Noises:**
A rattling or grinding sound during the drain cycle often indicates something is trapped in the pump or filter. Coins, buttons, or other hard objects can get lodged. When the pump tries to operate, these items make noise. Access and clean the filter immediately. Remove any foreign objects. If the noise persists after cleaning, the pump might have damage, or something is stuck deeper in the drain system.

**4. Error Codes Related to Draining:**
Many modern Speed Queen machines display error codes for drainage issues. If your display shows a code like "Nd" (no drain) or similar, consult your user manual. Often, the first recommended fix is to check and clean the drain pump filter. This simple step can clear the error.

**5. Water Leaking from the Filter Area:**
If you notice water leaking from the front panel where the filter is located, it means the filter cap is not sealed correctly. This can happen if it is not tightened enough or if the rubber gasket on the cap is damaged. Unplug the machine, ensure the filter cap is tightly secured, and check the gasket for cracks or wear. Replace the gasket if it appears damaged. Reassemble and test the machine carefully to confirm the leak stops.

Addressing these issues promptly keeps your Speed Queen operating reliably. Regular filter checks prevent most of these problems from starting.

### FAQ Section

**How often should I clean my Speed Queen washing machine filter?**
You should clean your Speed Queen washing machine filter every 3 to 6 months. High usage or washing items with a lot of pet hair may require more frequent cleaning, potentially every 2-3 months. Listen to your machine; slow draining signals it needs attention.

**Where is the filter located on a Speed Queen washing machine?**
On most Speed Queen front-load washers, the drain pump filter is at the bottom front of the machine, often behind a small access panel. Some top-loader models may have a self-cleaning pump system and no user-accessible filter. Always check your specific model's manual for its exact location.

**What happens if I don't clean my Speed Queen washer filter?**
Neglecting to clean the filter can lead to several problems. These include slow or no draining, unpleasant odors from your machine and laundry, reduced washing efficiency, and potential damage to the drain pump, which can be costly to repair.

**Do all Speed Queen washing machines have a filter?**
Most Speed Queen front-load washing machines have an accessible drain pump filter. Many top-load models, particularly older ones, may not have a user-accessible filter, as they use different lint-trapping or self-cleaning mechanisms. Consult your machine's manual to confirm its specific filter design.

**What tools do I need to clean the filter?**
You will need old towels, a shallow pan (like a baking sheet), a small brush (an old toothbrush works well), and possibly a flathead screwdriver to open the access panel. Warm water and mild detergent are also helpful for cleaning the filter itself.

**Can a dirty filter cause bad odors in my laundry?**
Yes, a dirty washing machine filter can definitely cause bad odors. Lint, hair, and detergent residue trapped in the filter create a breeding ground for mold, mildew, and bacteria. These can produce foul smells that transfer directly to your freshly washed clothes.
