## Replace Your Kenmore Refrigerator Control Board Easily

Is your Kenmore refrigerator acting strange? Perhaps it is not cooling correctly, or the ice maker stopped working. These problems often point to a failing control board. Replacing this component might seem like a big job. However, with the right guidance, you can often do it yourself.

This guide provides simple, step-by-step instructions. I will show you how to diagnose a bad board, gather the right tools, and safely replace your Kenmore refrigerator control board. You can save money on repair costs and get your fridge running again. We will cover safety, part identification, and post-installation checks. My goal is to help you restore your refrigerator's function quickly.

## Understanding Your Kenmore Refrigerator Control Board

Every modern refrigerator relies on a brain to function. This brain is called the control board. Also known as the main control board or PCB (Printed Circuit Board), it manages most of the refrigerator's operations. This includes temperature regulation, fan speeds, defrost cycles, and dispenser functions. The control board receives signals from various sensors. It then sends commands to components like the compressor, fans, and heating elements.

When this crucial part fails, your refrigerator's performance drops. Common signs include inconsistent cooling or no cooling at all. You might notice unusual noises, or the ice maker stops producing ice. The water dispenser might not work correctly. Sometimes, the refrigerator display panel might show error codes. These codes often point to a specific system fault.

A faulty control board can cause many different issues. It might send incorrect voltage signals. This can damage other parts over time. Sometimes, visible damage like burnt spots or bulging capacitors on the board indicates a problem. Before starting any repair, it is wise to confirm the control board is indeed the issue. You can often check continuity or resistance on specific pins. This helps confirm the board's condition. If you are unsure about testing, a professional can assist. Learning [how to test an LG refrigerator main control board](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-test-lg-refrigerator-main-control-board) might give you insights, as the principles are similar across brands. This step ensures you are replacing the correct part, saving time and money.

## Essential Tools and Safety Precautions

Before you begin any appliance repair, preparing your workspace is key. You need a few basic tools. These include screwdrivers (Phillips and flat-head), a nut driver set, and possibly a pair of needle-nose pliers. A multimeter is also helpful for testing electrical continuity. Safety glasses protect your eyes from debris. You might also want work gloves for better grip and protection.

Safety is the most important aspect of any DIY repair. Your Kenmore refrigerator uses electricity, which can be dangerous. Always unplug the refrigerator from the wall outlet before starting any work. This removes all power from the unit. Do not just turn off the appliance; pull the plug completely. This prevents accidental shocks.

Static electricity can also damage sensitive electronic components like the control board. Consider wearing an anti-static wrist strap. You can also touch a grounded metal object before handling the new board. This discharges any static buildup from your body. Work in a well-lit area. Make sure there is enough space around the refrigerator. This allows for safe movement and easy access to parts. Handle the new control board by its edges to avoid touching the components directly. These simple steps keep you safe and protect your appliance.

Finding the correct replacement part is crucial for a successful repair. Kenmore refrigerators come in many models. Each model can have specific parts. Using the wrong control board will not work. It could also damage your appliance. Therefore, accurate identification is necessary.

Start by locating your refrigerator's model and serial number. These numbers are usually on a sticker inside the refrigerator compartment. You might find it on the side wall, near the top, or behind the kick plate. Sometimes, the sticker is on the back of the unit. Once you find these numbers, write them down carefully. This information tells you exactly which refrigerator you have. Consider buying original equipment manufacturer (OEM) parts when possible. These parts offer the best compatibility and reliability. Aftermarket parts can be cheaper but might not perform as well. Verify the return policy of the retailer before making a purchase. This protects you in case the part does not fit or work correctly.

## Locating and Accessing the Control Board

The control board's location varies depending on your Kenmore refrigerator model. Knowing where to look saves time and effort. Most Kenmore refrigerators have the main control board in one of two common places. It can be on the back of the refrigerator, inside a plastic enclosure. Or it might be on the top of the refrigerator, under a service panel. Some side-by-side models might have part of the board in the fresh food compartment.

If the board is on the back, you will see a large metal or plastic cover. This cover usually protects the compressor and other electrical components. Use your nut driver or screwdriver to remove the screws holding this cover in place. Carefully lift the cover off and set it aside. You should then see the control board mounted inside. It will have many wires connected to it. Remember to always unplug the refrigerator before accessing any internal components. This ensures your safety while working on the appliance.

For models with the board on top, you might need to slide the refrigerator out from the wall. Look for a panel near the hinges or at the very back top of the unit. This panel often lifts or slides off after removing a few screws. Once inside, the control board will be visible. It is essential to take photos of the wire connections before disconnecting anything. This helps you reassemble everything correctly. Accessing internal components can sometimes involve similar steps to tasks like replacing an [evaporator fan motor on a Kenmore refrigerator](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-replace-kenmore-refrigerator-evaporator-fan-motor). This familiarity helps in accessing the right areas. Some components, like the [water inlet valve on a Kenmore refrigerator](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-replace-kenmore-refrigerator-water-inlet-valve), might be near the board, but they are separate systems. Knowing how to open up these access points is key.

## Step-by-Step Control Board Replacement

Once you have located the control board and taken safety precautions, you can begin the replacement. Remember, you must unplug the refrigerator from the wall outlet. This is your first and most important step. Do not proceed until the power is off. This prevents electrical shock.

Start by carefully documenting all wire connections. I use my phone to take clear photos of every wire before I touch it. This helps me remember exactly where each wire connects. Some wires might be color-coded, but photo documentation is always best. You can also use masking tape and a marker to label each wire and its corresponding terminal on the old board. This makes reinstallation much simpler.

Next, disconnect all the wires from the old control board. These wires often have push-on connectors or clips. Gently pull on the connector housing, not the wires themselves. Wires can break if you pull too hard. Once all wires are free, remove the screws or clips holding the old board in place. Lift the old board out. Pay attention to how it sits in its mounting bracket. This helps you install the new one correctly.

Now, take your new Kenmore refrigerator control board. Hold it by its edges to avoid touching the components. Place the new board into the mounting bracket. Secure it with the screws or clips you removed earlier. Make sure it sits firmly in place. Reconnect all the wires to their correct terminals. Refer to your photos or labels. Double-check every connection to ensure it is secure. A loose connection can cause the new board to malfunction. The process is similar to how you would [replace a GE refrigerator dispenser control board](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-replace-ge-refrigerator-dispenser-control-board), where careful wiring is essential. Ensure all wires are clear of moving parts or sharp edges.

## Testing and Post-Installation Steps

After installing the new Kenmore refrigerator control board and reconnecting all wires, it is time to reassemble the access panels. Secure any covers or panels you removed. Make sure all screws are tightened. Once everything is back in place, you can restore power to the refrigerator. Plug the appliance back into the wall outlet.

The refrigerator will likely power on immediately. Listen for the compressor to start running and the fans to turn on. It might take some time for the refrigerator to reach its set temperature. Give it at least 24 hours to stabilize. Check the temperature in both the fresh food and freezer compartments with a thermometer. Ensure they are within the recommended range. The fresh food section should be around 37-40°F (3-4°C). The freezer should be 0°F (-18°C).

Test all other functions. Check the ice maker to see if it starts producing ice. Dispense water from the dispenser. Verify that the interior lights work. Also, check the display panel for any error codes. If you see new codes, consult your refrigerator's user manual for troubleshooting steps. Sometimes, a control board might need a reset after installation. This is often done by simply unplugging and replugging the fridge again after a few minutes. Some models, like specific [Whirlpool French door refrigerators, might require a control board reset](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-reset-whirlpool-french-door-refrigerator-control-board) to clear old error codes or initiate proper function. If your refrigerator does not cool or perform correctly, recheck all wire connections. Make sure no wires are pinched. If problems persist, it might be a different issue, or the new board could be faulty. Sometimes, a refrigerator might [not be cold enough](https://homeessentialsguide.com/why-is-my-kenmore-refrigerator-not-cold-enough) even after a board replacement, indicating another component failure.

## When to Call a Professional

Replacing a Kenmore refrigerator control board can be a rewarding DIY project. However, there are times when calling a professional technician is the best choice. If you feel uncomfortable with electrical work, do not attempt this repair. Safety is always paramount. A professional has the training and tools to diagnose issues safely.

If you have replaced the control board and your refrigerator still does not work, the problem might lie elsewhere. Diagnosing complex electrical issues can be difficult without specialized knowledge. For example, a failing compressor or a sealed system leak needs expert attention. These repairs involve refrigerants and specialized equipment. Only certified technicians can handle them. If your refrigerator frequently [freezes up](https://homeessentialsguide.com/why-does-my-kenmore-refrigerator-keep-freezing-up) even after a new board, it might be a defrost system issue.

Moreover, if your refrigerator is still under warranty, performing DIY repairs might void it. Check your warranty terms before starting any work. In such cases, contacting Kenmore service or an authorized repair center is wise. They can perform the repair without voiding your coverage. If the initial diagnosis of a bad control board was incorrect, a professional can identify the true cause. They can save you from replacing parts unnecessarily. Consider professional help if the repair seems too complex or if multiple issues persist after your attempt.

## FAQ Section

### How much does a Kenmore refrigerator control board replacement cost?

The cost of a Kenmore refrigerator control board replacement varies. The part itself typically costs between $100 and $300. This depends on your specific Kenmore model. If you hire a professional technician, labor costs will add another $100 to $300. Doing it yourself saves you the labor fee, making the total cost much lower.

### What are common signs of a bad Kenmore control board?

Common signs of a faulty Kenmore control board include inconsistent cooling, a refrigerator not cooling at all, or no power to the unit. The ice maker or water dispenser may stop working. You might also see error codes on the display. Sometimes, lights do not work, or the defrost cycle fails.

### Can I replace my Kenmore control board myself?

Yes, many people can replace their Kenmore refrigerator control board themselves. It requires basic tools and an understanding of electrical safety. The process involves identifying the correct part, locating the board, disconnecting wires, and installing the new one. Following a detailed guide makes the job manageable for DIYers.

### Do Kenmore refrigerator control boards need programming after replacement?

Most Kenmore refrigerator control boards do not require special programming after replacement. They are usually plug-and-play components. Once installed and powered on, the new board should function immediately. However, some advanced models might require a simple reset procedure, like unplugging and re-plugging the appliance.

### Where is the control board located on most Kenmore refrigerators?

On most Kenmore refrigerators, the main control board is in one of two places. It is often located on the back of the refrigerator, behind a removable service panel. Alternatively, it might be found on the top of the refrigerator, also under a protective cover. Always unplug the unit before opening any access panels.
