## Why Is Your LG Washing Machine Not Draining? Find the Fix!

It is frustrating to open your washing machine door and see a tub full of standing water. This means your LG washing machine is not draining. A draining problem stops the laundry cycle cold. It leaves your clothes soaking wet. I know this issue can disrupt your entire day. This comprehensive guide will help you understand common reasons for this problem. You will also learn how to fix it yourself. We will explore simple clogs and more complex component failures.

### Takeaway

If your LG washing machine is not draining, check these items first:
*   Inspect the drain hose for any kinks or blockages.
*   Clean the drain pump filter, usually found at the front of your washer.
*   Ensure the lid switch or door latch assembly works correctly.
*   Verify the drain pump operates and is free of obstructions.
*   Check for issues with the water level pressure sensor or main control board.

Your LG washing machine is likely not draining due to a clogged drain pump filter, a kinked or blocked drain hose, or a malfunctioning drain pump itself. Less common causes include a faulty lid switch or door latch, an inaccurate pressure sensor, or a problem with the main control board, all of which prevent the washer from completing its crucial drainage cycle.

### Understanding the Drain Cycle in Your LG Washing Machine

Every washing machine must drain water to finish its cycle. Your LG washer follows a specific sequence to achieve this. First, the main control board sends an electrical signal. This signal activates the drain pump. The pump then pushes the water out of the drum. This water travels through the drain hose. Finally, the water exits into your household standpipe or utility sink.

Problems at any stage of this process can prevent drainage. A blockage in the hose will stop water flow. A faulty pump cannot push water out effectively. Even a small electrical issue can disrupt the entire cycle. Understanding these steps helps you pinpoint the exact location of the fault. This knowledge helps you fix the issue faster and more efficiently.

Your LG washing machine relies on a synchronized system. The water level sensor also plays a role. It tells the control board when water is present. If the sensor gives a wrong reading, the drain cycle may not start. This entire system works together to remove dirty water. When one part fails, the whole process stops.

### The Clogged Drain Pump Filter: A Common Culprit

One of the most frequent reasons an LG washing machine does not drain is a clogged drain pump filter. This filter is designed to catch lint, coins, buttons, and other small items. These objects should not enter the drain system. Over time, these collected items can completely block the filter. A blocked filter prevents water from reaching the pump. This stops the drainage process. I have often found surprising items in these filters, from socks to hairpins.

You will typically find the drain pump filter at the bottom front of your LG washing machine. It is usually behind a small access panel or kick plate. To clean it, first ensure the machine is turned off and unplugged for safety. Place a shallow pan or old towels under the filter area to catch residual water. Slowly unscrew the filter. Remove any debris you find inside. Rinse the filter thoroughly under running water. Then, screw it back in tightly and close the access panel. Regular cleaning of this filter, every 1-3 months, prevents major drainage issues and ensures your washer runs smoothly.

### Inspecting and Clearing the Drain Hose

The drain hose is another vital part of your washer's drainage system. It carries water from the washing machine to your home's drain. A common issue is a kink in the hose. If the hose is bent sharply, water cannot flow through it properly. The hose might also have an internal blockage. Lint, soap scum, or small foreign objects can build up inside. This creates a hidden obstruction over time.

Begin by pulling your LG washing machine away from the wall. This provides clear access to the back of the appliance. Inspect the entire length of the external drain hose. Look for any visible kinks, twists, or sharp bends. Straighten any you discover. If the hose appears clear externally, you may need to detach it. Disconnect it from both the washer's rear outlet and the standpipe or utility sink. Visually inspect the hose for internal blockages. You can also flush it with water or use a long, flexible brush to clear obstructions. Reconnect the hose securely afterward, ensuring proper routing to prevent future kinks.

*   **Hose Kinks:** Ensure the external drain hose forms a gentle curve. It should not be sharply bent or compressed against the wall.
*   **Internal Blockages:** Small items, lint, or detergent residue can accumulate inside the hose. This reduces its diameter and restricts water flow.
*   **Standpipe Height:** The drain hose should not be inserted too far into the standpipe. It also should not rest too low. This can cause siphoning issues where water drains prematurely. Consult your LG user manual for the recommended drain hose height and loop configuration.

### Addressing a Faulty Drain Pump

If the filter and hose are confirmed clear, the problem might lie with the drain pump itself. The drain pump is a motor-driven component that actively expels water from the washer drum. If this pump fails, water will remain in the tub. A faulty pump might make a loud humming or grinding noise but fail to drain water. It could also make no sound at all if it's completely dead. Sometimes, a foreign object can jam the impeller inside the pump. This stops it from spinning and moving water.

Testing the drain pump requires some electrical knowledge and safety precautions. Always unplug the washer from its power source before you begin.
