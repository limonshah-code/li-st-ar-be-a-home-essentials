### FAQ Section

#### How often should I vacuum my area rug?
You should vacuum most area rugs at least once a week. For high-traffic areas, homes with pets, or individuals with allergies, I recommend vacuuming two to three times per week. Regular vacuuming removes surface dirt, dust, and allergens, preventing them from settling deep into the fibers. This maintains a clean appearance and helps preserve the rug's quality.

#### Can I machine wash all area rugs?
No, you cannot machine wash all area rugs. Only rugs with specific care labels indicating "machine washable" should go into a washing machine. Most synthetic or cotton rugs, especially smaller ones, are suitable. I always advise checking the care tag on your rug first to prevent damage, as delicate materials like wool, silk, or jute often require professional cleaning or hand-washing.

#### When is it time for professional rug cleaning?
I generally recommend professional rug cleaning every 12 to 18 months for most area rugs. However, this frequency can increase to every 6-12 months for rugs in homes with pets, young children, or high foot traffic. Professional cleaning deeply extracts embedded dirt, revitalizes colors, and addresses tough stains that home methods cannot handle effectively.

#### How do I deal with pet accidents on my area rug?
Address pet accidents immediately to prevent odors and stains from setting. First, blot up as much liquid as possible with a clean, absorbent cloth, pressing firmly. Avoid rubbing, as this can spread the stain. Then, use an enzymatic pet stain remover, following the product instructions carefully. I always recommend testing the cleaner on an inconspicuous area of the rug first.

#### Does a rug pad affect cleaning frequency?
A rug pad does not directly affect how often you *clean* your rug, but it significantly helps *maintain* its cleanliness and extends its life. A good rug pad prevents dirt and dust from sifting through to your floor, making vacuuming more effective. It also adds cushioning, which protects rug fibers from wear and tear, meaning your rug stays in better condition between cleanings.
