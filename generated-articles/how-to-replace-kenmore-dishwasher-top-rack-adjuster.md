Empty your dishwasher completely. Remove all dishes, silverware, and any other items. Make sure both the top and bottom racks are empty. Also, take out the lower dish rack. This gives you more room to work. It prevents items from getting in the way. It also reduces the risk of breaking dishes.

## Step-by-Step Guide: Removing the Old Adjuster

Now that you have prepared your workspace and ensured safety, it is time to remove the faulty adjuster. This part requires careful attention. You need to understand how the existing adjuster is attached. This helps when installing the new one. I will guide you through each part of the removal process.

**1. Remove the Top Dishwasher Rack:**
First, you need to take the top rack out of the dishwasher.
*   **Pull the Rack Out:** Gently pull the top rack fully forward on its glides.
*   **Locate Rack Stops:** At the end of the glides, you will find stops. These prevent the rack from coming all the way out. Some Kenmore models have small plastic clips. You might need to unclip them. Other models might require you to push a tab. Still, others have simple rubber stoppers you can pull off.
*   **Slide the Rack Off:** Once the stops are removed or disengaged, carefully slide the entire top rack completely out of the dishwasher. Place it on your prepared soft work surface.

**2. Detach Adjuster Components:**
The top rack adjuster connects to both the rack and the rack glides. It usually involves a combination of clips, screws, or plastic tabs.
*   **Examine the Adjuster:** Look closely at how the old adjuster is connected. Take pictures with your phone if it helps. This visual reference can be helpful during reassembly.
*   **Remove Screws/Clips:** Use your screwdriver to remove any screws holding the adjuster in place. Carefully unclip any plastic tabs or clips. They often have small locking mechanisms. You might need a flathead screwdriver to gently pry them open. Be gentle with plastic parts; they can break easily if forced.
*   **Separate from Rack Rails:** The adjuster often has small wheels or rollers. These sit in the dishwasher's side rails. Disconnect these from the rack itself. The adjuster usually consists of a left and right side. They connect to the top rack. They also connect to the glide wheels.

**3. Inspect for Damage:**
Once the old adjuster is off, take a moment to look at it.
*   **Identify Failure Point:** See where it broke. This helps confirm you are replacing the correct part.
*   **Check Other Components:** Look at the rack tines. Inspect the rack wheels that connect to the adjuster. Are they also worn or damaged? If so, you might consider replacing those parts too. Catching these issues now saves future work.

I find that observing how things come apart is key to putting them back together correctly. Do not force anything. If a part seems stuck, look for a hidden clip or screw. Patience makes this step much easier. This systematic approach ensures a clean removal. It prepares you for a smooth installation of the new part. ## Installing Your New Kenmore Top Rack Adjuster

With the old, broken adjuster removed, you are ready to install the new one. This is the reverse of the removal process. Pay close attention to how the new part aligns. Make sure it fits snugly. I always take my time with new parts. This ensures they are installed correctly.

**1. Position the New Adjuster:**
*   **Match Sides:** The new adjuster usually comes as a left and right assembly. Make sure you have them on the correct sides of the rack. There are often markings like 'L' and 'R'.
*   **Align with Rack:** Align the new adjuster with the attachment points on the top rack. The new part should fit perfectly. It should line up with the holes or slots where the old one was.

**2. Secure the Components:**
*   **Attach to Rack:** Begin by attaching the new adjuster to the top rack itself. If there are screws, insert and tighten them. Do not overtighten, especially on plastic. If there are clips, push them firmly until they "click" into place. Ensure all connections are secure. The adjuster should feel firm on the rack. It should not wiggle.
*   **Connect to Glide Wheels/Rollers:** Next, attach the adjuster to the components that will slide onto the dishwasher rails. These are typically wheels or small plastic blocks. Make sure these are securely fastened to the adjuster. They should rotate or move freely as intended. This step might vary slightly depending on your specific Kenmore model.

**3. Reattach to Dishwasher Rack Glides:**
Now, you need to put the entire top rack assembly back into the dishwasher.
*   **Slide onto Rails:** Carefully slide the top rack, with the new adjusters attached, back onto the dishwasher's side rails. Guide the wheels or rollers of the adjuster into the tracks.
*   **Secure Rack Stops:** Reinstall the rack stops at the end of the glides. Push the clips back into place. Or, replace the rubber stoppers. This prevents the rack from accidentally sliding out during use.

**4. Test the Adjustment Mechanism:**
Before you celebrate, test the new adjuster.
*   **Cycle Through Heights:** Push the rack in and out a few times. Then, try adjusting its height. Most Kenmore adjusters have levers or buttons on either side. Press or lift these. See if the rack smoothly moves up and down. Does it lock securely into different height positions?
*   **Check for Level:** Ensure the rack is level in all positions. If it sags or leans, something might not be seated correctly. Recheck all connections.

I find that testing is key. It confirms your work is solid. It also gives you confidence. A smoothly operating rack means a successful repair. ## Testing and Final Checks

You have done a great job installing the new Kenmore dishwasher top rack adjuster. But the repair is not finished until you have tested everything thoroughly. This ensures safety and proper function. I always perform a series of checks. This confirms the repair is complete and robust.

**1. Cycle Through Heights:**
*   **Engage Adjustment:** Use the levers or buttons on each side of the top rack adjuster. Cycle the rack through all its possible height settings.
*   **Verify Locking:** Ensure the rack securely locks into each position. It should not slip or drop. Check both sides of the rack. They should move together evenly. If one side sticks or does not lock, recheck its connection. There might be a clip that is not fully engaged.

**2. Load Test (Optional but Recommended):**
*   **Add Lightweight Items:** Place a few light dishes or glasses onto the top rack.
*   **Check Stability:** Gently push and pull the rack. See if it feels stable. Does it wobble excessively? Does it stay level with the weight? This simple test simulates real-world use. It helps identify any lingering instability. If the rack feels wobbly, re-examine the adjuster connections.

**3. Reconnect Power:**
Once you are confident the adjuster is installed correctly and functioning well, it is time to restore power.
*   **Return to Breaker Box:** Go back to your home's circuit breaker panel.
*   **Flip Breaker ON:** Flip the dishwasher's circuit breaker back to the "ON" position. If you unplugged it, plug the dishwasher back into the outlet.

**4. Run a Short Cycle:**
*   **Empty Cycle:** I recommend running a short, empty wash cycle.
*   **Listen for Issues:** Pay attention during the cycle. Listen for any unusual noises coming from the rack area. Check for any signs of the rack moving improperly. The spray arms need to rotate freely. The rack should not obstruct them. This final check confirms everything works under normal operating conditions. ## Common Issues and Troubleshooting Tips

Even with a careful installation, you might encounter small issues. Do not get discouraged. Many common problems have simple solutions. I have dealt with these before. I can help you troubleshoot.

**1. Rack Not Level After Installation:**
*   **Check Connections:** This is often due to one side of the adjuster not being fully seated. Carefully re-examine both the left and right sides. Make sure all clips are snapped. Ensure any screws are tight.
*   **Verify Orientation:** Double-check that you installed the adjuster components on the correct sides. Some parts are specific to left or right. They will not work properly if reversed.
*   **Inspect Rails:** Sometimes the dishwasher's main slide rails themselves can get bent. Look closely at them. Make sure they are straight and free of obstructions.

**2. Difficulty Adjusting Rack Height:**
*   **Clear Obstructions:** Small debris or food particles can get into the adjustment mechanism. Clean the area around the levers or buttons.
*   **Lubricate (Carefully):** If the mechanism feels stiff, you can try a tiny amount of food-grade silicone lubricant. Apply it sparingly to the moving parts. Avoid using petroleum-based lubricants. These are not safe for dishwashers.
*   **Check for Bent Components:** Inspect the new adjuster carefully. Look for any bent plastic or metal pieces that might be catching. If you suspect a faulty new part, contact the supplier.

**3. Loose Connections or Rack Wiggles Excessively:**
*   **Tighten Screws/Clips:** Go back and ensure all screws are fully tightened. Check all clips. Push them until they are firmly locked. Sometimes a clip might look closed but is not fully engaged.
*   **Part Compatibility:** Reconfirm that you used the exact replacement part for your Kenmore model. An adjuster that is almost right can lead to a loose fit.
*   **Examine the Rack Itself:** The top rack frame might be slightly bent or damaged. This can prevent the adjuster from seating properly.

**When to Call a Professional:**
Most rack adjuster issues are DIY-friendly. However, if you have checked everything and the problem persists, it might be time for expert help.
*   **Persistent Leaks:** If you notice any water leaks after the repair, stop using the dishwasher and call a technician.
*   **Electrical Issues:** Any flickering lights or strange electrical smells mean you should unplug the dishwasher. Then call a professional immediately.
*   **Structural Damage:** If the main dishwasher tub or the rack rails themselves are severely bent or damaged, a professional can assess the extent of the repair needed.

I believe in tackling what you can. But I also know when to call in the experts. Your safety is most important. These tips help you identify and solve common issues. They also guide you on when professional assistance is needed.
