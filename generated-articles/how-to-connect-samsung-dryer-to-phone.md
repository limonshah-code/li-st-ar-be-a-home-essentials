### Step-by-Step Guide: Connecting Your Samsung Dryer

Connecting your Samsung dryer to your phone through SmartThings is a systematic process. It involves preparing the dryer, initiating the connection from your phone, and confirming the pairing. I always make sure I'm near the appliance during this part. This allows me to react quickly to any prompts on the dryer's display.

Follow these steps carefully for a successful setup. Each step builds on the last. Precision here ensures a smooth smart laundry experience.

#### Preparing Your Dryer for Connection

Before you open the SmartThings app, prepare your dryer. First, ensure your dryer is plugged in and powered on. Check that its display is active. Next, you need to put your dryer into "AP" (Access Point) mode or "Connect" mode. The exact method varies by model. Usually, you press and hold a specific button on the dryer's control panel. This might be the "Smart Control," "Smart Connect," or "Wi-Fi" button.

Refer to your dryer's user manual for precise instructions. The dryer's display will often show "AP" or a Wi-Fi icon blinking. This indicates it is ready to connect. If it does not enter this mode, try restarting the dryer by unplugging it for 30 seconds.

#### Initiating the Connection Process

Now, open the SmartThings app on your phone. On the main screen, tap the "Add device" or "+" icon. This is usually located in the top right corner. The app will then ask you what type of device you want to add. Select "Dryer" from the list of appliance types. If you don't see it, you can select "Scan for nearby devices" or "By brand" then choose Samsung.

The app will then guide you. It often suggests connecting to a temporary Wi-Fi network created by the dryer itself. This temporary connection helps the dryer join your home Wi-Fi. This process is similar to how you might [connect a Samsung air conditioner to Wi-Fi](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-connect-samsung-air-conditioner-to-wifi) or other smart appliances. Ensure your phone is close to the dryer during this step.

#### Completing the Pairing

After your phone connects to the dryer's temporary network, the SmartThings app will ask for your home Wi-Fi password. Enter your Wi-Fi network name (SSID) and password carefully. Double-check for typos. The app will then transfer these details to your dryer. Your dryer will attempt to join your home Wi-Fi network.

Once successful, the dryer's Wi-Fi indicator light will stop blinking and become solid. The SmartThings app will confirm the connection. You can then name your dryer within the app. Choose a clear name like "Laundry Room Dryer." This helps identify it if you have multiple smart appliances. Congratulations, your Samsung dryer is now connected to your phone!

Press this button until the corresponding indicator light turns on. This light confirms the dryer is ready to receive remote commands. If you don't activate this, the SmartThings app will only allow you to monitor status. It will not let you start a cycle. This step ensures that the dryer only starts when someone has physically prepared it for operation.

### Troubleshooting Common Samsung Dryer Connection Issues

Sometimes, the connection process does not go as smoothly as planned. It can be frustrating when your Samsung dryer won't connect to your phone. Don't worry; many common issues have simple fixes. I've encountered a few myself and found that patience and systematic checking usually resolve them.

This section covers the most frequent problems users face. We will offer solutions for Wi-Fi, app, and dryer-specific issues. A little troubleshooting can get you back on track.

#### Wi-Fi Connectivity Problems

One of the most common issues is Wi-Fi related. Ensure your home Wi-Fi network is active and stable. Check your router to confirm it is broadcasting. Your Samsung dryer needs to connect to a 2.4 GHz Wi-Fi band. Many modern routers have both 2.4 GHz and 5 GHz bands. During setup, make sure your phone is also connected to the 2.4 GHz band. After the dryer connects, your phone can switch back to 5 GHz if desired.

If your dryer still won't connect, try moving your router closer to the dryer. Obstacles like thick walls can weaken the signal. Restarting your Wi-Fi router and your dryer can also resolve temporary glitches. Unplug the router for 30 seconds, then plug it back in. Do the same for your dryer. For more specific Wi-Fi issues, you might want to read our guide on [why your Samsung dryer won't connect to Wi-Fi](https://homeessentialsguide.com/why-wont-my-samsung-dryer-connect-to-wifi).

#### App Malfunctions and Updates

The SmartThings app itself can sometimes cause issues. First, ensure your SmartThings app is updated to the latest version. App developers frequently release updates to fix bugs and improve performance. Visit your phone's app store and check for updates. If an update is available, install it.

If the app freezes or behaves unexpectedly, try force-closing it. Then, reopen the app. If that does not work, uninstall and reinstall the SmartThings app. This can clear corrupted data. Make sure you remember your Samsung account login details before reinstalling. Sometimes, a simple phone restart can also resolve minor app glitches.

#### Dryer Not Appearing in SmartThings

If your dryer is in AP mode but does not appear in the SmartThings app, check a few things. First, ensure your phone is close to the dryer. Proximity helps with initial device discovery. Make sure the dryer is still in AP mode. The light should be blinking. If it times out, you will need to re-enable it.

Confirm your phone is connected to the same 2.4 GHz Wi-Fi network you intend to connect the dryer to. If your network has a very long or complex password, simplify it temporarily if possible (then change it back). Sometimes, network security settings can block new devices. Temporarily disable any VPNs or advanced firewall settings on your phone or router during the connection process. If all else fails, a factory reset of your dryer's network settings (refer to your manual) might be necessary.

### FAQ Section

**Can I connect my old Samsung dryer to my phone?**
Most older Samsung dryers do not have Wi-Fi capabilities. Only models explicitly designed as "smart" or "Wi-Fi enabled" can connect to the SmartThings app. Check your dryer's control panel for a Wi-Fi symbol or a "Smart Control" button. Consult your user manual for specific model information regarding smart features.

**Do I need Wi-Fi to connect my Samsung dryer to my phone?**
Yes, a stable home Wi-Fi network is essential. Your Samsung dryer connects to your Wi-Fi network, and your phone, through the SmartThings app, communicates with the dryer over this network. Both your phone and dryer must be connected to the same Wi-Fi network (typically 2.4 GHz for initial setup).

**What can I do with my Samsung dryer connected to SmartThings?**
With your dryer connected, you can remotely start or pause cycles, monitor remaining time, receive notifications when a cycle finishes, and troubleshoot issues. Some models offer energy monitoring and cycle recommendations. It offers greater convenience and control over your laundry tasks from anywhere.

**Why is my dryer not showing up in the SmartThings app?**
Ensure your dryer is in AP (Access Point) mode, usually indicated by a blinking Wi-Fi light. Verify your phone is connected to the same 2.4 GHz Wi-Fi network. Check for strong Wi-Fi signal near the dryer. Restarting your router and dryer, or reinstalling the SmartThings app, can often resolve this issue.

**Can I connect my Samsung dryer to Google Home/Alexa?**
Yes, you can integrate your connected Samsung dryer with voice assistants like Google Home or Amazon Alexa. You will need to link your SmartThings account to your Google Home or Alexa app. Once linked, you can use voice commands to check dryer status or start cycles (after enabling remote control on the dryer).

**Is connecting my dryer to my phone secure?**
Samsung implements security measures for its smart appliances. However, your overall smart home security depends on your Wi-Fi network security. Use a strong, unique Wi-Fi password, enable WPA2/WPA3 encryption, and keep your dryer's firmware updated. This helps protect your appliance and data.
