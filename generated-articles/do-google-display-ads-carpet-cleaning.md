### Key Metrics to Track

*   **Impressions:** The number of times your ad was shown. This tells you about your ad's visibility.
*   **Clicks:** The number of times people clicked on your ad. This indicates interest in your offer.
*   **Click-Through Rate (CTR):** Clicks divided by impressions. A higher CTR means your ad is more engaging.
*   **Conversions:** Specific actions taken after clicking your ad, like a phone call or quote request. This is your ultimate goal.
*   **Cost Per Click (CPC):** The average cost you pay for each click. This helps manage your budget.
*   **Cost Per Conversion (CPA):** The total cost divided by the number of conversions. This metric shows how efficient your ads are at generating leads.

These metrics provide a clear picture of your campaign's health. They help you identify areas for improvement. Focusing on conversions and CPA is especially important for local businesses.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid with Google Display Ads

Running Google Display Ads for your carpet cleaning business can be very effective, but pitfalls exist. Avoiding common mistakes saves money and improves results. Many businesses make errors that hinder their campaigns. I want to highlight these issues so you can steer clear of them. Understanding these potential problems is the first step to successful advertising.

One frequent mistake is neglecting proper targeting. If your ads show up to everyone, everywhere, you waste money. Your ads might appear to people outside your service area or to individuals who will never need carpet cleaning. This leads to many impressions but very few meaningful clicks or conversions. Precise targeting is vital for local businesses.

Another common error is using weak or generic ad creatives. If your images are blurry, your message is unclear, or your call-to-action is missing, people will ignore your ad. In a visually saturated online world, your ad needs to capture attention instantly. Subpar visuals reflect poorly on your brand.

### Not Using Negative Placements

Negative placements are a crucial but often overlooked optimization tool. These allow you to prevent your ads from showing on specific websites or apps. For instance, you might find your carpet cleaning ads appearing on gaming sites or forums irrelevant to home services. Such placements generate impressions but no quality leads.

By regularly reviewing where your ads are shown, you can identify and exclude these irrelevant placements. This refines your audience further. It ensures your ads appear only on websites that align with your target customer's interests. This practice directly improves your ad spend efficiency.

### Failing to A/B Test Ads

Many businesses create one set of ads and run them indefinitely. This is a missed opportunity. Without A/B testing, you cannot know which ad elements are most effective. You will never discover if a different headline or a new image could double your click-through rate. Continuous testing is not optional; it is essential for optimization.

Always have at least two versions of your ad running. Compare their performance after a reasonable period. Learn from the data and adapt your strategy. This iterative process leads to stronger, more effective ads over time. A/B testing is a simple yet powerful way to improve your ad campaigns.

### Ignoring Conversion Tracking

If you are running ads, you need to know if they are generating results. Ignoring conversion tracking means you do not know if your ads are actually leading to phone calls, quote requests, or bookings. You are essentially flying blind. This makes it impossible to calculate your return on investment.

Set up conversion tracking in Google Ads from the beginning. Track every valuable action a user takes on your website.
