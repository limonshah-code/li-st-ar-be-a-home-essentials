## How To Reset Your KitchenAid Double Drawer Dishwasher

My KitchenAid double drawer dishwasher has always been a helpful kitchen appliance. But even the best machines sometimes need a quick fix. You might see strange error codes or unresponsive controls. A simple reset can often solve these common issues.

### Soft Resetting Your KitchenAid Double Drawer Dishwasher

A soft reset is a quick first step when your dishwasher acts up. It does not involve cutting power completely. This method is often enough for minor glitches. It clears temporary errors in the control panel.

To perform a soft reset, look for the "Cancel" or "Start/Reset" button. This button is usually on the control panel of each drawer. Press and hold this button for about three to five seconds. You should hear a beep or see the lights flash. This indicates the reset process has started.

After releasing the button, wait a moment. The dishwasher's control panel should clear. It might return to a default state. If the problem persists, you may need a hard reset. This soft reset is good for issues like a frozen display or a cycle that will not start. I always try this method first.

This type of reset is similar to restarting an application on your computer. It refreshes the system without fully shutting it down. It is a simple way to resolve small control problems. It helps when the double drawer dishwasher does not respond as it should.

Remember, each drawer has its own controls. You might need to perform the soft reset on the specific drawer that has issues. If both drawers are having problems, you can reset them one by one. This approach keeps things simple and direct.

### Performing a Hard Reset on Your KitchenAid Double Drawer Dishwasher

When a soft reset does not work, a hard reset is the next step. A hard reset completely removes power from the dishwasher. This forces all internal components to power down. It helps clear deeper electronic issues.

There are two main ways to perform a hard reset. First, you can unplug the dishwasher from its power outlet. Pull the unit out slightly to reach the cord. Wait for at least five minutes before plugging it back in. This wait time allows any residual charge to dissipate.

The second method involves using your home's circuit breaker. Find the circuit breaker panel in your house. Locate the breaker labeled for your kitchen or dishwasher. Flip it to the "off" position. Again, wait for at least five minutes. Then, flip the breaker back to the "on" position. This powers up the dishwasher.

I often find the circuit breaker method easier. It avoids moving the appliance. A hard reset is a powerful tool for fixing many issues. These include recurring error codes, total unresponsiveness, or cycles that stop midway. It completely restarts the internal computer system of your KitchenAid double drawer dishwasher.

This action ensures that all stored energy drains from the system. It helps to clear out any corrupted memory. When the dishwasher powers back on, it starts with a clean slate. This can solve many intermittent issues with your double drawer dishwasher.

### Troubleshooting After Resetting Your Dishwasher

After you reset your KitchenAid double drawer dishwasher, it should ideally work correctly. However, sometimes problems persist. This means the issue is more than a simple software glitch. Further troubleshooting is necessary.

First, observe the dishwasher's behavior closely. Does it display an error code immediately? Does it try to start a cycle but then stop? These observations provide important clues. Check your owner's manual for specific error code meanings. This book is a valuable resource for identifying problems.

Check the power supply again. Make sure the outlet works. Try plugging in another small appliance to test it. If the outlet has no power, check your circuit breaker. A tripped breaker will prevent the dishwasher from getting electricity.

Next, inspect the water supply. Ensure the water valve connected to the dishwasher is fully open. Low water pressure can cause problems. Also, check the inlet hose for kinks or blockages. These issues can prevent the dishwasher from filling with water.

Finally, look at the drainage system. Make sure the drain hose is not kinked. Check the air gap (if installed) for debris. A clogged drain or air gap will stop water from leaving the dishwasher. This can lead to standing water inside the tub. These steps help narrow down the cause of the problem after a reset. Maintaining your KitchenAid double drawer dishwasher can prevent many future problems. Regular care helps the appliance run smoothly. It reduces the need for frequent resets. I try to follow these steps to keep my unit in good shape.

Check the spray arms for blockages. Food particles can get stuck in the small holes. This stops water from reaching all dishes. Gently remove any blockages with a toothpick. Ensure the spray arms spin freely. This helps water reach all parts of the dishwasher.

Run a cleaning cycle monthly. Many dishwashers have a "clean cycle" option. If not, run an empty cycle with a dishwasher cleaner. You can also use a cup of white vinegar on the top rack. This helps remove mineral buildup and odors.

Finally, avoid overloading the drawers. Overloading prevents water from reaching all surfaces. It also strains the motor. Load dishes properly, allowing water and detergent to circulate. ## FAQ Section

### Q1: How long should I wait after a hard reset?
After performing a hard reset on your KitchenAid double drawer dishwasher, wait at least five minutes. This waiting period allows all residual electrical charge to dissipate completely. It ensures the internal computer system fully reboots from a fresh state when power returns. Plugging it back in too soon might not clear all stored errors.

### Q2: Can a reset fix all dishwasher problems?
No, a reset cannot fix all dishwasher problems. A reset is useful for clearing minor software glitches or temporary electronic malfunctions. It will not fix mechanical issues, broken parts, or persistent hardware failures. If issues continue after multiple resets, the dishwasher likely needs professional repair.

### Q3: Where is the reset button on a KitchenAid double drawer dishwasher?
KitchenAid double drawer dishwashers usually do not have a separate "reset button." Instead, you perform a soft reset by pressing and holding the "Cancel" or "Start/Reset" button on the control panel for about 3-5 seconds. A hard reset involves cutting power at the outlet or circuit breaker.

### Q4: Will resetting my dishwasher erase its settings?
A soft or hard reset on your KitchenAid double drawer dishwasher typically clears only temporary operational data. It usually does not erase permanent settings like language preferences or default wash cycles. These settings are stored in non-volatile memory. You may need to select your preferred cycle again after a reset.

### Q5: What if my dishwasher still displays an error code after a reset?
If your KitchenAid double drawer dishwasher still shows an error code after a reset, the issue is likely more serious. The error code points to a specific hardware failure or a persistent problem. Consult your owner's manual for the code's meaning. Then, contact KitchenAid customer support for professional diagnosis and repair.

### Q6: Is it safe to reset my dishwasher frequently?
Resetting your KitchenAid double drawer dishwasher occasionally is safe and will not harm the appliance. It is a standard troubleshooting step. However, if you find yourself needing to reset it very often, it suggests an underlying problem. Frequent resets indicate that a deeper issue needs investigation and repair.
