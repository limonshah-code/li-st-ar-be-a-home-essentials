## Replace Heating Element in KitchenAid Dishwasher

Imagine loading your KitchenAid dishwasher, running a cycle, and then opening it to find dishes still wet and sometimes not fully clean. This often points to a failing heating element. The heating element is a vital component. It heats the water during the wash cycle and dries the dishes after rinsing. When it stops working, your dishwasher cannot perform these crucial functions. I have fixed this issue myself. Replacing the heating element in your KitchenAid dishwasher is a common DIY repair. It can save you money on service calls. This guide provides step-by-step instructions. It will help you restore your dishwasher’s efficiency and ensure sparkling clean, dry dishes every time.

### Takeaway

Replacing a KitchenAid dishwasher heating element involves several key steps.
*   **Safety First:** Disconnect power before starting any work.
*   **Access the Element:** Remove the spray arm and lower rack.
*   **Locate Connections:** Find the heating element terminals under the dishwasher.
*   **Remove Old Part:** Disconnect wires, nuts, and old gaskets.
*   **Install New Element:** Attach the new element, secure connections, and apply new gaskets.
*   **Test Performance:** Run a short cycle to check for proper function and leaks.

To replace the heating element in a KitchenAid dishwasher, first disconnect power and water supply. Access the heating element from inside the tub by removing the lower spray arm and mounting nuts. Then, access the wiring connections from underneath the dishwasher. Detach the old element's wires and nuts. Install the new element with new gaskets, reconnect wiring, and reassemble the dishwasher for testing.

### Understanding Your KitchenAid Dishwasher's Heating Element

The heating element in your KitchenAid dishwasher plays two critical roles. First, it heats the water to the correct temperature during the wash and rinse cycles. This helps break down food particles and grease. Second, it generates heat during the drying cycle, evaporating water from your dishes. A working heating element ensures your dishes come out clean and dry. When it fails, you will notice problems.

Common signs of a malfunctioning heating element include dishes not drying, wash water not getting hot, or the element appearing visibly burned or corroded. Sometimes, error codes may display on your dishwasher. KitchenAid dishwashers typically have a visible heating element. It forms a large loop at the bottom of the tub. Identifying these symptoms early helps confirm that the heating element is the problem. Understanding its function prepares you for the replacement task.

The heating element is a resistance coil. It converts electrical energy into heat. This heat transfers to the water or the air inside the dishwasher tub. Without this heat, detergents do not work as effectively. Dishes also remain wet after the cycle ends. A visual inspection can often reveal issues. Look for discoloration or breaks in the element. My own experience with a failing element taught me to check these details first.

Recognizing the signs of a faulty heating element is your first step toward repair. Dishes come out cold or damp. Water spots may appear more frequently. These are all indicators that your heating element might be failing. Knowing what the element does helps you understand why these symptoms occur.

### Confirming the Faulty Heating Element

Before replacing the heating element, it is wise to confirm it is the actual cause of the problem. Sometimes other issues can mimic a faulty heating element. A simple diagnostic test can save you time and money. I always confirm the problem before ordering parts. This step is critical for effective troubleshooting.

The most reliable way to test a heating element is with a multimeter. A multimeter measures electrical properties like resistance or continuity. A working heating element should show continuity, meaning electricity can flow through it. If the multimeter shows no continuity, the element is open or broken. This confirms it needs replacement. You can find detailed instructions on this process in our article on [how to test KitchenAid dishwasher heating element](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-test-kitchenaid-dishwasher-heating-element).

*   **Locate the Heating Element Terminals:** The terminals are usually on the underside of the dishwasher. You need to pull the dishwasher out to access them.
*   **Disconnect Power:** Ensure the power is off at the breaker.
*   **Disconnect Wires:** Carefully disconnect the electrical wires from the heating element terminals.
*   **Set Multimeter:** Set your multimeter to the ohms (Ω) setting, or continuity test.
*   **Test for Continuity:** Touch one probe to each heating element terminal.
*   **Interpret Readings:**
    *   **Good Element:** The multimeter will display a low resistance reading (typically between 10-30 ohms) or beep for continuity.
    *   **Bad Element:** The multimeter will show "OL" (open loop) or "1" (infinite resistance), indicating a break in the circuit. No beep will sound on continuity mode.

Other factors can also cause drying issues. A malfunctioning thermostat or a control board problem might also prevent the element from heating. However, a continuity test directly checks the element's integrity. If the element passes the test, then the problem lies elsewhere. This diagnostic step helps you avoid unnecessary replacements. It ensures you fix the correct component.

### Accessing the Heating Element in Your KitchenAid Dishwasher

Accessing the heating element in a KitchenAid dishwasher requires a few preparatory steps. The element sits at the bottom of the tub. Its electrical connections are underneath the appliance. You need to gain access both inside the tub and under the unit. Patience and careful handling are key. I take my time during this stage to prevent damage.

*   **Drain the Dishwasher:** If there is any standing water in the bottom of the tub, remove it. Use a towel or a wet/dry vacuum. A dry tub makes the work much cleaner.
*   **Disconnect Power and Water:** Reconfirm that the power supply and water supply are both off. This is a critical safety step.
*   **Remove the Lower Dish Rack:** Pull out the lower dish rack from the dishwasher tub. This provides clear access to the bottom of the tub.
*   **Remove the Lower Spray Arm:** The lower spray arm often sits directly above the heating element. It usually twists off or unclips. Consult your KitchenAid dishwasher's manual if you are unsure about its removal method. Some models may have a retaining nut or clip.
*   **Identify the Heating Element Mounting Nuts:** Look for the small nuts that secure the heating element to the bottom of the tub. There are typically two to three of these nuts. They hold the heating element in place.
*   **Pull Out the Dishwasher:** Carefully pull the dishwasher out from its cabinet enclosure. You will need to access the underside of the unit. This often involves loosening mounting screws at the top or sides of the dishwasher. These screws secure it to the countertop or cabinets. Be gentle when pulling it out. Avoid kinking any water lines or electrical cords.

Once the dishwasher is pulled out, carefully tilt it onto its back or side. Place old towels or cardboard underneath to protect your floor. This position gives you a clear view of the heating element's terminals and wiring from the bottom. Take pictures of the wiring before disconnecting anything. This helps with reassembly.

This access stage sets the foundation for the actual replacement. Proper access prevents straining and makes subsequent steps easier. It also minimizes the risk of damaging other components.

### Removing the Old Heating Element Safely

With access gained, the next step is to carefully remove the old, faulty heating element. This involves disconnecting electrical wires and loosening the mounting hardware. Remember, patience is important here. Rushing can damage surrounding parts or cause frustration. I always work slowly and methodically during removal.

*   **Locate Electrical Connections:** From underneath the dishwasher, identify the two electrical terminals connected to the heating element. These terminals will have wires attached to them. They usually have small plastic or ceramic insulators.
*   **Disconnect Wiring:** Using pliers, carefully pull off the electrical connectors from the heating element terminals. Note which wire connects to which terminal. Taking a photo can be a helpful reminder. These connectors can be tight. Gentle wiggling often helps them slide off.
*   **Loosen Mounting Nuts (Underneath):** On the underside of the dishwasher, you will find the nuts that secure the heating element. Use your nut driver or socket wrench to loosen and remove these nuts. Keep track of the nuts and any washers.
*   **Remove Internal Mounting Nuts (Inside Tub):** Now, return to the inside of the dishwasher tub. The heating element passes through the bottom of the tub. There are usually retaining nuts or washers that secure the element from the inside. Remove these using pliers or a nut driver. These inner nuts often have rubber gaskets that create a watertight seal.
*   **Extract the Element:** Once all nuts and washers are removed, gently lift the old heating element out of the dishwasher tub. It might be slightly stuck due to mineral buildup or the old gaskets. Be careful not to scratch the tub's finish. Ensure all parts of the old element are out. Sometimes, parts of the rubber gaskets remain.

Inspect the old gaskets. They often become brittle or degrade over time. You will replace them with new ones. This ensures a watertight seal for the new heating element. Discard the old element and gaskets responsibly. This meticulous removal process prepares the way for a smooth installation of the new element. You are halfway to getting your KitchenAid dishwasher working like new again.

### Installing the New KitchenAid Heating Element

Installing the new heating element is essentially the reverse of the removal process. Accuracy and proper sealing are crucial. A correctly installed element prevents leaks and functions efficiently. I pay close attention to sealing during installation. Remember, our guide on [how to install dishwasher heating element](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-install-dishwasher-heating-element) provides additional tips.

*   **Prepare the New Element:** Ensure your new heating element comes with new rubber gaskets. These gaskets are vital for preventing leaks. If not included, purchase them separately.
*   **Insert New Gaskets:** Place the new rubber gaskets onto the ends of the heating element terminals. These gaskets will sit flush against the tub wall.
*   **Position the New Element:** From inside the dishwasher tub, carefully lower the new heating element into place. Align the terminal ends with the holes in the bottom of the tub. Ensure the element sits correctly in its designated grooves or clips.
*   **Secure Inside Tub:** From inside the tub, install the retaining nuts or washers onto the heating element terminals. Tighten them by hand first. This holds the element in place while you work underneath.
*   **Secure Underneath Dishwasher:** From underneath the dishwasher, push the terminal ends through the holes. Place the washers and nuts onto the threaded terminals. Use your nut driver or socket wrench to tighten these nuts. Do not overtighten, as this can damage the gaskets or the tub. Tighten them firmly until snug. The goal is a watertight seal.
*   **Reconnect Wiring:** Reattach the electrical wires to the new heating element terminals. Ensure they are firmly pushed onto the terminals. Refer to your photo if you took one to ensure correct wire placement. Double-check that all connections are secure. Loose connections can cause electrical issues or heating problems.

Once the element is physically secured and wired, double-check all connections. A quick visual inspection can catch any missed steps. The new heating element should be firmly in place and ready for testing. This careful installation ensures your KitchenAid dishwasher performs as expected.

### Reassembly, Testing, and Final Checks

After installing the new heating element, it is time for reassembly and crucial testing. This stage ensures everything is put back together correctly and the new element functions as intended. It also allows you to check for any leaks. I always run a test cycle. This gives me peace of mind.

*   **Reinstall Dishwasher:** Carefully slide the dishwasher back into its cabinet enclosure. Take care not to pinch any wires or hoses. Reattach the mounting screws that secure the dishwasher to the countertop or cabinets.
*   **Reattach Lower Spray Arm and Rack:** Inside the tub, replace the lower spray arm. Ensure it rotates freely. Then, slide the lower dish rack back into its tracks.
*   **Reconnect Water Supply:** Turn on the water shut-off valve under the sink. Check for any immediate leaks around the water inlet connection on the dishwasher.
*   **Reconnect Power:** Go to your home's circuit breaker panel. Turn the power breaker back on for the dishwasher.
*   **Run a Test Cycle:** Start a short wash cycle, ideally one with a heated dry option. During the cycle, listen for any unusual noises. Observe the dishwasher's performance.
    *   **Check for Leaks:** During the wash cycle, carefully check underneath the dishwasher for any water leaks around the heating element terminals. A flashlight is useful here. Even small drips need immediate attention. If you find a leak, turn off the power and water. Then, re-tighten the nuts securing the heating element.
    *   **Check for Heat:** Open the dishwasher door near the end of the wash cycle (carefully, as steam will escape). Feel the water or the interior. It should be noticeably hot.
    *   **Check Drying:** After the cycle completes, open the door. Your dishes should be hot and dry, or at least significantly drier than before.

If the test cycle runs smoothly and your dishes are clean and dry, congratulations! You have successfully replaced the heating element in your KitchenAid dishwasher. If issues persist, review your steps or consult a professional. Sometimes, a component like the thermostat or control board might also be at fault. ### Maintaining Your Dishwasher's Heating Element

Even after a successful replacement, proper maintenance helps extend the life of your new heating element. Dishwashers work hard, and regular care keeps them performing optimally. A well-maintained heating element works more efficiently and lasts longer. I learned the importance of maintenance the hard way. Neglecting it leads to repeat repairs.

*   **Use Proper Detergent:** Always use detergent specifically designed for dishwashers. Using too much or the wrong type of detergent can cause excessive suds. This can interfere with the washing process and leave residue. Residue can build up on the heating element.
*   **Scrape Dishes:** Scrape large food particles off dishes before loading them into the dishwasher. This prevents food from circulating and potentially getting baked onto the heating element. While the heating element is designed to handle some debris, excessive buildup reduces its efficiency.
*   **Regular Cleaning:** Periodically clean the dishwasher interior. Food debris, mineral deposits, and soap scum can accumulate. These can affect the heating element's performance. Our guide on [how to clean dishwasher heating element](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-clean-dishwasher-heating-element) offers detailed steps.
    *   **Vinegar Rinse:** Run an empty cycle with a cup of white vinegar placed in the bottom rack. Vinegar helps dissolve mineral deposits and hard water stains.
    *   **Baking Soda Scrub:** For tough stains, sprinkle baking soda on the bottom of the tub and run a short hot cycle.
*   **Check Water Hardness:** Hard water contains minerals that can build up on the heating element. If you have hard water, consider using a rinse aid or a dishwasher cleaner designed to combat mineral deposits. A water softener for your home can also be a long-term solution.
*   **Avoid Overloading:** Overloading your dishwasher can prevent water and detergent from circulating properly. This can lead to inefficient washing and drying, placing more strain on the heating element.

By following these simple maintenance tips, you can ensure your KitchenAid dishwasher’s new heating element works effectively for years to come. Regular care also helps you spot potential issues early. This saves you from future expensive repairs. Keeping the element clear of debris allows it to heat efficiently.

### Troubleshooting Common Issues After Replacement

Even after careful replacement, sometimes new issues arise or old ones persist. It is frustrating when your dishwasher does not work perfectly right away. Most post-replacement problems are minor and easy to fix. I have encountered several unexpected issues myself. Knowing how to troubleshoot helps a lot.

*   **Dishes Still Not Drying:**
    *   **Check for Proper Heating:** Feel inside the tub during the dry cycle. Is it warm? If not, recheck the electrical connections to the heating element. Ensure they are secure.
    *   **Heating Element Compatibility:** Double-check that the new heating element is the correct part number for your specific KitchenAid model. An incompatible part might not draw enough power.
    *   **Thermostat or Control Board:** If the element is receiving power and testing good, the issue might be with the thermostat or the main control board. These components control when the element turns on. You may need to test these parts or consult a service manual.
    *   **Rinse Aid:** Ensure you are using rinse aid. It helps water sheet off dishes, which improves drying even with a working heating element.
*   **Water Leaking from Underneath:**
    *   **Gasket Seal:** This is the most common cause of leaks after replacement. The heating element gaskets might not be seated correctly, or the nuts might not be tightened enough. Turn off power and water. Re-tighten the nuts, ensuring a firm but not overtightened seal. If the gaskets are old or damaged, replace them.
    *   **Hose Connections:** Check other hose connections that might have been disturbed when pulling out the dishwasher. Ensure the water inlet and drain hoses are secure.
*   **Dishwasher Not Starting or Showing Error Code:**
    *   **Power Connection:** Ensure the dishwasher is fully plugged in and the circuit breaker is on.
    *   **Door Latch:** The door must be securely latched for the dishwasher to start. Check if the latch mechanism is working correctly.
    *   **Control Panel Reset:** Sometimes, a simple reset can clear error codes. Disconnect power for a few minutes, then restore it.
    *   **Wiring Errors:** Double-check all wiring connections, especially to the heating element. Any loose or incorrect connection can prevent the dishwasher from operating.

If you have tried these troubleshooting steps and the problem continues, consider calling a qualified appliance technician. Some issues, especially those involving the control board, can be more complex. They might require specialized diagnostic tools.

### FAQ Section

#### How do I know if my KitchenAid dishwasher heating element is bad?
You know your KitchenAid dishwasher heating element is bad if dishes come out wet after a dry cycle. The wash water may also not feel hot to the touch. Another sign is visible corrosion or a break on the heating element itself. Using a multimeter to test for continuity is the most accurate way to confirm. A bad element will show no continuity.

#### Can I replace a dishwasher heating element myself?
Yes, you can replace a dishwasher heating element yourself. It is a common DIY repair. The process involves disconnecting power, accessing the element from inside the tub and underneath the dishwasher, disconnecting wires, and unbolting the old element. Then, you install the new one and reassemble. Always prioritize safety.

#### How long does a KitchenAid dishwasher heating element last?
A KitchenAid dishwasher heating element typically lasts between 8 to 12 years. Its lifespan depends on usage, water hardness, and maintenance. Hard water and frequent use can shorten its life. Regular cleaning and proper detergent use can help extend its function.

#### Is a heating element covered under KitchenAid warranty?
A heating element might be covered under your KitchenAid dishwasher's manufacturer warranty, especially if the appliance is new. Most standard warranties cover parts and labor for a period, usually one year. Check your specific warranty documentation. Damage from misuse or improper installation is usually not covered.

#### What happens if you run a dishwasher without a heating element?
If you run a dishwasher without a working heating element, your dishes will likely not get completely clean. The water will not reach the optimal temperature for detergent activation. Most importantly, your dishes will not dry properly and will come out wet. This often leads to water spots and less sanitary cleaning.

#### How much does it cost to replace a KitchenAid dishwasher heating element?
The cost to replace a KitchenAid dishwasher heating element varies. The part itself typically costs between $40 to $100. If you do it yourself, that is your main expense. Hiring a professional can add $100 to $300 or more in labor costs. DIY replacement offers significant savings.
