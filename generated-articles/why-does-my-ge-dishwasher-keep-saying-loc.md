## GE Dishwasher Showing "LOC"? Here's the Fix!

I know how frustrating it feels when your trusty GE dishwasher stops working as expected. You load the dishes, press the start button, and instead of hearing the gentle hum of cleaning, you see a cryptic "LOC" error message on the display. This "LOC" symbol effectively locks your dishwasher, preventing any cycles from starting. It feels like your appliance is holding your dishes hostage.

Don't worry, this is a common issue with GE dishwashers. The "LOC" error usually indicates that the control lock feature, also known as the child lock, is active. This safety feature prevents accidental button presses or children from tampering with the settings. While helpful, it can be a headache if you activate it by mistake. I am here to walk you through the simple steps to understand and resolve this "LOC" issue, getting your GE dishwasher back to cleaning your dishes efficiently. We will cover quick fixes, troubleshooting tips, and when to consider professional help.

### Takeaway: Unlock Your GE Dishwasher

*   **Understand "LOC":** It means "Control Lock" or "Child Lock" is active.
*   **Locate the Lock Button:** Find the "Lock" or "Control Lock" button on your GE dishwasher's panel.
*   **Press and Hold:** Press and hold this button for 3 seconds to deactivate the lock.
*   **Power Cycle if Needed:** If the button does not work, turn off power at the circuit breaker for 5 minutes.

"LOC" on your GE dishwasher means the Control Lock feature is engaged. This safety function prevents accidental use or changes to settings. You can usually disengage it by pressing and holding the "Lock" or "Control Lock" button for three seconds until the "LOC" indicator disappears.

### Understanding the GE Dishwasher "LOC" Error

The "LOC" message on your GE dishwasher's display is not a sign of a major breakdown. It simply tells you that the "Control Lock" feature is currently active. Many people, including myself, have encountered this without realizing what it meant. This feature is a safety measure designed to prevent unintended operation of the dishwasher.

Think of it as a digital padlock on your dishwasher's control panel. Its primary purpose is to keep children from starting cycles or changing settings. It also prevents accidental button presses if you lean against the counter. When "LOC" appears, the dishwasher will not respond to any commands from the control panel. This includes starting a cycle, changing wash settings, or even turning the appliance off. You need to unlock the control panel first. This feature is common across many modern appliances, not just dishwashers. For example, your GE oven might also show "LOC" for similar reasons. The "LOC" indicator then illuminates, confirming the lock is engaged. Sometimes, this activation happens accidentally. ### The Easiest Fix: Disabling the Control Lock

The most common and simplest solution for a GE dishwasher displaying "LOC" is to deactivate the control lock. This process is usually straightforward. Most GE dishwashers have a dedicated button or a combination of buttons for this function. I have found that this step resolves the issue quickly for most users.

Start by looking at your dishwasher's control panel. You will need to identify the button labeled "Lock," "Control Lock," or sometimes a small padlock icon. On some models, this function is combined with another button, such as "Delay Start," "Start/Reset," or "Heated Dry." If it is a combined button, there will often be "Control Lock" written nearby, or a padlock symbol next to it. Once you find it, simply press and hold this button for approximately three seconds. You should hear a beep, and the "LOC" indicator on the display should disappear. This signals that the control panel is now unlocked, and you can operate your dishwasher normally. ### Power Cycling Your GE Dishwasher to Clear "LOC"

Sometimes, a simple button press might not be enough to clear the "LOC" error. This can happen if the control board has a minor glitch or is temporarily stuck. In such cases, a power cycle, also known as a hard reset, often resolves the issue. It is similar to restarting your computer when it misbehaves. This process clears any temporary errors from the dishwasher's internal memory.

To power cycle your GE dishwasher, you need to completely cut off its power supply. The safest way to do this is to locate your home's main electrical panel, also known as the breaker box. Find the circuit breaker that controls your kitchen appliances or specifically your dishwasher. Flip this breaker to the "OFF" position. I always recommend waiting for at least five minutes after cutting the power. This waiting period allows any residual electricity to dissipate from the dishwasher's components, ensuring a full reset of the control board. After the waiting period, flip the circuit breaker back to the "ON" position.

Once power is restored, check your dishwasher's display. The "LOC" error should now be gone. You should be able to operate your dishwasher as usual. If your GE dishwasher previously had no power at all, and you resolved that, a power cycle is often a logical next step to clear any resulting error codes. For troubleshooting power issues, you might find useful information at [why does my GE dishwasher not have power](https://homeessentialsguide.mobi/why-does-my-ge-dishwasher-not-have-power). This reset can be particularly effective if the error was caused by a power fluctuation or a temporary software bug. It is a quick and effective troubleshooting step that you can easily perform yourself.

### Examining the Control Panel for Issues

If power cycling and attempting to deactivate the control lock directly do not resolve the "LOC" error, the problem might stem from the control panel itself. Sometimes, physical issues with the buttons or the panel's internal components can cause persistent "LOC" displays. This requires a closer look at the panel's condition. I have seen cases where external factors impacted the display.

Carefully inspect your GE dishwasher's control panel. Look for any visible signs of damage, such as cracks, dents, or wear and tear around the buttons. Pay special attention to the "Lock" or "Control Lock" button. Check if it appears stuck in the pressed position. Sometimes, spilled liquids, food debris, or even just dirt can get into the small gaps around the buttons, causing them to stick. A stuck button will continuously signal to the dishwasher's control board that the lock feature should remain active, thus keeping "LOC" on the display. If you suspect a sticky button, you can try gently cleaning around it with a soft cloth and a small amount of mild cleaner. Make sure to avoid excessive moisture.

A malfunctioning control panel can send incorrect signals to the dishwasher's main control board. If the internal circuitry of the panel is faulty, it might mistakenly register a continuous "lock" command. In some rare instances, moisture ingress or even insect activity inside the panel could cause shorts that lead to erratic behavior, including a persistent "LOC" error. While it might seem like a simple display issue, a faulty control panel needs attention. If your GE dishwasher also experiences issues like suddenly shutting off, it could be related to broader control system problems. You can explore potential causes of such power interruptions at [why does my GE dishwasher keep shutting off](https://homeessentialsguide.com/why-does-my-ge-dishwasher-keep-shutting-off). This examination is crucial before considering more complex solutions.

### Dealing with a Malfunctioning Main Control Board

If all the simpler troubleshooting steps fail, the persistent "LOC" error might point to a problem with your GE dishwasher's main control board. This board acts as the brain of your appliance. It receives commands from the control panel and sends instructions to all other components. If this critical component malfunctions, it can cause various errors, including a stubborn "LOC" display that you cannot clear. A failing control board might also be the reason why your GE dishwasher has power but will not start a cycle. You can read more on such situations at [why does my GE dishwasher have power but will not start](https://homeessentialsguide.com/why-does-my-ge-dishwasher-have-power-but-will-not-start).

Signs of a failing main control board include erratic behavior beyond just the "LOC" error. You might notice inconsistent cycle times, strange noises, or other error codes appearing intermittently. The board might have experienced a power surge, a manufacturing defect, or simply worn out over time. If the "LOC" error remains after trying the button reset and power cycling, and you have visually inspected the control panel for issues, the main control board becomes a strong suspect. It is important to note that diagnosing and replacing a main control board is a more complex repair. It often involves accessing the internal components of the dishwasher, which requires a certain level of technical skill and safety precautions.

I usually advise caution when dealing with internal electrical components. Attempting to replace the control board yourself without proper knowledge can be risky. You could damage other parts or even injure yourself. In many cases, replacing the main control board might require a specific part number that matches your dishwasher model precisely. For these reasons, if you suspect the main control board is the culprit, it is often best to consult a qualified appliance technician. They have the tools and expertise to accurately diagnose the problem. They can replace the board safely and effectively, ensuring your GE dishwasher works correctly again.

### FAQ Section

**Q1: What does "LOC" mean on a GE dishwasher?**
"LOC" on a GE dishwasher display signifies that the "Control Lock" or "Child Lock" feature is active. This safety function prevents the dishwasher from operating or having its settings changed accidentally. It is a common feature on many modern appliances.

**Q2: How do I unlock my GE dishwasher that says "LOC"?**
To unlock your GE dishwasher, locate the "Lock" or "Control Lock" button on the control panel. Press and hold this button for about three seconds. You should hear a beep, and the "LOC" indicator will disappear, allowing you to use your dishwasher normally.

**Q3: Can a power surge cause "LOC" on my GE dishwasher?**
Yes, a power surge or a brief power interruption can sometimes cause the control board to glitch and engage the "LOC" feature unexpectedly. A power cycle (turning off the breaker for 5 minutes) often resolves this issue by resetting the board.

**Q4: Is "LOC" a serious error on GE dishwashers?**
No, the "LOC" error is typically not a serious issue indicating a major fault. It is a safety feature that has been activated. Most of the time, it can be resolved with a simple button press or a quick power reset, making it a minor inconvenience.

**Q5: Why does my GE dishwasher randomly show "LOC"?**
Your GE dishwasher might randomly show "LOC" due to accidental activation of the control lock button. This can happen if you lean against the panel, or if children press buttons. Less commonly, a temporary control board glitch or a faulty button could be the cause.

**Q6: Do all GE dishwashers have a child lock feature?**
Most modern GE dishwasher models include a child lock or control lock feature. This is a standard safety feature for many household appliances. However, older or more basic models might not have this specific function. Always check your model's user manual to confirm.
