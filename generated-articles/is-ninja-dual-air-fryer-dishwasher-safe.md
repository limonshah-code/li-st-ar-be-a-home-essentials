## Parts That Are NOT Dishwasher Safe and Why

While some parts of your Ninja Dual Air Fryer offer cleaning convenience, many crucial components are strictly off-limits for the dishwasher. The most important rule to remember is this: never put the main unit of your air fryer in the dishwasher or submerge it in water. This unit houses all the electrical wiring, heating elements, and fans. Water damage to these parts can cause an electrical hazard. It can also permanently damage your appliance.

Beyond the main unit, be careful with any delicate accessories that came with your air fryer. Some models might include racks, skewers, or other inserts that are not meant for machine washing. Dishwashers use high heat and strong water jets. These can warp plastic, scratch coatings, or cause metal parts to rust. This is especially true for items not designed for such harsh conditions.

Putting non-dishwasher safe parts into the machine carries significant risks. You could face electrical shock if the main unit is damaged. The non-stick coating on baskets and plates, even if rated dishwasher safe, can degrade faster over time. This leads to food sticking and harder cleaning. It is always safer to hand wash parts not explicitly labeled as dishwasher safe. This protects your investment and ensures safe operation.

## Best Practices for Hand Washing Your Ninja Dual Air Fryer

Even if some parts are dishwasher safe, hand washing is often the best approach. It helps prolong the life of your air fryer's components. It is also the only way to clean the main unit and other non-dishwasher safe parts. Let me guide you through the process.

First, always ensure the air fryer is unplugged and completely cool. Safety comes first. For the cooking baskets and crisper plates, a simple soak can work wonders. Fill the basket with warm, soapy water and let it sit for about 10-15 minutes. This helps loosen baked-on food and grease. Then, use a soft sponge or cloth to wipe away the residue. Avoid abrasive scrubbers, steel wool, or harsh chemicals. These can scratch or damage the non-stick coating. Rinse thoroughly with clean water and dry completely before storing. My experience is that a gentle touch keeps the coating intact much longer.

For the main unit, use a damp cloth to wipe down the exterior. You can use a mild dish soap solution for stubborn spots. Never spray water directly onto the unit. For the interior cavity and heating element, a damp cloth is usually enough. Make sure the cloth is only damp, not dripping wet. Wipe gently around the heating coil to remove any food splatters. Always allow the interior to air dry completely before plugging in the appliance again. These steps protect the electrical components and prevent rust. ## FAQ Section

### Q1: Can I put the main unit of my Ninja Dual Air Fryer in the dishwasher?

No, you must never put the main unit of your Ninja Dual Air Fryer in the dishwasher. This part contains all the electrical components, including the heating element and fan. Submerging it in water or exposing it to dishwasher cycles can cause severe damage. It also poses a significant electrical safety risk. Always wipe the exterior with a damp cloth and never immerse it.

### Q2: How do I clean stubborn grease from the air fryer basket?

For stubborn grease, first, let the basket cool completely. Then, fill the basket with warm water and a few drops of dish soap. Allow it to soak for 15-30 minutes. This helps loosen the residue. Afterward, use a non-abrasive sponge or a soft-bristled brush to gently scrub the grease away. Rinse thoroughly and dry.

### Q3: Is it better to hand wash air fryer parts even if they are marked dishwasher safe?

Yes, in most cases, hand washing your Ninja Dual Air Fryer parts is generally better. Even if parts are labeled "dishwasher safe," the harsh detergents and high heat of a dishwasher can cause the non-stick coating to degrade more quickly over time. Hand washing with warm, soapy water and a soft sponge helps preserve the coating and extends the lifespan of your appliance.

### Q4: What kind of soap should I use for cleaning my Ninja Dual Air Fryer?

You should use a mild dish soap for cleaning your Ninja Dual Air Fryer. Look for soaps that are gentle and do not contain harsh chemicals or abrasive particles. Avoid using scouring powders, steel wool, or abrasive pads. These can scratch and damage the non-stick surfaces. A simple liquid dish soap is usually sufficient and safe.

### Q5: How often should I clean my Ninja Dual Air Fryer?

You should clean your Ninja Dual Air Fryer after every use. This prevents food residue and grease from building up. A quick wipe down of the baskets and crisper plates after each cooking session makes future cleaning much easier. For the main unit and interior cavity, a wipe-down every few uses or when you notice splatter is usually sufficient.
