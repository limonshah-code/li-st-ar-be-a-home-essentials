### Understanding Whirlpool's Date Code

Whirlpool's serial numbers often follow a YYWW (Year-Week) format. The first character is usually a letter representing the manufacturing year. The next two characters are often numbers indicating the week of that year. For example, a serial number starting with "C32" means the appliance was made in the 32nd week of year "C".

Whirlpool cycles through letters for years. "A" might represent a certain year, "B" the next, and so on. The company often skips letters like "I" or "O" to avoid confusion with numbers. There are charts available online that map these letters to specific years. These charts are crucial for accurate decoding.

For instance, if 'A' was 2010, 'B' would be 2011, 'C' 2012, and so on. This pattern makes it straightforward once you have the correct reference chart. ## Tips for Extending Your Whirlpool Dishwasher's Life

Knowing your Whirlpool dishwasher's age is only one part of the equation. Taking steps to extend its life is equally important. Proper care and proactive maintenance can significantly prolong its service. I always recommend these simple tips to keep your appliance running strong.

These tips focus on practical actions you can take. They cover everything from daily use to periodic deep cleaning. Implement these habits to maximize your Whirlpool dishwasher's operational life.

### Essential Cleaning Tips

Regular cleaning is fundamental to a long-lasting dishwasher. Start by scraping large food particles from dishes before loading. This prevents clogs in the filter and spray arms. Run a hot water cycle with an empty dishwasher and a cleaning agent every month.

Pay attention to the filter. Most modern Whirlpool dishwashers have a removable filter. Take it out and rinse it under running water to remove food debris. This simple task improves cleaning performance dramatically. If you need assistance, there are guides on [how to put the Whirlpool dishwasher filter back in](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-put-whirlpool-dishwasher-filter-back-in) correctly.

Also, clean the spray arms periodically. Small holes on the spray arms can get blocked by food particles or mineral deposits. Use a toothpick or small brush to clear them. This ensures proper water distribution during cycles.

### Proper Loading Techniques

How you load your dishwasher impacts its cleaning effectiveness and lifespan. Avoid overcrowding the racks. Overloading prevents water and detergent from reaching all surfaces. This leads to dirty dishes and can strain the motor.

Place dishes according to their size and shape. Face the dirtiest parts of dishes towards the spray arms. Ensure nothing blocks the spray arms from rotating freely. Taller items should go on the sides or back to avoid blocking the detergent dispenser.

Use the correct amount and type of detergent. Too much can cause excessive suds, leading to residue. Too little might result in poorly cleaned dishes. Following these simple loading guidelines helps your dishwasher work efficiently.
