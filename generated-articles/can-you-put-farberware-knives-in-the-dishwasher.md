### Can You Put Farberware Knives In The Dishwasher? A Direct Answer

No, you generally should not put Farberware knives in the dishwasher. Farberware, like most quality kitchen knives, can suffer damage from the high heat, harsh detergents, and jostling inside a dishwasher. Hand washing protects the blade's sharpness, prevents rust, and preserves the handle's integrity.

### Why Dishwashers and Knives Often Don't Mix

Dishwashers offer convenience, but they are not always ideal for knives. Many people believe all kitchen items are dishwasher safe. This is often untrue for sharp tools like Farberware knives. Several factors contribute to why dishwashers are bad for knives. These factors damage the blade, the handle, and overall performance.

First, dishwashers use very hot water and powerful detergents. The hot water softens food particles. It also helps to sanitize dishes. However, this high heat is detrimental to knife blades. It can cause micro-damage to the delicate edge. This makes the knife dull faster. Detergents contain abrasive chemicals. These chemicals are designed to cut through grease and grime. They can corrode the blade's surface. This leads to discoloration and rust spots. Even stainless steel knives can rust. This is because dishwashers often leave them sitting in a damp, high-mineral environment for too long. This physical impact can chip or bend the blade. It also damages the sharp edge. The tip of a knife is especially vulnerable to breaking. Moreover, the spray from the water jets can also contribute to this movement. This constant jostling dulls the blade significantly. You might think your knives are safe in a utensil basket. Finally, dishwasher use can also harm knife handles. Many knives have handles made from wood, plastic, or composite materials. Plastic handles might lose their grip or become brittle. This damage not only affects the knife's appearance but also its safety. A loose handle can be dangerous to use. Overall, the dishwasher creates an environment that actively works against knife longevity and performance. This is why experts recommend hand washing. ### The Best Way to Clean Farberware Knives: Hand Washing

Hand washing is the gold standard for cleaning Farberware knives. This method protects the blade's edge and preserves the handle's integrity. It is also the safest way to clean sharp utensils. By following a few simple steps, you can ensure your knives last for years. This care routine helps maintain their sharpness and appearance.

First, wash your knives immediately after use. Do not let food residue dry on the blade. Dried food can be harder to remove. This might lead to more aggressive scrubbing. Rinse the knife under warm water to remove loose particles. Use a mild dish soap and a soft sponge or cloth. Apply the soap to the sponge, not directly to the blade. Gently wipe the blade from the spine (the non-sharp edge) down to the cutting edge. Always keep the blade facing away from your body. Wash both sides of the blade and the handle thoroughly. Avoid abrasive scrubbers. These can scratch the finish on the blade and handle. Take your time to clean all parts of the knife. This ensures no food particles remain.

Second, rinse the knife completely under warm, running water. Make sure all soap residue is gone. Soap left on the blade can cause streaks or dull the finish. After rinsing, dry the knife immediately. Use a clean, soft towel. Drying is a crucial step. Air drying knives can lead to water spots and rust, even on stainless steel. Moisture is the enemy of any metal. Pay close attention to the area where the blade meets the handle. Water can collect here. Completely dry this joint to prevent corrosion. Store the knife properly once it is dry. This prevents damage and keeps it safe.

Proper hand washing offers several benefits. It prevents the blade from dulling. It also stops rust from forming. The handle remains in good condition. You avoid exposing your knives to harsh chemicals and high temperatures. This method extends the life of your Farberware knives. It keeps them performing at their best. Remember, a well-maintained knife is a joy to use. Taking these extra moments to hand wash your Farberware knives will pay off.
