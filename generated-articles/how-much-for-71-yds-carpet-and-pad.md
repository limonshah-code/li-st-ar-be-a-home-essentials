### Carpet Material Types and Their Price Points

The type of carpet fiber you choose significantly impacts the cost for your 71 yards carpet and pad project. Each material offers different benefits regarding durability, stain resistance, and softness. Your lifestyle and budget guide this decision. It is helpful to understand these differences before you buy.

Nylon is a popular choice known for its strength and resilience. It resists wear and tear well, making it ideal for high-traffic areas. Nylon carpet generally costs between $3 to $8 per square foot. It holds its shape and color for a long time.

Polyester (PET) carpet offers a soft feel and excellent stain resistance, especially against water-soluble spills. It is also more affordable, typically costing $2 to $5 per square foot. Polyester is a good option for bedrooms or areas with lighter traffic. However, it may flatten more over time in heavily used spaces.

Olefin (Polypropylene) is a budget-friendly option, often found in loop pile carpets like Berber. It resists moisture, mildew, and fading. Olefin carpet usually costs $1.50 to $3 per square foot. It is a good choice for basements or outdoor areas but can be less resilient than nylon.

Wool carpet is a natural, luxurious choice. It is soft, durable, and naturally fire-resistant. Wool also improves indoor air quality by absorbing common pollutants. However, it is the most expensive option, ranging from $5 to $12 per square foot. It is a premium choice for those seeking natural beauty and comfort.

Triexta, often sold under brand names like SmartStrand, combines durability and stain resistance. It is made partially from renewable resources. Triexta is soft and easy to clean, making it a strong contender for homes with kids or pets. Its price point typically falls between $3 to $7 per square foot.

### The Importance and Cost of Carpet Padding

Carpet padding is not just an extra layer; it is crucial for your carpet's performance and lifespan. A good pad enhances comfort, provides insulation, and protects the carpet backing from wear. When considering how much for 71 yds carpet and pad, the padding cost is a vital part of the total. Choosing the right pad saves you money in the long run.

Padding types vary in material and density. Rebond pad is common and made from recycled foam scraps. It offers good support and comes in different densities. Felt pads are dense and firm, good for Berber carpets. Rubber pads provide excellent cushioning and sound absorption.

The density of the pad is important. It is measured in pounds per cubic foot. A higher density means a firmer, more durable pad. For most residential carpets, a 6 to 8-pound density pad is recommended. Too soft a pad can cause the carpet to wear out faster. Understanding [what weight does carpet pad come in](https://homeessentialsguide.com/what-weight-does-carpet-pad-come-in) helps you make an informed choice for your specific carpet and usage.

Carpet padding costs typically range from $0.50 to $1.50 per square foot. Higher density or specialized pads, like moisture-barrier pads, may cost more. For your 71 yards (639 square feet) of carpet, this means the pad alone could add $319 to $958 to your total. Do not skip or skimp on the pad; it protects your investment.

A quality pad makes your carpet feel softer underfoot. It also helps insulate your home, reducing energy costs. This extra layer absorbs impact, which prevents the carpet fibers from crushing too quickly. Proper padding also helps with sound absorption, making your rooms quieter.

### Tips for Budgeting and Saving on Your Carpet Project

Budgeting for your 71 yards carpet and pad project requires careful planning. You can save money without sacrificing quality. Smart choices from the start help you stay within your financial limits. These tips guide you through the process.

**Get Multiple Quotes:** Always contact at least three different carpet retailers or installers. Get detailed, written quotes from each. Compare not just the total price but also what is included. **Shop During Sales:** Carpet retailers often have sales events, especially around holidays or seasonal changes. Timing your purchase can lead to significant savings on both materials and installation. Sign up for email lists from local stores to stay informed about upcoming promotions.

**Choose the Right Carpet Type:** As discussed earlier, carpet materials vary greatly in price. **Consider Pad Density Wisely:** While a good pad is crucial, you do not always need the most expensive option. For standard residential use, a good quality 6-pound or 8-pound rebond pad is often sufficient. Going higher might be overkill for certain areas, unnecessarily increasing your cost.

**DIY Old Carpet Removal and Furniture Moving:** To save on labor costs, consider removing your old carpet yourself. You can also move your furniture to an empty room or storage area before the installers arrive. These simple tasks can shave hundreds off your total bill. Be prepared for some heavy lifting.

**Ask About Remnants or Leftovers:** Sometimes, stores have remnant pieces from larger rolls. While 71 yards is a big area, smaller rooms or closets might be able to use remnants if you are doing multiple areas. This might not apply to your main 71-yard purchase but can save money on smaller, auxiliary projects.

**Understand Warranty Terms:** A good warranty protects your investment. Understand what the carpet and pad warranties cover. This includes stain resistance, wear, and installation. A strong warranty gives you peace of mind and can save you money on future repairs or replacements if issues arise.

### FAQ Section

**How is 71 yards of carpet converted to square feet?**
One square yard equals nine square feet. To convert 71 yards, you multiply 71 by 9. This gives you 639 square feet. Most carpet pricing and installation bids are based on square feet, so this conversion is essential for accurate cost estimation and understanding the scale of your project.

**What is the average cost per square foot for carpet and pad?**
The average cost for carpet material ranges from $2 to $8 per square foot. Carpet padding typically adds $0.50 to $1.50 per square foot. This means the combined material cost usually falls between $2.50 and $9.50 per square foot before installation.

**Does carpet pad quality really matter for my 71 yards?**
Yes, carpet pad quality matters greatly. A good quality pad extends the life of your carpet by absorbing impact and preventing the carpet fibers from crushing. It also enhances comfort and provides insulation. Investing in a decent pad protects your larger carpet investment.

**Are there hidden fees when buying carpet and pad?**
Hidden fees can include charges for old carpet removal, furniture moving, or subfloor preparation. Installation on stairs or in complex rooms can also incur extra costs. Always ask for a detailed, itemized quote to understand all potential expenses before committing to a purchase.

**How long does it take to install 71 yards of carpet?**
Installing 71 yards (639 square feet) of carpet typically takes one full day for experienced professionals. The exact time depends on the room layout, number of seams, and any unexpected subfloor issues. Larger or more complex installations might extend to a second day.

**Can I install carpet myself to save money?**
You can install carpet yourself, but it is a challenging job that requires special tools and skills. Incorrect installation can lead to ripples, premature wear, and voided warranties. For a 71-yard project, professional installation is highly recommended to ensure a long-lasting and aesthetically pleasing result.
