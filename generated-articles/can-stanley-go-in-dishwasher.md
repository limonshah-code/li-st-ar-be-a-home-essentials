## Manual Cleaning: The Safest Method for All Stanley Gear

### Step-by-Step Hand Washing Guide

1.  **Disassemble Everything:** Take apart the lid, straw, and any removable gaskets. This step is crucial for thorough cleaning.
2.  **Rinse Immediately:** Rinse all parts with warm water after each use. This removes loose debris and prevents drying.
3.  **Prepare Cleaning Solution:** Fill your Stanley cup with warm water and a few drops of dish soap. Use a mild soap.
4.  **Scrub the Interior:** Use a bottle brush to scrub the inside of the cup. Pay attention to the bottom and sides. Ensure you remove all drink residue.
5.  **Clean the Lid and Straw:** Use a small brush, like a straw brush, for the straw and lid crevices. A sponge or cloth works for larger lid surfaces. Ensure all parts of the lid are clean.
6.  **Rinse Thoroughly:** Rinse all components with clean, warm water. Make sure no soap residue remains.
7.  **Air Dry Completely:** Let all parts air dry completely before reassembling. This prevents mold and mildew growth.

### Tools for Effective Hand Washing

*   **Bottle Brush:** A long-handled bottle brush reaches the bottom of tall tumblers. It effectively scrubs the interior.
*   **Straw Brush:** A thin, flexible straw brush cleans the inside of reusable straws. This is important for hygiene.
*   **Small Bristle Brush:** This type of brush helps clean lid crevices and other small parts.
*   **Mild Dish Soap:** Choose a gentle dish soap. It should be effective but not harsh on materials.
*   **Soft Cloth or Sponge:** Use this for wiping exterior surfaces.

### Dealing with Stubborn Stains and Odors

Sometimes, your Stanley cup might develop stubborn stains or odors. This happens with coffee or strong-smelling drinks. A simple soap and water wash might not be enough. I have a few tricks for these situations.

For stains, try a mixture of baking soda and warm water. Make a paste with baking soda and a little water. Apply it to the stain inside the cup. Let it sit for 15-30 minutes. Then, scrub with your bottle brush and rinse well. Baking soda is a natural abrasive and deodorizer.

For odors, fill the cup with a solution of white vinegar and water. Use equal parts vinegar and water. Let it sit overnight. The vinegar neutralizes odors. In the morning, rinse thoroughly with soap and water. You might need to air it out. These methods keep your Stanley fresh.
