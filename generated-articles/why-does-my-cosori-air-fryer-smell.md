## Cosori Air Fryer Smell: What Causes It & How to Fix It

There is nothing quite like the convenience of an air fryer. Your Cosori air fryer quickly creates crispy, delicious meals. But what happens when that pleasant cooking aroma turns into something less desirable? If your **Cosori air fryer smells** unusual, you are not alone. Many users experience strange odors, ranging from a new plastic smell to a persistent burning scent.

Understanding the root cause of these smells is the first step toward a fresh-smelling kitchen. This article explores common reasons why your Cosori air fryer might smell. We will discuss everything from first-time use odors to stubborn food residues and potential electrical issues. My aim is to give you clear, actionable steps. You will learn how to identify the smell's origin and effectively eliminate it. Get ready to enjoy your air fryer once again, without any unwanted odors.

### Why Does My Cosori Air Fryer Smell?

Your Cosori air fryer smells typically due to new appliance off-gassing, accumulated food residue, grease buildup, or burning food particles. Less commonly, it could signal issues with internal components or the non-stick coating. Regular cleaning and proper ventilation often resolve most odor problems.

### New Air Fryer Smell: Breaking In Your Cosori

Bringing home a new Cosori air fryer is exciting. You might notice a distinct plastic or chemical smell during the first few uses. This odor is quite common and usually harmless. It comes from manufacturing oils, new plastic components, and protective coatings burning off. This process is called "off-gassing."

My new Cosori air fryer had this smell too. It is a normal part of the break-in period. Manufacturers apply various coatings and lubricants during production. These substances can emit odors when heated for the first time. The smell usually fades after a few uses.

#### Initial Burn-Off Process

To help eliminate the new air fryer smell faster, perform an initial "burn-off." This process helps to bake off any residual manufacturing substances. It is a simple step that many appliance manufacturers recommend. I always do this with new cooking devices.

Here are the steps for a proper burn-off:

*   Unpack your Cosori air fryer and remove all packaging materials.
*   Wash the air fryer basket and crisping tray with warm, soapy water. Dry them thoroughly.
*   Place the clean basket and tray back into the air fryer.
*   Plug in the air fryer.
*   Set the temperature to 400°F (205°C) and the timer for 10-15 minutes.
*   Run the air fryer in a well-ventilated area. Open windows or use an exhaust fan.
*   Allow the air fryer to cool completely after the cycle finishes.
*   You might repeat this process once or twice if the smell persists.

This burn-off helps to accelerate the release of odors. It prepares your air fryer for cooking without imparting strange tastes to your food. #### Material Odors

Beyond the initial manufacturing residues, the materials themselves can sometimes produce a faint odor. Cosori air fryers often use plastic and metal components. The internal wiring and heating elements might also contribute to a subtle smell initially. Rest assured, these odors typically dissipate quickly.

I found that after two or three burn-off cycles and a few cooking sessions, the new air fryer smell vanished completely. It is important to only use your air fryer in a space with good air circulation. This helps to clear away any fumes. If the plastic smell seems too strong or persists for many uses, it could indicate a defect. In such rare cases, contacting Cosori customer support is a good idea. They can offer further advice or arrange a replacement.

### Food Residue Buildup: The Common Culprit

The most frequent reason for a bad Cosori air fryer smell is leftover food residue. When food particles, grease, or oil accumulate inside the air fryer, they can burn and decompose during subsequent cooking cycles. This creates unpleasant odors. Think of old burnt popcorn crumbs or dried-up oil splatter.

Even tiny bits of food can cause a strong smell. Grease drips onto the heating element or into crevices. There it can become rancid or burn each time you cook. This often results in a smoky, stale, or even fishy smell, depending on what was previously cooked. Regular cleaning is essential to prevent this.

#### Cleaning the Basket and Tray

The air fryer basket and crisping tray are the parts that directly contact food. They collect most of the grease and food bits. These parts must be cleaned thoroughly after every use. Ignoring them allows oils to become sticky and difficult to remove.

Here is my recommended cleaning routine for these components:

*   **After Every Use:** Unplug the air fryer and let it cool completely. Remove the basket and tray. Wash them immediately with warm water and dish soap. A non-abrasive sponge or brush helps remove stuck-on food.
*   **For Stubborn Residue:** If food is really stuck, soak the basket and tray in hot, soapy water for 15-30 minutes. This softens the residue, making it easier to scrub off. You can also make a paste of baking soda and water to gently scrub tough spots.
*   **Dishwasher Safe:** Many Cosori air fryer baskets and trays are dishwasher safe. Check your model's manual to confirm. Putting them in the dishwasher saves time and ensures thorough cleaning. You can learn more about this here: [can you put Cosori air fryer basket in dishwasher](https://homeessentialsguide.com/can-you-put-cosori-air-fryer-basket-in-dishwasher).
*   **Dry Thoroughly:** Always ensure the basket and tray are completely dry before putting them back into the air fryer. Moisture can lead to mold or mildew growth, which also causes bad smells.

By consistently cleaning these parts, you eliminate the primary source of food-related odors. My experience shows that this simple step prevents most smell issues.

#### Deep Cleaning the Heating Element

While the basket and tray are easy to clean, the heating element and interior cavity also collect grime. Grease splatters upwards during cooking. It sticks to the heating coil and the interior ceiling of the air fryer. This often goes unnoticed until a burning smell starts.

Cleaning the heating element and interior requires more care:

*   **Safety First:** Always unplug the air fryer and ensure it is completely cool before attempting to clean the interior.
*   **Invert and Inspect:** Turn the air fryer upside down if possible. This gives you a better view of the heating coil.
*   **Soft Brush or Cloth:** Use a soft brush (like a toothbrush or a non-abrasive cleaning brush) to gently dislodge any food particles stuck to the heating element. You can also use a damp cloth, but avoid excess water.
*   **Damp Cloth for Interior:** Wipe the interior walls and ceiling with a damp cloth and a small amount of dish soap. For stubborn grease, a solution of water and white vinegar (50/50) can work wonders.
*   **Remove Old Oil:** Pay close attention to the bottom of the air fryer cavity, where oil might pool. Use paper towels to absorb any old, rancid oil.
*   **Dry Completely:** Let the interior air dry completely before plugging in and using the air fryer again. Any moisture on the heating element can cause smoke or even a short circuit.
*   **Dedicated Guide:** For a more detailed guide on cleaning this crucial part, refer to this resource: [how to clean Cosori air fryer heating element](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-clean-cosori-air-fryer-heating-element).

Cleaning the heating element is not a daily task. I recommend doing it every few weeks or whenever you notice a persistent smell. This deep cleaning removes hidden grease and food particles. It ensures your air fryer remains fresh.

### Burning Smell: Overheating or Electrical Issues

A burning smell is perhaps the most alarming odor from an air fryer. This smell often suggests something is genuinely burning. It could be harmless, like food splatter, or it could indicate a more serious electrical problem. It is important to stop cooking and investigate immediately if you detect this smell.

My first thought when I smell burning is always safety. Unplugging the unit right away is crucial. Then, you can safely inspect the cause. Do not continue using the air fryer if the burning smell is accompanied by smoke or strange noises.

#### Overloading and Overheating

One common reason for a burning smell is simply overloading the air fryer basket. Packing too much food into the basket restricts airflow. This leads to uneven cooking and can cause food to touch the heating element. When food directly touches the hot coil, it burns quickly. This generates smoke and a burning odor.

Here are related points:

*   **Avoid Overcrowding:** Always leave space between food items in the basket. This allows hot air to circulate freely. It ensures even cooking and prevents burning.
*   **High-Fat Foods:** Foods high in fat, like bacon or fatty cuts of meat, can cause more oil to drip. This oil can splatter onto the heating element and burn. Using a parchment paper liner or aluminum foil can help contain grease. Ensure liners do not block airflow.
*   **Cooking Temperature:** Setting the temperature too high for certain foods can also cause them to burn quickly. Adjust your cooking times and temperatures according to recipes.
*   **Oil and Sprays:** Using cooking sprays with a low smoke point inside the air fryer can cause a burning smell. Choose oils with a high smoke point, like avocado oil or grapeseed oil. Avoid aerosol sprays with propellants that can damage the non-stick coating.

If you notice your Cosori air fryer is smoking, especially with a burning smell, refer to our specific guide: [why is my Cosori air fryer smoking](https://homeessentialsguide.com/why-is-my-cosori-air-fryer-smoking). This resource provides detailed troubleshooting steps for smoke issues.

#### Electrical Component Issues

Less commonly, a burning smell can indicate an issue with the air fryer's electrical components. This is a more serious concern. If the smell is like burning plastic or ozone (a sharp, chemical smell), and you cannot find any burnt food residue, it might be an internal electrical problem.

Signs of electrical issues include:

*   **Persistent Chemical/Plastic Burn Smell:** Even after thoroughly cleaning the air fryer.
*   **Sparks or Flashes:** Visible sparks inside the unit.
*   **Tripped Circuit Breaker:** The air fryer causes your home's breaker to trip.
*   **Malfunctioning Controls:** Buttons or display not working correctly.
*   **Smoke without Food Burning:** Smoke appears even when there is no food or very little food in the basket.

If you suspect an electrical problem, immediately unplug the air fryer. Do not use it again. Electrical issues can pose a fire hazard. Contact Cosori customer support for assistance. Do not attempt to repair the electrical components yourself unless you are a qualified electrician. It is safer to seek professional advice or consider replacing the unit.

### Plastic or Chemical Odors: Material Breakdown & VOCs

Beyond the initial new appliance smell, some users report a persistent plastic or chemical odor. This can be more concerning. It might suggest material degradation or issues with the non-stick coating over time. Understanding the materials used in your Cosori air fryer can help explain these odors.

Most air fryers, including Cosori models, use a combination of plastics, metals, and non-stick coatings. When these materials are exposed to high heat repeatedly, they can sometimes emit volatile organic compounds (VOCs). These compounds produce a distinctive smell.

#### Non-Stick Coating Concerns

Cosori air fryer baskets typically feature a non-stick coating. These coatings, often PTFE (Teflon) or ceramic-based, are designed to withstand high temperatures. However, if the coating gets scratched or damaged, it can potentially degrade faster. This degradation might release odors.

Consider these points about non-stick coatings:

*   **Damaged Coating:** If you see flakes or scratches in your air fryer's non-stick coating, it is possible for the underlying material to be exposed to heat. This might contribute to a chemical smell. Always use silicone or wooden utensils to prevent scratching.
*   **Overheating Coating:** While non-stick coatings are heat-resistant, extreme, prolonged high temperatures could potentially cause them to break down. Stick to recommended cooking temperatures.
*   **PFOA/PFOS-Free:** Cosori states their air fryers are PFOA and BPA-free. This is important for health and safety. Still, if you are sensitive to chemical odors, any breakdown of the coating could be noticeable. If you're curious about the materials, you can read more here: [is Cosori air fryer made of teflon](https://homeessentialsguide.com/is-cosori-air-fryer-made-of-teflon).

If you suspect the smell is related to the coating, inspect your basket. If the coating is significantly damaged, replacing the basket might be necessary.

#### Plastic Parts Degradation

Some components of the air fryer are made of plastic. This includes handles, control panels, and internal supports. While these plastics are typically heat-resistant, prolonged exposure to extreme heat, especially from repeated cooking cycles, can cause them to weaken or degrade.

Signs of plastic degradation:

*   **Persistent Plastic Smell:** A strong, consistent plastic smell that does not go away after extensive cleaning and burn-off cycles.
*   **Discoloration or Warping:** Visible changes to plastic parts, such as yellowing, cracking, or warping.
*   **Smoke from Plastic Areas:** Smoke coming directly from plastic components, not from food.

If you observe any of these signs, stop using the air fryer immediately. A degrading plastic component can lead to more serious issues. Contact Cosori customer service for guidance. It is always better to be safe than sorry when dealing with appliance safety.

### Unusual Smells: Beyond the Obvious

Sometimes, your Cosori air fryer might emit smells that are not typical burning or plastic odors. These unusual smells can range from musty to sour or even rancid. They often point to less common issues that still require your attention. Ignoring these smells can affect the taste of your food and potentially lead to hygiene problems.

I always pay attention to any smell that feels "off." It is a good indicator something needs checking. These smells might not be as alarming as burning, but they still indicate a problem.

#### Mold and Mildew

If your Cosori air fryer smells musty or like old socks, you might have a mold or mildew problem. This occurs when moisture gets trapped inside the air fryer and is not allowed to dry properly. Mold can grow in damp, dark environments, especially in crevices or under components if residual water remains after cleaning.

How to address mold or mildew:

*   **Thorough Drying:** Ensure all parts of your air fryer, especially the basket, tray, and interior cavity, are completely dry before storage. I often leave them air-drying for a few hours after washing.
*   **Inspect and Clean:** Visually inspect all surfaces for any signs of mold. If found, clean the affected areas with a solution of white vinegar and water (50/50). Vinegar is a natural disinfectant and mold killer.
*   **Air Out:** After cleaning, let the air fryer sit open in a well-ventilated area to thoroughly air out.

Preventing moisture buildup is key. Never store your air fryer if any part is still damp.

#### Rancid Oils and Sprays

Old, rancid cooking oils are another source of unpleasant smells. If you use cooking sprays or oils directly in the air fryer, tiny droplets can settle on the heating element or the bottom of the unit. Over time, these oils can become rancid, especially if exposed to heat repeatedly. They will then emit a sour or stale odor when heated.

Tips to avoid rancid oil smells:

*   **High Smoke Point Oils:** Use oils with a high smoke point that are suitable for air frying. Examples include avocado oil, grapeseed oil, or canola oil. Olive oil has a lower smoke point and can produce more smoke and odors at air frying temperatures.
*   **Avoid Aerosol Sprays:** Many aerosol cooking sprays contain propellants and additives that can leave a sticky residue. This residue is difficult to clean and can turn rancid. Opt for pump spray bottles filled with your preferred high-smoke-point oil.
*   **Regular Deep Cleaning:** As mentioned earlier, regularly cleaning the heating element and the interior cavity will remove any accumulated oil before it has a chance to turn rancid.

By being mindful of the oils you use and ensuring thorough drying and regular cleaning, you can effectively prevent these unusual and unpleasant smells from developing in your Cosori air fryer.

### Prevention is Key: Maintaining a Fresh Cosori Air Fryer

Preventing bad smells in your Cosori air fryer is much easier than trying to eliminate them once they have taken hold. A consistent cleaning routine and proper usage habits are your best defense. I have found that a little effort after each use goes a long way in keeping my air fryer fresh and odor-free. This ensures your food always tastes its best.

Think of it like any other cooking appliance. You wouldn't leave a greasy pan unwashed for days. Your air fryer needs similar attention.

#### Daily Cleaning Habits

Making a habit of cleaning your air fryer after every use is the most effective preventative measure. This simple routine stops food residue and grease from accumulating and baking onto surfaces.

Follow these daily cleaning steps:

*   **Unplug and Cool:** Always unplug your Cosori air fryer and allow it to cool completely before cleaning. This prevents burns and electrical shocks.
*   **Wash Basket and Tray:** Remove the air fryer basket and crisping tray. Wash them immediately with warm, soapy water. Use a non-abrasive sponge to scrub away any food particles and grease.
*   **Wipe Down Interior:** Use a damp cloth to wipe the interior cavity of the air fryer. This removes any splatters or crumbs from the cooking process.
*   **Clean Exterior:** Wipe the exterior of the air fryer with a damp cloth. This keeps it looking good and prevents dust buildup.
*   **Dry Thoroughly:** Ensure all parts are completely dry before reassembling or storing the air fryer. This prevents mold and mildew.
*   **Specific Instructions:** For comprehensive cleaning instructions for your Cosori air fryer, check out our guide on [how to wash Cosori air fryer basket](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-wash-cosori-air-fryer-basket).

This routine takes only a few minutes. It significantly reduces the likelihood of future odor problems.

Deep cleaning steps:

Deep cleaning prevents old, hidden residues from becoming a source of bad smells. It maintains optimal air fryer performance.

#### Proper Ventilation

Ventilation plays a crucial role in preventing smells, especially new appliance odors or during heavy cooking. Your air fryer circulates hot air, and some of the steam, smoke, and odors from food will be expelled.

*   **Location:** Always place your Cosori air fryer on a stable, heat-resistant surface. Ensure there is enough clearance around it. Do not place it directly under cabinets that could trap expelled air.
*   **Airflow:** Make sure the vents on your air fryer are not blocked. Blocked vents can lead to internal overheating and the accumulation of odors.
*   **Kitchen Ventilation:** Use your kitchen's exhaust fan or open a window when air frying, especially when cooking strong-smelling foods like fish, bacon, or highly seasoned dishes. This helps to dissipate cooking odors quickly, preventing them from lingering in your kitchen or settling back into the air fryer.

Good ventilation ensures that any normal cooking smells or initial off-gassing dissipate harmlessly. It keeps your kitchen air fresh.

### When to Seek Professional Help or Replace

While most Cosori air fryer smells are manageable with cleaning and proper use, there are situations when you should consider professional help or even replacement. Prioritizing safety is paramount. If a smell seems persistent, dangerous, or causes discomfort, do not hesitate to act.

My rule of thumb is: if it smells like a fire hazard or something truly toxic, it is time to stop using the appliance. Do not risk it.

#### Persistent Unidentified Odors

If you have tried all cleaning methods—including deep cleaning the basket, tray, and heating element—and performed initial burn-off cycles, but a strong, unusual odor persists, it is a cause for concern. This is particularly true if the smell is chemical, ozone-like, or a consistent burning plastic smell that is not related to food.

*   **What to do:**
    *   **Double-Check:** Re-check all parts for hidden food bits or signs of material damage.
    *   **Contact Cosori Support:** If you cannot pinpoint the source and the smell is concerning, reach out to Cosori customer support. Provide them with details about your model, how long you have had it, and what troubleshooting steps you have taken. They may offer specific advice, replacement parts, or a warranty replacement if applicable.

#### Electrical Malfunctions

Any smell accompanied by signs of electrical malfunction is a red flag. These signs include:

*   **Sparks, Flashes, or Smoke:** Visible sparks from the air fryer, flashes of light, or smoke that is not food-related.
*   **Tripped Breakers:** Your home's circuit breaker trips when you use the air fryer.
*   **Abnormal Noises:** Grinding, buzzing, or popping sounds from the internal components.
*   **Control Panel Issues:** The display flickers, buttons do not respond, or the unit turns on/off randomly.
*   **Burning Plastic/Ozone Smell:** A distinct smell of burning plastic or ozone that is clearly not food.

*   **What to do:**
    *   **Immediately Unplug:** Disconnect the air fryer from the power source immediately.
    *   **Do Not Use:** Do not attempt to use the air fryer again.
    *   **Consult Professional/Replace:** Electrical issues pose a fire risk. Do not try to repair it yourself unless you are a certified professional. It is usually safer and more cost-effective to replace the unit.

#### Visible Damage or Degradation

If you notice obvious physical damage to critical components, it is time to consider replacing the air fryer. This includes:

*   **Melted or Warped Plastic:** Any plastic parts, especially near the heating element or vents, that appear melted, warped, or discolored.
*   **Damaged Non-Stick Coating:** Significant flaking, bubbling, or deep scratches in the non-stick coating of the basket or tray, especially if exposing the metal beneath. While cosmetic, it can contribute to smells and raise health concerns.
*   **Frayed Wires:** Visible frayed or damaged power cords or internal wiring (if you can safely see them).

*   **What to do:**
    *   **Assess Safety:** If the damage affects safety (e.g., electrical components, fire risk), discontinue use.
    *   **Replace Components (if possible):** For easily replaceable parts like the basket, you might buy a new one.
    *   **Consider New Unit:** For internal damage, it is usually safer and more practical to invest in a new Cosori air fryer.

It is always better to err on the side of caution. A malfunctioning air fryer can be a safety hazard. Your peace of mind and safety are worth the investment in a new appliance if necessary.

### FAQ Section

**Q1: Is it normal for a new Cosori air fryer to smell?**
Yes, it is common for a new Cosori air fryer to emit a plastic or chemical smell during its first few uses. This "new appliance smell" comes from manufacturing oils and protective coatings burning off. Perform an initial burn-off cycle in a well-ventilated area to help dissipate these odors faster. The smell usually fades completely after a few uses.

**Q2: How do I get rid of the plastic smell from my Cosori air fryer?**
To remove a plastic smell, first, perform an initial burn-off. Run the empty air fryer at 400°F (205°C) for 10-15 minutes in a well-ventilated space. Repeat if necessary. Ensure all packaging is removed. Clean the basket and tray thoroughly before and after the burn-off. The smell should diminish over time with regular use.

**Q3: Why does my Cosori air fryer smell like burning plastic?**
A burning plastic smell in your Cosori air fryer can indicate several issues. It might be the normal off-gassing of new components if the unit is new. If it's an older unit, it could signal plastic parts degrading due to overheating or an electrical malfunction. Immediately unplug the unit, inspect for visible damage, and if the smell persists or is accompanied by smoke/sparks, discontinue use and contact support.

**Q4: How often should I clean my Cosori air fryer to prevent smells?**
You should clean the Cosori air fryer basket and crisping tray after every single use to prevent food residue buildup. Wipe down the interior cavity with a damp cloth after each use as well. Perform a deep cleaning of the heating element and interior walls every few weeks or once a month, depending on your usage frequency, to remove hidden grease and crumbs.

**Q5: Can food stuck in the heating element cause my air fryer to smell?**
Yes, absolutely. Food particles, grease, or oil splatters can accumulate on the heating element and the interior top of your Cosori air fryer. When the air fryer heats up again, these stuck-on residues will burn, creating a strong, unpleasant smell. Regularly inspecting and gently cleaning the heating element is crucial for preventing this common odor source.
