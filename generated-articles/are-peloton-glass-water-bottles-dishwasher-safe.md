### The Glass Itself: Durability and Heat Resistance

The glass used in Peloton bottles is often borosilicate glass. This type of glass is known for its excellent durability. It resists thermal shock better than standard glass. Thermal shock is the stress caused by sudden temperature changes. This means it can handle hot and cold liquids without cracking. It also tolerates the high temperatures of a dishwasher cycle.

Borosilicate glass is also less prone to scratching. This keeps your bottle looking clear and new. Its non-porous surface resists odors and stains. This makes it a hygienic choice for a reusable water bottle. Properly caring for this glass ensures its longevity.

When dishwashing your Peloton glass bottle, placement matters significantly. Always place the glass body on the top rack of your dishwasher. The top rack is typically gentler. Placing delicate items like glass bottles on the bottom rack increases the chance of damage. Ensure the bottle is secure and does not move around during the wash cycle. Movement can lead to breakage. ## FAQ Section

### Are all Peloton water bottles glass?
No, Peloton offers various types of water bottles, including those made from plastic and insulated stainless steel, in addition to glass. The material choice often depends on the specific product line or promotional offerings. Always check the product description for material details. Each material has different cleaning requirements.

### Can I put the silicone sleeve in the dishwasher?
It is not recommended to put the silicone sleeve in the dishwasher. High heat and prolonged exposure to detergent can cause the silicone to warp, lose its elasticity, or degrade over time. Hand washing with warm, soapy water is the best method to preserve the sleeve's fit and integrity.

### How often should I clean my Peloton glass bottle?
You should clean your Peloton glass bottle after every use, especially if you use it for workouts or flavored drinks. Daily cleaning prevents bacterial growth and odor buildup. A deep clean using vinegar or baking soda is recommended weekly or bi-weekly.

### What if my bottle gets cloudy after washing?
Cloudiness on glass bottles often indicates hard water mineral deposits. To remove them, soak the bottle in a solution of equal parts white vinegar and water for several hours or overnight. Scrub with a bottle brush, then rinse thoroughly. Regularly using vinegar can prevent future cloudiness.

### Is it safe to use bleach on my glass bottle?
Using bleach on your glass bottle is generally not recommended. While glass can withstand it, bleach is a very harsh chemical. It can be difficult to rinse completely and may leave residues that are unsafe for consumption. Stick to milder cleaning agents like dish soap, vinegar, or baking soda.
