## The Primary Spot: Inside the Dishwasher Door Frame

Most Whirlpool dishwashers have their model number inside the door. This is the first place you should check. Open the dishwasher door completely. Look around the edges of the inner door.

### Along the Top Edge of the Door

Many Whirlpool models place the sticker here. It is often a thin label. It sits on the metal edge that faces upward when the door is open. You might see it on the tub's edge too.

This sticker contains several numbers. The model number is usually clearly labeled. It might say "Model No." or "MDL." The serial number will also be nearby. Take a moment to read the label carefully.

Sometimes, food particles or cleaning products cover the label. Use a damp cloth to gently clean the area. This helps make the numbers clear and easy to read. Do not scrub too hard, as this can damage the sticker.

### On the Side or Bottom Edge of the Door Frame

If it is not on the top edge, check the sides. Look on the metal frame that surrounds the opening of the dishwasher. It might be on the left or right side. It could also be near the bottom where the door hinges.

This spot is common for many brands. The sticker is usually small. It is often white or silver with black text. I found mine on the upper right side of the door frame once.

Remember to look both inside the tub itself and on the edge of the door. The sticker can be on either surface. One good example is a sticker directly on the inside of the door panel, facing you when the door is open. ## Checking the Kickplate and Front Panel

If the door frame does not show the number, look lower. The kickplate is another common location. This is the panel at the very bottom front of your dishwasher. It often hides components or wiring.

### Locating the Kickplate

The kickplate is the long, narrow panel below the main dishwasher door. It usually matches the color of your dishwasher. It sits flush with the floor. You might need to bend down to see it well.

Sometimes, the sticker is on the outer surface of the kickplate. This makes it easy to spot. Just look closely at the center or sides of this panel. It may be a small label.

More often, the sticker is on the *inside* of the kickplate. You will need to remove the kickplate to see it. This process is usually simple. It requires a screwdriver.

### Removing the Kickplate

Most kickplates attach with a few screws. Look for two or four screws along its top edge. Use a Phillips-head screwdriver to remove them. Be careful not to strip the screw heads.

Once the screws are out, gently pull the kickplate forward. It might slide out or pivot down. You should then see the sticker on the back of the panel. The model number will be clear there.

After you find the number, put the kickplate back. Align the screws and tighten them. Do not overtighten, as this can damage the panel or screws. This simple check gives you a good chance of finding the number. This is another area where you might find your appliance's unique identifier. ## Understanding Your Whirlpool Model and Serial Numbers

Once you find the sticker, you will see many numbers. It is important to know which one is the model number. You also need to understand the serial number. Each has a different purpose.

### What is the Model Number?

The model number identifies the specific product line. It tells you the design and features of your dishwasher. It is often a mix of letters and numbers. It might look something like WDT750SAKZ0 or GDT655SMNES.

The sticker usually labels it clearly. Look for "Model No.," "MDL," "Part No.," or "P/N." This is the number you need for parts and service. It helps ensure compatibility.

This number is fixed for a product line. All dishwashers of the same model have the same model number. It is not unique to your specific unit. It defines the product itself.

### What is the Serial Number?

The serial number is unique to your individual dishwasher. It is like your appliance's fingerprint. No two dishwashers have the same serial number. It tells you when and where your specific unit was made.

The serial number is typically longer. It is often near the model number. It might say "Serial No." or "SER." This number is important for warranty claims. It also helps track specific manufacturing batches.

Technicians use the serial number for recalls or service bulletins. It connects to the exact date your dishwasher left the factory. This helps with precise record-keeping. Always provide both model and serial numbers when calling for support.

### Manufacturing Date Codes

Some Whirlpool stickers also include a manufacturing date code. This tells you the month and year your dishwasher was made. It might be part of the serial number or a separate code.

Understanding these numbers helps you troubleshoot. It helps with appliance registration. It also makes future maintenance simpler. This information is key for accurate support.
