## Is Made In Stainless Steel Cookware Dishwasher Safe?

Made In stainless steel cookware is a favorite for many home cooks. It is known for its durability and even heating. You can put Made In stainless steel items in the dishwasher. They are designed to withstand the machine's cleaning cycles. This includes their popular stainless clad frying pans and saucepans.

However, Made In, like most premium cookware brands, suggests hand washing their stainless steel. Why? Dishwashers use very hot water and strong detergents. These can cause a cloudy film to form on stainless steel. This film is often called "etching" or "hard water spots." It does not affect cooking performance. But it can make your beautiful cookware look dull. High heat can also cause slight discoloration over time. This is especially true if you have hard water. Hand washing preserves the shine and finish.

When you hand wash stainless steel, use warm, soapy water. A soft sponge or cloth works well. For stubborn food, you can let the pan soak for a bit. Avoid abrasive scrubbers or steel wool. These can scratch the surface. After washing, dry the pan completely to prevent water spots. This simple habit keeps your stainless steel looking new. It also helps it perform its best for many years of cooking. Good care ensures your Made In stainless steel cookware maintains its quality.

## Caring for Made In Non-Stick Cookware: Dishwasher Avoidance

Made In non-stick cookware offers an easy cooking and cleanup experience. The non-stick surface makes cooking delicate foods simple. However, these pans are very sensitive to the dishwasher environment. Made In explicitly states that their non-stick cookware is *not* dishwasher safe. This rule is important to follow.

The high heat inside a dishwasher is the first problem. Extreme temperatures can break down the non-stick coating. This leads to flaking or bubbling. Harsh detergents are another issue. These chemicals can strip away the non-stick layer's properties. Over time, the coating loses its effectiveness. Your food will start to stick. The powerful water jets can also scratch or chip the delicate surface. This damage ruins the non-stick function of the pan. It can also release small particles into your food.

To properly care for your Made In non-stick pans, always hand wash them. Use warm, soapy water and a soft sponge. For stuck-on food, let the pan cool completely. Then, fill it with warm water and a little dish soap. Let it soak for 10-15 minutes. This loosens food particles. Gently wipe them away. Rinse thoroughly and dry immediately with a soft towel. Avoid metal utensils or harsh scrubbing pads when cooking or cleaning. Proper hand washing helps your non-stick pans last longer. It keeps them safe and effective for your kitchen needs. ## Dishwashing Made In Carbon Steel and Cast Iron Cookware

Made In offers carbon steel and cast iron cookware, prized by chefs for their performance. These materials require specific care. They are absolutely *not* dishwasher safe. Putting them in a dishwasher will cause immediate and significant damage. This is due to their unique properties and how they are seasoned.

Carbon steel and cast iron pans develop a "seasoning" layer. This layer is a baked-on coating of oil. It creates a natural non-stick surface. It also protects the metal from rust. A dishwasher uses hot water and strong detergents. These strip away the protective seasoning layer. This leaves the bare metal exposed. Once exposed, carbon steel and cast iron will rust very quickly. Rust makes the cookware unusable. It also ruins the cooking surface. Even a single run through the dishwasher can cause severe rust.

Proper cleaning for Made In carbon steel and cast iron involves hand washing. Use hot water and a stiff brush or non-abrasive scrubber. Avoid soap if possible, especially for cast iron, as it can remove seasoning. If you must use a tiny bit of soap, rinse quickly and thoroughly. Immediately dry the pan completely on the stovetop over low heat. Once dry, apply a thin layer of cooking oil. Wipe away any excess oil. This re-seasons the pan and keeps it protected. Regular hand seasoning helps these pans last a lifetime. They will continue to perform beautifully with this care. Taking care of your cookware and bakeware properly is vital for its longevity. Beyond symbols, best practices make a difference. When you do put Made In stainless steel in the dishwasher:

*   **Load Correctly:** Ensure items do not touch each other. This prevents scratches.
*   **Use Mild Detergent:** Avoid harsh or abrasive dishwasher pods.
*   **Choose Lower Heat Settings:** Opt for an "eco" or "light wash" cycle. High heat can lead to water spots or discoloration.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### Q1: Can I put Made In stainless clad pans in the dishwasher?

Yes, Made In stainless clad pans are generally considered dishwasher safe. They are robust and can handle machine washing. However, for the best care and to maintain their shine, Made In recommends hand washing them. Dishwashers can cause water spots or dull the finish over time due to harsh detergents and high heat.

### Q2: What happens if I accidentally put Made In carbon steel in the dishwasher?

If you put Made In carbon steel in the dishwasher, it will strip away its seasoning. This protective layer prevents rust and provides a non-stick surface. Without seasoning, the carbon steel pan will rust very quickly. It will also lose its non-stick properties. You will need to clean the rust and re-season the pan thoroughly.

### Q3: How do I clean Made In non-stick pans to extend their life?

To extend the life of your Made In non-stick pans, always hand wash them. Use warm water, mild dish soap, and a soft sponge. Avoid abrasive scrubbers or harsh detergents. Let the pan cool before washing. For stubborn food, soak the pan briefly. Always dry the pan completely after washing.

### Q4: Does dishwashing Made In cookware void the warranty?

Made In's warranty typically covers manufacturing defects. While machine washing stainless steel might not void the warranty outright, improper cleaning of materials like non-stick, carbon steel, or cast iron can lead to damage not covered. Always follow Made In's specific care instructions for each material to ensure your warranty remains valid for covered issues.

### Q5: Are Made In bakeware items dishwasher safe?

Made In offers various bakeware items, including stoneware and sheet pans. Many of their bakeware pieces, particularly their stoneware, are dishwasher safe. However, always check the specific product's care instructions. Like cookware, hand washing is often recommended for bakeware to preserve its finish and extend its overall lifespan.

### Q6: Which specific Made In items are fully dishwasher safe without any caveats?

Made In's stainless steel cookware is the most robust for dishwasher use. This includes their stainless clad frying pans, saucepans, and stock pots. While technically safe, even these benefit from hand washing to maintain their aesthetic appeal. All other materials like non-stick, carbon steel, and copper core pieces require hand washing.
