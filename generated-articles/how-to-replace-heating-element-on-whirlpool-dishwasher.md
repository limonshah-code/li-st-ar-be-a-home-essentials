## Replace Heating Element on Your Whirlpool Dishwasher

Imagine loading your dishwasher, running a cycle, and then finding your dishes still wet and cold. This is a frustrating problem many homeowners face. Often, the culprit is a faulty heating element. A broken heating element stops your Whirlpool dishwasher from drying dishes effectively and sanitizing them properly. I know the feeling of opening the dishwasher door to find a puddle of water.

Luckily, replacing a heating element on a Whirlpool dishwasher is a manageable DIY project. You do not always need to call a costly repair technician. This guide helps you through each step of the process. We will cover diagnosis, necessary tools, safety measures, and the actual replacement. You will learn how to restore your dishwasher's performance and enjoy sparkling, dry dishes again.

### Takeaway

*   Identify a failing heating element by wet dishes and error codes.
*   Gather necessary tools like screwdrivers, nut drivers, and a multimeter.
*   Always disconnect power and water before starting work.
*   Carefully remove the old element and install the new one.
*   Test the dishwasher to confirm the repair is successful.

Replacing a heating element on your Whirlpool dishwasher involves disconnecting power and water, accessing the dishwasher tub, removing the old element's retaining nuts and wires, and installing the new part, followed by reassembly and testing to ensure proper heating and drying function.

## Understanding Your Whirlpool Dishwasher's Heating Element

Your Whirlpool dishwasher uses a heating element for two main jobs. First, it heats the water during the wash and rinse cycles. This helps clean dishes better. Second, it dries the dishes at the end of the cycle. It warms the air inside the tub, allowing moisture to evaporate. This element looks like a metal coil at the bottom of the dishwasher.

When this part fails, you will notice problems. Your dishes may come out wet, even after a full cycle. They might also feel cool instead of warm. Understanding how this element works helps you identify when it needs attention. Replacing it yourself saves you money and brings back your dishwasher's top performance.

### Signs of a Failing Heating Element

Detecting a faulty heating element early saves you time and prevents frustration. I have personally experienced the signs of a failing element. Your dishwasher will not dry dishes well. This is often the first and most obvious sign.

*   **Wet Dishes After Cycle:** Dishes remain wet or damp at the end of the drying cycle. The element cannot produce enough heat to evaporate the water.
*   **Cold Dishes:** Dishes feel cold to the touch after the cycle finishes. Normally, they should be warm from the heat.
*   **No Hot Water:** The water inside the dishwasher does not get hot. This impacts cleaning performance.
*   **Dishwasher Error Codes:** Some modern Whirlpool models may display specific error codes related to heating issues. Check your user manual for details on these codes.
*   **Burn Marks or Odor:** In rare cases, you might see burn marks on the element or smell a burning odor. This indicates serious damage.

If you observe these symptoms, your heating element likely needs replacement. A clear diagnosis comes next.

## Diagnosing Your Whirlpool Dishwasher Heating Element

Before you replace the heating element, you must confirm it is the problem. A proper diagnosis prevents unnecessary repairs. I always double-check the issue before ordering parts. You can perform a simple visual check and an electrical test.

A faulty heating element typically shows signs like cold or wet dishes after a cycle. It fails to heat water or dry dishes properly. These symptoms point towards the heating element as the source of the issue.

### Visual Inspection Tips

Start by looking at the heating element. It is located at the bottom of the dishwasher tub. You might need to remove the lower dish rack for a clearer view. Turn on a flashlight and examine the element.

*   **Check for Cracks or Breaks:** Look for any visible cracks, breaks, or signs of physical damage on the metal coil. A broken element cannot conduct electricity.
*   **Examine for Discoloration:** Note any severe discoloration, burn marks, or blistering. These are signs of overheating or electrical shorts.
*   **Inspect Connections:** Ensure the electrical terminals where wires connect to the element are not loose or corroded. Loose connections can prevent power flow.
*   **Debris Around Element:** Sometimes, food debris or plastic items melt onto the element. This can cause issues. Clean any obstructions carefully.

A visible issue makes the diagnosis easy. If you see no obvious damage, the problem might be internal. This requires electrical testing.

### Testing the Heating Element with a Multimeter

The most reliable way to confirm a bad heating element is by testing its electrical continuity. This requires a multimeter. A multimeter measures resistance. A functional heating element will show continuity.

1.  **Locate the Heating Element Terminals:** The heating element has two metal terminals. These are usually underneath the dishwasher, accessible from the front or back after pulling the unit out.
2.  **Disconnect Wires:** Carefully disconnect the wires attached to these terminals. Note which wire goes to which terminal, though for a heating element, it often does not matter for polarity.
3.  **Set Multimeter:** Set your multimeter to the ohms (Î©) setting, specifically the lowest resistance range. This tests for continuity.
4.  **Test for Continuity:** Place one probe of the multimeter on each terminal of the heating element.
    *   **Good Element:** A working heating element will show a resistance reading between 10 and 30 ohms. The exact value varies by model.
    *   **Bad Element:** A reading of "OL" (open line) or infinity indicates no continuity. This means the element is broken internally and needs replacement.
5.  **Test for Ground:** While still disconnected, touch one multimeter probe to an element terminal and the other probe to a bare metal part of the dishwasher chassis. You should get an "OL" or infinite reading. If you get a resistance reading, the element is shorted to ground. This also means it is bad.

This test is definitive. If your multimeter shows an open circuit, your heating element is faulty. ## Step-by-Step Guide to Replacing the Heating Element

Replacing the heating element involves several steps. It requires careful attention to detail. I will walk you through the process based on my own experience. Take your time with each step.

You will typically access the heating element from the front of the dishwasher. This involves pulling the dishwasher out from its cabinet. Do not rush this process.

### Disconnecting Power and Water

This is the most critical first step. Safety depends on it. I cannot stress this enough.

1.  **Locate Circuit Breaker:** Find your home's main electrical panel. Identify the breaker labeled for your kitchen or dishwasher.
2.  **Turn Off Power:** Flip the circuit breaker to the "OFF" position. Test the dishwasher controls to confirm no power.
3.  **Turn Off Water Supply:** Look under your kitchen sink. Find the shut-off valve for the dishwasher water line. It is usually a small handle or knob. Turn it clockwise until it is completely closed.
4.  **Disconnect Water Line (Optional, but Recommended):** Place a towel and a shallow pan under the water supply line connection at the dishwasher. Use an adjustable wrench to carefully unscrew the water inlet hose. Some residual water may leak out.

### Accessing the Dishwasher's Interior

You need to pull the dishwasher out to reach the heating element's terminals.

1.  **Remove Mounting Brackets:** Most dishwashers are secured to the underside of the countertop with screws. Open the dishwasher door. Look at the top front of the dishwasher frame. Unscrew these mounting brackets.
2.  **Adjust Legs (If Necessary):** The dishwasher might have adjustable legs. If it is too snug, slightly lower the front legs to create clearance.
3.  **Carefully Pull Out Dishwasher:** Gently slide the dishwasher out from its cabinet. Be careful not to crimp or damage the water lines or electrical cord. Pull it out just enough to comfortably access the side or rear, depending on where the terminals are. Some Whirlpool models let you access the terminals from the front kick panel.

### Removing the Old Heating Element

Now you can remove the faulty element. This part is inside the tub.

1.  **Open Dishwasher Door:** Open the dishwasher door completely.
2.  **Remove Lower Rack:** Pull out the lower dish rack and set it aside. This gives you clear access to the bottom of the tub.
3.  **Locate Heating Element:** The element is the U-shaped or coil-shaped rod at the bottom of the tub.
4.  **Access Terminals (From Underside):** Go to the front of the dishwasher. You will likely need to remove the lower kick panel. This panel is usually held by a few screws. Once removed, you will see the heating element terminals at the bottom.
5.  **Disconnect Wires:** Carefully pull the electrical wires off the two terminals of the heating element. You may need pliers to gently pry them off. Note their positions if they differ, though often they are interchangeable.
6.  **Remove Retaining Nuts:** From the underside, locate the two nuts that secure the heating element to the bottom of the dishwasher tub. These are usually 1/4 inch or 5/16 inch nuts. Use a nut driver to unscrew them.
7.  **Remove Element from Tub:** Go back to the dishwasher interior. Gently push the element terminals down through the holes in the tub. Then, lift the heating element up and out of the dishwasher tub. Be careful not to scratch the tub.

You have successfully removed the old, broken part. You are halfway done.

### Installing the New Heating Element

This is where your new part comes in. Installation is essentially the reverse of removal.

1.  **Insert New Element:** Carefully place the new heating element into the dishwasher tub. Align the two terminal ends with the holes at the bottom of the tub.
2.  **Push Terminals Through:** Push the terminals down through the holes until the element sits flush with the tub bottom.
3.  **Secure with Nuts:** Go back to the underside of the dishwasher. Thread the new retaining nuts onto the heating element terminals. Tighten them firmly with your nut driver. Do not overtighten, as you could crack the tub. They should be snug enough to prevent leaks.
4.  **Reconnect Wires:** Reattach the electrical wires to the new heating element terminals. Ensure they are pushed on securely. A loose connection can cause problems.

The physical replacement is now complete.

### Reassembling Your Whirlpool Dishwasher

Now you need to put everything back together.

1.  **Replace Kick Panel:** If you removed the kick panel, screw it back into place at the front base of the dishwasher.
2.  **Slide Dishwasher Back:** Gently slide the dishwasher back into its cabinet opening. Be careful not to pinch any hoses or wires.
3.  **Secure Mounting Brackets:** Screw the dishwasher mounting brackets back to the underside of the countertop. This stabilizes the unit.
4.  **Reconnect Water Line:** If you disconnected the water line, reattach it to the dishwasher's inlet valve. Hand-tighten first, then use a wrench for a snug fit. Do not overtighten.
5.  **Turn On Water Supply:** Open the water shut-off valve under the sink. Check for any leaks around the water line connection. Wipe up any drips.
6.  **Turn On Power:** Go back to your circuit breaker panel. Flip the dishwasher's breaker back to the "ON" position.

Your dishwasher is now reassembled.

## Post-Replacement Checks and Maintenance Tips

After replacing the heating element, it is important to perform a test run. This confirms your repair was successful. I always do a quick test cycle. Good maintenance habits help extend the life of your new element.

### Testing the New Element

A test cycle ensures everything works as it should.

1.  **Run a Short Cycle:** Load a few dishes or run an empty wash cycle. Choose a short wash cycle, ideally one that includes a heated dry option.
2.  **Check for Heat:** During the wash cycle, open the door briefly and carefully. You should feel hot water.
3.  **Monitor Drying:** At the end of the cycle, open the door. Your dishes should be hot and dry. If they are, your new heating element works correctly.
4.  **Check for Leaks:** While the dishwasher runs, look underneath and around the unit for any water leaks. Tighten connections if you see any.

If your dishes are still wet or cold, recheck your electrical connections. Also, re-test the element with a multimeter. Ensure you installed the correct part.

### Extending Heating Element Lifespan

Proper care can help your new heating element last longer. I follow these tips to keep my dishwasher in good shape.

*   **Scrape Dishes:** Scrape large food particles off dishes before loading. Excessive food debris can accumulate on the heating element. It can cause blockages or burn onto the element. For tips on general maintenance, learning how to clean a dishwasher heating element can be useful, even if you just replaced yours. [How to clean dishwasher heating element](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-clean-dishwasher-heating-element) explains more.
*   **Use Proper Detergent:** Use only dishwasher-specific detergent. Excessive suds or incorrect detergents can leave residue.
*   **Avoid Plastic Items on Bottom Rack:** Some plastic items can melt if they fall onto the heating element. Place plastics on the top rack or away from the element.
*   **Regular Cleaning:** Periodically run a dishwasher cleaner or a cycle with vinegar. This helps remove mineral buildup and soap scum. Buildup can insulate the element, making it work harder.
*   **Check Water Hardness:** Hard water can lead to mineral deposits. Consider a water softener if your area has very hard water.

Following these simple steps keeps your Whirlpool dishwasher heating element performing efficiently.

## When to Call a Professional for Whirlpool Dishwasher Repair

While replacing a heating element is a common DIY fix, some situations warrant professional help. It is good to know your limits. I sometimes decide to call a pro when things get too complicated.

*   **Persistent Electrical Issues:** If you replaced the heating element, but the dishwasher still has no power or trips the breaker, a deeper electrical issue might exist. This could involve the control board or wiring harness. These are more complex repairs.
*   **Water Leakage:** If you find significant leaks after replacement, and you have tightened all connections, there might be a problem with the tub seal or another component. Leaks can cause damage to your floors or cabinets.
*   **Undiagnosed Problems:** You have performed the multimeter test, and the heating element shows continuity, yet your dishes remain wet or cold. The issue could be with the thermostat, thermal fuse, control board, or even the wash pump. These components require specialized diagnostic tools and knowledge.
*   **Lack of Confidence:** If you feel uncomfortable at any point during the repair process, it is wise to stop. Working with electricity and water carries risks. A qualified technician ensures safety and a proper fix.
*   **Warranty Concerns:** If your Whirlpool dishwasher is still under warranty, performing DIY repairs might void it. Check your warranty terms before starting any work.

Knowing when to call a professional ensures your safety and the longevity of your appliance. They have the experience and tools to diagnose and fix complex problems. ## FAQ Section

### How long does a Whirlpool dishwasher heating element typically last?
A Whirlpool dishwasher heating element generally lasts between 5 to 10 years. Its lifespan depends on usage frequency, water hardness, and maintenance. Hard water and frequent use can shorten its life. Regular cleaning can help prevent mineral buildup, which can cause premature failure.

### Can a dishwasher work without a heating element?
A dishwasher can run its cycles without a working heating element. However, it will not heat the water for washing or drying. This means your dishes may not get fully clean. They will also come out wet at the end of the cycle. The sanitizing function will not work either.

### Is it hard to replace a dishwasher heating element?
Replacing a dishwasher heating element is a moderately difficult DIY task. It requires basic tools and electrical safety knowledge. The process involves disconnecting power, pulling the dishwasher out, and accessing connections from the bottom. Most homeowners with some mechanical skill can do it.

### Why is my Whirlpool dishwasher not drying dishes?
Your Whirlpool dishwasher is likely not drying dishes because of a faulty heating element. This element heats the air inside the tub to evaporate moisture. Other causes include a broken vent fan, incorrect cycle selection, or overloading the dishwasher. Check the heating element first.

### How much does a new Whirlpool heating element cost?
A new Whirlpool dishwasher heating element typically costs between $30 and $100 for the part itself. The price varies based on your specific dishwasher model. Labor costs for professional installation can add another $100-$200, making DIY replacement a cost-effective option.

### What causes a dishwasher heating element to fail?
Dishwasher heating elements often fail due to regular wear and tear over time. Mineral buildup from hard water can also cause the element to overheat and burn out. Physical damage from falling dishes or melted plastic can also lead to failure. Overloading the dishwasher can also strain the element.
