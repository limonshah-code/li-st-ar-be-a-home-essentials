## Common Issues and Troubleshooting Tips

Even after replacing the handle, you might encounter minor issues. Knowing how to troubleshoot these problems helps. Most issues are minor adjustments. They do not require a complete re-do of the handle replacement. I want you to feel confident addressing these.

One common issue is the door not closing properly. This means the latch might not engage. First, check if the inner door panel is fully seated. Re-press the edges to ensure all tabs are clicked in. Sometimes, a screw might be too tight or too loose, causing a slight warp. Loosen or tighten screws as needed. Also, visually inspect the door latch itself. Is it bent or damaged? A bent latch may need professional attention. If the door does not fully close, the microwave will not operate. This is a safety feature.

Another issue could be the handle feeling wobbly. If the handle feels loose after reassembly, you likely did not tighten the handle mounting screws enough. Unplug the microwave. Open the door. Re-access the handle mounting screws. Tighten them more firmly. Remember not to overtighten and strip the plastic. A firm but gentle tightening is best. The handle should feel solid.

Sometimes, after reassembly, the microwave might not turn on. First, check if it is fully plugged in. Ensure the outlet has power. If it still does not turn on, the issue might be with the door interlock switches. These switches tell the microwave the door is closed.
