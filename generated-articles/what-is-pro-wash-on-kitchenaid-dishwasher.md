## The Science Behind Pro Wash: How It Works

The magic of KitchenAid's Pro Wash cycle lies in its sophisticated sensors. These sensors are often called turbidity sensors. They constantly monitor the water in the dishwasher. Turbidity measures how cloudy the water is. Cloudier water means more food particles. Clearer water means less food.

When the dishwasher starts, these sensors take readings. They analyze the amount of soil present. If the sensors detect heavy soil, the Pro Wash cycle responds. It automatically increases the wash time. It might also boost the water temperature. This helps to break down tough grease and food. The powerful spray arms then target the dishes. They ensure thorough coverage.

Conversely, if the sensors find the water is relatively clean, the cycle adapts. It might shorten the wash time. It could use less water or a lower temperature. This dynamic cleaning process saves resources. It still gives your dishes a perfect wash. This adaptive technology is what makes the "What Is Pro Wash On KitchenAid Dishwasher" feature so valuable.
