**What is LG washing machine Tub Clean?**

The LG washing machine Tub Clean feature is a self-cleaning cycle designed to remove detergent residue, lint, dirt, and odor-causing bacteria from inside the washer drum. ## How to Run the LG Tub Clean Cycle

Running the LG Tub Clean cycle is a simple process. It varies slightly depending on whether you have a front-load or top-load LG washing machine. I will guide you through the general steps for both types. Always check your specific LG washer manual for precise instructions.

### Running Tub Clean on LG Front-Load Washers

For front-load LG washers, the process typically goes like this:
1.  **Empty the Washer:** Make sure the drum is completely empty of clothes or other items.
2.  **Add Cleaner:** Pour a washing machine cleaner or two cups of white vinegar directly into the detergent dispenser or the tub itself. Do not use regular laundry detergent. For more on using vinegar, check out how to clean LG washing machine with vinegar.
3.  **Select Cycle:** Close the washer door. Turn the cycle selector dial to "Tub Clean" or "Self Clean." If your model does not have a dedicated dial setting, look for a button to activate this function.
4.  **Start Cycle:** Press the "Start/Pause" button to begin the cycle. The washer will fill, agitate, and drain. The cycle uses very hot water, so the door will lock for safety.
5.  **Wipe Down:** Once the cycle finishes, open the door. Wipe down the door gasket and drum interior with a clean cloth. This removes any remaining residue. Pay close attention to the rubber seal around the door. This area often collects grime. You can find more detailed steps on how to clean LG washing machine front load tub.

### Running Tub Clean on LG Top-Load Washers

For top-load LG washers, the steps are similar:
1.  **Empty the Washer:** Ensure no laundry is inside the washer.
2.  **Add Cleaner:** Add a washer cleaner or two cups of white vinegar directly into the wash tub. Do not use regular laundry detergent.
3.  **Select Cycle:** Turn the cycle selector to "Tub Clean" or "Smart Care" if available. Some models might have a "Clean Washer" or "Self Clean" button.
4.  **Start Cycle:** Press "Start/Pause." The washer will fill, agitate, and drain using hot water. The lid will lock during the cycle.
5.  **Wipe Down:** After the cycle completes, wipe the inside of the tub with a clean cloth. Also, clean the agitator or impeller area. You can get more specific instructions on how to clean LG washing machine top loader.

Running this cycle regularly keeps your LG washer fresh and ready for laundry.

## Optimal Frequency for LG Tub Clean Usage

Knowing how often to run the LG Tub Clean cycle is important for proper washing machine maintenance. There is a general guideline, but your specific usage habits also matter. Most manufacturers, including LG, recommend running the Tub Clean cycle at least once a month. This monthly routine helps prevent the buildup of residue, mold, and odors. I find that a consistent schedule makes it easier to remember.

However, some factors might require more frequent cleaning. If your household does a lot of laundry, especially heavily soiled items, you might need to run the cycle more often. Using a lot of fabric softener or liquid detergent can also contribute to quicker buildup. In these cases, running Tub Clean every two to three weeks could be beneficial. Hard water areas also tend to accumulate mineral deposits faster. This necessitates more frequent cleaning to prevent issues.

Conversely, if you do laundry less often, or primarily use powdered detergents and avoid fabric softener, you might be able to extend the interval slightly. Still, I would not recommend going longer than every two months. Many modern LG washers have a reminder feature that lights up after a certain number of wash cycles. This indicator lets you know when it is time to run Tub Clean. Pay attention to this reminder. Ignoring it can lead to unpleasant smells and reduced cleaning performance. Regular maintenance ensures your LG washing machine stays fresh and functional.

## Essential Cleaning Agents for LG Tub Clean

Choosing the right cleaning agent for your LG Tub Clean cycle is crucial. You cannot just use regular laundry detergent. Laundry detergents are designed to clean clothes, not the inside of your washing machine. Using them during Tub Clean can create excessive suds and leave more residue. I have seen this happen, and it defeats the purpose.

There are several effective options you can use:

### Commercial Washing Machine Cleaners
Many brands offer dedicated washing machine cleaning tablets or powders. These products are formulated to break down soap scum, mildew, and hard water deposits. They often contain strong cleaning agents that sanitize the tub effectively. Follow the product instructions for best results. Typically, you place one tablet directly into the empty drum or pour the powder into the dispenser. These cleaners are very convenient and highly effective.

### White Vinegar
White distilled vinegar is a natural and affordable alternative. It is an excellent choice for breaking down mineral deposits and mild soap scum. Vinegar also helps to neutralize odors and inhibit mold growth. For the Tub Clean cycle, I recommend using two cups of white vinegar. Pour it directly into the detergent dispenser or the main wash tub. Vinegar is a good option if you prefer natural cleaning solutions. You can learn more about using vinegar specifically for LG washers. This method is safe and readily available.

### Bleach (Use with Caution)
Some users opt for bleach for its sanitizing properties. If you choose to use bleach, it is essential to use it correctly and cautiously. Use no more than half a cup of liquid chlorine bleach. Pour it into the dispenser designed for liquid bleach. **Never mix bleach with vinegar or any other cleaning agent.** Mixing these can create dangerous fumes. I strongly advise against mixing them. Bleach is very effective at killing mold and bacteria. However, it is harsher on machine parts and fabrics if not rinsed properly. I generally prefer vinegar or commercial cleaners for routine maintenance.

Always ensure the washer is empty before adding any cleaning agent. Running the Tub Clean cycle with the correct agent will leave your LG washing machine sparkling clean and odor-free. This simple step protects your investment and keeps your laundry fresh.

## Beyond the Cycle: Other LG Washer Maintenance Tips

Running the Tub Clean cycle is vital, but it is just one part of keeping your LG washing machine in top condition. Several other simple maintenance steps can significantly extend your washer's life and improve its performance. I have found these tips helpful in my own laundry routine.

### Clean the Door Gasket (Front-Load Washers)
For front-load LG washers, the rubber door gasket is a notorious spot for mold and mildew. Water and lint often get trapped here. After every wash, wipe down the inside of the gasket with a cloth. Make sure to pull back the folds of the rubber seal. This prevents moisture buildup and mold growth. If you find mold, a mixture of equal parts water and white vinegar, or a mild bleach solution, can help clean it. This simple habit keeps your washer smelling fresh.

### Clean the Detergent Dispenser Drawer
The detergent dispenser drawer can accumulate sticky residue from detergents and fabric softeners. This buildup can clog the dispenser and even cause water to back up. Remove the dispenser drawer regularly, usually once a month. Wash it thoroughly under warm water. Use a small brush, like an old toothbrush, to scrub away any sticky residue. Rinse it well and let it dry before putting it back. This ensures your detergents dispense properly.

### Inspect and Clean the Drain Pump Filter
Many LG washers, especially front-loaders, have a drain pump filter. This filter traps small items like coins, lint, and hair before they reach the drain pump. A clogged filter can lead to drainage issues and unpleasant odors. Your user manual will show you where it is, usually behind a small door at the bottom front of the machine. Place a shallow pan underneath to catch water. Unscrew the cap slowly to drain the water. Remove the filter and clean off any debris. Rinse it under running water and reinsert it. I check mine every few months. If your LG washing machine has trouble draining, this filter is often the cause.

### Leave the Door Ajar
After each wash cycle, especially for front-load models, leave the washer door slightly ajar. This allows air to circulate inside the drum. It helps the interior dry completely. This simple step prevents moisture buildup, which is the primary cause of mold and mildew growth. It is a small habit that makes a big difference in preventing odors.

Following these additional maintenance tips, alongside regular Tub Clean cycles, ensures your LG washing machine remains efficient and odor-free. It helps you keep your clothes fresh and clean for years to come.

## Troubleshooting Common Tub Clean Issues

Even with regular maintenance, you might encounter some issues when running the LG Tub Clean cycle. Do not worry; most problems have simple solutions. I will cover a few common scenarios and how to address them.

### Washer Still Smells After Tub Clean
If your LG washing machine still has a lingering odor after running the Tub Clean cycle, there are a few things to check. First, you might need to run the cycle again. Sometimes, heavy buildup requires a second pass. Ensure you are using a strong cleaning agent. Commercial washing machine cleaners or white vinegar are usually effective. Second, check the door gasket on front-load washers. Mold and mildew often hide in the folds of the rubber seal. Wipe it thoroughly with a diluted bleach solution or vinegar. Third, clean the detergent dispenser drawer. Residue there can also cause smells. Lastly, check your drain pump filter. A clogged filter can trap smelly water.

### Tub Clean Cycle Not Starting or Finishing
If the Tub Clean cycle does not start, or if it stops midway, check a few basics. Make sure the washer door is fully closed and latched. On front-load models, the door must click shut. For top-loaders, the lid must be completely down. Also, confirm that there are no error codes displayed on the washer's screen. If you see an error code, consult your LG washer manual for specific troubleshooting steps. Sometimes, a simple reset of your LG washing machine can resolve minor glitches. Unplug the washer for a few minutes, then plug it back in.

### Excessive Suds During Tub Clean
Seeing too many suds during the Tub Clean cycle usually means you used the wrong cleaning agent. Remember, you should not use regular laundry detergent for this cycle. If you used a commercial cleaner, you might have used too much. If this happens, run a rinse and spin cycle to clear the suds. For future Tub Clean cycles, ensure you use only dedicated washer cleaners, white vinegar, or a small amount of bleach, as instructed.

Addressing these issues promptly ensures your LG washing machine stays clean and performs effectively. Regular attention to these details will prevent small problems from becoming big headaches.
