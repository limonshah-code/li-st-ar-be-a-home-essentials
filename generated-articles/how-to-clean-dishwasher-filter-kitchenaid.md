## Mastering Your KitchenAid: How To Clean the Dishwasher Filter

Do you notice your KitchenAid dishwasher is not cleaning dishes as well as it once did? Perhaps you see food particles left on plates or a foul odor inside the appliance. These issues often point to one common culprit: a dirty dishwasher filter. Cleaning this essential part of your machine is a simple task. It greatly improves your dishwasher's performance and prevents future problems.

I have faced this myself, and it is a quick fix. Ignoring the filter can lead to bigger issues, like water not draining properly or a decrease in cleaning power. This comprehensive guide will walk you through the process step-by-step. We will cover identifying your filter, the tools you need, and how to clean it thoroughly. By the end, you will know exactly how to clean your KitchenAid dishwasher filter, ensuring your dishes come out spotless every time.

### Takeaway:
*   Regularly clean your KitchenAid dishwasher filter for best performance.
*   Locate the filter, usually at the bottom of the dishwasher tub.
*   Remove the filter by twisting and lifting it out.
*   Rinse filter components under warm running water.
*   Scrub with a soft brush to remove stuck-on debris.
*   Reassemble the filter correctly before placing it back.

Cleaning your KitchenAid dishwasher filter involves simple steps. First, locate and remove the filter, typically found at the bottom of the wash tub. Next, rinse the filter components under warm water and gently scrub away food debris. Finally, reassemble and secure the clean filter back into place.

## Why Cleaning Your KitchenAid Dishwasher Filter Matters

Your KitchenAid dishwasher filter plays a crucial role in how well your machine cleans. It catches food particles and debris, stopping them from recirculating onto your dishes. This filter protects the pump and spray arms from clogs. A clean filter means spotless dishes and a longer life for your appliance.

Over time, this filter collects a lot of gunk. Bits of food, grease, and other debris build up. This buildup restricts water flow and reduces cleaning efficiency. You might notice dishes come out dirty or gritty. A clogged filter also causes unpleasant odors inside your dishwasher. The trapped food starts to decompose, releasing bad smells. I have experienced this many times, and it is never pleasant.

Regular cleaning prevents these issues. Think of it as routine maintenance for your car; it keeps everything running smoothly. A clean filter ensures water sprays correctly and drains effectively. This leads to cleaner dishes and a healthier dishwasher environment. It also helps avoid common problems like standing water at the bottom of the tub. Understanding the filter's function helps you appreciate why this task is important.

Neglecting the filter leads to frustration and potentially costly repairs. Food particles that bypass a clogged filter can damage the pump. They can also block spray arm nozzles. Cleaning the filter is a simple, proactive step. It keeps your KitchenAid dishwasher performing at its best. This simple habit saves you time and money in the long run.

## Identifying Your KitchenAid Dishwasher Filter

Before you can clean your KitchenAid dishwasher filter, you need to find it. Most KitchenAid dishwashers, like many modern models, use a two-part or three-part manual filter system. These filters are usually located at the bottom of the dishwasher tub. You will find it beneath the lower spray arm. It sits where the water collects before draining.

To find your filter, first open the dishwasher door completely. Pull out the lower dish rack to get full access to the bottom. Look directly below where the lower spray arm rotates. You will typically see a cylindrical component or a mesh screen. This is your filter assembly. Sometimes, it might be covered by a small plastic cap. For detailed visuals, you can learn [how to find your dishwasher filter](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-find-your-dishwasher-filter).

KitchenAid models usually feature a micro-filter screen and a coarser filter basket. The coarser filter is often a cup-shaped component with larger holes. This traps bigger food particles. The micro-filter is a fine mesh cylinder inside or around the coarser one. It catches smaller bits. Together, they create a comprehensive filtration system.

Understanding these parts makes removal easier. Some filters twist out. Others lift straight up. Knowing what to look for helps you proceed correctly. Always refer to your dishwasher's user manual if you have trouble. The manual provides specific diagrams for your model. It is a helpful resource for identifying all components.

## Gathering Your Tools for Filter Cleaning

Cleaning your KitchenAid dishwasher filter does not require many special tools. Most of what you need you likely already have in your home. Having everything ready before you start makes the process quick and efficient. This preparation also prevents interruptions once you begin. I always gather my supplies first, it saves so much time.

First, you will need rubber gloves. This protects your hands from food debris and detergents. It also keeps your hands clean during the process. Next, have a soft brush handy. An old toothbrush works perfectly for scrubbing the fine mesh of the filter. You can also use a small nylon scrub brush. Avoid anything abrasive, like a wire brush, which could damage the filter.

You will also need a sink with warm running water. This is for rinsing away the debris from the filter components. A little dish soap can be helpful too. It helps cut through grease and stubborn grime. Some people also keep a small bowl or bucket nearby to soak very dirty filter parts. This can loosen tough deposits.

Finally, have some paper towels or a clean cloth ready. These are for wiping down the filter housing and for drying the filter parts after cleaning. A small flashlight might be useful too. It helps you see into the filter area if lighting is dim. Gathering these simple items ensures a smooth cleaning experience. This small bit of planning makes the entire task much easier.

## Step-by-Step: Removing Your KitchenAid Dishwasher Filter

Removing your KitchenAid dishwasher filter is the first active step in the cleaning process. This task is straightforward, but it requires a gentle touch. Always ensure the dishwasher is off and cool before you begin. Safety first is a good rule of thumb. I always unplug my appliances before doing maintenance.

First, open the dishwasher door completely. Pull out the lower dish rack to give yourself clear access to the bottom of the tub. You will see the filter assembly, typically near the center. It is usually a two-part or three-part system. One part is a cylindrical mesh screen, and the other is a coarser filter cup. If you need more details on getting the filter out, you can also refer to our guide on [how to get dishwasher filter out](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-get-dishwasher-filter-out).

Most KitchenAid filters have a handle or a tab for easy gripping. To remove the primary filter (the coarser one), twist it counter-clockwise. You will hear a click as it unlocks. Once unlocked, gently lift the filter straight up and out of its housing. It should come out easily. If it feels stuck, do not force it. Sometimes, food debris can create a suction seal. For specific instructions on [how to open KitchenAid dishwasher filter](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-open-kitchenaid-dishwasher-filter), check our dedicated article.

After removing the coarse filter, you will likely find a fine mesh filter cylinder underneath or inside it. This component also needs removal. Carefully separate it from the coarse filter, or lift it out of its well, depending on your model. Some models have a small tab to press or a gentle twist. Knowing [how to remove KitchenAid dishwasher filter](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-remove-kitchenaid-dishwasher-filter) correctly prevents damage. Once both parts are free, you are ready for cleaning. Place them in your sink or a basin for the next step.

## Thorough Cleaning of Your KitchenAid Filter Components

Once you have removed your KitchenAid dishwasher filter components, it is time for the real cleaning. This is where you get rid of all the trapped food and grime. A thorough cleaning ensures optimal performance. I find this part surprisingly satisfying, seeing all the gunk disappear.

Start by holding each filter component under warm running water. Rinse away any loose food particles. You will be surprised how much comes off easily with just water pressure. For stubborn bits, use your soft brush or an old toothbrush. Gently scrub the mesh screens and the interior of the filter cups. Pay close attention to the fine mesh filter. Small particles often get lodged in its tiny holes. This section can be covered in more detail in our general guide on [how to clean filter in dishwasher](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-clean-filter-in-dishwasher).

If there is a lot of grease or dried-on food, apply a small amount of dish soap to your brush. Scrub the parts again with the soapy water. The soap helps break down oils and stuck-on debris. You can also let the components soak in warm, soapy water for 10-15 minutes if they are extremely dirty. This helps loosen any caked-on residue. After scrubbing, rinse everything thoroughly under clean running water. Make sure no soap residue remains.

Inspect the filter parts carefully after cleaning. Hold them up to the light to ensure all the mesh holes are clear. No food particles should remain. If you see any blockages, repeat the scrubbing process. A clean filter is vital for your dishwasher's efficiency. Also, take a moment to wipe the filter housing area inside the dishwasher. Use a damp cloth to clean any debris that fell during removal. This ensures a clean environment for your reinstalled filter.

## Reinstalling Your Clean KitchenAid Dishwasher Filter

After thoroughly cleaning your KitchenAid dishwasher filter, the next crucial step is reinstalling it correctly. Proper reinstallation ensures the filter functions as it should. It also prevents water leaks or further performance issues. This step is usually the reverse of removal, but precision matters. I always double-check my work at this stage. You can also refer to our article on [how to put dishwasher filter back in](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-put-dishwasher-filter-back-in) for general guidance.

Start by placing the fine mesh filter back into its designated well at the bottom of the dishwasher tub. This is typically the cylindrical part. Ensure it sits flat and is securely seated. Some models have a small tab that aligns with a groove. Make sure it clicks into place if your model has such a feature. Do not force it; it should slide in smoothly.

Next, place the coarser filter cup over the fine mesh filter. Align its tabs or arrows with the corresponding slots in the filter housing. Gently push down and twist clockwise until it locks into place. You should feel or hear a click, indicating it is secure. This locking mechanism is vital. It prevents the filter from dislodging during a wash cycle. If the filter is not properly locked, it can cause problems like dishes not getting clean or water pooling.

Once the filter assembly is fully reinstalled, give it a gentle wiggle. This confirms it is stable and correctly seated. Pull out the lower dish rack and then slide it back in. Make sure the rack moves freely and does not interfere with the filter. Closing the dishwasher door and running a quick rinse cycle is a good final check. This ensures everything is in working order. If you hear unusual noises or see water problems, recheck the filter's seating. If you are ever putting your whole [KitchenAid dishwasher back together](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-put-kitchenaid-dishwasher-back-together) after maintenance, pay close attention to the filter reinstallation.

Beyond the filter, regularly clean the interior of your dishwasher. Food particles, grease, and hard water deposits can build up on the spray arms, walls, and door seal. ## Troubleshooting Common Dishwasher Issues Related to Filters

Even with regular cleaning, sometimes issues arise with your KitchenAid dishwasher. Many common problems stem from the filter system or general cleanliness. Knowing how to troubleshoot these can save you time and the cost of a service call. I find that most issues have simple fixes if you know what to look for.

One frequent complaint is dishes coming out dirty or gritty. This is a primary sign of a clogged or improperly installed filter. If you have just cleaned it, double-check that the filter is locked into place. An unsecured filter allows food particles to recirculate. If the filter is clean and secure, then check the spray arm nozzles for blockages. Sometimes, a general lack of cleanliness within the machine is the issue. If your dishes are still dirty, perhaps it's time to learn [how to clean your KitchenAid dishwasher](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-clean-your-kitchenaid-dishwasher) thoroughly.

Another common problem is standing water at the bottom of the dishwasher after a cycle. This indicates a draining issue. While it can point to a larger drain pump problem, a severely clogged filter is often the culprit. The filter might be so packed with debris that water cannot pass through to the drain. Ensure the filter is completely clear. Also, check the air gap (if your system has one) or the drain hose for obstructions. A completely clogged filter will lead to water remaining in the tub. You can read more about [why does my KitchenAid dishwasher have water in the bottom](https://homeessentialsguide.com/why-does-my-kitchenaid-dishwasher-have-water-in-the-bottom) for more solutions.

Unpleasant odors are another clear indicator of filter problems. Trapped food particles decompose and create foul smells. Cleaning the filter usually solves this. If odors persist after filter cleaning, consider running an empty cycle with white vinegar or baking soda. This helps neutralize remaining smells. Check the door gasket for mold or mildew buildup as well.

Finally, if your dishwasher suddenly becomes noisy during operation, especially during the wash or drain cycle, a foreign object might be stuck in the filter area or near the pump. Remove the filter and carefully inspect the filter well for anything that might have fallen in. These simple troubleshooting steps cover most filter-related issues. They help you address problems quickly.

## FAQs

### How often should I clean my KitchenAid dishwasher filter?
You should clean your KitchenAid dishwasher filter at least once a month. If you use your dishwasher daily or often wash dishes with heavy food residue, clean it every two weeks. Regular cleaning prevents buildup and maintains good washing performance. This helps extend the life of your appliance.

### What happens if I do not clean my dishwasher filter?
Not cleaning your dishwasher filter leads to several problems. Dishes will come out dirty, gritty, or with food particles remaining. You may notice foul odors inside the dishwasher. Over time, a severely clogged filter can also cause draining issues or damage the dishwasher's pump.

### Can I put my KitchenAid dishwasher filter in the dishwasher?
No, you should not put your KitchenAid dishwasher filter in the dishwasher itself. The dishwasher's wash cycle is not effective at cleaning the fine mesh of the filter. Hot water and detergents can also cause the trapped food particles to bake onto the filter. Always clean the filter manually under running water.

### Do all KitchenAid dishwashers have a filter that needs manual cleaning?
Most modern KitchenAid dishwashers feature a manual filter system that requires periodic cleaning. Older models or some high-end self-cleaning dishwashers might have a built-in grinder that pulverizes food. However, it is always best to check your specific model's manual to confirm the type of filtration system.

### What kind of brush should I use to clean the filter?
Use a soft-bristled brush to clean your KitchenAid dishwasher filter. An old toothbrush works perfectly for reaching small crevices and scrubbing the fine mesh. A small nylon scrub brush is also suitable. Avoid using abrasive brushes, like metal wire brushes, as they can damage the filter material.

### How do I know my KitchenAid dishwasher filter is properly reinstalled?
You will know your KitchenAid dishwasher filter is properly reinstalled when it sits flat in its housing and locks into place with a gentle twist. You should feel or hear a distinct click. It should also not wobble or lift easily when gently wiggled. Proper seating prevents performance issues.
