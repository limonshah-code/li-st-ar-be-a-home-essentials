The Whirlpool warranty clearly differentiates between manufacturing defects and normal wear and tear. A manufacturing defect means the appliance had a flaw from the start. For example, a pump motor fails due to a faulty part. This issue would likely be covered.

Normal wear and tear refers to parts that degrade over time with regular use. Gaskets wearing out or cosmetic scuffs are examples. These are usually not covered by the standard warranty. ## FAQs About Whirlpool Dishwasher Warranties

### Q1: Can I transfer my Whirlpool dishwasher warranty?

No, Whirlpool warranties are typically non-transferable. The warranty applies only to the original purchaser of the appliance. If you sell your dishwasher, the new owner usually does not receive the remaining warranty coverage. Always check the specific terms in your warranty document.

### Q2: Does my warranty cover cosmetic damage?

Generally, no, the standard Whirlpool dishwasher warranty does not cover cosmetic damage. This includes dents, scratches, or discoloration. Cosmetic issues usually result from daily use, accidents, or improper cleaning. The warranty focuses on functional defects.

### Q3: What if my dishwasher breaks down just after the warranty expires?

If your dishwasher fails shortly after the warranty expires, you are usually responsible for repair costs. However, it is still worth contacting Whirlpool. Sometimes, they offer goodwill assistance for recent out-of-warranty failures. Have your purchase details ready when you call.

### Q4: How do I find my dishwasher's model and serial number?

The model and serial numbers for your Whirlpool dishwasher are usually located on a data plate. This plate is typically found inside the dishwasher door, along the tub frame. It might also be on the side of the door or on the back of the appliance.

### Q5: Is a warranty repair free?

Yes, if your Whirlpool dishwasher issue is covered under the limited warranty, the repair is free. This includes the cost of parts and labor. You should not be charged for any components or technician services that fall under the warranty terms.

### Q6: Does using non-Whirlpool parts void the warranty?

Using non-authorized or non-genuine replacement parts for repairs can void your Whirlpool dishwasher warranty. Whirlpool specifies that only authorized parts should be used for service. This ensures quality and compatibility. Always use Whirlpool-approved components for repairs to keep your warranty valid.
