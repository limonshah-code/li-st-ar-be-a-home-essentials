## Mastering the Clean: How To Clean Inside Ninja Dual Air Fryer

Is your Ninja Dual Air Fryer losing its sparkle? Over time, food particles and grease build up inside. This residue affects cooking performance and can even create unpleasant odors. Knowing how to clean inside your Ninja Dual Air Fryer keeps it running efficiently. A clean air fryer cooks food better and lasts longer.

This article provides a comprehensive guide to cleaning the interior of your Ninja Dual Air Fryer. We will cover routine care and deep cleaning methods. You will learn about the right tools and supplies to use. We also explain how to clean the baskets, drawers, and the essential heating element. Finally, we offer tips for tackling tough grease and lingering odors, ensuring your appliance remains in top condition for every meal.

### Takeaway

Keeping your Ninja Dual Air Fryer clean inside is simple.
*   Always unplug the unit and let it cool completely before cleaning.
*   Clean baskets and drawers with warm soapy water or in a dishwasher if safe.
*   Use a damp cloth and mild soap for the main unit's interior.
*   Carefully clean the heating element with a soft brush and damp cloth.
*   Address stubborn grease and odors with baking soda or vinegar.
*   Perform routine cleaning after each use for best results.

Cleaning inside your Ninja Dual Air Fryer involves removing baskets and drawers first. Wash these parts with warm, soapy water or place them in the dishwasher if they are dishwasher safe. For the main unit, wipe down the interior walls and the heating element with a damp cloth and a small amount of mild dish soap after the unit has cooled down and is unplugged.

### Step-by-Step Guide to Deep Cleaning Your Ninja Dual Air Fryer Baskets and Drawers

The baskets and drawers of your Ninja Dual Air Fryer see the most direct contact with food. They often accumulate the most grease and stuck-on residue. Deep cleaning these parts is vital for hygiene and performance. I clean these parts after almost every use. A thorough deep clean every few weeks keeps them looking new.

#### Initial Preparation and Soaking

First, always ensure your air fryer is unplugged. Let it cool completely. Never clean a hot air fryer. Remove both cooking baskets and their crisper plates from the air fryer drawers. Empty any loose crumbs into the trash.

Fill your kitchen sink with warm water. Add a few drops of mild dish soap. Submerge the baskets and crisper plates in the soapy water. Let them soak for at least 15-30 minutes. If food is really stuck, let them soak longer, even up to an hour. Soaking helps loosen dried food and grease. This makes scrubbing much easier.

#### Scrubbing and Rinsing

After soaking, use a soft sponge or a non-abrasive brush to scrub the baskets and crisper plates. Pay attention to the corners and crevices. These areas can trap small food bits. Gently scrub any remaining stuck-on food. The non-stick coating is delicate. Avoid harsh scrubbing that could damage it. I often use a silicone brush for this step. It is firm enough to clean but soft enough not to scratch.

For stubborn residue, you can make a paste of baking soda and water. Apply this paste to the affected areas. Let it sit for 5-10 minutes. Then, gently scrub it away. Baking soda is abrasive enough to lift grime but safe for non-stick surfaces. After scrubbing, rinse all parts thoroughly under clean running water. Make sure no soap residue remains. Soap left behind can burn and cause odors during your next use.

#### Drying and Reassembly

Thoroughly dry all cleaned parts. Use a clean cloth or paper towels. You can also let them air dry completely. Ensure no water remains in any crevices. Moisture can lead to rust or other issues over time. Once completely dry, place the crisper plates back into the baskets. Then slide the baskets back into the air fryer drawers. Reinsert the drawers into the main unit. Your baskets and drawers are now clean and ready for cooking.

### Cleaning the Heating Element and Interior Cavity of Your Ninja Dual Air Fryer

Cleaning the heating element and the main interior cavity is a critical step for a truly clean air fryer. These areas are harder to reach. They still accumulate grease and food particles. Always prioritize safety when cleaning these parts. You are dealing with electrical components.

First, ensure the air fryer is completely unplugged from the wall outlet. This is non-negotiable. Always wait for the appliance to cool down completely. A hot heating element can cause burns. I usually let mine cool for at least an hour after use. Sometimes, I clean it the next day.

To access the heating element and upper interior, you might need to tilt the air fryer gently. Some models allow you to turn the unit upside down. Check your Ninja Dual Air Fryer manual for specific instructions. This position gives you a better view and easier access.

Use a damp, soft cloth to wipe down the interior walls of the air fryer cavity. You can add a small amount of mild dish soap to the cloth. Wipe away any grease or splatters you see. For tough spots, dampen the cloth with water and a tiny bit of baking soda. Gently rub the area. Then wipe it clean with a fresh damp cloth. Be careful not to let excess water drip into the electrical components.

The heating element itself requires special attention. It is a coil located at the top of the air fryer's interior. This part often collects baked-on grease or carbonized food bits. Do not scrub the heating element vigorously. Use a soft-bristled brush, like a bottle brush or a dedicated cleaning brush. You can gently brush away any loose debris. For stubborn buildup, moisten a soft sponge or cloth with a tiny amount of water and dish soap. Carefully wipe the heating coil. Avoid getting the coil too wet.

For baked-on residue on the heating element, a paste of baking soda and a little water can work wonders. Apply the paste with a brush or sponge. Let it sit for 5-10 minutes. Then, gently wipe it off with a clean, damp cloth. Make sure you remove all paste residue. You must ensure the heating element is completely dry before plugging the air fryer back in. Let it air dry for several hours. This step is crucial for preventing electrical issues. ### Tackling Stubborn Grease and Odors Inside Your Ninja Dual Air Fryer

Even with regular cleaning, stubborn grease stains and lingering odors can develop inside your Ninja Dual Air Fryer. These issues often arise from cooking high-fat foods. They can also come from not cleaning spills immediately. Thankfully, there are simple and effective ways to combat them using common household items. I have found these methods work very well for my own air fryer.

For persistent grease, baking soda is your best friend. Create a thick paste by mixing baking soda with a small amount of water. The paste should be like toothpaste. Apply this paste directly onto the stubborn grease spots inside the air fryer cavity. Use a soft brush or sponge to spread it evenly. Let the paste sit for 15-30 minutes. For very tough stains, you can leave it for an hour or two. The baking soda works to break down the grease. After waiting, gently scrub the area with a damp cloth or soft brush. The abrasive nature of the baking soda helps lift the grime without scratching. Finally, wipe the area clean with a fresh, damp cloth. Ensure all baking soda residue is removed.

Lingering odors can be unpleasant. They can transfer unwanted smells to your next meal. White vinegar is excellent for neutralizing odors. Mix equal parts white vinegar and water in a spray bottle. Lightly mist the interior of the air fryer cavity. Do not spray directly onto the heating element or electrical parts. Let the vinegar solution sit for 10-15 minutes. The vinegar will absorb the smells. After that, wipe the interior thoroughly with a clean, damp cloth. You can also place a small bowl of white vinegar inside the closed air fryer for a few hours. This helps absorb strong odors.

Another natural deodorizer is lemon. Cut a lemon into quarters. Place them in the air fryer basket. Run the air fryer at a low temperature, around 200°F (93°C), for 10-15 minutes. The lemon scent will fill the air fryer. It will help eliminate food smells. Afterwards, wipe the interior clean with a damp cloth. This leaves a fresh, citrus scent.

Remember, proper ventilation is also key. When cleaning, ensure the area is well-ventilated. This helps dissipate any cleaning solution fumes. Addressing stubborn grease and odors makes your Ninja Dual Air Fryer more pleasant to use. It also ensures your food tastes its best. Regular and deeper cleaning prevents these issues from becoming serious.

### Routine Maintenance Tips for a Pristine Ninja Dual Air Fryer Interior

Regular maintenance is key to keeping your Ninja Dual Air Fryer in top shape. It also reduces the need for frequent deep cleaning. A little effort after each use goes a long way. These habits prevent food buildup and odors. I find that quick cleanups save me a lot of time later.

First, make it a habit to wipe down the baskets and drawers after every cooking session. Once the air fryer has cooled, remove the baskets. Use a paper towel to wipe out any loose crumbs and grease. Then, wash them with warm, soapy water. If you used a lot of oil or cooked something very greasy, a quick hand wash is essential. This prevents grease from drying and sticking. Many people wonder if their specific model's baskets are dishwasher safe. For the Ninja Dual, [you can typically put Ninja two basket air fryer parts in the dishwasher](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-clean-ninja-two-basket-air-fryer) if they are explicitly marked as such. However, hand washing is often gentler on the non-stick coating, preserving its lifespan.

Second, always clean up spills immediately. If food or oil spills into the main cavity during cooking, let the unit cool down. Then, carefully wipe it away with a damp cloth. Spills can burn onto the surface if left. This makes them much harder to remove later. Addressing them promptly prevents this issue.

Third, consider using air fryer liners or parchment paper. These liners are placed in the bottom of the basket. They catch drips and crumbs. This significantly reduces the amount of grease that gets onto the basket. It also helps keep the main drawer cleaner. Always ensure liners do not block airflow. This can affect cooking performance. They should fit snugly in the basket. You can also use foil, but be careful not to block airflow. Check if [you can put foil in Ninja Dual Air Fryer](https://homeessentialsguide.com/can-you-put-foil-in-ninja-dual-air-fryer) for guidelines.

Fourth, perform a quick wipe of the interior cavity after every few uses. Even if you use liners, some splatters might escape. Unplug the air fryer. Once cool, use a damp cloth to wipe down the inside walls. This prevents light grease films from building up. This proactive approach keeps the interior fresh.

Finally, ensure all parts are completely dry before reassembling and storing the air fryer. Moisture can cause mold or rust. Air drying or wiping with a dry cloth ensures no dampness remains. Implementing these routine maintenance tips will keep your Ninja Dual Air Fryer clean. It will perform at its best for years to come.

### Frequently Asked Questions About Cleaning Your Ninja Dual Air Fryer Interior

**Q1: Can I use oven cleaner inside my Ninja Dual Air Fryer?**
No, you should never use oven cleaner inside your Ninja Dual Air Fryer. Oven cleaners contain harsh chemicals. These chemicals can damage the non-stick coating and other interior materials. They are too strong for air fryer surfaces. Always stick to mild dish soap, baking soda, and vinegar for cleaning. Using recommended gentle cleaners protects your appliance.

**Q2: How often should I deep clean the inside of my Ninja Dual Air Fryer?**
The frequency of deep cleaning depends on how often you use your air fryer. If you use it daily, aim for a deep clean every 2-4 weeks. For less frequent use, a deep clean every 1-2 months is usually sufficient. Regular wiping after each use helps extend the time between deep cleans.

**Q3: What if food gets stuck on the heating element of my air fryer?**
If food gets stuck on the heating element, ensure the air fryer is unplugged and fully cool. Use a soft-bristled brush, like a silicone brush or an old toothbrush, to gently brush away the stuck food. For stubborn bits, you can moisten a cloth with a baking soda paste. Carefully wipe the element. Avoid getting the element wet. Ensure it is dry before next use.

**Q4: Is it safe to submerge the air fryer base in water for cleaning?**
Absolutely not. You must never submerge the main body or base of your Ninja Dual Air Fryer in water. This part contains electrical components. Submerging it will cause serious damage to the appliance. It creates a safety hazard. Only clean the exterior with a damp cloth. Always keep water away from the electrical parts.

**Q5: How do I remove a burning plastic smell from my Ninja Dual Air Fryer?**
A burning plastic smell suggests plastic might be melting or burning inside. First, unplug the air fryer and let it cool. Carefully inspect the interior for any melted plastic, packaging, or accidental items. Remove anything found. Then, deep clean the entire interior, especially the heating element. You can also try running the air fryer with lemon slices or a bowl of white vinegar to help remove lingering odors after cleaning. If the smell persists, contact Ninja support.
