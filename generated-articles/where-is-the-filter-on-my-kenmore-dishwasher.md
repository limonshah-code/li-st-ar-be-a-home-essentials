## Different Kenmore Dishwasher Filter Types and Locations

Not all Kenmore dishwashers use the exact same filter system. There are mainly two types: manual clean filters and self-cleaning filters. Understanding which type your Kenmore has is important. It affects how often you clean it and its exact placement. Most modern Kenmore dishwashers use a manual clean filter.

### Manual Clean Filters

Manual clean filters are the most common in newer Kenmore models. They are usually located at the bottom of the tub, as described earlier. These filters consist of a coarse filter screen and a fine mesh cylindrical filter. They work together to trap all sizes of food particles. These filters need regular cleaning by hand. They do not grind food scraps. This makes them quieter during operation. This is a big advantage for open-plan living.

To find this type, remove the lower rack. You will see a round or semi-circular component in the center. It often has a handle or tabs to twist and lift. The coarse screen might cover the fine mesh cylinder. Both parts must be removed and cleaned. This design is highly effective at preventing clogs. It ensures very clean dishes.

### Self-Cleaning Filters (Hard Food Disposers)

Older Kenmore models, and some specific newer ones, might feature a "self-cleaning" filter. This system is also known as a hard food disposer. Instead of a removable filter you clean, these models have a small grinding mechanism. It chops up food particles into tiny pieces. Then, these pieces flush down the drain. This means you do not remove and clean a filter by hand.

These models are typically louder during operation. The grinding action creates noise. If your Kenmore dishwasher is noticeably loud during wash cycles, it might have this system. The primary "filter" in these models is less about physical removal and more about a screen that prevents larger items from entering the disposer. While it does not require manual cleaning, debris can still accumulate around the drain area or in the sump. This can affect performance. [Where the filter is in a Kenmore Elite dishwasher](https://homeessentialsguide.com/where-is-the-filter-in-a-kenmore-elite-dishwasher) can also vary. Some Elite models might feature advanced filtration systems or a combination of both. Always consult your user manual for precise details.

Knowing your filter type helps you with maintenance. Manual clean filters need regular attention from you. Self-cleaning ones require less direct intervention but benefit from occasional inspection.

## Step-by-Step Guide to Removing Your Kenmore Dishwasher Filter

Once you have located your Kenmore dishwasher filter, the next step is safe removal. This process is generally simple, but handling the components with care is important. Following these steps ensures you do not damage the filter or the dishwasher. Always make sure the dishwasher is empty and cool before you start.

1.  **Open the Dishwasher Door and Remove the Lower Rack:** Pull the lower dish rack out completely. Set it aside. This gives you full access to the bottom of the wash tub where the filter sits.
2.  **Identify the Filter Components:** Most manual clean Kenmore filters have two main parts. There is a coarse filter (sometimes a flat screen) and a cylindrical fine filter. The coarse filter often sits on top of or surrounds the fine filter. It might have a handle or small tabs for gripping.
3.  **Twist the Cylindrical Filter to Unlock:** The inner cylindrical filter usually locks into place with a quarter-turn twist. Look for arrows or "lock/unlock" indicators. Grip the top of the cylindrical filter firmly. Then, twist it counter-clockwise. You should feel it release from its locked position. This makes it easier to remove.
4.  **Lift Out the Cylindrical Filter:** Once unlocked, gently lift the cylindrical filter straight up and out of the dishwasher. Be careful not to spill any trapped debris back into the tub. It can be quite full of gunk.
5.  **Remove the Coarse Filter/Filter Screen:** After removing the cylindrical filter, you will usually see the flat, coarse filter screen underneath. This screen often just lifts straight up. Some models may have small clips holding it in place. Gently release these clips if present. Then, lift the screen out.

Always be gentle during removal. Force can break plastic components. If you encounter resistance, recheck your model's manual. Some models may have a slightly different locking mechanism. Once both parts are out, you are ready to clean them. Understanding [how to get dishwasher filter out](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-get-dishwasher-filter-out) empowers you to keep your appliance running smoothly. I find that using gloves helps, especially if the filter is really dirty.

## Cleaning Your Kenmore Dishwasher Filter for Optimal Performance

Cleaning your Kenmore dishwasher filter is a straightforward task. It will significantly improve your dishwasher's performance. A clean filter prevents food particles from redepositing on dishes. It also helps prevent odors. This process should be part of your regular appliance maintenance.

1.  **Rinse Under Running Water:** Take the removed filter components to your sink. Hold each piece under warm, running water. This will wash away loose food particles. You will see a lot of debris come off.
2.  **Use a Soft Brush and Dish Soap:** For stubborn food residue or grease, use a soft-bristled brush. An old toothbrush works perfectly. Apply a small amount of liquid dish soap to the brush. Gently scrub all surfaces of both the coarse filter and the fine mesh cylinder. Pay extra attention to the mesh holes. These are where small particles get stuck.
3.  **Inspect for Blockages:** Hold the filter parts up to the light. Ensure all mesh holes are clear. If you see any remaining blockages, continue scrubbing. You might need to rinse and scrub a few times. This ensures thorough cleaning.
4.  **Rinse Thoroughly:** Rinse both filter parts again under running water. Make sure all soap residue is gone. Soap left on the filter can cause excessive suds in your next wash cycle. This is an important step for [how to clean dishwasher filter Kenmore](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-clean-dishwasher-filter-kenmore) properly.
5.  **Clean the Filter Housing:** While the filter parts are out, take a moment to wipe down the filter housing area at the bottom of the dishwasher. Use a damp cloth to remove any debris or grime. This ensures a clean environment for the filter.

I always feel a sense of satisfaction after cleaning the filter. The difference in wash quality is noticeable. It is amazing how much gunk a small filter can collect. This simple act of cleaning helps your Kenmore dishwasher operate at its best. It reduces wear on internal parts. It also keeps your dishes sparkling clean.

3.  **Twist to Lock the Cylindrical Filter:** Once the cylindrical filter is fully seated, twist it clockwise. You should feel a click or a definite stop as it locks into place. ## Signs Your Kenmore Dishwasher Filter Needs Attention

Recognizing the signs of a dirty Kenmore dishwasher filter helps you address the problem promptly. Ignoring these signals can lead to larger issues. It can also cause persistent frustration with your appliance's performance. Knowing what to look for makes maintenance easier.

*   **Dirty or Gritty Dishes:** This is the most common and obvious sign. If dishes come out with food particles, sand-like grit, or a cloudy film, your filter is likely clogged. The filter cannot trap debris when it is full. This allows debris to redeposit on your dishes during the wash or rinse cycles. I noticed this first when my glasses looked dull.
*   **Standing Water in the Bottom of the Tub:** After a cycle completes, some water might remain in the sump area. However, if there is standing water covering the entire bottom of the tub, the filter might be blocking the drain path. A clogged filter hinders water from properly exiting the dishwasher.
*   **Unpleasant Odors:** Food particles trapped in a dirty filter will decompose over time. This creates a foul smell inside your dishwasher. The odor can transfer to your clean dishes. If your dishwasher smells, even after a wash cycle, check the filter.
*   **Unusual Noises:** While self-cleaning dishwashers can be noisy, an unusually loud grinding or straining sound from a manual clean Kenmore might indicate a filter issue. The motor could be working harder to push water through a restricted filter. This extra effort creates abnormal sounds.
*   **Longer Wash Cycles:** If your dishwasher seems to take longer than usual to complete a cycle, a clogged filter could be the cause. Restricted water flow or drainage can confuse the dishwasher's sensors. This makes it try to compensate, extending the wash time.

Addressing these signs quickly saves you from bigger headaches. A quick filter check and clean often solves these performance issues. Regular inspection helps you catch problems before they worsen. This keeps your Kenmore dishwasher running smoothly and efficiently.

It also ensures your dishes always come out sparkling. The frequency depends on how often you use your dishwasher and what kind of food goes into it.

*   **Monthly Cleaning for Average Use:** For most households, cleaning the Kenmore dishwasher filter once a month is a good general guideline. This frequency is suitable if you run your dishwasher several times a week. It prevents significant buildup of food particles and grease. Households with large families or those who often wash dishes with significant food residue may need this. For example, if you scrape plates but do not rinse them, more frequent cleaning is wise.
*   **As-Needed Cleaning for Specific Issues:** Always check your filter if you notice any of the signs discussed earlier. These include dirty dishes, standing water, or odors. Do not wait for your scheduled cleaning if a problem appears. A quick, unscheduled clean can often resolve immediate issues.

This ensures all parts of the dishwasher, including hidden areas, stay clean. It removes mineral deposits and soap scum buildup.
*   **Consult Your User Manual:** Your Kenmore dishwasher's specific user manual might offer recommended maintenance intervals. These are often tailored to your model's design. It is always a good idea to check this resource for brand-specific advice.

## Essential Tips for Kenmore Dishwasher Filter Care

Beyond regular cleaning, a few essential tips can further extend the life and efficiency of your Kenmore dishwasher filter. These simple practices help reduce the burden on your filter. They contribute to overall better appliance performance. Implementing these habits can save you time and effort in the long run.

*   **Scrape Plates Thoroughly:** Always scrape large food particles off your plates before loading them into the dishwasher. While the filter is designed to catch debris, it works best when it is not overwhelmed. Removing excess food reduces the amount of work your filter needs to do. This prevents it from clogging too quickly.
*   **Avoid Pre-Rinsing (Mostly):** Most modern Kenmore dishwashers, especially those with manual clean filters, are designed to work with some food residue. The enzymes in dishwasher detergents activate best when food particles are present. Excessive pre-rinsing can sometimes remove too much food. This makes the detergent less effective. However, very large or hard particles should still be scraped off.
*   **Use Quality Dishwasher Detergent:** High-quality dishwasher detergents contain enzymes that break down food and grease. This reduces the amount of debris the filter needs to trap. Avoid using too much detergent. This can leave a film on dishes and internal components, including the filter.
*   **Clean the Spray Arms:** Periodically check and clean the spray arms. These can get clogged with hard water deposits or small food bits. Clogged spray arms reduce wash effectiveness. This leaves more work for the filter. Removing them and rinsing helps ensure full water pressure.
*   **Inspect the Drain Area:** Occasionally, inspect the drain area around the filter. Ensure no large items, like broken glass or small plastic pieces, are blocking the drain or the filter's seating. These items can damage the filter or the drain pump.
*   **Run a Hot Cycle Regularly:** Running your Kenmore dishwasher on a hot cycle, especially with a dishwasher cleaner, helps break down grease and detergent buildup. This benefits the entire system, including the filter. This helps to flush out residues that might contribute to clogs.

By following these tips, you actively support your filter. You ensure your Kenmore dishwasher delivers exceptional cleaning results every time. These small efforts protect your investment. They give you peace of mind with clean dishes.

## Troubleshooting Common Filter-Related Issues

Even with regular cleaning, you might encounter specific issues related to your Kenmore dishwasher filter. Knowing how to troubleshoot these problems can save you from calling a technician. Most filter-related problems have simple solutions.

### Dishwasher Not Draining

If your Kenmore dishwasher is not draining, a clogged filter is a prime suspect. Remove and thoroughly clean both parts of the filter. Also, check the filter housing and the sump area for any large obstructions. Sometimes, a forgotten piece of plastic or a bone can block the drain path. Ensure the filter is reinstalled correctly and locked. An improperly seated filter can block drainage.

### White Film on Dishes

A white film on dishes can sometimes indicate hard water or detergent issues. However, a dirty filter can also contribute. When the filter is full, it cannot remove suspended minerals and food particles effectively. These can then redeposit as a film. Clean the filter. Also, consider using a rinse aid. Rinse aid helps water sheet off dishes. If the film persists, you might need a dishwasher cleaner to remove mineral buildup from the entire tub.

### Dishwasher Smells After Cleaning Filter

If your dishwasher still smells bad after cleaning the filter, the problem might be elsewhere. Check the spray arms for trapped food. Inspect the drain hose for kinks or blockages. Sometimes, mold or mildew can grow in hidden crevices. Running a hot wash cycle with white vinegar or baking soda can often eliminate lingering odors. Place a cup of white vinegar in the top rack and run a hot cycle. This helps to sanitize the internal components.

### Filter Appears Clean but Dishes Are Still Dirty

If your Kenmore dishwasher filter looks clean but dishes are still dirty, other factors might be at play.
*   **Loading Issues:** Ensure you are loading dishes correctly. Do not block the spray arms. Overloading prevents water from reaching all items.
*   **Water Temperature:** The water might not be hot enough. Your dishwasher needs hot water to activate detergents and effectively clean. Check your water heater setting. It should be around 120-125°F (49-52°C).
*   **Detergent Quality or Age:** Old or poor-quality detergent loses effectiveness. Use fresh, high-quality dishwasher detergent.
*   **Clogged Spray Arms:** As mentioned, check and clean the spray arm holes. Blocked holes reduce water pressure and distribution.
*   **Rinse Aid:** Using rinse aid helps water bead up and run off, preventing spots and film. Ensure your rinse aid dispenser is full.

Troubleshooting takes a systematic approach. Start with the easiest fix, which is usually the filter. Then, move to other common issues. This method helps pinpoint the exact cause.

## FAQ Section

### How often should I clean my Kenmore dishwasher filter?
You should clean your Kenmore dishwasher filter monthly for average use. If you use your dishwasher daily or wash heavily soiled dishes, clean it every two to three weeks. Always clean it immediately if you notice dirty dishes, poor draining, or bad odors, regardless of schedule.

### Can I run my Kenmore dishwasher without the filter?
No, you should never run your Kenmore dishwasher without its filter. The filter protects the drain pump and other internal components from food particles and debris. Running it without the filter can cause serious damage, leading to expensive repairs or even permanent appliance failure.

### What happens if I do not clean my dishwasher filter?
If you do not clean your dishwasher filter, food particles will accumulate. This causes dirty dishes with gritty residue, poor drainage, and unpleasant odors. It also makes the dishwasher's motor work harder, potentially leading to clogs in the drain pump and reducing the appliance's lifespan.

### Is my Kenmore dishwasher filter self-cleaning?
Most newer Kenmore dishwashers feature manual clean filters that require periodic removal and hand-washing. Older models or some specific higher-end Kenmore dishwashers might have a "hard food disposer" or self-cleaning filter. If your dishwasher is noticeably loud during operation, it might have a self-cleaning system. Check your user manual for confirmation.

### Can a dirty filter cause my dishwasher to smell bad?
Yes, a dirty filter is a primary cause of bad smells in a dishwasher. Food particles trapped in the filter will decompose over time, creating foul odors. These smells can then transfer to your dishes. Cleaning the filter regularly is key to a fresh-smelling dishwasher.

### What kind of brush should I use to clean my dishwasher filter?
Use a soft-bristled brush, such as an old toothbrush or a small kitchen scrub brush. Avoid abrasive brushes or harsh scouring pads, as these can damage the delicate mesh of the filter. A soft brush helps gently remove stuck-on food and grease without causing damage.
