#### Step-by-Step Daily Handwashing

Here is how I handwash my 64hydro bottle for daily cleaning:

1.  **Disassemble the Bottle:** First, I take apart all removable components. This includes the lid, straw, and any silicone gaskets or seals. This step allows access to all surfaces that need cleaning.
2.  **Rinse Immediately:** I rinse the bottle and all its parts with warm water after each use. This removes any loose food particles or drink residue before it dries and sticks.
3.  **Prepare Soapy Water:** I fill a clean sink or basin with warm water and add a few drops of mild dish soap. I prefer a gentle, non-abrasive soap to protect the bottle's finish.
4.  **Scrub the Bottle Interior:** I use a long-handled bottle brush to thoroughly scrub the inside of the bottle. I make sure to reach the bottom and sides to remove any film or residue.
5.  **Clean the Lid and Components:** I use a smaller brush or a sponge to clean the lid, straw, and gaskets. For straws, a dedicated straw brush is very helpful for reaching inside. I pay close attention to crevices where residue might hide.
6.  **Rinse Thoroughly:** I rinse all parts meticulously under running warm water. I ensure no soap residue remains, as this can affect the taste of your drinks.
7.  **Air Dry Completely:** I place all cleaned parts on a drying rack, upside down, allowing them to air dry completely. This prevents water spots and, most importantly, inhibits the growth of mold and mildew. Leaving them uncapped helps air circulate.

#### Tackling Stubborn Stains and Odors Safely

Sometimes, despite regular cleaning, your 64hydro bottle might develop stubborn stains or lingering odors. This is common with coffee, tea, or protein shakes. For these situations, I use a few safe and effective tricks:

*   **Baking Soda Paste:** For stains, I make a paste using baking soda and a little water. I apply it to the stained areas inside the bottle and let it sit for a few hours or overnight. Then, I scrub it gently with a bottle brush and rinse thoroughly. Baking soda is a natural abrasive and deodorizer.
*   **Vinegar Soak:** To eliminate odors, I fill the bottle with a solution of equal parts white vinegar and warm water. I let it soak for 30 minutes to an hour, or even overnight for strong odors. Afterward, I rinse it several times until the vinegar smell is gone. Vinegar is excellent for sanitizing and neutralizing smells.
*   **Bottle Cleaning Tablets:** Specialized bottle cleaning tablets are also an option. They use effervescent action to clean and deodorize. I simply drop a tablet into the bottle with warm water and let it dissolve and work its magic. Then, I rinse well.
*   **Lemon Juice:** For a fresh scent and mild stain removal, I sometimes use lemon juice. A mix of lemon juice and warm water left to soak for a while can work wonders.

Always ensure you rinse thoroughly after using any cleaning solution to remove all traces of the cleaning agent.
