## Understanding Centrifugal Juicers: The Core Technology

Both the Nutribullet Juicer Pro and most Breville juicers operate on centrifugal technology. This means they use a fast-spinning mesh filter. Produce gets shredded by a blade and then spun against the filter. Centrifugal force separates the juice from the pulp. This process is very fast, making it ideal for busy mornings. It quickly turns whole fruits and vegetables into fresh juice.

Centrifugal juicers differ from slow or masticating juicers. Slow juicers crush and press produce slowly. This extracts juice with minimal heat and oxidation. Centrifugal juicers generate more heat due to their speed. This can slightly impact nutrient content and juice shelf life. However, they are significantly faster. Understanding this core technology helps you appreciate their performance.

For example, a Nutribullet Juicer Pro is not a cold press juicer. It uses a high-speed motor. This is common among centrifugal models. Breville also primarily offers centrifugal juicers, though they do have some more advanced models. ### How Centrifugal Juicing Works

The operation of a centrifugal juicer is straightforward. You push fruits and vegetables down a feed chute. A spinning disc with tiny shredding blades grates the produce. The shredded bits then fly outward due to centrifugal force. They press against a fine mesh strainer. Juice passes through the strainer into a collection pitcher. The pulp remains inside the strainer or in a separate container. This method extracts juice quickly. It is efficient for many types of produce.

### Pros and Cons of Centrifugal Juicers

Centrifugal juicers offer distinct advantages. Their speed is unmatched. They are also generally more affordable than masticating juicers. Cleaning parts are often dishwasher safe. However, they also have downsides. The high-speed rotation introduces more air into the juice. This can cause more foam. It also means juice oxidizes faster, reducing its shelf life. They are also less efficient with leafy greens. Harder produce like carrots works well.

Breville juicers, like the Juice Fountain series, are well-known centrifugal models. They operate very similarly to the Nutribullet Pro.
