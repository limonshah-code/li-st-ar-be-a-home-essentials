### The Double-Wall Vacuum Insulation

Most Brumate products feature double-wall vacuum insulation. This means there are two layers of stainless steel. A vacuum space sits between these layers. This vacuum greatly reduces heat transfer. It keeps your drinks cold for hours, or hot for a long time. High heat from a dishwasher can compromise this vacuum seal. The intense heat can cause the metal to expand. This expansion might break the seal. Once the seal breaks, your Brumate loses its insulation power. It will no longer keep drinks cold or hot effectively. This damage is often permanent. You lose the main benefit of your Brumate.

## Step-by-Step Guide to Hand Washing Your Brumate

Hand washing your Brumate is the best way to keep it in top condition. It is a simple process. It does not take much time. Following these steps ensures a thorough and safe clean. You will protect the finish and insulation. This preserves your Brumate's function and appearance. Let's walk through the proper hand washing method.

### Essential Cleaning Supplies

Gather your cleaning tools first. You will need a mild dish soap. Avoid harsh detergents or abrasive cleaners. A soft sponge or a non-scratch dishcloth is ideal. For inside the bottle, a bottle brush is very helpful. It reaches the bottom and sides easily. A small brush, like a toothbrush, helps clean lid crevices. Warm water is essential for effective cleaning. These simple supplies protect your Brumate. They also ensure a hygienic clean.

### Cleaning the Body

Start by rinsing your Brumate body with warm water. Apply a few drops of mild dish soap to your sponge or bottle brush. Gently scrub the interior of the Brumate. Make sure to reach all surfaces. Scrub the exterior with the soft sponge. Pay attention to any spills or marks. Rinse the Brumate thoroughly with warm water. Ensure all soap residue is gone. Soap residue can leave an odd taste. It can also cause streaks.

### Cleaning Lids and Gaskets

Disassemble the lid completely. Remove any silicone gaskets or O-rings. Wash these small parts individually. Use warm water and mild soap. A small brush works well for small spaces. Clean the underside of the lid and any sliding mechanisms. Silicone gaskets are prone to mildew. Wash them carefully. Inspect them for any residue. Rinse all lid components well. Ensure no soap remains.

### Drying and Storage

After washing, dry all parts completely. You can use a clean dishcloth for the exterior. For the interior, let it air dry. Place the Brumate upside down on a drying rack. This allows water to drain fully. Allow gaskets and lid components to air dry separately. Reassemble your Brumate once all parts are dry. Store it with the lid slightly ajar. This prevents moisture buildup and odors. Proper drying is crucial. It keeps your Brumate fresh and ready for next use. ## Common Brumate Cleaning Mistakes to Avoid

Even with good intentions, some cleaning habits can harm your Brumate. Knowing what to avoid is just as important as knowing what to do. These common mistakes can damage the finish, insulation, or seals. They can also affect the taste of your drinks. Let us review these pitfalls. This helps you keep your Brumate in perfect condition.

### Using Abrasive Cleaners and Tools

Never use abrasive cleaners or scouring pads on your Brumate. Steel wool, abrasive sponges, or harsh chemical cleaners can scratch the powder coat finish. They can also damage the stainless steel interior. These scratches compromise the appearance. They can also create tiny spaces for bacteria to grow. Stick to mild dish soap and a soft sponge or cloth. For stubborn stains, try a paste of baking soda and water. This is a gentle yet effective abrasive.

### Soaking for Too Long or Improper Drying

Leaving your Brumate submerged in water for too long is another mistake. While hand washing is good, excessive soaking can be bad. Water can seep into seals or areas not meant for prolonged immersion. This can compromise the insulation or lead to rust. Always dry your Brumate thoroughly. Do not leave it to air dry in a closed, damp environment. Moisture can lead to mildew and unpleasant odors. Use a drying rack. Ensure good airflow around all parts. Proper drying is crucial for hygiene and longevity.

### Ignoring Lid and Gasket Cleaning

Many people focus only on the cup's interior. They might neglect the lid and gaskets. These parts come into direct contact with your drinks. They have many small crevices. Food particles and drink residue can build up here. This leads to bacterial growth. It also causes bad odors. Always disassemble and clean your lid and gaskets. Use a small brush to reach all areas. This prevents health hazards. It keeps your Brumate truly clean.

## FAQ Section

### Q1: Can I put Brumate lids in the dishwasher?
Yes, most Brumate lids and silicone gaskets are generally safe for the top rack of your dishwasher. Always separate all lid components, including gaskets, before placing them in the dishwasher. This ensures a thorough clean and prevents food particles from hiding in crevices. However, check the specific product care instructions, as models can vary.

### Q2: What happens if I put my Brumate body in the dishwasher?
Putting your Brumate body in the dishwasher can cause permanent damage. High heat can compromise the vacuum insulation, making the Brumate lose its ability to keep drinks cold or hot. Harsh detergents and high-pressure water jets can also chip, peel, or fade the exterior powder coat finish, affecting its appearance and durability.

### Q3: How do I remove odors from my Brumate?
To remove odors, thoroughly hand wash your Brumate with warm water and mild dish soap. For persistent odors, mix a solution of baking soda and water (about 1-2 tablespoons per cup of water) and let it sit in the Brumate overnight. You can also use a mixture of white vinegar and water. Rinse thoroughly afterward and air dry completely.

### Q4: How often should I clean my Brumate?
You should clean your Brumate after every use, especially if you are putting sugary drinks, coffee, or milk inside. A quick rinse and hand wash with soap and water suffice for daily cleaning. Perform a deeper clean, including disassembling and washing all lid components and gaskets, at least once a week to prevent buildup and odors.

### Q5: Is all insulated drinkware hand-wash only?
No, not all insulated drinkware is hand-wash only. Some brands and specific models are designed to be fully dishwasher safe. However, many high-quality, vacuum-insulated products, like most Brumates, recommend hand washing for the main body to protect their insulation and exterior finish. Always check the manufacturer's care instructions for your specific item.

### Q6: Can I use a bottle brush to clean my Brumate?
Yes, using a bottle brush is highly recommended for cleaning the interior of your Brumate. A bottle brush allows you to reach the bottom and sides of the tumbler or bottle effectively. It helps scrub away any residues that a sponge might miss. Make sure the bottle brush is soft and non-abrasive to avoid scratching the stainless steel interior.
