## The "Made in USA" Label: What It Truly Means for Appliances

The "Made in USA" label carries specific meanings. The Federal Trade Commission (FTC) regulates this claim. For a product to be labeled "Made in USA," it must be "all or virtually all" made in the United States. This means all significant parts and processing must be of U.S. origin.

For appliances like refrigerators, achieving "all or virtually all" can be complex. It implies that raw materials, manufacturing, and assembly must happen domestically. If a KitchenAid refrigerator has this label, it meets these strict criteria. This label provides a high level of assurance.

However, many products might be "Assembled in USA." This claim means the product's principal assembly occurs in the U.S. However, a significant portion of its components can be foreign-made. Most KitchenAid refrigerators fall into this "assembled in USA" category. This distinction is vital for consumers who want to understand the true domestic content of their appliance.

## How to Identify the Origin of Your KitchenAid Refrigerator

Finding the manufacturing origin of your specific KitchenAid refrigerator is usually straightforward. The most reliable way is to check the product's data plate or label. This label is typically inside the refrigerator compartment, often near the top or on a side wall. Sometimes, it can be on the back of the unit.

The data plate lists important information. This includes the model number, serial number, and manufacturing location. Look for phrases like "Made in USA" or "Assembled in USA with domestic and imported components." This information directly tells you where the final assembly took place.

If you are buying a new refrigerator, ask the sales associate. They can usually provide details on specific models. You can also visit KitchenAid's official website.
