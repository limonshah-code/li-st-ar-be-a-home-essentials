### FAQ Section

**What is the best Ryobi leaf blower for a small yard?**
For small yards, the Ryobi 18V ONE+ cordless leaf blower is a great choice. It is lightweight, easy to use, and provides enough power for light debris. It does not need gas or cords. This makes it perfect for quick cleanups.

**How long does a Ryobi 40V battery last?**
A Ryobi 40V battery's life depends on the specific blower model and power setting. For typical use, it can last 15-30 minutes on a single charge. Running it on a higher speed setting will drain the battery faster.

**Are Ryobi gas leaf blowers powerful?**
Yes, Ryobi gas leaf blowers are powerful. They are designed for larger yards and tougher jobs. They offer high CFM and MPH ratings. This allows them to move heavy, wet leaves and stubborn debris with ease.

**Can I use an 18V Ryobi leaf blower for wet leaves?**
An 18V Ryobi leaf blower may struggle with large piles of wet leaves. It has less power than 40V or gas models. For light, damp leaves, it might work. For heavy, matted wet leaves, a more powerful model is better.

**What is the warranty on Ryobi leaf blowers?**
Ryobi generally offers a 3-year limited warranty on their power tools, including leaf blowers. Batteries usually have a separate warranty, often 3 years as well. Always check the specific product details for exact warranty information.

**Do Ryobi leaf blowers come with batteries and chargers?**
Some Ryobi leaf blower kits include a battery and charger. Other times, they are sold as "tool-only" versions. This is common for the ONE+ system, where users might already have batteries. Always check the product description before buying.
