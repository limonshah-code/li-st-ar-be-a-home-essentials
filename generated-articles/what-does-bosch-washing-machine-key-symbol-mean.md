The key symbol on your Bosch washing machine display tells you one main thing: the child lock is on. This is a safety feature. It protects your appliance settings from little hands or curious pets. When the child lock is active, the control panel becomes unresponsive. You cannot change the wash program. You also cannot stop or start the machine using the usual buttons.

This feature is very helpful for homes with young children. Kids love to press buttons. They might accidentally change the cycle or even stop the machine mid-wash. The child lock stops this from happening. It keeps your laundry routine smooth. It also keeps your children safe from the appliance itself. The key symbol is a clear visual cue that this protective mode is engaged.

The child lock is not an error code. It does not mean your washing machine is broken. Instead, it indicates a chosen safety setting. If you see the key symbol, your machine is simply in a secure mode. You just need to know how to unlock it to regain control. Understanding this simple symbol makes appliance use much easier. The child lock also prevents the door from opening. This is important during a wash cycle. It stops water from spilling out. It also prevents access to hot water or moving parts. ## Activating and Deactivating the Bosch Child Lock

Turning the child lock on or off on your Bosch washing machine is quite simple. It usually involves pressing a combination of buttons on the control panel. The exact buttons can vary slightly between different Bosch models. However, the general method remains the same. You typically need to press and hold two specific buttons simultaneously for a few seconds.

Most Bosch washing machines use the 'Spin' and 'Rinse' buttons. You might also find models that use the 'Start/Pause' button or two other function buttons. Look for a small key icon printed near these buttons. This symbol indicates their role in activating the child lock. Once you press and hold these buttons for about three to five seconds, you will see a change on the display. The key symbol will either appear or disappear, confirming the change.

### Common Button Combinations

Many Bosch washing machines have a universal way to control the child lock. Find the two buttons that have a small key symbol or a lock symbol printed above them. The key symbol on the display will then either light up or turn off. This confirms the child lock is now active or inactive.

### Cleaning the Pump Filter

Cleaning the pump filter is a simple yet crucial maintenance step. First, make sure the machine is off and unplugged. Place a shallow tray or towel under the filter cover to catch any residual water. Carefully open the access panel. Unscrew the filter slowly. Water will drain out, so be prepared. Remove any lint, debris, or foreign objects from the filter. Rinse the filter under running water.

Once clean, screw the filter back in tightly. Close the access panel. This simple task can prevent drainage errors. It also ensures your machine empties properly. ## FAQ Section

### Q1: Why does the key symbol appear on my Bosch washing machine?
The key symbol on your Bosch washing machine display indicates the child lock feature is active. This safety function prevents accidental changes to settings or opening the door during operation. It protects young children and pets from interfering with the washing cycle. It is a common indicator, not an error.

### Q2: How do I turn off the child lock on my Bosch washing machine?
To turn off the child lock, press and hold the designated buttons on your control panel for 3 to 5 seconds. For many Bosch models, these are often the 'Spin' and 'Rinse' buttons or buttons with a small key symbol printed nearby. The key symbol on the display will then disappear.

### Q3: Can I run a wash cycle with the key symbol showing?
Yes, the washing machine can still run a cycle with the key symbol showing. The child lock only prevents changes to the settings and opening the door. It does not stop the ongoing wash program. Your machine will complete its cycle as programmed, but you cannot adjust it until the child lock is deactivated.

### Q4: What if the key symbol does not go away?
If the key symbol does not go away, try power cycling your machine. Unplug it from the wall outlet, wait 5-10 minutes, then plug it back in. Then try deactivating the child lock again. If it still persists, consult your user manual for specific troubleshooting. If the issue continues, contact Bosch customer support.

### Q5: Is the key symbol the same as an error code?
No, the key symbol is not an error code. It is an indicator for a safety feature: the child lock. Error codes on Bosch washing machines usually start with "E" (e.g., E18, E23) and signify a malfunction or problem requiring attention. The key symbol simply means the controls are locked.

### Q6: Does the child lock work for all Bosch washing machine models?
Most modern Bosch washing machine models include a child lock feature. The method to activate and deactivate it might vary slightly between series or model years. Always refer to the user manual specific to your washing machine model for the most accurate instructions on managing the child lock.
