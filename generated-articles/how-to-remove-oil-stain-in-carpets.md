## Reclaim Your Carpet: How to Remove Oil Stains

Imagine this: a perfect evening, then a dropped plate or a knocked-over cooking oil bottle. Suddenly, a greasy, dark spot mars your beautiful carpet. An oil stain on your carpet can feel like a disaster. It is one of the most stubborn types of household marks. But do not worry. You can remove oil stains from carpets effectively. This guide helps you tackle these unsightly spots. I will share methods using common household items. We will cover quick responses and deeper cleaning techniques. We will also explore ways to prevent future spills.

### Takeaway:

*   Act fast to absorb as much oil as possible from the carpet.
*   Use absorbent powders like baking soda or cornstarch first.
*   Dish soap helps break down oil for easier removal.
*   Target old stains with a multi-step approach and patience.
*   Prevent future spills with care and protective measures.

Removing an oil stain from a carpet often requires immediate action and the right technique. You can effectively lift most oil stains by first blotting the area to remove excess oil. Then you apply an an absorbent powder, let it sit, and vacuum it up. Follow this with a gentle scrub using a dish soap solution.
