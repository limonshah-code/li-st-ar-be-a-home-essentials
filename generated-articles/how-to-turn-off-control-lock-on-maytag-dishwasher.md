### FAQ Section

**Q1: Why does my Maytag dishwasher have a control lock?**
A1: Your Maytag dishwasher has a control lock as a safety feature. It prevents accidental button presses. This is especially useful in homes with children or pets. It stops anyone from inadvertently changing cycle settings or starting/stopping the dishwasher during operation. This ensures your wash cycle runs without interruption and keeps your family safe.

**Q2: Can a power cycle fix a locked Maytag dishwasher?**
A2: Yes, a power cycle can often fix a locked Maytag dishwasher. If the control lock will not turn off using the standard button method, try unplugging the dishwasher from its power outlet. Wait for at least one minute. Then, plug it back in. This hard reset can clear temporary electronic glitches and restore control panel functionality.

**Q3: What if my Maytag control lock light won't turn off?**
A3: If your Maytag control lock light won't turn off after pressing the button and performing a power cycle, the issue might be more serious. Double-check your owner's manual for specific instructions. If it still persists, there could be a faulty control board or a damaged control panel. In such cases, professional help is recommended to diagnose and fix the problem.

**Q4: Is the control lock the same as a child lock?**
A4: Yes, the control lock is often the same as a child lock feature on Maytag dishwashers. The terms are frequently used interchangeably. Its primary purpose is to "lock" the control panel, making it unresponsive to touches. This prevents children from tampering with the dishwasher settings or starting it accidentally, ensuring safety and uninterrupted cycles.

**Q5: How do I know if my Maytag dishwasher has a control lock?**
A5: You can identify if your Maytag dishwasher has a control lock by checking its control panel. Look for a button labeled "Control Lock," "Lock," "Child Lock," or a symbol of a padlock. If this feature is active, you will usually see a "LOC" message on the display or a padlock icon light up on the panel.

**Q6: Does the control lock affect a running cycle on my Maytag?**
A6: The control lock primarily prevents *changes* to a running cycle or *starting* a new one. If the control lock is activated while a cycle is running, the dishwasher will typically continue its cycle as programmed. However, you will not be able to pause, stop, or change any settings until the control lock is disengaged. It secures the chosen settings.
