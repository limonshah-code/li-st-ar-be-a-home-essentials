Most Brumate cups are not dishwasher safe. Brumate specifically recommends hand washing for its insulated drinkware. High heat from dishwashers can damage the vacuum insulation. This damage leads to a loss of temperature retention. Harsh detergents can also degrade the cup's exterior finish.

## The Science Behind Insulated Drinkware and Dishwashers

Insulated drinkware, like Brumate cups, uses a special design to keep beverages at temperature. This design involves two walls of stainless steel with a vacuum between them. The vacuum acts as a barrier. It stops heat transfer through conduction and convection. This is how your cold drinks stay cold and hot drinks stay hot. I always find this science fascinating when I use my Brumate.

When you place an insulated cup in a dishwasher, it experiences extreme conditions. Dishwashers use very hot water and high-temperature drying cycles. These high temperatures can affect the vacuum seal. The heat causes the metal layers to expand. When the cup cools, the layers contract. Repeated expansion and contraction can compromise the vacuum seal. A lost vacuum means the cup loses its insulating ability. It becomes just like a regular, uninsulated cup. This is a common issue that causes many insulated cups to fail, similar to [why RTIC cups are not dishwasher safe](https://homeessentialsguide.com/why-are-rtic-cups-not-dishwasher-safe).

Harsh detergents also play a role. Dishwasher detergents are strong and contain abrasive chemicals. These chemicals can react with the stainless steel finish. They can strip away protective coatings or cause discoloration. This is especially true for cups with special finishes, such as powder coatings or metallic paints. I have seen many cups lose their shine because of this. The high-pressure water jets can also contribute to wear and tear. They can loosen seals or damage external decals.

### Protecting Your Brumate's Vacuum Seal

To protect the vacuum seal, always wash your Brumate cups by hand. Use lukewarm water, not boiling hot water. A mild dish soap is also important. Avoid abrasive sponges or harsh brushes. These can scratch the finish or damage the seal around the lid. Gentle care ensures the vacuum remains intact. It allows your Brumate to perform as intended.

It is also important to dry your Brumate cup thoroughly after washing. Trapped moisture can lead to mold or mildew growth, especially in hard-to-reach areas. Inverting the cup or using a soft cloth helps dry it completely. Proper drying also helps maintain the cup's overall hygiene. This careful approach maintains the integrity of the cup for years of use.
