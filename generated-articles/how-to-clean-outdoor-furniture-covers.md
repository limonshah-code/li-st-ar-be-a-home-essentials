## How To Clean Outdoor Furniture Covers

Your outdoor furniture covers work hard. They stand between your beautiful patio furniture and the elements. Over time, dirt, dust, pollen, and even mildew build up. This grime not only looks bad but also shortens the cover's life. Keeping these covers clean is important for their function and appearance.

I often think about how much abuse my own outdoor covers take. A little regular cleaning keeps them looking great. It also ensures they continue to protect my furniture effectively. This guide will show you simple ways to clean your outdoor furniture covers. You will learn about hand washing, machine washing, and stain removal. Let's make your covers look new again.

### Takeaway

*   **Regular Cleaning:** Brush off loose debris frequently.
*   **Spot Treat Stains:** Address mold, mildew, or sap quickly.
*   **Choose Method Wisely:** Hand wash for large or delicate covers; machine wash for smaller, durable ones.
*   **Air Dry Completely:** Always air dry to prevent shrinkage and mildew.
*   **Reapply Protection:** Consider waterproofing after deep cleaning.

### Main Query Answer

To clean outdoor furniture covers, remove them from furniture. Brush off loose debris. Spot clean any specific stains with a mild solution. Then, hand wash with mild soap and water or machine wash on a delicate cycle, if recommended. Rinse thoroughly and air dry completely.

### The Importance of Cleaning Outdoor Furniture Covers

Outdoor furniture covers are your first line of defense against the weather. They shield your chairs, sofas, and tables from sun, rain, and snow. Without them, your outdoor furniture would quickly deteriorate. These covers face constant exposure to nature's elements. They collect dust, dirt, bird droppings, and tree sap. Over time, these materials can degrade the cover fabric.

Allowing dirt and grime to accumulate on the covers is not ideal. It creates a breeding ground for mold and mildew. This can lead to unpleasant odors and damage the cover material. Furthermore, dirty covers transfer grime to your clean furniture. This defeats their purpose of protection. Regular cleaning extends the lifespan of your covers. It also helps them maintain their protective qualities.

A clean cover looks better too. It contributes to a more inviting outdoor space. Think about sitting on your patio with fresh, clean covers. This simple act of cleaning protects your investment. It ensures your outdoor living area stays beautiful and functional. I always feel better knowing my covers are in good shape.

### Gathering Your Cleaning Arsenal: Essential Tools and Supplies

Before you start cleaning, gather all your supplies. Having everything ready makes the job easier. You will need a few basic items from around your home. Specific cleaning solutions may be necessary for tough stains. Prepare your workspace outdoors or in a large area. This helps you avoid making a mess inside.

Here is a list of essential tools and supplies:

*   **Soft-bristle brush:** This helps remove loose dirt without damaging the fabric. A car wash brush or a scrub brush works well.
*   **Garden hose:** A hose is essential for rinsing the covers thoroughly. Make sure it reaches your cleaning area.
*   **Buckets:** You will need one or two buckets for mixing cleaning solutions.
*   **Mild soap:** Dish soap or a gentle laundry detergent works well. Avoid harsh chemicals unless dealing with specific issues like mildew.
*   **White vinegar or bleach alternative:** These are useful for mold and mildew stains. Always test on an inconspicuous area first.
*   **Spray bottle:** This helps apply spot treatments or diluted solutions evenly.
*   **Clean cloths or sponges:** Use these for scrubbing stains and wiping surfaces.
*   **Gloves:** Protect your hands, especially when using stronger cleaning agents.
*   **Drying rack or clothesline:** These provide a good way to air dry covers.

Having these items ready will streamline your cleaning process. It will also help you get the best results. I find that starting with the right tools always makes a task less daunting.

### Step-by-Step Guide to Hand Washing Outdoor Furniture Covers

Hand washing is often the best method for outdoor furniture covers. It is gentle on the fabric. It is also suitable for larger covers or those with delicate features. This method gives you direct control over the cleaning process. It helps ensure a thorough clean without causing damage. I find it very satisfying to hand wash my covers.

Here are the steps to hand wash your outdoor furniture covers:

1.  **Remove and Brush Off Debris:** First, take the covers off your furniture. Lay them flat on a clean surface. Use a soft-bristle brush to remove all loose dirt, leaves, and cobwebs. This dry brushing prevents dirt from becoming muddy during washing.
2.  **Rinse Thoroughly:** Use your garden hose to spray down the covers. Rinse both sides to wet the fabric completely. This also helps dislodge more surface dirt.
3.  **Prepare Cleaning Solution:** Fill a bucket with warm water. Add a small amount of mild liquid soap. About 1/4 cup per gallon of water is a good starting point. Stir the water to create suds.
4.  **Apply and Scrub:** Dip your soft-bristle brush or sponge into the soapy water. Apply the solution to the cover fabric. Gently scrub the entire surface. Pay extra attention to heavily soiled areas or stains. For stubborn spots, let the solution sit for a few minutes.
5.  **Rinse Completely:** After scrubbing, rinse the covers again with your garden hose. Rinse both sides until no soap suds remain. Soap residue can attract dirt later or cause fabric damage. Ensure all cleaning solution is gone.
6.  **Spot Treat Tough Stains (if needed):** If any stains persist, use a specialized spot cleaner. A diluted solution of white vinegar works well for mildew. Always follow product instructions for commercial cleaners. For more details on tackling specific types of stains, you might find our guide on [how to clean an outdoor rug](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-clean-an-outdoor-rug) helpful, as some principles apply.
7.  **Air Dry:** Hang the covers over a clothesline or drying rack. Make sure air circulates freely around them. Allow them to dry completely before putting them back on furniture or storing them. This prevents mildew growth. Do not put them in a dryer.

This hand washing method helps maintain your covers' integrity. It keeps them clean and ready to protect your outdoor furniture.

### Machine Washing Patio Furniture Covers: When and How

Machine washing offers convenience for some outdoor furniture covers. This method is best for smaller, more durable covers. Always check the care tag on your cover first. Some materials are not machine washable. Ignoring care instructions can damage the fabric or the cover's protective coating. I only machine wash covers when I am certain it is safe.

Here are the guidelines for machine washing patio furniture covers:

1.  **Check Care Label:** Look for a tag with washing instructions. Most outdoor covers recommend hand washing or spot cleaning. If machine washing is allowed, it will specify the water temperature and cycle.
2.  **Pre-Treat Stains:** Before placing covers in the washing machine, pre-treat any visible stains. Apply a suitable stain remover directly to the spot. Allow it to soak for a few minutes. This helps the machine wash be more effective.
3.  **Load the Machine:** Place one or two covers in the washing machine at a time. Do not overload the machine. Overloading prevents thorough cleaning and rinsing. It can also strain your machine.
4.  **Select Settings:** Use a gentle cycle with cold water. Cold water protects the fabric and its UV-protective coatings. Hot water can cause fading or shrinkage.
5.  **Add Mild Detergent:** Use a small amount of mild laundry detergent. Avoid harsh detergents, fabric softeners, or bleach. Fabric softeners can break down water-resistant coatings. For detailed guidance on detergents and machine settings, you may refer to our comprehensive article on [how to wash patio furniture covers in washing machine](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-wash-patio-furniture-covers-in-washing-machine).
6.  **Run Cycle and Rinse:** Allow the machine to complete the wash and rinse cycles. If your machine has an extra rinse option, use it. This ensures all detergent residue is removed.
7.  **Air Dry:** Remove covers immediately after the cycle finishes. Hang them to air dry completely. Never put outdoor furniture covers in a tumble dryer. The heat can damage the fabric and its weather-resistant properties.

Machine washing saves time for appropriate covers. Always prioritize the care label's instructions. This ensures your covers remain functional and look good.

### Tackling Common Stains: Mold, Mildew, and Tree Sap

Outdoor furniture covers face tough opponents: mold, mildew, and tree sap. These stains need specific treatment. Ignoring them can lead to permanent damage or unpleasant smells. Addressing them quickly is key to successful stain removal. I have dealt with many of these myself.

Here is how to tackle common outdoor cover stains:

#### Mold and Mildew

Mold and mildew thrive in damp, shaded environments. They appear as black, green, or gray spots. They also carry a musty odor.

1.  **Brush Off Loose Growth:** First, dry brush any visible mold or mildew. Do this outdoors to avoid spreading spores indoors.
2.  **Prepare a Solution:** Mix one part white vinegar with one part water in a spray bottle. Alternatively, use a commercial mildew remover formulated for outdoor fabrics. A very diluted bleach solution (1 part bleach to 10 parts water) can be used on light-colored, bleach-safe fabrics. Always test in an inconspicuous area first.
3.  **Apply and Soak:** Spray the solution directly onto the affected areas. Let it soak for 10-15 minutes. For stubborn stains, you may need a longer soak time.
4.  **Scrub and Rinse:** Use a soft-bristle brush to gently scrub the stained areas. Rinse thoroughly with a garden hose. Repeat if necessary until the mold and mildew are gone. This process is similar to how you would clean other outdoor items, like [how to clean outdoor furniture](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-clean-outdoor-furniture) itself.

#### Tree Sap

Tree sap is sticky and difficult to remove. It often comes from overhanging trees.

1.  **Harden the Sap:** Place an ice cube on the sap for a few minutes. This hardens the sap, making it easier to remove.
2.  **Scrape Gently:** Use a dull knife or a plastic scraper to gently scrape off the hardened sap. Be careful not to damage the fabric.
3.  **Apply Solvent:** If residue remains, dab a small amount of rubbing alcohol onto a clean cloth. Gently blot the sap stain. You can also use a small amount of mineral spirits, but test it first.
4.  **Wash and Rinse:** Once the sap is gone, wash the area with mild soap and water. Rinse thoroughly with a hose.

Always test any cleaning solution on a small, hidden area first. This prevents accidental damage or discoloration. Prompt action makes these tough stains much easier to remove.

### Drying and Storing Your Clean Outdoor Covers Correctly

Proper drying and storage are just as important as cleaning. Incorrect drying can lead to mildew, even after a thorough wash. Improper storage can damage covers or invite pests. I always ensure my covers are completely dry before putting them away. This protects my investment.

Here are the best practices for drying and storing your outdoor furniture covers:

#### Drying

1.  **Air Dry Only:** Always air dry your outdoor furniture covers. Hang them over a clothesline, a railing, or a large drying rack. Ensure good air circulation around both sides of the cover.
2.  **Avoid Direct Sunlight (for extended periods):** While sunlight helps drying, prolonged exposure can fade colors. If drying in direct sun, turn the covers periodically. This promotes even drying and reduces fading.
3.  **No Tumble Dryer:** Never put outdoor furniture covers in a tumble dryer. High heat can shrink the fabric. It also damages the water-resistant and UV-protective coatings. This significantly reduces the cover's effectiveness and lifespan.
4.  **Check for Complete Dryness:** Feel the covers all over. Make sure they are completely dry, especially in seams and folded areas. Even slight dampness can cause mildew to grow when stored.

#### Storing

1.  **Fold Neatly:** Once completely dry, fold your covers neatly. This prevents excessive creasing. It also makes them easier to store.
2.  **Choose a Dry Location:** Store covers in a cool, dry place. Garages, sheds, or storage bins are good options. Avoid damp basements or areas with high humidity.
3.  **Use a Storage Bag (Optional):** Many covers come with a storage bag. Using this bag protects the cover from dust and pests during off-season storage. 4.  **Elevate from Ground:** If storing on a concrete floor, place covers on a pallet or shelf. This keeps them off the cold, potentially damp ground.
5.  **Prevent Pests:** Ensure your storage area is free from rodents or insects. Pests can chew through fabric, damaging your covers. For more detailed tips on protecting and preserving your covers during the off-season, explore our guide on [how to store outdoor furniture covers](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-store-outdoor-furniture-covers).

Following these drying and storage tips will keep your covers in excellent condition. They will be ready for use season after season.

### Maintaining Your Covers: Tips for Long-Term Protection

Cleaning your outdoor furniture covers is a start. Long-term maintenance ensures they last even longer. A little ongoing care makes a big difference. It prevents major cleaning tasks later. My own covers benefit greatly from regular attention.

Here are some tips for long-term protection:

*   **Regular Brushing:** Make it a habit to brush off loose dirt and debris. Do this weekly or after strong winds. A quick brush prevents grime from embedding into the fabric. It also keeps your covers looking tidy.
*   **Prompt Stain Removal:** Address spills and stains immediately. The longer a stain sits, the harder it is to remove. Keep a spray bottle with a mild soap solution ready for quick clean-ups.
*   **Reapply Water Repellent:** Over time, the water-resistant coating on your covers can wear off. After a deep clean, or every few seasons, reapply a fabric guard. These products restore water repellency. They help water bead up and roll off. This extends the cover's protective life. Follow product instructions carefully.
*   **Proper Fit is Key:** Ensure your covers fit your furniture well. Covers that are too tight can tear. Covers that are too loose can flap in the wind. This causes wear and allows water to pool. If you are ever unsure about the right size, learning [how to measure for patio furniture covers](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-measure-for-patio-furniture-covers) can help ensure a perfect fit, which is crucial for both protection and longevity.
*   **Ventilation:** If possible, choose covers with built-in vents. These vents allow air circulation. This helps prevent moisture buildup and mildew growth underneath. If your covers do not have vents, lift them occasionally on sunny days. This lets trapped moisture escape.
*   **Seasonal Deep Cleaning:** Perform a thorough hand wash or machine wash (if suitable) at least once or twice a year. This is ideal at the start or end of the outdoor season. It removes accumulated grime and prepares them for the next period of use or storage.

These simple maintenance steps protect your covers. They ensure your outdoor furniture stays safe and sound for years to come.

### FAQ Section

#### How often should I clean my outdoor furniture covers?

You should brush off loose debris weekly or bi-weekly. Perform a thorough hand wash or machine wash at least once or twice a year. This is ideal at the beginning and end of the outdoor season. Immediate spot cleaning is essential for spills or stains. Regular care prevents grime buildup.

#### Can I use bleach on outdoor furniture covers?

Most outdoor furniture covers are not suitable for bleach. Bleach can damage fabric fibers and fade colors. It also degrades water-resistant coatings. Only use a very diluted bleach solution on light-colored, bleach-safe fabrics for mold or mildew. Always test it on a hidden area first.

#### What if my covers have mold or mildew?

For mold or mildew, start by dry brushing the affected area. Then, spray with a solution of one part white vinegar and one part water. Let it sit for 10-15 minutes. Gently scrub with a soft brush and rinse thoroughly. Repeat if needed. Specialized outdoor fabric mildew removers are also effective.

#### Can I put outdoor furniture covers in the dryer?

No, never put outdoor furniture covers in a tumble dryer. High heat will shrink the fabric. It also damages the cover's water-resistant and UV-protective coatings. Always air dry your covers completely by hanging them on a clothesline or rack.

#### How do I waterproof my covers after cleaning?

After a deep clean, you can reapply a fabric guard or water-repellent spray. These products restore the cover's water resistance. Ensure the cover is completely clean and dry before applying. Follow the product's instructions for application and curing time.

#### What's the best detergent for outdoor covers?

The best detergent is a mild liquid soap or gentle laundry detergent. Avoid harsh chemicals, strong detergents, or fabric softeners. These can strip protective coatings from the fabric. Dish soap mixed with water also works well for hand washing. Always rinse thoroughly to remove all soap residue.
