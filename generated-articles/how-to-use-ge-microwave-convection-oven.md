### What is a Convection Microwave?

A convection microwave is a hybrid appliance. It does more than just reheat leftovers. It includes a fan and a heating element inside. These elements work together to circulate hot air evenly. This hot air distribution cooks food from all sides, just like a traditional oven.

This means you get crispy, browned results. Microwaves, by contrast, heat water molecules in food. This makes them fast for reheating or steaming. A convection microwave gives you the best of both worlds. You can choose rapid heating or thorough baking.

## Essential Accessories and Cookware for Your GE Oven

The right cookware makes a big difference in your cooking results. Using the wrong type of dish can even damage your appliance. Your GE microwave convection oven needs specific types of accessories for different cooking modes. Understanding these requirements ensures safe and effective cooking. Always check the labels on your dishes. Look for "microwave safe" or "oven safe" markings.

Most GE convection microwaves come with a metal rack. This rack is essential for convection cooking. It lifts food, allowing hot air to circulate freely. Do not use this metal rack during microwave-only cooking. It can cause arcing or damage. Knowing which dishes to use prevents problems.

### Safe Cookware for Microwave Mode

For microwave-only cooking, stick to non-metallic dishes. Glass, ceramic, and microwave-safe plastic containers are ideal. Many plastic containers have a microwave-safe symbol, often wavy lines or a small microwave icon. Paper plates and towels are also generally safe for short bursts. Avoid using metal, including aluminum foil, in microwave mode.

Metal reflects microwaves. This can create sparks or damage the magnetron. Do not use dishes with metallic glazes or trims either. These also pose a risk. If you are unsure about a dish, check its manufacturer's guidelines. A quick test is to place the empty dish in the microwave with a cup of water. Microwave for one minute on high. If the dish remains cool and the water heats, it is likely microwave-safe.

### Convection-Friendly Cookware

When using the convection or combination mode, you can use most traditional oven-safe cookware. This includes metal baking pans, roasting dishes, and casserole dishes. Glass and ceramic bakeware also work well. Silicone bakeware is another excellent option. These materials tolerate high heat. Ensure the dishes fit comfortably inside your oven.

Always use the metal rack provided for convection cooking. It promotes even heating. You can also use aluminum foil in convection mode. It is safe because there are no microwaves involved in this mode. However, for combination modes, check your manual regarding aluminum foil. Some manufacturers advise against it due to potential interactions. ## FAQ Section

### Can I use metal in my GE microwave convection oven?
Yes, you can use metal cookware in the convection and combination modes of your GE oven. The metal racks provided are specifically for these modes. However, never use metal, including aluminum foil, when using the microwave-only function, as it can cause arcing and damage.

### What is the difference between convection and microwave modes?
Microwave mode heats food quickly by exciting water molecules. It is ideal for reheating and defrosting. Convection mode uses a fan and heating element to circulate hot air, cooking food evenly and allowing for browning and crisping, like a traditional oven.

### How do I preheat my GE convection microwave?
To preheat, select the "Convection" mode, then set your desired temperature. The oven will start heating and usually display a preheating message. Wait for the oven to beep or signal that it has reached the set temperature before placing your food inside.

### Can I bake cakes in my GE convection microwave?
Absolutely, your GE convection microwave is perfect for baking cakes. Use the convection mode with appropriate oven-safe bakeware, such as glass or ceramic cake pans. Remember to preheat the oven and place the cake on the metal rack for even heat circulation.

### Why is my food not cooking evenly in convection mode?
Uneven cooking often results from overcrowding the oven or blocking airflow. Ensure food is placed on the provided metal rack and that there is enough space around the dish for hot air to circulate. Flipping or rotating food halfway through cooking can also help.

### Do I need special cookware for combination cooking?
For combination cooking, use cookware that is safe for both microwave and high oven temperatures. Glass, ceramic, and silicone dishes are generally suitable. Always use the metal rack if your model requires it for combination mode, and avoid aluminum foil unless your manual specifically states it is safe for that mode.
