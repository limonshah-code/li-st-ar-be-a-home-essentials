## Finding Your KitchenAid Dishwasher Model Number

Locating your KitchenAid dishwasher model number is usually straightforward. Manufacturers place this vital information in easily accessible spots. You do not need special tools or extensive searching. Knowing where to look saves you time and effort. I usually check these spots first when I need to find information about my own dishwasher.

The most common place to find the model number is on a label around the dishwasher's door frame. When you open the door, look along the top, side, or bottom edges of the frame. This label also contains the serial number, which is equally important for warranty or service. This label is often metallic or a sticker. It will list "Model No." or "MDL." followed by the alphanumeric code.

Other potential locations include the inside of the control panel door, or sometimes on the kickplate at the very bottom front of the appliance. Some older models might have the label on the back, but this is rare for dishwashers due to installation. Always check the door frame first. Once found, I recommend writing it down. This saves you from having to search for it again.

### Common Locations for the Model Number

*   **Along the Door Frame:** This is the most frequent location. Open the dishwasher door fully. Look at the edges of the tub opening. The label can be on the left, right, or top side of the frame. It is usually a small sticker or plate.
*   **Inside the Control Panel:** Less common, but some models might have a label inside the control panel assembly. You might need to gently pull open the panel if it has an access point.
*   **On the Kickplate:** The kickplate is the panel at the very bottom, beneath the dishwasher door. Sometimes, the label is placed here. You might need to bend down to see it clearly.
*   **Owner's Manual or Purchase Receipt:** If you still have your original owner's manual or the sales receipt, the model number will be listed there. This is a reliable source if you cannot find the physical label.

Make sure you write down both the model number and the serial number. The serial number identifies the specific unit from the production line. The model number identifies the product type. Both are important for support and warranty claims.

## Common Questions About KitchenAid Dishwasher Model Numbers

### What is the difference between a model number and a serial number?
A model number identifies the specific product type or series of your KitchenAid dishwasher. All units of the same model have the same model number. The serial number, however, is unique to your individual appliance. It tracks its manufacturing date and location. Both are important for product identification, warranty claims, and recall information.

### Can I find my KitchenAid dishwasher's age from its model number?
The model number itself does not directly indicate the manufacturing date. However, the serial number often contains coded information about the production year and week. You might need to consult KitchenAid's customer service or an online serial number decoder specific to Whirlpool (KitchenAid's parent company) to determine the exact age.

### Do all KitchenAid dishwashers follow the same model number format?
KitchenAid generally maintains a consistent format for its dishwasher model numbers, with letters often indicating control type and numbers detailing features. However, minor variations or new prefixes may appear with new product lines or significant design changes. The core structure, which segments information, remains largely the same across their modern dishwasher range.

### Why is my model number different from what I see online for a similar dishwasher?
Even slight variations in features, finish, or manufacturing batches can result in different suffixes or numbers in the model code. A "KDTM354ESS" and "KDTM354EVG" might be nearly identical but have different finishes. Always use your exact model number to ensure accurate information.

### What if I cannot find the model number on my KitchenAid dishwasher?
First, recheck all common locations, especially the door frame and kickplate. If it is still missing, check your owner's manual or original purchase receipt. As a last resort, contact KitchenAid customer support with any identifying features you know, like the color or approximate purchase year. They might be able to help identify it using other details.
