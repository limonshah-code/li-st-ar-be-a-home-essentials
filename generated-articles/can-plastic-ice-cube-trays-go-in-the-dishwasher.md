### Identifying Dishwasher-Safe Plastic: Symbols and Types

Knowing which plastic types are safe for your dishwasher is key. Manufacturers use specific symbols to help consumers. You usually find these symbols on the bottom of plastic items. Look for a symbol that looks like a dishwasher rack with water spraying. Sometimes, it just says "dishwasher safe." This label means the plastic can withstand the heat and detergents.

Another important indicator is the plastic resin identification code. This code is a number inside a chasing arrows triangle. Different numbers represent different types of plastic. For dishwasher safety, we often look for code #5. This code represents polypropylene (PP). Polypropylene is known for its high heat resistance. It is commonly used for food containers and kitchenware. Therefore, plastic ice cube trays made from #5 PP are generally safe. Other plastic types, like #1 (PETE/PET) or #6 (PS), are less heat-resistant. These plastics can soften or melt at lower temperatures. They are not suitable for dishwashers. Placing them in a hot wash cycle can ruin them. It can also create a mess inside your dishwasher. Always confirm the plastic type before washing. This helps prevent damage to your trays and appliance.

When you identify a dishwasher-safe tray, place it on the top rack. The top rack is further away from the heating element. This reduces direct exposure to intense heat. Lower temperatures on the top rack help prevent warping. This simple step adds an extra layer of protection. This practice applies to most plastic kitchen items. [Is plastic safe in dishwasher](https://homeessentialsguide.com/is-plastic-safe-in-dishwasher) often depends on the type and placement.

### The Risks of Washing Non-Dishwasher-Safe Plastic Trays

Putting non-dishwasher-safe plastic ice cube trays in your machine carries several risks. I have seen firsthand what high heat can do to unsuitable plastics. The most immediate problem is warping. Plastic softens when exposed to high temperatures. If it cools in a deformed state, it stays warped. A warped ice tray becomes difficult to use. It will not sit flat in your freezer. This makes filling it with water a messy task. The ice cubes also come out in odd shapes.

Another serious risk is melting. If the plastic gets too hot, it can melt completely. This often happens if the tray falls onto the heating element. Melted plastic sticks to surfaces inside the dishwasher. It is extremely difficult to remove. The melted plastic can also clog spray arms. This affects the dishwasher's cleaning performance. In some cases, it might even damage the appliance itself. Removing [how do you get plastic off dishwasher element](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-do-you-get-plastic-off-dishwasher-element) can be a frustrating and time-consuming repair.

Chemical leaching is another concern. Some plastics contain chemicals like BPA or phthalates. High heat can cause these chemicals to leach out. They can then contaminate other dishes in the wash. These chemicals are harmful. They can pose health risks if they get into your food or drinks. Dishwasher-safe plastics are designed to resist this. They do not leach harmful substances under normal wash conditions. This is why checking for the "dishwasher safe" label is so important.

Finally, non-dishwasher-safe plastic trays can retain odors. The high heat can make the plastic more porous. This allows it to absorb smells from food particles or detergents. Over time, your ice might taste like the dishwasher or old food. This problem is hard to fix. It can make your ice cube trays unusable. Always err on the side of caution. When in doubt, hand wash your plastic ice trays. This protects your trays, your dishwasher, and your health.

### Hand Washing Plastic Ice Trays: A Gentle Alternative

Hand washing is always a safe bet for plastic ice cube trays. It gives you complete control over the temperature and cleaning agents. This method is ideal for trays without "dishwasher safe" labels. It is also perfect for delicate plastics. I often choose hand washing to ensure my trays last a long time. It helps prevent any potential damage.

To hand wash your plastic ice trays, start with warm water. Hot water can still cause warping, especially with thinner plastics. Fill your sink with warm water and add a few drops of mild dish soap. I use standard liquid dish soap for this. It is gentle yet effective. Submerge your ice trays in the soapy water. Let them soak for about 10-15 minutes. This helps loosen any residue or grime. Soaking makes cleaning easier.

After soaking, use a soft sponge or a brush. Gently scrub all surfaces of the ice tray. Pay attention to the corners and crevices. Food particles and mineral deposits can build up in these areas. A small bottle brush can reach tight spots effectively. Ensure you remove any visible dirt or film. If you have any tough spots, a paste of baking soda and water can help. Apply it, let it sit, then scrub gently. This paste is a natural abrasive.

Once scrubbed, rinse the trays thoroughly under running warm water. Make sure to rinse away all soap residue. Soap can leave an unpleasant taste if not fully removed. After rinsing, let the trays air dry completely. You can place them on a drying rack. Or, you can use a clean towel to dry them. Ensuring they are fully dry prevents water spots. It also stops mold or mildew from growing. Hand washing is a simple and effective way to clean your trays. It extends their lifespan.

Plastic ice cube trays can develop common problems. Stains, odors, and accidental melting are typical issues. I have dealt with all of these at some point. Knowing how to fix them helps keep your trays usable. It also saves you from buying new ones often.

To remove stains, try a baking soda paste. Mix baking soda with a little water to form a thick paste. Apply this paste to the stained areas. Let it sit for several hours or overnight. Then, scrub it off with a soft brush. For stubborn stains, a diluted bleach solution (one tablespoon bleach per gallon of water) can work. Soak the tray for 30 minutes, then rinse thoroughly. Then, soak them in a mixture of warm water and white vinegar (equal parts). Let them soak for an hour. Vinegar neutralizes odors. Another method is to sprinkle baking soda generously over the dry trays. Let it sit overnight, then rinse. This also absorbs smells. ### FAQ Section

**Q1: Can all plastic ice cube trays go in the dishwasher?**
No, not all plastic ice cube trays are dishwasher safe. Only trays specifically marked "dishwasher safe" or made from heat-resistant plastics like polypropylene (PP, code #5) should go in the dishwasher. Others may warp, melt, or leach chemicals due to high heat. Always check the manufacturer's label or the plastic's resin code.

**Q2: What happens if I put non-dishwasher-safe plastic in the dishwasher?**
Putting non-dishwasher-safe plastic in the dishwasher can cause several problems. The plastic may warp and lose its shape. It could melt and stick to the dishwasher's heating element, causing damage and a difficult mess. High heat can also cause harmful chemicals to leach from certain plastics, contaminating other dishes.

**Q3: Is it better to put plastic ice cube trays on the top or bottom rack?**
It is always better to place plastic ice cube trays on the top rack of the dishwasher. The bottom rack is much closer to the heating element. This exposes items to higher temperatures. The top rack offers a gentler wash with less intense heat. This significantly reduces the risk of warping or melting for plastic items.

**Q4: How can I tell if my plastic ice cube tray is "dishwasher safe"?**
Look for a specific symbol on the bottom or side of your plastic ice cube tray. This symbol often looks like a dishwasher rack with water spraying over it. Alternatively, the tray may have the words "dishwasher safe" printed on it. You can also check for a plastic resin code, with #5 (polypropylene) usually indicating dishwasher safety.

**Q5: What is the best way to clean plastic ice cube trays by hand?**
To hand wash plastic ice cube trays, use warm (not hot) water and a few drops of mild dish soap. Let them soak for 10-15 minutes to loosen residue. Use a soft sponge or brush to scrub all surfaces and crevices. Rinse thoroughly to remove all soap. Then, allow the trays to air dry completely to prevent water spots and mildew.

**Q6: Can I use hot water when hand washing plastic ice cube trays?**
It is best to use warm water when hand washing plastic ice cube trays. Very hot water, similar to high dishwasher temperatures, can still cause some plastics to soften and potentially warp. Warm water is effective for cleaning without posing a risk of heat damage. This helps maintain the tray's original shape and integrity.
