### The Most Common Locations for Your Whirlpool Model Number

Finding your Whirlpool refrigerator's model number is usually straightforward. Most manufacturers place this information in easily accessible spots. Knowing these primary locations will save you time and effort. I usually start my search right inside the refrigerator door.

#### Inside the Fresh Food Compartment

The most frequent place to find the model and serial number is within the fresh food compartment. Open the main refrigerator door fully. Look along the top, side walls, or on the ceiling inside the compartment. The label is often a white or silver sticker, typically about 2x3 inches in size, with black text. It might be near the crisper drawers or on the side wall above them.

Sometimes, the label is slightly hidden. It might be behind one of the removable shelves or drawers, especially in older models. Take a moment to clear out a few items and shine a flashlight into the corners. The label contains more than just the model number; it often lists the serial number, manufacturing date, and electrical specifications. Always look for a clear, legible label first.

#### On the Door Frame (Top or Side)

Another very common spot for the model number is on the refrigerator's door frame. This is the edge of the refrigerator body that the door seals against when closed. Check both the top and side frames. The label might be tucked into the crease or directly on the visible part of the frame.

For side-by-side models, check both the freezer and fresh food door frames. The label is typically on the fresh food side. You may need to open the door fully to see the entire frame. This location is popular because it is usually protected from spills and wear, keeping the label legible for a long time. It is a quick check that often yields results.

### Checking Behind the Crisper Drawers and Kick Plate

Sometimes, the model number tag is in a less obvious but still common location. These spots are often chosen to protect the label from everyday wear and tear. It might take a bit more effort to reach them, but they are worth checking.

#### Under the Crisper Drawers

Many Whirlpool refrigerators place the model and serial number tag underneath the crisper drawers. These are the clear plastic bins at the bottom of your fresh food compartment. To check this spot, you will need to remove one or both of the crisper drawers. This is a simple process; just pull them out until they come free from their tracks.

Once the drawers are out, look at the floor of the refrigerator compartment where the drawers normally sit. The label is usually affixed to the plastic or metal surface there. This location keeps the label protected from direct exposure to food and moisture, helping it remain legible over many years. After finding the number, simply slide the crisper drawers back into place.

#### Behind the Kick Plate (Grille)

The kick plate, also known as the toe grille, is the removable panel at the very bottom front of your refrigerator. It covers the condenser coils and other mechanical components. For some Whirlpool models, especially older ones, the model and serial number tag is located behind this panel. You will need to remove the kick plate to access it.

Typically, the kick plate is held in place by two or three screws or by clips. Use a screwdriver (often a Phillips head) to remove the screws, or gently pull on the plate if it is clip-on. Once removed, shine a flashlight into the area behind it. The label will be on the frame of the refrigerator, usually on the right or left side. This spot can be dusty, so be prepared to wipe it clean to read the numbers. For maintenance, it is also a good opportunity to [clean your Whirlpool side-by-side refrigerator coils](https://homeessentialsguide.com/how-to-clean-whirlpool-side-by-side-refrigerator-coils).

### Understanding Your Whirlpool Model and Serial Number

Once you locate that elusive label, you will notice it contains more than just one set of numbers and letters. It usually includes both a model number and a serial number. Understanding the difference between these two identifiers is important. They serve different purposes but both provide valuable information about your Whirlpool appliance.

The **model number** tells you the specific design and features of your refrigerator. It is a general identifier that applies to thousands of identical units produced by Whirlpool. For example, all Whirlpool refrigerators with model number "WRF555SDFZ" are the same type of refrigerator, with the same design, capacity, and basic features. When you need a replacement part or a user manual, the model number is what you use. It helps Whirlpool categorize their products and provide universal support for that specific design.

The **serial number**, on the other hand, is unique to *your individual unit*. No two Whirlpool refrigerators, even of the exact same model, will have the same serial number. Think of it like a vehicle identification number (VIN) for a car. This number provides specific details about when and where your particular refrigerator was manufactured. The first few digits or letters of the serial number often encode the manufacturing date. For example, it might indicate the year and week your specific appliance rolled off the assembly line.

Both numbers work together. The model number defines the product type. The serial number identifies the individual unit within that product type. When contacting customer service, they often ask for both. This allows them to identify the exact product type and also track the specific unit for warranty, recall, or service history purposes. Knowing both helps you fully understand your appliance's background.

### Tips for Successfully Finding Your Model Number

Finding the model number can sometimes be tricky. The label might be faded, in an awkward spot, or simply hard to read. A few simple tips can make your search much easier and more successful. I have used these methods myself when hunting down appliance information.

First, **ensure good lighting**. The labels are often small and printed with fine text. Use a flashlight, your phone's light, or a headlamp to illuminate the area. This can make a huge difference in distinguishing characters, especially if the label is in a dark corner or has discolored over time. Even in a well-lit kitchen, internal compartments can be dim.

Second, **clean the area gently**. Model number labels, especially those inside the refrigerator or behind the kick plate, can accumulate dirt, dust, or food debris. Use a damp cloth to wipe the area clean before attempting to read the number. Do not scrub too hard, as this could further damage a faded label. A clean surface makes the numbers much clearer.

Third, **take a photo with your phone**. If the label is in an awkward position or hard to see clearly with your eyes, take a picture with your smartphone. Your phone's camera often has better zoom and can capture details you might miss. You can then zoom in on the photo to read the numbers at your leisure. Sometimes, changing the angle of the photo helps reduce glare.

Fourth, **be patient and thorough**. Do not just glance at a spot and move on. Take your time to carefully inspect all the common locations. Whirlpool sometimes places labels in less intuitive spots. Run your hand along surfaces to feel for the edge of a sticker you might not immediately see. A quick search might miss a cleverly hidden label.

Finally, **know what to do if it's unreadable**. If the label is completely gone or illegible, do not give up. As mentioned earlier, check your owner's manual or original receipt. If those are gone too, contacting Whirlpool customer support with any other details you have (like approximate purchase date or features) is your next step. They often have ways to help you identify your model even without a perfect label.
